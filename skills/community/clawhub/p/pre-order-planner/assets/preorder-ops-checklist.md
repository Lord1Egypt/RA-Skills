# Pre-Order Operations Checklist

Use this before launching any pre-order campaign.

---

## Before Announcing (T-14 Days+)

### Supplier & Production
- [ ] Final product specification locked (no changes post-announcement)
- [ ] Production lead time confirmed with supplier in writing
- [ ] Ship date committed: [DATE] — add 14-day buffer to supplier promise date
- [ ] Minimum order quantity (MOQ) confirmed — enough to cover expected pre-orders + safety stock
- [ ] Payment terms to supplier agreed upon
- [ ] Quality control inspection scheduled (if applicable)

### Fulfillment
- [ ] 3PL / warehouse notified of expected volume and ship date
- [ ] Packaging confirmed (boxes, inserts, custom packaging if applicable)
- [ ] Shipping rates locked in for the expected order volume tier
- [ ] International shipping zones confirmed (if applicable)
- [ ] Duties/customs handling confirmed for international orders

### Platform Setup
- [ ] Pre-order product listing created and set to "pre-order" state
- [ ] Correct ship date displayed on product page
- [ ] Refund policy explicitly stated on product page
- [ ] Payment capture timing confirmed (at order vs. at ship)
- [ ] Early-bird pricing rules configured correctly
- [ ] Quantity limits set (if using tiered early-bird caps)

---

## Campaign Launch Day

- [ ] Waitlist landing page live and tested on mobile
- [ ] Email/SMS opt-in confirmed working
- [ ] First announcement email queued and proofread
- [ ] Social content drafted and ready to publish
- [ ] Team briefed on expected questions + approved FAQ responses
- [ ] Analytics tracking confirmed (conversion events, pixel)

---

## During Campaign (Weekly)

- [ ] Waitlist count updated on landing page (if displaying live)
- [ ] Weekly subscriber update email sent
- [ ] Any production updates communicated proactively
- [ ] Remaining early-bird quantity updated (if showing live counter)
- [ ] Pre-order total vs. forecast tracked

---

## Pre-Ship (T-7 Days)

- [ ] Final order count sent to supplier / 3PL
- [ ] Fulfillment file (name, address, SKU, quantity) prepared and verified
- [ ] Shipping labels / tracking numbers confirmed available at ship date
- [ ] "Your order shipped" email template ready in ESP
- [ ] Customer service queue cleared and team prepped for delivery questions

---

## Delay Contingency

If ship date will be missed:

- [ ] Notify customers immediately — don't wait until the original date
- [ ] Email subject: "Update on your [Product Name] order"
- [ ] Be specific: new expected ship date, reason (brief), and what you're doing about it
- [ ] Offer option: keep order at original price, or request full refund, no questions asked
- [ ] Do not offer partial refunds as the only option — full refund must be available

Template:

> Hi [first name],
>
> I need to give you an honest update on your [Product Name] order.
>
> [Brief, specific reason for delay]
>
> Your new expected ship date is [date].
>
> If you'd prefer not to wait, you can request a full refund here: [link]. No forms, no hoops.
>
> If you're willing to stick with us, thank you — truly. We'll make it worth the wait.
>
> – [Founder name]

---

## Post-Delivery (Days 7–30)

- [ ] Review request email sent (Day 7)
- [ ] Social UGC prompt published
- [ ] Return/exchange rate monitored
- [ ] Customer satisfaction data captured (NPS survey or simple reply prompt)
- [ ] Lessons documented for next pre-order campaign
- [ ] Waitlist captured for future drops
