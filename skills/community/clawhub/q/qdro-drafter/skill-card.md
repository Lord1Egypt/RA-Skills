## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill drafts legal order language that could be incorrect, incomplete, or unsuitable for a specific retirement plan or court. <br>
Mitigation: Require matrimonial-attorney review before plan-administrator submission or court entry, and treat missing facts as unresolved information required before submission. <br>
Risk: Using the wrong order type for TSP, CSRS/FERS, uniformed-services retired pay, IRA, or governmental plans can cause rejection or loss of rights. <br>
Mitigation: Complete plan typing first, route to the correct QDRO, COAP, TSP RBCO, MPDO, governmental DRO, or IRA transfer mechanism, and use plan-published model rules when available. <br>
Risk: Full SSNs or account identifiers could be exposed in the working draft. <br>
Mitigation: Use last-4 identifiers in the draft body and reserve full identifiers for a sealed identifying-information addendum or required cover-letter enclosure. <br>
Risk: Participant retirement, loan, disability, or death before entry can reduce or extinguish alternate-payee rights. <br>
Mitigation: Flag timing risks during intake and route the draft for plan-administrator pre-approval when accepted. <br>
