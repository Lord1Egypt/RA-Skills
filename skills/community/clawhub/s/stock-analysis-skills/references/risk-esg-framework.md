# Risk & ESG Assessment Framework — Reference Guide

Methodology for Module 7 (Risk Analysis) and Module 8 (ESG & Governance Assessment).

---

## Module 7: Risk Analysis

### Risk Rating Criteria

Assign one of three risk levels to each risk category:

| Risk Level | Definition |
|------------|------------|
| **Low** | Low probability of occurrence, and/or limited financial impact even if realized; mitigation measures are adequate |
| **Medium** | Moderate probability or significant impact; management has identified and partially mitigated |
| **High** | High probability, and/or material impact on earnings, cash flow, or equity value; mitigation measures are limited |

---

### 1. Business Risk

#### Customer Concentration
- **High**: Single customer contributes >20% of revenue, or top 5 customers combined >50%
- **Medium**: Single customer contributes 10–20% of revenue
- **Low**: Diversified customer base; no single customer >10%

- **Follow-up questions:**
- Is revenue from long-term contracts or at-will/cancelable arrangements?
- Does the company disclose customer concentration in its 10-K risk factors and major customer disclosures?
- Have any key customer relationships recently shown signs of deterioration or displacement risk?

#### Product Obsolescence / Technology Risk
- **High**: Core product faces imminent disruption from AI, new entrants, or shifting demand preferences; R&D / product pipeline is weak
- **Medium**: Product under competitive pressure but company is adapting; R&D / product pipeline offers some hedge
- **Low**: Product has high stickiness, mission-critical deployment; company is at the forefront of technological change

**Disruption Risk Checklist:**
- [ ] Has a new technology emerged that could materially reduce demand for the core product?
- [ ] Are new entrants taking share at scale?
- [ ] Is the company's YoY revenue growth rate persistently declining?
- [ ] Is R&D spending increasing or decreasing as a % of revenue?

---

### 2. Financial Risk

#### Leverage & Debt
Key metrics:
- **Net Debt / EBITDA**: <1.5x = low leverage; 1.5–3.0x = moderate; >3.0x = high; >5.0x = highly stressed
- **Interest Coverage Ratio** (EBIT / Interest Expense): >5x = comfortable; 3–5x = moderate; <3x = concerning
- **Debt Maturity Profile**: Under elevated rate / tight credit conditions, near-term maturity walls heighten refinancing risk

**High financial risk signals:**
- High leverage combined with declining EBITDA
- Floating-rate exposure in a rising rate environment rapidly increasing interest burden
- Thin debt covenant headroom (within 15–20% of trigger levels)
- Heavy reliance on revolver / short-term borrowings to fund operations

#### Liquidity Risk
- **Current Ratio** (Current Assets / Current Liabilities): <1.0 = liquidity stress signal
- **Quick Ratio** ((Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities): more informative for companies with slow inventory turns
- **Cash burn**: For unprofitable companies, estimate "cash runway" = Cash / Quarterly net cash burn
- **Free Cash Flow (FCF)**: For profitable companies, consistently positive and growing FCF is one of the strongest liquidity signals

---

### 3. Market Risk

#### Beta & Volatility
- **Beta**: Measures sensitivity to broad market moves (US context: benchmark against S&P 500, Russell 2000, or Nasdaq Composite as appropriate)
  - Beta < 0.8: Defensive, lower volatility than market
  - Beta 0.8–1.2: In line with market
  - Beta > 1.2: High beta, amplifies market moves (common in tech, biotech, small-caps)
  - Beta > 2.0: Highly speculative, extreme market sensitivity

- **Historical Volatility**: Annualized standard deviation of daily returns. Higher volatility = wider potential return range = higher holding risk.

#### Macro Sensitivity
Some sectors are more cyclical and sensitive to macro variables:
| Sector | Key Macro Risk |
|--------|---------------|
| Banks | Credit losses in recession; NIM compression |
| Autos | Consumer confidence; credit availability |
| Airlines | Jet fuel prices; consumer discretionary cycle |
| Homebuilders | Mortgage rates; housing demand |
| Energy | Oil & gas price cycles; energy transition |
| Luxury goods | Wealth effect; consumer confidence |

### 4. Regulatory & Compliance Risk

**Regulatory exposure assessment framework:**

| Exposure Level | Characteristics |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **High** | Subject to regulatory investigation (antitrust, environmental, financial, disclosure, etc.); pending legislation would directly impact revenue model; history of significant penalties/remediation |
| **Medium** | In a heavily regulated sector with evolving rules; no active investigation but high policy uncertainty |
| **Low** | Limited direct regulatory exposure; low government contract dependence; minimal litigation and penalty history |

**Key compliance areas to verify (select based on company's sector):**
- Antitrust & unfair competition (FTC, DOJ enforcement)
- Data & privacy (CCPA/CPRA, state laws, proposed federal data privacy legislation)
- Environmental compliance (EPA permits, environmental penalties, carbon-related liabilities)
- Financial compliance (SEC/ exchange rules, FDIC/OCC oversight, AML/KYC)
- Healthcare & pharma (FDA regulation, PBM pricing, Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement, compliance in promotional practices)
- Export controls & sanctions compliance (especially for companies with international operations and critical technology/equipment)

### 5. ESG-Related Risk

ESG risk has transitioned from a "soft issue" to quantifiable financial materiality, driven by:
- Increasing integration of ESG factors by institutional investors into screening and pricing
- Tighter disclosure requirements (e.g., SEC climate disclosure rules, EU CSRD for companies with EU operations)
- Declining consumer and supply chain preference for low-ESG performers
- Higher ESG risk companies face elevated insurance and financing costs

**High ESG risk signals:**
- Persistent environmental violations/penalties or significant accident exposure
- Labor compliance issues, major labor disputes, or class action litigation
- Board independence insufficient; CEO/chairperson power overly concentrated with weak checks; dual-class shares with limited guardrails (if applicable)
- Executive compensation clearly misaligned with performance / shareholder returns (where comparable data is available)
- Significant revenue exposure to ESG-sensitive sectors (fossil fuels, tobacco, defense/weapons, gaming — adjust per market and regulatory context)

## Module 8: ESG & Governance Assessment

### E — Environmental Dimension

**Key metrics and disclosure points:**
- Total carbon emissions: Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (purchased electricity/heat), Scope 3 (supply chain + product use)
- Net-zero / carbon neutrality commitments: target year? Reliance on offsets?
- Renewable energy as a % of total energy consumption
- Water stress: operations in water-scarce regions?
- Waste generation and recycling rates
- Physical climate risk: are core assets in flood/extreme weather high-risk zones?

**ESG ratings to reference (where available):**
- MSCI ESG Rating (AAA to CCC)
- Sustainalytics ESG Risk Score (lower is better; <10 negligible, 10–20 low, 20–30 medium, 30–40 high, >40 severe)
- S&P Global ESG Score (0–100)
- CDP Climate Score (A to D-)

---

### S — Social Dimension

**Key dimensions:**
1. **Employee relations**: Employee satisfaction scores, voluntary turnover rate, safety records (e.g., TRIR), union/labor relations (reference Glassdoor, Blind, and company disclosures)
2. **Diversity & Inclusion (D&I)**: Board and senior leadership diversity composition (assess based on available disclosure, which varies by company)
3. **Supply chain standards**: Supplier code of conduct, audit mechanisms, supply chain labor compliance and human rights risk (focus based on sector and international exposure)
4. **Community impact**: CSR programs, community investment, philanthropic activities
5. **Product safety & quality**: Recall history, major safety incidents, product liability litigation

---

### G — Governance Dimension

**Board quality indicators:**
| Governance Factor | Best Practice | Red Flag |
|-------------------|---------------|----------|
| Board independence | High proportion of independent directors, balanced expertise | Insufficient independence or complex related-party relationships |
| Chair/CEO separation | Clear separation of roles, effective checks and balances | Power overly concentrated without effective oversight |
| Diversity | Balanced composition | Homogeneous structure |
| Tenure structure | Mix of experience and fresh perspectives | Excessively long average tenure risks "groupthink" |
| Overboarding | Manageable number of outside directorships | Directors serving on too many boards to fulfill duties effectively |
| Ownership structure | One share, one vote; transparent governance | Dual-class shares with weak guardrails (if applicable) |
| Compensation | Aligned with performance / shareholder returns; equity tied to long-term metrics (e.g., TSR) | Excessive guaranteed cash; pay disconnected from performance |

**Governance controversies to investigate:**
- Material litigation and arbitration (including securities fraud, major compliance disputes)
- Financial restatements, unusual audit opinions
- Material internal control weaknesses (e.g., adverse or disclaimer of opinion on internal controls)
- Whistleblower reports and major adverse event disclosures
- Related-party transactions, fund occupation, and guarantee-related governance risks

---

### ESG Scoring Guidance

| Score | Environmental | Social | Governance |
|-------|---------------|--------|------------|
| 5 | Clear net-zero / carbon neutrality targets; top-tier institutional ratings | Strong employment & safety record; high employee satisfaction; robust supply chain auditing | Highly independent board; compensation aligned; clean governance structure |
| 4 | Proactive emissions reduction with disclosure | Generally good with minor gaps | Substantially independent; incentives reasonably aligned |
| 3 | Industry average; limited commitments | Industry standard; no material controversies | Meets minimum governance requirements |
| 2 | Lags peers; poor disclosure | Significant controversies / litigation | Clear governance weaknesses; some red flags present |
| 1 | Non-compliance and high climate liabilities; poor disclosure | Serious labor violations / safety incidents / supply chain crises | Weak board independence; misaligned incentives; poor shareholder rights protections |

Module 8 total score: equal-weight average of the three component scores (Environmental, Social, Governance).
