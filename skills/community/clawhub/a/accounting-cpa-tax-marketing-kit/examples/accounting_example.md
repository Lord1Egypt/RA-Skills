### LinkedIn Post Set

**Post 1 — Tax Mistake Education**
Real estate investors in Las Vegas: the most common mistake I see at tax time is forgetting to track "basis" — what you originally paid for the property plus improvements.

When you sell, your taxable gain is calculated from basis, not the original purchase price. Skipping this means overpaying. 

What counts toward basis: purchase price, closing costs (most of them), capital improvements (new roof, HVAC, addition — not repairs).

What doesn't: regular maintenance, property management fees, depreciation already claimed.

If you've done multiple transactions without tracking this, an accounting review is worth doing before your next sale. A lot can be reconstructed from records.

*This is general tax education, not advice for your specific situation. Consult a qualified tax professional.*

**Post 2 — Thought Leadership (S-Corp)**
The question I get most from Las Vegas small business owners: "Should I be an S-Corp?"

Short answer: it depends on net profit and how much you pay yourself.

S-Corp election can reduce self-employment tax (15.3%) on profit above a reasonable salary. But it adds complexity: payroll, separate tax return, more bookkeeping.

General rule of thumb financial professionals often discuss: the savings start to outweigh the cost somewhere in the $50K-$80K net profit range, but the exact breakpoint is different for every business.

The decision also depends on your state (Nevada has no state income tax — that changes the math versus California).

If you're wondering whether it applies to your situation, that conversation is best had with your CPA before year-end, not in April.
