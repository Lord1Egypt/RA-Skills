# Prompt 03 — Reputation Management & Referral System
## Auto Body & Collision Repair Marketing Kit v1.0

**Purpose:** Generate review request sequences, GBP crisis response scripts, referral programs, and B2B outreach letters for auto body shops.

**All 7 compliance moats active:**
- No fabricated reviews, no incentivized reviews without FTC disclosure
- Responses to negative reviews: legal-safe, no admission of liability
- B2B letters: no "ASE-certified shop" or "eco-friendly" without documentation
- Warranty references: include terms disclosure

---

## GBP REVIEW RESPONSES (15 templates)

### Positive Reviews (5)

**5-star, general:**
Thank you for sharing your experience. We're glad the repair met your expectations — and we appreciate you choosing [Shop Name] for your collision work. If you ever need us again, we're here.

**5-star, mentions specific technician:**
Thank you for mentioning [technician name] — we'll pass this along. Our team takes the technical side seriously, from the estimate through delivery, and it means a lot when customers notice. Appreciate your business.

**5-star, insurance claim:**
Insurance claims can be stressful. We're glad we could make the documentation and repair process straightforward. Thank you for trusting us through that process.

**5-star, PDR:**
PDR results depend on picking the right approach for the right damage — we're glad we got it right on your vehicle. Thanks for the review.

**5-star, mentions waiting:**
We appreciate the patience and the kind words. Accurate repairs take the time they take — we'd rather take the extra day than rush the job. Thank you for understanding that.

### Mixed/Critical Reviews (10 — all legally safe, no admissions of liability)

**Supplement delay complaint:**
Thank you for taking the time to leave feedback. Insurance supplement reviews can add time to repairs that neither we nor the vehicle owner control — the insurer's review cycle for additional damage documentation varies by adjuster and by carrier. We should have communicated the timeline better on your repair. If you'd like to discuss the specifics, please call [phone] — we're available.

**Parts wait complaint:**
Appreciated, and understood. Parts availability for [model year vehicles] has been uneven — OEM backordering on [part category] has created delays we couldn't control but should have been clearer about upfront. We've adjusted how we communicate parts timelines. Thank you for the feedback.

**Estimate accuracy dispute:**
Thank you for the feedback. Hidden damage identified during disassembly can add to an original estimate when the initial inspection can't see behind panels. We provide written authorization before proceeding with any additional work — if you have questions about a specific charge, please call [phone] and we'll walk through the repair order line by line.

**"Color doesn't match" complaint:**
Color matching in auto refinishing involves variables — paint code accuracy, film build, blending technique, and clear coat type all affect the final appearance. If you believe there's a match issue, please call [phone] so we can see the vehicle in person. We stand behind our refinishing work and want to review this with you directly.

**"Car took too long" complaint:**
Thank you for the feedback. Repair timelines depend on parts availability, supplement approval, and inspection cycles — all of which affect how quickly we can complete and deliver. We should have kept you better informed along the way. If you'd like to discuss your specific repair, please call [phone].

**ADAS recalibration dispute:**
Thank you for raising this. ADAS sensor recalibration after bumper or panel replacement is a documented requirement for proper system function — it's not an optional add-on. If you have questions about why recalibration was required on your vehicle and what it involved, please call [phone] and we'll walk through it.

**"Didn't use OEM parts" complaint:**
Thank you for this feedback. All parts types used in your repair should have been disclosed on your repair order before installation. If parts used differed from what was authorized, that's a problem we want to review. Please call [phone] and ask for [manager name] — we'll pull the repair order and address this directly.

**Deductible dispute:**
Thank you for the feedback. We don't waive deductibles — we've found that accurate billing and complete documentation is a better long-term practice for both our customers and our insurer relationships. If you have questions about what your insurer covered versus your out-of-pocket, please call [phone] and we'll help you understand the supplement.

**"Previous damage wasn't disclosed" complaint:**
Thank you for sharing this. Pre-existing condition documentation is something we do as part of intake — we photograph vehicles on arrival to record pre-existing damage. If there's a discrepancy between what was documented and what was repaired, please call [phone] with your repair order number and we'll review it together.

**OSHA/paint safety concern from customer:**
Thank you for raising this. Our spray booth operations are conducted in accordance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.94 standards and our technicians use appropriate respiratory protection for isocyanate-containing coatings. If you have a specific concern about your experience in our facility, please call [phone] so we can address it directly.
