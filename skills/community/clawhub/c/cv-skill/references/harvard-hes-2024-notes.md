# Harvard HES 2024 Notes

Paraphrased checklist from the 2024 Harvard Extension School resume and cover letter guide.

## Resume Quality Bar

- Treat the resume as a brief, role-relevant summary, not a full biography.
- Tailor content to the position, industry, and employer priorities.
- Make the first scan easy: clear headings, consistent spacing, consistent emphasis, and enough white space.
- Order sections by strength and relevance. Within sections, use reverse chronological order unless a project or skills section needs stronger topical grouping.
- Use direct, active language. Prefer evidence, scope, and impact over adjectives.
- Quantify or qualify results when source material supports it.
- Check for spelling, grammar, missing phone/email, passive phrasing, weak organization, and lack of tailoring.

## Resume Content Rules

Include when relevant:

- name and contact line
- education
- experience
- projects or research
- activities, volunteer work, or leadership
- skills, languages, certifications, publications, or awards

Avoid:

- personal pronouns
- photos
- age or marital status
- references
- slang or colloquial phrasing
- numbered or lettered sections
- lines that begin with dates instead of role or institution context
- unsupported claims or invented metrics

## Bullet Rules

Strong bullets usually combine:

- action verb
- task or scope
- skill, method, tool, or context
- outcome, impact, or scale

Use role-appropriate active verbs. Keep categories available while rewriting:

- leadership and operations
- communication and stakeholder work
- research and analysis
- technical implementation
- teaching or training
- quantitative and finance
- creative and content
- helping, service, and support
- organization and process improvement

## Education Rules

- Show degree, field, institution, location when useful, and completion or expected date.
- Include coursework, honors, projects, campus involvement, or GPA only when they support the target role.
- Move education lower when experience is more relevant.
- For Harvard Extension School entries, represent the school and degree accurately; do not obscure the Extension School name or invent Harvard affiliations.

## Cover Letter Rules

Use cover letters as concise writing samples. They should be employer-specific, factual, and no longer than one page.

Recommended structure:

- sender contact block when needed
- date
- recipient name, title, company, and address when known
- greeting to a specific person when available
- opening: role, employer, why this position, why this candidate
- middle: 1-2 concrete examples connected to job requirements
- closing: interest, contribution, thanks, and interview availability
- signature

Style rules:

- research the employer before writing
- connect examples to the role instead of repeating the full resume
- use action language
- avoid overusing "I"
- match resume font and size
- export to PDF and inspect formatting before sending
