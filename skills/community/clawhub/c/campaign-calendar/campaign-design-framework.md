# Campaign Design Framework

This framework guides the design of individual campaigns within the calendar. Each campaign follows a four-phase structure: pre-heat, launch, sustain, and wind-down. The framework applies to Tier 1 and Tier 2 campaigns. Tier 3 events use an abbreviated version (launch + wind-down only).

---

## Phase Overview

Every campaign moves through four sequential phases. The total campaign duration and the length of each phase scale with the campaign's tier and the size of the associated event.

| Phase | Tier 1 Duration | Tier 2 Duration | Purpose | Budget Share |
|-------|----------------|----------------|---------|-------------|
| Pre-heat | 10-14 days | 5-7 days | Build awareness, grow retargeting audiences, prep content pipeline, brief creators | 15-20% |
| Launch | 2-5 days | 1-3 days | Maximum output across all channels during the event peak | 40-50% |
| Sustain | 5-10 days | 3-5 days | Maintain momentum with retargeting, follow-up content, extended offers | 20-25% |
| Wind-down | 2-4 days | 1-2 days | Close out promotions, transition messaging, collect data, reallocate budget | 5-10% |

**Total typical duration:** Tier 1 campaigns run 19-33 days end-to-end. Tier 2 campaigns run 10-17 days.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Heat

**Objective:** Generate awareness, build retargeting audiences, and ensure all campaign assets are ready before the peak selling window opens.

### Activities

**Audience building:**
- Launch video view campaigns targeting broad interest audiences to build retargeting pools. Goal: accumulate at least 1,000 video viewers or page visitors before launch phase begins.
- Activate email list with teaser messaging. For Tier 1 events, send 2-3 pre-heat emails (announcement, sneak peek, countdown). For Tier 2, send 1-2 emails.
- Post organic teaser content on TikTok to build anticipation. Use trending sounds and formats to maximize organic reach.

**Creator and affiliate preparation:**
- Brief all participating creators at least 10 days before the launch phase. The brief should include: product details, key talking points, discount codes or affiliate links, content format requirements, and embargo date (if applicable).
- Ship product samples to creators if they do not already have the product. Allow 5-7 business days for shipping plus 5-7 days for content production.
- Confirm creator posting schedules and align them with the launch phase timeline.

**Platform setup:**
- Submit product listings for any platform-sponsored promotional slots (flash deals, featured products, mega sale participation). Submission deadlines are typically 2-3 weeks before the event.
- Set up promotional pricing, coupon configurations, and bundle deals in the platform's seller tools. Test the checkout flow at maximum discount depth to verify pricing accuracy.
- Prepare livestream scheduling if applicable (book time slots, create event pages, set up product pins).

**Content production checkpoint:**
- All launch-phase content should be in final review or approved status by the midpoint of pre-heat. If production is behind, activate backup content plans (repurposed top-performing evergreen content).

### Phase Transition Criteria (Pre-Heat to Launch)

Move to launch phase when all of the following are true:
- Retargeting audience size meets the minimum threshold (1,000+ for Tier 2, 3,000+ for Tier 1)
- All launch-phase content assets are approved and scheduled
- Platform promotional listings are confirmed and active
- Creator content is received, reviewed, and cleared for posting
- Inventory is confirmed sufficient for projected demand (at least 2x the average weekly sell-through rate)

If any criterion is not met 48 hours before planned launch, escalate to the campaign owner for a go/no-go decision. Options: delay launch by 2-3 days, proceed with reduced scope, or cancel and reallocate budget.

---

## Phase 2: Launch

**Objective:** Maximize reach, traffic, and conversions during the peak event window. All channels fire simultaneously with coordinated messaging.

### Activities

**Paid advertising:**
- Deploy peak ad spend. Activate all planned ad sets simultaneously on launch day. Do not stagger launches across multiple days during the peak window -- the event traffic boost is time-limited.
- Run a minimum of 3-5 ad creative variants per ad set to enable rapid optimization. Include at least one product demo video, one UGC-style video, and one promotional graphic or text overlay video.
- Set campaign bid strategy to maximize conversions (not clicks or impressions) during launch. If ROAS target is critical, use target ROAS bidding but set the target 10-15% below your actual goal to avoid under-delivery during the learning phase.
- Monitor ad performance at 4-hour intervals on launch day. Pause any ad set with zero conversions after $50+ in spend and reallocate to the top performer.

**Content execution:**
- Publish all planned launch content on schedule. Priority order if resources are constrained: (1) livestream, (2) short-form video posts, (3) promotional graphics, (4) email/SMS.
- For TikTok Shop events: run at least one livestream during peak traffic hours (typically 7-10 PM local time). Pin promoted products and use event-specific hashtags.
- Affiliate creators should post during the first 24 hours of the launch window. Coordinate posting times to avoid overlap (stagger across the day for sustained feed presence).

**Promotion activation:**
- Activate all planned discounts, coupons, and deals at the start of the launch window. Verify that platform coupons are live and stacking correctly with shop-level discounts.
- Monitor for pricing errors -- check the first 5-10 orders to confirm effective prices match expectations.
- If a flash deal slot is part of the campaign, ensure the product is fully set up and inventory is allocated before the slot opens.

**Communication:**
- Send launch-day email to the full subscriber list with a clear call to action and direct links to promoted products.
- Post across all organic social channels with consistent messaging and promotion details.
- Notify any customer service team members about active promotions so they can handle inquiries accurately.

### Phase Transition Criteria (Launch to Sustain)

Move to sustain phase when:
- The peak event window has closed (platform event end date has passed)
- Launch-phase ad spend is fully deployed
- Initial performance data is available for at least the first 24-48 hours

Before transitioning, conduct a rapid performance review:
- Identify top 3 and bottom 3 ad sets by ROAS
- Note best-performing content type and messaging angle
- Check inventory levels -- if a promoted SKU is at risk of selling out, adjust sustain-phase plans to shift focus to other products

---

## Phase 3: Sustain

**Objective:** Maintain sales momentum after the peak event window by retargeting engaged audiences, addressing buyer objections, and extending promotions at reduced intensity.

### Activities

**Retargeting focus:**
- Shift ad budget from prospecting to retargeting. Target audiences: video viewers from pre-heat and launch phases, product page visitors who did not purchase, cart abandoners, and past customers who did not purchase during the event.
- Reduce daily ad spend to 50-60% of launch-phase daily rate. Concentrate spend on the top 2-3 performing ad sets from launch.
- Refresh ad creative -- audiences who saw launch-phase ads may experience fatigue. Introduce testimonial-based or social-proof creative (e.g., "1,000+ sold during our sale" or customer review compilations).

**Content continuation:**
- Publish follow-up content that addresses common purchase objections: product comparisons, FAQ videos, "how to use" demonstrations, and customer testimonial compilations.
- If a TikTok Shop event ran during launch, continue posting with the event hashtag while it remains active -- some events maintain algorithmic boost for several days after the official end date.
- Send a follow-up email to non-purchasers 2-3 days after the launch window. Lead with social proof (sales volume, customer reviews) rather than repeating the promotional offer.

**Promotion adjustment:**
- Reduce discount depth by 5-10 percentage points from launch-phase levels if margin allows. Use language like "extended sale" or "last chance" rather than maintaining the same offer indefinitely.
- If platform coupons have expired, consider replacing them with a shop-level coupon at a reduced value to maintain some promotional incentive.
- Monitor margin closely -- sustain-phase sales should be profitable even at reduced volume. If ROAS drops below break-even threshold, reduce spend immediately rather than waiting for the sustain phase to end naturally.

**Data collection:**
- Compile and review performance data from the launch phase in detail. Calculate actual ROAS, CPA, conversion rate, and average order value by channel and by ad set.
- Identify the single best-performing content piece and the single best-performing audience segment. These become the foundation for the wind-down phase and for future campaign planning.

### Phase Transition Criteria (Sustain to Wind-Down)

Move to wind-down when any of the following occur:
- Sustain-phase planned duration has elapsed
- Daily ROAS drops below 1.5 for 2 consecutive days (the audience is fatigued)
- A subsequent campaign's pre-heat phase is scheduled to begin (avoid running two campaigns' paid advertising simultaneously)
- Promoted inventory is sold out or below the reorder point

---

## Phase 4: Wind-Down

**Objective:** Close out the campaign cleanly, capture final conversions with urgency messaging, collect performance data, and transition to the next campaign or evergreen activity.

### Activities

**Final push:**
- Run "last chance" or "ending soon" messaging on remaining ad sets. Narrow targeting to highest-intent audiences only (cart abandoners, repeat visitors with 3+ sessions).
- Send a final email with urgency framing ("sale ends tonight" or "last day for free shipping"). This email typically generates a small but meaningful spike in conversions.
- Deactivate all promotions at the scheduled end time. Do not extend promotions beyond the planned end date unless there is a deliberate strategic reason (e.g., the next campaign intentionally overlaps).

**Budget reallocation:**
- Pause all campaign-specific ad sets. Any remaining unspent budget returns to the period budget pool or the contingency reserve.
- If the next campaign's pre-heat phase begins within 3 days, reallocate remaining budget directly into pre-heat audience-building spend for the next campaign.

**Transition content:**
- Publish content that bridges from the campaign theme to the next campaign or to evergreen messaging. Example: a BFCM campaign winds down with a "thank you for shopping with us" post that teases the Holiday Mega Sale coming in two weeks.
- Resume normal (non-promotional) content cadence within 1-2 days of wind-down completion.

**Post-campaign review:**
- Complete the post-campaign review within 3 business days. Document:
  - Final metrics vs. targets (revenue, ROAS, CPA, conversion rate, AOV) by campaign, phase, and channel
  - Content performance ranking (which assets drove the most conversions?)
  - Audience insights (which segments converted best?)
  - Budget efficiency (was spend allocated correctly across phases and channels?)
  - Three specific actions to implement in the next similar campaign
- Store review data in a centralized location so it is accessible when planning the next quarter's calendar.

## Abbreviated Framework for Tier 3 Events

Tier 3 events do not justify a full four-phase campaign. Use this abbreviated approach:

**Duration:** 3-5 days total.

**Activities:**
- Create 2-3 content assets tied to the event theme (e.g., a holiday-themed product post, a timely promotion graphic)
- Activate a lightweight promotion (single discount tier, no complex stacking)
- Allocate a small ad budget ($100-500) to boost the top-performing content piece
- No dedicated pre-heat or sustain phases; launch and wind-down only

**When to upgrade a Tier 3 to Tier 2:** If a Tier 3 event's initial content performs in the top 10% of recent organic posts (measured by engagement rate or click-through rate within the first 24 hours), consider upgrading it mid-flight by adding sustain-phase activities and reallocating budget from the contingency reserve.

---

## Campaign Overlap Management

When two campaigns overlap in the calendar (e.g., a product launch sustain phase overlaps with a platform event pre-heat phase), follow these rules:

1. **Never run pre-heat and launch phases of two different campaigns simultaneously.** If overlap is unavoidable, shorten the earlier campaign's sustain or wind-down phase to create space.

2. **Sustain and pre-heat overlap is acceptable** if the campaigns target the same audience. Use the sustain-phase retargeting from Campaign A as the audience-building mechanism for Campaign B's pre-heat.

3. **Deduplicate content.** If two overlapping campaigns require similar content types, produce content that serves both purposes (e.g., a product demo that includes a teaser for the upcoming event).

4. **Budget must remain separated.** Even if campaigns overlap, track spend separately by campaign to enable accurate post-campaign ROAS calculation. Shared audiences are acceptable; blended budgets are not.
