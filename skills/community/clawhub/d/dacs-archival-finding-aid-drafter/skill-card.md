## Description: <br>
Use this skill when an archivist wants to draft, review, or refine a DACS finding aid for a processed or partly processed archival collection, including ArchivesSpace-ready descriptive notes, EAD3 field mapping, access and use restrictions, reparative-description checks, and archivist sign-off boundaries. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Archivists and collections staff use this skill to draft or review DACS-compliant finding aids for processed or partly processed collections before entry into ArchivesSpace or an equivalent system. It supports collection-level description, access and use notes, EAD3 mapping, and professional sign-off boundaries. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Restricted donor agreements, third-party PII, or culturally sensitive archival records could be shared in an AI session without institutional permission. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm the repository's confidentiality posture before intake and avoid sharing restricted information unless institutional policy permits it. <br>
Risk: Generated finding-aid text could be incomplete, inaccurate, or unsuitable for publication without professional review. <br>
Mitigation: Require processing-archivist and head-of-collections review before publication, EAD export, PDF release, or access-system updates. <br>
