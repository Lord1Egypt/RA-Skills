# Stage Strategy Playbooks

## Overview

Each lifecycle stage requires fundamentally different marketing, pricing, inventory, and product development strategies. Applying Growth-stage tactics to a Decline product (or vice versa) wastes resources and accelerates negative outcomes. This playbook provides specific, actionable strategies for each stage.

## Introduction Stage Strategies

### Pricing
- **Penetration pricing**: Set price 10-20% below category average to encourage trial
- **Introductory offers**: Limited-time discounts or bundle deals to generate initial velocity
- **Free shipping threshold**: Include the product in free shipping calculations to reduce purchase friction
- **Price monitoring**: Track competitor responses weekly — adjust if competitors match or undercut

### Marketing
- **Channel priorities** (budget allocation):
  1. Paid search/PPC (40%) — capture active searchers in the category
  2. Social media ads (25%) — build awareness with targeted audiences
  3. Influencer seeding (20%) — send product to micro-influencers for authentic reviews
  4. Email to existing customers (15%) — cross-sell to your most loyal base
- **Creative focus**: Education and differentiation — explain why this product exists and what problem it solves
- **Review generation**: Prioritize getting first 10-25 reviews through Vine, early reviewer programs, or post-purchase email sequences

### Inventory
- **Posture**: Test quantities — order conservatively, plan for rapid replenishment if demand materializes
- **Safety stock**: Low — accept potential stockout risk rather than over-committing to unproven demand
- **Reorder triggers**: Set aggressive reorder points to avoid missing early demand signals

### Product Development
- **Feedback loop**: Actively collect and analyze customer reviews and return reasons
- **Variant testing**: If initial product shows promise, begin planning size/color/configuration variants
- **Listing optimization**: A/B test product titles, images, and descriptions weekly

## Growth Stage Strategies

### Pricing
- **Value capture**: Gradually increase price as demand proves inelastic — test 5% increases monthly
- **Premium positioning**: If reviews and ratings support it, price above commodity level
- **Bundle creation**: Create bundles with complementary products to increase AOV
- **Competitive moat**: If price-sensitive category, hold price steady and win on volume

### Marketing
- **Channel priorities** (budget allocation):
  1. Paid search/PPC (35%) — maximize capture of growing search volume
  2. Retargeting/remarketing (20%) — convert browsers who didn't purchase
  3. Social media ads (20%) — expand audience beyond initial target
  4. Content marketing/SEO (15%) — build organic foundation for long-term
  5. Email marketing (10%) — nurture repeat purchases
- **Creative focus**: Social proof and FOMO — highlight reviews, bestseller status, trending signals
- **Budget scaling**: Increase ad spend in proportion to revenue growth — maintain or improve ROAS

### Inventory
- **Posture**: Build stock — increase inventory position ahead of demand
- **Safety stock**: High — stockouts during Growth phase are extremely costly (lost sales + lost momentum)
- **Forecasting**: Use acceleration-adjusted forecasting — linear projections underestimate Growth product demand
- **Supplier relationships**: Negotiate volume discounts and priority allocation

### Product Development
- **Line extensions**: Launch variants (colors, sizes, configurations) to capture adjacent demand
- **Quality improvements**: Address any recurring complaints before they limit growth ceiling
- **Complementary products**: Develop or source products that pair with the Growth product

## Maturity Stage Strategies

### Pricing
- **Competitive pricing**: Match market rate — avoid both premium and discount positioning
- **Loyalty pricing**: Offer returning customers exclusive prices or bundles
- **Price defense**: Monitor competitor pricing daily — respond to undercuts within 48 hours
- **Margin optimization**: Reduce COGS through supplier negotiation, packaging optimization, or sourcing alternatives

### Marketing
- **Channel priorities** (budget allocation):
  1. Email/CRM (30%) — retention and repeat purchase is most efficient
  2. Paid search/brand terms (25%) — defend branded search traffic
  3. Loyalty/rewards programs (20%) — increase customer lifetime value
  4. Social proof amplification (15%) — leverage existing review base
  5. Retargeting (10%) — win back lapsed customers
- **Creative focus**: Loyalty, reliability, and trust — emphasize track record, review count, and brand strength
- **Budget efficiency**: Reduce overall spend while maintaining share — shift from acquisition to retention

### Inventory
- **Posture**: Maintain — match inventory to stable demand patterns
- **Safety stock**: Moderate — balance availability against carrying cost
- **Forecasting**: Use historical averages with seasonal adjustments
- **Cost reduction**: Negotiate longer payment terms, consolidated shipments, and volume commitments

### Product Development
- **Refresh**: Update packaging, imagery, or product descriptions to maintain relevance
- **Bundling**: Create value bundles with Introduction or Growth products to drive cross-sell
- **Cost engineering**: Reduce COGS without impacting quality to extend profitable lifespan
- **Next generation planning**: Begin developing the successor product

## Decline Stage Strategies

### Pricing
- **Harvest pricing**: If demand is inelastic (loyal customers), maintain price and harvest margin
- **Progressive markdown**: If volume-dependent, reduce price 20-30-50% on a defined schedule
- **Clearance**: If exit timeline is set, price for maximum sell-through speed
- **Do not invest in price wars**: Let competitors have the declining market

### Marketing
- **Channel priorities** (budget allocation):
  1. Email to existing customers (50%) — cheapest channel, most likely buyers
  2. Clearance/deal promotions (30%) — move remaining inventory
  3. Cross-sell to replacement product (20%) — migrate customers to successor
- **Creative focus**: Scarcity and transition — "while supplies last" messaging, introduce replacement product
- **Budget reduction**: Cut acquisition spend to near zero — only spend on customers who already know the product

### Inventory
- **Posture**: Draw down — stop or minimize reorders
- **Liquidation**: Execute exit strategy (see SKU Rationalization skill's liquidation playbook)
- **Transfer**: If product sells well in a different channel or market, redirect inventory there
- **Write-off planning**: Budget for potential inventory write-off if liquidation doesn't fully clear stock

### Product Development
- **Sunset planning**: Set firm discontinuation date and communicate to stakeholders
- **Customer migration**: Create transition path from declining product to successor
- **Lessons learned**: Document what drove decline for future product development
- **IP and tooling**: Decide whether to retain molds, formulas, or designs for potential future use
