## Arbitration Overview

### Arbitration vs. Litigation

| Dimension | Arbitration | Litigation |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Jurisdiction basis | Mutual agreement | Legal mandate (compulsory) |
| Procedural flexibility | High (customizable rules, venue, language) | Fixed procedures |
| Confidentiality | Private hearing as principle | Public hearing as principle |
| Appeal levels | Single-instance (final and binding) | Two instances (+ retrial) |
| Cost | Higher (proportionate to claim) | Lower (proportionate to claim) |
| Speed | 4-6 months (domestic) | 6-12 months (first instance) |
| Cross-border enforcement | 170+ countries under New York Convention | Bilateral judicial assistance treaties |

### Types of Arbitration

| Type | Legal Basis | Major Institutions |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Commercial arbitration | 《仲裁法》(1995) | CIETAC, BAC, SHAC, SCIA |
| Labor arbitration | 《劳动争议调解仲裁法》 | Local labor dispute arbitration committees |
| Agricultural contract arbitration | 《农村土地承包经营纠纷调解仲裁法》 | Local arbitration committees |
| Maritime arbitration | 《仲裁法》+ special provisions | CMAC |
| International investment arbitration | ICSID Convention et al. | ICSID, SIAC, HKIAC, SCC |

## Arbitration Agreement

### Requirements for a Valid Arbitration Agreement

The arbitration agreement is the foundation of arbitration — without a valid agreement, the arbitration commission lacks jurisdiction.

**Form Requirements:**
- Written form (arbitration clause in contract / separate arbitration agreement / other written forms)
- May be made before or after the dispute arises

**Substantive Requirements (《仲裁法》Art. 16):**
1. **Expression of intent to arbitrate**: clear, unambiguous
2. **Matters to be arbitrated**: which disputes are submitted to arbitration
3. **Designated arbitration commission**: specific institution name

### Recommended Model Clauses

**CIETAC:**
> "Any dispute arising from or in connection with this Contract shall be submitted to China International Economic and Trade Arbitration Commission (CIETAC) for arbitration which shall be conducted in accordance with CIETAC's arbitration rules in effect at the time of applying for arbitration. The arbitral award is final and binding upon both parties."

**BAC (Beijing):**
> "Any dispute arising from or in connection with this Contract shall be submitted to Beijing Arbitration Commission / Beijing International Arbitration Center for arbitration in accordance with its rules. The arbitral award is final and binding upon both parties."

**Common Pitfalls (Problematic Clauses):**
- ❌ "Submit to arbitration or court" → Arbitration agreement invalid (ambiguous)
- ❌ "Beijing Arbitration Commission" but wrong official name → should be exact
- ❌ "Arbitration in Beijing" → Institution unclear
- ❌ Old CIETAC sub-commission names → verify if still operational

### Validity of Arbitration Agreements

**Grounds for Invalidity:**
1. Dispute falls outside arbitrable scope (marriage/adoption/guardianship/inheritance/administrative)
2. Made by a person without civil capacity or with limited capacity
3. Made under duress
4. Insufficiently specific and cannot be supplemented

**Authority to Determine Validity:**
- Arbitration commission (decides)
- People's court (ruling)
- Party must object to validity before the first hearing

## Arbitration Application and Acceptance

### Conditions for Filing

1. Valid arbitration agreement
2. Specific claims and factual basis
3. Within the arbitration commission's scope

### Application Materials

| Material | Description | Copies |
|----------|-------------|:------:|
| Arbitration application | Party info, claims, facts & reasons, evidence sources | N (defendants) + 1 |
| Arbitration agreement | Contract clause or separate agreement | 1 |
| Evidence | Supporting evidence | N (defendants) + 1 |
| Power of attorney | If represented | 1 |
| Identity documents | Individual or entity qualification | 1 |

### Arbitration Application Template

```
ARBITRATION APPLICATION

Claimant: [Name, address, contact]
(If legal entity: [Name, address, unified social credit code])
Legal Representative: [Name, title]

Respondent: [Name, address, contact]
(If legal entity: [Name, address, unified social credit code])
Legal Representative: [Name, title]

ARBITRATION BASIS:
Arbitration clause in contract dated [Date] / separate arbitration
agreement dated [Date].

CLAIMS:
1. [Specific claim, e.g., order respondent to pay RMB XXX]
2. [Respondent to bear arbitration costs]
3. [Other claims]

FACTS AND REASONS:
[Chronological narrative with evidentiary support]

[Cite specific legal basis]

Sincerely submitted to:
[Arbitration Commission Name]

Claimant: [Signature/Seal]
[Date]
```

### Acceptance and Counterclaim

- Commission decides whether to accept within **5 days** of receiving application
- Upon acceptance: notice to claimant; notice + claim copy to respondent
- Respondent submits answer within **15 days** (domestic) / 30-45 days (international)
- Respondent may submit a **counterclaim** during the answer period

### Arbitration Costs

Approximate fee schedule (CIETAC, 2024 standard):

| Amount in Dispute (RMB) | Fee |
|:-----------------------:|:---:|
| 1,000 or below | 100 |
| 1,001 - 50,000 | 100 + 5% of amount |
| 50,001 - 100,000 | 2,600 + 4% of amount |
| 100,001 - 200,000 | 4,600 + 3% of amount |
| 200,001 - 500,000 | 7,600 + 2% of amount |
| 500,001 - 1,000,000 | 13,600 + 1% of amount |
| 1,000,001 - 5,000,000 | 18,600 + 0.75% of amount |
| 5,000,001+ | Decreasing proportionally |

> Note: Rates vary by institution; verify with each commission's latest schedule.

## Arbitral Tribunal Formation

### Number of Arbitrators

- **Three-member tribunal (default)**: each party selects one, third (presiding) selected jointly or appointed by commission chair
- **Sole arbitrator (optional)**: selected jointly or appointed by commission chair

### Arbitrator Selection

Arbitrator roster: each commission maintains a roster of experts from legal, commercial, technical, and other fields.

**Process:**
1. Commission provides roster/suggested list
2. Claimant selects one (three-member) / jointly (sole)
3. Respondent selects one (three-member)
4. Parties jointly select presiding arbitrator (or chair appoints)
5. **Selection deadline:** 15 days (domestic) / 20 days (international) from receiving notice

### Arbitrator Disqualification/Recusal

**Mandatory recusal (《仲裁法》Art. 34):**
1. Is a party or close relative of party/agent
2. Has an interest in the case
3. Has another relationship with party/agent that may affect impartiality
4. Privately met party/agent or accepted gifts

**Recusal Request:**
- Filed before the first hearing
- If grounds discovered after, may file before end of final hearing

## Arbitration Hearing Procedure

### Hearing Methods

| Method | Application | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Oral hearing (principle) | Ordinary cases | Private (confidentiality) |
| Written hearing (exception) | Clear facts + both agree or rules permit | No hearing, decided on documents only |

### Hearing Flow

```
Tribunal formed → Material exchange → Hearing (if any) → Mediation (optional) → Award
```

### Pre-Hearing Preparation

1. **Material exchange**: tribunal organizes exchange of application, answer, evidence
2. **Evidence rules**: tribunal may reference civil procedure evidence rules or set its own deadlines
3. **Terms of reference**: tribunal may confirm scope of review, claims, and disputed issues with parties
4. **Interim measures**: party submits preservation application → commission forwards to competent court

### Oral Hearing

**Hearing flow (similar to litigation but more flexible):**

```
Session opened → Identity verification → Tribunal states case overview
→ Claimant states claims and facts
→ Respondent answers
→ Evidence presentation and cross-examination
→ Tribunal questions
→ Debate
→ Final statements
→ Mediation (tribunal may proactively mediate)
→ Session closed
```

**Arbitration hearing characteristics:**
- Private (unless parties agree to public and no state secrets involved)
- Tribunal-led, relatively flexible
- Parties may agree to streamline (merge issues, shorten debate)
- Hearing record (transcript) subject to party confirmation

### Evidence in Arbitration

- General rule: "he who asserts must prove"
- Tribunal may require parties to produce evidence
- Tribunal may investigate and collect evidence when deemed necessary
- Expert appointment: tribunal may decide to engage experts

### Interim Measures in Arbitration

| Type | Application Method | Decision Maker | Security Required |
|------|------------------|----------------|:-----------------:|
| Asset preservation | Via commission → court | Court | Typically required |
| Evidence preservation | Via commission → court | Court | Discretionary |

### Arbitration-Mediation Combined Process

A distinctive feature of Chinese arbitration — the tribunal may mediate during the proceedings.

- Successful mediation → **mediation statement** or **award based on settlement**
- Failed mediation → proceedings continue
- Settlement agreement may be submitted for a consent award
- Mediation statement and arbitral award have equal legal force

## Arbitral Award

### Types of Awards

| Type | Application |
|------|-------------|
| Final award | All issues resolved, decides all claims |
| Partial award | Part of facts already established, urgent disposition needed |
| Interim award | Procedural/jurisdictional issues |
| Consent award | Based on settlement/mediation agreement |

### Award Making

- Three-member tribunal: majority decision; if no majority, presiding arbitrator's opinion prevails
- Sole arbitrator: sole arbitrator decides

### Award Content

```
[Arbitration Commission Name] AWARD
[Case No.]

I. Party information
II. Case origin and hearing summary
III. Claims, answers, and counterclaims
IV. Facts found by the tribunal
V. Tribunal's opinion (factual findings + legal application)
VI. Award
VII. Cost allocation
VIII. Arbitrators' signatures + commission seal + date
```

### Effect of Award

**Final and binding (《仲裁法》Art. 9):**
- The award takes effect immediately upon issuance
- Parties may not re-apply for arbitration or file a lawsuit on the same dispute
- The award is binding from the date of issuance

## Setting Aside an Arbitral Award

### Application to Set Aside

**Deadline:** Within **6 months** of receiving the award

**Competent court:** **Intermediate People's Court** where the arbitration commission is located

**Statutory grounds (《仲裁法》Art. 58):**
1. No arbitration agreement
2. Dispute not within scope of arbitration agreement or commission's authority
3. Composition of tribunal or arbitration procedure violated statutory procedure
4. Evidence on which the award is based was forged
5. Opposing party concealed evidence sufficient to affect the award
6. Arbitrator engaged in corruption, bribery, or perversion of justice

**Court shall set aside if award violates public interest.**

### Setting Aside Procedure

1. Party files application with intermediate court
2. Court forms collegial panel for review
3. Court may request tribunal to re-arbitrate (if curable defects)
4. Court issues ruling within **2 months** of receiving application
5. Ruling: set aside or dismiss application

### Effect of Setting Aside

- Award set aside: parties may re-arbitrate (new agreement) or file lawsuit in court
- Application dismissed: award continues in effect

> Note: Setting aside is an **extraordinary remedy**, not an appeal. The court reviews procedural legality, not substantive correctness.

## Enforcement of Arbitral Awards

### Application for Enforcement

**Deadline:** **2 years** from receiving the award (commencement/suspension/interruption follows civil procedure rules)

**Competent court:** **Intermediate People's Court** at the debtor's domicile or **property location**

**Application materials:**
1. Enforcement application
2. Arbitral award (original + copy)
3. Arbitration agreement (original + copy)
4. Proof of service
5. Debtor's asset leads (if any)

### Refusal of Enforcement

Grounds for debtor's application to refuse enforcement (《仲裁法》Art. 63 + 《民诉法》Art. 244):

**Domestic awards:** Same as setting aside grounds (see Art. 58 above)

**Foreign-related awards (《仲裁法》Art. 70-71):**
1. No arbitration agreement
2. Debtor not given proper notice of arbitrator appointment or proceedings
3. Award exceeds scope of arbitration agreement
4. Tribunal composition or procedure inconsistent with arbitration rules
5. Subject matter not arbitrable
6. Violates public policy

**Enforcement court review period:** within **2 months** of receiving application

## International Arbitration Special Provisions

### Major International Arbitration Institutions

| Institution | Abbr. | HQ | Features |
|------------|:-----:|:--:|----------|
| China Int'l Economic & Trade Arbitration Comm. | CIETAC | Beijing | China's most recognized international arbitration institution |
| Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre | HKIAC | Hong Kong | Leading in Asia, applies Hong Kong law |
| Singapore International Arbitration Centre | SIAC | Singapore | Efficient, suitable for SE Asia disputes |
| International Chamber of Commerce Int'l Court of Arb. | ICC | Paris | Most globally recognized |
| Stockholm Chamber of Commerce Arbitration Institute | SCC | Stockholm | Suitable for China-Europe disputes |
| London Court of International Arbitration | LCIA | London | Common law expertise |

### International Arbitration Special Rules

1. **Applicable law**: parties may choose governing law; if none, law with closest connection applies
2. **Language**: parties may agree (often Chinese/English bilingual); tribunal decides if none agreed
3. **Foreign counsel**: foreign lawyers may represent in international arbitration (but not domestic)
4. **Emergency measures**: CIETAC rules allow Emergency Arbitrator procedure (since 2015)
5. **Award enforcement**: under the New York Convention in 170+ contracting states

### New York Convention Enforcement Framework

- China acceded to the New York Convention in 1987
- China made "commercial reservation" and "reciprocity reservation"
- Foreign awards in China: apply to intermediate court at debtor's domicile or property location
- Chinese awards abroad: apply for enforcement in local courts under the Convention

## Labor Arbitration (Employment Disputes)

### Unique Features of Labor Arbitration

Labor arbitration differs significantly from commercial arbitration:

| Aspect | Commercial Arbitration | Labor Arbitration |
|--------|:---------------------:|:-----------------:|
| Legal basis | 《仲裁法》 | 《劳动争议调解仲裁法》 |
| Arbitration agreement | Written agreement required | Not required (statutory precondition) |
| Jurisdiction | Agreed by parties | Place of performance or employer location |
| Cost | Proportionate to claim | **Free** |
| Finality | Single-instance | Some awards appealable; some final |
| Institution | Arbitration commissions | Labor dispute arbitration committees |

### Mandatory Pre-Litigation Procedure

Labor dispute litigation requires prior labor arbitration — "arbitration first, then litigation."

**Acceptable dispute scope:**
- Employment relationship confirmation
- Conclusion/performance/amendment/termination of employment contract
- Removal/resignation/resignation/dismissal
- Working hours/rest & leave/social insurance/welfare/training/labor protection
- Remuneration/medical expenses for work injuries/economic compensation/penalties

**Application deadline:** **1 year** from when party knew or should have known of rights infringement

**Tribunal composition:** Three arbitrators (one presiding + two people's assessors) or sole arbitrator

**Trial period:** **45 days** from acceptance (may be extended by 15 days)

**Final awards (binding on employer, employee may sue):**
- Small claims (amount < 12 months of local minimum wage)
- Disputes over national labor standards (working hours, rest & leave, social insurance)

### Labor Arbitration Procedure

```
Dispute → Consultation (optional) → Mediation (optional)
→ Labor arbitration (mandatory prerequisite)
    ├── Employee dissatisfied → sue in court within 15 days
    └── Employer dissatisfied with final award
        ├── Apply to set aside (statutory grounds)
        └── Non-final award → sue in court within 15 days
```
