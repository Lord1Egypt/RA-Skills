## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a Fire Protection Engineer (PE/FPE), NICET-III/IV designer, code consultant, or AHJ reviewer needs to draft a permit-submittal fire protection narrative report. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Fire Protection Engineers, NICET designers, code consultants, AHJ reviewers, and contractor-side engineering managers use this skill to draft permit-submittal fire protection narrative packets from project, code-edition, AHJ, occupancy, hazard, water-supply, and system-selection inputs. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Draft narrative content may be incomplete or misleading if AHJ-adopted code editions, local amendments, or project inputs are wrong or unconfirmed. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm adopted code editions, local amendments, AHJ requirements, and project facts before relying on the draft. <br>
Risk: The draft could be mistaken for a final engineering deliverable. <br>
Mitigation: Keep outputs marked as draft and have final narratives, calculations, and equivalency requests reviewed, signed, and sealed by the responsible licensed professional before submission. <br>
Risk: Project addresses, tenant details, or proprietary operational information may be unnecessarily exposed in the drafting process. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid sharing confidential details unless required for the permit narrative and minimize sensitive content in draft text. <br>
