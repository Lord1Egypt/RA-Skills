# ICP Scoring

Use this reference when qualifying, cleaning, or prioritizing lead lists.

## Buyer Fit

Score buyer fit by asking:

- Does the prospect visibly have the problem the offer solves?
- Is the problem recurring rather than one-off?
- Is there evidence they already pay for related services, tools, ads, staff, agencies, software, events, or vendors?
- Is the target large enough to afford the user's minimum deal size?
- Is the offer naturally urgent for this niche?

## Signal Types

Strong signals:

- recent hiring for marketing, operations, sales, growth, web, design, content, or support
- active events, promotions, launches, campaigns, webinars, product drops, new locations, or partnerships
- poor website, weak landing page, broken forms, stale content, slow site, missing tracking, weak SEO, or inconsistent branding
- visible founder-led or owner-led business with direct decision-maker access
- public complaint, review pattern, customer support bottleneck, or operational friction the offer can address

Medium signals:

- active social presence but weak conversion path
- good business but unclear owner or contact route
- similar companies buy this type of help
- recent news without clear pain evidence

Weak signals:

- generic directory listing only
- inactive website or no current activity
- tiny local business with no spend signals
- broad category match but no specific problem evidence

## Fit Score

5: Strong buyer, strong pain signal, strong timing, clean contact route.

4: Strong buyer and pain signal, but one missing element such as contact quality or timing.

3: Plausible buyer, but needs manual research before sending.

2: Weak fit or weak evidence. Usually skip.

1: Exclude due to mismatch, risk, prior opt-out, or no buying ability.

## Segment Angles

Local service business:
Focus on missed leads, weak website conversion, slow follow-up, review capture, quote requests, and Google Business Profile quality.

Agency or consultant buyer:
Focus on client delivery bottlenecks, overflow, white-label capacity, reporting, audits, and repeatable campaign assets.

SaaS or digital product:
Focus on onboarding, content, conversion pages, paid acquisition waste, lifecycle email, support deflection, and churn signals.

Ecommerce:
Focus on product pages, abandoned carts, seasonal campaigns, upsells, email flows, ad creative, reviews, and collection pages.

Events or communities:
Focus on ticket sales, sponsor assets, registration pages, recurring announcements, attendee communication, and partner visibility.

## Batch Selection

For first outreach, choose:

- 5-15 leads if the user is testing an offer
- 20-50 leads if the user has a proven offer and copy
- one segment per batch
- one clear offer angle per batch

Avoid mixing too many niches in one send batch because the copy becomes generic.
