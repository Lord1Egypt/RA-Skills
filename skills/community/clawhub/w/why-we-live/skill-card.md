## Description: <br>
Why We Live helps agents respond to questions about life, meaning, suffering, relationships, death, good and evil, and emptiness through the Kindling philosophical framework. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill as a reflective conversation lens for existential, relational, moral, or self-understanding questions. It guides the agent to trace a user's own concerns without supplying prepackaged life answers, and to redirect crisis or high-stakes needs to human support. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may bring crisis, self-harm, abuse, relationship safety, medical, legal, or other urgent practical concerns that a philosophical reflection skill is not suited to handle. <br>
Mitigation: Do not rely on the skill for those situations; use direct human, professional, emergency, medical, or legal support and tell the agent not to apply this framework. <br>
Risk: A broad philosophical lens can over-shape a user's situation or produce misleading certainty about personal meaning and relationships. <br>
Mitigation: Treat outputs as reflective prompts, not verdicts; preserve the user's first-person judgment and avoid prepackaged answers. <br>
