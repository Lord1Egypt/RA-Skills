# Prompt 3: Brand Voice & Messaging Guide

## Instructions
Run this after you have your brand name and tagline locked. This gives you a reusable voice guide your whole team (or just you) can reference forever.

---

## Prompt

You are a brand strategist creating a brand voice and messaging guide. This document will be used by the business owner, any future writers, social media managers, and AI tools to ensure consistent communication across all channels.

**Brand Details:**
- Brand name: [e.g. SunVault]
- Tagline: [e.g. Power That Pays You Back]
- Industry / business type: [e.g. residential solar installation]
- Target audience (be specific): [e.g. Nevada homeowners ages 35-65, household income $75K+, skeptical of sales pitches, value reliability over price, own their home]
- Three words that describe your brand personality: [e.g. confident, straightforward, neighborly]
- One brand you admire the voice of (any industry): [e.g. Patagonia, Mailchimp, Basecamp, Apple, Wendy's — optional]

**Generate the following:**

### Section 1: Brand Voice Spectrum

Rate the brand on these 5 axes (show as a labeled scale from 1-10 with the brand's position marked):

1. Formal (1) ←→ Casual (10): Brand sits at [X] because [reason]
2. Serious (1) ←→ Playful (10): Brand sits at [X] because [reason]
3. Technical (1) ←→ Plain English (10): Brand sits at [X] because [reason]
4. Reserved (1) ←→ Bold/Opinionated (10): Brand sits at [X] because [reason]
5. Traditional (1) ←→ Innovative (10): Brand sits at [X] because [reason]

### Section 2: Vocabulary Guide

**20 Words/Phrases to USE** (organized by category: power words, action verbs, descriptors):
[List with brief note on why each fits the brand]

**10 Words/Phrases to AVOID** (and what to say instead):
| Avoid This | Say This Instead | Why |
|------------|-----------------|-----|
| [word] | [alternative] | [reason] |

### Section 3: Sentence Structure Guidelines

- **Ideal sentence length:** [range] words for body copy; [range] words for headlines
- **Paragraph length:** [X] sentences max
- **Active vs. passive voice:** [rule]
- **Use of questions:** [when to use / avoid]
- **Numbers and stats:** [spell out or use digits? Rule + examples]
- **Punctuation personality:** [Oxford comma? Em dashes? Exclamation points? Policy for each]

### Section 4: Channel-Specific Voice Rules

For each platform, give 3 rules + 1 example post (10 words or fewer as a demo):

**LinkedIn** (professional, thought leadership context)
**Instagram/TikTok** (visual, short-form, younger-skewing)
**Twitter/X** (fast, opinionated, conversational)
**Email** (1-to-1, personal, action-oriented)
**Website copy** (scannable, SEO-conscious, trust-building)

### Section 5: Headline Formula

Give 3 fill-in-the-blank headline formulas that work for this brand's voice:
1. Formula: ___
   Example: ___
2. Formula: ___
   Example: ___
3. Formula: ___
   Example: ___

### Section 6: Voice-in-Action Rewrites

Take these 3 generic sentences and rewrite each in the brand's voice:

1. Generic: "We offer high-quality solar panel installation services at competitive prices."  
   In [Brand Name]'s voice: ___

2. Generic: "Our team of experienced professionals will handle your project from start to finish."  
   In [Brand Name]'s voice: ___

3. Generic: "Contact us today to learn more about our services."  
   In [Brand Name]'s voice: ___

### Section 7: The Brand Voice in One Sentence

End with a single memorable sentence the team can keep on a sticky note:  
"[Brand Name] sounds like ________, talks like ________, and never ________."
