# Description Frameworks Reference

Copywriting frameworks for structuring product descriptions. Each framework is suited to different product types and audiences. Choose based on the product's primary selling angle.

---

## 1. AIDA ‚Äî Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

### Definition
A linear persuasion sequence that first grabs attention, builds intellectual interest, creates emotional desire, then prompts action.

### Best for
New-to-market products, innovative products, products with a strong "wow factor" or visual appeal. Works well when the product's existence is the selling point.

### Structure
1. **Attention:** A bold opening that stops the scroll ‚Äî a surprising fact, a provocative question, or a vivid image
2. **Interest:** Specific details that build credibility and engagement ‚Äî features that matter, comparisons, technical proof
3. **Desire:** Emotional connection ‚Äî how the product improves the buyer's life, social proof, aspirational imagery
4. **Action:** Clear next step ‚Äî add to cart, limited availability, bonus offer, guarantee

### Ecommerce Application
- **Title:** Attention element (the hook)
- **First bullet:** Interest (the standout feature)
- **Bullets 2-4:** Desire (benefits, social proof, lifestyle connection)
- **Last bullet or CTA:** Action (urgency, guarantee, bundle offer)

### Example Snippet
**Attention:** "The kitchen timer that actually understands cooking."
**Interest:** "Three independent countdown zones with voice alerts ‚Äî track your roast, pasta, and sauce simultaneously without touching a screen."
**Desire:** "Join 50,000+ home cooks who stopped burning side dishes. Rated 4.8 stars by people who cook dinner every night."
**Action:** "Order today with our 60-day no-questions-asked return policy."

## 2. PAS ‚Äî Problem, Agitate, Solution

### Definition
Identifies a pain point the buyer experiences, intensifies the emotional weight of that problem, then presents the product as the resolution.

### Best for
Problem-solving products: health and wellness, home organization, pain relief, cleaning products, productivity tools. Any product where the buyer's motivation is escaping discomfort rather than pursuing pleasure.

### Structure
1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain point the buyer recognizes
2. **Agitate:** Amplify the problem ‚Äî show its consequences, frequency, or hidden costs
3. **Solution:** Present the product as the direct answer, with proof it works

### Ecommerce Application
- **Title:** Solution-oriented (includes the benefit, not just the product)
- **First bullet:** Problem statement (connect with the buyer's frustration)
- **Bullet 2:** Agitation (why existing solutions fail, the cost of inaction)
- **Bullets 3-5:** Solution details (how this product specifically solves it, with proof)

### Example Snippet
**Problem:** "Tired of waking up with neck pain that takes hours to fade?"
**Agitate:** "Standard pillows lose their shape within months, forcing your neck into unnatural positions night after night. You spend $5,000 on a mattress but sleep on a $15 pillow that's working against you."
**Solution:** "The CerviRest Contour Pillow maintains its ergonomic shape for 3+ years with medical-grade memory foam. Designed with orthopedic input to keep your cervical spine aligned in any sleep position."

## 3. FAB ‚Äî Feature, Advantage, Benefit

### Definition
A structured approach that connects every product feature to its competitive advantage and ultimate customer benefit. Ideal for products where specifications matter and need translation.

### Best for
Technical products, electronics, tools, equipment, cookware, sporting goods ‚Äî any product where buyers compare specs but ultimately care about outcomes.

### Structure
1. **Feature:** The specific product attribute (material, dimension, technology)
2. **Advantage:** What this feature does better than alternatives
3. **Benefit:** How this translates to the buyer's experience

### Ecommerce Application
- **Each bullet point** follows the FAB sequence
- **Title:** Highlights the most compelling FAB chain
- **Long description:** Groups related FAB chains into logical sections

### Example Snippet
**Feature:** "2,500mAh rechargeable lithium battery"
**Advantage:** "Delivers 40% more runtime than standard 1,800mAh batteries in this category"
**Benefit:** "Run your power drill for a full day of deck-building without stopping to recharge"

**Bullet format:** "Build all day without recharging ‚Äî the 2,500mAh lithium battery delivers 40% more runtime than standard drill batteries, giving you 6+ hours of continuous use on a single charge."

## 4. Before-After-Bridge

### Definition
Paints a picture of the buyer's current situation (Before), shows them the improved reality (After), then explains how the product bridges the gap.

### Best for
Lifestyle products, fashion, home decor, wellness, beauty, subscription services. Products where the emotional transformation matters more than technical specifications.

### Structure
1. **Before:** The buyer's current reality ‚Äî relatable, specific, slightly uncomfortable
2. **After:** The improved reality ‚Äî aspirational but believable
3. **Bridge:** The product as the connection between the two states

### Ecommerce Application
- **Short description:** Compressed Before-After-Bridge in 25-50 words
- **Long description:** Full narrative with each section as a paragraph or headed section
- **Bullets:** Each bullet can be a mini Before-After for different product aspects

### Example Snippet
**Before:** "Your morning routine is a negotiation with your closet. Nothing matches, half your clothes need ironing, and you're already running late."
**After:** "You open your closet and everything works together. Five minutes to get dressed, zero stress, and you actually like what you see in the mirror."
**Bridge:** "Our 12-piece capsule collection is designed as a system ‚Äî every piece pairs with every other piece. Built from wrinkle-resistant fabrics that look polished straight from the drawer."

## Platform-Specific Formatting Rules

### Amazon

- **Title:** ‚j$200 characters. Format: Brand + Product Name + Key Feature + Size/Variant + Color
- **Bullet points:** 5 bullets, each ‚â§500 characters (200-250 ideal for readability). Start each with a CAPITALIZED benefit phrase
- **Product description:** ‚â§2,000 characters. HTML is limited (bold, line breaks). No external links
- **A+ Content:** If Brand Registered ‚Äî use modules for enhanced images and comparison charts
- **Backend search terms:** 250 bytes. No commas needed, no repetition of title words
- **Prohibited:** Subjective claims without evidence, time-sensitive language ("sale", "limited"), competitor mentions, HTML in bullets

### Shopify

- **Title:** No strict limit, but ‚j$70 characters recommended for SEO (Google truncation)
- **Description:** Full HTML support ‚Äî use `<h2>`, `<h3>`, `<ul>`, `<strong>`, `<p>` for structure
- **Meta description:** ‚â§155 characters, manually set or auto-generated from first paragraph
- **SEO considerations:** H1 is typically the product title. Description should use H2/H3 for subsections. First 160 characters are critical for Google snippet
- **Rich media:** Can embed video, comparison tables, size charts within description HTML

### Walmart

- **Title:** ‚â§75 characters ideal (‚â§200 max). Format: Brand + Product + Key Attribute + Size
- **Short description:** ‚â§500 characters, appears above the fold
- **Long description:** ‚j$4,000 characters, supports basic HTML
- **Key features:** Up to 10 bullet points, ‚â§80 characters each (shorter than Amazon)
- **Content standards:** No promotional language ("best seller"), no pricing, no competitor mentions

### Etsy

- **Title:** ‚â§140 characters. Keyword-rich, comma-separated attributes common
- **Description:** No HTML. Plain text with line breaks. Story-driven copy performs best
- **Tags:** 13 tags, ‚â§20 characters each. Multi-word phrases encouraged (long-tail keywords)
- **Style:** Handmade/personal narrative works. First-person acceptable. Emphasize craftsmanship, materials, maker story
