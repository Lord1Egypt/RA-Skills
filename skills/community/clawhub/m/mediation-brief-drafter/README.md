# Mediation Brief Drafter

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Mediation / Alternative Dispute Resolution

## Purpose

Drafts a **two-part mediation brief** for a single scheduled mediation session — a **SHARED brief** that will be exchanged with the opposing party and the mediator, and a **CONFIDENTIAL mediator-only brief** that contains the strategy content (BATNA / WATNA / ZOPA, settlement-authority structure, leverage and weakness candour, impasse-breaker proposals) that counsel will not show the other side. The skill walks counsel through case-strategy intake, settlement-authority structuring, BATNA / WATNA discipline, ZOPA hypothesis-building, and a do-not-share audit against the controlling mediation-confidentiality rule, and produces a DRAFT brief pair plus a redaction checklist and a settlement-authority worksheet — for licensed counsel review before any exchange.

The skill is for **civil mediation** under JAMS, AAA, FINRA, private, court-annexed, EEOC, and community-mediation rules in commercial, employment, personal-injury, construction, IP, partnership, divorce / family (where mediation is non-court-mandated), and consumer disputes. The skill is **not** a settlement-decision tool — it structures counsel's preparation; the licensed attorney and the client make the settlement call.

## When to Use

- Drafting a mediation brief for a scheduled mediation session
- Updating a prior mediation brief when a new round is scheduled
- Pre-mediation strategy session with the client to align on authority and walk-away
- Aligning co-counsel and insurer / indemnitor / parent company on a single brief and a single authority chain
- Building a BATNA / WATNA worksheet that survives client review and partner review
- Auditing a partner-drafted brief for accidental disclosure of confidential strategy content into the SHARED brief
- Preparing a mediator's-proposal request package

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Case and Session Intake**
1. Captures matter caption with confidentiality posture, jurisdiction, mediator (name, JAMS / AAA / FINRA / private / court-annexed / EEOC / community), mediation date and session length, party roster with named representatives and authority levels, opposing counsel, prior demand and offer history, case theme in one sentence, audience analysis for each brief, brief-exchange convention (exchanged simultaneously / in advance / submitted only to mediator)

**Phase 2: SHARED Brief Drafting**
2. Drafts the brief that the opposing party will read: procedural posture, confidentiality cover page, undisputed facts, disputed facts with party position, claims and defences with controlling authority, damages framework with itemised computation, key exhibits index, motions and rulings to date, opposing counsel's strongest arguments named candidly, settlement history, proposed framework for the session

**Phase 3: CONFIDENTIAL Mediator-Only Brief Drafting**
3. Drafts the strategy brief: **BATNA** (named, costed, time-discounted, probability-weighted), **WATNA** (named, costed, time-discounted, probability-weighted), **ZOPA** hypotheses (floor / target / ceiling with rationale), settlement-authority structure (client authority ladder, authority secured, authority gates, insurer / co-defendant presence), leverage-and-weakness candour, impasse-breaker proposals (bracket proposals, mediator's proposal, structured settlement, non-monetary terms, escrow / Mary-Carter / Pierringer / high-low arrangements where applicable), confidentiality and release-language preferences

**Phase 4: Do-Not-Share Audit**
4. Verifies the controlling mediation-confidentiality rule (Federal Rule of Evidence 408, Uniform Mediation Act where adopted, state-specific mediation privilege, EU Mediation Directive 2008/52/EC and national transpositions, contractual confidentiality, court-annexed mediation order), applies a redaction checklist to the SHARED brief, verifies dual-audience separation, and flags attorney work-product carve-outs

**Phase 5: Settlement-Authority Worksheet**
5. Confirms authority in writing pre-session, names the decision-maker reachable during session, sets the escalation protocol, captures the opening / target / walk-away numbers, ranks non-monetary deal terms, prepares a signed-term-sheet template, plans the post-mediation execution chain

**Phase 6: Assembly**
6. Produces the DRAFT brief pair (SHARED + CONFIDENTIAL) with redaction checklist, settlement-authority worksheet, BATNA / WATNA / ZOPA appendix, dual-audience separation audit, and unresolved-information list

## Output

A DRAFT mediation brief package with:
- **SHARED brief** (procedural posture, undisputed facts, disputed facts, claims and defences with authority, damages framework, key exhibits index, motions and rulings, opposing counsel's strongest argument named candidly, settlement history, proposed framework, signature block)
- **CONFIDENTIAL mediator-only brief** (BATNA / WATNA with probability weighting and time discount, ZOPA hypotheses with floor / target / ceiling, settlement-authority structure, leverage-and-weakness candour, impasse-breaker proposals, confidentiality and release-language preferences)
- Redaction checklist applied to the SHARED brief
- Settlement-authority worksheet with named decision-maker and escalation protocol
- BATNA / WATNA / ZOPA appendix with the math behind the numbers
- Dual-audience separation audit (every paragraph tagged SHARED-OK / CONFIDENTIAL-ONLY)
- Attorney work-product carve-out flag where applicable
- Mediation-confidentiality-rule citation (FRE 408, UMA, state mediation privilege, EU Mediation Directive, contractual, court order)
- Post-mediation execution plan (term sheet, releases, payment schedule, confidentiality)
- Open-questions / unresolved-information list

## Notes

This skill **drafts** the brief pair to support — never replace — the licensed counsel's strategic and settlement judgement and the client's authority decision. The skill does not transmit the brief to the mediator or to the opposing party, does not represent the client, does not opine on the strength or weakness of the case as a binding legal conclusion, does not predict trial outcome as a guarantee, does not commit the client to a settlement number, and does not breach the mediation-confidentiality privilege. The skill enforces a strict **dual-audience separation** rule: every paragraph is tagged SHARED-OK or CONFIDENTIAL-ONLY, and the do-not-share audit refuses to leak BATNA / WATNA / floor / authority / leverage candour content into the SHARED brief. The skill flags the controlling mediation-confidentiality rule (FRE 408, Uniform Mediation Act where adopted, state-specific privilege, EU Mediation Directive 2008/52/EC, contractual, court-annexed order) and refuses to draft against an unstated confidentiality posture. The skill refuses to produce a "litigation-style position paper" — mediation briefs that omit BATNA / WATNA / ZOPA analysis waste the session.
