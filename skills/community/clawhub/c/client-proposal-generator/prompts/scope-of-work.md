# Prompt: Scope of Work

## Purpose
Generate a crystal-clear scope of work section that sets expectations, prevents scope creep, and protects both parties.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a professional consultant writing the Scope of Work section of a client proposal.

PROJECT DETAILS:
- Project type: [WEB_DESIGN / MARKETING / IT / COPYWRITING / CONSULTING / OTHER]
- Specific deliverables: [LIST_WHAT_YOU_WILL_DELIVER]
- Timeline: [TOTAL_DURATION, e.g. "6 weeks"]
- Number of revision rounds: [NUMBER]
- Key milestones: [MILESTONE_1, MILESTONE_2, etc. — or "evenly spaced"]

Write a professional Scope of Work section for a client proposal. Include:

**IN SCOPE** (bulleted list)
- List every deliverable clearly and specifically
- Include formats, quantities, and quality standards where relevant
- Include any discovery/kickoff activities

**PROJECT TIMELINE**
- Phase-based breakdown with week numbers or dates
- Each phase: name, duration, what gets delivered

**REVISION POLICY**
- How many rounds are included
- What counts as a revision vs. a new request
- Turnaround time for revisions

**OUT OF SCOPE**
- 3–5 bullets of things explicitly NOT included (prevents "I thought that was included" disputes)
- Note that out-of-scope items can be quoted separately

Tone: Clear, professional, protective of your time without sounding defensive.
Format: Use headers and bullets. Keep each bullet to one line.
```

---

## Example Inputs

- Project type: Website redesign (Shopify)
- Deliverables: "New homepage, 3 product collection pages, mobile-optimized cart, speed optimization"
- Timeline: 6 weeks
- Milestones: "Week 1 kickoff/wireframes, Week 3 design mockups, Week 5 development, Week 6 QA + launch"
- Revisions: 2 rounds per deliverable

---

## Tips

- The out-of-scope section is as important as in-scope — it protects you
- Be specific with quantities: "5 pages" not "a few pages"
- If uncertain about timeline, build in a 20% buffer — better to deliver early than late
- Add a "client responsibilities" clause if the project depends on their inputs (content, access, approvals)
