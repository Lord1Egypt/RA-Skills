## Description: <br>
Critical Thinking helps an agent evaluate claims, arguments, plans, or beliefs by clarifying assertions, weighing evidence, surfacing assumptions, testing reasoning, considering alternatives, and producing a proportionate conclusion. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Employees, external users, developers, and agents use this skill to review a claim, plan, argument, or belief with a structured critical-thinking pass. It is intended for evaluative requests such as red-team review, bias and fallacy scans, decision-quality review, or epistemic calibration. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill can make responses more structured and evaluative when critique is requested or implied, which may be a poor fit for purely creative, wording, or execution-only tasks. <br>
Mitigation: Invoke it explicitly for critical-thinking reviews and avoid using it when the user only wants creative generation, wording changes, formatting, or direct execution. <br>
Risk: A structured critique can still produce incorrect or misleading judgments if missing evidence or assumptions are treated as established facts. <br>
Mitigation: Keep cited and missing evidence separate, label ungrounded premises, and review conclusions before relying on them for decisions. <br>
