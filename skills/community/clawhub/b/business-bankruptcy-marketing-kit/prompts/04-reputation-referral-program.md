## CPA and Financial Advisor Referral Program

**RPC 7.2 compliance:** Referral fees to non-attorneys are prohibited under RPC 7.2(b). This program is educational co-marketing only — no fee splitting, no referral payments, no reciprocal referral arrangements requiring mutual fee sharing.

**What this program offers CPAs and financial advisors:**

**Co-Marketing Materials Available:**
- Co-branded PDF: "When Your Client's Business Is in Financial Distress — A CPA's Guide to the Referral Conversation" (covers: Subchapter V eligibility indicators, preference risk signals, entity standing requirements, when to refer vs. when to wait)
- Co-branded PDF: "Nevada Business Insolvency Indicators Checklist" (for CPA use in annual review conversations)
- Quarterly lunch-and-learn: CLE-eligible presentation for accounting firms on Nevada business bankruptcy options and CPA liability when clients file bankruptcy

**Referral Process:**
1. CPA calls [PHONE] or emails [EMAIL] with client name and brief situation description
2. We provide same-week consultation (urgent situations: same-day)
3. After consultation, we contact CPA to confirm appointment was made (no confidential details shared)
4. Monthly summary email: number of referrals received from firm, no client-specific information

**What CPAs can say to clients (suggested language):**
"I want to make sure you've spoken with a business bankruptcy attorney before we make any decisions about the company's direction. I work with [ATTORNEY] at [FIRM] — they handle exactly this kind of situation and I think a consultation would be valuable. I can make an introduction if you'd like."

**What CPAs should NOT say:** Anything guaranteeing an outcome, suggesting bankruptcy is the right path, or indicating that [FIRM] has handled their specific situation before.

## Bank Workout Officer Referral Pipeline

**Target:** Commercial loan officers and workout departments at Nevada's regional banks and credit unions.

**Program:** Quarterly educational lunch meeting with workout teams covering:
- Chapter 11 secured creditor rights (adequate protection, § 362(d) relief motions)
- Subchapter V and small business reorganization — what it means for bank collateral
- DIP financing and priming lien process
- Cash collateral orders and bank consent strategy
- NRS 104 (Nevada UCC) perfection intersection with bankruptcy

**What the bank gets:** Direct line to bankruptcy counsel they trust when a commercial borrower files. Faster adequate protection and cash collateral coordination.

**What this is NOT:** Fee splitting, kickbacks, or reciprocal referral agreements. This is relationship-based legal networking consistent with RPC 7.2(b)(1) (lawyer referral service permitted) and RPC 7.3 (permissible in-person contact in a professional context).
