# Competitive Analysis Framework

## Part 1: Competitor Discovery

### Where to Find Competitors
- **G2 / Capterra**: category pages for SaaS
- **Product Hunt**: recent launches in your space
- **App Store / Google Play**: search category keywords
- **Crunchbase**: filter by industry + funding stage
- **LinkedIn**: company search by keyword + employee count
- **SEO tools** (Ahrefs/SEMrush): who ranks for your target keywords

### Competitor Classification
```
Tier 1 – Direct competitors: same product, same customer, same problem
Tier 2 – Indirect competitors: different product, overlapping use case
Tier 3 – Substitutes: alternative approaches (e.g., Excel instead of analytics tool)
```

## Part 3: Positioning Analysis

### Perceptual Map (2×2)
Choose two axes that matter to your customers:
- Price (low ↔ high)
- Ease of use (simple ↔ complex)
- Target market (SMB ↔ Enterprise)
- Feature depth (basic ↔ advanced)

Plot competitors to find whitespace.

### Value Proposition Deconstruction
For each key competitor, extract:
```
Headline claim: "..."
Primary benefit: ...
Target persona: ...
Proof points: (case studies, metrics, logos)
Unique differentiator: ...
```

## Part 5: Win/Loss Pattern Analysis

When you have access to sales data or customer interviews:

### Win Patterns
- What do customers say when they choose you over X?
- Which features are cited most in wins?
- Which customer segments do you win most?

### Loss Patterns
- What are the top reasons for losing deals?
- Which competitors do you lose to most?
- Is it price, features, brand, or relationships?

### Displacement Opportunities
```
Competitor X weaknesses → Your attack surface:
- Weakness: poor mobile experience
  → Our response: highlight mobile-first design
- Weakness: complex onboarding (avg 3 weeks)
  → Our response: "Live in 24 hours" campaign
```

## Part 6: Monitoring Competitors Ongoing

### Free Monitoring Stack
- **Google Alerts**: set for competitor name + "pricing", "new feature"
- **LinkedIn**: follow competitor company pages
- **ProductHunt**: subscribe to their profile
- **G2 / Trustpilot**: monitor new reviews weekly
- **Job postings**: hiring signals reveal product roadmap bets
- **Twitter/X**: follow founders, track mentions

### Signal Interpretation
| Signal | What It Might Mean |
|--------|-------------------|
| Hiring ML engineers | Building AI features |
| Opening enterprise sales roles | Moving upmarket |
| Cutting prices 20%+ | Growth slowdown / new competition |
| Partnership announcement | Expanding distribution |
| Acquiring small startup | Filling feature gap |
