# Prompt 3: Client Retention & Referral Campaign

## Purpose
Stop losing clients after the first visit. Generate a complete retention system: new client nurture sequence, referral program, Google review sequence, lapsed client win-back, front desk rebooking scripts, and VIP loyalty framework.

## The Revenue Math
- Average salon ticket: $85-150
- New client visits per year without retention system: 1.4
- New client visits per year with rebooking system: 4.2
- Revenue difference per client: $255-420/year
- For a salon with 50 new clients/month: $12,750-21,000/year in recovered revenue

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a salon business coach and retention marketing specialist. Generate a complete client retention and referral system for the business below.

BUSINESS DETAILS:
- Business name: [NAME]
- Business type: [hair salon / nail studio / med spa / massage / esthetician / day spa]
- Location: [CITY, STATE]
- Top service: [e.g., "Balayage and color services"]
- Average ticket: $[AMOUNT]
- Current rebooking rate: [e.g., "about 40% rebook within 90 days"]
- Current Google review count: [NUMBER]
- Owner/manager first name: [NAME]

GENERATE THE FOLLOWING:

1. 5-EMAIL NEW CLIENT NURTURE SEQUENCE
   Day 0 — Welcome (send immediately after first visit):
   - Subject line + preview text
   - 200-word email: welcome → highlight specific service received → what to expect next → care instructions → rebooking CTA with urgency ("We book out 3-4 weeks — your next appointment is easier to get if you book before you leave")
   - P.S. line: teaser for next visit upgrade

   Day 3 — Check-in:
   - Subject line + preview text
   - 100-word email: ask how they're feeling about their [service] → answer common Day 3 questions → soft rebooking reminder

   Day 7 — Google Review Ask:
   - Subject line + preview text
   - 125-word email: gratitude → why their review matters (helps other clients find the salon) → direct Google Maps review link placeholder → "takes under 2 minutes"

   Day 14 — Rebooking Offer:
   - Subject line + preview text
   - 175-word email: time-sensitive offer (10% off or free add-on if they book by [specific date]) → consequences of waiting (fading, roots, regrowth) → book now CTA

   Day 30 — Missed You + Loyalty Invite:
   - Subject line + preview text
   - 150-word email: check-in without pressure → VIP program introduction → book now or refer a friend offer

2. REFERRAL PROGRAM LAUNCH KIT
   a) Referral program announcement email (200 words): introduce the program, state the reward clearly, how it works (3 steps), book now CTA
   b) In-studio card copy (fits on 3.5" × 2" card):
      - Front: bold headline + offer
      - Back: 3-step how it works + code/URL placeholder
   c) "Refer a Friend" Instagram post (caption + 15 hashtags):
      - Personal story angle (why referrals are special to the business)
      - Clear offer stated
      - Tag a friend CTA

3. 3-TOUCH GOOGLE REVIEW REQUEST SEQUENCE
   Touch 1 — Day 7 email (from Email Sequence above — reference it here)
   Touch 2 — Day 21 SMS (TCPA-compliant, under 160 chars):
      [BUSINESS NAME]: Hi [First Name], loved having you in! If you have 2 min, a Google review means the world to us: [LINK]. Reply STOP to opt out.
   Touch 3 — Day 45 personal note (hand-written card template):
      5-sentence handwritten card copy — thank them for being a client, mention their specific service, invite them back, ask for review

4. LAPSED CLIENT WIN-BACK SEQUENCE
   (For clients who haven't booked in 60+ days)

   30-Day Lapsed Email:
   - Subject: "[First Name], we noticed you haven't been in..."
   - 175-word email: personal check-in (not corporate) → "life gets busy" acknowledgment → irresistible reason to come back (new service, seasonal treatment, team update) → special offer (15% off or free add-on) with 10-day expiration → easy book now CTA

   60-Day Lapsed Email:
   - Subject line that implies scarcity/change
   - 150-word email: acknowledge time has passed → show what's new since they were last in → FOMO angle (before/after transformation story) → "We saved a spot for you" offer with specific expiration

   90-Day Lapsed SMS (TCPA-compliant):
   [BUSINESS NAME]: [First Name], it's been a while! We miss you. Book this week and get [OFFER]. [BOOKING LINK] Reply STOP to opt out.

5. FRONT DESK REBOOKING SCRIPTS — 5 OBJECTIONS
   For each: the client's exact words → the front desk response (2-3 sentences) → the close

   Objection 1: "I'll call when I'm ready / I'll book online later"
   Objection 2: "It's a bit expensive right now"
   Objection 3: "I'm so busy, I don't know when I'll have time"
   Objection 4: "I want to try somewhere else / I'm exploring my options"
   Objection 5: "I'm moving / I won't be in the area much longer"

   For each: explain WHY this objection occurs and what the client actually needs to hear.

6. VIP LOYALTY PROGRAM FRAMEWORK
   a) Three tier names (beauty/wellness themed, not generic Gold/Silver/Bronze):
      - Entry tier (after 3 visits): name + 3 perks
      - Core tier (after 8 visits or $500 spend): name + 5 perks
      - Elite tier (after 15 visits or $1,200 spend): name + 7 perks + exclusive perk

   b) Launch announcement email (200 words): introduce program → explain tiers → tell existing clients their current status → CTA to rebook and unlock next tier

   c) Tier milestone SMS template (TCPA-compliant):
      [BUSINESS NAME]: [First Name], you just hit [TIER NAME] status! You've unlocked [PERK]. Book your next visit: [LINK]. Reply STOP to opt out.

   d) Annual birthday email template (150 words): personal, not transactional → birthday offer (free add-on or $20 off) → expires in 30 days
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Provide the real average ticket and rebooking rate — the revenue math in the email copy will be calibrated to your actual numbers
- The front desk scripts work best when role-played with staff before use — include them in your next team meeting

## The Rebooking Window
The best time to rebook is at checkout, not via email. Use the email sequence for clients who left without rebooking. The front desk scripts are for the 90-second conversation at the register that determines whether the client comes back.
