# Diagnosis — Make Friends

Use this before suggesting tactics. Friendship strategy fails when fit, logistics, and energy are vague.

## Friend-Fit Snapshot

Capture these in plain language:

| Area | What to learn | Why it matters |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Friend type | Creative, active, nerdy, local, sober, family-stage, ambitious, calm | Better fit means easier repetition |
| Life rhythm | Work hours, weekends, caregiving, recovery time | Filters impossible plans |
| Geography | Neighborhood, transit tolerance, car or no car | Distance kills weak ties |
| Social energy | Introvert, socially anxious, high-energy, mixed | Sets conversation and event intensity |
| Budget | Free, cheap, moderate | Prevents self-sabotage by friction |
| Environment | Small groups, classes, sports, volunteering, co-working, online-to-offline | Repeated context matters most |

## Opportunity Map

Build three columns:

| Bucket | Examples | Target count |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Repeatable habitats | Weekly class, run club, volunteering shift, game night, co-working | 2-3 |
| Warm contacts | Coworker, neighbor, friend-of-friend, old acquaintance | 3-5 |
| Low-pressure invites | Coffee, walk, same event next week, group plan, shared errand | 2-3 ready |

## Friction Audit

Find what actually blocks friendship:

- Not enough repeated contexts
- Too many one-off events
- Good conversation, no follow-up
- Bad fit in current scenes
- Social anxiety before or after events
- Schedule too full for consistency
- Strong online ties, no offline bridge

Fix the bottleneck before scaling effort.

## Output Standard

After diagnosis, the agent should be able to answer:

1. What kind of friends the user wants more of
2. Which 2-3 habitats are worth repeating
3. Which 1-3 people deserve follow-up this week
4. What smallest realistic next action fits current energy
