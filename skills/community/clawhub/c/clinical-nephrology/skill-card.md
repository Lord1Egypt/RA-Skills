## Description: <br>
Clinical nephrology support for kidney disease questions: AKI, CKD, proteinuria, hematuria, glomerular disease, acid-base and electrolytes, critical care nephrology, CRRT, dialysis, kidney replacement therapy, transplant, interventional nephrology, pregnancy kidney disease, and onconephrology. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Clinicians, clinical educators, and medically supervised users use this skill to structure nephrology reasoning, identify red flags, plan literature searches, and prepare guideline-oriented consult-style discussion. It supports education and clinical reasoning but does not replace local clinical judgment, bedside assessment, institutional protocols, or specialist care. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Medical outputs could be incorrect, outdated, or inappropriate for a specific patient. <br>
Mitigation: Treat outputs as support for education and clinical discussion; verify current guidelines, drug details, and procedure details before use. <br>
Risk: Urgent, personalized, pregnancy, transplant, oncology, ICU, pediatric, or dosing decisions require context beyond the skill. <br>
Mitigation: Rely on qualified clinicians, local protocols, and specialist teams for patient-specific decisions in these scenarios. <br>
