## Description: <br>
ZM Quality Production Workflow guides agents through standardized inputs, sample gates, specialist production, dual-agent review, revision loops, final acceptance, and delivery-package archiving for formal content work. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Content, course, design, and delivery teams use this skill to organize formal outputs such as course packages, PPT or HTML decks, comics or handbooks, public articles, and industry AI proposals. It helps agents collect required inputs, identify dependency gaps, route work to specialist agents, run independent reviews, and archive a complete delivery package. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Process guidance can lead an agent to produce incorrect or incomplete formal content if goals, source materials, or acceptance criteria are unclear. <br>
Mitigation: Use the standard input card, dependency gap list, sample gate, dual-agent review, and final acceptance record before treating the output as complete. <br>
Risk: Agents may be tempted to fill missing logos, QR codes, cases, data, screenshots, or other real-world materials with plausible but false content. <br>
Mitigation: Mark missing required materials as dependency gaps or BLOCKED, use placeholders only when clearly labeled, and require real assets or source data before full production. <br>
Risk: The workflow may prepare local delivery-package files or publishing materials that could be sent externally. <br>
Mitigation: Keep confirmation gates in place for any external upload, send, delete, payment, or publishing action, and review generated files before use. <br>
