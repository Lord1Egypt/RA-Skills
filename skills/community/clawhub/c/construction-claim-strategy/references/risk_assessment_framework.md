# Risk Assessment Framework

Use this framework during the Risk Assessment dimension to evaluate your position honestly and systematically.

---

## Risk Categories

| Category | Key Questions to Ask | Typical Mitigation |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Notice Timing** | Was notice given on time? When did awareness occur? | Explain awareness date; use Factual Distinction if appropriate |
| **Causation** | Can I clearly link event to delay/productivity loss? | Gather best evidence; consider expert support |
| **Quantum Strength** | Are records complete and reliable? | Defer detailed quantum if records are weak |
| **Contract Interpretation** | How might the other side interpret key clauses? | Prepare strong textual and factual arguments |
| **Evidential Gaps** | What key evidence is missing? | Identify gaps early and plan how to address them |
| **Relationship Risk** | Will this damage the commercial relationship? | Maintain professional tone throughout |
| **Time Bar / Limitation** | Are there contractual or legal time limits? | Check early and address proactively |

---

## Practical Risk Assessment Questions

Ask yourself these questions during the Risk Assessment stage:

1. What is the **single weakest point** in my position?
2. What is the **most likely counter-argument** the other side will raise?
3. If I were advising the other side, how would I attack this claim?
4. What **evidence am I still missing**?
5. What is the **worst-case outcome** if this goes to dispute?

## Best Practice

Be **honest** during Risk Assessment. The goal is not to make your position look perfect, but to understand it realistically so you can:
- Strengthen weak areas before submission
- Prepare for pushback
- Decide on appropriate disclosure levels
- Choose the right argument strategy
