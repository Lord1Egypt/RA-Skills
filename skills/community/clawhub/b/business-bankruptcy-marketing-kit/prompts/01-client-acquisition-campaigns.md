# Module 1 — Client Acquisition Campaigns

**Nevada Business Bankruptcy Law Marketing Kit — Skill #253**

All campaigns must include on every output: "This is an advertisement." (SCR 192). All bankruptcy attorney ads must include: "We are a debt relief agency. We help people file for bankruptcy relief under the Bankruptcy Code." (11 USC § 528(a)(4)).

Never use: "specialist," "expert," or certification claims without State Bar certification. Never guarantee outcomes: "save your business," "keep your equity," "eliminate your debt." Never advise paying specific creditors before filing without preference analysis.

### Email 2 of 3 — Follow-Up (Subchapter V Eligibility Focus)

Subject: The bankruptcy law most business attorneys don't tell you about

[FIRST NAME],

I want to make sure you're aware of Subchapter V before we meet.

In 2019, Congress passed the Small Business Reorganization Act, creating a streamlined Chapter 11 path (11 USC §§ 1181–1195) for businesses with total non-contingent, liquidated debts of $7.5 million or less.

**What Subchapter V eliminates:**
- Disclosure statement (typically $15,000–$50,000 in legal fees to prepare and get approved)
- Creditors' committee (committee's attorneys are paid by YOUR estate)
- Competing plans from creditors or committees
- Full absolute priority rule (equity can retain interests if plan funded from projected disposable income)

**What Subchapter V requires:**
- Standing trustee (reviews plan but doesn't operate business)
- Plan filed within 90 days of petition date
- Feasibility showing adequate cash flow

If your business has under $7.5M in non-contingent debt, Subchapter V is almost certainly the right starting point for our analysis.

### Email 3 of 3 — Pre-Consultation Checklist (Preference + Entity Focus)

Subject: 3 things to check before our meeting — your business's bankruptcy exposure

[FIRST NAME],

Three items to check before we meet. Each one affects our strategy significantly.

**1. Payments made in the last 90 days**
Any payment to a non-insider creditor of $6,825 or more in the 90 days before a bankruptcy filing can be clawed back by the trustee (§ 547). For insider payments (officers, family, affiliated companies), the lookback period is 1 year. Bring a list of significant payments — we'll flag preference exposure before it becomes a problem.

**2. Your entity's Nevada standing**
Go to nvsilverflume.gov and check: is your corporation or LLC in good standing? Revoked articles = potential dismissal of a Chapter 11 filing. If you're not in good standing, we can fix it before filing — but we need to know now.

**3. Your personal guarantee exposure**
Bankruptcy protects the business entity. Your personal guarantees survive the business filing. We'll map your personal exposure separately so you understand the complete picture.

Bring these three items to our consultation and we'll have a much clearer picture of your options in the first 30 minutes.
