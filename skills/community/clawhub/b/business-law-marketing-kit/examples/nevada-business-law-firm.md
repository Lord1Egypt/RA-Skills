## Compliance Blockers Found in Original Marketing — All 14 Corrected

### Blocker 1 [MOAT 1]: Missing SCR 192 disclosure across all materials
**Original:** Zero "This is an advertisement." disclosures on any ads, GBP posts, or email.
**Corrected:** "This is an advertisement." added to every ad description, GBP post footer, email signature, and website footer. Nevada SCR 192 requires this on all attorney advertising.

### Blocker 2 [MOAT 1]: Outcome guarantee in headline
**Original:** "We'll Protect Your Business — Guaranteed"
**Corrected:** "Nevada Business Law Counsel — Licensed Attorney" — process-focused, no outcome claim.

### Blocker 3 [MOAT 1]: Specialization claim
**Original:** "Las Vegas's Leading Business Law Specialist"
**Corrected:** "Las Vegas Business Law Attorney — NV Bar No. 18432" — no specialist claim (Nevada State Bar has no "business law specialist" certification under RPC 7.1(b)).

### Blocker 4 [MOAT 2]: Flat fee LLC ad missing state fee disclosure
**Original:** "$895 All-In Nevada LLC Formation"
**Corrected:** "$895 attorney fee for Nevada LLC formation. State filing fee ($75 online / $425 expedited), registered agent ($50–$200/year), and annual list ($150) are additional. Fee agreement provided in writing before work begins."

### Blocker 5 [MOAT 2]: Contract review scope undefined
**Original:** "Contract Review — $350 Flat Fee"
**Corrected:** "$350 attorney fee covers review of contracts up to 20 pages, written summary of key risks, and one round of revision recommendations. Additional pages and negotiation are billed separately. Scope and fee confirmed in written engagement letter before work begins."

### Blocker 6 [MOAT 3]: Missing Nevada Modified Business Tax disclosure
**Original:** "Nevada LLCs Pay Zero Income Tax — Form Yours Today"
**Corrected:** "Nevada LLCs have no state income tax on pass-through income. Note: Nevada Modified Business Tax (NRS 363B) applies to employers with taxable wages exceeding $50,000/quarter. Consult a CPA about your specific tax obligations."

### Blocker 7 [MOAT 3]: Series LLC overpromise
**Original:** "Series LLC — The Ultimate Asset Protection Tool"
**Corrected:** "Nevada Series LLC (NRS 86.296) may provide asset protection between series for properly maintained structures. Note: liability isolation between series is untested in federal bankruptcy court and may not be recognized in states where the LLC does business. Consult an attorney to determine if a Series LLC is appropriate for your situation."

### Blocker 8 [MOAT 3]: Delaware vs. Nevada overclaim
**Original:** "Nevada Corporations Are Better Than Delaware — Period."
**Corrected:** "Nevada corporations offer no franchise tax and strong privacy protections. For VC-backed startups seeking venture funding, Delaware corporations remain preferred due to the Court of Chancery and established case law. Carlos Reyes can help you select the right jurisdiction for your specific goals."

### Blocker 9 [MOAT 4]: Non-compete enforcement promise
**Original:** "We Draft Ironclad Non-Compete Agreements That Hold Up in Court"
**Corrected:** "Non-compete agreements in Nevada are subject to enforceability requirements under NRS 613.195. Non-competes are void for hourly employees. For other employees, enforceability depends on legitimate business interest, reasonable scope, and Nevada choice-of-law. Carlos Reyes reviews and drafts non-competes that comply with Nevada's 2021 reform."

### Blocker 10 [MOAT 4]: Missing hourly employee prohibition
**Original:** Non-compete service page made no mention of NRS 613.195(4).
**Corrected:** Prominent disclosure added: "Nevada law (NRS 613.195(4)) prohibits non-compete agreements for employees compensated solely on an hourly basis. Non-compete enforceability for salaried employees requires: (1) valuable consideration, (2) legitimate business interest, (3) reasonable geographic and temporal scope, and (4) Nevada choice-of-law clause."

### Blocker 11 [MOAT 5]: Equity-for-fees marketed without RPC 1.8(a) disclosure
**Original:** "We Invest In Our Clients — We Accept Equity In Your Startup"
**Corrected:** "Reyes Business Law offers equity compensation arrangements for qualifying startup clients as an alternative to traditional hourly fees. These arrangements are governed by Nevada RPC 1.8(a) and require: written disclosure of the transaction terms, the client's opportunity to seek independent legal counsel, and the client's written informed consent. Contact us to learn more about our startup engagement process."

### Blocker 12 [MOAT 6]: False LegalZoom comparison
**Original:** "We Do Everything LegalZoom Does, But Better"
**Corrected:** "Online formation services provide document preparation — not legal advice. Carlos Reyes, as a licensed Nevada attorney, provides: legal advice tailored to your situation, attorney-client privilege (which document prep services cannot provide), operating agreement drafting that accounts for Nevada's specific requirements, and ongoing legal counsel as your business evolves."

### Blocker 13 [MOAT 7]: Investment offering overclaim
**Original:** "We Help Nevada Startups Raise Money From Investors"
**Corrected:** "Reyes Business Law advises Nevada startups on securities law compliance for investment offerings. Federal securities law (SEC Reg D) applies to all private investment offerings regardless of state. Rule 506(b) permits up to 35 non-accredited investors (no general solicitation). Rule 506(c) permits general solicitation with accredited investors only. A Form D must be filed with the SEC within 15 days of first sale. We help clients select and comply with the appropriate exemption — we do not guarantee successful fundraising."

### Blocker 14 [MOAT 7]: Nevada intrastate exemption overclaim
**Original:** "Nevada Businesses Can Raise Money From Local Investors Without SEC Filing"
**Corrected:** "Nevada's intrastate offering exemption (NRS 90.530(11)) is narrow: all purchasers must be Nevada residents, and the issuer must be incorporated in Nevada and doing business primarily in Nevada. This exemption does NOT apply when any purchaser is an out-of-state resident and does NOT exempt the offering from federal securities law. Most Nevada businesses raising investment capital require federal Reg D compliance. Consult a securities attorney before accepting investor funds."
