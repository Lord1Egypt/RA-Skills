# Prompt 2: Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Narrative

## Prompt

You are a strategic account director preparing for a high-stakes Quarterly Business Review. Write the complete QBR narrative — the written content that the agency will present and leave behind as a document. This must show business impact, build strategic trust, and set up a confident conversation about the next quarter.

**Client Context:**
- Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]
- Industry: [e.g., B2B SaaS / E-Commerce / Healthcare]
- Quarter: [e.g., Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar)]
- Contract Value: [e.g., $5,000/month = $15,000 for the quarter]
- Services: [e.g., Content Marketing + SEO + Email Marketing]
- Meeting Format: [e.g., 60-min Zoom call / in-person lunch]

**Q Goals vs Results:**
[List each goal set at the start of the quarter with its actual result]
- Goal 1: Increase organic traffic 20% → Actual: +31%
- Goal 2: Generate 150 marketing-qualified leads → Actual: 142 MQLs
- Goal 3: Launch email list to 1,000 subscribers → Actual: 847 subscribers
- Goal 4: Publish 12 long-form blog posts → Actual: 11 published

**Key Campaigns/Projects:**
[What were the major initiatives this quarter?]

**Competitive Context:**
[Any relevant market shifts, competitor moves, or industry changes?]

**Q+1 Strategic Priorities:**
[What are the 3 big bets for next quarter?]

---

Generate the QBR document with these sections:

1. **QBR Agenda** (with time allocations for a [60-min] meeting)
   - Example: 0:00-0:05 Welcome & Housekeeping / 0:05-0:20 Q1 Results / etc.

2. **Quarter in Review** (2 paragraph narrative — what the quarter was about and how it unfolded)

3. **Results vs Goals Scorecard** (table)
   | Goal | Target | Actual | Delta | Grade |
   (Grade A = exceeded, B = met, C = within 10%, D = missed, F = significantly missed)

4. **What Drove Performance** (3 key drivers — what worked and why, with evidence)

5. **What We'd Do Differently** (2-3 honest retrospective points — shows maturity and earns trust)

6. **Q+1 Strategy Brief**
   - 3 strategic priorities with 1-2 sentence rationale each
   - Expected outcomes (what success looks like in 90 days)
   - Key metrics we'll track

7. **Success Metrics for Next Quarter** (table: Metric | Q1 Actual | Q2 Target)

8. **Verbal Talking Points** (bulleted notes for the agency presenter — not a script, just key points to hit in each section)

**Tone:** Executive. Confident but humble. Show the "why" behind every result — don't just list numbers. The client should leave feeling that the agency is a strategic partner, not a vendor.
