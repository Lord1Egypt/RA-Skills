## Grammar Error Scripts

Use these explanations when teaching specific error types. Always quote the learner's error first, then explain.

### Subject-Verb Agreement

> **Error:** "The data show that usage has increased."
> **Type:** Subject-verb agreement
> **Fixed:** "The data _shows_ that usage has increased."
> **Rule:** "Data" is treated as a singular uncountable noun in formal academic writing. The verb must be singular: _shows_, not _show_.
> **Other examples:** "Information is..." / "Evidence suggests..." / "Research shows..."

### Article Errors (a / an / the / zero)

> **Error:** "Technology has changed the communication."
> **Type:** Article error — unnecessary definite article
> **Fixed:** "Technology has changed _communication_."
> **Rule:** Abstract nouns used in a general sense take zero article: communication, society, education, technology, information. Use "the" only when referring to a specific instance already introduced.
> **Tip for learners:** Ask: "Am I talking about this thing in general, or a specific one I already mentioned?" → General = no article. Specific = "the".

### Tense Consistency

> **Error:** "The report _shows_ that sales increased in 2023 and _will grow_ further."
> **Type:** Tense shift error
> **Fixed:** "The report shows that sales increased in 2023 and _are projected to grow_ further."
> **Rule:** Stay in the same time frame unless you have a clear reason to shift. Mixing present simple, past simple, and future in one sentence without logical reason creates confusion.

### Sentence Fragments

> **Error:** "Technology has many benefits. Such as improving communication."
> **Type:** Sentence fragment — "Such as improving communication" cannot stand alone.
> **Fixed:** "Technology has many benefits, such as improving communication."
> **Rule:** A fragment is a group of words punctuated as a sentence but missing a subject, verb, or complete thought. Attach it to the main clause.

### Run-on Sentences

> **Error:** "Social media connects people worldwide, this has changed how we communicate."
> **Type:** Run-on sentence — two independent clauses joined only by a comma (comma splice)
> **Fixed options:**
>
> - "Social media connects people worldwide. _This has changed_ how we communicate."
> - "Social media connects people worldwide, _and this has changed_ how we communicate."
> - "Social media connects people worldwide; _this has changed_ how we communicate."
>   **Rule:** Two complete sentences cannot be joined by a comma alone. Use a full stop, a semicolon, or a conjunction.

### Countable vs. Uncountable Nouns

> **Error:** "She gave me some good advices."
> **Type:** Uncountable noun error
> **Fixed:** "She gave me some good _advice_."
> **Uncountable nouns (no plural, no "a"):** advice, information, knowledge, research, equipment, furniture, progress, evidence, feedback, news, work, homework
> **Common pattern:** "_A piece of_ advice" is correct if you want to count it.

### Preposition Errors

> **Error:** "I am interested _on_ this topic."
> **Type:** Preposition error
> **Fixed:** "I am interested _in_ this topic."
> **Common fixed prepositions:**
>
> - interested **in** / dependent **on** / responsible **for** / result **in** / reason **for**
> - capable **of** / effect **on** / advantage **of** / disadvantage **of** / similar **to**
>   **Tip:** Prepositions are mostly memorized, not rule-based. Build a personal list of collocations.

### Passive Voice (Misuse)

> **Error:** "The government was increased the tax."
> **Type:** Passive voice error — incorrect formation
> **Fixed:** "The government _increased_ the tax." (active) OR "The tax _was increased_ by the government." (passive)
> **Rule:** Passive = "to be" + past participle. "Was increased" is already passive — you cannot add another verb before it. If you want passive, remove the agent ("The government") or move it to "by the government."

### Parallel Structure

> **Error:** "The benefits include _improving_ productivity, _lower_ costs, and _to boost_ morale."
> **Type:** Parallelism error
> **Fixed:** "The benefits include _improving_ productivity, _lowering_ costs, and _boosting_ morale."
> **Rule:** Items in a list must be the same grammatical form. If you start with a gerund (-ing), all items must be gerunds.

### Missing Topic Sentence

> **Error:** [Paragraph starts with an example before stating the main idea]
> **Feedback:** "This paragraph doesn't have a topic sentence. The reader doesn't know what the paragraph is about until sentence 3. A topic sentence states the main idea of the paragraph in the first sentence."
> **Fix:** "Add a topic sentence before your example. Try: 'One major benefit of remote work is increased employee productivity. For example...'"

## Vocabulary Feedback Scripts

### Repetition Warning

> "You used the word _'important'_ three times in this paragraph. Repetition weakens impact. Here are alternatives with slightly different nuances:
>
> - **crucial** (emphasizes that it cannot be omitted)
> - **significant** (emphasizes measurable impact)
> - **essential** (emphasizes necessity)
> - **vital** (emphasizes urgency or life/death stakes)
>   Choose based on what you're emphasizing."

### Register Mismatch

> "The word _'stuff'_ is too informal for an academic essay. In this context, try _'aspects'_, _'elements'_, or _'factors'_ instead. Save _'stuff'_ for informal writing — messages, notes, casual speech."

### Vague Language

> "The phrase _'a lot of'_ is vague and informal. In academic writing, be specific:
>
> - Use numbers/percentages when you have them: 'over 60% of respondents'
> - Use formal quantifiers: 'a significant proportion', 'the majority of', 'a substantial number of'"

### False Cognate / Wrong Word

> "The word _'actually'_ in English doesn't mean 'currently' or 'at the moment' — it means 'in fact' or 'surprisingly'. This is a common confusion for [Vietnamese/Spanish/French] speakers. The correct word here is _'currently'_ or _'at present'_."

## Revision Prompts

Use these after giving feedback to guide the revision stage:

**For a weak thesis:**

> "Your current introduction doesn't take a clear position. Rewrite the last sentence of your introduction as a thesis. It should: (1) state your opinion clearly, (2) preview the two reasons you'll discuss. Try: 'This essay argues that [position] because [reason 1] and [reason 2].'"

**For a paragraph with no topic sentence:**

> "Rewrite this paragraph. Start with a topic sentence that tells the reader exactly what this paragraph is about. Everything else in the paragraph should support that one sentence."

**For missing cohesion:**

> "Your paragraphs feel disconnected. Add one cohesive device at the start of body paragraph 2. Choose from: 'Furthermore,' / 'In addition,' / 'Another significant factor is...' / 'A second reason for this is...'"

**For over-simple vocabulary:**

> "In your revision, replace the following words with more precise alternatives: [word 1] → [option A or B], [word 2] → [option A or B]. Then resubmit this paragraph."

**For grammar drill (article errors):**

> "Rewrite these 5 sentences, adding or removing articles correctly:
>
> 1. Technology is changing the society.
> 2. I received a good advice from my manager.
> 3. The education is important for all children.
> 4. She made the progress in her studies.
> 5. We need to protect the environment."

## Cohesive Device Mini-Lesson Script

Use when a learner's text has no connectors or uses them incorrectly:

> "Your essay reads like a list of separate points. Good writing connects ideas using cohesive devices — words and phrases that show the relationship between sentences and paragraphs.
>
> Here are the main types with examples from your topic:
>
> **Adding:** 'Furthermore, remote work also reduces commuting time.'
> **Contrasting:** 'However, not all employees benefit equally from this arrangement.'
> **Cause/Effect:** 'As a result, productivity levels have increased significantly.'
> **Exemplifying:** 'For instance, companies like Google have adopted hybrid models successfully.'
>
> Now rewrite your second body paragraph using at least two cohesive devices. Underline them so I can check."

## Session Closing Scripts

### Standard close

> "Good work today. You went from a [X]/10 draft to a [Y]/10 revision — that's real improvement. The key thing to practice before next time is [ONE PRIORITY]. Try writing one more paragraph on this topic tonight using that skill."

### Exam prep close

> "Under timed conditions, your writing is at approximately Band [X]. To reach Band [Y], the most important change is [specific]. Between now and your exam, practice one timed Task 2 per day and check your own topic sentences before anything else."

### Encouragement for struggling learner

> "Writing is one of the hardest skills to improve — but you're doing the right thing by practicing with feedback rather than just writing alone. The errors you're making are very common and very fixable. Focus on one thing at a time. Today's focus was [X]. Next time, we'll build on that."
