# Prompt 4 — 30-Day Social Calendar + Newsletter + Google Posts

## Purpose
Generate a complete 30-day content system: social media calendar, patient newsletter, Google Business Profile posts, blog article with SEO schema, and referral program materials.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a content marketing specialist for chiropractic and physical therapy practices. Generate a complete 30-day content system. All content must comply with HIPAA (no patient clinical details), FTC (no absolute health outcome claims, testimonials must disclose individual results), ACA/APTA advertising ethics (no superiority claims over other providers), and state board advertising rules (no unsubstantiated superlatives).

**Practice name:** [PRACTICE NAME]
**Provider name(s) and credentials:** [e.g., Dr. James Okafor DC, CCSP | Dr. Lisa Chen DPT, OCS]
**Location:** [City, State + neighborhood]
**Primary specialties:** [e.g., auto injury, sports, family wellness, prenatal]
**Current seasonal angle:** [e.g., spring sports season, summer heat/hydration, back-to-school posture, holiday travel strain]
**Patient newsletter opt-in list size (approx):** [e.g., 200 patients]
**Social platforms active on:** [Instagram | Facebook | LinkedIn | TikTok | Google Posts]

---

**OUTPUT 1: 30-DAY SOCIAL MEDIA CALENDAR**

Generate 30 posts with the following breakdown:

4 × Educational posts (anatomy, injury prevention, exercise tips):
- Format options: carousel, short video hook script (15 sec), static image + caption
- No treatment promises. No scare tactics. Information only.
- Examples: "3 stretches for desk workers," "What your posture is telling you," "The difference between soreness and injury"

4 × Patient education posts (FAQs, myth-busting, what to expect):
- Common questions the practice gets, answered in post format
- No clinical advice — informational and "consult your provider" framing
- Examples: "Do I need a referral for PT? (In [state], the answer might surprise you)," "Is it normal to feel sore after a chiro visit?"

4 × Social proof posts (outcomes-focused, FTC-compliant):
- Use aggregate language ("many of our patients," "patients often tell us") NOT individual testimonials without disclosure
- If using specific testimonial: MUST include "Individual results may vary. This person's experience may not reflect typical outcomes."
- Format: quote card, text-heavy carousel, or short video script

3 × Community/culture posts (team, local partnership, behind the scenes):
- Team introduction or spotlight
- Local business partnership shoutout (referral relationship building)
- Behind-the-scenes: treatment room, equipment demo, day-in-the-life

3 × Promotional posts (new patient offer, seasonal campaign, referral program):
- New patient offer with FTC-compliant framing
- Seasonal angle tied to [SEASONAL ANGLE] provided above
- Referral program announcement (no cash-for-referrals — professional referral recognition only)

3 × Platform-specific Instagram Reel hook scripts (15-25 seconds each):
- Hook: First 3 seconds (pattern interrupt question or statistic)
- Body: 1 key point or visual demo
- CTA: schedule, follow, comment
- Topics: [1 injury-prevention tip, 1 patient FAQ, 1 seasonal/trending topic]

2 × LinkedIn articles (professional audience — MDs, attorneys, HR directors, insurance adjusters):
- Topic 1: Documentation and care coordination for PI/workers' comp cases (professional, referral-building)
- Topic 2: Ergonomics and workplace wellness ROI (HR director audience)
- Length: 400-600 words each
- Tone: peer-to-peer, not salesy

2 × Facebook event posts (community engagement, in-office events):
- Event 1: Free community workshop or health fair participation (e.g., "Free posture screening at [local gym/event]")
- Event 2: Practice open house or new service launch
- Include: date placeholder, RSVP instruction, FTC-compliant benefit language

2 × TikTok concept outlines (trending format, educational):
- Concept 1: "POV: You're a [chiropractor/PT] and someone comes in saying [common myth]" — myth-bust format
- Concept 2: Quick anatomy tip or "watch this before you [do common activity]" format
- Note: TikTok healthcare content rules — no medical advice, educational only, "see a provider" CTA

For each of the 30 posts, provide:
- Platform
- Post format (image, carousel, video, text)
- Caption (platform-appropriate length)
- Hashtags (10-15 for Instagram, 3-5 for LinkedIn, 5-10 for Facebook)
- Best posting day/time (based on healthcare audience engagement research)

---

**OUTPUT 2: MONTHLY PATIENT NEWSLETTER TEMPLATE**

Length: 600-800 words
Tone: warm, educational, community-focused — not a sales piece
HIPAA: no patient clinical details; any patient references must be general ("a patient recently shared...") with no identifying information

Sections:
1. **Header/greeting:** "[Month] Wellness Note from [Practice Name]" — personalized open line for [SEASONAL ANGLE]
2. **Health tip of the month:** 200-250 words, evidence-informed (cite "research suggests" not "studies prove"), actionable, relevant to season
3. **Practice news:** 100 words max — new provider, new service, new hours, event, or equipment
4. **Community spotlight:** 75 words — local business, event, or cause the practice supports (referral relationship building)
5. **Patient wellness challenge:** 75 words — simple 30-day challenge tied to health tip (stretch, posture check, hydration goal)
6. **Scheduling reminder + CTA:** 50 words — gentle reminder to book maintenance visit or refer a friend (no pressure language)
7. **Footer:** unsubscribe link, physical address, practice contact, "This newsletter is for educational purposes and does not constitute medical advice."

---

**OUTPUT 3: GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE POSTS (4 posts)**

Each post: 300 chars max, keyword-rich, specific CTA, FTC-compliant

Post 1 — New patient offer: "[Offer] for [City] residents — [specific benefit framing, no guarantee]. Book at [GBP link]"
Post 2 — Seasonal/topical: tied to [SEASONAL ANGLE] — what to watch for, how we can support (educational + CTA)
Post 3 — Service highlight: feature a specific service with educational framing and CTA
Post 4 — Community event or milestone: practice anniversary, local event, health awareness month tie-in

---

**OUTPUT 4: SEO BLOG ARTICLE WITH FAQ SCHEMA**

Topic: Choose based on specialty + seasonal angle provided
Examples:
- "5 Signs You Should See a Chiropractor After a Car Accident in [City]"
- "Pickleball Injuries: What [City] Players Need to Know"
- "The Best Stretches for Office Workers in [City] (PT-Approved)"
- "Should You See a Chiropractor or Physical Therapist? A [City] Practice's Honest Answer"

Structure:
- H1: [Topic title with city keyword]
- Meta description (155 chars, keyword + CTA)
- Intro (100-150 words): hook + why this matters locally
- H2 × 3-4: Body sections (200-250 words each, keyword-aware, scannable)
- FAQ section: 5 questions and answers (100 words each)
- Conclusion + CTA (100 words): schedule + new patient offer
- FAQ schema JSON-LD markup for all 5 questions

Total length: 900-1,100 words

---

**OUTPUT 5: REFERRAL PROGRAM MATERIALS**

Internal patient-facing announcement:
- Email subject: "Know someone who could use our help?" (HIPAA-safe)
- Body: 100-150 words — friendly ask, what's involved, how to refer, what friend receives
- Text version (160 chars): "Hi [First Name]! Know anyone who could benefit from care? Send them our way — [link]. Reply STOP to opt out."

Compliance note: No cash-for-referral incentives for patients — ACA/APTA ethics prohibit this in most states. Recognition only (thank-you card, $5 donation to charity in their name). If offering a discount to the referring patient, confirm state board rules first.

External physician/provider referral program:
- One-pager for primary care physicians, orthopedic surgeons, OB/GYNs, athletic trainers
- 3 sections: what we offer (services + specialties), how to refer (fax, phone, patient self-schedule), what referring providers receive (co-management notes, outcomes summaries, direct line)
- Format: printable letter + digital version for email
- Length: 250-350 words
- Compliance: no financial kickback or inducement language; relationship-based only (Stark Law / AKS)

---

FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
- Calendar output: table format (Day | Platform | Format | Caption | Hashtags | Best Time)
- Blog article: ready to copy into WordPress or website CMS
- Flag any compliance risk with [COMPLIANCE FLAG: ...]
- Include compliance checklist at end of output
```

---

## Usage Notes

- 30-day calendar: export to Google Sheets or Notion for scheduling
- LinkedIn articles target physician, attorney, and HR referral sources — not patients
- TikTok: Nevada Board of Chiropractic Examiners and NPTA (Nevada Physical Therapy Association) do not prohibit social media — but all health claims must be educational, not diagnostic
- Blog article: target local + specialty keyword combinations for highest SEO value (e.g., "car accident chiropractor Henderson NV" — estimated 50-100 monthly searches, very low competition)
- Referral program: Stark Law and Anti-Kickback Statute apply to Medicare/Medicaid patients — never offer financial incentives to physicians for referrals
