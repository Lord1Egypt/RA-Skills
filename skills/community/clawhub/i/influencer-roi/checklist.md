# Influencer ROI Analysis — Quality Checklist

Use before finalizing and sharing any influencer ROI report. Each category must be fully checked before the report is considered complete.

---

## 1. Data Collection (8 items)
- [ ] Attribution data pulled from ecommerce backend (Shopify/WooCommerce), not platform self-reported numbers
- [ ] UTM-based orders and discount-code orders both captured and deduplicated by order ID
- [ ] Campaign dates confirmed for each creator (start and end of active posting period)
- [ ] Product seeding quantities and COGS verified against purchase orders or inventory records
- [ ] Shipping costs for samples confirmed (actual invoices, not estimates)
- [ ] All creator fee invoices collected and matched to contract amounts
- [ ] Content licensing agreements reviewed and usage rights fees captured
- [ ] Agency/management fees included if applicable

## 2. Attribution Accuracy (6 items)
- [ ] Discount code leakage checked on RetailMeNot, Honey, CouponBirds, Dealspotr, and Groupon
- [ ] Leakage factor applied where coupon site presence confirmed
- [ ] UTM parameters verified as correctly formatted and tracking in GA4
- [ ] Platform-reported conversion numbers NOT used as primary attribution source
- [ ] Attribution window documented (e.g., 30-day click for UTM, campaign duration for discount codes)
- [ ] Assisted conversions pulled from GA4 path exploration and credited appropriately

## 3. Cost Completeness (7 items)
- [ ] Product seeding cost uses COGS, not retail price
- [ ] Gift packaging and presentation costs included
- [ ] International shipping and customs duties included where applicable
- [ ] Commission calculated on actual attributed orders (not estimated)
- [ ] Content licensing fees captured (including whitelisting if content was used in paid ads)
- [ ] Platform subscription or booking fees included
- [ ] Internal team time included (optional but recommended for true ROI)

## 4. Calculations (6 items)
- [ ] Net Revenue = Attributed Revenue × (1 − Leakage Factor) — formula applied
- [ ] Gross Profit uses the correct margin for the specific products promoted (not blended store margin)
- [ ] Net Profit = Gross Profit − TCC — calculated correctly
- [ ] ROAS = Net Revenue ÷ TCC — not revenue ROAS, not blended ROAS
- [ ] CPA = TCC ÷ Attributed Orders — calculated per creator
- [ ] 30-day normalization applied to all creators for apples-to-apples comparison

## 5. Creator Comparison (5 items)
- [ ] All creators normalized to the same 30-day window
- [ ] ROAS compared against a consistent target (category benchmark, not arbitrary number)
- [ ] Renewal decisions documented with clear rationale
- [ ] Creators with "Test Again" status have specific hypotheses for what to test
- [ ] Seasonal context noted if campaign ran during a peak or trough period

## 6. Report Quality (6 items)
- [ ] Creator performance table is complete with no blank cells
- [ ] Budget allocation recommendation for next cycle is included
- [ ] Key insights section highlights at least 3 actionable findings
- [ ] Leakage flags are called out prominently for any affected creator
- [ ] Hidden cost findings are surfaced where costs exceeded budget
- [ ] Report reviewed by someone who wasn't involved in the campaign setup

## 7. Decisions & Follow-Through (4 items)
- [ ] Renewal/pause decisions communicated to the creator management team
- [ ] Budget reallocation approved by relevant decision-maker
- [ ] Next cycle briefing updated with learnings from this campaign
- [ ] Creator contracts updated if licensing or fee terms are changing

---

**Total items: 42**
**Minimum to ship report: All of sections 1–4 complete, plus section 6 complete**
