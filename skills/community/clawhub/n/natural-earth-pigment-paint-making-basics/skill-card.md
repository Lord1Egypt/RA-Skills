## Description: <br>
Guides users through small-scale natural earth pigment paint making by helping identify candidate pigment sources, calculate binder and extender ratios, schedule preparation steps, track tests, and produce inventory or barter templates while humans perform all physical work. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and makers use this skill to plan safe, repeatable natural paint experiments from local earth, clay, charcoal, and plant materials. It supports source screening, ratio calculation, multi-day preparation schedules, testing logs, inventory records, and barter copy for non-professional craft use. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may share precise location data or collect pigment from contaminated or unknown soil sources. <br>
Mitigation: Use coarse location information when possible, avoid suspicious or regulated collection sites, and test only small batches from sources that can be screened for safety. <br>
Risk: Grinding dry earth or mixing materials can create dust or irritation hazards. <br>
Mitigation: Use dust protection, work outdoors or with strong ventilation, pause if irritation occurs, and keep physical handling under human judgment. <br>
Risk: Natural paint guidance may be unsuitable for regulated, structural, waterproof, children's, food-contact, or historic-preservation coatings. <br>
Mitigation: Limit use to small-scale, non-professional experiments and consult qualified professionals or applicable regulations for specialized coating requirements. <br>
