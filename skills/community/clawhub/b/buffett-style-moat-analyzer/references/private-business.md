# Private Business And Startup Analysis

Use this reference for startups, websites, acquisition targets, small businesses, and business ideas.

## Private Business Scorecard

Score:

- demand durability
- repeat purchase behavior
- customer concentration
- owner dependency
- margin quality
- operational simplicity
- local or niche dominance
- transferability
- hidden capex risk
- growth without breaking the business

## Acquisition Target Questions

Ask:

- Why is the seller really selling?
- How much revenue depends on the owner?
- What happens in the first 90 days after transfer?
- Are customers under contract or just loyal to the seller?
- Are the books clean enough to trust?
- What must be reinvested immediately after purchase?
- Can prices be raised?
- Is there an obvious operational improvement?

## Startup Idea Questions

Ask:

- Is this a painkiller or vitamin?
- Who has urgent budget for it?
- Why now?
- What distribution advantage exists?
- What gets stronger with scale?
- What stops a larger player copying it?
- Does the business improve with more users, data, workflow, or brand trust?
- Can it reach revenue before heavy investment?

## Website Or Digital Product Questions

Ask:

- Is the audience obvious?
- Is the offer clear in 5 seconds?
- Is there a reason to trust it?
- Is there a reason to act now?
- Is the product differentiated or just better-looking?
- Is acquisition organic, paid, partner-led, marketplace-led, or founder-led?
- Can traffic compound?

## Final Verdict For Ideas

Use:

- **Build after pre-sale**: strong pain and reachable buyers, but proof is needed.
- **Test with outreach**: clear buyer, uncertain urgency.
- **Build a free tool for search demand**: search demand exists and product can capture leads.
- **Kill or rethink**: weak buyer, weak urgency, weak distribution.
- **Too hard pile**: economics or market are too unclear.
