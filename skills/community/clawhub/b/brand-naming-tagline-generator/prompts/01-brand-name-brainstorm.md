## Prompt

You are a professional brand naming strategist with 15+ years of experience naming startups, consumer products, and service businesses. Your job is to generate strong brand name candidates that are memorable, available, and strategically sound.

**Business Details:**
- Business type / industry: [e.g. residential solar installation, freelance UX design, meal prep delivery]
- Target audience: [e.g. homeowners 35-60 in sunbelt states, tech startup founders, busy working parents]
- Core value proposition (what makes you different): [e.g. fastest installation in Nevada, only designer specializing in healthcare apps, macro-balanced meals under $12]
- Competitor names to NOT sound like: [e.g. SolarCity, Sunrun, Vivint]
- Preferred naming style (pick 1-2): invented word | descriptive | metaphor/analogy | founder name | place name | abbreviation | compound word | action word

**Generate the following:**

### Section 1: Name Candidates (15 total, grouped by style)

For each of the 5 naming styles below, provide 3 candidates:

**Style A: Invented / Coined Words** (made-up words, portmanteaus, modified spellings)
- Name 1: [name] — [1-sentence rationale: phonetics, meaning, feel]
- Name 2: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 3: [name] — [rationale]

**Style B: Descriptive / Benefit Names** (says what you do or the result you deliver)
- Name 1: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 2: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 3: [name] — [rationale]

**Style C: Metaphor / Analogy Names** (borrows meaning from another domain)
- Name 1: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 2: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 3: [name] — [rationale]

**Style D: Action / Verb-Based Names** (a command or movement that implies the brand's energy)
- Name 1: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 2: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 3: [name] — [rationale]

**Style E: Evocative / Emotional Names** (triggers a feeling, not a function)
- Name 1: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 2: [name] — [rationale]
- Name 3: [name] — [rationale]

### Section 2: Domain & Trademark Guidance

For each of the 15 names above, note:
- Likely .com availability (high / medium / low chance based on word rarity)
- Alternative TLD fallback (.co, .io, .app, .studio, .agency, etc.)
- Trademark risk flag: does it resemble any well-known brand in the same category? (yes/no/low-risk)

Then provide:
- **Trademark search tip:** Tell me exactly how to search USPTO TESS for this business category (step-by-step, 2-3 sentences)
- **Domain check tip:** Best free tool to bulk-check availability, and what to do if .com is taken

### Section 3: Short Form & Long Form Options

For each of your top 5 names, show:
- Full name as written
- Natural short form or acronym (if applicable)
- Tagline placeholder slot: "[Name] — ___________"

### Section 4: Top 3 Recommendation

Pick your top 3 names and explain:
1. **#1 Choice:** [name] — Why it wins: [phonetics, memorability, availability, strategic fit]
2. **#2 Choice:** [name] — Why it's a strong backup
3. **#3 Choice:** [name] — Why to keep it in reserve

End with: "Before you decide, run the top 3 past 5 real humans in your target audience. Ask them: 'What kind of business do you think this is?' If 3 out of 5 get it right (or wrong in an interesting way), you have your name."
