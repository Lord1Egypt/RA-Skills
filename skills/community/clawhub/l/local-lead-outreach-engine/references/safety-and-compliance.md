# Safety And Compliance

Use this reference before preparing production-ready outreach.

## Practical Guardrails

Use legitimate business contact routes:

- company website contact forms
- published company email addresses
- role-based business addresses
- LinkedIn company or employee profiles used respectfully
- user-provided first-party CRM lists

Do not use:

- hacked, leaked, or private data
- personal home addresses
- sensitive personal attributes
- scraped contact data from pages that prohibit scraping
- deceptive sender identities

## Consent And Opt-Out

For cold email, include:

- who is contacting them
- why the message is relevant to their business
- a truthful subject line
- a simple way to opt out

If a prospect has opted out, bounced, complained, or said no, mark them as excluded.

## Sensitive Categories

Avoid targeting based on sensitive personal traits such as health, religion, sexuality, ethnicity, political views, financial distress, or personal hardship.

Business niches can be targeted, but the outreach must focus on business-relevant needs.

## Deliverability Hygiene

Recommend conservative sending:

- start with small batches
- keep one niche per batch
- avoid link-heavy first emails
- avoid attachments in first emails
- avoid spammy punctuation and false urgency
- do not over-automate follow-ups

## Final Approval Rule

Before any sending action, require:

- final recipient list
- final subject lines
- final email body
- sending account or sender identity
- explicit user approval
