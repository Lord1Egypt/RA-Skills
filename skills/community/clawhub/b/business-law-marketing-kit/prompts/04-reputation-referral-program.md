SECTION A: REVIEW ACQUISITION PROGRAM

Review Request Sequence (3 touchpoints — compliant with RPC 7.1(c), FTC 2023, and Nevada SCR 192):

COMPLIANCE GATE — ALL REVIEWS: 
- Do NOT request reviews describing specific legal outcomes or results ("They saved me $200K" = FTC 2023 endorsement requiring atypical-result disclaimer)
- Do NOT offer incentives for reviews (creates FTC material disclosure obligation and potential bar complaint)
- Do NOT selectively delete negative reviews (FTC 2023 enforcement priority)
- Reviews should focus on process, communication, responsiveness, and client experience
- RPC 1.6: Do not request clients to reveal the subject matter of their legal representation in a public review

Touchpoint 1 — Immediate post-close email (sent within 24 hours of matter closing):
Subject: Thank you from [Firm Name] — [Attorney Name]
Body: [150 words — genuine thank-you, brief summary of what was accomplished (in general terms — not revealing confidential matter details), mention that client's feedback helps Nevada businesses find qualified legal counsel, no request for review yet. No pressure. Attorney name and NV Bar No. in signature. Include: "This is an advertisement." CAN-SPAM opt-out.]

Touchpoint 2 — Review request (sent 14 days after matter closes):
Subject: One quick ask — [Firm Name]
Body: [175 words — brief review request. Ask client to share their experience with the PROCESS: communication, responsiveness, clarity of explanations, ease of working with the firm. Provide direct link to Google Business Profile review. Note that a review about their experience helps other Nevada business owners find qualified legal counsel. DO NOT ask about outcomes or results of their matter. DO NOT offer any incentive. Include: "This is an advertisement." CAN-SPAM opt-out. Attorney name and NV Bar No. in signature.]

Touchpoint 3 — LinkedIn connection request (sent 30 days after matter closes, if business client):
Subject: [LinkedIn InMail — if not connected] Connecting on LinkedIn — [Attorney Name]

NO review request in this touchpoint. NO promotional CTA. This is a professional network connection. If connected on LinkedIn: not an advertisement. If sent as InMail promotional message: must comply with LinkedIn ad rules and include disclosure if it is attorney advertising.]

COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — REVIEWS:
[ ] No outcome-based review requests
[ ] No incentives for reviews

[ ] FTC 2023 — atypical-result disclaimer on any displayed result testimonial

SECTION B: CPA & ACCOUNTANT REFERRAL PROGRAM

Context: CPAs and accountants are the #1 referral source for business law attorneys. Every Nevada business with an accountant has an accountant who regularly encounters clients who need legal counsel (LLC formation, contract issues, employment questions, tax disputes). This is a structured CPA outreach program.

COMPLIANCE GATE — REFERRAL FEES: Nevada RPC 7.2(b) governs referral fees between attorneys and non-attorneys. Referral fees to non-attorneys are generally NOT permitted. Reciprocal referrals (I refer to you, you refer to me) are permitted as long as no money changes hands. "Thank you" gifts must be modest (industry standard: $25–50 gift card acceptable; large gifts create quid pro quo appearance). Do NOT offer CPAs a financial incentive per referral — this violates RPC 7.2.

Generate the following CPA referral program materials:

1. Initial CPA Outreach Letter (snail mail or email):
Subject: Professional introduction — [Attorney Name], [Firm Name], Nevada business law
Body: [225 words — professional introduction from attorney to CPA. Note: mutual clients benefit when their advisors coordinate; attorney focuses on Nevada business formation, contracts, and disputes; attorney can refer clients who need CPA services (reciprocal, no fee involved); offer to meet for coffee or a brief call. List 3 specific situations where the CPA's clients might need business legal counsel: (1) client forming a new entity and needs operating agreement, (2) client in a contract dispute with a vendor, (3) client wants to sell or acquire a business. NO mention of referral fees or compensation. Professional, peer-level tone. Attorney name and NV Bar No.]

2. CPA Referral One-Pager (PDF leave-behind):
Content: [Generate a 1-page professional summary of when CPA clients may need a business attorney — 5 specific scenarios with brief descriptions. Bullet format. [Firm Name] contact info, NV Bar No., and website. Footer: "This material is for professional reference only. Attorney advertising under Nevada SCR 192. Results depend on individual circumstances."]

3. CPA Follow-Up Email (sent 3 weeks after initial outreach):
Subject: Following up — Nevada business law resources for your clients
Body: [150 words — soft follow-up. Offer to provide a brief educational presentation at the CPA's office on Nevada business law basics for their client base (entity selection, contract basics, common business disputes). No pitch for referrals — offer value first. Include updated contact information. CAN-SPAM opt-out. "This is an advertisement."]

4. Quarterly CPA Update Email (ongoing, once per quarter):
Subject: Nevada business law update — Q[X] [Year] — [Firm Name]
Body: [175 words — one relevant Nevada business law update (e.g., Nevada Modified Business Tax rate change, NRS 613.195 non-compete reform reminder, annual list filing deadline reminder). Educational, no sales pitch. Position attorney as a resource. Include: "This is an advertisement." CAN-SPAM opt-out. NV Bar No. in signature.]
