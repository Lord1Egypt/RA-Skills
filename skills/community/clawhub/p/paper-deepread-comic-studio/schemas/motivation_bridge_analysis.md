# Motivation-Bridge Analysis

Do not only describe the final algorithm. Also identify how the paper tries to make the jump from motivation to method scientifically believable.

Look for:

- small experiments
- probes
- pilot designs
- explanatory ablations
- toy demonstrations
- visualizations that justify the mechanism

For each bridge item, record:

- what claim it is trying to support
- what doubt it tries to preempt
- what was observed
- whether it strongly, weakly, or only suggestively closes the gap between motivation and algorithm

## Research-Generative Bridge Add-on

Also ask how the paper made its main surrogate mechanism feel inevitable:

- What ideal mechanism would have solved the problem in an easier setting?
- Why is that ideal mechanism unavailable here?
- What proxy signal remains available?
- How does the proposed module transform that proxy into a surrogate mechanism?
- Which hidden assumption must hold for the surrogate to work?
- If that hidden assumption fails, what new research question appears?

Record this as:

| Module | Failure fixed | Ideal unavailable mechanism | Available proxy | Surrogate design | Hidden assumption | New idea under violated assumption |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
