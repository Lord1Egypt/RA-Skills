# Organ Congestion Pathology Analysis

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- A patient has clinical signs of heart failure (e.g., dyspnea, hepatomegaly)
- Imaging or histopathology reveals organ congestion
- You need to differentiate between pulmonary congestion (left heart failure) and hepatic congestion (right heart failure)
- You must assess disease stage and anticipate complications such as pulmonary fibrosis or congestive hepatopathy

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Identify the Affected Organ
- **Lung**: Indicates left heart failure
- **Liver**: Indicates right heart failure

### Step 2: Assess Histopathological Features

#### For Pulmonary Congestion:
- **Acute**: Lung enlarged, dark red; alveolar capillaries dilated, alveolar walls thickened, alveolar spaces filled with edema fluid and red blood cells
- **Chronic**: Capillary dilation worsens, interalveolar septa fibrotic; presence of "heart failure cells" (hemosiderin-laden macrophages)
- **End-stage**: Brown induration—firm, brown lungs due to fibrosis and hemosiderin deposition

#### For Hepatic Congestion:
- **Acute**: Liver enlarged, dark red; central veins and sinusoids dilated with RBCs; centrilobular hepatocyte atrophy/fatty change
- **Chronic**: "Nutmeg liver" appearance—red (congested center) and yellow (fatty periphery) on cut surface; centrilobular necrosis, peripheral steatosis
- **Long-term severe**: Centrilobular collapse, fibrosis, and "congestive cirrhosis" with impaired liver function

### Step 3: Correlate with Clinical Manifestations
- **Pulmonary**: Dyspnea, cyanosis; acute pulmonary edema → pink frothy sputum, respiratory distress, near-death sensation
- **Hepatic**: Right upper quadrant pain, hepatomegaly, jaundice in advanced stages

### Step 4: Determine Stage and Prognostic Implications
- Early-stage: Reversible with heart failure management
- Chronic/end-stage: Irreversible structural changes; high risk of organ dysfunction

> **Note**: Do not apply this skill to non-cardiogenic congestion (e.g., inflammatory, toxic, or obstructive causes).
