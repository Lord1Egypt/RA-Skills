# QDRO Drafter

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** U.S. Family Law — Retirement-Plan Division (QDRO / COAP / MPDO / Governmental DRO)

## Purpose

A drafting partner for matrimonial attorneys, QDRO specialists, and divorce financial professionals dividing a retirement benefit between a **Participant** and an **Alternate Payee** under U.S. federal and state law. Produces a **plan-typed**, **plan-specific** draft retirement-division order — a **QDRO** (ERISA-governed defined-contribution and defined-benefit plans under **ERISA § 206(d)(3) / I.R.C. § 414(p)**), a **COAP** (federal civil-service **CSRS / FERS** under **5 CFR Part 838**), an **MPDO** (uniformed-services retired pay under the **Uniformed Services Former Spouses' Protection Act**, **10 U.S.C. § 1408**), or a state-specific **governmental-plan DRO** (state, county, municipal, judicial, teacher retirement systems) — with the **pre-approval cover letter** to the plan administrator that best practice and DOL/IRS/PBGC guidance both recommend.

## When to Use

- Drafting a QDRO for a 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), profit-sharing, ESOP, money-purchase, or pension defined-benefit plan
- Drafting a COAP for a federal civilian retiree (CSRS / FERS / TSP — note TSP uses a Retirement Benefits Court Order, not a QDRO)
- Drafting an MPDO for a uniformed-services member or retiree under USFSPA (active, reserve, or retired pay; survivor benefit plan election)
- Drafting a state, county, municipal, judicial, or teacher-retirement-system DRO
- Rewriting a domestic-relations order **rejected** by the plan administrator (qualification deficiency letter)
- Preparing the **pre-approval submission** to the plan administrator before court entry to avoid a post-entry rejection
- Auditing a settlement agreement or decree's retirement-division provisions for QDRO-implementability before signing

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Plan typing and posture**
1. Captures plan type (ERISA DC / ERISA DB / CSRS / FERS / TSP / Uniformed Services / Governmental / IRA — note IRAs do not require a QDRO and divide by transfer incident to divorce under I.R.C. § 408(d)(6))
2. Confirms governing law — ERISA + Internal Revenue Code (private plans), 5 CFR Part 838 (federal civil service), 10 U.S.C. § 1408 (uniformed services), state statutes (governmental plans)
3. Captures plan name, plan sponsor, plan administrator contact, plan ID, and whether the administrator publishes a model order; if yes, the model is the **starting point**
4. Captures the divorce-case caption, decree date, controlling settlement / decree provisions on retirement division
5. Captures whether the order is being drafted **before** decree entry (preferred), **simultaneous** with decree entry, or **after** decree entry (cure posture)

**Phase 2: Award structure**
6. Determines award structure — **separate-interest** (alternate payee receives an independent benefit; available primarily in DB plans for a non-retired participant) or **shared-payment** (alternate payee receives a portion of each benefit payment as paid to the participant; required if participant is already in pay status or for certain plan types)
7. Captures award amount — **fixed dollar**, **fixed percentage**, or **coverture / marital-fraction formula** (e.g., (months of marriage during plan participation) / (total months of plan participation at benefit-commencement date))
8. Confirms valuation date(s) — for DC plans, the date the account balance is determined and whether gains/losses attach from valuation date to segregation/distribution date
9. Captures whether the alternate payee receives a proportional share of **outstanding loans** (typically excluded from segregable balance unless explicitly included)

**Phase 3: Survivor and ancillary benefits**
10. **Survivor benefits** — for DB plans, captures whether the alternate payee is treated as the **surviving spouse** for **Qualified Pre-retirement Survivor Annuity (QPSA)** and **Qualified Joint and Survivor Annuity (QJSA)** purposes (in full or in part) under ERISA / IRC; for separate-interest awards, captures whether the alternate payee's benefit is converted to her/his own life expectancy
11. **Early-retirement subsidies** — captures whether the alternate payee shares in subsidies the participant later earns
12. **Cost-of-living adjustments (COLA)** — captures whether the alternate payee receives proportional COLA
13. **Disability benefits, lump-sum death benefits, supplemental benefits** — captures whether included or excluded
14. **Form of payment** for separate-interest DB awards (single life, joint and survivor, period certain) and commencement date election rules

**Phase 4: Federal-plan specifics (when applicable)**
15. **COAP (CSRS / FERS)** — drafts under 5 CFR Part 838 using OPM-acceptable language; specifies "gross annuity" vs "net annuity" vs "self-only annuity"; addresses former-spouse survivor annuity (FSSA) election and refund of contributions
16. **TSP** — drafts a Retirement Benefits Court Order (RBCO) under TSP rules (TSP-3 / TSP-44); fixed dollar or percentage of vested balance as of a specified date, no coverture formula by post-segregation; gains/losses attach from valuation to processing
17. **Uniformed Services MPDO** — applies the **10/10 rule** (10 years of marriage overlapping 10 years of creditable service) for direct DFAS payment; captures Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) "former spouse" election and the **one-year deemed-election** deadline; expresses award as fixed-dollar, fixed-percentage of disposable retired pay (under USFSPA), or a coverture formula keyed to creditable service

**Phase 5: Drafting**
18. Drafts the order with plan-specific captions, recitals, definitions, award provision, survivor-benefit election, gains/losses provision, tax provision (alternate payee taxed on receipt under I.R.C. § 402(e)(1)(A); spouse / former-spouse rollover under § 402(c)(9) preserved), administrative-fee allocation, plan-administrator-rejection cure provision, and reservation-of-jurisdiction clause
19. Drafts a **pre-approval cover letter** to the plan administrator requesting pre-entry qualification review, with the parties' identifying information disclosure (using last-4 only of SSN in the working draft; full SSNs go in the sealed addendum permitted by many plans)
20. Drafts a draft notice / consent block for the alternate payee on tax election (lump-sum vs rollover) and survivor election

**Phase 6: Pre-rejection audit**
21. Runs a pre-rejection audit covering boilerplate-failure red flags — generic plan caption, missing plan-administrator address, missing valuation date for DC plans, missing benefit-commencement date assumption for DB coverture, missing survivor-benefit election, missing gains/losses provision, missing alternate-payee identification (name, address, DOB, last-4 SSN), missing tax / rollover election language, missing administrative-fee allocation, conflict with decree, and over-reach beyond plan terms (a QDRO cannot require a benefit the plan does not provide)

## Output

A DRAFT order packet with:

- Plan-typed order (QDRO / COAP / MPDO / Governmental DRO / TSP RBCO)
- Pre-approval cover letter to the plan administrator
- Award-structure summary (separate-interest vs shared-payment, fixed-dollar / fixed-percentage / coverture)
- Survivor-benefit election summary
- Gains/losses, COLA, early-retirement-subsidy, and disability-benefit election summary
- Tax / rollover language and alternate-payee notice block
- Federal-plan-specific recitals (USFSPA 10/10 finding, OPM gross/net annuity election, TSP valuation date)
- Pre-rejection audit (boilerplate-failure red flags)
- Matrimonial-attorney review and sign-off block
- Unresolved-information list

## Safety

This skill drafts a court order, **not** a filing or a benefit-administration instruction. Every output is labeled **DRAFT — MATRIMONIAL ATTORNEY REVIEW REQUIRED BEFORE PLAN-ADMINISTRATOR SUBMISSION OR COURT ENTRY**. The skill never files an order, never logs into a plan-administrator portal, OPM, DFAS, TSP, or court system, and never communicates with the participant, alternate payee, plan administrator, or court directly. PII is minimized in the working draft (last-4 of SSN; full SSNs go in the sealed identifying-information addendum or separate cover letter as the plan and court rule require). The skill flags **timing risk** if the participant is approaching retirement, has taken a loan, has become disabled, or has died — and surfaces the standing risk that an alternate payee's rights can be permanently extinguished if a qualified order is not entered in time. The skill never gives the user tax advice as to her or his individual situation; the alternate-payee tax / rollover language is plan-side mechanics, not personal tax advice.
