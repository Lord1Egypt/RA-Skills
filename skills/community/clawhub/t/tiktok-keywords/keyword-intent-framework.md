# Keyword Intent Framework for TikTok Shop

This framework classifies keywords by buyer intent level specific to TikTok's ecommerce ecosystem. Use it during Step 3 of the keyword research workflow to sort every keyword candidate into the correct intent tier and determine its optimal placement.

---

## The Three Intent Tiers

TikTok Shop keywords fall into three intent tiers that reflect where the buyer is in their decision process. Each tier has distinct signal words, placement priorities, and conversion expectations.

---

### Tier 1: Browse Intent

**Definition:** The user is exploring a category or topic without a defined purchase need. They are consuming content, gathering inspiration, or casually discovering products.

**Conversion likelihood:** Low (1-3% of browse-intent searchers make a purchase within the session)

**Signal words and patterns:**
- "Ideas" -- "desk organization ideas," "gift ideas for mom"
- "Inspo" / "inspiration" -- "bedroom inspo," "outfit inspo for fall"
- "Aesthetic" (standalone modifier) -- "aesthetic room decor," "aesthetic skincare"
- "Routine" -- "morning skincare routine," "cleaning routine"
- "Haul" -- "TikTok Shop haul," "skincare haul"
- "Collection" -- "my lipstick collection," "sneaker collection"
- "Trend" / "trending" -- "trending products," "what's trending on TikTok Shop"
- "TikTok made me buy it" -- engagement-driven, not purchase-driven search
- Question formats -- "what is hyaluronic acid," "how does retinol work"

**Examples with analysis:**

| Keyword | Why it is Browse | Conversion Note |
|---|---|---|
| skincare routine for beginners | User exploring routines, not seeking a specific product | May convert over multiple sessions as they build a routine |
| aesthetic desk setup ideas | Seeking visual inspiration, not a specific desk product | Useful for content discovery but not direct sales |
| TikTok Shop haul under 50 | Entertainment-seeking behavior, watching others shop | Low direct conversion but builds product awareness |
| trending kitchen gadgets 2025 | Browsing what is popular, no defined purchase need | May bookmark products for later research |

**Placement strategy for Browse keywords:**
- Do NOT place in product titles or descriptions (wastes high-value real estate)
- Use in video captions and hashtags to attract top-of-funnel audiences
- Include in affiliate briefs as content angle suggestions for creators
- These keywords drive views and awareness, not direct listing conversions

---

### Tier 2: Research Intent

**Definition:** The user has identified a need or desire and is actively comparing options. They know what category of product they want but have not decided on a specific item.

**Conversion likelihood:** Medium (5-12% of research-intent searchers make a purchase within the session)

**Signal words and patterns:**
- "Best" -- "best vitamin c serum," "best desk lamp for studying"
- "Top" / "top rated" -- "top rated face moisturizer," "top kitchen organizers"
- "vs" / "versus" -- "niacinamide vs vitamin c," "bamboo vs plastic organizer"
- "Review" / "reviews" -- "CeraVe moisturizer review," "LED desk lamp reviews"
- "For [specific need]" -- "serum for oily skin," "organizer for small desks"
- "For [specific audience]" -- "moisturizer for men," "backpack for college students"
- Material/feature modifiers -- "bamboo desk organizer," "wireless charging desk lamp"
- Comparison structures -- "affordable alternative to [brand]," "dupe for [product]"

**Examples with analysis:**

| Keyword | Why it is Research | Conversion Note |
|---|---|---|
| best vitamin c serum for acne scars | "Best" + specific skin concern = active comparison | High likelihood of converting if product matches need |
| bamboo desk organizer with drawers | Material + feature specification = narrowing options | User knows what they want, evaluating specific attributes |
| affordable dupe for Drunk Elephant | Brand comparison + price sensitivity = option exploration | Strong conversion if product credibly matches the reference |
| moisturizer for dry sensitive skin | Dual need-state modifiers = defined problem seeking solution | Will convert on the product that best addresses both concerns |

**Placement strategy for Research keywords:**
- Place in product descriptions, especially the first 100 characters
- Use in backend search terms to capture comparison shoppers
- Include in video captions when creator content is review-oriented
- These keywords connect need-aware shoppers to your listing

**Sub-classification within Research tier:**

Research keywords can be further split for prioritization:

- **Feature-research** ("with drawers," "wireless," "fragrance-free") -- User knows desired attributes. High conversion when product matches.
- **Comparison-research** ("best," "top rated," "vs") -- User is ranking options. Moderate conversion, depends on competitive positioning.
- **Problem-research** ("for acne scars," "for small spaces") -- User has a defined problem. High conversion when product clearly solves it.

---

### Tier 3: Purchase-Ready Intent

**Definition:** The user has decided to buy and is searching for the right product at the right price. They are looking for where to purchase, not whether to purchase.

**Conversion likelihood:** High (15-30% of purchase-ready searchers complete a purchase within the session)

**Signal words and patterns:**
- Price modifiers -- "under $20," "under $30," "cheap," "affordable" (when paired with specific product)
- "Buy" / "shop" / "order" -- "buy vitamin c serum," "shop desk organizers"
- "TikTok Shop" (explicit platform mention) -- "face serum TikTok Shop," "desk lamp TikTok Shop"
- "Free shipping" -- "skincare free shipping," "desk organizer free shipping"
- "On sale" / "discount" / "deal" -- "skincare deals," "desk organizer on sale"
- Size/variant specifics -- "30ml vitamin c serum," "large desk organizer black"
- "Add to cart" / "link in bio" -- "where to buy [product]," "link for [product]"
- Brand + product specifics -- "[brand name] vitamin c serum 20%"

**Examples with analysis:**

| Keyword | Why it is Purchase-Ready | Conversion Note |
|---|---|---|
| vitamin c serum under 15 dollars | Explicit price ceiling = budget set, ready to buy | Highest conversion among all tiers when product meets price point |
| desk organizer free shipping TikTok Shop | Platform + shipping modifier = purchase logistics query | User is at checkout stage, seeking the listing |
| buy bamboo desk organizer black | "Buy" + specific material + color = defined purchase target | Near-certain conversion if listing matches |
| affordable brightening serum 30ml | Price sensitivity + size specificity = purchase decision made | User knows exactly what they want and what they will pay |

**Placement strategy for Purchase-Ready keywords:**
- Prioritize in product titles (highest indexing weight, direct conversion impact)
- Place in the first 34 visible characters of the title when possible
- Use in video captions for direct product showcase videos
- Include price-point keywords in backend terms if not natural in title/description
- These keywords are the highest-value terms in your entire keyword list

## Intent Scoring Matrix

Use this matrix to assign the Intent dimension score (1-5) during Step 4 of the workflow.

| Score | Intent Level | Defining Characteristic | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | Purchase-ready | Contains explicit purchase modifiers (price, "buy," shipping) | "serum under $20 free shipping" |
| 4 | Strong research | Contains comparison modifiers ("best," "top rated," "vs") | "best brightening serum 2025" |
| 3 | Need-state research | Contains specific need/audience modifiers ("for oily skin") | "desk organizer for college dorm" |
| 2 | Category browse | Generic category term with no intent modifiers | "face serum," "desk organizer" |
| 1 | Informational/entertainment | Question format, trend-watching, or content-consumption pattern | "skincare routine ideas" |

## Handling Ambiguous Keywords

Some keywords straddle two intent tiers. Apply these rules to resolve ambiguity.

**Rule 1: Context of source determines tier.**
If "aesthetic desk organizer" was found in TikTok Shop autocomplete (not general search), classify it as Research rather than Browse. The Shop search context implies purchase consideration.

**Rule 2: When a keyword could be Research or Purchase-ready, check for specificity.**
"Best serum for dark spots" (Research) vs. "best serum for dark spots under $20" (Purchase-ready). The addition of a price modifier elevates the intent tier.

**Rule 3: Creator-native phrases default to Browse unless they contain intent modifiers.**
"TikTok made me buy it skincare" is Browse. "TikTok made me buy it serum under $15" is Purchase-ready. The price modifier overrides the entertainment framing.

**Rule 4: When still uncertain, assign the lower tier.**
It is better to underestimate intent and place a keyword in a lower-priority zone than to overestimate intent and waste title real estate on a low-conversion term.

## Intent Distribution Targets

A well-balanced keyword research report should approximate the following distribution across intent tiers.

| Intent Tier | Target Percentage of Final Keywords | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase-ready | 25-35% | These are your highest-conversion terms; every listing needs them but the pool is smaller |
| Research | 40-50% | The largest pool of keywords; these drive the majority of qualified traffic |
| Browse | 15-25% | Used only for content and hashtag strategy; kept in the report for affiliate briefs |

If your report skews heavily toward Browse keywords (over 40%), the research likely relied too much on general TikTok search and hashtag data rather than TikTok Shop search specifically. Re-harvest from Shop autocomplete.

If your report has fewer than 20% Purchase-ready keywords, expand the research with explicit price-point, shipping, and "buy" modifier variations.

## Intent Tier Interaction with Seasonality

Seasonal events shift the distribution of intent tiers over time.

**8+ weeks before peak:** Browse keywords dominate. Users are in discovery mode ("back to school ideas," "holiday gift inspo"). Capture these for content strategy.

**4-8 weeks before peak:** Research keywords surge. Users are narrowing choices ("best dorm room desk organizer," "top gifts for teenage girls"). Optimize listings for these terms.

**0-4 weeks before peak:** Purchase-ready keywords spike. Users are buying ("desk organizer under $25 free shipping," "gift set for mom on sale"). Ensure title and description are loaded with purchase-intent terms.

**After peak:** All seasonal keyword volume drops rapidly. Transition back to evergreen keyword optimization within one week of the peak passing.

Time your keyword refresh cycle to match these intent-tier shifts for seasonal products.
