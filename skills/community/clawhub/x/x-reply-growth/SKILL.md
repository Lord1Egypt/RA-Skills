# X Reply Growth

Replies are not filler. They are distribution probes into the right audience graph.

## Pick Targets

Prioritize accounts that:

- share your target audience
- post often
- get real replies
- are not so huge that every reply disappears
- sometimes engage with thoughtful replies

Good range: roughly 5k-200k followers, but quality of audience matters more than size.

## Reply Types

### Add Evidence

```text
I saw the same thing with [specific example].
The surprising part was [lesson].
```

### Add A Framework

```text
I’d split this into three cases:
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
Most people overfocus on [case].
```

### Add A Counterexample

```text
Mostly agree. The exception is [scenario].
In that case, [different principle] matters more.
```

### Ask A Useful Question

```text
How would you handle this when [real constraint]?
```

## Rules

- Do not write “great post” unless the rest adds value.
- Do not hijack threads with self-promo.
- Do not summarize the original post back to the author.
- Keep it useful without requiring a profile click.
- Make one clear contribution.

## Daily Routine

- 10 thoughtful replies under target accounts.
- 5 replies to peers.
- 3 follow-up replies under your own posts.
- 1 quote post only if you add a stronger frame.

## Output

Return:

- **Target accounts to look for**
- **Reply angle**
- **3 reply drafts**
- **Profile-click bridge**
- **Follow-up post if it performs**

## Operating Principle

Treat the public X algorithm as a signal map, not a cheat code. Optimize for posts and replies that earn useful positive actions (dwell, replies, reposts, shares, profile clicks, follows) while avoiding negative actions (not interested, mute, block, report, fast skips).
