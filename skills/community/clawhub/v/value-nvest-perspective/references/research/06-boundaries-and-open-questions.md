# 06 Boundaries And Open Questions

## Hard Boundaries

The final skill must not invent:

- exact strike selection rules
- exact expiry selection rules beyond a general long-duration preference
- Greeks-based formulas
- a systematic short-premium framework
- a hedge or roll protocol not directly supported by evidence
- real-time judgments without user-supplied price context

## Supported Claims

The evidence is strong enough to support:

- focus on high-quality growth names
- selective contrarian entries during fear
- `long call` as the dominant options expression
- staged adding and staged trimming
- pairing aggressive exposure with steadier ballast
- patience and ignoring noise as a core discipline

## Open Questions

1. Does he systematically prefer certain maturities beyond “long enough”?
2. How does he choose strikes in practice?
3. Does he ever rely on multi-leg spreads as a default rather than a one-off tool?
4. How does he handle losing option positions when the thesis is delayed rather than invalidated?
5. What share of exposure sits in underlying stock versus options during a normal campaign?

## Fallback Behavior For The Final Skill

When users ask for unsupported detail, the skill should:

1. state the visible pattern
2. state the evidence gap
3. give a bounded, conditional answer instead of a fake precise one

Example fallback:

- “按这个风格，更像会优先看高质量标的的 long call 或正股配 long call，但公开材料不足以证明他固定用哪种行权价和到期日。如果你给我现价、期限和你是纯期权还是带正股仓位，我可以按这个框架给条件式建议。”

## Research Honesty Standard

This skill is only defensible if it keeps the difference clear between:

- what the account directly said
- what the community says he teaches
- what we infer from repeated behavior
