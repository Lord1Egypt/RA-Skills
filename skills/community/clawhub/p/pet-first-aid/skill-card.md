## Description: <br>
Emergency first aid and health assessment for dogs and cats. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External pet owners use this skill to assess urgent dog and cat health situations, follow basic first-aid checklists, and decide when to contact an emergency veterinarian or poison-control service. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may rely on informational first-aid guidance during a veterinary emergency. <br>
Mitigation: Escalate difficulty breathing, poisoning, seizures, collapse, bloat, serious bleeding, burns, heatstroke, eye injuries, suspected fractures, and worsening conditions to an emergency veterinarian or poison-control service immediately. <br>
Risk: Incorrect medication or vomiting advice can harm pets. <br>
Mitigation: Do not administer human medications or induce vomiting unless a veterinarian or poison-control expert confirms it for the specific pet and situation. <br>
