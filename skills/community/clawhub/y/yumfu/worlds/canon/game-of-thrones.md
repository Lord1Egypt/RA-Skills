## 1. One-line premise
In a brutal medieval-fantasy world where "winter" is a years-long apocalypse, noble houses scheme, betray, and slaughter for the **Iron Throne** — while almost everyone ignores the real existential threat gathering in the frozen North.

## 2. World & era
- **Setting:** the continent of **Westeros** (Seven Kingdoms) plus **Essos** across the Narrow Sea.
- **Era anchor:** roughly **298–305 AC** (After Aegon's Conquest) — from the death of Jon Arryn through the War of the Five Kings and beyond.
- **Tone:** low-magic-but-rising; political realism, moral grey, sudden death of major characters. Magic returns as dragons hatch and the dead walk.
- **Two clocks:** the **game of thrones** (human power struggle) vs. **"Winter is Coming"** (the Others / White Walkers + the Long Night).

## 4. Main plot scaffolding (stages for anchoring)

**Stage 1 · The spark**
- Hand of the King Jon Arryn dies; King Robert Baratheon asks Ned Stark to be the new Hand.
- Ned uncovers that the royal children are not Robert's but products of Lannister incest (Cersei & Jaime).

**Stage 2 · War of the Five Kings**
- Ned is executed; the realm fractures. Robb Stark, Stannis & Renly Baratheon, Joffrey, and Balon Greyjoy all press claims.
- Key catastrophes: Blackwater (Tyrion defends King's Landing), and the **Red Wedding** (Robb & Catelyn betrayed by Frey/Bolton/Lannister).

**Stage 3 · Daenerys rises in the East**
- Exiled Targaryen heir **Daenerys** hatches three dragons, frees slave cities, builds an army to reclaim Westeros.

**Stage 4 · The threat in the North**
- Beyond the Wall, the **White Walkers / Others** mass; **Jon Snow** of the Night's Watch becomes the key figure warning a realm too busy fighting itself.

**Stage 5 · Convergence & the Long Night**
- The living must unite against the dead; the throne struggle and the supernatural war finally collide. (Show and books diverge in the endgame — leave room to branch.)

## 6. Magic / setting anchors
- **Dragons** — extinct for ~150 years, reborn with Daenerys; living siege weapons + symbols of Targaryen legitimacy.
- **The Wall & the Others** — 700-foot ice wall; the undead wights and White Walkers are the true winter.
- **Wargs / greenseers** — skin-changing into animals; prophetic visions via weirwood trees.
- **Faceless Men** — the assassin cult of Braavos (Arya's path).
- **R'hllor (the Lord of Light)** — fire-priest magic, resurrection, prophecy (Melisandre).
- **Valyrian steel & dragonglass** — among the few things that kill White Walkers.

## 7. Theme & "anchor vs. freedom"
- **Core themes:** power vs. honor; the cost of integrity; ignoring real danger for petty rivalry; "the things we do for love."
- **Canon anchors (stay close):** house rivalries, the War of the Five Kings beats (Hand's death → Red Wedding), Daenerys' eastern rise, the looming Long Night, dragons & White Walkers.
- **Free to diverge:** the player need not be Jon or Dany; they can back any house, switch sides, survive or seize the throne. The endgame is open — both books and show leave room.

## 8. Notes for YumFu
- Prefer **real Westeros geography / houses / characters** (Winterfell, King's Landing, Dragonstone, the Wall, Sunspear; Lannister/Stark/Targaryen) when generating moves, fronts, NPC pressure, and travel.
