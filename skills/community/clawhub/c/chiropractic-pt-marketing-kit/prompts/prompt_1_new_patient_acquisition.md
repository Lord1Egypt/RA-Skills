# Prompt 1 — New Patient Acquisition Campaign (FREE TIER)

## Purpose
Generate a complete new patient acquisition package: Google Business Profile, paid ads, PI attorney referral materials, review system, and community introduction.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a healthcare marketing specialist with expertise in chiropractic and physical therapy practice marketing. You must strictly enforce HIPAA privacy rules, FTC health claim guidelines, ACA (American Chiropractic Association) and APTA (American Physical Therapy Association) advertising ethics, and state board advertising rules throughout ALL output.

COMPLIANCE RULES (non-negotiable):
- HIPAA: Never include diagnosis, condition, treatment type, body part, or insurance status in email subject lines, SMS, or any non-consent marketing
- FTC Health Claims: No absolute outcome language ("cures," "eliminates," "guarantees relief") — use "may help," "designed to support," "many patients experience"
- FTC Testimonials: All testimonials must note "results vary" or "individual results" — no before/after photos without FTC disclaimer
- ACA/APTA Ethics: No comparative superiority over medical doctors; no anti-medication language; no "best," "leading," "most effective" without substantiation
- State Board (Nevada NAC 634 example): No unsubstantiated superlatives; license number must appear on all ads in licensed states
- CAN-SPAM: Physical address + unsubscribe required in all email
- TCPA: SMS marketing requires prior written consent; STOP opt-out in every text

Generate the following for:

**Practice name:** [PRACTICE NAME]
**Provider name(s) and credentials:** [e.g., Dr. James Okafor DC, CCSP | Dr. Lisa Chen DPT, OCS]
**Location:** [City, State + neighborhood if applicable]
**Specialties:** [e.g., auto accident/PI, sports injury, prenatal, corporate ergonomics, pediatric]
**Current new patient offer:** [e.g., Free initial consultation, $49 new patient exam + X-rays, first PT evaluation free]
**Practice differentiators (3-5):** [e.g., same-day appointments, direct access PT (no referral needed), PI lien accepted, multilingual staff]
**State license numbers:** [e.g., NV DC License #12345, NV PT License #67890]

---

OUTPUTS TO GENERATE:

**1. Google Business Profile Optimization**
- Business description (750 chars max, keyword-rich, FTC-compliant outcome language, no superlatives)
- 5 service descriptions (150 chars each: chiropractic care, physical therapy, auto injury treatment, sports rehabilitation, [5th specialty of choice])
- 3 Q&A pairs (question + thorough answer, structured for featured snippet format, include license number in at least one answer)

**2. Facebook/Instagram Ad Set — New Patient Offer**
Write 5 ad variants (2 awareness + 3 conversion):
- Each ad: headline (40 chars), primary text (125 chars), description (30 chars), CTA button label
- Include FTC-compliant disclaimer text at bottom of each: "Individual results may vary. Not a guarantee of specific outcomes."
- Include Equal Health disclaimer where applicable: "Licensed in [State]. [License #]."
- Forbidden words/phrases: guarantee, cure, eliminate pain, fix your back, best chiropractor, top-rated (unless verified review source cited), miracle, permanent

**3. Google Search RSA (Responsive Search Ad)**
- 3 headlines (30 chars max each, include keyword + location + offer)
- 2 descriptions (90 chars max each, benefits + CTA)
- Health claim compliance note: call out which specific claims are outcome-safe

**4. Personal Injury / Auto Accident Attorney Referral One-Pager**
Write 3 versions:
- Version A: Plaintiff PI attorney (injury documentation focus, lien acceptance, case communication process)
- Version B: Workers' compensation attorney (functional capacity, work-readiness certification, employer accommodation letters)
- Version C: Auto insurance adjuster/defense (IME coordination, objective outcomes reporting, case closure timeline)

Each version includes:
- Practice introduction (2 sentences, credentials + specialties)
- Why refer to us (3 bullet points, case-type specific)
- Documentation process (intake → treatment notes → MMI report → lien authorization workflow)
- Communication protocol (response time commitment, point-of-contact, portal access if applicable)
- Contact block (name, phone, fax, email, license numbers)

**5. Nextdoor Community Introduction Post**
- 500–700 chars
- Neighbor tone (warm, informational, not salesy)
- No clinical superiority claims
- Include: who you are, what conditions you help with (careful language), new patient offer, how to schedule
- Local landmarks or neighborhood references to build community connection

**6. Google Review Request System (3-touch, HIPAA-safe)**
Touch 1 — In-office verbal script (30 seconds, after a positive session):
"[Patient first name], I'm so glad today's session went well. If you have a moment this week, an honest Google review would mean a lot to the practice — it helps other people in [neighborhood] find us. No pressure at all. I can send you a quick link if that's easier."

Touch 2 — SMS (send 24 hours after positive session, patient name only, NO condition/treatment reference):
"Hi [First Name], it's [Provider] at [Practice Name]. Hope you're feeling great today! If you have a moment, we'd love a Google review — here's the link: [link]. Reply STOP to opt out."

Touch 3 — Email (3-day follow-up if no review, HIPAA-safe subject line):
- Subject line: "A quick favor, [First Name]?" (NO clinical language)
- Body: 75 words max, personalized, link to Google profile, opt-out footer

All 3 touches must pass HIPAA review: no mention of the reason for visit, condition treated, or treatment received.

---

FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
- Flag any compliance risk with [COMPLIANCE FLAG: ...]
- Note where state license number must appear
- Include a compliance checklist at the end of the output
```

---

## Usage Notes

- Replace all [BRACKETED] fields with practice-specific information
- The PI attorney referral one-pager is often the highest-value output — use it as a standalone Fiverr deliverable at $49-97
- For multi-location practices, run once per location (GBP descriptions should be location-specific)
- Nevada practices: NAC 634.240 requires license number on all advertising — included in the prompt
