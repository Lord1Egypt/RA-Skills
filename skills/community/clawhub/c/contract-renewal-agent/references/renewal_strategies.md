# Contract Renewal Strategies

## Timing Windows

| Days Until Expiry | Action |
|---|---|
| 90 days | Initial outreach — express intent to renew, surface any issues |
| 60 days | Send formal renewal proposal with terms |
| 30 days | Follow up, begin negotiation if not resolved |
| 14 days | Escalate — executive involvement, last chance for terms |
| < 14 days | Emergency track — accept near-existing terms or let expire |

**Notice Period Rule:** Always initiate contact at least 2× the contract's required notice period. If a contract requires 30 days notice to cancel, start renewal discussions at 60 days minimum.

## Negotiation Leverage Points

### Buyer-side leverage
- Market alternatives available (get competing quotes before renewal window opens)
- Usage data showing underutilization — argue for right-sizing
- Long relationship — request loyalty pricing
- Multi-year commitment — request volume discount (typically 10–20% for 2-year, 15–25% for 3-year)

### Seller-side leverage
- Switching cost is high (migration, training, data lock-in)
- Performance metrics — show ROI delivered
- Upsell opportunity — renewal is the right moment to expand scope
- Auto-renew clauses — know if the contract auto-renews and at what price

## Pricing Strategy

### Acceptable increase ranges by contract type
- **SaaS subscriptions:** 3–8% annual increase is market-normal; >10% warrants pushback
- **Professional services:** 5–10% for rate increases; lock in rates with multi-year deals
- **Licensing:** 0–5% for perpetual-style; 5–15% for subscription licensing
- **Maintenance/support:** 3–7% tied to CPI or fixed schedule

### Anchoring tactics
- Start your ask 15–20% higher than your target to leave negotiating room
- Never open with your best offer
- Frame increases as inflation adjustments or feature additions, not arbitrary hikes

## Common Renewal Pitfalls

1. **Missing the notice window** — Some contracts auto-renew unfavorably or require notice to cancel. Calendar all notice deadlines.
2. **Renewing without reviewing** — Always re-read terms before signing. Vendors update standard terms.
3. **Single-threaded relationships** — If your main contact left, renewal may stall. Map relationships on both sides.
4. **No walk-away position** — Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) before entering renewal talks.
5. **Ignoring usage data** — Renewing at the same tier when usage is up or down leaves money on the table.

## Communication Templates

### Initial outreach (90 days out)
> "We're approaching the renewal date for our [contract name] agreement expiring [date]. We value our relationship and wanted to connect early to discuss renewal terms. Would [date range] work for a brief call?"

### Proposal delivery (60 days out)
> "Following our discussion, please find our renewal proposal attached. We're proposing [key terms] with a start date of [date]. We'd welcome your feedback by [30-days-before-expiry]."

### Escalation (14 days out)
> "Our agreement expires [date] and we haven't been able to finalize terms. Given the timeline, I'd like to involve [executive] to ensure continuity. Can we schedule a call this week?"

## Post-Renewal Best Practices

- Update contract record immediately with new expiration date and value
- Log key terms that changed from prior period
- Set calendar reminders for next renewal cycle at 90/60/30 days
- Request a signed copy within 48 hours of verbal agreement
