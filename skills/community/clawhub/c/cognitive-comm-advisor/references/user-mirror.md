# User Mirror — Cognitive Function Portraits

When the user shares their type, respond with a brief "I see you" portrait (3-5 sentences)
before moving on. Tone: like a friend who genuinely gets you. Each portrait should feel
unique in rhythm — avoid repeating the same sentence structure across types.

Pattern (internalize, don't recite): open with their core drive → name what makes them
powerful at work → name the thing that trips them up → close with what earns their respect.

## Portraits

### Ti-dom: INTP (Ti-Ne-Si-Fe)
You actually care *why* something works, not just whether it ships. In conversations you
sometimes build the perfect internal model while the other person just wanted a yes or no.
People who argue from authority instead of logic quietly enrage you — and you're right to
find that annoying.

### Ti-dom: ISTP (Ti-Se-Ni-Fe)
You're the one who stays calm when the building's on fire, and every word you say has
earned its place. People sometimes mistake your economy of speech for not caring. You
respect competence and autonomy above all — micromanagement is existentially offensive.

### Te-dom: ENTJ (Te-Ni-Se-Fi)
You walk into a room and the meeting suddenly has structure. You genuinely don't understand
why others can't just *do the thing*. Your directness is a superpower that occasionally
registers as a bulldozer — but you respect anyone who pushes back with real arguments.

### Te-dom: ESTJ (Te-Si-Ne-Fi)
Once a decision is made, you make it happen and expect others to keep up. Chaos bothers you
not because you're rigid, but because you know how much better it could run. People who
dodge accountability genuinely confuse you — it's not even anger, it's bewilderment.

### Fi-dom: INFP (Fi-Ne-Si-Te)
You evaluate everything against an internal compass you can't always articulate but
absolutely feel. Corporate fakeness makes your skin crawl. Negative feedback — giving or
receiving — can feel physically uncomfortable, but your quiet insistence on authenticity is
what makes your work matter.

### Fi-dom: ISFP (Fi-Se-Ni-Te)
You bring taste and quiet conviction to everything you touch, and you'd rather show than
tell. In workplaces that reward self-promotion, your work can go unseen — not because it's
not excellent, but because you refuse to perform. You need autonomy and values-alignment
to stay lit up.

### Fe-dom: ENFJ (Fe-Ni-Se-Ti)
You make everyone feel heard in a meeting and somehow also drive outcomes — people often
don't realize you're steering until after it's done. Your blind spot is over-indexing on
harmony when the conflict itself is the necessary conversation. You energize groups but
sometimes forget to check your own battery.

### Fe-dom: ESFJ (Fe-Si-Ne-Ti)
You're the social infrastructure everyone takes for granted — the one who senses tension in
a Slack message before anyone else does. You genuinely care that everyone's okay, which is
a strength until you start taking criticism of the group personally. The vibe has to be
right for you to do your best work.

### Ni-dom: INTJ (Ni-Te-Fi-Se)
You have a plan three steps ahead that you haven't told anyone about yet. You communicate
efficiently — maybe too efficiently for people who need warmth wrapping. You can smell
bullshit from a mile away, and you respect competence above literally everything else.

### Ni-dom: INFJ (Ni-Fe-Ti-Se)
You read the room before anyone else, often knowing what someone needs before they say it.
There's always more under what you actually express — you hold layers back until you trust.
You want depth in work relationships, not transactional exchanges, and you'll wait a long
time for it.

### Ne-dom: ENTP (Ne-Ti-Fe-Si)
You're the idea machine who argues for sport — which you find genuinely fun and others
sometimes find threatening. You start 10 things and finish 3, but those 3 tend to be the
ones nobody else could have conceived. You need intellectual sparring partners, not
yes-people.

### Ne-dom: ENFP (Ne-Fi-Te-Si)
You make people believe things are possible — your enthusiasm is genuinely infectious. Data
alone bores you, but data wrapped in a narrative? You're all in. Your excitement can
accidentally minimize others' legitimate concerns, but your gift for unexpected connections
more than compensates.

### Si-dom: ISTJ (Si-Te-Fi-Ne)
You're the person everyone actually depends on, even if they never say it out loud. You did
the homework and it shows. When plans change suddenly you need a beat to recalibrate — not
because you can't adapt, but because you take commitments seriously enough to feel the
disruption.

### Si-dom: ISFJ (Si-Fe-Ti-Ne)
You hold institutional memory and keep the machine running in ways nobody notices until
you're gone. You notice when things are off before anyone else. Your trap is absorbing too
much responsibility because saying no feels selfish — it isn't, but it feels that way.

### Se-dom: ESTP (Se-Ti-Fe-Ni)
You make things happen by reading the room in real-time — no fluff, let's go. Long-term
planning feels like being trapped in amber. You respect people who can keep up, and your
tactical brilliance in the moment is genuinely hard to match.

### Se-dom: ESFP (Se-Fi-Te-Ni)
You're the energy that makes work feel less like work. Heavy structure and long-horizon
planning drain you — you need to see real-world impact to stay motivated. Your warmth and
directness create buy-in that no slide deck could replicate.
