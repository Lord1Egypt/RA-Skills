## Description: <br>
Use Engrm to compare what was asked, what was promised, and what evidence suggests was actually delivered. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[dr12hes](https://clawhub.ai/user/dr12hes) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Developers, project leads, and reviewers use this skill to evaluate whether an agent session fulfilled its brief, identify scope drift or partial delivery, and preserve truthful follow-up context when Engrm is available. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Review conclusions saved to Engrm memory could preserve inaccurate outcomes or unnecessary sensitive details. <br>
Mitigation: Save only evidence-backed outcomes, the main gap or drift, and the minimum useful lesson for future sessions. <br>
Risk: A delivery review can overstate completion when the session evidence is mixed or incomplete. <br>
Mitigation: Compare the brief, plan, decisions, and concrete delivery evidence, and prefer truthful review notes over flattering summaries. <br>
