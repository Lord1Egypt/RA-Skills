# Scenario: Salaried with Loans

Use when user has education loan or home loan and wants to understand tax treatment.

## Home loan — Self-occupied (Old regime)
- Section 24(b): interest deduction up to 2,00,000
- Section 80C: principal repayment up to 1,50,000 (within overall 80C limit)
- Section 80EE: additional 50,000 if loan sanctioned 1-Apr-2016 to 31-Mar-2017
- Section 80EEA: additional 1,50,000 if first-time buyer, loan sanctioned 1-Apr-2019 to 31-Mar-2022, and 80EE not claimed

## Education loan (Old regime)
- Section 80E: entire interest amount deductible
- No upper limit on interest
- Available for higher education of self or relative
- Available for 8 years starting from the year repayment begins

## New regime limitation
- **Home loan interest and principal deductions are NOT available in new regime**
- Only 80CCD(2) employer NPS and 80CCH Agnipath apply
- If home loan is a major deduction, old regime may be better

## Example comparison
User: 15L salary, 2L home loan interest, 1.5L 80C
- Old regime taxable: 15L - 50K std - 2L (24b) - 1.5L (80C) = 11L
- Old regime tax: ~1,42,500 + cess
- New regime taxable: 15L - 75K std = 14.25L
- New regime tax: ~1,95,000 + cess
- **Old regime saves ~52,500+ in this case**
