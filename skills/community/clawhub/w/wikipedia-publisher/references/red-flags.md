# Wikipedia Publishing Red Flags

Use this checklist before publishing.

## Source quality

- Are most citations primary, affiliated, or press-release style?
- Is notability resting on trivial mentions rather than substantial coverage?
- Are there missing dates, names, or metrics that are only asserted by the subject?
- Are there dead links or sources that do not support the exact claim?

## Tone

Remove or rewrite:
- best, leading, world-class, pioneering, innovative, trusted, premier
- official supplier, official partner, industry leader
- vague scale claims: global, major, extensive, large-scale
- sales language: customers, solutions, excellence, premium, bespoke

## Structure

- Does the lead depend on primary sources?
- Is there an overly long project/client list?
- Is there a section that exists only because it sounds impressive?
- Are you including routine corporate facts that do not help encyclopedic value?

## COI safety

If the user is connected to the subject:
- prefer `User:` sandbox or `Draft:`
- avoid mainspace publication in the first move
- use extra restraint with praise, rankings, and commercial framing
- make edit summaries boring and factual

## Good defaults

- Shorter article
- Fewer claims
- Better sources
- Clear attribution
- Sandbox first
