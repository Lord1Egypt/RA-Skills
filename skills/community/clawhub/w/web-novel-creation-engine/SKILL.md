## Workflow

### Phase 1: Genre & Market Analysis
Select genre, analyze market trends, identify differentiation angle.

### Phase 2: Character Development
Create main characters with arcs, relationships, and distinctive traits.

### Phase 3: Plot Architecture
Build novel outline with chapter breakdown, plot points, and pacing.

### Phase 4: Opening & Chapter Optimization
Craft compelling opening chapters with strong hooks and sustainable pacing.

## Output Format
- **Market Analysis Report** (Genre popularity, competition, opportunity spaces)
- **Character Profiles** (Detailed bios with appearance, personality, background, arc)
- **Novel Outline** (Chapter-by-chapter summary with plot points and emotional curve)
- **Opening Chapters** (First 3 chapters with annotations)
- **Pacing Chart** (Visual timeline of tension and release)
- **Platform-specific Recommendations**

## Examples

**Example 1: Urban Fantasy Romance**
```
Genre: Urban Fantasy + Romance
Platform: Hongxiu (female readers)
Output:
- Concept: CEO werewolf x human doctor with healing powers
- Characters: Cold CEO with hidden soft side, strong female lead with special ability
- Plot: 100 chapters with 3 major arcs (meet→conflict→resolution)
- Opening: Medical emergency scene with supernatural element revealed
- Pacing: Fast romance development with fantasy elements introduced gradually
```

**Example 2: Gaming Infinite Flow**
```
Genre: Gaming + Infinite Flow
Platform: Qidian (male readers)
Output:
- Concept: Pro gamer enters infinite game world, uses real skills to dominate
- System: Leveling, skills, items, guilds
- Plot: Multiple game worlds with increasing difficulty
- Satisfaction: Knowledge advantage, strategic wins, team leadership
- Pacing: Constant action with strategic planning breaks
```

## Notes
- Respect author's original style, provide suggestions not mandates
- Pacing and satisfaction points should match target platform reader preferences
- Different strategies for long-form (百万字) vs short-form novels
- Character consistency is crucial for reader engagement
- Chapter endings should always have hooks to continue reading
- Balance between formulaic success and creative originality
