## Description: <br>
Applies counterfactual thinking to explore alternative histories, understand causation, learn from past decisions without hindsight bias, and evaluate whether different choices might have led to better outcomes. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Employees, external users, and analysts use this skill when they need an agent to structure retrospective analysis, build plausible counterfactual scenarios, separate causation from luck, and extract lessons for future decisions. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Counterfactual scenarios can become speculative or misleading if treated as factual conclusions. <br>
Mitigation: Anchor the analysis in the established factual history, use plausible minimal changes, label confidence, and distinguish known facts from estimates. <br>
Risk: Broad retrospective reasoning may be over-applied to medical, legal, security, mental health, financial, or other high-stakes topics. <br>
Mitigation: Keep the analysis bounded as decision-support reasoning and defer to appropriate domain safeguards or qualified review for high-stakes conclusions. <br>
