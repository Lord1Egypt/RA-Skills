External users, policy analysts, strategists, and researchers use this skill to structure automation-transition analysis, post-work policy design, distribution modeling, and labor-market scenario planning. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill may proactively frame adjacent future-of-work or automation topics through a post-labor economics lens. <br>
Mitigation: Use it when that framing is desired, and disable or avoid it when the user needs neutral discussion outside this policy area. <br>
Risk: Policy and macroeconomic recommendations can be misleading if assumptions, values, or political constraints are presented as settled facts. <br>
Mitigation: Maintain the evidence ledger requested by the artifact and label claims as empirical findings, modeling assumptions, normative preferences, or political constraints. <br>
Risk: The artifact supports optional local memory for policy drafts, indicators, and scenarios. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for explicit confirmation before writing files and keep local artifacts within ~/post-labor-economics/. <br>
