# Proof Asset Lifecycle

## Flow

1. Intake
   - Gather project facts, approval status, confidentiality constraints, and candidate outcomes.

2. Outcome interview
   - Interview buyer, operator, reviewer, and technical owner.

3. Evidence review
   - Separate verified metrics, qualitative observations, quotes, and internal-only details.

4. Asset selection
   - Choose anonymized/internal/public case study, testimonial, sales asset, web/social content, or approval checklist.

5. Draft
   - Use approved facts and mark anything needing approval.

6. Client approval
   - Confirm quotes, client name, metrics, screenshots, systems, and attribution.

7. Reuse
   - Feed approved proof into email, proposal, website, LinkedIn, referral, portfolio, and sales one-pagers.

## Handoff To Other Skills

- `agentic-email-skill`: use approved proof snippets in nurture and follow-up.
- `agentic-proposal-skill`: use approved proof in executive summaries and proposal credibility sections.
- `agentic-delivery-skill`: pull evaluation reports, acceptance packets, post-launch reviews, and lessons learned.
- `agentic-discovery-skill`: feed common pain patterns back into discovery questions.
- `agentic-contract-skill`: do not modify contract claims unless proof is approved and legally reviewed.

## Approval Checklist

Before public use, verify:

- client name approved
- quote approved
- metrics approved
- screenshots approved
- system/tool names approved
- confidential details removed
- regulated-use claims avoided
- publication channel approved
