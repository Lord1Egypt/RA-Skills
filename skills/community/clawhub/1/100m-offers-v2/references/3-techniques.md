## The Offer Creation Toolbox

### 1. The Value Equation Worksheet

**Formula:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**Walk through each variable with your customer in mind:**

| Variable | Question | Example Answer (for a fitness coach) |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | What does success look like for them, specifically? | "Lose 20 pounds in 90 days and keep it off for life" |
| Perceived Likelihood | How can you increase their belief they'll achieve it? | "I've helped 47 people with your exact stats. Here are their photos." |
| Time Delay | How can you make results happen faster? | "You'll feel different in week 1. Visible changes by week 3." |
| Effort & Sacrifice | What effort/time/sacrifice can you remove? | "No gym required. No meal prep. I create your grocery list." |

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer Checklist

Before launch, confirm your offer has:

- [ ] **Premium Price** — At least 4x what you were charging before
- [ ] **Starving Crowd** — People who desperately want this and are already spending money
- [ ] **Value Equation balanced** — High dream outcome, high likelihood, low time delay, low effort
- [ ] **Scarcity** — Limited availability (real or communicated)
- [ ] **Urgency** — Time limit on the offer
- [ ] **Bonuses stacked** — 5-10 high-perceived-value bonuses at near-zero cost
- [ ] **Guarantee** — Strong enough to remove all buyer risk
- [ ] **Dream Outcome name** — Name that communicates the result
- [ ] **Category of one** — Offer is incomparable to any competitor

### 3. The Trim & Stack Template

**Trim (remove from your offer):**

| Component | Current Cost | Customer Value | Keep or Cut? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly report | 2 hours | Low | Cut |
| Admin support | 1 hour/week | Low | Cut |
| [Your low-value item] | | | |

**Stack (pack into one premium offer):**

| Component | Perceived Customer Value | Your Actual Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Core service | $5,000 | $500 |
| Guarantee (until results) | $3,000 | $0 (rarely claimed) |
| Bonus #1: Done-for-you template | $500 | $5 (hosting) |
| Bonus #2: Private community access | $1,000 | $0 |
| Bonus #3: 30-min strategy session | $500 | $0 (your time, already have) |
| **Total Grand Slam Offer** | **$10,000 value → $2,997 price** | **$505 cost** |

### 4. The Guarantee Ladder

| Level | Guarantee Statement | Expected Conversion Boost |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "30-day money-back guarantee" | 1.5x |
| 2 | "Double your money back" | 2x |
| 3 | "I'll work with you until you get results" | 3x |
| 4 | "Pay only after you get results" | 5x+ |

### 5. The Bonuses Stack Formula

**Rule:** At least 5 bonuses. Total perceived value of bonuses should exceed your core offer price.

**Types of bonuses (high perceived, low cost):**
- Templates / swipe files
- Checklists / cheat sheets
- Pre-recorded training / course
- Private community / group access
- Exclusive software / tool
- VIP support / priority access
- Physical item (book, swag) — only if digital is insufficient

> **Case: The book itself as a lead magnet** (Chapter 2): $100M Offers is sold at cost. The real offer? Acquisition.com's investment in qualifying entrepreneurs. The book serves as the "salty pretzel"—solves a narrow problem (how to make offers), reveals a bigger problem (getting more customers/capital to scale), then offers the core solution (investment and infrastructure). **Key takeaway:** Your Grand Slam Offer can be something you give away nearly free, as long as it leads to the bigger transaction.

### 6. The Starving Crowd Finder

**Step 1:** Look for industries with these signals:
- High spend on advertising (they can afford to acquire customers)
- Low customer satisfaction (everyone hates their provider)
- High churn (customers constantly switching)
- Fragmented market (no dominant player)
- Universal need (everyone in the industry has this problem)

**Step 2:** Map the 9 desires to your audience:
- More money? → Sell to business owners
- Better health? → Sell to people with chronic issues
- More time? → Sell to overwhelmed professionals
- Less pain? → Sell to people with specific frustrations

### 7. The Naming Template

**Structure:** [Timeframe] + [Dream Outcome] + [Container]

Examples:
- "The 90-Day Body Transformation"
- "The $100K Agency Blueprint"
- "The 30-Day Client Explosion System"
- "The Zero-Risk Lead Generation Machine"
- "The Done-With-You Business Exit Program"

**Bad names to avoid:** "Premium Package," "Gold Level," "Advanced Service," "VIP Plan." These communicate nothing.

> **Case: The challenge offer that built an empire** (Chapter 14): When Hormozi was broke, his only product was a "30-Day Challenge" at a single gym. He charged $400 up front, ran ads to fill it, and collected the cash immediately. The offer: "Join our 30-day challenge. If you don't see results, your next month is free." Simple. Clear. No confusion. That single offer template was what his salesman sold $120K worth of in one month. **Key takeaway:** The simplest offers are often the most powerful. "One thing, one price, one guarantee" beats complicated multi-tier plans every time.
