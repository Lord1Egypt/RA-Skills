# Compliance Claims Guide — What Creators May and May Not Say

Put the relevant banned claims VERBATIM in the brief's don'ts section. "Follow platform rules" is not a brief instruction; "never say 'cures acne'" is.

## Universal rules (all categories, all platforms)

- **Disclosure is mandatory:** sponsored/affiliate relationship must be disclosed (#ad, paid-partnership toggle, or verbal). FTC (US) and platform policy both require it; the BRAND is liable too.
- **No guaranteed outcomes:** "guaranteed results", "everyone sees", "100% works" — banned phrasing in every category.
- **No fake scarcity:** "only 3 left" when untrue violates platform policy and consumer law.
- **Price claims must be accurate for the campaign window:** if the discount ends June 16, the creator may not imply it's permanent.

## Beauty & skincare

| Banned | Compliant alternative |
|---|---|
| "Cures / erases wrinkles" | "Reduces the appearance of fine lines" |
| "Removes scars" | "Helps fade the look of scarring over time" |
| "Medical-grade results" (unless cleared) | "FDA-cleared device" (only if literally true) |
| Before/after with unrealistic timeframe | Before/after matching the studied timeframe, labeled |

TikTok additionally restricts before/after imagery for some skin conditions — when in doubt, demo texture/routine instead of skin transformation.

## Supplements & wellness

- Structure/function claims ONLY: "supports immune health", "helps maintain energy levels".
- Banned: any disease claim — "treats anxiety", "prevents colds", "fixes gut issues".
- Required in many regions: "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease" — include in caption guidance.
- No claims about specific lab values ("lowers cholesterol by 20%").

## Food & beverage

- No weight-loss promises tied to timeframes ("lose 5kg in 2 weeks" — banned).
- "Sugar-free", "organic", "non-GMO" only if certified — attach the certificate reference in the brief.
- Allergen statements may not be improvised by the creator.

## Electronics

- Battery/performance numbers must match the spec sheet — give the creator exact figures.
- Safety certifications (UL, CE) may be shown but not paraphrased ("totally safe" → don't).
- No claims of compatibility that haven't been tested ("works with any phone").

## Kids & baby products

- Strictest category. Age-grading must be stated exactly as certified.
- No "100% safe" phrasing; use the specific certification ("ASTM F963 tested").
- Creators may not show unsupervised-use scenarios for products requiring supervision.

## Financial-adjacent (earning claims in LIVE/affiliate recruiting content)

- No income guarantees ("you'll make $500/week"). Ranges with "can" + visible assumptions only.

## Brief integration checklist

- [ ] Category identified and its table reviewed
- [ ] Banned phrases copied verbatim into don'ts (max 7 — pick the ones this campaign could actually trigger)
- [ ] Compliant alternatives provided next to each banned phrase
- [ ] Disclosure method specified (platform toggle + verbal/caption)
- [ ] Price/discount claims bounded to campaign dates
- [ ] Required disclaimers included in caption guidance
