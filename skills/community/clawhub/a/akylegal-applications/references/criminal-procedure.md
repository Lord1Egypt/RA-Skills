## Criminal Procedure Overview

```
Case filing → Investigation → Prosecution review → First instance → Second instance → Execution
                                                       ↘ Death penalty review (specific cases)
```

---

## Investigation Stage

### Detention and Arrest

**Criminal Detention (decided by public security):**
- General: within 3 days
- Extended (fugitive/repeat/criminal gang): up to 30 days
- Family notified within 24 hours (except when national security involved or notice impossible)

**Arrest (approved by procuratorate):**
- Conditions: evidence of crime + likely sentence of imprisonment + poses social danger
- Procuratorate review: 7 days
- After arrest: interrogation within 24 hours

### Defense Lawyer's Rights During Investigation
1. Right to meet: present lawyer's license, firm letter, and power of attorney; meeting arranged within 48 hours
2. Right to correspond
3. Right to learn suspected crime and case circumstances from investigation authorities
4. Right to file complaints and accusations on client's behalf
5. Right to apply for compulsory measure modification (bail pending trial/residential surveillance)

### Investigation Period
- General: 2 months
- Extension: 1 month approved by higher procuratorate
- Special extension (remote areas/major criminal groups/fugitives/broad scope): another 2 months approved by provincial procuratorate
- Extreme extension (likely 10+ years sentence): another 2 months approved by provincial procuratorate
- Maximum: 7 months

### Investigation Conclusion
- Must be factually clear with reliable and sufficient evidence
- Prepare prosecution recommendation opinion → transfer to procuratorate

## Prosecution Review Stage

### Procuratorate Review
- Review period: 1 month (complex cases may extend 15 days)
- Supplementary investigation: return to public security, 1 month each, max 2 times
- After supplementary investigation, review period recalculates

### Review Outcomes

| Outcome | Applicable Circumstances |
|---------|------------------------|
| Public prosecution | Clear facts, reliable and sufficient evidence |
| Non-prosecution (statutory) | Minor offense/statute of limitations expired/pardon/no complaint/death |
| Non-prosecution (discretionary) | Minor crime, no punishment needed per criminal law |
| Non-prosecution (insufficient evidence) | Insufficient evidence, does not meet prosecution conditions |
| Conditional non-prosecution | Minor suspected by a minor (likely ≤1 year sentence) |

### Defense Lawyer's Role During Prosecution Review
1. Case file review: access, copy all case materials
2. Meetings: unlimited
3. Evidence collection: request procuratorate to collect exculpatory/mitigating evidence
4. Legal opinions: custody necessity review, non-prosecution opinions
5. Plea bargaining negotiation: participate in sentencing negotiation

## Trial Stage

### Pre-Trial Preparation

**1. Court Review of Indictment:** 7 days

**2. Pre-Trial Conference (《刑诉法》Art. 187):**
Applicable: major complex cases / extensive evidence / significant disputes
Handles:
- Jurisdictional objections
- Recusal applications
- Illegal evidence exclusion applications
- Witness/expert appearance list
- Evidence disclosure
- Summary of disputed issues

**3. Pre-Trial Defense Preparation:**
- Detailed case file review, prepare reading notes
- Meet defendant, verify evidence, determine defense direction
- Investigate and gather evidence (self-collect or apply for court collection)
- Draft defense outline
- Comparable case research
- Prepare questioning outline (defendant/witnesses/experts)

### First Instance Trial Procedure

```
Session opened → Court investigation → Court debate → Defendant's final statement → Deliberation and judgment
```

#### Court Investigation (Core)

**Prosecutor reads indictment**
→ **Defendant makes statement** (may admit/partially admit/deny charges)
→ **Prosecutor interrogates defendant**
→ **Defense counsel questions defendant**
→ **Presiding judge questions**

→ **Evidence presentation and cross-examination** (by group or item)

Evidence presentation order:
1. Prosecution evidence (incriminating evidence system)
2. Defense evidence (exculpatory/mitigating evidence)
3. Court-collected evidence (if any)

Cross-examination focus (three-dimension test):
- **Authenticity**: whether evidence is objective and genuine; no forgery/alteration
- **Legality**: whether collection subject, procedure, and form are lawful
- **Relevance**: logical connection between evidence and facts to be proved

→ **Witnesses/experts/specialized knowledge persons appear in court**

#### Court Debate

Prosecutor delivers closing argument → Victim states opinion → Defendant self-defends → Defense counsel delivers defense statement

Second round: addresses disputed issues from first round

#### Defendant's Final Statement
- Right to complete statement, not to be interrupted
- Key: express remorse/defense/leniency request

### Judgment

- Guilty verdict: clear facts, reliable and sufficient evidence
- Not guilty verdict: insufficient evidence / does not constitute crime per law
- Scheduled verdict: judgment served within 5 days of pronouncement

### Summary / Fast-Track Procedure

| Procedure | Conditions | Trial Period |
|-----------|-----------|:------------:|
| Summary (简易) | Basic court + guilty plea + consent | 20 days (if >3 yrs possible: 1.5 months) |
| Fast-track (速裁) | Basic court + guilty plea + ≤3 yrs | 10 days (if >1 yr possible: 15 days) |

## Appeal Procedure

**Appeal/Protest Deadline:**
- **Judgment: 10 days**
- **Ruling: 5 days**
- Commencement: day after receiving written judgment/ruling

**Methods of Appeal:**
- Through the original trial court
- Directly to the appellate court

**Principle of No Additional Punishment on Appeal (《刑诉法》Art. 237):**
- When only the defendant appeals, sentence cannot be increased on appeal
- Not applicable when procuratorate protests or private prosecutor appeals

**Appeal Hearing Methods:**
- Must hold oral hearing: protest cases + death penalty appeal cases
- May skip oral hearing: when facts are clear

**Appeal Rulings:**
- Affirm original judgment
- Amend (legal application error / improper sentencing / factual error)
- Vacate and remand (unclear facts/insufficient evidence/procedural violation)
- Cases remanded and re-appealed must have oral hearing

---

## Death Penalty Review Procedure

- Immediate execution: Supreme People's Court approval required
- Suspended death sentence: Higher People's Court approval
- Defense counsel may submit defense opinions to Supreme Court
- Supreme Court must interrogate defendant
- If not approved, may remand for retrial or amend judgment

---

## Petition and Retrial

- Petition does not suspend execution of judgment
- Target: legally effective judgments/rulings
- Submit to original court or higher court
- Statutory retrial grounds (《刑诉法》Art. 253):
  1. New evidence proves original judgment wrong
  2. Conviction/sentencing evidence is unreliable or insufficient
  3. Legal application error in original judgment
  4. Procedural violation of law
  5. Judge engaged in corruption/bribery/abuse of power perverting justice
