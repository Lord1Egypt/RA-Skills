## Description: <br>
Creates a comfort-focused room airflow map, timing routine, and weekly review checklist for stuffy rooms without HVAC diagnosis, repair advice, allergy guidance, or unsafe window use. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[harrylabsj](https://clawhub.ai/user/harrylabsj) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill to create a household comfort routine for stale or stuffy rooms using observed room conditions and airflow options they already have. It produces a room airflow map, daily timing routine, comfort observation log, weekly review checklist, and safety reminders while staying outside HVAC repair, medical, allergy, and hazardous-air guidance. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The security review says prompt examples may lead an assistant toward overconfident home ventilation, HVAC, product, or health-adjacent recommendations. <br>
Mitigation: Keep generated guidance limited to observation-based comfort routines using existing safe airflow options, and defer equipment, symptoms, hazardous air, smoke, carbon monoxide, mold, severe heat or cold, and safety concerns to official guidance, emergency services, or qualified professionals. <br>
Risk: Some artifact examples describe HVAC distance, vent booster fans, energy-bill analysis, product purchases, CO2 explanations, and window-opening details that can exceed the stated comfort-only boundary. <br>
Mitigation: Treat those examples as non-authoritative evidence of risk; reviewers should revise examples so they match the comfort-only scope, avoid product and diagnosis claims, and preserve window, weather, security, child, pet, and building-rule constraints. <br>
