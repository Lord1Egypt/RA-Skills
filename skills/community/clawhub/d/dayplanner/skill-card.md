## Description: <br>
Dayplanner is a command-line planner for weekly and monthly planning, time blocking, meeting scheduling, deadline management, and local task tracking. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[ckchzh](https://clawhub.ai/user/ckchzh) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users and developers use Dayplanner to capture tasks, plans, reminders, reviews, priorities, and habit tracking entries from the terminal. It is suited to lightweight file-based personal productivity workflows that need local logs, search, summaries, and export. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Planner entries are saved as local plain-text files under ~/.local/share/dayplanner. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid entering secrets, credentials, regulated data, or highly sensitive work notes; delete the data directory to remove stored entries and exports. <br>
