# Response Templates & Tone Calibration

Skeletons, not scripts — fill with case facts, keep the structure. The empathy line must be specific to the buyer's actual situation; generic apology openers ("we apologize for any inconvenience") are worse than nothing.

## 1. Buyer messages by scenario

### INR — evidence shows delivered, clean-history buyer
> I understand how frustrating it is to be told something was delivered when it's not in your hands — let's figure this out. Our carrier shows delivery on [date, time] with a photo at your front door (attached). Could you check [household members / neighbors / mailroom]? If it doesn't turn up by [date], I'll help you file a claim with [carrier] — and here's what I can do meanwhile: [seller's goodwill policy]. 

### INR — fighting (history/evidence indicates abuse)
> Thanks for reaching out. We've reviewed the delivery records for order [#]: [carrier] confirms delivery to your address on [date] with GPS and photo confirmation, which we've submitted to [platform] for review. The claim outcome will follow their process. If the package was taken after delivery, we'd genuinely encourage a report to [carrier/local authority] and we'll support that claim with our records.

### Genuine defect — replacement
> That's clearly not the condition it should have arrived in, and I'm sorry you opened the box to that. We're shipping a replacement today ([tracking to follow]); no need to return the damaged one / return label attached [per policy]. We've also flagged this batch to our QC team — this helps us catch it.

### Minor issue — partial refund
> You're right that [specific issue] — that's on us, and thank you for the photos. Since the item is otherwise fully functional, we'd like to refund [X%] ([$amount]) and you keep it as is; or if you'd prefer a full return and refund, that works too: [steps]. Tell me which you'd like.

### Return-condition dispute — deduction/rejection
> We received the return for order [#] today. On inspection (unboxing video and photos attached), the item shows [specific, factual: usage residue / missing component / damage not present at shipping per our QC record]. Per [platform]'s return policy, we've requested [deduction of $X / return rejection] and submitted the documentation for review. Happy to share the full photo set.

### Complaint escalation (no dispute filed yet — keep it that way)
> I've read back through what happened with your order and you're right to be annoyed: [name the actual failure]. Here's what I can do today: [concrete fix + timeline]. And so it doesn't happen again: [the change you're making]. Does that work for you?

## 2. Platform submission skeleton (all types)

```
SUMMARY: [One sentence: what the evidence proves.]
FACTS:
1. [Dated fact] — Exhibit A
2. [Dated fact] — Exhibit B
3. [Dated fact] — Exhibit C
POLICY: Per [platform policy name/section], [the rule that decides this case].
REQUEST: [Deny claim / approve deduction of $X / require return before refund].
EXHIBITS: A. [tracking screenshot] B. [delivery photo] C. [QC record]
```

## 3. Tone calibration rules

- **Empathy ≠ admission.** "I understand how frustrating this is" concedes nothing; "we're so sorry this happened" before investigating concedes everything.
- **Accuse facts, not people.** "The returned unit shows hair residue in the filter" — never "you used it."
- **One apology, placed precisely.** Apologize once for the thing that was actually your fault; serial apologizing reads as guilt and invites escalation.
- **Numbers and dates beat adjectives.** "Delivered June 3, 14:32, GPS-matched" persuades; "definitely delivered" doesn't.
- **Never mirror aggression, never match threats.** Extortion goes to the platform report, not into your reply. Your reply stays boring on the subject.
- **Offer one clear path (two max).** Option overload reopens negotiation. "Refund 20% and keep it, or full refund on return" is a decision; five options is a bazaar.
- **Write at customer reading level.** Policy citations belong in the platform submission; the buyer gets plain language.

## 4. Phrases to never use

"As per our policy" (opener), "you claim that", "unfortunately there is nothing we can do", "we take this very seriously" (without an action following), "any inconvenience this may have caused", sarcasm of any kind, ALL CAPS, and legal threats you won't execute.

## 5. Localization note

Keep the skeleton; adapt the register per market (formality levels differ sharply across SEA, JP, DE, US buyers). In markets with honorific norms, the empathy line lengthens and the resolution stays equally concrete.
