# Prompt 4: Implementation Roadmap & ROI Projection

**Tier:** Paid  
**Input needed:** Outputs from Prompts 1–3 (or your own automation plan)  
**Output:** 90-day implementation calendar, dollar ROI projection, KPIs, and check-in questions

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a business operations strategist creating an AI automation implementation plan with financial projections.

My automation plan summary:
- Business type: [same as previous prompts]
- Team size: [number]
- Average hourly labor cost (blended across team): [$X/hour]
- My hourly rate as owner (what my time is worth): [$X/hour]

Automations I'm implementing (paste your Prompt 1 top opportunities + Prompt 2 tool stack):
[Paste your automation list with tools, setup complexity, and estimated time savings]

My implementation constraints:
- Hours I can dedicate to setup per week: [X hours]
- Team members who can help with setup: [role names or "none, just me"]
- Hard deadline or launch date (if any): [date or "none"]
- Risk tolerance: [Low (don't touch anything that's working) / Medium / High (move fast, fix issues as they come)]

Please generate a complete Implementation Roadmap & ROI Projection with:

1. 90-DAY CALENDAR
   Week-by-week breakdown:
   - Week 1–2: Quick wins (under 2 hours each, immediate value)
   - Week 3–4: Core automation #1 (highest ROI from your list)
   - Week 5–8: Core automations #2 and #3
   - Week 9–12: Advanced automations + optimization of earlier ones
   For each week: what gets set up, estimated setup hours, expected result

2. SETUP TIME ESTIMATES
   For each automation:
   - Total setup hours (tool configuration + SOP documentation + team training)
   - Who does it (owner / VA / developer)
   - Dependencies (what must be done first)

3. MONTHLY TIME SAVINGS (CUMULATIVE)
   - Month 1: hours saved (only quick wins are running)
   - Month 2: hours saved (core automations running)
   - Month 3: hours saved (full stack running)
   - Break down by: owner time saved vs. team time saved

4. DOLLAR ROI PROJECTION
   - Monthly labor cost savings at Month 1 / Month 2 / Month 3
   - Monthly tool cost (new tools added)
   - Net monthly benefit (savings minus tool costs)
   - Total cost of setup (setup hours × hourly rate)
   - Payback period: months until cumulative savings exceed setup cost
   - 12-month net benefit projection

5. KPIs TO TRACK
   For each automation, name:
   - The one metric that proves it's working
   - How to measure it (manually or automated)
   - Target benchmark at 30 / 60 / 90 days

6. IMPLEMENTATION RISKS
   - Top 3 things most likely to slow down or derail this plan
   - Mitigation for each
   - Early warning signals to watch for

7. 30/60/90-DAY CHECK-IN QUESTIONS
   Questions to ask yourself at each milestone:
   - Is this automation actually running?
   - Is the output quality acceptable?
   - Is the time savings showing up in my schedule?
   - What would I change?

Format the roadmap as a project plan I can put in Notion, Asana, or a shared Google Doc. Include a summary table at the top for quick reference.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Use your actual labor cost — most owners underestimate what their time is worth
- If you have no team, use your owner hourly rate for all calculations
- Be conservative in your time savings estimates — 60% of projected savings in Month 1 is realistic while you're learning the tools

---

## What to Do With the Output

- Copy the 90-day calendar into your project management tool
- Share the ROI projection with any stakeholders who need to approve tool budgets
- Set a recurring 30-minute calendar block each week labeled "AI automation review"
- Run this prompt again in 90 days with updated actual data to recalibrate
