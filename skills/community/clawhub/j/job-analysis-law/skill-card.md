## Description: <br>
Assists learners discussing Chapter 6, "Job Analysis and the Law," with U.S. employment-law concepts tied to job analysis, including EEO, EEOC, OFCCP, Title VII, Equal Pay Act, ADEA, Rehabilitation Act, ADA, Uniform Guidelines, adverse impact, selection validation, essential functions, reasonable accommodation, pay equity, and related cases. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Students, instructors, and agents use this skill as a study aid for job-analysis law topics, especially when connecting workplace decisions to U.S. federal employment law, professional standards, and textbook examples. It provides concise explanations, mainly in Chinese with key English legal terms. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may mistake educational employment-law study guidance for current legal advice. <br>
Mitigation: Use it as a study aid only; for real employment decisions or compliance questions, verify current EEOC/OFCCP guidance and consult qualified counsel. <br>
Risk: The skill is based on chapter-focused reference material and may not cover current, state, local, or non-U.S. legal requirements. <br>
Mitigation: Limit conclusions to the textbook-learning context unless authoritative current sources for the relevant jurisdiction have been checked. <br>
