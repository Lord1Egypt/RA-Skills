SECTION 1: GOOGLE LOCAL SERVICES ADS (LSA) — QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST

Generate a 12-item Google LSA qualification checklist for auto body shops:
- Required licenses: Nevada NV DMV body shop license (NRS 487); business license (Clark County); NV contractor's bond if applicable
- Required insurance: General liability ($1M minimum); workers' compensation (required if any employees in Nevada); garage keepers' liability (covers customer vehicles on premises)
- Background check: Google LSA requires background checks for technicians who go to customer locations (mobile PDR)
- Required profile elements: physical address; service area (specify zip codes, not "all of Las Vegas"); hours; photos (shop exterior, certifications, sample work); response time
- Prohibited in LSA: review gating; undisclosed incentives; false credential claims
- I-CAR/certification display: "I-CAR Gold Class" (not "certified"); include gold class logo (licensed use required)
- Bid strategy for body shops: "maximize leads" for high-volume months; "target CPA" at $150-200/lead for shops with strong close rates

## Budget Allocation Notes

For a $2,500/month budget in Las Vegas:
- **Google LSA:** $800/month — highest intent; post-accident search captures insurance-ready buyers
- **Google Search (RSA):** $900/month — branded + collision + insurance keywords; bidding on "DRP" terms if independent shop
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):** $600/month — hail season awareness + retargeting
- **GBP:** Free — but requires consistent posting; the 30-day calendar handles this

Higher spend on Google LSA is justified for collision repair because:
1. Post-accident buyers search immediately (Google, not social)
2. Insurance timeline creates urgency — rental car clock starts at accident
3. Average repair ticket ($4,200) makes $150-200 cost-per-lead extremely profitable
