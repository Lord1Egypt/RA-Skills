### The 7 Moats

**Moat 1 — SCR 192 + RPC 7.1 + 11 USC § 528 [ANCHOR]**
Three violations in one ad: no SCR 192 disclaimer, "specialist" without bar certification, missing § 528 "debt relief agency" disclosure. Business bankruptcy attorneys often assume § 528 applies only to consumer debtors — courts in the 9th Circuit have applied it broadly. This skill enforces all three in every output.

**Moat 2 — Subchapter V vs. Standard Chapter 11 (SBRA 2019) [SHARPEST]**
80% of Nevada small businesses qualify for Subchapter V (≤ $7.5M non-contingent debt): no disclosure statement, no creditors' committee, modified absolute priority rule, 60–80% lower professional fees than standard Chapter 11. No AI tool mentions it. An attorney marketing only standard Chapter 11 is driving away their most common client profile. This skill evaluates Sub V eligibility in every engagement recommendation.

**Moat 3 — § 547 Preference Actions [SHARPEST #2]**
AI tools routinely say "pay your vendors before filing." That's a preference action target — the trustee claws it back and the client pays to defend it. 90-day lookback for non-insiders, 1-year for insiders. This skill flags preference exposure in every campaign and teaches clients why pre-filing payment strategy requires legal analysis, not common sense.

**Moat 4 — § 365 Executory Contracts: 120-Day Deadline**
"Walk away from bad leases" — true, but the 120-day deadline (§ 365(d)(4)), assumption cure requirement, and § 502(b)(6) rejection damage cap make this far more complex. This skill explains the actual process and timeline in every lease-related output.

**Moat 5 — NRS 78/86 Nevada Entity Law Intersection**
Revoked Nevada articles = Chapter 11 dismissal risk. LLC manager fiduciary duty shifts toward creditors in zone of insolvency. NRS 112.180 UFTA extends fraudulent transfer lookback to 4 years. No AI tool intersects state entity law with federal bankruptcy law. This skill does.

**Moat 6 — § 1129(b) Cram-Down and Absolute Priority Rule**
"Keep your equity" is only true in Subchapter V or if all unsecured creditors consent or are paid in full. The absolute priority rule is the most misrepresented concept in business bankruptcy marketing. This skill explains which rule applies and why it matters for plan strategy.

**Moat 7 — § 363 Cash Collateral and § 364 DIP Financing**
"Continue operating your business" ignores § 363(c)(2) — the prohibition on using secured-collateral cash without court order. Day 1 without a cash collateral motion = no payroll. This skill explains first-day emergency motions in every operations-related output.
