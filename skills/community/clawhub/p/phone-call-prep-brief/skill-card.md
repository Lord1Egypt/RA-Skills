## Description: <br>
Build a one-page call brief before an AI agent or human places or takes an important phone call — objective, talking points, consent/disclosure checklist, voicemail script, and fallback plan. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users, employees, and agent operators use this skill to prepare concise call briefs, talking points, voicemail scripts, recording-disclosure language, and pre-dial checklists before important outbound, inbound, or callback phone calls. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Recording-consent or regulated-call language may be incomplete for the user's jurisdiction or industry. <br>
Mitigation: Review generated consent language before use and seek qualified review for regulated, high-stakes, or location-specific calls. <br>
Risk: Users may include sensitive account, payment, medical, identity, or credential details while preparing the call. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid entering sensitive numbers or secrets; redact unnecessary details and keep only the minimum context needed for the call. <br>
Risk: A thin prompt can lead to invented dates, prices, commitments, or permissions in the call brief. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm assumptions, must-cover items, and do-not-say boundaries before the call starts. <br>
