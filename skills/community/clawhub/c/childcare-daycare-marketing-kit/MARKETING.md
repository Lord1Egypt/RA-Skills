## Why Compliance Is the Differentiator

Childcare is one of the most legally exposed marketing categories:

1. **COPPA (1998, updated 2013, updated 2024)**: FTC can fine operators up to $51,744 per COPPA violation. Running ads on child-directed platforms = exposure. Most childcare owners don't know which ad placements trigger COPPA.

2. **State Childcare Licensing**: 48 states require display of license number in advertising. Most childcare marketing tools generate "licensed and insured" copy without the number.

3. **NAEYC Accreditation**: NAEYC's Standards and Criteria explicitly prohibit misrepresenting accreditation status. Centers citing lapsed accreditation can be reported to NAEYC and lose future accreditation eligibility.

4. **FTC Endorsement Guides (2023)**: Testimonials showing exceptional child outcomes ("reading at 2!") without typical-results disclosure = violation.

5. **CACFP**: USDA CACFP explicitly prohibits advertising participation without confirmed enrollment.

The typical childcare director is a trained educator, not a marketer. They copy-paste from other centers' websites, often inheriting compliance problems. This skill fixes that at generation time.
