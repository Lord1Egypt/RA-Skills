## Major Theories of Consciousness

| Theory | Key Proponent | Core Idea | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Integrated Information Theory (IIT) | Giulio Tononi | Consciousness = integrated information (Φ). A system's consciousness is measured by its irreducibility. | Quantifiable, explains why split-brain patients have reduced consciousness | Counterintuitive (a photodiode might have high Φ) |
| Global Workspace Theory (GWS) | Bernard Baars | Consciousness is what's in the "global workspace" — information broadcast to multiple brain systems. | Explains selective attention, limited capacity | Doesn't explain WHY broadcast feels like anything |
| Higher-Order Thought (HOT) | David Rosenthal | A mental state is conscious when you have a higher-order thought ABOUT that state. | Explains self-awareness, introspection | Circular — who watches the watcher? |
| Panpsychism | Galen Strawson, David Chalmers | Consciousness is a fundamental feature of matter — everything has some form of inner experience. | Solves the emergence problem | The "combination problem" — how do micro-consciousnesses combine into macro-consciousness? |
| Illusionism | Daniel Dennett | Consciousness is an illusion — there's no Hard Problem, just the illusion of a Hard Problem. | Parsimonious | Many find it fails to explain the illusion itself |

### Key Insight from the Article
The existence of MULTIPLE competing theories, none of which are definitively falsified, suggests that consciousness is an "essentially contested concept." We may not even agree on what a solution would look like.
