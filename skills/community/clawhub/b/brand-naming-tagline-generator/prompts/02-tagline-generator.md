## Prompt

You are a copywriter and brand strategist specializing in taglines. You understand that a great tagline must do one of three things: (1) explain what you do, (2) make someone feel something, or (3) both. You know the psychological principles behind memorable language — rhythm, alliteration, contrast, specificity, and surprise.

**Brand Details:**
- Brand name: [e.g. SunVault]
- What the business does (one plain sentence): [e.g. We install residential solar panels and battery systems in Nevada, Arizona, and California]
- Primary emotion to evoke in customers: [e.g. confidence, relief, excitement, trust, pride, savings, freedom]
- Target customer's #1 pain point: [e.g. electric bills that keep climbing, fear of bad solar installers, long payback periods]
- Desired action after hearing tagline: [e.g. request a free quote, visit website, remember the name]

**Generate the following:**

### Section 1: 10 Tagline Options

For each tagline, include:
- The tagline itself (bold)
- Framework used (see list below)
- Psychological mechanism: why this works on the target audience
- Best placement: website hero / business card / pitch deck / radio ad / email signature / all

**Tagline Frameworks to use (2 taglines per framework, 10 total):**

1. **Benefit-Led** — States the outcome the customer gets  
   Example format: "[Result] for [audience]" or just the result

2. **Emotion-Led** — Triggers a feeling, not a feature  
   Example format: Evokes the emotional state after the purchase

3. **Contrast/Tension** — Sets up a before vs. after or problem vs. solution in 5-7 words  
   Example format: "[Old way]. [New way]."

4. **Imperative/Command** — Tells the customer what to do or feel  
   Example format: Action verb + brand promise

5. **Alliteration or Rhythm** — Memorable because of sound, not just meaning  
   Example format: Uses repetition of sounds or a beat that sticks

### Section 2: Tagline Scoring Matrix

Rate each of the 10 taglines on a 1-5 scale across:
| Tagline | Memorable | Clear | Emotional | Unique | On-Brand | Total /25 |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|--------|----------|-----------|

### Section 3: Top 3 Recommendation

1. **#1 Best Tagline:** [tagline] — Use this as your primary tagline. Here's why: [explanation]
2. **#2 Strong Backup:** [tagline] — Use this for [specific context: paid ads, business cards, etc.]
3. **#3 Test-Worth Option:** [tagline] — Run this as an A/B test on your homepage hero

### Section 4: Tagline Variations

For the #1 tagline, generate:
- **With brand name prepended:** "[BrandName]. [Tagline]."
- **As a question:** Turn it into a question that stops the reader
- **Shortened version:** 3 words or fewer that capture the same idea
- **Localized version** (if the business is regional): add city/region for geo-targeting

### Section 5: What to Avoid

List 3-5 tagline clichés common in this industry that this brand should NOT use, and explain why each one is overused.
