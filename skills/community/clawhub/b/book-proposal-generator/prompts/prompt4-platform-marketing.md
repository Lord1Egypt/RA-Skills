# Prompt 4: Author Platform + Marketing Plan + Publisher Pitch Email

**Run this last. This section convinces agents you can actually sell the book.**

---

## THE PROMPT

```
You are a literary agent at a top-tier agency who specializes in business, self-help, and narrative nonfiction. You know that in today's market, publishers want authors with platforms — not because the author will do all the marketing, but because platform signals credibility and a built-in first reader community.

I need you to write the Author Platform, Marketing Plan, and Query Letter sections of my nonfiction book proposal.

**About Me:**
- Name and title: [Your name and professional title]
- Book title: [From Prompt 1]
- Core expertise: [1-3 sentences on your professional background]

**My Current Platform (fill in real numbers — agents verify these):**
- Email list: [subscribers, open rate if known]
- Newsletter: [name, frequency, subscriber count]
- Social media:
  - LinkedIn: [followers, post frequency]
  - Twitter/X: [followers]
  - Instagram: [followers]
  - TikTok: [followers]
  - YouTube: [subscribers]
- Podcast (if you host one): [name, episodes, avg downloads/episode]
- Speaking: [number of paid talks/year, typical audience size, top 3 venues/conferences]
- Media appearances: [podcasts you've appeared on, articles written, press features]
- Online courses or programs: [names, number of students if applicable]
- Community: [Discord, Circle, Facebook Group — name, member count]
- Other notable credibility: [awards, board positions, advisory roles, major clients]

**Comparable Authors (whose audience overlaps with my target reader):**
1. [Author name] — [their platform strength — e.g., 500K LinkedIn, 3 NYT bestsellers]
2. [Author name] — [their platform strength]
3. [Author name] — [their platform strength]

**My Marketing Capabilities (what I will actually do — be specific):**
- Podcast tour: [how many shows you'll pitch, do you have existing relationships?]
- Newsletter announcement: [your list, partner newsletters you can guest in]
- Speaking: [upcoming conferences or events where you'll promote the book]
- Partnerships: [companies, associations, or communities that would promote this book]
- Course integration: [do you have a course where this book becomes required reading?]
- Corporate sales: [bulk purchase opportunities — speaking clients, associations]

Please produce:

1. **AUTHOR BIO (3 versions)**
   
   **Short Bio (50 words):** For agent query email signature and back-of-book
   
   **Medium Bio (150 words):** For speaker introduction and podcast appearances — reads conversationally, written in third person
   
   **Proposal Bio (250-300 words):** For the formal proposal document. Includes full credentials, speaking history, media, platform stats, and why this specific book is the natural output of this specific career

2. **AUTHOR PLATFORM SECTION (200-250 words)**
   Present your platform as an asset, not a credential list. Format:
   - Lead with your strongest number or your most impressive "proof point" (a talk that went viral, a newsletter with 40% open rates, an episode that hit #1 in its category)
   - Quantify everything you can
   - Show audience overlap with the book's target reader
   - End with a sentence about your content cadence (how often you publish, how engaged your audience is)
   
   Write this to answer the question publishers actually ask: "If we publish this book, where will the first 500 buyers come from?"

3. **MARKETING PLAN (300-350 words)**
   
   Structure in three phases:
   
   **Pre-Publication (12-6 months before launch):**
   - ARC (Advance Reader Copy) strategy: Who will you ask for endorsements and early reviews?
   - Podcast tour: How many appearances can you book before launch? Which shows specifically?
   - Newsletter: How will you build anticipation with your existing list?
   - Pre-order campaign: What incentive will you offer?
   
   **Launch Month:**
   - Press targets: 3-5 specific publications or journalists you have relationships with
   - Speaking engagements: Any keynotes or events timed to launch?
   - Social campaign: Platform-specific launch strategy
   - Bulk sales: Corporate, association, or community bulk purchase opportunities
   - Launch team: Size and engagement plan
   
   **Post-Launch (ongoing):**
   - Evergreen content: How will the book fuel your content machine long-term?
   - Course integration: How does the book become a course entry point?
   - Speaker bureau: Does this book position you for higher speaking fees?
   - International rights: Any translation opportunities based on your platform?

4. **QUERY LETTER (250-300 words — EXACTLY)**
   
   This is the email agents receive first. It must make them request the full proposal.
   Format:
   - Paragraph 1 (3-4 sentences): Hook — the book's title, category, word count, and one-sentence premise. No pleasantries. Drop them into the problem.
   - Paragraph 2 (3-5 sentences): What the book is about — the key argument, the narrative thread, the transformation. Make it urgent and specific.
   - Paragraph 3 (2-3 sentences): Why this book now — the cultural moment, the gap in the market.
   - Paragraph 4 (2-3 sentences): Author credentials — who you are and why you're qualified. Platform numbers only if strong (10K+ email list, 50K+ followers).
   - Closing (1-2 sentences): "I'd be happy to share the full proposal at your request. Thank you for your time."
   
   Tone: Professional, confident, direct. No exclamation points. No "I've always dreamed of writing a book." No plot synopsis language. Treat it like a B2B pitch.

5. **PUBLISHER PITCH EMAIL (150-200 words)**
   For authors who have a referral into a specific editor (bypassing agents). More direct than a query letter:
   - Open with the referral: "[Name] suggested I reach out directly..."
   - Book + category + word count in sentence 1
   - Core premise in 2-3 sentences
   - Platform highlights (2-3 most impressive stats)
   - The ask: "I have a complete proposal — would you like to take a look?"
   - Clean close

Format everything as a professional proposal document section, ready to paste and submit.
```

---

## Pro Tips

- **On platform:** If your numbers are small, emphasize quality and engagement over size. A 2,000-person list with 50% open rates is more impressive to an agent than 20,000 with 8%.
- **On the query letter:** Keep it under 300 words. Agents receive hundreds per week. Brevity is respect.
- **On the marketing plan:** The pre-order campaign and launch team are the two things publishers specifically look for. If you don't have a plan for both, they'll have doubts.
- **On bulk sales:** If you speak to corporations, associations, or run training programs — mention this. A 500-copy bulk order on day one changes the economics of publishing.
- **On the endorsement strategy:** Start collecting endorsement commitments before you even submit. Agents love seeing "[Notable name] has agreed to endorse" in a proposal.
