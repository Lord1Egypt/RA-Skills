## Prompt

You are a senior copy editor. Using the Brand Voice Profile and style preferences above, generate a complete **Writing Style Rules & Grammar Guide** with the following sections:

**Section 1: The 20 Writing Rules**
Create exactly 20 rules organized into categories:

*Sentence & Paragraph Structure (Rules 1–5)*
- Ideal sentence length range
- Paragraph length guideline
- When to use short sentences for impact
- Active vs. passive voice rule
- Rule about sentence variety

*Punctuation & Formatting (Rules 6–10)*
- Oxford comma ruling
- Em dash, en dash, ellipsis policy
- Exclamation point policy
- Capitalization rules (headers, job titles, product names)
- Bullet point formatting rules

*Numbers, Dates & Data (Rules 11–13)*
- Number formatting rule
- Date/time formatting
- Statistics and data presentation

*Tone Consistency (Rules 14–17)*
- Reading level target (name the Flesch-Kincaid level)
- Humor guideline (when/how much)
- Empathy language guideline
- CTA language guideline

*Voice-Specific Rules (Rules 18–20)*
- Brand's signature opening construction (how we start emails, posts, articles)
- One structural pattern to always use
- One pattern to always avoid

For each rule: state the rule, then give a DO example and a DON'T example.

**Section 2: Brand Vocabulary**
Three lists:
- **Preferred terms** (15 words/phrases we use): word → why we use it / what it signals
- **Banned terms** (15 words/phrases we never use): word → what to say instead
- **Jargon policy**: which industry terms are acceptable, which need definitions, which to avoid entirely

**Section 3: Headline Writing Formula**
The brand's preferred headline formula for:
- Blog posts / articles
- Email subject lines
- Social media posts
- Ad headlines

For each: the formula + 3 example headlines.

**Section 4: Quick Reference Card**
A single-page summary with:
- The 5 personality adjectives (from Prompt 1)
- The 5 most important style rules (your picks from the 20)
- 5 example sentences that nail the voice
- 3 warning signs that copy is off-brand
