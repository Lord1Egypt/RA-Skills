# Unboxing Experience Quality Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate the completeness and quality of an unboxing experience design before production. Each item should be confirmed with a pass, fail, or not-applicable designation. A production-ready unboxing experience should pass at minimum 80% of applicable items.

---

## Brand Alignment (5 items)

- [ ] Color palette, logo placement, and typography match brand guidelines
- [ ] Tone of voice on all printed copy matches brand voice guidelines
- [ ] Overall aesthetic is consistent with the brand's website and digital presence
- [ ] Target customer preferences are reflected in material and design choices
- [ ] Packaging investment matches the brand's price point positioning

## Packaging Layers (6 items)

- [ ] Layer count is appropriate for AOV and category; each layer has a defined purpose
- [ ] Product is adequately protected (drop test, shake test, compression test passed)
- [ ] Box is right-sized with minimal excess void space
- [ ] Opening sequence is intuitive and builds anticipation progressively
- [ ] Packaging can be assembled by fulfillment staff in under 90 seconds per unit
- [ ] Packaging survives standard carrier handling without damage

## Insert Cards (7 items)

- [ ] Single dominant primary CTA with clear visual hierarchy across all CTAs
- [ ] Copy is concise, on-brand, and error-free
- [ ] QR code is present (min 1"x1"), links to mobile-optimized page with sub-3s load time
- [ ] Tracking parameters (UTM, unique codes) are embedded in QR URLs
- [ ] At least two card variants exist for A/B testing with a defined test plan
- [ ] Paper stock and finish are specified with vendor-confirmed availability
- [ ] Card dimensions fit within the package without bending or being hidden

## Social Sharing (6 items)

- [ ] Branded hashtag is confirmed unique and not co-opted by unrelated content
- [ ] Platform-specific sharing prompts are tailored per platform
- [ ] UGC incentive is defined and redemption mechanism is tested and functioning
- [ ] QR code leads to pre-filled or template-assisted sharing flow
- [ ] UGC monitoring plan is in place with assigned owner
- [ ] Hashtag and prompts are printed in legible font on packaging or insert

## Sensory Elements (5 items)

- [ ] At least one intentional sensory element beyond visual design is included
- [ ] Each sensory element is justified by impact on perceived value relative to cost
- [ ] Tactile and auditory elements are specified with vendor samples approved
- [ ] Olfactory elements (if any) are confirmed non-allergenic and product-compatible
- [ ] Sensory design is documented for fulfillment team execution

## Sustainability (6 items)

- [ ] All materials assessed for recyclability or compostability with disposal instructions printed
- [ ] Mixed materials minimized; packaging is easy to separate for recycling
- [ ] Plastic use eliminated or minimized with documented justification
- [ ] Paper and cardboard are FSC-certified or verified recycled content; inks are soy/vegetable-based
- [ ] Box sizing optimized to reduce shipping weight and dimensional waste
- [ ] Sustainability story is communicated to the customer on packaging

## Cost Management (5 items)

- [ ] Per-unit cost calculated and documented for every component
- [ ] Packaging cost as percentage of AOV falls within target range for brand tier
- [ ] Cost comparison (current vs. proposed) documented with deltas
- [ ] ROI model projects value from social sharing, reviews, and repeat purchases
- [ ] Volume pricing negotiated with break points documented for scaling

## Photography and Video Readiness (5 items)

- [ ] Packaging photographs well in natural and studio lighting without glare or color shifts
- [ ] Flat-lay arrangement of all elements creates a visually appealing composition
- [ ] Unboxing sequence creates 3-5 natural photo or video moments
- [ ] Brand name and hashtag are visible in natural camera angles
- [ ] Complete unboxing can be filmed in 30-60 seconds for short-form video

---

## Scoring Guide

**Total items**: 50

| Score | Rating | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 43-50 passed | Excellent | Ready for production |
| 35-42 passed | Good | Address gaps before production |
| 25-34 passed | Needs Work | Significant iteration required |
| Below 25 passed | Incomplete | Return to design phase |

### Fix Priority Order

1. **Product protection** -- non-negotiable for shipping
2. **Brand alignment** -- misaligned packaging harms more than generic
3. **Insert card CTA clarity** -- highest-ROI element
4. **Cost management** -- unsustainable costs force later cuts
5. **Social sharing mechanics** -- must work before launch
6. **Sensory elements** -- can be phased in
7. **Photography readiness** -- adjustable post-production
8. **Sustainability** -- improve progressively
