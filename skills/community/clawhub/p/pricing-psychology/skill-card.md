## Description: <br>
Apply cognitive biases and strategic pricing principles to design pricing pages, tier structures, proposals, and offers that improve conversion and perceived value. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Entrepreneurs, marketers, product teams, and sales operators use this skill to frame prices, compare pricing models, design subscription tiers, prepare proposals, and evaluate pricing pages. It is for pricing perception and conversion strategy, not cost accounting, commodity pricing, or fixed regulatory rate schedules. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Pricing recommendations may encourage anchors, scarcity, urgency, or social-proof claims that are misleading if the underlying claim is not true. <br>
Mitigation: Review generated recommendations for truthful anchors, real scarcity or urgency, and accurate customer, install, or ROI claims before use. <br>
Risk: Pricing psychology guidance can conflict with advertising, consumer-protection, industry, or regional pricing rules. <br>
Mitigation: Have responsible business or legal reviewers confirm compliance before publishing pricing pages, promotions, proposals, or urgency claims. <br>
Risk: The skill focuses on perceived value and conversion, not margins, costs, commodity pricing, or fixed rate schedules. <br>
Mitigation: Validate any suggested price against unit economics, cost accounting, competitive context, and applicable regulated pricing requirements. <br>
