# Economic Damages Calculation Memo

**Domain:** Forensic Accounting · Litigation Consulting · Economic Damages
**Platforms:** Claude · Codex

## Purpose

Drives an AICPA SSFS No.1- and Daubert-aligned economic damages workflow that turns engagement facts, pleadings, and financial records into a DRAFT calculation memo. The memo frames the cause of action and damages theory, anchors the loss period to the alleged wrongful act and the mitigation cut-off, selects and parametrizes the damages method (before-and-after, yardstick / benchmark, market-share / market-model, sales-projection, cost-plus, hypothetical negotiation for IP, life-care plan for personal injury), distinguishes variable from fixed costs, captures mitigation and other offsets, documents discount-rate and prejudgment-interest treatment, addresses tax treatment, and exposes a source-document index — built so the engagement partner, retaining counsel, opposing counsel, and the trier of fact can follow every number to its source. The output is always a DRAFT for engagement-partner and counsel review — never an executed expert report, never legal advice.

## When to Use

- Breach-of-contract lost-profits damages (commercial disputes, supply-chain disruption, distributor termination)
- Business interruption damages (insurance dispute or tort) — extra expense and lost gross earnings under the policy or at common law
- Intellectual-property infringement damages — lost profits, reasonable royalty (Georgia-Pacific hypothetical negotiation), unjust enrichment / disgorgement, statutory damages
- Wrongful-termination / employment-discrimination / wrongful-death back-pay and front-pay
- Personal-injury earnings-loss and life-care-plan damages support
- Post-acquisition / earn-out / shareholder-oppression / partner-dispute damages
- Marshalling damages exhibits and working memos to support a Rule 26 expert report or trial testimony

## What It Does

1. Collects role (CPA, ABV, CFF, CFE, MAFF, financial analyst working under a credentialed expert), matter reference (case caption code only, no PII / privileged data), matter type, jurisdiction, governing law, trier of fact (judge, jury, arbitration panel, mediator), pleadings available, retention type (consulting vs testifying), and deadlines (expert disclosure, deposition, hearing) through one-question-at-a-time intake
2. Anchors the cause(s) of action to the pleadings, the alleged wrongful act, the legal damages standard (reasonable-certainty, foreseeability, mitigation, but-for causation, hypothetical-negotiation framework for IP), and the loss period (start: date of breach / injury; end: trial, judgment, mitigation, contract term)
3. Captures the damages theory and selects method(s) per element (before-and-after, yardstick / benchmark, market-share / market-model, sales-projection, cost-plus, hypothetical negotiation, statutory, capitalized-earnings, lost-business-value)
4. Builds the incremental-cost analysis — separating variable from fixed costs, identifying avoided costs as offsets to lost revenue, and reconciling to contribution margin rather than gross profit
5. Captures mitigation and other offsets — substitute sales, replacement employment, salvage, insurance recoveries, contractual cure, set-off
6. Documents the discount-rate / present-value framework (risk-free + risk premium; WACC for going-concern lost-business-value; Norfolk & Western framework for personal-injury earnings) and prejudgment-interest treatment per the governing law
7. Addresses tax treatment — Norfolk & Western lost-net-of-tax for personal injury and wrongful death; gross-up for back-pay where the jurisdiction allows; tax-affecting decisions for closely-held businesses
8. Builds the source-document index — every input traced to a Bates-stamped production document, deposition citation, public filing, or industry source
9. Runs a Daubert / Kumho reliability self-check (relevance, fit, methodology, error rate / sensitivity, peer review or general acceptance, reasonable-certainty, no ipse dixit) and an SSFS No.1 self-check (objectivity, professional standards, communication, retention)
10. Outputs a complete DRAFT damages memo with assumption-and-limitation register, sensitivity scenarios, an unsigned engagement-partner sign-off block, and a verbatim "draft for engagement-partner and counsel review" banner

## Notes

This skill produces a **DRAFT damages calculation memo** for engagement-partner and retaining-counsel review. It is not an executed expert report, not Rule 26 disclosure, not deposition or trial testimony, and not legal advice. The drafting agent is never the testifying expert, never the engagement partner, and never counsel of record. Privileged communications, attorney work product, deposition transcripts, and produced documents must be handled per the engagement's protective order; the working memo uses a case caption code, exhibit identifiers, and Bates ranges only. Final memo and any related expert report require engagement-partner sign-off, counsel review, and (when testifying) the credentialed expert's signature.
