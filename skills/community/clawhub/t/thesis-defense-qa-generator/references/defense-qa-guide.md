## Core Function

The purpose of this skill is to help the user anticipate defense questions and prepare defensible answers. A strong defense answer shows that the student knows:

- What they did
- Why they did it
- How they did it
- What they found
- What the findings mean
- What the study can and cannot claim

The final oral defense usually examines the whole thesis, but it places more emphasis on the results and interpretation than on the literature rationale. A proposal or opening defense usually focuses more heavily on the research plan and methodology.

## Language Matching

Use the same dominant language as the thesis:

- If most of the thesis is Chinese, write all questions and answers in Chinese.
- If most of the thesis is English, write all questions and answers in English.
- If the thesis is bilingual, use the language requested by the user; otherwise use the language of the main body.
- Keep technical terms consistent with the thesis. For example, if the thesis uses "self-efficacy", do not randomly switch to another translation unless clarifying.

When the user asks in a different language from the thesis, prioritize the user's explicit request. If no explicit request is made, match the thesis.

## Evidence Boundaries

Answers must be grounded in the user's document. Do not invent:

- Sample size, participants, instruments, scales, or interviewees
- Statistical tests, p values, coefficients, or findings
- Qualitative themes, quotations, or case details
- Literature, author names, or publication years
- Contributions, limitations, or implications not supported by the paper

If the thesis content is incomplete, mark the answer as provisional:

> 参考回答：根据目前提供的内容，可以回答为……。但这一回答需要结合论文中关于……的具体内容进一步补充。

English:

> Reference answer: Based on the materials currently provided, I would answer that .... This answer should be revised after confirming the thesis section on ....

## Proposal Defense vs Final Defense

### Proposal or Opening Defense

Likely focus:

- Why this topic matters
- Research problem and literature gap
- Theoretical framework
- Research questions or hypotheses
- Feasibility of the design
- Sampling and participant recruitment
- Instruments and their validity/reliability
- Interview, observation, or document-analysis procedures
- Data analysis plan
- Ethics, confidentiality, and risk control
- Limitations before implementation

Common committee logic: when they approve a proposal, they are approving the method as adequate for answering the research questions.

### Final Oral Defense

Likely focus:

- What the student did
- What was found
- What the findings mean
- How findings relate to theory and literature
- Why certain results were unexpected or non-significant
- Whether conclusions are supported by data
- Practical implications
- Limitations and future research
- Revisions and document quality

The final defense normally covers the whole document, but literature and method are often reviewed more briefly than results and discussion.

## Question Categories

Generate questions from the following categories as relevant to the thesis.

### Topic and Motivation

- Why did you choose this topic?
- What is the practical or theoretical significance of this study?
- What problem does your thesis attempt to solve?
- Why is this topic suitable for an undergraduate/master's/doctoral thesis?
- What is the core contribution of your study?

### Literature Review

- What are the main streams of research in this area?
- What gap in the literature does your thesis address?
- How is your study different from previous studies?
- Which studies most directly influenced your design?
- Why did you choose these concepts/variables/themes?

### Theory or Conceptual Framework

- What theoretical framework supports your study?
- Why is this theory appropriate for your topic?
- How do the variables or themes connect within your framework?
- Did your findings support, extend, or challenge the theory?

### Research Questions and Hypotheses

- How did you formulate your research questions?
- How do your research questions correspond to the method?
- Are your hypotheses supported by the findings?
- If a hypothesis was not supported, how do you explain it?

### Methodology

- Why did you choose this research method?
- Why did you choose this sample or participant group?
- How did you determine the sample size?
- How did you collect the data?
- Why did you use these instruments or interview questions?
- How did you ensure validity and reliability?
- How did you ensure trustworthiness in qualitative analysis?
- How did you analyze the data?
- What are the internal and external validity threats?
- Can your findings be generalized?

### Ethics

- How did you protect participants' privacy?
- Was participation voluntary?
- How did you handle informed consent?
- How did you ensure anonymity and confidentiality?
- Are there any psychological or social risks in your study?

### Results

- What are the most important findings?
- Which finding surprised you most?
- How do the results answer each research question?
- Why were some results non-significant?
- How do you interpret a particular table or figure?
- What do the qualitative themes reveal?

### Discussion and Contribution

- What do your findings mean?
- How do your findings relate to previous literature?
- What is the theoretical contribution?
- What is the practical value?
- What does your study add to the field?

### Limitations and Future Research

- What are the main limitations of your study?
- How would you improve this study if you had more time?
- What future research would you recommend?
- Which variables, groups, or methods should be added later?
- What suggestions from the committee could be placed in future research?

### Document Quality and Revisions

- Why is this table/figure formatted this way?
- Are all references complete and consistent?
- Why did you organize the thesis chapters this way?
- What revisions would you make after the defense?

## Answer Construction

Every answer should be:

- Direct: answer the question first.
- Evidence-based: refer to the thesis content.
- Concise: do not give a lecture.
- Respectful: acknowledge the committee's concern.
- Scientific: use design, data, theory, and literature as the basis.
- Cautious: do not claim more than the data support.

Use this structure:

1. Direct answer.
2. Thesis evidence.
3. Rationale or explanation.
4. Limitation or caution if needed.
5. Closing sentence that returns to the study's scope.

Chinese answer frame:

> 这个问题可以从……方面回答。首先，本文之所以……，是因为……。其次，从论文的研究设计/结果来看，……。需要说明的是，本文的结论主要适用于……情境，因此我在论文中也将……作为研究不足，并建议后续研究……。

English answer frame:

> I would answer this in three points. First, the reason I chose ... is that .... Second, the design/results of the thesis show that .... I should also note that this conclusion is limited to ..., so I treat ... as a limitation and suggest ... for future research.

## Methodology Questions

Methodology questions are common because the committee wants to know whether the method can answer the research questions.

### Sampling

Question:

> 你为什么选择这个样本？样本是否具有代表性？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究选择……作为样本，主要是因为……与研究问题直接相关。样本获取采用……方式，能够满足本研究对……群体的考察需要。需要说明的是，由于样本来源主要集中在……，其代表性存在一定限制，因此本文结论更适合解释……情境，不能简单推广到所有……群体。

### Instrument Validity and Reliability

Question:

> 你如何保证问卷/量表的信度和效度？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究使用的……量表来源于……，已有研究表明其具有较好的信效度。在本研究中，我也对量表进行了……检验，结果显示……。因此，该工具基本能够满足本研究测量……变量的需要。当然，受样本范围和自陈式测量影响，仍可能存在一定偏差。

If the user created their own instrument, ask for or generate questions about pilot study, expert review, item revision, and reliability/validity testing.

### Qualitative Trustworthiness

Question:

> 你如何保证访谈分析的可信度？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究主要通过……提高质性分析的可信度。具体包括：对访谈文本进行反复阅读和编码，保留编码过程记录；在主题形成过程中结合原始资料进行核对；必要时通过同伴讨论或参与者反馈检查解释是否贴近资料。由于质性研究强调情境理解，本文不追求统计推广，而强调研究发现对相似情境的参考价值。

### Data Analysis

Question:

> 你的数据分析方法为什么适合回答研究问题？

Answer pattern:

> 本文的研究问题主要关注……，因此采用……分析方法。该方法能够检验/呈现……，与研究问题中关于……的关注点相对应。换言之，研究问题、变量/主题和数据分析方法之间是一一对应的。

## Results and Discussion Questions

### Main Finding

Question:

> 你的研究最重要的发现是什么？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究最重要的发现是……。这一发现直接回应了论文的……研究问题。它说明……，并且在实践上提示……。同时，这一发现仍受到……限制，因此需要在后续研究中进一步检验。

### Unexpected Finding

Question:

> 你的结果中有没有与预期不一致的地方？你如何解释？

Answer pattern:

> 有，论文中……结果与最初预期不完全一致。我认为可能有三方面原因：第一，样本特征可能影响了结果；第二，测量工具或资料来源可能存在局限；第三，研究设计本身可能没有充分控制……因素。因此，这一结果需要谨慎解释，也为后续研究提供了进一步探讨的方向。

### Meaning of Findings

Question:

> 这些发现说明了什么？

Answer pattern:

> 这些发现说明……。从理论上看，它支持/补充/修正了……观点；从实践上看，它提示……。不过，本文基于……样本和……方法，因此结论主要是对……情境下……现象的解释。

## Literature and Theory Questions

### Literature Gap

Question:

> 你的论文弥补了什么研究空白？

Answer pattern:

> 现有研究主要关注……，但对……的讨论相对不足。本文尝试从……角度切入，使用……方法分析……，因此在一定程度上补充了已有研究对……群体/情境/机制的认识。

### Theory Choice

Question:

> 你为什么选择这个理论作为理论基础？

Answer pattern:

> 我选择……理论，是因为该理论能够解释……。论文的核心问题是……，而该理论中的……概念与本研究的……变量/主题具有较强对应关系。因此，它可以为研究问题提出、变量关系解释和结果讨论提供支撑。

## Ethics, Validity, and Trustworthiness Questions

### Confidentiality

Question:

> 你如何保护研究对象的隐私？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究在数据收集前向参与者说明研究目的、参与方式和资料使用范围，并强调自愿参与。数据整理时使用匿名编号替代个人信息，论文呈现中不披露可识别身份的信息，原始资料也仅用于本研究分析。

### Generalization

Question:

> 你的结论能推广到其他群体吗？

Answer pattern:

> 需要谨慎。由于本研究样本主要来自……，结论首先适用于……群体或相似情境。对于其他地区、年龄段或背景群体，仍需后续研究进一步验证。因此，我更倾向于将本研究结论表述为对……情境的经验解释，而不是普遍性结论。

## Limitations and Future Research Questions

### Main Limitations

Question:

> 你的研究最大的不足是什么？

Answer pattern:

> 本研究的主要不足包括……。其中最核心的是……，因为它可能影响……。因此，在论文中我将其作为研究不足，并建议未来研究通过……加以改进。

### Future Research

Question:

> 如果继续做这个研究，你会如何改进？

Answer pattern:

> 如果继续推进，我会从三个方面改进：第一，扩大样本范围或增加样本量；第二，优化研究方法，例如加入访谈/纵向追踪/实验设计；第三，进一步纳入……变量或情境因素。这样可以更全面地解释……。

## Difficult Committee Questions

Use these strategies when generating answers for challenging questions.

### If the Committee Mentions an Article the Student Has Not Read

Response:

> 谢谢老师提醒。关于您提到的这篇文献，我目前还没有系统阅读，因此不适合直接评价其结论。会后我会补充阅读这篇文献，并根据其与本文主题的相关性，考虑是否在文献综述或讨论部分补充。

English:

> Thank you for pointing that out. I have not read that article closely enough to evaluate it responsibly. I would be glad to read it after the defense and consider whether it should be incorporated into the literature review or discussion.

### If a Suggestion Is Valuable but Beyond the Scope

Response:

> 老师的建议很有价值。由于本文的研究范围主要限定在……，如果在当前研究中完全展开，可能会超出本文设计。对此，我可以在"未来研究展望"中补充这一方向，作为后续进一步研究的内容。

### If the Student Disagrees with a Suggested Procedure

Response:

> 我理解老师的建议。本文之所以采用目前的处理方式，主要基于以下考虑：第一，……；第二，……；第三，……。当然，老师提出的方法也能从另一个角度强化研究，后续研究可以进一步比较不同处理方式对结果的影响。

### If the Committee Points Out an Easy Revision

Response:

> 老师指出得很准确。这个修改可以使论文表述/表格/逻辑更加清楚，我会在答辩后按照建议进行修改。

### If Asked About an Older Draft Issue Already Revised

Response:

> 这个问题我在修改稿中已经进行了补充，主要体现在第……页/第……节。具体来说，我增加了……，以回应此前关于……的意见。

### If a Comment Is Long but Not a Clear Question

Response:

> 谢谢老师，这一点很重要。您的意见提示我可以在论文的……部分进一步加强说明。

Do not interrupt long comments. Acknowledge the point respectfully and respond only to the actionable part.

## Chinese Templates

### Opening Summary for Q&A

> 根据论文内容，答辩问题很可能集中在选题依据、研究方法、结果解释、研究不足和实践启示几个方面。以下问题和回答均以论文已有内容为基础，回答时建议保持简洁、尊重和证据导向。

### General Answer Template

> 这个问题可以从三个方面回答。第一，……。第二，……。第三，……。因此，本文的处理方式与研究目的和研究设计是相匹配的。需要说明的是，……也是本文的一个限制，后续研究可以进一步完善。

### Scope Boundary Template

> 这个问题很有意义，但它已经部分超出了本文的研究范围。本文主要关注……，因此没有进一步展开……。我会在后续修改中将这一点放入未来研究展望，说明后续可以从……角度继续深入。

## English Templates

### Opening Summary for Q&A

> Based on the thesis, the defense questions are likely to focus on the rationale for the topic, the research design, interpretation of findings, limitations, and practical implications. The following answers are grounded in the thesis and should be delivered concisely and respectfully.

### General Answer Template

> I would answer this in three points. First, .... Second, .... Third, .... Therefore, the choice made in the thesis is consistent with the research purpose and design. I should also acknowledge that ... is a limitation, and future research could address it by ....

### Scope Boundary Template

> That is an important point, but it goes beyond the scope of the current thesis. This study focuses on ..., so it does not fully examine .... I can add this point to the section on future research and suggest that later studies investigate it through ....

## Revision Checklist

Before finalizing the Q&A set, check:

- Does the output use the same language as the thesis?
- Are the questions tailored to the actual thesis rather than generic only?
- Are answers grounded in the provided document?
- Are missing details marked as needing verification?
- Are methodology questions included?
- Are results and discussion questions emphasized for final defense?
- Are ethics, validity, reliability, or trustworthiness questions covered when relevant?
- Are limitations handled honestly?
- Are difficult committee scenarios included?
- Are answers concise enough for oral delivery?
- Are unsupported claims, invented citations, or fabricated data avoided?
