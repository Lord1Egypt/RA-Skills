# Incentive Design Guide — Pre-Order Planner

How to design early-bird incentives that maximize waitlist conversion without destroying margin.

## The Incentive Hierarchy

Not all incentives are equal. Ranked by effectiveness:

1. **Exclusivity** — something never available to non-early-buyers (special edition, color, bundle)
2. **Access** — being first (early access, founder status, numbered certificate)
3. **Gift** — a free add-on product included with the order
4. **Discount** — a price reduction
5. **Intangible recognition** — a "Founding Customer" badge, thank-you, or name in credits

Discount alone is the weakest single-incentive option. Combine it with at least one higher-tier incentive.

## Incentive Structure Templates

### Template A — "Founding Customer"
- First [N] orders: 15–20% off + exclusive bundle item + "Founding Customer" designation
- Standard pre-order: 10% off + guaranteed stock
- Works well for: First product launch, brand with strong story

### Template B — Tiered Access
- Waitlist Day 1: Access 48 hours before general public, no discount required
- Waitlist Day 2: 10% off pre-order discount
- General launch: Full price
- Works well for: Brands with engaged social audiences that value exclusivity over savings

### Template C — Gift With Purchase
- Pre-order only: Product + bonus item (sample, accessory, or complementary SKU)
- Post-launch: Product only, no bonus
- Works well for: Brands with complementary product lines; bonus item should have high perceived value, low COGS

### Template D — Early-Bird Flash (Creator Model)
- First 24 hours only: Deepest discount (25–30% off)
- Hours 25–72: Medium discount (15% off)
- After 72 hours: 5% off or standard price
- Works well for: Creators and influencers with highly engaged audiences used to "drops"

## Quantity Limit Strategy

Setting a quantity cap on early-bird tiers creates real urgency:

- **Tight cap** (50–100 units): Sells out fast, generates social proof ("Sold out in 2 hours"). Risk: alienates latecomers.
- **Medium cap** (200–500 units): Sustainable conversion window of 2–5 days. Best for most DTC brands.
- **Open cap with time limit**: No unit cap but early-bird pricing expires in 72 hours. Removes sellout risk but reduces urgency.

## FOMO Architecture

FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) drivers for pre-orders, ranked:
1. Waitlist counter: "3,847 people are waiting" — show the number prominently
2. Quantity counter: "Only 200 Founding Customer spots available" — update in real time
3. Timer: "Early-bird pricing expires in 47 hours"
4. Sold counter: "127 pre-orders placed today"
5. Social proof: Real customer names/avatars of early buyers (with permission)

## Incentive Economics

Before committing to an incentive structure, model the margin impact:

```
Revenue per unit = Pre-order price
COGS = Manufacturing cost + packaging + 3PL/shipping
Incentive COGS = Discount lost + free gift COGS + special packaging premium
Margin = Revenue - COGS - Incentive COGS

Example:
Launch price: $45
Pre-order price (20% off): $36
Unit COGS: $12
Free gift COGS: $2
Shipping: $5
Margin per Founding Customer unit: $36 - $12 - $2 - $5 = $17 (47%)
```

Compare this to standard price margin: $45 - $12 - $5 = $28 (62%). Decide if the pre-order demand value is worth the 15-point margin compression.

## Platform Restrictions

- **Shopify**: Full support for pre-orders; use "Continue selling when out of stock" or a pre-order app (e.g., Pre-Order Now, PreOrder Me)
- **Amazon**: Pre-orders allowed; ship date must be within 30 days or listing is suppressed
- **TikTok Shop**: Pre-orders via affiliate/creator links to landing page; not natively supported in-app for all sellers
- **Kickstarter/Indiegogo**: Built for pre-orders; best for new inventors, not established DTC brands
