# Prompt: Executive Summary Generator

Generate a professional executive summary for a business plan. The executive summary is the first—and sometimes only—section an investor or lender reads. It must be compelling, concise, and complete.

---

## Your Input

Fill in the brackets below, then run this prompt:

```
Business Name: [e.g., FitTrack]
Industry: [e.g., SaaS / fitness technology]
Problem: [1-2 sentences: what painful problem exists for your target customer?]
Solution: [1-2 sentences: what does your product/service do to solve it?]
Target Customer: [who specifically buys this? e.g., "independent personal trainers in the US"]
Revenue Model: [how do you make money? e.g., "$49/month subscription"]
Stage: [idea | pre-revenue | early revenue (specify MRR if applicable)]
Traction (optional): [users, waitlist, pilots, revenue, press, partnerships]
Funding Ask (optional): [how much are you raising and what for?]
Vision (optional): [where is the company in 5 years?]
```

---

## Prompt

You are an expert business plan writer who has helped 200+ startups raise funding from angels, VCs, and SBA lenders. Write a compelling executive summary for the following business.

**Business:** [Business Name]
**Industry:** [Industry]
**Problem:** [Problem]
**Solution:** [Solution]
**Target Customer:** [Target Customer]
**Revenue Model:** [Revenue Model]
**Stage:** [Stage]
**Traction:** [Traction or "Pre-launch"]
**Funding Ask:** [Funding Ask or "Bootstrapped"]
**Vision:** [Vision or "Omit this section"]

Write a 400–600 word executive summary formatted as a business plan section. Include:

1. **The Problem** — Open with the pain point. Use market data or a vivid scenario to make the problem tangible. (2-3 sentences)

2. **Our Solution** — What [Business Name] does and why it's better than the status quo. Avoid jargon. (2-3 sentences)

3. **Target Market** — Who the customer is, how many of them exist (with a market size estimate), and why now is the right time. (2-3 sentences)

4. **Business Model** — How we make money. Be specific: pricing, transaction size, revenue streams. (2-3 sentences)

5. **Traction** — What we've proven so far (users, revenue, pilots, partnerships, press). If pre-launch, describe validation (waitlist, customer interviews, LOIs). (2-3 sentences)

6. **The Team** — Who is behind this and why we're the right team to execute. Focus on relevant experience and unfair advantages. (2-3 sentences)

7. **The Ask** — If raising: how much, at what valuation/terms (if known), and what the capital will be used for. If bootstrapped: omit this section.

8. **Vision** — One sentence on the long-term impact or scale.

**Tone:** Professional but direct. Confident, not arrogant. Write for a busy investor who skims.  
**Format:** Use the section headers above. Each section is a short paragraph, not bullet points.  
**Do not** include placeholder boilerplate. Make every sentence earn its place.
