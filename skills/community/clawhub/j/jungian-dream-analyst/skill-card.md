## Description: <br>
Provides structured Jungian dream interpretation, archetypal analysis, compensation analysis, and active imagination guidance for reflective dream exploration. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and agents use this skill to analyze dreams through Jungian frameworks, identify archetypal motifs, and generate reflective active-imagination exercises. It is intended for educational and self-reflective use, not therapy, diagnosis, or crisis support. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may over-rely on symbolic dream interpretation as therapy, diagnosis, or crisis support. <br>
Mitigation: Present outputs as reflective and educational, and direct users to qualified professionals or crisis resources for clinical, emergency, or high-distress situations. <br>
Risk: Active-imagination exercises can be destabilizing for users who feel overwhelmed, detached from reality, or in acute distress. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid or stop active-imagination work in those states, keep exercises grounded and optional, and encourage professional support when distress is present. <br>
Risk: Archetypal labels may be experienced as definitive judgments about the user or other people. <br>
Mitigation: Frame interpretations as hypotheses, ask for the user's personal associations, and avoid imposing fixed meanings. <br>
