## Description: <br>
Model product UI actions from user intent questions after a meaningful outcome, completion, created resource, imported data, uploaded file, synced integration, report, deployment, automation, review state, handoff, or workflow milestone. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Product designers, UX practitioners, product managers, and frontend engineers use this skill to decide which post-outcome UI actions to show, hide, group, label, prioritize, defer, or place. It supports workflows where users reach a meaningful completion, generation, import, upload, sync, review, deployment, automation, handoff, or recovery milestone. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Post-outcome action recommendations may be treated as final UX decisions even when the product context has regulatory, data loss, destructive action, or user trust implications. <br>
Mitigation: Review the proposed action model with product, design, and risk owners before shipping workflows that include destructive, irreversible, recovery, approval, publish, export, or handoff actions. <br>
Risk: The skill provides text-only product design guidance and does not inspect live product behavior, user research, analytics, permissions, or implementation constraints. <br>
Mitigation: Validate the proposed labels, scope distinctions, and placements against real user workflows, accessibility requirements, and product permissions before implementation. <br>
