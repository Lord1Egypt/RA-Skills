# tcfd-climate-risk-screening

Guides a sustainability, risk, or finance team through a structured TCFD-aligned climate risk assessment — screening physical and transition risks against IPCC AR6 scenarios, assessing financial materiality, and producing a DRAFT four-pillar TCFD disclosure narrative for board and auditor review.

## Who It's For

- CFOs, sustainability leads, and ESG reporting officers
- Enterprise risk managers and board audit committees
- Climate risk consultants and advisors
- In-house counsel and compliance teams managing IFRS S2, UK TCFD, or CSRD ESRS E1 obligations

## What It Produces

A DRAFT TCFD disclosure package including:

- Physical risk register (acute and chronic hazards across IPCC AR6 1.5°C / 2°C / 4°C scenarios)
- Transition risk register (policy, technology, market, reputational)
- Scenario analysis summary (qualitative or quantitative)
- Financial materiality table (labeled PRELIMINARY — MANAGEMENT ESTIMATE)
- TCFD four-pillar disclosure narrative (Governance / Strategy / Risk Management / Metrics & Targets)
- Open-questions and data gaps list
- Sustainability Lead / Board / Audit Committee Review Block

## What It Does NOT Do

- Conduct asset-level quantitative climate modelling (flags as DATA GAP when needed)
- Certify IFRS S2, CSRD, or TCFD compliance (requires qualified advisors)
- Submit disclosures to any regulatory body

## How It Relates to Other Skills

- For GHG emissions measurement, use `ghg-corporate-inventory-drafter`
- For double materiality assessment under CSRD ESRS, use `double-materiality-assessment`
- This skill covers the climate risk and scenario analysis dimension distinct from emissions accounting and broad materiality
