# Onconephrology

Use this reference for cancer-associated kidney disease, chemotherapy and immunotherapy nephrotoxicity, tumor lysis syndrome, myeloma kidney, anti-VEGF kidney effects, immune checkpoint inhibitors, obstructive uropathy, and medication adjustment in kidney dysfunction.

## Core approach

1. Identify cancer type, treatment regimen, timing of kidney injury, baseline kidney function, urinalysis, proteinuria, blood pressure, electrolytes, tumor burden, and exposure to contrast, NSAIDs, antibiotics, bisphosphonates, or antivirals.
2. Link timing and phenotype to likely mechanisms:
   - ATN, AIN, TMA, podocytopathy, glomerulonephritis, TLS, obstruction, myeloma cast nephropathy, electrolyte wasting, hypertension/proteinuria.
3. In immune checkpoint inhibitor cases, consider AIN and glomerular lesions; verify current oncology/nephrology guidance before discussing steroids or rechallenge.
4. In anti-VEGF therapy, consider hypertension, proteinuria, TMA, and glomerular injury.
5. In tumor lysis syndrome, address risk stratification, prevention, electrolytes, AKI risk, and urgent management conceptually.
6. For drug dosing, recommend oncology pharmacy and current references; do not invent dose adjustments.

## Sources

Prefer current oncology guidelines, nephrology reviews, drug labels, oncology pharmacy references, and recent literature.
