# Prompt 1 — Brand Voice Discovery & Personality Profile

## Instructions for Claude

You are a brand strategist building a brand voice foundation document. Generate a complete Brand Voice Personality Profile using the inputs provided.

---

## User Inputs Required

```
Business name: [your business name]
Industry: [e.g., SaaS, e-commerce, consulting, nonprofit, B2B services]
Target audience: [describe your primary customer — who they are, what they do, what they care about]
3 adjectives for your brand: [e.g., bold, warm, direct]
3 brands you admire (any industry): [e.g., Patagonia, Notion, Mailchimp]
3 brands that feel "wrong" for you: [e.g., Goldman Sachs, Wendy's, WebMD]
Current biggest communication problem: [e.g., "We sound corporate when we want to sound human" / "Every team member writes differently"]
```

---

## Prompt

You are a senior brand strategist. Using the inputs above, generate a complete **Brand Voice Personality Profile** with the following sections:

**Section 1: Brand Archetype**
Identify the primary archetype from the 12-archetype system (Innocent, Everyman, Hero, Outlaw, Explorer, Creator, Ruler, Magician, Lover, Caregiver, Jester, Sage). Name the primary archetype and a secondary archetype. Explain in 2–3 sentences why these archetypes fit based on the brand's audience, admired brands, and described personality.

**Section 2: Tone Spectrum Position**
Rate the brand on each spectrum from 1–10 (1 = far left, 10 = far right):
- Formal (1) ←→ Casual (10)
- Serious (1) ←→ Playful (10)
- Authoritative (1) ←→ Approachable (10)
- Reserved (1) ←→ Bold (10)
- Technical (1) ←→ Plain-language (10)

For each spectrum, add one sentence explaining the rating.

**Section 3: Brand Personality Adjectives**
List exactly 5 personality adjectives that define the brand voice. For each:
- The adjective
- A 1-sentence definition of what it means FOR THIS BRAND (not the dictionary definition)
- One example sentence that demonstrates the adjective in action

**Section 4: Sounds Like Us / Never Sounds Like Us**
Write 3 example sentences that are perfectly on-brand (label each with which adjective it demonstrates).
Write 3 example sentences that are distinctly off-brand. After each off-brand example, write the corrected on-brand version.

**Section 5: Brand Voice Manifesto**
Write a single paragraph (80–120 words) that captures the brand's voice philosophy. Written in second person ("You are..."), as if briefing a new writer on how to speak as this brand. This paragraph should feel like the brand speaking about itself — not a dry strategy document.

**Section 6: One-Line Voice Summary**
Finish this sentence: "[Brand name]'s voice is ___________." Make it specific enough that two different writers would produce similar copy from it.
