# Direction Playbook

Use these patterns when live research is unavailable or as a baseline before adapting to research.

## 带货口播

Best for products with clear appearance, price/activity appeal, or giftability.

Structure:
1. Hook: name the buying scenario or pain point.
2. Product close-up.
3. One fast use/demo moment.
4. Emotional or practical payoff.
5. Offer/occasion CTA.

Tone: direct, fast, conversational.

## 场景种草

Best for lifestyle products, beauty, food, home, fashion, gifts.

Structure:
1. Everyday scene.
2. Product appears naturally.
3. Before/after mood shift.
4. Detail close-up.
5. Soft recommendation.

Tone: lived-in, aspirational but not too polished.

## 促销活动

Best for 618, 双11, Black Friday, launch deals, bundles.

Use this direction when the user explicitly chooses促销活动 or mentions a campaign/deal context. Promotion language is allowed here; outside this direction, keep scripts evergreen unless the user requested a campaign.

Structure:
1. Urgency hook.
2. One-sentence product reason.
3. Best use/gift scenario.
4. Deal reminder.
5. Simple CTA.

Tone: energetic, clear, no overclaiming.

## 测评对比

Best for products with visible effects, durability, texture, convenience, or price/value contrast.

Structure:
1. Skeptical hook.
2. Test setup.
3. Product demo.
4. Result close-up.
5. Verdict.

Tone: believable, slightly critical, evidence-first.

## 海外 TikTok 风格

Default to UGC-style seeding ads for TikTok Shop and cross-border product testing. This direction should feel like a creator's urgent product discovery, not a polished brand ad.

Best for products that can solve a visible pain point, create a sensory reaction, trigger curiosity, or produce quick before/after feedback: beauty, food, drink, gadgets, fashion, accessories, home, novelty products.

Core structure:
1. For TikTok UGC scripts, the first 3 seconds must show a visible product-relevant conflict, frustration, unmet desire, awkward moment, or sensory discomfort.
2. Choose the conflict based on the product category, visible product traits, likely use case, and target audience.
3. Before the product appears, show the user in a clear negative or unsatisfied state.
4. When the product appears, position it as a rescue object, instant upgrade, unexpected fix, mood switch, or shortcut solution.
5. Fast visual feedback: use quick handheld cuts, close-ups, usage motion, reaction shots, texture/sensory proof, before/after mood shift, or immediate result cues.
6. End with a visible before/after state change that validates the product benefit without a hard sales pitch.

Hook patterns:
- "I was literally done with..."
- "This looked so random, but..."
- "I did not expect this to fix..."
- "Nobody told me this was..."
- "I bought it as a joke, and now..."

Tone: urgent, emotional, native UGC, slightly messy, sensory, fast, self-aware, and conversion-oriented without sounding like a formal ad.

Voiceover length:
- For 15-second English TikTok videos, keep the full voiceover around 22-35 words.
- Prefer short lines, pauses, and reaction beats over dense selling points.
- If the product needs more explanation, move it into visual action instead of adding more spoken words.

Avoid:
- Slow product introduction.
- Pure comedy with no selling point.
- Generic "I bought this because it looked cool" openings unless there is a strong conflict.
- Heavy Chinese shopping-festival language unless the campaign specifically targets Chinese-speaking users.

## 直播间真人带货

Best for products that need high trust, host demonstration, or urgency.

Structure:
1. Host calls attention.
2. Product in hand near camera.
3. 2-3 selling points.
4. Use/gift scenario.
5. Limited-time close.

Tone: warm, energetic, real-time.

## 商业广告/TVC

Best for premium products, brand assets, gift boxes, hero products.

Structure:
1. Visual hero shot.
2. Lifestyle aspiration.
3. Product detail montage.
4. Emotional payoff.
5. Brand/activity end frame.

Tone: polished, sensory, cinematic.

Video-generation workflow, only after the user explicitly chooses `直接生成视频`: storyboard first. Ask the video tool to generate 5 key visual frames, then use those 5 frames as multi-reference images to generate the final 15s video.
