# Forms School Feng Shui (形峦风水)

The Forms School (形峦派) is the oldest Feng Shui tradition, analyzing the physical landscape features and their shapes. Essential for external environment assessment.

---

## 1. The Four Noble Animals (四灵兽)

The foundational framework for any Feng Shui site analysis.

| Animal | Position | Should Be | Character |
|:------:|:--------:|:----------|:----------|
| **朱雀 (Zhu Que)** — Red Phoenix | **Front** (南/向) | Open space, clear view, gentle slope | Welcoming, allowing qi to gather |
| **玄武 (Xuan Wu)** — Dark Warrior | **Back** (北/坐) | Higher ground, hill, gentle mountain | Support, stability, protection |
| **青龙 (Qing Long)** — Azure Dragon | **Left** (东) | Slightly higher than the right | Protection, yang energy, male influence |
| **白虎 (Bai Hu)** — White Tiger | **Right** (西) | Slightly lower than the left | Protection, yin energy, female influence |

### Ideal configuration:
- Back: protective mountain/hill (靠山)
- Front: open space/mingtang (明堂) — a gathering space for qi
- Left (Dragon): slightly elevated, winding
- Right (Tiger): slightly lower, shorter
- Center: the building/site itself

### Imbalance indicators and remedies:

| Imbalance | Meaning | Remedy |
|:----------|:--------|:-------|
| Tiger higher than Dragon (白虎高过青龙) | Female dominates, tension | Plant trees on left (Dragon side), or place stone lion on left |
| No backing (无靠山) | Lack of support, vulnerable | Build a wall, plant tall trees, use a tall screen behind |
| Mingtang too small (明堂狭窄) | Stagnant, limited opportunities | Keep front area open and clear |
| Road/gate facing directly (路冲) | Sudden attacking qi | Wind chimes, plants, or a screen at entrance |

## 2. Landscape Elements Analysis (地理五要素)

| Element | Meaning | Good Form | Bad Form |
|:-------:|:--------|:----------|:---------|
| **龙 (Dragon)** - Mountain range | Lineage, source of qi | Winding, undulating, continuous | Broken, sharp, straight |
| **穴 (Cave)** - The site location | The sweet spot where qi gathers | Sheltered, balanced, protected | Exposed, windy, or waterlogged |
| **砂 (Sand)** - Surrounding hills | Protective formations | Rounded, graceful, layered | Pointed, broken, or ominous shapes |
| **水 (Water)** - Water features | Wealth, flow, movement | Clear, curved, slow-moving | Dead/stagnant, straight, rushing away |
| **向 (Direction)** - Orientation | Alignment to maximize qi | Aligned with favorable period and trigram | Facing unlucky or oppressive features |

### Water (水) — The most important modern element:
- Water in front (朱雀位) → wealth gathers
- Water flowing toward the house → qi arriving
- Water flowing away from house → qi departing (wealth leakage)
- Curved water (抱水) → embracing, protective
- Straight water → qi rushes away
- Road = water in modern context (roads function like water channels)

## 3. Shape Analysis (形法)

### Building shapes and their element correspondences:

| Shape | Element | Meaning |
|:-----:|:-------:|:--------|
| **Square** | 土 (Earth) | Stability, good for offices |
| **Rectangle - horizontal** | 木 (Wood) | Growth, good for schools |
| **Triangle / Sharp angle** | 火 (Fire) | Aggressive — avoid pointing toward door |
| **Round / Curved** | 金 (Metal) | Creativity, good for arts |
| **Wavy / Irregular** | 水 (Water) | Fluidity, good for water features |

### Common negative shapes (形煞 — Form Sha):

| Shape/Feature | Name | Effect | Remedy |
|:--------------|:----:|:-------|:-------|
| T-junction pointing at house | **路冲** | Sudden qi attack, loss | Screen, mirror, wind chime |
| Sharp corner of another building | **尖角煞** | Hostile energy | Plant, screen, Ba Gua mirror |
| Large tree directly in front | **顶心煞** | Blocks qi entry | Trim or relocate |
| Overhead beam | **横梁压顶** | Pressure, headaches | Drop ceiling, lift bed away |
| Empty space behind the bed | **背后无靠** | Vulnerability | Headboard, solid wall |
| Stairs facing door | **穿心煞** | Qi flows out | Screen, door always closed |
| Hospital/church/temple facing | **孤阴煞** | Yin overload | Strong yang element, lights |
| Electrical pylon nearby | **电磁煞** | Disrupts qi | Block with dense plants, curtains |

### How to assess exterior forms:
1. Walk around the building (exterior)
2. Check surrounding buildings: height, shape, distance
3. Check road systems: curves, T-junctions, roundabouts
4. Check water: rivers, ponds, drains
5. Check prominent features: towers, antennas, cranes
6. Identify any poison arrows (sharp angles pointing at the building)
7. Check entry path: approach to the main door should be gradual, not direct
