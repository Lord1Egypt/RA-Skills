# Agentic Development Discovery Lifecycle

## Flow

1. Quick qualification
   - Decide whether there is a repeated workflow worth scoping.

2. Client intake
   - Gather business context, workflow owner, systems, timeline, and constraints.

3. Stakeholder discovery
   - Interview operators, reviewers, technical owners, and decision makers.

4. Current-state mapping
   - Document inputs, steps, handoffs, systems, decisions, and pain points.

5. Future-state agentic workflow design
   - Identify retrieval, drafting, tool routing, approvals, logs, and monitoring.

6. Readiness and risk review
   - Check data, APIs, permissions, sensitivity, exclusions, and dependencies.

7. Evaluation and success criteria
   - Define representative examples and acceptance measures.

8. Pilot readiness and handoff
   - Decide whether to recommend assessment, pilot, POC, proposal, or no-go.

## Handoff To Other Skills

- `agentic-email-skill`: use discovery facts for follow-up and proposal-send emails.
- `agentic-proposal-skill`: use recap, requirements brief, scorecard, and success criteria to draft proposal/SOW.
- `agentic-contract-skill`: use approved scope, assumptions, exclusions, acceptance criteria, and support terms.
- `agentic-invoice-skill`: use pricing model, milestones, deposit, retainer, and change-order triggers.
- `agentic-certificate-skill`: use only if training or attendance certificates are included.

## Minimum Handoff Facts

- workflow name
- client owner and reviewer
- current pain
- current systems/tools
- proposed agent actions
- human approval gates
- success criteria
- evaluation examples
- assumptions
- exclusions
- dependencies
- recommended next step
