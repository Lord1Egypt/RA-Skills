## Description: <br>
Dispel System helps an agent analyze suspected coercive or controlling relationships through structured screening, safety checks, system mapping, and Mirror, X-Ray, and Wake-Up response modes. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[web-seeker](https://clawhub.ai/user/web-seeker) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users and agents use this skill when a user asks to examine possible manipulation, gaslighting, domestic violence, or coercive control in a relationship. It guides the agent to screen for fit, prioritize safety, gather behavior facts, and produce structured analysis without diagnosis, legal advice, or directing decisions. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill is used in sensitive relationship-control and domestic-violence contexts where an unsafe next step could increase real-world danger. <br>
Mitigation: Follow the artifact's safety gates first, pause analysis on danger escalation signals, and prioritize local emergency, domestic-violence, legal, medical, or mental-health professionals when safety or formal action is involved. <br>
Risk: Users may treat the analysis as clinical diagnosis, legal advice, or a decision to leave or stay. <br>
Mitigation: Keep outputs framed as relationship-control analysis, avoid psychiatric labels and legal directives, and preserve the user's decision authority. <br>
Risk: Device or network monitoring can expose a user who is seeking help in a controlling relationship. <br>
Mitigation: Include digital-safety guidance before analysis, such as considering private browsing, trusted devices, public devices, and secure communication channels. <br>
