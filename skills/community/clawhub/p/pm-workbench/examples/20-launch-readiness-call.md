# Example 20 — Launch readiness call under pressure

## Scenario

Marketing wants a date. Product is not fully ready. Leadership wants a confident answer tomorrow.

## Input

> "Marketing wants to lock a launch date for our new AI workflow in 3 weeks because they need campaign lead time. Product and design think the core experience is promising, but activation is still patchy and support flows are not ready. My boss wants a confident answer by tomorrow. Help me decide what to recommend and how to frame it upward."

## What good output looks like

A strong `pm-workbench` response should:

- recognize this is both a decision problem and a communication problem
- avoid fake confidence without collapsing into vague caution
- make the current recommendation explicit
- separate what should be decided now from what still needs validation
- frame the ask in a way leadership can approve, challenge, or narrow

## Expected output shape

**Executive Summary** or a compressed launch-decision brief

## Example target output

# Launch Decision Brief

## 1. Current recommendation

Do **not** lock the full public launch date tomorrow. Lock a decision checkpoint instead, with a gated launch path if the readiness gaps close inside the next 7-10 days.

This is not a recommendation to stop the launch. It is a recommendation to avoid committing marketing to a date before activation and support readiness are credible.

## 2. Readiness gap

The core experience sounds promising, but two launch-critical areas are not ready:

- activation is still patchy, so too many users may fail before reaching the product's value
- support flows are not ready, so launch volume could create avoidable user frustration and internal load

## 3. Consequence of pushing ahead anyway

If we lock the date now and launch broadly in 3 weeks, we risk spending marketing attention on a product path that cannot reliably convert or support new users. That could weaken trust with users and make the campaign look worse than the product's underlying potential.

## 4. Decide now vs validate next

### Decide now

- Do we agree that activation and support readiness are launch gates?
- Do we want to preserve the option of a limited / gated launch instead of full launch?
- What is the latest date marketing needs a final launch decision?

### Validate next

- Can activation reach an acceptable threshold in the next 7-10 days?
- Are support flows documented and staffed enough for launch volume?
- Does the first-run experience work for the target launch segment without high-touch intervention?

## 5. Leadership ask

Ask leadership to approve a launch checkpoint instead of a full date lock:

- keep the campaign plan warm
- set a readiness review in 7-10 days
- authorize a limited launch if core readiness improves but support risk remains
- avoid full public launch unless activation and support gates are met

## 6. Spoken version

My recommendation is that we should not lock the full launch date tomorrow. The product has promise, but activation and support readiness are still not strong enough to support a broad campaign. I would ask leadership to lock a readiness checkpoint instead: keep the marketing plan moving, review activation and support gates in 7-10 days, and decide then whether we go full launch, gated launch, or delay.

## Why this is useful

This is a classic high-pressure PM moment. The goal is not to sound calm — it is to make a usable call under imperfect readiness.
