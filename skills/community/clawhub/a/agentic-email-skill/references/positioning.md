# Agentic Development Email Positioning

## Offer Frame

CompleteTech LLC agentic development is best framed as practical workflow implementation, not vague AI transformation.

Core promise:
- Design, build, evaluate, and document agentic software workflows.
- Add tool routing, retrieval, approvals, logging, monitoring, and handoff.
- Start with a bounded pilot before production rollout.
- Keep humans in the loop for external communications, production changes, purchases, data export, and material business decisions.

Good shorthand:
- "agentic workflow pilot"
- "bounded automation with approval gates"
- "AI-assisted workflow that drafts, routes, checks, and logs work"
- "prototype, evaluation, monitoring, and handoff"
- "turn a repeated operational workflow into a reviewed agent workflow"

Avoid:
- "fully autonomous business decisions"
- "replace your team"
- unsupported savings percentages
- invented case studies
- claims of legal, medical, financial, employment, housing, insurance, credit, biometric, surveillance, or safety-critical compliance

## Buyer Pains

Use the buyer's real situation where available. If not, choose one plausible pain and keep it tentative.

- Leads, tickets, or requests arrive faster than staff can triage them.
- Internal knowledge is scattered across docs, inboxes, chats, tickets, and spreadsheets.
- Sales or operations teams repeat the same drafting, checking, and routing work.
- Hand-offs fail because context is missing.
- Manual review is needed, but the prep work can be automated.
- The team tried AI tools but lacks evaluation, approvals, logging, and deployment discipline.
- Leadership wants AI progress without uncontrolled production risk.

## Differentiators

- Workflow-first, not demo-first.
- Human approval gates before sensitive actions.
- Evaluation plan before rollout.
- Monitoring and run logs for post-launch review.
- Clear excluded-use boundaries.
- Documentation and handoff for technical administrators and business stakeholders.
- Contract-ready delivery language: scope, acceptance, support period, and change control.

## Proof Rules

Use proof only when supplied by the user. If no proof is supplied, use low-claim credibility:
- "I can map the workflow and identify the approval points."
- "I can show what a pilot would look like before you commit to a build."
- "The first step can be a scoped workflow review."
- "The output can be a prototype, evaluation checklist, and handoff plan."

## CTA Ladder

Use the smallest sensible ask for the stage:

1. Cold first touch: "Worth a 15-minute look?"
2. Early follow-up: "Should I send a one-page pilot outline?"
3. Trigger email: "Want me to map this workflow?"
4. Discovery: "Can we compare the current path and the reviewed-agent path?"
5. Proposal: "Should I send the fixed-scope pilot terms?"
6. Closing: "Are you comfortable starting with the scoped pilot?"
7. Stalled deal: "Should I close the loop or revisit next quarter?"
8. Post-sale: "Can you confirm the owner for approvals and test data?"

## Tone

Default tone:
- direct
- useful
- concrete
- low hype
- short paragraphs
- one ask

Do not over-apologize in follow-ups. Do not guilt the recipient. Do not stack multiple CTAs.
