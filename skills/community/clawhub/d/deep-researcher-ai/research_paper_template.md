# Research Paper Structure Template
## For the Deep Researcher Skill (30-40 Page Comprehensive Papers)

---

## TABLE OF CONTENTS
1. Title Page
2. Abstract (300-500 words)
3. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
4. Chapter 1: Introduction (3-4 pages)
5. Chapter 2: Literature Review (6-8 pages)
6. Chapter 3: Methodology (4-5 pages)
7. Chapter 4: Data Collection (3-4 pages)
8. Chapter 5: Analysis & Findings (8-10 pages)
9. Chapter 6: Discussion & Implications (3-4 pages)
10. Chapter 7: Conclusion & Recommendations (2-3 pages)
11. References (5-8 pages)
12. Appendices (Optional)

---

## DETAILED OUTLINE

### 1. TITLE PAGE
- **Research Title**: Clear, specific, and descriptive
- **Author**: Deep Researcher AI
- **Date**: Publication date
- **Keywords**: 5-8 relevant terms (for indexing)
- **Disclaimer**: AI-generated research (if applicable)

### 2. ABSTRACT (300-500 words)
- Brief overview of the research problem
- Summary of methodology (one sentence)
- Key findings (bulleted or concise paragraphs)
- Main implications
- 3-5 keywords for search optimization

### 3. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1-2 pages)
- High-level context for decision-makers
- Business/academic relevance
- Top 3-5 takeaways
- Recommendations for action
- Visual summary (charts, graphs if applicable)

### 4. CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION (3-4 pages)
#### 4.1 Background and Context
- Historical context of the topic
- Current state of research/practice
- Real-world relevance

#### 4.2 Problem Statement
- Specific problem being addressed
- Why this problem matters
- Gaps in existing knowledge/practice

#### 4.3 Research Objectives
- Primary objective
- 3-5 secondary objectives
- Research questions (RQ1, RQ2, RQ3...)

#### 4.4 Significance & Contribution
- What knowledge will be gained?
- Practical applications
- Theoretical contributions

#### 4.5 Scope & Limitations
- Boundaries of the research
- What is excluded
- Acknowledged limitations

#### 4.6 Chapter Summary
- Brief review of what was covered
- Transition to literature review

### 5. CHAPTER 2: LITERATURE REVIEW (6-8 pages)
#### 5.1 Theoretical Framework
- Key theories/models relevant to the topic
- Historical evolution of theories
- Contemporary interpretations

#### 5.2 Key Themes Identified
- Theme 1: [Name]
- Theme 2: [Name]
- Theme 3: [Name]
- (Organized thematically, not chronologically)

#### 5.3 Major Studies & Research
- Seminal works (cited with year)
- Recent breakthroughs (last 3-5 years)
- Conflicting findings (if any)

#### 5.4 Gaps in Literature
- What previous research missed
- Methodological limitations identified
- Areas requiring further investigation

#### 5.5 Conceptual Framework
- How previous work informs current study
- Visual model/diagram (if applicable)
- Hypothesis formulation

### 6. CHAPTER 3: METHODOLOGY (4-5 pages)
#### 6.1 Research Design
- Qualitative, Quantitative, or Mixed Methods?
- Justification for chosen approach
- Overall structure

#### 6.2 Data Sources
- Primary sources
- Secondary sources
- Tertiary sources (any databases used)

#### 6.3 Search Strategy
- Search queries used
- Boolean operators
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria

#### 6.4 Analysis Techniques
- Qualitative: Thematic analysis, content analysis, etc.
- Quantitative: Statistical tests, regression analysis, etc.
- AI-assisted analysis tools used

#### 6.5 Validity & Reliability
- How bias was minimized
- Triangulation methods used
- Peer review/verification process

### 7. CHAPTER 4: DATA COLLECTION (3-4 pages)
#### 7.1 Quantitative Data
- Sample size
- Data types (survey scores, metrics, revenue, etc.)
- Measurement instruments
- Collection timeline

#### 7.2 Qualitative Data
- Interview/observation details
- Document types reviewed
- Field notes collected

#### 7.3 Data Preprocessing
- Cleaning procedures
- Transformation steps
- Outlier handling

#### 7.4 Ethical Considerations
- Permission/consent obtained
- Anonymization methods
- IRB/Ethics committee approval (if applicable)

### 8. CHAPTER 5: ANALYSIS & FINDINGS (8-10 pages)
#### 8.1 Descriptive Statistics/Summary Findings
- Key metrics from data
- Demographic breakdowns
- Trend observations

#### 8.2 Quantitative Analysis Results
- Statistical test results (with p-values, significance levels)
- Regression coefficients, R-squared
- Correlation matrices
- Tables of key findings

#### 8.3 Qualitative Analysis Results
- Major themes identified
- Participant verbatim quotes (for support)
- Pattern recognition
- Content analysis codes

#### 8.4 Comparative Analysis
- Cross-group comparisons
- Longitudinal trends
- Benchmarking against existing data

#### 8.5 Visual Correlation
- Figures/Charts interpreting findings
- Text-box highlights for key insights
- Annotated graphs

#### 8.6 Unexpected Findings
- Surprising outcomes
- Anomalies discovered
- Alternative interpretations

### 9. CHAPTER 6: DISCUSSION & IMPLICATIONS (3-4 pages)
#### 9.1 Interpretation of Key Findings
- What the data means in context
- Alignment with hypotheses
- Comparison to prior research

#### 9.2 Theoretical Implications
- How theory needs to evolve
- New models proposed
- Paradigm shifts suggested

#### 9.3 Practical Implications
- Actionable recommendations for practitioners
- Policy implications
- Industry applications

#### 9.4 Strategic Insights
- Long-term strategic value
- Competitive advantages
- Market opportunities

#### 9.5 Counter-Arguments & Limitations
- Acknowledged weaknesses
- Alternative explanations
- Where findings may not generalize

### 10. CHAPTER 7: CONCLUSION & RECOMMENDATIONS (2-3 pages)
#### 10.1 Restatement of Objectives
- What was set out to achieve
- What was accomplished

#### 10.2 Summary of Main Contributions
- Knowledge contributions
- Methodological contributions
- Practical contributions

#### 10.3 Clear Recommendations
- 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations
- Priority ranking (High/Medium/Low)
- Implementation steps

#### 10.4 Future Research Directions
- Questions left unanswered
- Suggested next research topics
- Required methodologies or resources

#### 10.5 Final Takeaways
- Key message summary
- Call to action for stakeholders

### 11. REFERENCES (5-8 pages)
- **Format**: APA 7th Edition (or as specified)
- **Organization**: Alphabetical by author
- **Completeness**: All in-text citations listed here
- **Hyperlinks**: DOI URLs included where available
- **Total Targets**: 40-80 sources for comprehensive coverage

Example format:
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of the article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), Page Range. DOI Link
Author, B. B. (Year). Title of the book. Publisher.
```

## QUALITY METRICS FOR 30-40 PAGE PAPER

### Content Coverage
- ✅ Topics covered: Minimum 15-20 distinct research points
- ✅ Source diversity: 4-5 different types of databases
- ✅ Recency: 70%+ sources from last 5 years
- ✅ Depth: Multiple authoritative sources per topic

### Academic Rigor
- ✅ Citation density: 1+ citations per 150-200 words
- ✅ Methodological transparency: Clear, reproducible steps
- ✅ Evidence-based: Every claim backed by at least 1 source

### Readability
- ✅ Clarity: No jargon without explanation
- ✅ Structure: Logical flow between chapters
- ✅ Visual aids: At least 2 charts/tables in main body

### Completeness
- ✅ All research questions answered
- ✅ No logical gaps in argumentation
- ✅ Proper attribution of all ideas

## FORMATTING STANDARDS

### Document Structure
- Font: Times New Roman or Arial, 11-12pt
- Line Spacing: 1.15 - 1.5
- Margins: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
- Paragraph Indent: 0.5 inch

### Visual Elements
- Figures: Numbered and captioned (Figure 1.1, 1.2, etc.)
- Tables: Numbered and captioned (Table 3.1, 3.2, etc.)
- Charts: Clear labels, legends, and source attribution

### Writing Style
- Tone: Formal, academic, objective
- Active voice preferred where appropriate
- Third-person perspective (unless first-person justified)
