## Description: <br>
Provides injury-prevention guidance on proper body mechanics for physical work, including safe lifting, carrying, standing, repetitive-strain prevention, warmups, and warning signs. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[howtousehumans](https://clawhub.ai/user/howtousehumans) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Employees and external users with physically demanding jobs use this skill to get general safety guidance for lifting, carrying, standing, repetitive work, warmups, and early warning signs of strain. Agents can tailor the guidance to the user's job demands, location, and reported discomfort while keeping it separate from medical diagnosis or treatment. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may treat injury-prevention guidance as medical care. <br>
Mitigation: Frame responses as general workplace safety guidance and direct users to a clinician, physical therapist, occupational medicine provider, or supervisor for persistent, severe, radiating, or worsening symptoms. <br>
Risk: Workplace safety rules, weight units, compensation processes, and local authorities vary by region. <br>
Mitigation: Adapt references and measurements to the user's location, such as OSHA, HSE, Safe Work Australia, CCOHS, EU-OSHA, pounds, or kilograms. <br>
Risk: Recurring reminders could become intrusive outside physically demanding work or explicit user interest. <br>
Mitigation: Keep reminders scoped to the user's stated physical work context, reported pain follow-up, or requested technique review. <br>
