## Description: <br>
Help a beginner or early-stage game team estimate the likely budget for a game concept based on scope, target milestone, current team, skill coverage, work model, and geography. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[stanestane](https://clawhub.ai/user/stanestane) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users and game developers use this skill to estimate practical budget ranges for game prototypes, vertical slices, releases, and live or online projects. It helps identify scope assumptions, team coverage, missing cost buckets, and ways to reduce spend. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Budget estimates may be misleading if users omit confidential or sensitive planning details, contracts, payroll records, or investor materials. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for approximate roles, regions, scope, rates, and work-model assumptions instead of sensitive source documents or exact compensation records. <br>
Risk: Early game budget ranges can be mistaken for precise financial forecasts. <br>
Mitigation: Present estimates as rough ranges with explicit assumptions, confidence limits, and the cost drivers most likely to change the result. <br>
