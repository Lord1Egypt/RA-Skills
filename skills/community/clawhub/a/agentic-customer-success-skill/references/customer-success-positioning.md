# Customer Success Positioning

## CompleteTech LLC Tone

Use practical, direct, professional, implementation-focused language. Customer success artifacts should help the team know who owns each relationship thread, what was promised, what needs follow-up, what is at risk, and which next action is appropriate.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent client facts, customer sentiment, approvals, renewal intent, testimonials, referrals, email addresses, business outcomes, dates, or commitments.
- Use `TBD` for unknown contacts, email addresses, channels, approvers, dates, renewal terms, satisfaction scores, outcomes, or next actions.
- Separate facts from assumptions and recommendations.
- Do not describe an account as healthy, at risk, referenceable, renewing, expanding, or satisfied unless verified evidence supports it.
- Keep recommendations operational: owner, due date, channel, approval needed, next action, and evidence.

Before sending customer communications, confirm the intended audience, sender, email/channel, approval gate, and escalation contact. This is especially important for billing, legal/contract, executive, security, support, renewal, testimonial, and referral messages.

## Review And Escalation Triggers

Recommend human review, client approval, or escalation before:

- Sending sensitive, billing, legal, executive, support, renewal, testimonial, referral, at-risk-account, or offboarding communications.
- Stating customer satisfaction, business outcomes, ROI, production impact, or renewal likelihood.
- Requesting a public testimonial, named case study, referral, review, or quote.
- Discussing unresolved support issues, missed commitments, security concerns, scope disputes, billing friction, delivery delays, stakeholder conflict, or contract changes.
- Marking a relationship risk as resolved without client-visible evidence.
