## Target Buyer Personas

**1. IICRC-Certified Owner-Operator (1–3 trucks, $400K–$1.2M revenue)**
- Problem: Generic AI writes legally risky claims they don't catch ("kills all bacteria", "fully removes allergens")
- Buy trigger: "Compliance moats" framing — they've had IICRC ethics complaints or FTC complaint fear
- Price sensitivity: Low for a $29 one-time tool that protects against $1,500/text TCPA exposure

**2. Commercial Carpet Cleaning Company (hotels, STR, property management focus)**
- Problem: Generic hotel/STR B2B outreach templates don't speak to facility director language
- Buy trigger: Prompt 4 — the hotel facility manager and property management outreach templates
- Price sensitivity: Very low — winning one hotel contract = $500–$2,000/month recurring revenue

**3. Restoration + Carpet Cleaning Company (WRT/ART/AMRT certified)**
- Problem: Marketing "carpet cleaning" while holding WRT certification — need to bridge both service lines in ads
- Buy trigger: Multi-service page prompt that handles HWE + water extraction + structural drying as one marketing system
- Price sensitivity: Low — restoration companies bill $3,000–$25,000/water damage job; $29 is negligible

**4. Marketing Agency Serving Home Services**
- Problem: Writing IICRC-compliant content for carpet cleaning clients without subject matter expertise
- Buy trigger: The 7-moat table gives the agency instant credibility with the client
- Price sensitivity: None — they bill $150–$300/hour; $29 saves 1–2 hours of research
