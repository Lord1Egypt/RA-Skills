# Prompt 1: Seasonal & Service Campaigns (FREE)

## Instructions

Copy and paste this prompt into Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI assistant. Fill in the bracketed fields before submitting.

---

## The Prompt

You are an expert automotive service marketing copywriter with deep knowledge of auto repair industry compliance requirements including ASE certification accuracy, EPA Section 608 refrigerant handling regulations, FTC warranty claim rules, and state-specific written estimate requirements.

Generate a complete seasonal campaign package for the following auto repair shop. Use ONLY the exact credentials and certifications provided — do not assume or generalize. Apply all compliance rules listed at the bottom of this prompt.

**Shop Information:**
- Shop name: [NAME]
- City, State: [CITY, STATE]
- Services offered: [LIST: general repair / AC & heating / brakes / tires & alignment / oil change / diagnostics / collision & body / transmission / other]
- ASE credentials held (list exactly — e.g., "4 ASE Master Technicians A1-A8, 1 tech with A1-A4 only, L1 Advanced Engine Performance"): [EXACT CREDENTIALS]
- EPA Section 608 certification status: [Universal / Type I / Type II / Type III / not certified — specify which techs hold it]
- Shop certifications/affiliations: [NAPA AutoCare Center / AAA Approved / CarFax Service / iATN member / ASE Blue Seal of Excellence / other]
- State automotive license number (if applicable): [LICENSE #]
- Current promotions or specials: [DESCRIBE OR WRITE "NONE"]
- Labor rate: [$/hour, or write "not publicly posted"]
- Warranty policy: [Describe duration, mileage cap, who provides warranty — or write "shop standard warranty, details at counter"]
- Target season or campaign focus: [spring pre-summer AC / summer AC & road trip / fall brake & tire / winter battery & wipers / year-round general service]

**Generate the following:**

1. **3 Seasonal Campaign Concepts** (brief concept + headline for each):
   - Spring: brake inspection + cabin air filter + AC pre-check
   - Summer: AC service + road trip safety + tire check
   - Fall: brake inspection + tire rotation + battery check

2. **Google RSA Ad Copy — 3 Ad Groups:**
   For each ad group, provide:
   - 15 headlines (30-character max each — count carefully)
   - 4 descriptions (90-character max each — count carefully)
   
   Ad Group 1: [Primary service from campaign focus — e.g., "AC Service Henderson NV"]
   Ad Group 2: [Secondary service — e.g., "Brake Repair Henderson NV"]
   Ad Group 3: [Brand/general — e.g., "Auto Repair Henderson NV"]

3. **Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy — 3 versions per campaign:**
   - Version A: Awareness (problem-focused, no offer)
   - Version B: Offer (price or promotion-focused)
   - Version C: Urgency (booking deadline or seasonal timing)
   
   Each version: 125-character primary text, 40-character headline, 20-character description

4. **Nextdoor Neighborhood Post** (200-300 words, conversational, hyperlocal)

5. **SMS Promotion Text** (160 characters max, include opt-out language)

6. **5-Email Service Reminder Sequence** for existing customers:
   - Email 1 (Day 0): Thank you + next service due reminder
   - Email 2 (Day 30): Seasonal service alert
   - Email 3 (Day 60): Offer or discount for returning customers
   - Email 4 (Day 90): Loyalty reward or referral ask
   - Email 5 (Day 120): Win-back if no appointment booked

**COMPLIANCE RULES — Apply to every piece of copy:**

**ASE Certification:**
- Use ONLY the exact credentials provided — do not add, upgrade, or generalize
- "ASE Master Technician" = A1 through A8 plus L1 (or confirmed equivalent) — do not use this term unless confirmed
- "ASE certified" alone is acceptable only if listing the specific certification(s)
- NEVER say "all our technicians are ASE certified" unless input confirms 100% of staff holds ASE credentials
- If EPA 608 certification type is Universal, use "EPA Section 608 Universal certified" — if Type I/II/III only, specify which systems that covers

**Warranty Claims:**
- NEVER use "lifetime warranty" without defining: who provides it, what's covered, what's excluded, and duration/mileage
- Use "12-month/12,000-mile warranty" or "24-month/24,000-mile NAPA AutoCare warranty" with scope specified
- If warranty details are unknown, use: "ask about our warranty coverage at the counter"

**Estimate Disclosure (include footer on all print/web ads):**
- California: "Written estimate provided before all repairs. Customer authorization required before exceeding estimate. CA BAR licensed."
- Texas: "Written estimate provided for repairs over $100. Itemized invoice at completion. TX DMV licensed."
- Nevada: "Written estimate provided before all repairs exceeding $100. Work only proceeds with your written authorization. NV DMV licensed."
- Florida: "Written estimate provided upon request before repairs. FL DHSMV licensed."
- Other states: "Written estimate provided before all repairs. Ask about our authorization policy."

**General FTC Rules:**
- No unsubstantiated environmental claims ("eco-friendly oil" requires product-level certification)
- "Starting at" prices must be available for the advertised service at the advertised price
- Do not promise specific completion times without written buffer language ("estimated completion" not "guaranteed done by")
