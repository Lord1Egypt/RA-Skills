# Quality Checklist

The standard for UniqueStaysUSA journal content. Every post must score 8.0/10 or higher on the weighted rubric before publishing.

## Scoring Rubric

Score each criterion 1-10. Multiply by weight. Sum must equal 8.0 or higher.

| Criterion | Weight | 10 = Exceptional | 7 = Acceptable | 5 or below = Fails |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Voice match** | 20% | Reads like the Joshua Tree or treehouses post. Warm, first-person, no corporate tone. | Generally matches but has 1-2 flat paragraphs or corporate phrasing. | Sounds like a generic travel blog or AI content. |
| **Specificity (Irish Storytelling Test)** | 15% | 3+ concrete details per stay that could not apply to anywhere else. Named rivers, specific tree species, exact distances. | 1-2 specific details per stay. Some generic phrases remain. | Generic descriptors: "beautiful views," "stunning location." |
| **Feeling-first principle** | 15% | Every stay section opens with the feeling of being there, not the features. Features woven in naturally after. | Most sections feel-first. 1-2 sections lead with features. | Feature lists, amenity catalogs, or "this X-bedroom Y features Z" constructions. |
| **Banned word compliance** | 10% | Zero banned words. Zero exclamation marks. No hedging phrases. | 1 banned word or 1 exclamation mark caught and fixable. | Multiple banned words or entire paragraphs that read like a resort brochure. |
| **SEO integration** | 10% | Target keyword in title, metaTitle, first 100 words, and at least one H2. Long-tail variations woven naturally. Keyword never feels forced. | Target keyword present in title and body. Some long-tail variants missing. | Keyword stuffed, missing from body, or forced into editorial prose. |
| **Stay embed quality** | 10% | Every embedded stay has editorial context before and after. Embeds break the text naturally. Reader understands why this stay is here. | Embeds present and contextual. 1-2 feel like they were dropped in without enough setup. | Embeds without context, or stays that don't match the article thesis. |
| **Practical value** | 10% | Price, rating, review count, and booking context present for every stay. Practical section adds genuine value. | Most stays have pricing. Practical section present but thin. | No pricing, no seasonal context, no practical information. |
| **20% cut test** | 10% | Removing any 20% of the article leaves it still excellent. No filler paragraphs. No redundant descriptions. | 1-2 paragraphs could be cut without loss. | Significant padding, repetitive descriptions, or sections that exist only for word count. |

**Passing score: 8.0/10 weighted average.** If the draft scores below 8.0, return to the edit phase with specific improvement instructions.

## Banned Words

Zero tolerance. If any of these appear, the post fails the quality gate.

### Absolutely never use
stunning, breathtaking, magical, life-changing, perfect, amazing, incredible, unforgettable, cozy, hidden gem, wanderlust, bucket list, must-see, don't miss, game-changer, nestled, escape the everyday, "whether you're...", "perfect for", "off the beaten path", "like something out of a fairy tale", "a dream come true", "tick all the boxes"

### Avoid unless earned through context
unique (describe *why* it's unique instead), luxurious, paradise, oasis, retreat, haven, sanctuary, gem, treasure, spectacular, epic, unforgettable (unless used with irony)

## Banned Patterns

These constructions kill editorial quality:

- **Feature-led openings:** "This 2-bedroom treehouse features a hot tub..." — always lead with feeling
- **Hedging openers:** "Whether you're a solo traveler or a family of four..." — take a position
- **Exclamation marks** — zero tolerance in journal posts
- **Passive voice in headlines or opening sentences**
- **"Affordable" or "budget-friendly"** without the actual number
- **Paragraphs that exist only for word count** — every paragraph must advance the reader's understanding
- **"Perfect for" constructions** — show, don't tell
- **Meta-commentary headers:** "Why this matters," "The takeaway," "Key insight" — the content should demonstrate value, not explain it

## Brand Voice Tests

Run these three tests before scoring:

### The Stoic Test
*Does this copy respect the reader's intelligence and their time?*

If it oversells, hedges, or performs enthusiasm it doesn't feel — rewrite it. UniqueStaysUSA is not selling escapism. It's selling the kind of place that earns your full presence.

### The Irish Storytelling Test
*Is there at least one specific detail in here that could only apply to this place?*

An algorithm can write "beautiful treehouse with stunning views." It cannot write "the kind of quiet you only find in old-growth forests, where the wind stays up in the canopy and the ground is almost completely still."

Specificity is the proof of presence.

### The Aloud Test
*If you stumble reading a sentence, rewrite it.*

If it sounds like it was written by a committee, rewrite it. If it sounds like a press release, delete it and start over. Long sentences build atmosphere. Short sentences create impact. Know which you need.

## The 20% Cut Test

Identify the weakest 20% of the article. For each weak paragraph, ask:

1. **Does this advance the reader's understanding?** If not, cut it.
2. **Does this repeat something said earlier?** If yes, cut the weaker version.
3. **Does this exist only for word count?** If yes, cut it.
4. **Could this paragraph be replaced by a single sentence?** If yes, replace it.
5. **Does this section sound like a different author wrote it?** If yes, rewrite to match voice.

Common cut targets:
- Redundant qualifiers ("if applicable", "if different from primary")
- Transitional filler ("Now let's look at...", "Moving on to...")
- Summary paragraphs that restate what was just said
- Opening paragraphs that warm up instead of starting cold
- Closing paragraphs that moralize instead of opening a door

## What a 9/10 Looks Like

Based on the published corpus (Joshua Tree, Treehouses, Workcation):

- Original angle no competitor covers
- 3-5 concrete details per stay with real specificity
- Framework or decision model the reader can apply (not just advice)
- Honest positioning — no advertorial tone
- Strong internal linking woven naturally into editorial prose
- Zero AI-detectable patterns
- The reader finishes thinking "this person actually understands this place"

## What a 7/10 Looks Like (needs work)

- Generally good voice but 2-3 flat paragraphs
- Some generic descriptors remain alongside specific details
- Embeds present but 1-2 feel disconnected from surrounding prose
- Practical section present but shallow
- Keyword present but forced in 1-2 places
- 2-3 paragraphs that could be cut without loss
