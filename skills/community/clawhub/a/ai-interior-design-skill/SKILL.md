# AI Interior Design Planner

AI Interior Design Planner helps turn room notes, photos, constraints, and inspiration into a practical redesign plan and AI-ready prompt pack.

Official website: [AI Interior Design](https://ai-interior-design.net/)

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- Interior design direction for a room or whole home.
- AI room redesign prompts from a photo or text description.
- Furniture layout ideas and space planning.
- Color palette, materials, lighting, and styling guidance.
- Renter-friendly or budget-conscious room refresh plans.
- Before/after concept planning for bedrooms, living rooms, kitchens, offices, studios, or small apartments.

## Intake checklist

Collect only what is needed:

- **Room type and size:** room name, rough dimensions, ceiling height if known.
- **Current state:** empty, cluttered, dated, dark, awkward layout, no storage, too small, etc.
- **Users and lifestyle:** solo, couple, family, pets, kids, guests, work-from-home.
- **Target mood:** calm sanctuary, bright creative studio, social entertaining space, luxury hotel feeling, cozy family room.
- **Style direction:** Japandi, organic modern, Scandinavian, mid-century, coastal, farmhouse modern, moody contemporary, or user-provided references.
- **Constraints:** rental rules, low budget, low natural light, must-keep furniture, accessibility needs, landlord approval, pet durability.
- **Desired output:** plan only, AI prompt only, shopping checklist, or full design package.

If the user provides a photo, ground recommendations in visible evidence and label assumptions clearly.

## Core workflow

### 1. Design brief

Summarize the goal in one sentence:

> Create a room that feels `[mood]`, supports `[lifestyle]`, and solves `[main problem]` while respecting `[constraints]`.

### 2. Style direction

Pick one primary style and one optional supporting influence. Avoid combining too many aesthetics.

Good pairings:

- Japandi + warm minimalism
- Organic modern + Mediterranean texture
- Scandinavian + soft contemporary
- Mid-century modern + warm eclectic
- Coastal + relaxed modern
- Moody contemporary + art deco accents

### 3. Palette and materials

Use a simple 60 / 30 / 10 model:

- **Dominant:** wall or large-surface color.
- **Secondary:** wood, upholstery, rug, cabinetry, or large furniture tone.
- **Accent:** art, pillows, lamps, metal finishes, greenery, ceramics.

Add realistic materials such as linen, oak, walnut, plaster, travertine, rattan, wool, bouclé, stoneware, matte black metal, or brushed brass.

### 4. Layout and furniture

Consider:

- Traffic paths and door swings.
- Focal point: window, fireplace, bed wall, TV wall, art wall, dining table, or view.
- Scale: furniture should fit the room, not just the aesthetic.
- Storage: hidden storage first, decorative storage second.
- Multi-function: especially for studios, guest rooms, and small apartments.

When helpful, offer 2–3 layout options with pros and cons.

### 5. Lighting plan

Layer lighting:

- **Ambient:** ceiling fixture, pendant, track, large floor lamp.
- **Task:** reading lamp, desk lamp, under-cabinet light, bedside sconce.
- **Accent:** picture light, LED strip, plant uplight, shelf light.

Use warmer light around 2700K–3000K for relaxing rooms and neutral/cooler light for work areas when appropriate.

### 6. AI prompt pack

When the user wants image-generation prompts, produce:

- Main prompt.
- Negative prompt.
- 2–3 variations by budget, angle, or style intensity.
- Notes for preserving existing room features when using image-to-image tools.

Prompt formula:

```text
[room type], [style] interior design, [mood], [key layout], [palette], [materials], [lighting], [important furniture], [camera / realism phrase], [constraints]
```

Negative prompt formula:

```text
clutter, distorted geometry, unrealistic windows, oversized furniture, harsh ceiling light, glossy plastic, overdecorated shelves, low quality render, warped walls
```

### 7. Implementation checklist

End with practical steps:

1. Measure and photograph the room.
2. Remove visual clutter.
3. Choose palette and key materials.
4. Solve layout and storage before decor.
5. Add lighting layers.
6. Buy textiles and accessories last.
7. Verify safety and installation requirements.
