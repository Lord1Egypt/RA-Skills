# 3PL Evaluation — Output Template

Use this structure for every 3PL comparison or audit deliverable. Never omit Red Flags, Assumptions, or Negotiation Levers.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Recommendation:** [provider / renegotiate / stay / hybrid] in one sentence
- **Landed cost per order (regular / peak):** [winner's numbers vs. current state]
- **Estimated switching cost & payback:** [$ and months, if switching]
- **Top risk:** [one sentence]
- **Decision deadline driver:** [contract renewal date, peak prep cutoff, etc.]

## 5. Red Flags Found

Numbered list, per candidate. Contract clauses, uncapped surcharges, reference-call concerns, capacity doubts, integration gaps.

## 6. Negotiation Levers (preferred candidate)

| Lever | Ask | Fallback |
|---|---|---|
| e.g., Peak surcharge | Cap at 12% of invoice | Cap at 15% with 30-day notice |
| Onboarding fee | Waive | Credit against month 3 |
| SLA credits | 2%/point below 99.3% accuracy | 1%/point |
| Exit clause | 60-day no-cause | 90-day |

## 7. Switching / Transition Plan (if applicable)

- One-time costs: inventory transfer freight, dual-running overlap (weeks × cost), integration setup, onboarding fees, team time → **total**
- Payback period vs. savings
- Cutover timing recommendation (volume trough month) and rollback trigger
- First-90-days invoice audit plan

## 8. Assumptions & Data Gaps

Numbered. Every estimated rate, every basket simplification, every unverified vendor claim.

---

### Formatting rules

- All per-order costs to 2 decimals; one-time costs rounded to hundreds.
- Mark estimated/unverified numbers with "(est.)".
- Winner column bolded in tables; sort candidates by weighted total descending.
- Keep executive summary under 120 words.
