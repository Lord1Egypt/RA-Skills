## Description: <br>
Use when interpreting research findings, evaluating scientific evidence, analyzing mechanisms, comparing competing hypotheses, designing experiments, or constructing scientific arguments. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Researchers, developers, and external users use this skill to structure scientific answers around problem framing, evidence provenance, competing hypotheses, claim calibration, limitations, and next steps. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Implicit invocation may shape research-adjacent answers when the user did not explicitly request scientific reasoning. <br>
Mitigation: Disable or narrow implicit invocation when the skill should only run for explicit research, experiment-design, or evidence-evaluation requests. <br>
Risk: In medical, safety-critical, or other high-stakes topics, structured reasoning can still produce misleading guidance if the underlying evidence is incomplete. <br>
Mitigation: Require explicit evidence provenance, uncertainty boundaries, and human review before relying on outputs in high-stakes decisions. <br>
