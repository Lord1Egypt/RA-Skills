Style Pack: whiteboard-sketch (whiteboard sketch)
- Favor bright didactic explainer layouts, timelines, concept boards, mirrored comparisons, teaching grids, and structured split slides; use full-bleed slides only when the content is short and the hand-drawn explainer tone can still stay clear.
- Use warm paper-white or soft cream surfaces with dark slate or charcoal body text, blue marker-like accents, and occasional restrained soft-gold highlight lines.
- Typography attitude: clean bold sans or contemporary educational headline with readable sans body text; keep the text typed and crisp while diagrams, arrows, and iconography feel hand-drawn.
- Surface/material cues: notebook paper, whiteboard sheet, soft paper grain, light sketchbook texture, and lightly imperfect drawn strokes rather than polished corporate vectors.
- Diagram rendering language: hand-drawn outline icons, marker arrows, rounded sketched boxes, loop diagrams, timeline curves, and doodled technical callouts.
- Composition attitude: strongly organized but not rigid; prefer implied zones, floating teaching modules, and reading rhythm over obvious card containers.
- Keep the slide bright and instructional, with low clutter and strong section hierarchy; this pack is excellent for technical walkthroughs, product explainers, educational decks, and self-contained teaching slides.
- Avoid cinematic shading, glossy UI polish, photoreal hero scenes, or dark dramatic backgrounds.
