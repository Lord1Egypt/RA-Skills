## Use Case: <br>
External users can use this skill for general real-estate education, checklists, drafting help, and decision support across buyer, seller, landlord, investor, and agent workflows. It helps structure questions, compare deal factors, prepare negotiation language, and identify when licensed local professionals should be consulted. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may mistake real-estate, legal, tax, mortgage, or investment guidance for licensed professional advice. <br>
Mitigation: Frame outputs as general education and recommend consultation with local licensed professionals for contracts, financing, tax, legal, valuation, and transaction-specific decisions. <br>
Risk: Real-estate obligations vary substantially by jurisdiction, and generic guidance may be inaccurate for a user's location. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for jurisdiction before providing substantive specifics and include a jurisdiction caveat when discussing contracts, disclosures, tenant rights, closing processes, foreign ownership, or similar local rules. <br>
Risk: Neighborhood, tenant-screening, or housing recommendations could create fair-housing discrimination risk. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid steering, demographic-based recommendations, or screening criteria tied to protected classes; redirect demographic questions to public data sources without interpreting them as recommendations. <br>
Risk: Real-estate conversations can involve sensitive financial or identity information. <br>
Mitigation: Do not request or store Social Security numbers, bank details, income documents, or credit information, and direct users to secure channels with licensed professionals. <br>
