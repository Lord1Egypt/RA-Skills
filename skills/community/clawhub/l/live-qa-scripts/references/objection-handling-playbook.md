# Objection-Handling Playbook

Reusable patterns for turning live-stream hesitation into purchase. Apply the framework, then fill with product specifics.

## The A-P-C Framework

Every objection answer has three beats:

1. **Acknowledge** — name the concern so the viewer feels heard ("Totally fair question.").
2. **Prove** — give one concrete proof: a spec, a live demo, a number, or a guarantee.
3. **Close** — end with a CTA pointing at the cart, code, or timer.

Keep all three inside 1–2 spoken sentences.

## Objection Patterns

### Price ("too expensive")
- Reframe to value or per-use cost.
- Stack the active promo and the guarantee to lower risk.
- Pattern: "It's {per-use cost} a use, and you're covered by {guarantee} — {code} takes it to {price} right now."

### Quality / durability
- Demo it live; let them watch the proof.
- Pattern: "Watch — {live demo}. That's why we back it with {warranty}."

### Authenticity ("is it legit")
- Cite the proof of authenticity (official store, certification, batch).
- Pattern: "We're the official store; every unit ships with {proof}. Tap the cart to get the real one."

### Sizing / fit
- Give a simple rule, remove the guesswork.
- Pattern: "If you're between sizes, {rule}. And returns are {policy}, so it's risk-free."

### Shipping speed
- State the window plainly; add urgency.
- Pattern: "Orders today ship {window}. Lock it in before the timer's up."

### Returns / risk
- Lead with the guarantee to remove fear.
- Pattern: "If it's not right, {returns policy} — zero risk to try it."

### Comparison to a competitor
- Acknowledge, then differentiate on one clear point. Never disparage.
- Pattern: "Great product too — the difference here is {one differentiator}."

## Escalation Rules

- If you can't substantiate a claim, drop it — don't improvise.
- If a viewer is hostile, answer once factually, then move on; don't feed conflict on stream.
- If a question is out of policy (medical, legal), give the safe general line and redirect to support.

## Momentum Tips

- Batch similar comments and answer once to the whole room.
- Always return to a CTA; curiosity without a next step is a lost sale.
- Re-pin the product and restate the code every few minutes for new arrivals.
