# GB/T 47041-2026 Operational Guide

This guide is a concise working summary of GB/T 47041-2026, `孤独症儿童康复机构服务质量及评价规范`. Use it for orientation, then verify exact wording in `../assets/GBT-47041-2026.md`.

## Basic Facts

- Standard: GB/T 47041-2026.
- Chinese title: 孤独症儿童康复机构服务质量及评价规范.
- English title: Specification for service quality and assessment for the rehabilitation institution of children with autism.
- Published: 2026-01-28.
- Implemented: 2026-05-01.
- Scope: service quality management and service quality evaluation for institutions providing rehabilitation services to children with autism.
- Proposal/administration: proposed by the Ministry of Civil Affairs and under SAC/TC148.

## Terms

- `孤独症`: a neurodevelopmental condition characterized mainly by social communication difficulty, restricted interests/activities, and repetitive or stereotyped behavior.
- `康复训练`: intervention training and inclusive education activities covering perception, movement, language, self-care, cognition, social communication, and emotional/behavioral areas.
- `孤独症儿童康复机构`: a professional social unit that provides rehabilitation training services for children with autism and their families, with appropriate technology, personnel, information, and management capacity.
- `转介`: support for a child moving between stages or institutions, such as adjusting service content, moving to kindergarten/school, hospital, or another suitable institution.

## Service Principles

- `专业化`: use evidence-based intervention methods suited to child development and needs; emphasize function improvement, environmental support, and social inclusion.
- `规范化`: build a quality assurance system, standard service流程, and collaboration between institution and family.
- `康教结合`: combine education-led intervention with rehabilitation/medical support through multidisciplinary cooperation.

## Service Quality Requirements

### Service Safeguards

- Institution: lawful registration or business qualification; clear charter; quality-control mechanism; file management system.
- Staff: service and support teams should be stable and sufficient. Professional staff to enrolled children should preferably be about 1:3 and must not exceed 1:6.
- Professional staff: include a full-time业务主管, professional assessment personnel, and professional teachers/trainers. Staff should have relevant education, systematic autism rehabilitation training, certificates where applicable, and at least one continuing-education activity each year.
- Support staff: no infectious disease or criminal record; appropriate ethics, competence, language, and behavior for interacting with children with autism.
- Site: comply with acoustic, fire safety, safety sign, and accessibility requirements. The environment should be orderly, simple, comfortable, visually supportive, and not overstimulating.
- Functional rooms: include reception/consulting, assessment, individual training, group/class training, sensory integration, language, social game, sports/activity, multifunction rooms, or equivalent spaces.
- Equipment: teaching aids, toys, rehabilitation equipment, assessment tools, books/resources, office/fire/logistics equipment; toys must meet GB 6675.1.

### Service Flow

The expected flow is:

1. Intake; refer elsewhere if the case is not suitable.
2. Establish rehabilitation file.
3. Conduct initial functional assessment.
4. Create an individualized service plan.
5. Provide rehabilitation training and individualized family support.
6. Assess rehabilitation effect.
7. Continue and adjust if effective; adjust plan/training/support if not effective.
8. End service and transfer/referral to a suitable institution or setting when appropriate.

### Service Content

- Rehabilitation file: create after intake, complete progressively, and protect privacy.
- Initial function assessment: match age and functional level; use evidence-based tools consistent with ICF-CY ideas. Examples named by the standard include VB-MAPP for 0-4, PEP-3 for 2-7, and ICF assessment/training system-ASD for 2-17.
- Service plan: based on assessment, child characteristics, function, and needs. Include case facts, assessment strengths/weaknesses, long- and short-term goals, curriculum, teaching method, schedule, outcome presentation, and evaluation.
- Training domains: sensory/perceptual, gross/fine motor, self-care, speech/language, cognition, social communication, and emotional/behavioral intervention.
- Effect assessment: use functional assessment tools before/after training, preferably every 3-6 months. Analyze, record, archive, and adjust the plan or refer as needed.
- Family support: respond to parent needs, provide consultation/training, encourage parent participation, create home-institution collaboration, conduct follow-up, provide monthly practical family/community-inclusion training, invite experts annually, and keep records.

## Evaluation Framework

The evaluation system has three levels:

- First-level indicators: service conditions, service norms, service effects.
- Second-level indicators: 13 total.
- Third-level indicators: 34 direct scoring indicators.
- Total: 100 points using additive scoring.

### Service Conditions: 20 Points

- Institution requirements, 3 points:
  - lawful operation and no adverse credit record, 1.
  - development plan, operating mechanism, security systems and emergency plans, 1.
  - complete and standardized systems/file management, 1.
- Personnel requirements, 7 points:
  - stable team with business lead, assessment personnel, teachers, and support posts, 1.
  - professional staff-to-child ratio about 1:3 and not above 1:6, 2.
  - college degree or above; relevant majors exceed 70%, 2.
  - relevant certificates/training and qualified support staff, 1.
  - employee training/continuing education at least annually, 1.
- Site requirements, 3 points:
  - safety requirements met, 1.
  - suitable rooms/environment for autism rehabilitation, 2.
- Facilities/equipment requirements, 7 points:
  - assessment tools, 2.
  - rehabilitation training equipment, 2.
  - teaching auxiliary devices and toys/activity equipment/monitoring, 2.
  - teacher, parent, and professional books, 1.

### Service Norms: 20 Points

- Functional assessment, 4 points:
  - complete, standardized, professional service flow, 1.
  - initial assessment/file/service plan/parent guidance, 3.
- Service plan, 4 points:
  - individualized plan based on functional assessment, plus staged process assessment and adjustment records, 4.
- Rehabilitation training, 4 points:
  - curriculum aligned with individualized plan, evidence-based methods, required training domains, and completion rate at least 80%, 4.
- Methods/techniques, 3 points:
  - training integrates diverse methods, at least two methods/techniques; see Appendix A, 3.
- Training modes, 2 points:
  - adjust time/frequency and proportions based on assessment and plan; examples include individual training at least 30 minutes per person per day and game/social interaction class at least 3 times per week, 2.
- Support services, 3 points:
  - weekly parent-child co-training with records, monthly inclusion activity or course, quarterly community inclusion activity, clear goals and participation, plus parent training/guidance records, 3.

### Service Effects: 60 Points

- Functional improvement, 48 points:
  - overall function improves in semiannual assessment, 8.
  - verbal and nonverbal communication improves, 6.
  - social interaction improves, 6.
  - emotional/behavioral condition improves, 6.
  - cognitive understanding improves, 6.
  - social adaptation improves, 6.
  - self-care improves, 5.
  - sensory and motor ability improves, 5.
- Parent satisfaction, 6 points:
  - survey rate at least 90%, 2.
  - regular feedback and timely resolution; satisfaction at least 70%, 4.
- Parent cooperation, 6 points:
  - monthly parent courses and annual expert training with complete records, 2.
  - practical training suited to autism characteristics and using published/standard materials, 2.
  - parent knowledge of autism and rehabilitation above 60%, 2.

## Evaluation Result

- Calculation: add all element scores.
- Grades:
  - `优秀`: 90 and above.
  - `良好`: 80-89.
  - `合格`: 60-79.
  - `不合格`: below 60.
- Evaluation report should cover institution overview, evaluation process, results, conclusion, problems, and improvement suggestions.
- Results may support internal assessment, organizational evaluation, and social supervision; institutions should analyze results and improve continuously.

## Evaluation Implementation

- Principles: clear goals/indicators/scoring, standardized process, evidence collection and analysis, consistent criteria, and improvement orientation.
- Methods: document review, field observation, questionnaire survey, interviews, random class observation, professional assessment, and social supervision.
- Questionnaire sampling: survey all or at least 80% of parents and relevant staff where a questionnaire is used.
- Interview sampling: randomly interview at least 10% of parents or relevant staff where interviews are used.
- Self-evaluation: institution should form an internal quality-control group and preferably evaluate every six months.
- Third-party evaluation: institution or authority may commission qualified third-party evaluators, including autism rehabilitation and special education experts.
- Quality improvement: use meetings, satisfaction feedback, social supervision, annual inspection, and third-party findings to identify causes and implement corrective measures.

## Informative Appendices

Appendix A lists common methods and modes, including ABA, PRT, PBS, NDBI, TEACCH, social/communication training, social education, speech therapy, sensory integration, occupational therapy, and physical therapy.

Appendix B provides a parent satisfaction questionnaire covering institution conditions, service content, service attitude, rehabilitation effect, and open comments. The overall judgment uses counts of satisfied/basic satisfied/unsatisfied selections.
