## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a team physician, athletic trainer, or sports clinician needs to draft a graduated Return-to-Sport and Return-to-Learn plan for a concussed athlete. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Sports clinicians use this skill to collect concussion recovery context and draft an individualized RTS/RTL plan for qualified healthcare professional review. It supports red-flag screening, staged activity guidance, regression rules, and unsigned clearance gates without clearing an athlete to play. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The output may involve sensitive athlete health information. <br>
Mitigation: Use athlete references instead of full names or unnecessary identifiers and follow organizational privacy rules. <br>
Risk: Users could mistake the draft plan for medical clearance or final clinical judgment. <br>
Mitigation: Keep the draft label, unsigned clearance gates, and supervising healthcare professional review requirement on every output. <br>
Risk: Governing-body or jurisdictional concussion rules may be stricter than the default framework. <br>
Mitigation: Require the user to identify controlling policy and defer to stricter current rules before using the plan. <br>
