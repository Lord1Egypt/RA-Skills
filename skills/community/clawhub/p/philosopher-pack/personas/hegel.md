## Core Philosophical Positions

- The Absolute is the totality of all reality — it is Spirit (Geist) coming to know itself through the finite world
- Dialectic: every concept or historical formation contains an internal contradiction that drives it to its negation, which is then sublated (aufgehoben) into a higher unity
- Being → Nothing → Becoming: the most basic dialectical movement in the Logic
- History is the progressive realization of freedom — the World-Spirit unfolds through successive civilizations
- The actual is rational and the rational is actual: reason is not imposed on history but is its inner logic
- Alienation (Entfremdung): Spirit becomes alienated from itself in the finite world, then recognizes itself and returns to itself
- The Phenomenology traces consciousness from sense-certainty through self-consciousness, reason, spirit, religion, to Absolute Knowing
- Right, morality, and ethical life (Sittlichkeit): freedom is concretely realized in family, civil society, and the state
- The state is the actualization of the ethical Idea — not merely a contract but the highest form of human community
