# well-completion-design-memo

Draft a structured hydraulic fracture completion design memo for an oil or gas wellbore, ready for licensed PE review before operations begin.

## Overview

Petroleum engineers and completion engineers at E&P operators, independents, and service companies must design and document hydraulic fracture completion programs for every new drill, re-completion, or workover. A well-structured completion design memo covers: target interval identification and staging, perforating design, fluid and proppant selection, pump schedule summary, production forecast basis, and mandatory NORM/H2S/HPHT hazard flags.

Without a structured process, completion design documentation is inconsistent, omits critical hazard disclosures, or lacks the traceability required for state oil and gas commission compliance and internal authorization workflows.

This skill guides the complete intake and produces a DRAFT completion design memo labeled for licensed PE review and sign-off before any completion operations begin.

## Use When

- A petroleum engineer, completion engineer, or reservoir engineer needs to document a hydraulic fracture completion program for a new horizontal or vertical well
- A re-completion or workover completion design needs to be documented for internal authorization
- A completion engineer wants a structured intake to ensure all required design elements (staging, fluid, proppant, hazard flags) are captured before the service company pump schedule is ordered

## Out of Scope

- Real-time pump job execution instructions or wireline perforation commands
- Reservoir simulation or fracture geometry modeling (requires specialized software)
- Signed wellbore schematics or state regulatory filing documents
- Production guarantee or reserve certification

## Workflow Summary

1. Well and project identification (name, API, geometry, wellbore status)
2. Target formation and interval identification (zone, depths, petrophysics)
3. Staging and perforating design (stage count, cluster spacing, isolation method)
4. Fluid and proppant selection (type, volume, mesh, concentration)
5. Pump schedule summary (MTP, BPM rate, ramp)
6. Production forecast basis (analog EUR, DCA, IP30/IP90 estimate)
7. Hazard screening (NORM, H2S, HPHT, water disposal)
8. DRAFT memo assembly with PE authorization block

## Output

A DRAFT well completion design memo with:
- Staging plan table
- Fluid and proppant program table
- Pump schedule narrative
- Production forecast basis (labeled PRELIMINARY ESTIMATE)
- NORM/H2S/HPHT WARNING blocks (where applicable)
- Documentation gap list
- PE authorization sign-off block

All output is labeled **DRAFT — NOT AUTHORIZED FOR OPERATIONS** until a licensed PE reviews and signs off.
