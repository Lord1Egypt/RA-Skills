## Compliance Audit Results

### Violation 1 — "Chapter 11 bankruptcy specialist"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1(b)  
**Issue:** No State Bar of Nevada specialization certification exists for bankruptcy law. Claiming "specialist" without certification is a disciplinable offense.  
**Fix:** Remove "specialist." Replace with: "Chapter 11 and Subchapter V reorganization attorney."  

### Violation 2 — No "This is an advertisement" disclaimer
**Rule:** SCR 192  
**Issue:** Missing on Google Ad, website, GBP description, and all solicitation materials.  
**Fix:** Add "This is an advertisement." to every piece of marketing content.  

### Violation 3 — No § 528 debt relief agency disclosure
**Rule:** 11 USC § 528(a)(4)  
**Issue:** "We are a debt relief agency. We help people file for bankruptcy relief under the Bankruptcy Code." is missing from all advertising.  
**Fix:** Add the required disclosure to every bankruptcy attorney advertisement.  

### Violation 4 — "Save your business"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (outcome guarantee)  
**Issue:** Guarantees an outcome — prohibited.  
**Fix:** Replace with: "We assess your situation and identify the path — reorganization, restructuring, or orderly closure."  

### Violation 5 — "Let you keep your equity while you rebuild"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (false/misleading)  
**Issue:** In standard Chapter 11 with dissenting unsecured creditors, the absolute priority rule prohibits equity retention without full payment to unsecured classes. This statement is false in most standard Chapter 11 cases.  
**Fix:** "In Subchapter V cases, equity retention without full unsecured payment may be possible if funded from projected disposable income. In standard Chapter 11, the absolute priority rule applies — we explain which rules apply to your situation in the consultation."  

### Violation 6 — "Nevada's top business reorganization firm"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (unverifiable superlative)  
**Issue:** "Top" is a superlative that cannot be verified and implies superiority without objective basis.  
**Fix:** Remove. Replace with specific experience indicators if available: "Representing Nevada businesses in Chapter 11 and Subchapter V reorganization since [YEAR]."  

### Violation 7 — "Pay your vendors what you can afford"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (false/misleading — creates preference exposure)  
**Issue:** Payments to vendors within 90 days of filing can be clawed back by the bankruptcy trustee as preferences (§ 547). Advising clients to pay vendors before filing without a preference analysis is false guidance that can cost the estate money.  
**Fix:** Remove. Add: "We perform a preference analysis before you file — payments made in the 90 days before filing may be recoverable by the trustee."  

### Violation 8 — "Eliminate bad leases" (incomplete)
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (misleading by omission)  
**Issue:** § 365 rejection is available but the 120-day deadline (§ 365(d)(4)), cure requirement for assumption, and § 502(b)(6) rejection damage cap are all omitted. Creates false expectation of unconditional lease exit rights.  
**Fix:** "Chapter 11 gives you a 120-day window to decide: assume the lease (with cure of arrears) or reject it (with landlord damages capped under § 502(b)(6)). We map your lease portfolio before you file."  

### Violation 9 — "Stop creditor harassment" (incomplete)
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (misleading by omission)  
**Issue:** § 362 automatic stay has exceptions (§ 362(b)): tax authority actions, criminal proceedings, domestic support collection, police/regulatory power. "Stop ALL creditor contact" creates false expectations.  
**Fix:** "The automatic stay halts most collection actions — with specific exceptions for tax authority assessments, criminal proceedings, and domestic support. We assess which creditors are affected."  

### Violation 10 — "Full access to your business accounts" (AI FAQ answer)
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (false/misleading)  
**Issue:** § 363(c)(2) prohibits use of cash collateral without court order or secured creditor consent. Day 1 without a cash collateral order means no access to accounts subject to security interests.  
**Fix:** "You can continue operating, but access to accounts and receivables subject to security interests requires a court order called a cash collateral authorization. We file this before your filing date."  

### Violation 11 — "Guarantee personalized service" (GBP)
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (guarantee of service quality)  
**Issue:** Guaranteeing "personalized service" creates an implied quality guarantee.  
**Fix:** Remove "guarantee." Replace with: "Every engagement begins with a direct consultation with [ATTORNEY NAME], NV Bar [NUMBER]."  

### Violation 12 — "Helped hundreds of businesses"
**Rule:** RPC 7.1 (unverifiable claim)  
**Issue:** "Hundreds" is a quantitative claim that must be verifiable. If not accurate, it's a false statement.  
**Fix:** Use actual verifiable numbers or remove the quantity claim entirely.  

### Violation 13 — No Subchapter V mention
**Issue (competence, not bar rule):** A business bankruptcy attorney marketing Chapter 11 in 2024+ without mentioning Subchapter V is likely driving away clients with under $7.5M in debt who could benefit from a much lower-cost reorganization path.  
**Fix:** Add Subchapter V content to website, GBP, and all advertising.  

### Violation 14 — Entity standing not mentioned
**Issue (competence):** No mention of Nevada entity good standing verification before filing. A revoked entity cannot maintain a Chapter 11 case.  
**Fix:** Add to intake checklist and website FAQ: "We verify Nevada Secretary of State standing before filing."  

---

## Violations Corrected: 14/14. AUDIT PASS.

**FAQ Answer (Compliant):**
> Q: Can we keep operating during Chapter 11?  
> A: Yes — but Day 1 requires a cash collateral order. Under § 363(c)(2), you cannot use cash, bank accounts, or receivables that are subject to a security interest without court authorization or secured creditor consent. This means access to your accounts may be restricted without an emergency court order. We file a cash collateral motion before your filing date so the order is in place before you open for business post-filing.
