# comprehensive-eye-exam-report

A structured documentation skill for Doctors of Optometry (OD) and optometric residents that converts encounter data into a complete, AOA-aligned comprehensive eye exam report.

---

## What It Does

Guides an OD or clinical documentation specialist through every section of a comprehensive eye examination — entering visual acuity, manifest refraction, binocular vision testing, anterior and posterior segment findings, intraocular pressure, ancillary test results, diagnoses, and treatment plan — and produces a DRAFT report for licensed OD review before any prescription issuance or medical record entry.

## When to Use

- Documenting a comprehensive eye exam (CPT 92002 / 92004 / 92012 / 92014)
- Completing structured optometric records for EMR entry
- Drafting clinical documentation that includes a refraction prescription
- Any encounter where a complete, structured exam report is needed for billing, referral, or continuity of care

## What It Produces

- Visual acuity table (entering and best corrected, each eye)
- Manifest refraction results with sphere/cylinder/axis/add
- Binocular vision and ocular motility findings
- Anterior segment findings (slit-lamp biomicroscopy)
- Intraocular pressure measurements
- Posterior segment findings (fundus examination)
- Ancillary test summary (OCT, visual fields, fundus photo — if obtained)
- Assessment with ICD-10 diagnoses and medical referral flags
- Treatment and follow-up plan
- Licensed OD review and signature block

## Scope and Boundaries

- Produces DRAFT documentation only — all content requires licensed OD review and signature before any prescription is issued or any content enters the medical record
- Does not issue prescriptions or constitute a clinical decision
- Medical referral flags are decision-support reminders, not clinical directives
- PII handling: collects initials + date-of-birth year only; never stores or transmits full patient name, date of birth, or insurance information

## Requirements

- Encounter data from the OD or documenting staff (VA measurements, refraction findings, slit-lamp and fundus notes)
- ICD-10 coding reference (the skill prompts for diagnoses but does not guarantee coding accuracy; licensed OD confirms codes)
