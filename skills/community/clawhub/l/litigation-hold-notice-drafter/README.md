# Litigation Hold Notice Drafter

**Domain:** Legal · E-Discovery & Preservation
**Platforms:** Claude · Codex

## Purpose

Drafts a complete, four-part **litigation hold (preservation notice) packet** that in-house counsel, outside litigation counsel, a supervising litigation paralegal, or a compliance officer (working with counsel) can review and approve before distribution. Aligned to **FRCP 26(b)(1)**, **FRCP 34**, **FRCP 37(e)**, **Zubulake v. UBS Warburg**, and the **Sedona Conference Principles**.

The packet has four linked deliverables:

1. **Custodian Hold Notice** — the message custodians receive
2. **ESI / Data-Source Inventory** — a worksheet IT and counsel use to scope collection
3. **Acknowledgment Form** — what each custodian returns
4. **Counsel Review & Release-Criteria Checklist** — counsel-only worksheet covering trigger documentation, scope justification, departing employees, backups, privilege, cross-border data, update cadence, release criteria, and FRCP 37(e) sanctions awareness

Output is always labeled **DRAFT — FOR COUNSEL REVIEW — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION**.

## When to Use

- A complaint, demand letter, subpoena / CID, government investigation, internal investigation, regulatory inquiry, or credible threat of suit has triggered (or may have triggered) a duty to preserve
- A litigation team needs a defensible, structured preservation packet ready for counsel review
- A long-running matter needs a re-issued hold or a tailored variant for a custodian segment (executive, departing employees, IT, sales)
- Counsel needs a one-page Release-Criteria checklist to standardize how holds are lifted

## What It Does

1. Gates the session on counsel-of-record, role, and jurisdiction / rule set
2. Walks one-question-at-a-time intake of trigger event, reasonable-anticipation date (counsel's call), claims, opposing party, events-at-issue date range, subject-matter categories, custodians, data sources, third parties, sensitive-scope flags, retention / auto-delete posture, and tone
3. Confirms a scope summary with counsel before drafting
4. Drafts the four-deliverable packet, with every scope choice traceable to a counsel input
5. Runs a gap and accuracy check covering counsel of record, trigger documentation, scope traceability, third-party control, privilege, cross-border data, draft labeling, and FRCP 37(e) sanctions awareness
6. Appends an Unresolved Information block for every item counsel must verify

## Notes

This skill produces a **DRAFT preservation packet**, not legal advice. It does **not** decide whether the duty to preserve has been triggered, **does not lift or narrow a hold**, **does not advise custodians on personal exposure**, and **does not send the notice**. Distribution, scope decisions, third-party-control determinations under FRCP 34(a)(1) / Sedona Principle 6, cross-border (GDPR / UK-GDPR / PIPL / LGPD / blocking-statute) decisions, and hold release are reserved to licensed counsel.

The matter codename is used throughout. Do not paste real client names, opposing-party names, employee names, claim numbers, or account numbers into intake; counsel inserts those only where the workflow requires.
