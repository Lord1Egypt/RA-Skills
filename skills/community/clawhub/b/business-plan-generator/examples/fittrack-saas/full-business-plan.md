## Section 1: Executive Summary

### The Problem

Personal trainers are buried in administrative overhead. The average independent trainer uses four to six separate apps to run their practice — one for scheduling, another for billing, a spreadsheet for client notes, and a fitness app for workout delivery. This fragmentation costs trainers an estimated eight hours per week in context-switching and manual data entry, according to a 2024 survey of 1,200 fitness professionals by NASM. For a trainer billing $80–$150 per session, that lost time represents $640–$1,200 in unrealized revenue every week.

### Our Solution

FitTrack is an all-in-one practice management platform built specifically for independent personal trainers. Trainers use one tool to build and deliver workout programs, track client progress, schedule sessions, collect payments, and message clients — eliminating the app stack entirely. Unlike generic fitness apps, FitTrack is designed for the trainer workflow, not the end consumer.

### Target Market

FitTrack targets the 340,000 independent personal trainers in the United States — professionals who run their own client books outside of a gym employment arrangement [IBIS World, 2024]. The market is growing at 11% annually as remote and virtual training accelerates post-pandemic. The addressable market for trainer SaaS tools in the US alone is estimated at $680M.

### Business Model

FitTrack operates on a freemium SaaS model. The Free tier supports up to 3 clients and serves as the acquisition funnel. Pro ($49/month) unlocks unlimited clients, automated billing, and progress analytics — targeting trainers with 5+ active clients. An Agency tier ($119/month) serves boutique gyms and multi-trainer studios.

### Traction

FitTrack has a 60-person waitlist acquired through Instagram organic content and two fitness professional Facebook groups. Three trainers are on a paid pilot at $25/month and provided the product requirements that shaped the current feature set. NPS from pilots: 72. Average session time: 23 minutes/day.

### The Team

Marcus Chen (CEO) spent eight years as a Senior Product Manager at Peloton, where he owned the instructor-facing coaching tools used by 4M+ members. Dana Torres (CTO) is a full-stack engineer and former Fitbit platform architect who has scaled B2C fitness apps to 500K users. Combined, the founding team brings product-market intuition and technical depth that purpose-built fitness SaaS requires.

### The Ask

FitTrack is raising $75,000 in pre-seed funding on a SAFE note at a $1.5M cap. Capital will be deployed over 12 months: 60% to product development (mobile app, billing automation), 25% to customer acquisition (paid social, content), 15% to operations. This runway takes us to 200 paying customers and $9,800 MRR — the target threshold for a $500K seed round.

### Vision

In five years, FitTrack is the operating system for independent fitness professionals globally — the tool every trainer uses to run their business from the moment they go independent.

## Section 2: Market Analysis

### Market Overview

The global fitness app market was valued at $1.54B in 2023 and is projected to reach $4.6B by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 17.1% [Grand View Research, 2024]. Within this market, the B2B fitness professional segment — software sold to trainers, coaches, and studios rather than consumers — is a distinct and underserved niche currently dominated by three players with aging technology stacks. Independent personal trainers represent the fastest-growing segment, fueled by the rise of online coaching (which grew 300% from 2019-2023), hybrid training models, and the decline of traditional gym employment. Four macro tailwinds drive demand: remote/virtual training normalization, creator economy monetization tools, demand for personalized health experiences, and mainstream adoption of wearable data.

### Market Trends

- **Virtual training growth:** 42% of personal trainers now offer hybrid or fully virtual sessions, up from 12% pre-2020 [NASM, 2024] — expanding the total addressable user base geographically and increasing demand for digital client management tools.
- **Creator monetization:** Fitness professionals increasingly treat their practice as a media business; tools that support client acquisition and retention (not just delivery) command premium pricing.
- **Wearable data integration:** Apple Watch + WHOOP + Garmin data is now expected in premium training packages — trainers who can surface this data in client check-ins command higher rates, creating demand for integrations.
- **Payment automation:** Gen Z clients expect Stripe-level payment UX; trainers on Venmo/Zelle lose credibility with premium clients. Automated billing is becoming table stakes.
- **Trainer burnout:** The #1 reason trainers leave the profession is administrative overwhelm, not income — tools that reduce admin have strong word-of-mouth in trainer communities.

### Financial Risks + Mitigations

- **Churn exceeds 5%/month:** If churn rises above model assumptions, MRR compounds negatively. Mitigation: in-app onboarding checklist targeting activation within 7 days (activation rate >60% is the leading indicator of churn control).

- **Runway shortfall:** If seed raise is delayed beyond Month 12, runway tightens. Mitigation: revenue at Month 9 covers operational overhead; the $75K raise is not a survival necessity post-Month 9 — it's a growth accelerator.

### Operations Plan

FitTrack is a cloud-native SaaS application built on React (web), React Native (iOS/Android), Node.js API, PostgreSQL on AWS RDS, S3 for video/asset storage, and Stripe for billing. New customer workflow: (1) Trainer signs up free → (2) Completes 5-step onboarding (add first client, build first workout, send first check-in) → (3) 7-day activation window → (4) In-app Pro upgrade prompt at client 4.

The platform scales horizontally on AWS ECS; current architecture supports 10,000 concurrent users without re-architecture. At 5,000 customers, we introduce a CDN layer (CloudFront) for video delivery and upgrade to RDS Multi-AZ.

**Key operational metrics:** Daily Active Trainer rate (target: >60%), workout completion rate per client (target: >70%), billing collection rate (target: >95%), support ticket resolution time (target: <4 hours), monthly churn (target: <3%).
