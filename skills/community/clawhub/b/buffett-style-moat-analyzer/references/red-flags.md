# Red Flags

Use this reference when the user asks for a harsh review, downside analysis, or acquisition/stock risk screen.

## Fatal Business Quality Questions

Ask:

- What if growth stops?
- What if the largest customer leaves?
- What if paid acquisition costs double?
- What if the platform changes the rules?
- What if a better-capitalized competitor copies it?
- What if interest rates stay high?
- What if the founder is removed?
- What if the product becomes a commodity?

## Accounting And Financial Red Flags

Watch for:

- revenue growing while free cashflow gets worse
- repeated "one-time" adjustments
- aggressive capitalization of costs
- rising receivables faster than revenue
- inventory growth that outpaces sales
- frequent equity issuance
- debt-funded buybacks
- acquisitions masking weak organic growth
- management emphasizing adjusted EBITDA while cashflow is poor

## Strategy Red Flags

Watch for:

- no clear customer
- no reason customers must stay
- growth dependent on discounts
- "AI" or trend language replacing economic evidence
- expansion into unrelated areas
- weak distribution
- dependence on a marketplace, app store, search engine, or social platform
- brand claims without willingness-to-pay evidence

## Private Business Red Flags

Watch for:

- owner is the business
- messy books
- customer concentration
- supplier concentration
- no repeat revenue
- no documented process
- staff likely to leave after sale
- unpaid family labor
- old equipment or hidden capex
- revenue inflated by temporary demand
- local reputation that cannot transfer

## Too-Hard Pile Triggers

Use "too hard pile" when:

- economics are unclear
- moat depends on technical claims you cannot verify
- management is promotional
- financials are too complex
- the business requires precise macro predictions
- the investment case depends mostly on future hype
- the user provided too little evidence
