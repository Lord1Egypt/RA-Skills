# Dunnage Selection Guide

Pick dunnage based on how the product actually fails in transit, not based on what looks good in the photo.

## The four damage modes

1. **Crush** — box is compressed vertically in the stack. Threat: boxes collapsed, cardboard cartons dented, glass cracked.
2. **Shock** — package is dropped or hits a conveyor belt wall. Threat: glass broken, screens cracked, electronics fail.
3. **Abrasion** — product rubs against inner surfaces or itself. Threat: painted labels scratched, finishes scuffed, leather creased.
4. **Environmental** — moisture, heat, UV during storage or outdoor delivery. Threat: corrugate weakens, labels ink-jet bleeds, cosmetics separate.

A given product usually has two dominant damage modes. Design for both.

## Dunnage options, by cost and protection

| Method | Protection strength | Unit cost (USD at 10k) | Visual / brand feel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Molded pulp | High — rigid, product-fit | $0.25–$0.80 | Premium, natural, recyclable |
| Honeycomb kraft | High — rigid, flat-pack | $0.15–$0.40 | Craft, sustainable, structural |
| Die-cut foam insert | Very high — any shape | $0.40–$1.50 | Industrial, tech |
| Air pillows | Medium — cushion | $0.02–$0.08 | Cheap, visually invisible |
| Tissue paper | Low — surface only | $0.03–$0.06 | Soft, premium when branded |
| Corrugate dividers | High — for multi-unit | $0.10–$0.25 | Utilitarian |
| Kraft void fill (crinkle) | Low–medium | $0.05–$0.15 | Rustic, artisanal |
| Packing peanuts | Medium — messy | $0.02–$0.05 | Avoid; environmentally bad |
| Bubble wrap | Medium | $0.03–$0.10 | Avoid for premium brands |

## Match dunnage to damage mode

- **Glass, cosmetics, fragrance** → molded pulp clamshell, or foam insert. Never loose fill alone.
- **Apparel, soft goods** → tissue + polybag + box. No hard dunnage needed.
- **Electronics** → die-cut foam, or molded pulp with foam tray for the device surface.
- **Books, prints, paper** → rigid board mailer or corrugate divider to prevent bend.
- **Ceramics, homewares** → double-walled box + molded pulp + tissue cradle. Budget for it.
- **Food, perishable** → insulated liner (kraft-backed foil or recycled denim) + gel packs.

## Cost vs. damage tradeoff

If your current damage rate is 2% and each damaged unit costs you $40 in refunds, reships, and LTV loss, and upgrading dunnage from air pillows to molded pulp costs $0.35/unit — you break even at a damage rate improvement of just 0.875 percentage points. Almost any upgrade from loose fill to molded protection pays back.

## Sustainability — what actually matters

Customers (and increasingly marketplaces) care about recyclability. In order of actual-recycled-at-curbside:

1. Corrugate, kraft, molded pulp → widely recycled.
2. LDPE polybags (with store drop-off mark) → dropped at grocery store bins, not curbside.
3. Mixed paper/plastic laminates → usually landfill.
4. "Compostable" PLA bioplastic → only commercial composting, not home.

Don't claim compostable if the customer realistically has nowhere to compost it.
