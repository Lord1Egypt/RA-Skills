## Description: <br>
Computes IFRS 9 expected credit loss workflows, including forward-looking PD adjustment, survival-analysis PD estimation, loan amortization schedules, and Basel III impairment support. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Developers, finance engineers, and risk analysts use this skill to generate or adapt IFRS 9 ECL, PD, LGD, EAD, staging, amortization, and related financial-analysis workflows. Review carefully because the bundled evidence also describes market-data, backtesting, and trading-oriented behavior. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The release is labeled as an IFRS 9 credit-loss tool, but security evidence says the files also steer agents toward stock and crypto strategy building, market-data collection, backtesting, and broker-style trading workflows. <br>
Mitigation: Read the files before installation, restrict use to the intended financial-risk workflow, and require explicit manual confirmation for any trade, purchase, or account-affecting action. <br>
Risk: The server evidence flags crypto, purchasing capability, and sensitive credentials as capabilities. <br>
Mitigation: Use an isolated environment and avoid broker, paid-data, exchange, wallet, or other sensitive credentials unless they are sandboxed, read-only, and necessary for the task. <br>
Risk: Financial and regulatory outputs can be misleading if staging, amortization windows, PD/LGD/EAD inputs, or floating-point validation are wrong. <br>
Mitigation: Validate inputs, review stage-specific ECL windows and ECL formula components, and have qualified finance or model-risk reviewers approve outputs before relying on them. <br>
