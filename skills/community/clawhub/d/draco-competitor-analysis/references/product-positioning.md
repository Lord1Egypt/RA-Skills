# Product Positioning Competitor Analysis

Use when the task is to compare how products position themselves, define users, frame scenarios, articulate value, or build differentiation.

## Positioning formula

A product can be described as:
> a product category for **target users**, in **specific scenarios**, solving **specific problems**.

## Five basic elements

### 1) User
Ask:
- Who exactly is the product for?
- What are the user profile, spending habits, and deeper motivations?
- What emotional or psychological need is being triggered?

Useful layers:
- user profile
- consumption preference
- latent motivation

### 2) Scenario
Ask:
- In what life / emotional / social situation is the need activated?
- Is the scenario clear and specific?
- Is it high-frequency, high-value, or newly created?

### 3) Problem
Ask:
- What pain point, itch point, or delight point is being addressed?
- Is the problem strong enough to matter?

### 4) Solution
Ask:
- How does the product solve the problem at the functional level?
- What emotional value does it provide?
- What social / identity value does it create?

### 5) RTB / support point
Ask:
- Why should the user believe this promise?
- What proof exists: technology, trust endorsement, word of mouth, design quality, brand strength?

## What to conclude

Do not end with “their positioning is clear.”
End with statements like:
- which user-scenario-problem chain is strongest
- which differentiation is real vs cosmetic
- where the competitor's RTB is strong or weak
- what gap or whitespace exists for us
