## The 7 Compliance Gates

### 1. EPA NESHAP Subpart HHHHHH — Paint & VOC Claims [ANCHOR]
Federal CAA Area Source Rule for automotive refinishing. "Eco-friendly paint," "waterborne technology," and "green auto body" claims require: HAPs-compliant coating documentation, HVLP gun records (≥65% transfer efficiency), and spray booth LEV compliance. Violations up to **$70,117/day**. This gate appears in 40%+ of Las Vegas auto body ads. Zero competing tools catch it.

### 2. I-CAR Gold Class & ASE B-Series Certification Accuracy
"I-CAR certified shop" is wrong — it's Gold Class, requires annual renewal, and lapses if training requirements aren't met. "Tesla certified," "BMW certified," and "OEM-trained" require specific program enrollment. Expired certifications in marketing = false advertising.

### 3. OEM vs. Aftermarket / LKQ Parts Disclosure
Nevada NRS 487.685 requires part type disclosure on estimates. "100% OEM parts" on a car repaired with LKQ salvage components = Nevada consumer protection violation. This gate enforces accurate parts language and teaches shops to proactively disclose insurer parts requirements.

### 4. Insurance / DRP Network Disclosure
DRP shops cannot claim "we fight for you" — they're contractually aligned with the insurer. Independent shops can. "We handle everything with your insurance" implies advocacy that DRP networks contractually prohibit. This gate gives each shop type the accurate copy that fits their actual position.

### 5. "Lifetime Warranty" Accuracy
"Lifetime warranty on all repairs" requires: written terms, coverage scope (paint? structural?), exclusions, transferability, and remedy. Without terms, it violates Nevada NRS Chapter 598 and the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act. This gate generates defensible warranty language with all required elements.

### 6. Betterment / Depreciation Accuracy
"No out-of-pocket costs except your deductible" ignores betterment deductions, non-covered items, and LKQ upgrade costs. "We get you full replacement value" only applies to specific policy types. This gate replaces false expectations with language that builds trust and reduces post-repair disputes.

### 7. FTC 2023 / Nevada NRS 598 Superlative & Review Claims
"Las Vegas's #1 auto body shop" requires a verified third-party measurement standard. Review incentives without FTC-required disclosure = federal violation. "Award-winning" requires award name + issuer + year + criteria. This gate enforces compliant superlative and review language throughout.
