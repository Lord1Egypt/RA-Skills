## Description: <br>
A Cognitive Immune System for OpenClaw that interrupts common LLM sycophancy failure modes, forces objective pushback, and uses Madhyamaka epistemology to break binary traps. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and developers use this skill to make an agent persistently more critical, blunt, and structured when reviewing ideas, plans, strategic choices, and possible blind spots. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill deliberately makes assistant responses more blunt, skeptical, and structured, which may be unsuitable where a neutral tone or direct answer is expected. <br>
Mitigation: Install it only for workflows that benefit from persistent critical review, and avoid it for neutral assistance or direct-answer conversations. <br>
Risk: Automatic premise challenges and decision deconstruction can slow planning conversations or redirect a user away from the requested answer. <br>
Mitigation: Use it when blind-spot discovery is desired, and disable or avoid it when uninterrupted planning or concise execution guidance is the priority. <br>
Risk: The skill is instruction-only, but its persistent behavior can influence every natural-language analysis turn. <br>
Mitigation: Review the skill behavior before deployment and verify that the always-on posture matches the intended agent experience. <br>
