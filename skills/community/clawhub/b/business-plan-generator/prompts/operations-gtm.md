# Prompt: Operations Plan + Go-to-Market Strategy

Generate the operations and go-to-market sections of a business plan, including team structure, 90-day launch plan, GTM channels, milestones, and risk analysis.

---

## Your Input

```
Business Name: [e.g., FitTrack]
Product/Service Type: [SaaS | physical product | service | marketplace | other]
Founding Team: [list each founder: Name, Role, Relevant Background]
Current Team Gaps: [skills/roles you still need to hire, e.g., "need backend engineer, Q3"]
Tech Stack / Delivery Method: [e.g., "React frontend, Node.js API, AWS, Stripe" OR "shipped via Shopify + 3PL"]
Primary Sales Channels: [e.g., "content marketing, paid social, outbound LinkedIn, referral"]
Target Launch Date: [e.g., "June 1, 2026" or "live now"]
Key Partnerships: [distributors, integrations, affiliates, resellers — or "none yet"]
Geographic Focus: [e.g., "United States, English-speaking markets"]
Top 3 Risks: [what could go wrong? e.g., "low adoption, competitor copies feature, churn"]
```

---

## Prompt

You are a startup operations strategist and go-to-market expert. Write the operations and go-to-market section of a business plan for the following company.

**Business:** [Business Name]
**Product Type:** [Product/Service Type]
**Team:** [Founding Team]
**Team Gaps:** [Current Team Gaps]
**Tech/Delivery:** [Tech Stack / Delivery Method]
**Sales Channels:** [Primary Sales Channels]
**Launch Date:** [Target Launch Date]
**Partnerships:** [Key Partnerships]
**Geography:** [Geographic Focus]
**Risks:** [Top 3 Risks]

Write the following sections:

---

### 1. Team & Organization (150-200 words)

For each founder/key hire, write 2-3 sentences covering their role, relevant experience, and why they're the right person for that role. Then add:
- **Organizational structure:** who reports to whom (text description, no org chart needed)
- **Current gaps:** which roles are unfilled and the plan/timeline to hire
- **Advisors:** placeholder for 1-2 advisor bios (mark as "[Add advisor name + background]")

### 2. Operations Plan (150-200 words)

Describe how the business delivers its product/service:
- **Infrastructure:** key systems, tools, platforms (use the tech stack provided)
- **Delivery workflow:** step-by-step how a customer goes from sign-up to value
- **Scalability:** how operations scale as customer count grows (what breaks at 100 customers? at 1,000?)
- **Key operational metrics:** the 3-5 numbers that tell you operations are healthy (e.g., uptime %, support ticket resolution time, order fulfillment rate)

### 3. Go-to-Market Strategy

**Phase 1: Launch (Days 1-30)**
List 5-7 specific tactical actions for the first 30 days. Each action = 1 bullet with: the tactic, the goal, and the success metric.

**Phase 2: Growth (Days 31-90)**
List 5-7 tactical actions for Days 31-90. Focus on what's been validated in Phase 1 and how to scale it.

**Channel Analysis Table:**
| Channel | Target Audience | Key Tactic | Est. CAC | Timeline to Results | Priority |
|---------|----------------|-----------|----------|--------------------|---------:|

Include the channels from the input. Add 1-2 complementary channels that make strategic sense.

**Referral / Viral Loop (if applicable):**
Describe the mechanism for organic growth (referral program, word-of-mouth, social sharing, community). If no viral loop exists, suggest one.

### 4. 12-Month Milestone Roadmap

Create a milestone table:
| Milestone | Target Date | Success Metric | Owner |
|-----------|------------|---------------|-------|

Include 8-10 milestones covering: product launch, first 10/100/1000 customers, first revenue, break-even, first hire, key partnerships, geographic expansion (if applicable).

### 5. Risk Analysis

For each of the top 3 risks provided, plus 2 additional risks you identify:

| Risk | Likelihood (L/M/H) | Impact (L/M/H) | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|-------------------|---------------|---------------------|

After the table, write 1-2 sentences on the single highest-priority risk and the contingency plan if the mitigation fails.

---

**Tone:** Operational and tactical. Investors want to see that you've thought through execution, not just the idea.  
**Format:** Use the headers and tables above. Be specific — avoid vague language like "leverage social media" in favor of "post 3x/week on LinkedIn targeting personal trainers, measuring follower growth and inbound leads."
