# Review Rubric

Use this for CEO/product, portfolio, and publish/unpublish decisions.

## Shelf-Space Rule

Treat scarce release slots as shelf space. A new candidate must beat a named incumbent or it stays draft.

Score each candidate 0-5, weighted to 100:

| Weight | Category | Question |
|---:|---|---|
| 30 | Demand reality | Does current usage, growth, retention, or user pull exist? |
| 20 | Strategic coherence | Does this compound the product line and audience? |
| 15 | Distribution efficiency | Does search/listing/channel fit make acquisition plausible? |
| 15 | Quality and trust | Are permissions, privacy, reviews, and policy risk acceptable? |
| 10 | Focus cost | Does it fragment attention or duplicate another product? |
| 10 | Option value | Is the test reversible and likely to teach something useful? |

## Gates

Green:
- Real demand or a strong specific wedge
- Clear user and job
- Low policy and trust risk
- Measurable 30-day success test

Yellow:
- Plausible but evidence-light
- Needs clearer positioning
- Overlaps another candidate
- Keep as draft until it beats a live item

Red:
- Near-zero users and no growth signal
- High policy/trust risk
- Novelty-only or time-bound
- Exists because it was easy to ship

## Required Output

Include:
- Ranked keep list
- Ranked cut list
- Hold-as-draft list
- The exact evidence behind each cut
- The first irreversible action that needs explicit approval
