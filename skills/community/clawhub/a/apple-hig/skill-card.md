## Description: <br>
Apple HIG provides practical Apple Human Interface Guidelines reference material for macOS and iOS UI decisions, including colors, typography, control sizing, layout spacing, Dark Mode, accessibility, and platform differences. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Developers and product designers use this skill when designing, implementing, or auditing macOS and iOS interfaces for an Apple-native look and feel. It is especially relevant for Electron, SwiftUI, AppKit, and iOS application UI work. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill summarizes Apple-style design practices and may not reflect the latest official Apple Human Interface Guidelines. <br>
Mitigation: Check important UI decisions against Apple's official Human Interface Guidelines before release. <br>
Risk: Platform-specific sizing and interaction guidance can be misapplied across macOS, iOS, Android, Windows, or web-only interfaces. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm the target platform and adapt control sizes, navigation patterns, and accessibility requirements accordingly. <br>
