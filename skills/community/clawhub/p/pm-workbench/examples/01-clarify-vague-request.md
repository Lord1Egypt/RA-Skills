# Example 01 — Clarify a vague request

## Scenario

A boss gives a fuzzy direction instead of a clear requirement.

## Input

> "My boss said our AI product needs more of a wow factor. Help me think about what that actually means."

## What good output looks like

A strong `pm-workbench` response should:

- recognize this is not a ready-made requirement
- separate the real problem from possible solutions
- identify what “wow factor” could mean in business terms
- ask 1-2 critical clarifying questions if needed
- turn the vague ask into a clearer product question

## Example target output

**Bottom line:** "wow factor" is not ready to become a feature request yet. It could mean demo drama, faster first value, more trustworthy AI output, or stronger product differentiation. Those are different problems and would lead to different roadmaps.

## Request Clarification Brief

### 1. Original ask

The boss is saying the AI product needs more "wow factor." The trigger seems to be competitor demos feeling more exciting.

### 2. Likely intent behind the ask

The request is probably not just asking for prettier UI. It may be pointing to one of four possible concerns:

- **Demo competitiveness:** competitors look more impressive in sales or investor moments.
- **First-use activation:** users do not feel value quickly enough.
- **Perceived intelligence:** the AI output feels ordinary or unreliable.
- **Differentiation:** the product lacks a memorable workflow advantage.

### 3. Problem vs proposed solution

**Likely problem:** the product is not creating enough perceived value in the moments that influence adoption, sales, or retention.

**Embedded solution assumption:** "add wow" implies visible new features may be the fix. That is premature. The right fix could be onboarding, output quality, workflow focus, or demo packaging.

### 4. Critical gaps

1. Where are we losing today: demos, signup-to-first-value, retention, sales conversations, or competitor comparisons?
2. What exactly looks stronger in competitors: flashier presentation, better output, faster time-to-value, or a more ownable workflow?

### 5. Reframed product question

What product gap is making our AI product feel less compelling in the moments that matter most, and what improvement would close that gap without turning into random feature-chasing?

### 6. Recommended next step

Do a clarification pass before brainstorming features. Ask the requester to identify the moment where "not enough wow" is hurting the business, then route the work into either feature evaluation, demo/readiness improvement, or core product quality work.

## Why this is useful

Without structure, vague leadership asks usually turn into loose brainstorming. This workflow helps PMs convert ambiguous direction into a usable decision context.
