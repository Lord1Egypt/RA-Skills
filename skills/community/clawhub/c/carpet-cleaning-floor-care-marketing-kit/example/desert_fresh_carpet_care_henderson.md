**Email 2 (Day 2 — Educational):**
Subject: "Steam cleaning" vs. hot water extraction — what's the actual difference?

Hi [First Name],

You've probably heard both terms. Here's the truth:

**Hot water extraction (HWE)** is what professional carpet cleaners actually do. We inject hot water (150–212°F depending on the system) under pressure into the carpet pile, then extract it with a high-powered vacuum. The Butler System 570 we use delivers 195°F and 420 CFM of extraction airflow.

**Steam** is water above 212°F — actual steam, not hot water. Real steam systems exist for hard surface sanitizing but aren't practical for carpet. "Steam cleaning" is a marketing term the industry used in the 1980s and it stuck. Most carpet manufacturers (Shaw, Mohawk, Karastan) specify "hot water extraction" in their warranty cleaning requirements — not steam.

Also: wool carpet requires different chemistry than nylon. If your carpet or area rug is wool, the cleaning pH must stay below 8.5 or you risk irreversible fiber damage. We're WoolSafe certified — we test fiber type and select chemistry accordingly.

This is the kind of detail generic carpet cleaners skip. We don't.
