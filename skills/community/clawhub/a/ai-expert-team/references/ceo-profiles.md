# CEO Profiles

Compact CEO profiles for the Phase 1 Expert Council. Treat each profile as a decision lens, not as persona roleplay.

## Munger

- Trigger domains: investment-like decisions, incentives, trade-offs, risk of self-deception, business judgment.
- Decision lens: invert the problem, inspect incentives, stay inside the circle of competence, avoid stupidity before seeking brilliance.
- Challenge style: asks what incentives are being ignored, what obvious failure mode is being rationalized, and what would make the decision foolish.
- Honest boundaries: not ideal for fast creative ideation, UX taste, or domains needing current technical implementation detail without specialist evidence.
- Research before answer: financial facts, legal constraints, market claims, current company data.

## Feynman

- Trigger domains: learning, explanations, conceptual debugging, unclear technical understanding, first-principles analysis.
- Decision lens: explain simply, separate naming from understanding, test the idea against reality, resist self-deception.
- Challenge style: asks for the simplest example, the observable test, and whether the explanation predicts anything.
- Honest boundaries: not a default business operator or brand strategist.
- Research before answer: scientific claims, version-sensitive technology behavior, numerical claims.

## Taleb

- Trigger domains: tail risk, fragile systems, irreversible downside, robustness, uncertainty.
- Decision lens: identify ruin, convexity, skin in the game, optionality, and hidden fragility.
- Challenge style: asks what breaks under stress, who pays the downside, and whether the upside justifies exposure to ruin.
- Honest boundaries: can over-penalize upside or speed if no counterbalance exists.
- Research before answer: finance, safety, infrastructure, production, and security claims.

## Steve Jobs

- Trigger domains: product taste, UX, focus, prioritization, simplification, launch narrative.
- Decision lens: start from user experience, remove noise, say no, protect coherence.
- Challenge style: asks what should be removed, what ruins the experience, and whether the product tells one clear story.
- Honest boundaries: not enough alone for backend reliability, security, or deep technical verification.
- Research before answer: user data, current product constraints, platform rules.

## Paul Graham

- Trigger domains: startup strategy, early product, user discovery, founder decisions, contrarian but practical product bets.
- Decision lens: make something users want, talk to users, optimize for learning, favor scrappy traction over polish.
- Challenge style: asks whether real users want this, what the smallest useful version is, and what surprising truth the plan depends on.
- Honest boundaries: not ideal for mature enterprise governance or high-compliance operations without additional specialists.
- Research before answer: market facts, user behavior, competitor claims.

## Elon Musk

- Trigger domains: technical architecture, aggressive execution, first-principles simplification, manufacturing or systems constraints.
- Decision lens: reduce to physics or fundamentals, remove parts, accelerate iteration, integrate when interfaces dominate cost.
- Challenge style: asks what requirement is fake, which part can be deleted, and what the fastest test is.
- Honest boundaries: can push unsafe speed or underweight organizational harm if not balanced by risk and verification specialists.
- Research before answer: engineering feasibility, safety, production systems, current code behavior.

## Karpathy

- Trigger domains: AI/ML systems, data/model loops, Software 2.0, educational technical explanation.
- Decision lens: inspect data, make the system visible, build from primitives, debug through concrete examples.
- Challenge style: asks for dataset examples, failure cases, evals, and simple baselines.
- Honest boundaries: not a general legal, finance, or marketing CEO.
- Research before answer: model behavior, library versions, eval results, benchmark claims.

## Ilya Sutskever

- Trigger domains: AI research direction, scaling, representation learning, long-horizon technical bets.
- Decision lens: learning signal, scale, prediction, emergent capability, deep research intuition.
- Challenge style: asks what capability should emerge, what bottleneck dominates, and whether the research bet has signal.
- Honest boundaries: not ideal for day-to-day product execution or UX decisions alone.
- Research before answer: current AI research, model capabilities, benchmark claims.

## Naval

- Trigger domains: wealth strategy, leverage, long games, specific knowledge, career leverage.
- Decision lens: ownership, compounding, permissionless leverage, long-term games with long-term people.
- Challenge style: asks where leverage comes from, what compounds, and whether the plan depends on trading time for money.
- Honest boundaries: not enough for operational execution details or compliance-sensitive advice.
- Research before answer: current market, legal, financial, and personal-context claims.

## MrBeast

- Trigger domains: content strategy, retention, packaging, attention systems, creative testing.
- Decision lens: audience retention, title/thumbnail promise, rapid testing, extreme clarity of viewer payoff.
- Challenge style: asks why anyone would click, where attention drops, and what measurable test proves the idea.
- Honest boundaries: may over-optimize for attention if not balanced by brand and ethics constraints.
- Research before answer: platform rules, current channel analytics, audience data.

## 張一鳴

- Trigger domains: product + organization, rational management, algorithmic decision systems, delayed gratification.
- Decision lens: reduce ego, build feedback loops, optimize information flow, use systems over slogans.
- Challenge style: asks what data would update the decision, what feedback loop is missing, and where emotion is distorting judgment.
- Honest boundaries: not enough alone for high-empathy UX or public narrative choices.
- Research before answer: organizational facts, product metrics, market data.

## Trump

- Trigger domains: adversarial negotiation, media attention, polarized branding, opponent modeling.
- Decision lens: attention control, leverage, framing, dominance in negotiation.
- Challenge style: asks who controls the frame, where leverage exists, and how opponents will react.
- Honest boundaries: high reputational and ethical risk; use as adversarial lens only, not default CEO.
- Research before answer: legal, PR, reputational, and stakeholder facts.

## 孫宇晨

- Trigger domains: crypto, narrative speed, attention arbitrage, fast execution in speculative markets.
- Decision lens: speed, visibility, distribution, opportunistic narrative capture.
- Challenge style: asks what narrative can move fastest and where attention can be captured.
- Honest boundaries: high-risk lens; must be paired with Taleb or legal / compliance review.
- Research before answer: legal status, token economics, platform constraints, current market data.
