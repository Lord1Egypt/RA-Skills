# Example 11 — Founder strategy decision

## Scenario

A founder must choose between short-term narrative momentum and deeper product quality investment.

## Input

> "I’m the founder of an AI product. We can either ship a fast but shallow ‘AI assistant everywhere’ layer in 4 weeks to create market buzz, or spend 8-10 weeks improving answer reliability and workspace memory so the product is materially better for existing teams. Cash runway is okay for now, but investor narrative pressure is real. Help me decide."

## What good output looks like

A strong `pm-workbench` response should:

- restate the true decision objective under current company conditions
- weigh investor narrative pressure against long-term product trust and compounding
- compare the two paths using explicit trade-offs
- recommend one path or a staged path with clear conditions
- make the next founder-level decision and communication move obvious

## Expected output shape

**Decision Brief**

## Example target output

# Decision Brief

## 1. Decision to be made

Should the company spend the next period on a fast "AI assistant everywhere" narrative layer, or on deeper reliability and workspace memory improvements?

## 2. What the company should optimize for now

Because runway is okay, the scarcest resource is not immediate survival. It is product trust and focus. Investor narrative matters, but it should be supported by a product story the company can keep defending after the demo.

## 3. Trade-off analysis

### Fast AI layer

Strong for short-term visibility and investor-facing momentum. Weakness: it may create a shallow story if existing users still feel reliability and memory gaps.

### Reliability + workspace memory

Stronger for durable product value, retention, and trust. Weakness: slower external narrative unless the team packages progress clearly.

## 4. Recommendation

Choose **reliability + workspace memory as the primary current-period bet**.

Use a narrow narrative wrapper to communicate the work externally, but do not make the shallow assistant layer the main product investment.

## 5. Why not the fast AI layer now

It spends the current period's scarce product attention on marketable surface area before the product has earned enough trust. That may help one demo cycle but weaken the next user or investor conversation if the core experience does not hold up.

## 6. What would change this call

The fast layer could become the right choice if runway becomes tight, if investor timing becomes existential, or if user data shows existing reliability is already good enough and discoverability is the real blocker.

## 7. Immediate next step

Frame the next 8-10 weeks as a trust-building product bet with visible milestones: answer reliability, memory quality, and a demoable workflow that shows why the deeper work matters.

## Why this is useful

Founder product decisions are rarely about features alone. They are about sequencing, trust, and what kind of company story the product can honestly support.
