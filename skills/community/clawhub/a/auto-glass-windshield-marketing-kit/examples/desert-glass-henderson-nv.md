### ADAS Recalibration Explainer Section

**Why ADAS Recalibration Is Required After Windshield Replacement**

Vehicles equipped with Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) — automatic emergency braking, lane keeping assist, adaptive cruise control, and more — rely on a forward-facing camera typically mounted at the top-center of the windshield. When the windshield is replaced, even a fraction-of-a-degree shift in camera angle can cause these systems to misread lane markings, fail to detect vehicles, or generate false alerts.

**Who needs recalibration?** Any vehicle with a camera behind the windshield. Common systems include Toyota Safety Sense (TSS), Honda Sensing, Subaru EyeSight, Volvo Pilot Assist, GM Super Cruise, Ford Co-Pilot360, and Hyundai SmartSense. Most vehicles manufactured after 2018 are equipped.

**Static vs. dynamic calibration:** Static calibration is performed in our calibrated bay using precision targets — no driving required. Dynamic calibration requires a road drive at highway speed while the vehicle's system learns the new camera position. Some vehicles require both. Desert Glass performs whichever your vehicle's system specifies.

⚠️ **Safety note:** Driving a vehicle with an uncalibrated ADAS camera is a safety risk. ADAS system malfunction due to improper recalibration is a recognized liability issue. We recalibrate in-house and provide written confirmation.
