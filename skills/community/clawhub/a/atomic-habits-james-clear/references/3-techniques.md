> Source: Atomic Habits by James Clear, Chapters 4-15

# Techniques: The Four Laws in Practice

## 1st Law — Make It Obvious

### Implementation Intention

Formula: "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."

> **Case: Voting study** (Chapter 4): People who were asked "At what time and on what route will you vote?" were far more likely to follow through than those who were simply asked "Will you vote?" The specific plan turned intention into action.

### Habit Stacking

Formula: "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."

Examples:
- After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for one minute.
- After I sit down for dinner, I will share one thing I learned today.

### Habit Scorecard

List your daily behaviors without judgment. Mark each as + (good habit), - (bad habit), or = (neutral). Awareness is the first step to change.

## 2nd Law — Make It Attractive

### Temptation Bundling

Formula: "After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT]."

Examples:
- After I exercise, I will listen to my favorite podcast.
- After I finish my work block, I will watch one YouTube video.

### Cultural Influence

Join a culture where your desired behavior is normal. We imitate the close, the many, and the powerful.

> **Case: The clean hotel room** (Chapter 9): Hotel guests were more likely to reuse towels when told that 75% of other guests reused them. Social proof is one of the most powerful motivators.

## 3rd Law — Make It Easy

### The 2-Minute Rule

When you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do. Master the habit of showing up, then scale up.

> **Case: The writer who wrote one sentence** (Chapter 13): A novelist described his approach as "writing one sentence a day." But he would always write more once he started. The trick was making the first action so easy there was no excuse not to start.

### Environment Design

Arrange your space so the right thing is the easy thing:
- Want to read more? Put your book on your pillow.
- Want to eat healthier? Put fruit on the counter, junk food in the back of the cupboard.
- Want to exercise? Sleep in your workout clothes.

### Commitment Device

Lock in future good behavior by making bad behavior harder:
- Leave your credit card at home to avoid impulse buying.
- Use a timed lockbox for your phone.

## 4th Law — Make It Satisfying

### Immediate Rewards

The brain prioritizes immediate pleasure over delayed gratification. Pair your habit with an immediate small reward.

> **Case: The chewing gum study** (Chapter 15): People were more likely to floss when they got a small reward immediately after — like a piece of gum. The delayed reward (healthy teeth) is not enough. The immediate reward is what makes the habit stick.

### Habit Tracker

Visual progress is a powerful motivator. Marking an X on a calendar creates a chain you don't want to break. "Don't break the chain."

### Accountability Partner

Create an immediate cost for missing a habit. Knowing someone will check on you creates the immediate consequence you need.
