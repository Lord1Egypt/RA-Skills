# Ecommerce Event Calendar Reference

This reference lists major ecommerce events, platform sale days, and seasonal demand peaks organized by month. Use it during Step 1 (Date Mapping) and Step 2 (Event Prioritization) of the campaign calendar workflow.

Traffic impact ratings are relative to an average non-event week for a typical ecommerce seller. Actual impact varies by category, platform, and audience.

**Traffic impact scale:**
- **5 -- Massive:** 3x+ normal traffic; tentpole event requiring full campaign treatment
- **4 -- High:** 2-3x normal traffic; strong event worth a dedicated campaign
- **3 -- Moderate:** 1.5-2x normal traffic; worth a focused push or tie-in
- **2 -- Mild:** 1.2-1.5x normal traffic; worth a content tie-in or lightweight promotion
- **1 -- Niche:** Minimal broad traffic lift but relevant for specific categories

## Recurring Platform Patterns

Beyond specific dated events, these recurring patterns affect campaign timing:

**TikTok Shop monthly promotional windows.** TikTok Shop frequently runs shorter (2-3 day) promotional events at the beginning or middle of each month. These are announced in Seller Center with 1-3 weeks of lead time. Check Seller Center weekly for new event announcements.

**Payday cycles.** Consumer spending spikes around common paydays (1st and 15th of the month in the US, last Friday of the month in many markets). Scheduling promotions to land during these windows can increase conversion rates.

**Platform algorithm boosts.** During major platform events, TikTok Shop typically boosts visibility for participating sellers. This applies to product listings, livestreams, and short-form video content. Participating in official events, even at a Tier 3 level, can yield outsized organic reach.

**Creator and affiliate cycles.** Many creators batch their content production and posting around platform events. Briefing creators 2-3 weeks before major events ensures your products are included in their event-related content.
