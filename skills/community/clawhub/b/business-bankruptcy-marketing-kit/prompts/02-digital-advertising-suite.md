### 5 GBP Q&A (Factually Compliant)

**Q1: What is Subchapter V and does my business qualify?**
Subchapter V is a streamlined Chapter 11 reorganization path created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 (11 USC §§ 1181–1195). To qualify, your business must have non-contingent, liquidated debts of $7.5 million or less at the time of filing. Key advantages: no disclosure statement (saves significant legal fees), no creditors' committee, debtor-only plan, and a modified absolute priority rule that lets equity retain interests if the plan is funded from projected disposable income. Most Nevada small and mid-size businesses with meaningful debt qualify. We verify your eligibility as the first step in our consultation. This is an advertisement. We are a debt relief agency.

**Q2: Can we keep operating the business during Chapter 11?**
Yes, but Day 1 requires a cash collateral motion. Under § 363(c)(2), a Chapter 11 debtor cannot use cash, accounts receivable proceeds, or other collateral securing a loan without the secured creditor's consent or a court order. This means your bank account and revenue may be frozen without emergency relief. We file a cash collateral motion before your filing date so court authorization is in place when you open for business on Day 1. We also handle DIP financing under § 364 if additional operating capital is needed. This is an advertisement. We are a debt relief agency.

**Q3: What happens to our leases and contracts when we file?**
Under § 365, Chapter 11 gives you the right to assume (keep) or reject (walk away from) executory contracts and unexpired leases — but with strict deadlines and conditions. For commercial real estate leases, you must assume or reject within 120 days of filing (extendable once to 210 days). Assumption requires paying all past-due rent and other defaults. Rejection means the lease is terminated as of the petition date, and the landlord's damages are capped under § 502(b)(6). We map your lease and contract portfolio before you file so you enter with a strategy, not a deadline surprise. This is an advertisement.

**Q4: What is a preference action and how does it affect us?**
A preference action allows the bankruptcy trustee to recover ("claw back") payments made to creditors in the 90 days before filing — or within 1 year for insiders (officers, family members, affiliates). The threshold for business debts is $6,825 in aggregate. Defenses exist: ordinary course of business, contemporaneous exchange for new value, new value provided after the payment. We perform a preference analysis in every engagement before you file — identifying exposure and, where possible, structuring pre-filing payments in ways that support the ordinary course defense. This is an advertisement. We are a debt relief agency.

**Q5: Can we keep our equity in the reorganized business?**
In Subchapter V, yes — if the plan is funded from the business's projected disposable income, existing equity can retain its interests even if unsecured creditors are not paid in full (§ 1191 modified absolute priority rule). In standard Chapter 11, the absolute priority rule applies: equity retains nothing while any dissenting class of unsecured creditors remains unpaid, unless the equity contributes new capital equal to the retained value (new value exception). We explain which rule applies to your case in the initial consultation. This is an advertisement.
