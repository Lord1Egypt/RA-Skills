## 1. Conclusion

**Policy Stance:** The Federal Reserve faces a **policy bind** — conflicting signals between growth slowdown (dovish pressure) and sticky inflation + oil price shock (hawkish pressure). The Fed will **hold rates at 3.50-3.75%** at the March 2026 FOMC meeting, maintaining restrictive stance despite softening labor market.

**Key Decision Metrics:**
- **Growth:** Slowing (Treasury yield curve flattening, 10Y at 4.20%)
- **Inflation:** Sticky + oil-driven upside risk (Brent $102 → +2.5-3.5% CPI transmission)
- **Labor:** Softening (eToro analyst note)
- **External:** Geopolitical risk premium (Iran war, Hormuz disruption)

**Policy Bind:** Classic stagflation setup — Fed cannot ease into oil-driven inflation without anchoring inflation expectations higher. Hold + wait is the only viable path.
