## Description: <br>
Humanizer rewrites AI-sounding text to sound more natural and human-written by identifying patterns such as inflated significance, promotional phrasing, vague attribution, em dash overuse, formulaic lists, and chatbot artifacts. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[Lion504](https://clawhub.ai/user/Lion504) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Writers, editors, and agents use this skill to revise pasted text or selected documents so the output reads less like generated prose while preserving the original meaning and intended tone. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill can rewrite text to reduce signs of AI generation, which may be inappropriate where school, work, publishing, or legal rules require disclosure of AI involvement. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only for allowed editing workflows, specify exact text or files, and review changes before accepting them. <br>
