# Failure Modes

Use this reference for pre-mortems, downside reviews, and brutal idea filters.

## Market Failure

The market failure usually sounds like:

- people like the idea but do not pay
- pain is real but not urgent
- buyer is too small
- buyer has no budget
- buyer already has a good-enough workaround
- problem happens too rarely
- market education is too expensive

Early warning:

- polite praise but no deposits
- no clear search demand
- low reply rate from the exact buyer
- users ask for many custom changes before paying

## Distribution Failure

Common causes:

- no existing audience
- no repeatable outbound channel
- paid ads too expensive
- SEO takes too long
- marketplace discovery is weak
- social content does not reach buyers
- founder avoids direct selling

Early warning:

- "we will go viral"
- "we will post daily"
- "people will share it"
- no list of reachable buyers

## Economics Failure

Common causes:

- low price with high support burden
- custom work disguised as software
- high churn
- high acquisition cost
- bad gross margin
- refunds or disputes
- too much manual fulfillment

Early warning:

- every sale needs a call
- every customer needs custom setup
- delivery takes longer than expected
- "we will automate later"

## Incentive Failure

Common causes:

- buyer is not the user
- user benefits but decision-maker pays
- channel partner wants a bigger cut
- sales team is rewarded for wrong behavior
- customer success hides product weakness
- marketplace benefits from volume, not quality

Early warning:

- everyone says someone else should pay
- stakeholders agree in theory but no one owns the purchase

## Execution Failure

Common causes:

- too many features
- vague owner
- weak follow-up
- no test deadline
- unclear success metric
- build quality matters less than buyer access, but builder overbuilds

Early warning:

- roadmap grows before first sale
- customer feedback is used as an excuse to delay selling

## Reputation Failure

Common causes:

- spammy outreach
- exaggerated claims
- using a famous name too aggressively
- poor fulfillment
- shallow AI output sold as expertise
- legal or compliance overreach

Early warning:

- copy feels deceptive
- product promise is bigger than proof
- user would be embarrassed if the pitch was public
