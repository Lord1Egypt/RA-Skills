## Scope of Acceptable Claims

### Actionable Administrative Acts (《行政诉讼法》Art. 12)

| Type | Typical Examples |
|------|-----------------|
| Administrative penalties | Fines, license revocation, administrative detention, production suspension |
| Administrative compulsory measures | Seizure, impoundment, freezing, restriction of personal freedom |
| Administrative enforcement | Compulsory demolition, vicarious performance |
| Administrative licensing | Denial/no response/revocation/amendment/renewal |
| Administrative confirmation | Rights confirmation, work-related injury determination |
| Administrative expropriation | Land expropriation, housing expropriation and compensation decisions |
| Administrative agreements | Government concession agreements, expropriation compensation agreements |
| Administrative inaction | Failure to perform statutory duties |
| Administrative benefits | Pensions, social insurance benefits |
| Government information disclosure | Refusal to disclose / no response |
| Others | Administrative rulings, reconsideration decisions |

### Non-Actionable Acts (《行政诉讼法》Art. 13)
- National defense, foreign affairs and other state acts
- Administrative regulations, rules, and normative documents (may be collaterally reviewed)
- Internal personnel decisions of administrative organs
- Final administrative rulings (where law provides for finality)

## Conditions and Deadlines for Filing

### Filing Conditions
1. Plaintiff is the counterparty or interested party of the administrative act
2. Clear defendant
3. Specific claims and factual basis
4. Within court's scope of acceptance and jurisdiction

### Filing Deadlines (Critical!)

| Situation | Deadline | Commencement |
|-----------|:--------:|-------------|
| After reconsideration | 15 days from receiving reconsideration decision | Date of receipt |
| Reconsideration period expired | 15 days after reconsideration period ends | Date of expiry |
| Direct filing (knew of act) | **6 months** (《行政诉讼法》Art. 46) | When knew or should have known |
| Direct filing (did not know) | Max 5 years (20 for real property) | Date act was made |
| Administrative agreements | Reference civil: 3 years (《行政协议规定》Art. 25) | When knew or should have known |

> ⚠️ Note: Before the May 1, 2015 amendment, direct filing period was 3 months (old law).

## Jurisdiction Rules

**Hierarchical Jurisdiction:**
| Level | Applicable Cases |
|-------|-----------------|
| Basic court | General first-instance admin cases |
| Intermediate court | Defendant is county-level gov't/customs/State Council dept./major complex cases |
| High/Supreme Court | Major complex cases within their region/nationwide |

**Territorial Jurisdiction:**
- General rule: court where the administrative organ that issued the act is located
- After reconsideration: court where original organ or reconsideration organ is located
- Restriction of personal freedom: court where defendant OR plaintiff is located
- Real property: place of property (exclusive jurisdiction)

## Litigation Participants

**Plaintiff:**
- Counterparty of administrative act
- Interested parties (fair competition right holders/neighboring right holders/victims)

**Defendant:**
- Administrative organ
- Organization authorized by law/regulation/rules
- Reconsideration upheld → original organ + reconsideration organ as co-defendants
- Reconsideration changed → reconsideration organ as defendant

**Third Party:**
- Person with legal interest in the disputed administrative act
- Court notifies or party applies to participate

---

## Burden of Proof

**Defendant (Administrative Organ)** → Bears burden of proving legality of the disputed act
- Must provide evidence within **15 days** of receiving complaint
- Failure to provide / unjustified delay → deemed to have no evidence

**Plaintiff:**
- Prove filing meets statutory conditions
- Inaction cases: prove application was submitted
- Administrative compensation/indemnity: prove damage and causation
- New evidence: plaintiff may submit evidence showing the administrative act is unlawful

> Note: If plaintiff's evidence fails to prove the act unlawful, it does not relieve the defendant of its burden of proof.

## Trial Procedure

### First Instance
- Ordinary procedure: collegial panel
- Summary procedure: single judge (clear facts + minor dispute + small claim)
- Trial period: ordinary 6 months / summary 45 days
- Mediation generally not applicable (exceptions: compensation/indemnity/discretion)
- Enforcement generally not suspended (but court may order suspension)

### Second Instance (Appeal)
- Appeal period: 15 days (judgment) / 10 days (ruling)
- Hearing method: collegial panel with oral hearing
- Written review: may proceed without oral hearing when facts are clear
- Trial period: 3 months

### Retrial
- Application deadline: 6 months after judgment takes effect
- Statutory grounds: reference civil procedure rules

## Judgment Types

| Judgment Type | Applicable Circumstances |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| Dismiss claims | Administrative act is lawful |
| Vacatur | Insufficient evidence / legal error / procedural violation / ultra vires / abuse of power |
| Performance order | Defendant failed/delayed performance of statutory duty |
| Payment order | Defendant should pay pensions/social security/minimum subsistence |
| Confirm unlawfulness | Act is unlawful but vacatur is inappropriate or moot |
| Confirm nullity | Act is substantially and obviously unlawful |
| Modification order | Administrative penalty is clearly improper / amount assessment is wrong |
| Compensation order | Unlawful act caused damage |
| Continued performance/compensation (admin agreements) | Agreement valid but defendant breached |
