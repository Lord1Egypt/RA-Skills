# Prompt 3: Review, Reputation & Referral System (PAID)

## Instructions

Use this prompt to build a complete review acquisition and reputation management system for your shop. Include your state and any known complaint patterns so responses are accurate.

---

## The Prompt

You are an expert reputation management specialist for automotive service businesses. Generate a complete review and referral system for the following shop. Apply FTC Endorsement Guide compliance rules — no incentivized reviews without disclosure, no review-gating, no directing only happy customers to review sites.

**Shop Information:**
- Shop name: [NAME]
- City, State: [CITY, STATE]
- Primary review platforms: [Google / Yelp / CarFax Service / Facebook / RepairPal / other]
- Current average rating: [X.X stars — or "unknown"]
- Most common complaint types: [wait times / pricing transparency / unexpected additional charges / part failure after repair / incorrect diagnosis / other]
- Referral reward offered: [Describe: dollar off next service, free oil change, gift card — OR "none"]
- Customer communication preference: [text / email / in-person handoff / all three]
- Services where reviews matter most: [e.g., transmission, AC, collision — high-anxiety jobs]

**Generate the following:**

1. **20 Post-Service Review Request Templates** (by service type):
   - 4 templates: Oil change / routine maintenance
   - 4 templates: Brake job / safety repair
   - 3 templates: AC service / climate control
   - 3 templates: Diagnostics / check engine light
   - 3 templates: Collision / body repair
   - 3 templates: Major repair (transmission, engine, etc.)
   
   Each template: SMS version (160 chars) + email subject + email body (100-150 words)
   Include: shop name, technician name (if provided), specific service performed
   DO NOT: offer incentive for leaving a review (FTC violation)
   DO NOT: say "if you had a great experience" or direct only satisfied customers to review sites (review-gating — Yelp TOS violation + FTC concern)

2. **15 Review Response Templates:**
   - 5 templates: 5-star / enthusiastic praise responses
   - 2 templates: 4-star / minor concern responses (acknowledge + invite back)
   - 3 templates: Pricing complaint responses (transparent, non-defensive)
   - 2 templates: Wait time complaint responses (empathetic + process improvement language)
   - 2 templates: "Part failed after repair" complaint (safety-first, offer to inspect)
   - 1 template: Incorrect diagnosis complaint (careful — legal liability language)
   
   Response guidelines:
   - NEVER reveal customer-specific repair details publicly (privacy)
   - NEVER make counter-accusations without documented proof
   - NEVER offer refund in public response (take to DM/phone)
   - DO invite reviewer to call or visit to resolve — shows future customers you care
   - Incorrect diagnosis responses: do NOT admit fault publicly; acknowledge concern, invite inspection

3. **FTC-Compliant Review Request Guide** (1-page PDF content):
   - What is review-gating and why it's illegal (FTC + Yelp TOS)
   - How to ask all customers (not just happy ones) for reviews
   - When and how to disclose review incentives (if any)
   - How to respond to negative reviews without creating liability
   - Fake review red flags and how to report them (Google, Yelp processes)

4. **Referral Program Framework:**
   - Program name suggestion
   - Reward structure (compliant: discount on future service, not cash-for-review)
   - 3 referral ask scripts (in-person, SMS, email)
   - Referral tracking system (simple — referral code or "mention [name]")
   - "Refer a Neighbor" program: shop is local — neighbor referrals are high-trust
   
   Compliance note: Incentive is for referring a customer — NOT for leaving a review. These are separate programs. Never tie the referral reward to posting a review.

5. **30-Day Reputation Recovery Plan** (for shops with sub-4.0 rating):
   Week 1: Audit existing reviews — categorize issues by type
   Week 2: Respond to all unanswered reviews (positive + negative)
   Week 3: Launch structured review request process for all completed jobs
   Week 4: Monitor + respond within 24 hours; flag any review-gating risk

**COMPLIANCE RULES:**

**FTC Endorsement Guides (2023):**
- Material connections must be disclosed: if you give a discount for a review, the review must say "received discount for honest review"
- Review-gating (asking only satisfied customers to review) is an unfair or deceptive practice under FTC Act Section 5
- "Leave us a 5-star review" is manipulative — ask for an "honest review" only
- Fake reviews: don't purchase, don't create fake personas, don't ask employees to review without disclosure

**Yelp-Specific:**
- Yelp TOS prohibits: asking customers to review, any incentive for reviews, review solicitation kiosks
- Yelp Consumer Alerts are triggered by: patterns of suspicious reviews, businesses that direct customers to review on Yelp
- Safe Yelp strategy: "We're on Yelp" — let customers find it themselves; respond promptly to existing reviews

**Google-Specific:**
- Google review policies prohibit: incentivizing reviews, posting fake reviews, restricting negative reviews
- Safe Google strategy: general "we appreciate reviews" language in email footer; QR code at front desk pointing to Google review link (applies to all customers equally)

**Negative Review Response — Legal Caution:**
- Do NOT reveal specifics of repair, customer history, or internal communications in public responses
- "Incorrect diagnosis" complaints: do NOT admit liability; "We take every concern seriously — please call [NUMBER] so we can inspect the vehicle at no charge"
- If review appears to be from a non-customer (fraudulent): report to platform first; do NOT publicly accuse reviewer of lying (defamation risk)
