# Prompt 2: Service Department & Fixed Ops Marketing

## Use This For
Service reminder campaigns, upsell scripts for service advisors, quick lane differentiation, and declined service follow-up sequences. Use this to turn the service drive into a revenue-generating marketing channel.

---

## The Prompt

You are an automotive fixed operations marketing specialist. I need a complete service department marketing kit for a car service business.

**Business Details:**
- Business name: [NAME]
- Type: [Franchise dealership / Independent shop / Chain (Jiffy Lube / Midas / Pep Boys) / Specialty (diesel, European, EV)]
- Location: [CITY, STATE]
- Services featured: [e.g., "Oil changes, tires, brakes, AC service", "Transmission specialty", "Full-service dealership service dept"]
- Current campaign: [e.g., "summer AC prep", "winter readiness", "spring tire rotation season", "general retention"]
- Pricing note: [e.g., "$49.95 conventional oil change, $89.95 synthetic", "competitive pricing, not price leaders", "dealership premium positioning"]
- Service hours: [e.g., "M-F 7AM-6PM, Sat 8AM-4PM, closed Sunday"]
- Express/Quick Lane: [Yes/No — if yes, describe: "45-min guarantee" etc.]

**Generate the following assets:**

### 1. Seasonal Service Campaign
- **Email campaign** (2 emails, 7-day sequence): Subject lines (2 variants each), 180-word body addressing the seasonal service need, CTA to online scheduling. No guaranteed timelines without substantiation.
- **SMS blast** (under 160 chars): Seasonal service reminder with opt-out. Business name + phone number.
- **Google Business Profile Post** (300 chars): Seasonal service offer with expiration date and call-to-action.
- **Facebook ad brief**: Headline, body copy, image direction, targeting (owners of vehicles 3-8 years old within 10 miles).

### 2. Service Advisor Upsell Scripts (Word-for-Word)

Write 5 scripts for the most common upsell scenarios:

**Script A — Tire rotation upsell during oil change**
Opening line, benefit statement (tread wear + safety), price disclosure, close. Under 45 seconds spoken.

**Script B — Cabin air filter upsell**
Hook (show the dirty filter moment), benefit, price, close. Under 30 seconds.

**Script C — Brake inspection finding upsell**
Opening: "I want to show you something on the inspection sheet..." Severity framing (warning vs. urgent), safety emphasis, price, close. Under 60 seconds. No fear-mongering; FTC deceptive repair practices standard.

**Script D — Battery test result upsell**
Hook (show voltage reading), failure probability framing (not guaranteed failure), price, close. Under 45 seconds.

**Script E — Next appointment set before checkout**
Word-for-word close to pre-book the next visit at delivery. "Before you head out, I want to make sure we hold your spot for [next service]..." Under 30 seconds.

### 3. Vehicle Service Reminder Sequence (4-message lifecycle)
Based on mileage intervals. Personalized with [FIRST_NAME], [VEHICLE_YEAR_MAKE_MODEL], [LAST_SERVICE_DATE]:
- **3,000-mile reminder** (SMS, under 160 chars): Early heads-up. Friendly, not urgent.
- **5,000-mile reminder** (email, 120 words): Service due. Link to online scheduler. Include seasonal angle if applicable.
- **7,500-mile reminder** (email, 150 words): Overdue framing. "Your [VEHICLE] is [X] miles past its recommended service interval." Include NHTSA safety framing (not alarmist).
- **Lapsed/overdue > 6 months** (email, 180 words): Win-back. "We miss your [VEHICLE]." Offer incentive ($10 off, free multi-point inspection). CAN-SPAM compliant footer.

### 4. Declined Service Follow-Up Sequence (3 messages, FTC-compliant)
For customers who declined a recommended repair at the service drive:
- **Same-day SMS** (under 160 chars): "We noticed you passed on [SERVICE] today. Totally fine — just a reminder it's on our radar for next time." Friendly, zero pressure.
- **2-week email** (150 words): Reminder with documentation ("Here's what we noted on [DATE]"), updated estimate offer, scheduler link. No "safety risk" language unless technically documented and disclosed.
- **6-week final email** (120 words): Last reminder before service record closes. Value-add: "We can check if anything has changed since your last visit."

### 5. Quick Lane / Express Service Differentiation Campaign
(Skip if express service not applicable)
- **Guarantee messaging**: "In and out in 45 minutes or your next oil change is on us." — Compliance note: include qualifying conditions.
- **vs. competitors positioning** (without naming competitors): "Unlike appointment-only shops..." framing.
- **Waiting room experience email** (to new customers post-first-visit, 150 words): Reinforce the express promise, ask for review, pre-book next visit.

## Output Format
Deliver each section with its label. All scripts in quotation marks with stage directions in [brackets]. Flag any claim I've inputted that may create liability and suggest the compliant alternative.
