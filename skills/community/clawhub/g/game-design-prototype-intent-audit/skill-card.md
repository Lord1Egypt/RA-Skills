## Description: <br>
Audit a game feature, system concept, prototype plan, or preproduction proposal to determine whether the prototype is meant to sell the idea or reveal unknowns, and whether the prototype scope matches that intent. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[stanestane](https://clawhub.ai/user/stanestane) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Game designers, producers, and developers use this skill to audit whether a proposed game prototype is a selling demo, a learning prototype, or a confused hybrid. It helps teams align prototype scope with the decision the prototype is meant to unlock. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Prototype audits may require details about game concepts or prototype plans that could be proprietary. <br>
Mitigation: Share only material that is acceptable for the agent environment and avoid unnecessary confidential design or business details. <br>
Risk: A guidance-only audit can misclassify a prototype if the team provides vague goals, audiences, or success criteria. <br>
Mitigation: Clarify the prototype ask, intended audience, decision to unlock, and success criteria before relying on the recommendation. <br>
