# Analysis Frameworks Reference

Detailed methodologies for each of the 6 competitor analysis categories. Use these frameworks to ensure consistent, thorough analysis across all competitor assessments.

---

## 1. Listing Quality Framework

### Title Analysis

**What to evaluate:**
- **Length:** Character count relative to platform maximum. Amazon allows 200 but 80 is the mobile-visible portion. Shopee allows 120.
- **Keyword placement:** Is the primary keyword in the first 40 characters? This affects both search ranking and mobile visibility.
- **Structure pattern:** Brand + Product + Feature + Size/Variant is standard for Amazon. Shopee and TikTok Shop favor keyword-heavy titles.
- **Differentiation:** What unique claim or modifier appears in the title? "2024 Upgraded Version," "FDA Approved," "As Seen on TikTok."
- **Compliance:** Does the title follow platform guidelines? No promotional language, no special characters, no ALL CAPS?

**Scoring guide:**
- 5: Keyword-optimized, mobile-friendly, compliant, with clear differentiator
- 4: Well-structured with minor optimization opportunities
- 3: Adequate but generic — no clear differentiation
- 2: Poorly structured — keyword buried, too long or too short
- 1: Non-compliant or confusing

### Image Assessment

**What to evaluate:**
- **Main image:** White background (Amazon requirement), product fills 85%+ of frame, high resolution
- **Lifestyle images:** Product shown in use context, relatable scenarios
- **Infographics:** Feature callouts, dimension diagrams, comparison charts
- **Social proof images:** Award badges, certification logos, "as seen in" media mentions
- **Video:** Product demo, unboxing, or lifestyle video. 15-60 seconds optimal.
- **Total count:** Platform maximums vary. Amazon allows 7 images + 1 video. Use all slots.

**Red flags:**
- Fewer than 5 images on Amazon (below category average)
- No lifestyle images (missed emotional connection)
- Blurry or inconsistent image quality
- Stock photos instead of original product photography

### Bullet Point Analysis

**What to evaluate:**
- **Opening pattern:** Does each bullet lead with a CAPITALIZED benefit phrase?
- **Benefit-feature connection:** Is every feature translated to a customer benefit?
- **Specificity:** Numbers, measurements, time savings, percentages vs vague adjectives
- **Social proof integration:** Review references, awards, certifications within bullets
- **Keyword integration:** Secondary keywords distributed naturally across bullets
- **Length:** 150-250 characters per bullet is the sweet spot for readability

### A+ / Enhanced Content

**What to evaluate:**
- **Presence:** Does the competitor have A+ content at all? Brand Registry required on Amazon.
- **Module types:** Comparison charts, story modules, feature highlights, lifestyle banners
- **Cross-selling:** Links to other products in the brand's catalog
- **SEO value:** A+ content is indexed by Google but not by Amazon's A9 algorithm
- **Quality:** Professional design vs template-based vs poorly formatted

## 2. Pricing Strategy Framework

### Price Mapping

**Data to collect:**
- Listed price (the number shown on the page)
- Effective price (after all automatic discounts, coupons, Subscribe & Save)
- Per-unit price (normalized for multi-packs, different sizes)
- Landed price (including shipping for non-free-shipping items)
- Price history (if available via tools like CamelCamelCamel, Keepa, or platform equivalents)

### Price Position Analysis

**Classify the competitor's pricing strategy:**

| Strategy | Indicator | Typical Margin | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Penetration** | Priced 20%+ below category average, new listing, high ad spend | Low (15-25%) | High — unsustainable long-term |
| **Competitive** | Priced within 5% of top 3 competitors | Medium (25-40%) | Medium — price war vulnerability |
| **Value** | Mid-range price with strong perceived value (bundles, extras) | Medium-High (30-50%) | Low — defensible position |
| **Premium** | Priced 15%+ above category average | High (40-60%+) | Low if brand is strong, High if not |
| **Skim** | Initially high, gradually reducing. Common for innovations | Varies | Medium — timing dependent |

### Promotion Pattern Analysis

Track the following over 30-90 days if possible:

- **Coupon frequency:** Always active vs occasional vs never
- **Coupon depth:** 5% off vs 15% off vs 30%+ off
- **Lightning Deal / Flash Sale participation:** How often? What discount level?
- **Subscribe & Save discount:** Standard 5% or deeper at higher tiers?
- **Bundle pricing:** Pure bundle vs mixed bundle vs BOGO

### Competitive Price Response Modeling

Before recommending a price change, model the likely response:

- If you cut price, will the competitor match? (Check: do they have history of price matching?)
- If you add a coupon, does the competitor typically respond with their own?
- Is there a price floor where the competitor cannot follow without going below cost?

## 3. Review Intelligence Framework

### Quantitative Analysis

**Metrics to calculate:**
- **Total review count** and **total ratings count** (ratings without text)
- **Rating distribution:** Percentage at each star level
- **Review velocity:** Reviews per month (total / months since first review)
- **Recent velocity:** Reviews in last 30 days vs monthly average (accelerating or decelerating?)
- **Verified purchase percentage:** Higher = more credible
- **Photo/video review percentage:** Higher = stronger social proof

### Qualitative Analysis

**Sentiment mining approach:**

Read the 20 most recent reviews and the top 10 critical reviews (1-2 star). Categorize mentions into:

| Theme | Positive Mentions | Negative Mentions | Net Sentiment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build quality | [N] | [N] | [Positive/Negative/Neutral] |
| Value for money | [N] | [N] | [Positive/Negative/Neutral] |
| Ease of use | [N] | [N] | [Positive/Negative/Neutral] |
| Packaging/shipping | [N] | [N] | [Positive/Negative/Neutral] |
| Customer service | [N] | [N] | [Positive/Negative/Neutral] |

### Competitive Gap Identification

The most valuable output from review analysis is identifying complaints about the competitor that YOUR product solves. These become:
- Bullet point content ("Unlike other [products], ours features...")
- A+ content comparison points
- PPC keyword targets (customers searching for solutions to those problems)
- Product development priorities

## 4. Promotional Tactics Framework

### On-Platform Promotions

| Tactic | Where to Look | Data Points |
|---|---|---|
| Coupons | Product page, coupon clipper | Value, expiry, minimum purchase |
| Lightning Deals | Deals page, product page badge | Frequency, discount depth, claimed % |
| Subscribe & Save | Product page | Discount tiers, eligible products |
| Virtual bundles | Storefront, product page | Bundle composition, effective discount |
| Brand promotions | Storefront, deals page | Store-wide vs product-specific |

### Off-Platform Promotions

| Channel | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Social media | Active platforms, posting frequency, engagement rate, content themes |
| Influencer partnerships | Tagged posts, affiliate links, "gifted" disclosures |
| Email marketing | Subscribe prompts on storefront, email capture landing pages |
| Content marketing | Blog posts, YouTube channels, how-to guides linking back to listings |
| PR/Media | Press mentions, award features, "as seen in" badges |

### Promotional Calendar Mapping

Map competitor promotions against:
- **Platform events:** Prime Day, 11.11, 12.12, Black Friday, Mega Sale
- **Category seasonality:** Back to school, holiday gifting, summer, New Year
- **Competitor patterns:** Do they run promos on predictable schedules?

## 5. Search Visibility Framework

### Keyword Research Method

**Step 1:** Identify the top 15-20 search terms for the category
**Step 2:** Check competitor visibility for each term (organic and sponsored)
**Step 3:** Map keyword overlap and gaps between you and the competitor

### Keyword Coverage Matrix

| Keyword | Monthly Volume | You (Organic) | You (Sponsored) | Competitor (Organic) | Competitor (Sponsored) | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Term 1] | [Est. volume] | [Rank or N/A] | [Yes/No] | [Rank or N/A] | [Yes/No] | [Your advantage / Their advantage / Parity] |

### Search Strategy Classification

| Strategy | Indicators |
|---|---|
| **Brand defense** | Bidding on own brand name, high organic rank for brand terms |
| **Category capture** | Targeting broad category terms, high ad spend on generic searches |
| **Long-tail focus** | Optimized for specific, lower-volume phrases with high intent |
| **Competitor conquest** | Running ads on competitor brand names |
| **Hybrid** | Mix of the above, typically with budget allocated by priority |

| Customer service reputation | [Good/Neutral/Poor] | [Based on review responses] |

### Positioning Statement (Reverse-Engineered)

Based on the competitor's listing, pricing, and messaging, articulate their implied positioning:

"[Competitor brand] is positioned as a [budget/value/premium] [product category] for [target audience] who prioritize [key benefit 1] and [key benefit 2] over [trade-off]."

Compare this to your own positioning to identify differentiation opportunities and potential conflicts.
