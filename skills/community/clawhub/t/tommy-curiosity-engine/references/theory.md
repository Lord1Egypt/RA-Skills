# Theoretical Grounding — Curiosity Engine

Condensed citations for the frameworks underlying this skill.

## Cognitive Science

- **Loewenstein, G. (1994).** "The psychology of curiosity: A review and reinterpretation." *Psychological Bulletin, 116*(1), 75-98.
  Core insight: Curiosity = aversive state from perceived information gap. Requires partial knowledge — the more you know, the more gaps you perceive.

- **Berlyne, D. E. (1960).** *Conflict, Arousal, and Curiosity.* McGraw-Hill.
  Core insight: Two modes — diversive (seek stimulation when bored) and epistemic (seek specific answers when uncertain). Triggered by collative variables: novelty, complexity, conflict, surprise.

- **Panksepp, J. (1998).** *Affective Neuroscience.* Oxford University Press.
  Core insight: SEEKING system is always-on appetitive drive. Pleasure is in anticipation, not just finding.

- **Deci, E. L., & Ryan, R. M. (1985, 2000).** Self-Determination Theory.
  Core insight: Competence, autonomy, relatedness are fundamental psychological needs. Competence satisfaction → intrinsic motivation → engagement → more competence.

- **Gruber, M. J., Gelman, B. D., & Ranganath, C. (2014).** "States of curiosity modulate hippocampus-dependent learning via the dopaminergic circuit." *Neuron, 84*(2), 486-496.
  Core insight: Focused attention during curious states creates opportunities for incidental learning.

- **Litman, J. A. (2005).** "Curiosity and the pleasures of learning." *Cognition & Emotion, 19*(6), 793-814.
  Core insight: I-type curiosity (interest, approach) vs D-type curiosity (deprivation, need-to-know).

## Clinical Psychology

- **Martell, C. R., Dimidjian, S., & Herman-Dunn, R. (2010).** *Behavioral Activation for Depression.* Guilford Press.
  Core insight: Externalize the reward loop — track activities and outcomes to reshape behavior without requiring felt reward. This is the primary clinical framework we're adapting.

- **Hayes, S. C., et al. (2006).** Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT).
  Core insight: Values clarification + value-consistent behavior scheduling. The SOUL.md integration is a values clarification exercise.

- **Bandura, A. (1977).** Self-efficacy theory.
  Core insight: Perceived competence increases willingness to attempt challenges. The competence map implements this.

## AI Agent Systems

- **Wang, G., et al. (2023).** "Voyager: An Open-Ended Embodied Agent with Large Language Models." *arXiv:2305.16291*.
  Core insight: Automatic curriculum — "what's the most interesting thing I could learn next?" — produces emergent exploratory behavior.

- **Shinn, N., et al. (2023).** "Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning." *arXiv:2303.11366*.
  Core insight: Verbal self-reflection stored in memory → applied to future attempts. 91% pass@1 on HumanEval.

- **Park, J. S., et al. (2023).** "Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior." *arXiv:2304.03442*.
  Core insight: Memory importance scoring + periodic reflection → emergent intention formation.

- **Schmidhuber, J. (1991–2010).** Compression progress / formal theory of creativity.
  Core insight: Reward learning rate, not knowledge level. Seek the boundary between known and unknown.
