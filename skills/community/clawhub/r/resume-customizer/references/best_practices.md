# Resume Writing Best Practices

## Action Verbs by Category

### Leadership/Management
- Led, Managed, Directed, Supervised, Coordinated, Oversaw, Orchestrated, Guided, Mentored, Coached

### Achievement/Results
- Achieved, Accomplished, Delivered, Exceeded, Surpassed, Outperformed, Maximized, Optimized, Improved, Enhanced

### Communication/Collaboration
- Collaborated, Partnered, Communicated, Presented, Negotiated, Facilitated, Mediated, Coordinated, Worked with, Engaged with

### Technical/Development
- Developed, Built, Designed, Implemented, Created, Engineered, Programmed, Coded, Architected, Deployed

### Analysis/Problem Solving
- Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Identified, Solved, Resolved, Diagnosed, Investigated, Researched, Tested

### Organization/Planning
- Organized, Planned, Scheduled, Coordinated, Arranged, Prepared, Managed, Allocated, Prioritized, Streamlined

## Achievement Quantification Guidelines

### Use Numbers Whenever Possible
- **Bad**: "Responsible for improving sales"
- **Good**: "Increased sales by 35% in Q4 2023, generating $2M in revenue"

### Metrics to Include
- Percentages (% increase/decrease)
- Dollar amounts ($ revenue saved/generated)
- Time metrics (hours/days saved, deadlines met)
- Team size (managed team of X)
- Scale (served X customers, processed X transactions)

### Quantification Formula
**Action Verb + Specific Task + Quantified Result**

Examples:
- "Developed Python scripts to automate data processing, reducing manual effort by 20 hours/week"
- "Led cross-functional team of 12 to deliver project 2 weeks ahead of schedule"
- "Reduced server costs by 40% ($50K/year) through cloud migration"

## Summary/Objective Writing Tips

### Professional Summary (Experienced Candidates)
- 2-3 sentences
- Highlight 3-5 key qualifications
- Tailor to target role
- Include years of experience

Example:
```
Senior Software Engineer with 8+ years of experience building scalable web applications.
Proficient in Python, React, and AWS. Led team of 10 engineers to deliver 15+ projects
on time and under budget.
```

### Career Objective (Entry-Level/Career Change)
- 1-2 sentences
- Focus on what you offer, not what you want
- Mention relevant skills/education
- Align with company goals

Example:
```
Recent Computer Science graduate with strong Python and machine learning skills.
Seeking software engineering role to leverage academic projects and internship
experience in AI development.
```

## Section Ordering Recommendations

### Traditional Order (Most Common)
1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary/Objective
3. Work Experience (reverse chronological)
4. Education
5. Skills
6. Projects (if relevant)
7. Certifications
8. Awards/Honors

### Skills-First Order (Career Change/Relevant Skills)
1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary
3. Skills (prominent placement)
4. Work Experience
5. Education
6. Certifications

### Education-First Order (Recent Graduates)
1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary/Objective
3. Education
4. Internships/Relevant Experience
5. Projects
6. Skills
7. Activities/Leadership

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes
- ❌ Using passive voice ("Was responsible for...")
- ❌ Including irrelevant personal information (age, marital status)
- ❌ Using generic statements ("Hard worker", "Team player")
- ❌ Listing job duties without achievements
- ❌ Including reasons for leaving previous jobs

### Formatting Mistakes
- ❌ Inconsistent formatting (dates, fonts, spacing)
- ❌ Too long (>1 page for entry-level, >2 pages for experienced)
- ❌ Too many graphics/colors (hurts ATS parsing)
- ❌ Typos and grammatical errors
- ❌ Using tables or text boxes

### Keyword Mistakes
- ❌ Keyword stuffing (unnatural repetition)
- ❌ Using acronyms without spelling out (AWS vs. Amazon Web Services)
- ❌ Missing industry-specific keywords from JD
- ❌ Using generic skill terms ("Computer skills" vs. "Python, SQL, Tableau")

## Length Recommendations

### Entry-Level (0-3 years)
- **1 page maximum**
- Focus on education, internships, projects, skills
- Remove irrelevant work experience (server, retail, etc.)

### Mid-Level (3-10 years)
- **1-2 pages**
- Focus on achievements and progression
- Include most relevant experience

### Senior-Level (10+ years)
- **2 pages maximum**
- Focus on leadership, impact, and recent experience
- Remove experience older than 15 years (unless highly relevant)

## Tailoring for Specific Roles

### Analyze the JD
1. Highlight required skills
2. Note preferred qualifications
3. Identify company values/culure keywords
4. Research company products/services

### Customize Content
1. Reorder skills to match JD priority
2. Highlight relevant achievements
3. Use JD keywords naturally
4. Adjust summary to align with role

### Example Tailoring
**Original**: "Experienced in software development"
**Tailored for Data Science Role**: "Experienced in software development with strong Python and SQL skills, developed 5+ machine learning models in production"

## Industry-Specific Tips

### Technology/Software Development
- List programming languages, frameworks, tools
- Include GitHub/portfolio links
- Mention specific projects and technologies used
- Quantify impact (users, performance improvements)

### Data Science/Analytics
- Highlight tools (Python, R, SQL, Tableau)
- Mention specific techniques (regression, clustering, NLP)
- Include project results (accuracy, insights generated)
- List relevant coursework/certifications

### Product Management
- Emphasize cross-functional collaboration
- Quantify product impact (users, revenue, engagement)
- Mention methodologies (Agile, Scrum, Lean)
- Highlight customer-centric achievements

### Marketing/Sales
- Quantify campaign results (ROI, conversion rates)
- Mention specific channels (social media, email, SEO)
- Highlight creative and analytical skills
- Include relevant tools (Google Analytics, HubSpot)

### Finance/Accounting
- Mention certifications (CPA, CFA, MBA)
- Quantify financial impact (cost savings, revenue growth)
- Highlight relevant software (Excel, SAP, Oracle)
- Emphasize compliance and attention to detail
