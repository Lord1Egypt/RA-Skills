# TCM Constitution Types & Clinical References (体质与临床参考)

---

## 1. Nine Constitution Types (九种体质)

Based on the 《中医体质分类与判定》standard (ZYYXH/T157-2009).

| Type | Characteristics | Susceptibility | TCM Pattern | Lifestyle |
|:----:|----------------|:--------------:|:-----------:|:----------|
| **平和质** (Balanced) | Normal physique, energetic, good appetite/sleep/stools | Healthy, rarely ill | — | Maintain balance |
| **气虚质** (Qi Deficient) | Fatigue, shortness of breath, weak voice, spontaneous sweating, easily catch colds | Common cold, organ prolapse | Spleen/lung qi deficiency | Avoid overexertion; warm foods |
| **阳虚质** (Yang Deficient) | Cold sensation, cold hands/feet, prefers warmth, aversion to cold | Cold disease, diarrhea, edema | Spleen/kidney yang deficiency | Avoid cold/raw foods; warming herbs |
| **阴虚质** (Yin Deficient) | Dry mouth/throat/skin, hot sensation, night sweats, thin body | Yin deficiency, dry cough, insomnia | Kidney/lung/stomach yin deficiency | Avoid spicy/drying foods; moistening herbs |
| **痰湿质** (Phlegm-Dampness) | Overweight, greasy face/head, chest tightness, heavy sensation | Diabetes, stroke, hypertension | Spleen deficiency with dampness | Light diet; avoid fatty/sweet foods |
| **湿热质** (Damp-Heat) | Oily face, bitter taste, yellow urine, prone to skin issues | Skin disease, jaundice, UTIs | Damp-heat | Avoid spicy, alcohol; cooling herbs |
| **血瘀质** (Blood Stasis) | Dark complexion, purple lips, bruising easily, fixed pain | Pain, tumors, cardiovascular | Blood stasis | Exercise; blood-moving herbs |
| **气郁质** (Qi Stagnation) | Depressed, irritable, sighing, chest/rib distension, PMS | Depression, anxiety, insomnia, breast lumps | Liver qi stagnation | Stress reduction; qi-regulating herbs |
| **特禀质** (Intrinsic/Allergic) | Allergies, asthma, urticaria, drug/food sensitivities | Allergic disease | Varies | Avoid allergens; stabilize wei-qi |

**Clinical application:** When evaluating a patient, identify their constitutional type and adjust formula accordingly:
- 气虚质 patient with cold → supplement qi first, warm second
- 阴虚质 patient with exterior pattern → gentle exterior release, protect yin
- 痰湿质 patient needing supplement → add dampness-resolving herbs to prevent stagnation

### Pediatric Considerations
- Reduced doses: typically 1/3 to 1/2 of adult dose
- Lighter herbs preferred
- Avoid strong purgatives and toxic herbs
- Monitor digestive function closely

### Geriatric Considerations
- Reduced doses: typically 1/2 to 2/3
- Supplementing herbs often needed
- Avoid harsh purgatives
- Address underlying deficiency before attacking

## 4. Famous Physician Case References (历代名医医案参考)

### Zhang Xichun (张锡纯, 1860-1933) — Integration of Chinese and Western Medicine
- Famous for: 升陷汤 (chest-uplifting decoction), 镇肝熄风汤 (liver-pacifying wind-extinguishing decoction)
- Method: Good at using large doses of Huangqi (up to 120g); advocated for combining TCM with Western anatomy
- Quote: "中医用药如用兵, 要知彼知己"

### Ye Tianshi (叶天士, 1666-1745) — Warm Disease School
- Famous for: Wei-qi-ying-xue differentiation system
- Method: Light, floating herbs for exterior; heavy, sinking for interior
- Quote: "在卫汗之, 到气清气, 入营犹可透热转气, 入血直须凉血散血"

### Zhu Danxi (朱丹溪, 1281-1358) — Yin-Nourishing School
- Famous for: "阳常有余, 阴常不足" theory
- Method: Emphasized nourishing yin, reducing fire
- Formulas: 大补阴丸, 越鞠丸

### Zhang Jingyue (张景岳, 1563-1640) — Warm Supplementation School
- Famous for: "阳非有馀, 阴常不足" (revised Zhu Danxi's view)
- Method: Emphasized warming and supplementing kidney yin/yang
- Formulas: 右归丸 (supplement yang), 左归丸 (supplement yin)

### Li Dongyuan (李东垣, 1180-1251) — Spleen-Stomach School
- Famous for: "内伤脾胃, 百病由生" theory
- Method: Emphasized elevating spleen qi
- Formulas: 补中益气汤, 升阳益胃汤

## 5. Clinical Differentiation of Similar Patterns (古法鉴别)

### Key Differential Diagnostics

| Confusing Pair | Same Symptoms | Differentiating Feature |
|:--------------:|:-------------:|:------------------------|
| 麻黄汤 vs 桂枝汤 | Aversion to cold, fever, headache | Sweating vs no sweating; pulse floating tight vs floating moderate |
| 桂枝汤 vs 小建中汤 | Abdominal pain, fatigue | Pain timing: paroxysmal + after eating (小建中) vs continuous (桂枝汤) |
| 白虎汤 vs 竹叶石膏汤 | Thirst, fever, dry mouth | Pulse surging large (白虎) vs deficient large (竹叶石膏); with/without heat damage to qi |
| 理中汤 vs 小建中汤 | Abdominal pain, poor appetite | Pain: persistent (理中) vs intermittent (小建中); pulse: deep slow vs moderate |
| 五苓散 vs 猪苓汤 | Edema, dysuria | Pulse: floating (五苓) vs thin rapid (猪苓); with/without heat signs |
| 小青龙汤 vs 射干麻黄汤 | Cough, asthma | Frothy phlegm (小青龙) vs loud wheezing sound (射干麻黄); 
| 真武汤 vs 苓桂术甘汤 | Dizziness, edema | Edema significant (真武) vs mild (苓桂); cold limbs (真武) vs mild (苓桂) |
| 半夏泻心汤 vs 生姜泻心汤 | Epigastric fullness, nausea | Nausea prominent + food odor (生姜泻心) vs general dull mass (半夏泻心) |
