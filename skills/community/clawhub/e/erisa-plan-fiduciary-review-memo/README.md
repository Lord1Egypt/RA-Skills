# erisa-plan-fiduciary-review-memo

Drafts a fiduciary prudent-process review memo for ERISA retirement and health-and-welfare plans — covering investment monitoring, fee reasonableness, service-provider review, and plan-document compliance.

## When to Use

Use this skill when an ERISA plan sponsor, retirement committee member, plan administrator, or benefits counsel needs to draft a fiduciary prudent-process review memo for a 401(k), 403(b), defined-benefit pension, 457, or health-and-welfare plan. Covers investment monitoring, fee reasonableness analysis (§ 408(b)(2)), service-provider review, plan-document and operational compliance flags, and committee decision logging.

## What It Produces

- **DRAFT fiduciary review memo** structured as a committee-ready document
- **Investment monitoring table** with performance and fee flags per fund
- **Fee reasonableness analysis** with 408(b)(2) disclosure checklist and benchmarking conclusion
- **Service-provider review table** with contract expiration and fidelity bond status
- **Plan document and operational compliance checklist** with status codes and recommended actions
- **Cybersecurity review** (optional module) with DOL 2021 guidance gaps flagged
- **Open action items table** with owner and target date columns
- **Attorney review block** (unsigned) and Drafting Notes section flagging information gaps

## Workflow Summary

1. **Intake** — plan identification, committee attendees, review scope selection (5 modules: investment, fees, service providers, compliance, cybersecurity)
2. **Investment Monitoring** — fund-by-fund performance/fee matrix with Watch/Remove/Replace flags
3. **Fee Reasonableness** — 408(b)(2) checklist, benchmarking comparison, fee reasonableness conclusion
4. **Service-Provider Review** — contract expiration flags, fidelity bond coverage check
5. **Plan Document Compliance** — restatement status, amendments, operational failures, Form 5500, participant notices
6. **Cybersecurity Review** — SOC 1 report, breach history, DOL guidance alignment
7. **Memo Assembly** — DRAFT committee memo with all modules, open items, and attorney review block

## Scope and Boundaries

- Covers U.S. ERISA plans: 401(k), 403(b), defined-benefit, 457(b)/(f), and health-and-welfare plans.
- Does not file Form 5500, submit VCP applications, or make binding fiduciary determinations.
- Does not provide legal advice — routes all CRITICAL deficiencies to ERISA counsel.
- Does not store, repeat, or include participant SSNs, account numbers, or personal data.
- All output is DRAFT until reviewed and adopted by qualified ERISA counsel.
