# Career Success Predictor

> 10 multiple-choice questions custom-built for your target career. Find out whether you're suited, what your odds are, and what to do next.

## What it does

When you're wondering "Am I cut out for X?" or "Can I really make this career switch?", this skill will:

1. **Learn your target career and context** — the more specific the career, the sharper the assessment
2. **Design 10 questions just for that occupation** — measuring real behavior and situational instincts, never self-flattery (no "do you consider yourself resilient?" questions)
3. **Assess on two layers** —
   - **Fit**: are your traits and conditions suited to this path? (given as a range, e.g. 61%–73%)
   - **Readiness**: how far along are you? (Exploring → Validating → Building → Ready to launch)
4. **Deliver a report you can read in 3 minutes** — verdict first, then one philosophical line distilled from your own answers, then your story told back to you like a knowledgeable friend would, ending with a concrete 1/3/6-month script

## Design highlights

- **No assessment paradox**: people who haven't figured things out yet are exactly who this is for — "undecided" never lowers your score; it becomes your action plan instead
- **Scores are pre-assigned when questions are written**, not improvised after your answers
- **Asks for more evidence when the picture is blurry**: may ask about your MBTI, Big Five, or other existing test results as supplementary input (weighted by their scientific validity)
- **The report is an answer for the soul, not an audit**: no cold score tables; honest cold water, but every splash is followed by a walkable path
- **Speaks your language**: the questions and report follow whatever language you're using

## How to trigger

Just say: "Am I cut out to be a product manager?", "Assess my odds of going freelance", "Can I make it as a digital nomad?" — in any language.

## Disclaimer

Results are based on self-reported answers. This is a reference-grade fit assessment — not a statistical probability, and never a verdict on your life.
