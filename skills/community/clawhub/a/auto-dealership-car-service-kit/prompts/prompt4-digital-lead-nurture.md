# Prompt 4: Digital Advertising & Lead Nurture System

## Use This For
Google Search Ads RSAs, Facebook/Instagram ad creative briefs, email lead nurture from inquiry to showroom visit, unsold prospect follow-up, and conquest campaigns against competitive makes.

---

## The Prompt

You are a digital automotive marketing strategist specializing in paid search, social advertising, and CRM-integrated lead nurture. Generate a complete digital advertising and lead nurture system.

**Dealership/Business Details:**
- Business name: [NAME]
- Location: [CITY, STATE]
- Make(s)/Type: [e.g., "Toyota new + CPO", "Multi-brand used", "Service only"]
- Key offers: [e.g., "$0 down, 1.9% APR for 60 months WAC", "Price match guarantee", "Lifetime powertrain warranty"]
- Lead sources: [e.g., "Cars.com", "AutoTrader", "website form", "phone", "walk-in"]
- Average response time: [e.g., "Under 5 minutes via text/email", "Next-day follow-up"]
- CRM: [e.g., "VinSolutions", "DealerSocket", "HubSpot", "Manual/spreadsheet"]
- Conquest targets: [e.g., "Honda Accord lease expirations", "Ford F-150 owners", "all makes within 15 miles"]
- Budget indicator: [e.g., "boutique spend ($2K/month)", "mid-size ($5K-$15K/month)", "large dealer group ($25K+/month)"]

**Generate the following assets:**

### 1. Google Search Ads — Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)

Write 3 complete RSAs:

**RSA #1: New [MAKE] Inventory**
- 15 unique headlines (max 30 chars each): Mix brand + inventory + offer + location + urgency
- 4 unique descriptions (max 90 chars each): Include CTA, Reg Z disclosure reference for any financing claim
- Compliance flags: Any "best," "lowest," or superlative → flag and offer alternative
- Extension pack: 4 sitelinks (titles + 35-char descriptions each), 4 callouts, 2 call extensions

**RSA #2: Used/CPO Inventory**
Same structure as RSA #1. Include FTC Used Car Rule reference: "Buyers Guide on all used vehicles." No mileage guarantees. CPO claims must reference manufacturer program name.

**RSA #3: Service Department**
- 15 headlines (30 chars max): appointment, express service, makes serviced, location
- 4 descriptions (90 chars max): timing expectation (e.g., "same-day oil changes"), CTA to online scheduler
- Extension pack: 4 sitelinks (services offered), 4 callouts, location extension note

### 2. Facebook/Instagram Ad Creative Briefs (5 ads)

For each ad: Headline (40 chars), Primary text (125 chars), Description (25 chars), CTA button, Visual description (what photo/video to use), Targeting brief, Compliance note.

**Ad #1: New Inventory — Model Spotlight**
Target: In-market auto shoppers (Facebook automotive audience), 25-55, household income top 25%, within 20 miles.

**Ad #2: Trade-In Value Hook**
Hook: "What's your [YEAR] [MAKE] worth?" Lead gen form campaign. Target: owners of [competitive makes], 35-60, vehicle age 3-6 years.

**Ad #3: Service Offer (Seasonal)**
Target: vehicle owners within 10 miles, all ages 25-65, prior service customers (custom audience).

**Ad #4: Financing / "Drive for Less" Campaign**
Reg Z disclosure in primary text or comment. Target: first-time buyers, households income $40K-$80K, ages 22-40.

**Ad #5: Retargeting — Website/VDP Visitors**
Target: Custom audience (website visitors last 30 days, VDP viewers). "You were looking at the [VEHICLE]. It's still here." Dynamic inventory retargeting brief.

### 3. Email Lead Nurture Sequence (6 messages, inquiry to showroom)

For a customer who submitted a lead form or called in. Personalized with [FIRST_NAME], [VEHICLE_INTEREST], [SALESPERSON_NAME]:

- **Email 1 (immediate, within 5 min of inquiry)**: Subject (2 variants), 150 words. "I got your inquiry about the [VEHICLE]." Include specific vehicle details, next step (schedule test drive / call back), salesperson direct contact.
- **Email 2 (Day 1, if no reply)**: Subject, 100 words. Different angle: "quick question about your [VEHICLE_INTEREST] search." Soft ask.
- **Email 3 (Day 3)**: Subject, 120 words. Value-add (e.g., "3 things to know before buying a [MAKE]"), soft inventory nudge.
- **Email 4 (Day 7)**: Subject, 100 words. Market urgency ("inventory is tight for [MAKE] this spring"). Non-pushy but factual.
- **Email 5 (Day 14)**: Subject, 120 words. New inventory/offer angle. "Something just arrived you might like."
- **Email 6 (Day 30, final)**: Subject, 90 words. "Before I close your file..." — curiosity hook, one last CTA. If no response, move to unsubscribe per CAN-SPAM.

### 4. Unsold Prospect Follow-Up (4 messages after showroom visit)

For a customer who visited but didn't buy:

- **Day 2 SMS** (under 160 chars): "It was great meeting you. The [VEHICLE] is still available. Any questions I can answer?" Personalized, low pressure.
- **Day 7 email** (100 words): Subject: "Checking in on your search." New information angle (price adjustment, similar vehicle arrived). Salesperson personal tone.
- **Day 14 email** (100 words): Subject: "Still looking for your [MAKE MODEL]?" Offer to find a vehicle not currently in stock. Service-forward.
- **Day 30 email** (90 words): "Last check-in before I update your file." Final soft CTA. CAN-SPAM footer.

### 5. Conquest Campaign — Competitive Make Targeting

Target customers currently driving a competing make whose lease or finance is expiring:

- **Conquest email** (2 variants, 180 words each): Lead with brand proof point vs. the competing make (fuel economy, warranty, residual value, reliability) without naming the competitor ("compared to average in its class"). Reg Z compliant if financing mentioned.
- **NADA compliance note**: Generate a compliance checklist for conquest advertising:
  - No false comparisons
  - No disparagement of competitors by name in ways that can't be substantiated
  - All comparative claims need substantiation source (EPA, JD Power, Consumer Reports, NHTSA)
- **Conquest SMS** (under 160 chars): "Your [COMPETITOR MAKE] lease may be expiring soon. Trade up to a [YOUR MAKE] — we'll match your payment. STOP to opt out."
- **Direct mail postcard brief**: Front: visual + headline. Back: offer + compliance footer. Target list note: owners of [competing make], 3-5 year old vehicles, within 15 miles.

---

## Output Format
Deliver each section labeled. All ad copy in quotation marks with character counts noted. Compliance issues flagged in [COMPLIANCE NOTE: ...]. Reg Z disclosures in {brackets}.
