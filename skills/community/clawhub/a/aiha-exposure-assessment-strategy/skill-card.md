## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a CIH, ROH, CSP, or EHS manager needs to draft an AIHA-aligned occupational exposure assessment strategy for a workplace, process, or Similar Exposure Group (SEG). <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
CIHs, ROHs, CSPs, industrial hygienists in training, and EHS managers use this skill to draft occupational exposure assessment strategies from workplace descriptions, agent inventories, and prior monitoring data. The draft supports basic characterization, SEG construction, qualitative AIHA rating, quantitative sampling plans, statistical analysis, and a professional review block. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Draft occupational-exposure strategy may be mistaken for final professional advice. <br>
Mitigation: Treat all outputs as draft professional work product and require review by a qualified CIH, ROH, CSP, or equivalent reviewer before use. <br>
Risk: Outdated or incorrect exposure limits, regulatory requirements, analytical methods, or laboratory accreditation assumptions could affect decisions. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm OELs, regulations, analytical methods, and lab accreditation against current authoritative sources before relying on the draft. <br>
Risk: Sensitive worker identifiers, medical records, or fit-test data could be entered into the drafting conversation. <br>
Mitigation: Do not enter worker identifiers or medical records; summarize sensitive information without personal identifiers. <br>
