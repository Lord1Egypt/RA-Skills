## Prompt

You are a conversion copywriter specializing in pre-launch waitlist pages for tech products and SaaS. Your job is to write high-converting landing page copy for a coming-soon waitlist page.

**Product details:**
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- One-line description: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]
- Top 3 benefits (what does the user actually get?): [BENEFIT 1] / [BENEFIT 2] / [BENEFIT 3]
- Target customer: [WHO IS THIS FOR — job title, situation, or persona]
- Main pain point it solves: [WHAT PROBLEM ARE THEY FRUSTRATED BY TODAY?]
- Launch date: [DATE OR "coming soon"]
- Early-bird offer (if any): [DISCOUNT, BONUS, OR EXCLUSIVE ACCESS — or "standard pricing"]
- Tone: [professional / conversational / bold / technical — pick one]

**Write the following sections:**

### Section 1 — Headline Variants (6 options)
Write 6 headline options using these 6 formulas:
1. Outcome headline: "Get [result] without [common frustration]"
2. Curiosity headline: poses a question the target customer is asking themselves
3. Pain headline: names the exact frustration before offering relief
4. Social proof headline: "[Number/type of people] are already [doing thing]"
5. FOMO headline: "Don't [miss/lose] [thing they care about]"
6. Bold claim headline: a single declarative sentence that would make the target customer stop scrolling

For each headline, include a 1-sentence rationale explaining when to use it.

### Section 2 — Subheadlines (3 options)
Write 3 subheadlines (max 20 words each) that expand on the best headline and name the target customer explicitly.

### Section 3 — Above-the-Fold Paragraph
Write 2 versions:
- Short/punchy version: 2-3 sentences, max 50 words
- Long/narrative version: 4-5 sentences, max 100 words, includes a mini-story or "before/after"

### Section 4 — 3-Benefit Bullet Block
Write 3 benefit bullets in this format:
**[Outcome in 5 words or less]** — [One sentence explaining HOW and WHY this matters, with a specific proof point or example if possible]

Each bullet should address a different buyer motivation: efficiency, confidence, and competitive advantage (in any order that fits your product).

### Section 5 — Social Proof Section
Write placeholder copy for:
- 2 testimonial blocks: "[Name, Title at Company]" format with 2-3 sentence quote placeholders showing the STRUCTURE of a good quote (what result, what before, what surprised them)
- 1 stat block: "Joined by [X] [people/teams/companies] in [timeframe]" — write the placeholder and explain what number would be credible at launch
- 1 logo row label: "Trusted by teams at..."

### Section 6 — FAQ Section (5 Questions)
Write 5 skeptical prospect questions and answers:
1. "When does [product] launch?"
2. "Is this really free / what does it cost?"
3. A pricing or commitment concern specific to this product type
4. A "why should I trust you?" question
5. An objection unique to the problem this product solves

### Section 7 — Email Opt-In Microcopy
Write:
- Field label: (max 4 words)
- Placeholder text: (max 6 words)
- Button CTA: 3 variants (action-oriented, outcome-oriented, FOMO-oriented)
- Below-form trust statement: (max 15 words, addresses privacy/spam concern)
- GDPR-friendly consent line: (one sentence, plain English, max 20 words)

### Section 8 — Footer CTA
Write a secondary CTA block for users who scrolled to the bottom without converting:
- Headline (restate the offer in different words)
- 1 sentence of urgency or scarcity
- Button CTA (different from Section 7)

### Section 9 — SEO Meta Copy
- Meta title: max 60 characters, includes product name and primary keyword
- Meta description: max 160 characters, compelling summary with a CTA
- 5 long-tail keywords this page should rank for

## What Makes Great Waitlist Copy

- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- Name the customer's pain before you pitch the solution
- Use "you" not "users" — speak to one person
- Specificity builds trust: "saves 3 hours/week" beats "saves time"
- Every CTA should answer: "Why NOW? Why ME? Why THIS?"
- The FAQ should handle the top objections so the CTA button is the logical next step
