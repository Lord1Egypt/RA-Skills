# Marketing / Campaign Competitor Analysis

Use when the task is to analyze campaigns, launches, social buzz, node marketing, co-branding, or communication strategy.

## Core principle

Do not judge a campaign by creativity alone. Judge it by whether the background, objective, strategy, creative expression, execution, and result fit together.

## Six-part framework

Analyze in this order:
1. **Background** - why did they need to do this?
2. **Objective** - what did they want to achieve?
3. **Strategy** - what path did they choose?
4. **Creative** - how did they express the strategy?
5. **Execution** - how did they land it?
6. **Effect** - what did they get?

## Key questions by section

### Background
- Why was the move necessary?
- What internal or external condition made it possible?
- Was the timing right?
- Is this comparable to our context?

### Objective
- Which KPIs mattered most?
- What was primary vs secondary?
- Was the goal awareness, engagement, conversion, repositioning, or defense?

### Strategy
- What path connected the goal to action?
- How did they frame the problem?
- Why this route instead of alternatives?

### Creative
- Did the expression actually serve the strategy?
- Did the message resonate with the intended user?
- Is it memorable for the right reason?

### Execution
- Where did they speak?
- Who spoke?
- In what sequence?
- How were channels and resources coordinated?
- What was the rhythm?

### Effect
Look across four metric families:
- **Exposure**: views, reads, impressions, reach, hot-topic presence
- **Interaction**: likes, saves, comments, reposts, UGC volume, discussion intensity
- **Conversion**: PV, UV, leads, add-to-cart, purchase, ROI, CAC, new users
- **Efficiency**: spend vs result, resource leverage, execution efficiency

## Output expectation

Your conclusion should say:
- which part of the campaign logic is reusable
- which part depends on non-replicable budget / brand power / timing
- what we should do differently in our own campaign
