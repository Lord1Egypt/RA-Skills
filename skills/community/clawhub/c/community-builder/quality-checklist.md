# Community Builder Quality Checklist

## Pre-Launch Checklist

### Platform Setup
- [ ] Platform selected based on audience research, not convenience
- [ ] Community name tested — searchable, memorable, not trademarked
- [ ] URL / invite link secured and short
- [ ] Profile image, banner, and description complete
- [ ] Privacy settings configured (public/private/hidden)
- [ ] Member approval questions written (for private groups)

### Channel Architecture
- [ ] Fewer than 10 channels at launch
- [ ] Each channel has a written purpose statement
- [ ] Welcome/rules channel pinned at the top
- [ ] Archive or "parking lot" channel for overflow (not visible to new members)
- [ ] Channel names are intuitive without explanation

### Community Guidelines
- [ ] Written guidelines posted and pinned
- [ ] Guidelines cover: respectful communication, spam rules, self-promotion policy, competitor mentions
- [ ] Consequences clearly stated (warning → temp ban → permanent ban)
- [ ] Link to guidelines included in welcome message

### Moderation
- [ ] At least one moderator assigned per platform timezone
- [ ] Moderator handbook written and shared
- [ ] Report/escalation procedure documented
- [ ] Automated filters configured (slurs, spam links, excessive mentions)
- [ ] Welcome bot or automation configured for new members

### Content Seeds
- [ ] Minimum 10 threads posted before first member invited
- [ ] Introduction/welcome thread pinned
- [ ] First weekly ritual thread created
- [ ] Brand insider/exclusive content posted
- [ ] FAQ or getting-started guide published

## Launch Checklist

### Cohort Invitation
- [ ] 50–200 high-engagement customers identified
- [ ] Personalized invitations drafted (not mass email blast)
- [ ] Exclusive cohort period defined (2–4 weeks before open)
- [ ] Founder/team plan to respond to every cohort post in first week

### Growth Integrations
- [ ] Post-purchase email sequence updated with community invite (Day 7)
- [ ] Packaging insert / unboxing card updated with QR code
- [ ] Social media bios updated with community link
- [ ] Website footer or product page includes community CTA
- [ ] Support email signature includes community link

### Metrics Baseline
- [ ] Baseline metrics recorded before public launch
- [ ] 30-day and 90-day targets set
- [ ] Reporting cadence established (weekly or monthly)
- [ ] Team member assigned to community reporting

## Ongoing Health Checklist (Monthly)

### Engagement Health
- [ ] Monthly active member rate calculated (target: >15%)
- [ ] New member 30-day retention measured (target: >40%)
- [ ] Top contributors identified and recognized
- [ ] Posts-per-week trending (flat, up, or down?)
- [ ] Are members answering each other's questions or waiting for brand?

### Content Quality
- [ ] All weekly rituals executed consistently
- [ ] No week with zero brand-seeded posts
- [ ] Content mix checked: >80% community value, <20% brand promotion
- [ ] Monthly event or challenge executed

### Moderation
- [ ] Moderation log reviewed — any recurring issues?
- [ ] Community guidelines updated if needed
- [ ] Moderator team status check — burnout risk?
- [ ] Spam or fake accounts purged

### Business Connection
- [ ] CLV of community members vs. non-members tracked
- [ ] Support ticket deflection measured
- [ ] UGC and testimonials harvested for marketing use
- [ ] Product feedback from community summarized for product team
- [ ] ROI narrative updated for internal stakeholders

## 90-Day Review Questions

1. Is the community growing, stable, or declining in active members?
2. What percentage of members have posted at least once?
3. What is the most common topic of organic (unprompted) conversation?
4. Have any unexpected use cases or sub-communities emerged?
5. What content or events drove the most engagement?
6. What is the measurable business impact so far (CLV, ticket deflection, UGC)?
7. Does the original purpose statement still accurately describe what the community does?
