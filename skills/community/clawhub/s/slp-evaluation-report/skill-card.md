## Description: <br>
Drafts initial Speech-Language Pathology diagnostic evaluation reports from completed assessments, including case history synthesis, standardized score tables, clinical impressions, functional impact statements, and evidence-based recommendations for licensed SLP review and signature. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Licensed SLPs, CF-SLPs, and clinical supervisors use this skill to turn completed speech-language assessments into structured draft evaluation reports for clinical review, co-signature, IEP eligibility, insurance authorization, or physician referral workflows. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Draft clinical documentation could contain incomplete or incorrect assessment interpretation. <br>
Mitigation: A licensed SLP must review, edit, and sign the report before clinical, educational, insurance, or third-party use. <br>
Risk: Sensitive patient identifiers could be entered while drafting an evaluation report. <br>
Mitigation: Use a case ID or pseudonym only, and do not enter names, DOBs, MRNs, insurer IDs, addresses, phone numbers, or other direct identifiers. <br>
