## Description: <br>
Safe identification and harvesting of common wild edible plants. Use when someone wants to supplement food supply with wild plants, learn plant identification skills, or understand what's safe vs dangerous in their local environment. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[howtousehumans](https://clawhub.ai/user/howtousehumans) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill for educational guidance on common wild edible plants, safe foraging ground rules, region-specific lookalike checks, and sustainable harvesting practices. It is not a real-time confirmation that an unidentified plant is safe to eat. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may treat educational plant descriptions as confirmation that an unknown plant is safe to eat. <br>
Mitigation: Require 100% identification certainty, multiple plant features, local field guides or expert confirmation, and advise users not to eat any plant when uncertain. <br>
Risk: The skill includes broadly triggered instructions for eating unknown wild plants. <br>
Mitigation: Frame any unknown-plant protocol as survival-only, prefer known edible species and local expert resources, and direct users to poison control or emergency services after ingestion or symptoms. <br>
Risk: Mushroom identification and regional plant lookalikes can be dangerous when handled through text alone. <br>
Mitigation: Redirect mushroom and high-risk lookalike decisions to local mycological societies, extension offices, regional field guides, or experienced in-person foragers. <br>
