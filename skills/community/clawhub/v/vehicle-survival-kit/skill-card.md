## Description: <br>
Practical vehicle maintenance and roadside emergency skills for tire changes, jump-starts, breakdowns, fender benders, and basic car maintenance. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Drivers and agents assisting drivers use this skill to prepare for roadside emergencies, handle common vehicle incidents, and perform basic maintenance checks. It is especially useful for walking a user through checklists and safety-first procedures during or before a vehicle problem. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Vehicle-specific procedures, jack points, tire pressure, oil weight, and maintenance steps may differ by make and model. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for vehicle details when relevant and direct users to verify vehicle-specific steps in the owner's manual before acting. <br>
Risk: Road rules, emergency numbers, insurance requirements, measurement units, and seasonal advice vary by location. <br>
Mitigation: Localize guidance to the user's jurisdiction and current conditions, and encourage users to follow local accident-reporting and emergency rules. <br>
Risk: Incident workflows may involve sensitive driver's license, insurance, claim, or location details. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid storing unnecessary personal or incident details in agent state and keep only information needed for the immediate task. <br>
Risk: Roadside repair attempts can create physical safety hazards when traffic, heat, unstable lifting, or brake problems are involved. <br>
Mitigation: Prioritize getting out of traffic, visibility, professional help for brake issues, never opening a hot radiator cap, and never going under a vehicle supported only by a jack. <br>
