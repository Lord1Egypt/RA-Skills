# External Best-Practice Sources Used For The Teaching-Explainer Overlay

These sources informed the teaching overlay through v1.2.0. They are not a substitute for paper-grounded evidence in a concrete report.

## Paper reading and reconstruction

- S. Keshav, "How to Read a Paper" / ACM SIGCOMM CCR: three-pass reading method; use for orientation, content, and deep reconstruction.
  - URL: https://ccr.sigcomm.org/online/files/p83-keshavA.pdf

## Role-based discussion and seminar teaching

- Alec Jacobson and Colin Raffel, "Role-Playing Paper-Reading Seminars": rotate roles such as researcher, practitioner, hacker, social-impact assessor; the goal is deeper critical analysis and discussion rather than one-way summary.
  - URL: https://colinraffel.com/blog/role-playing-seminar.html
- Stanford CS324 paper discussions: panel roles including Archaeologist, Social Impact Assessor, Industry Practitioner, Researcher, Salesperson, and Bug Hunter.
  - URL: https://github.com/stanford-cs324/winter2022/blob/master/paper-discussions.md
- CS496 seminar guidance: paper presentations should cover high-level ideas, key technical details, experimental results, background/related work, shortcomings, and 3-5 discussion questions.
  - URL: https://github.com/andrewcrotty/cs496-f22/blob/main/README.md

## Story and audience framing

- MIT EECS Communication Lab, "Paper: Introduction": problem, motivation, solution, contributions; work backward from what was done to construct the narrative.
  - URL: https://mitcommlab.mit.edu/eecs/commkit/journal-article-introduction/

## Review and evidence criteria

- AISTATS 2026 Reviewer Guidelines: evaluate soundness, significance, novelty, clarity, relation to prior work, and non-conventional contributions; reviews should be specific, fair, useful, and constructive.
  - URL: https://virtual.aistats.org/Conferences/2026/ReviewerGuidelines
- ICLR 2026 Author Guide: reproducibility statement should reference details such as code, assumptions, proofs, data processing, and appendix/supplementary materials.
  - URL: https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide
- Bytedance Deer Flow academic-paper-review skill: includes review axes such as soundness, novelty, reproducibility, experimental design, statistical rigor, scalability, and significance level.
  - URL: https://github.com/bytedance/deer-flow/blob/main/skills/public/academic-paper-review/SKILL.md

## Public skill patterns

- ClawHub paper-reading skill: emphasizes thorough paper reading, Markdown report output, optional architecture diagrams, and epistemic tags such as paper-grounded vs inferred vs external claims.
  - URL: https://clawhub.ai/wooonster/paper-reading
- Datawhale OpenClaw paper assistant tutorial: demonstrates an automated research-assistant workflow from paper fetching to filtering, deep-reading, and push notification. This v1.2.0 skill does not reopen retrieval inside the deep-read stage; it only borrows the idea that paper understanding should close the loop into usable delivery artifacts.
  - URL: https://github.com/datawhalechina/hello-claw/blob/main/docs/cn/university/paper-assistant/index.md
