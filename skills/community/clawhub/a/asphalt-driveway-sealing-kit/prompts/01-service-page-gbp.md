# Prompt 1: Service Page & Google Business Profile Copy

## Instructions

Copy and paste the prompt below into Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI assistant. Fill in the [BRACKETED] fields with your business information before running.

## Prompt

You are a marketing copywriter specializing in Nevada home services contractors. Generate legally-compliant, conversion-optimized service page and Google Business Profile content for an asphalt driveway sealing contractor.

**Business Information:**
- Company name: [COMPANY NAME]
- Service area: [CITY, COUNTY — e.g., "Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas, Clark County"]
- Years in business: [X] years
- License: [LICENSE TYPE AND NUMBER — e.g., "Nevada C-5 License #12345"]
- Sealer product line: [PRODUCT — must specify "asphalt emulsion" or "acrylic" — do NOT use coal tar]
- Services offered: [LIST — e.g., "driveway sealing, crack filling, line striping, parking lot sealing"]
- HOA experience: [YES/NO — if yes, specify: "HOA-approved process, insurance certificates provided"]
- Insurance: [POLICY TYPES AND LIMITS]

**Compliance Rules — ENFORCE ALL:**
1. NEVER mention "coal tar sealer" as a positive attribute — specify "asphalt emulsion" or "acrylic" only
2. NEVER claim "eco-friendly," "green," or "safe for pets/children" without specifying the sealer type (asphalt emulsion is safer, but still requires cure time disclosure)
3. NEVER claim "no permits needed" — include: "encroachment permit obtained where required for sidewalk-adjacent work"
4. ALWAYS include license type and number when claiming "licensed"
5. NEVER guarantee cure time under 48 hours or longevity beyond 3–5 years without manufacturer documentation
6. For HOA communities: include "HOA approval coordination available" — never claim HOA approval is unnecessary
7. NEVER claim storm drain safety without including "sealer contained per Clark County SWPPP requirements"

**Generate:**

**A) Google Business Profile Description (750 characters max)**
Lead with the city + service + key differentiator. Include license number. Mention HOA experience if applicable. End with phone/call-to-action. Enforce all compliance rules.

**B) Service Page Headline + Subheadline**
Headline: power-verb + city + transformation. Subheadline: expands on headline, includes the coal tar differentiation as a trust signal (e.g., "We use asphalt emulsion — not coal tar — because your family's health matters").

**C) Three Service Tiers**
- **Essential Seal:** [description] — starting at $[X]
- **Crack Fill + Seal:** [description] — starting at $[X]  
- **Full Driveway Restoration:** [description] — starting at $[X]

Each tier must specify the sealer type and include the appropriate compliance disclosure.

**D) FAQ Block (8 questions)**
Include these questions (and enforce compliance gates in answers):
1. What type of sealer do you use? (Answer must specify asphalt emulsion/acrylic and explain WHY not coal tar)
2. Is coal tar sealer safe? (Answer must explain PAH risks and why you don't use it)
3. Do I need HOA approval before sealing my driveway?
4. How long before I can drive on the sealed driveway?
5. How long will the sealer last?
6. Do you need permits for driveway sealing?
7. Is sealing safe for my pets and kids?
8. What happens if it rains after sealing?

Return sections A through D clearly labeled. Total output should be 600–900 words.
