# Platform Playbooks

Use this file only when platform-specific adaptation matters.

## X
- Best for fast opinions, concise arguments, contrarian hooks, and conversational threads.
- Open strong in the first line.
- Earn replies by stating a claim people can agree with, challenge, or extend.
- Keep the structure tight: hook -> point -> proof/example -> close.
- Avoid over-explaining.

## LinkedIn
- Best for professional credibility, operational lessons, founder/operator perspective, and B2B lead generation.
- Open with a crisp observation or tension.
- Use short paragraphs and clean logic.
- Give a takeaway people can reuse.
- CTA should feel natural, not salesy.

## Instagram
- Best for visually packaged ideas, concise educational carousels, and saveable summaries.
- Keep the copy scannable.
- Favor punchy headlines, clear sequencing, and emotional relevance.
- Strong for creator and lifestyle-adjacent brands.

## TikTok
- Best for spoken explanation, demos, behind-the-scenes context, and personality-led delivery.
- Optimize the first 2-3 seconds.
- Script for speech, not for reading.
- Think in scenes or beats, not paragraphs.
- Use pattern breaks to retain attention.

## Xiaohongshu
- Best for relatable scenarios, practical recommendations, routines, product use cases, and saveable notes.
- Specificity beats abstraction.
- Titles should feel close to a real user problem or lived experience.
- Favor useful detail, lists, and concrete scenarios.
- Avoid corporate tone.

## WeChat Official Accounts
- Best for trust-building, deeper education, founder letters, strategic commentary, and more complete explanations.
- Readers accept more depth if the structure is clean.
- Use section headers and information-rich paragraphs.
- Prioritize trust and reasoning over novelty.
