# Output Examples

Use these examples as shape guidance. Do not copy claims unless the user provides matching evidence.

## Public Company Prompt

User:

```text
Analyze Costco through the Buffett-style moat analyzer.
```

Output shape:

```text
# Costco Moat Analysis

Educational analysis only. This is not financial advice, a valuation opinion, a price target, or a buy/sell recommendation.

## One-Line Verdict
Costco looks like a high-quality, understandable business where the moat comes from trust, scale, membership behavior, and a deliberate low-price culture.

## Business Model
Costco sells goods at low margins and earns a meaningful share of profit from recurring membership fees.

## Scorecard
| Area | Score | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Moat strength | 9 | Scale, purchasing power, habit, and membership renewal behavior reinforce each other. |
| Pricing power | 7 | It has pricing power in membership fees, but intentionally limits merchandise markups. |
```

## Startup Idea Prompt

User:

```text
Analyze my AI tool that writes local SEO pages for dentists.
```

Output shape:

```text
# AI Local SEO Tool Moat Analysis

## One-Line Verdict
This is a useful offer, but not yet a moat. The risk is that generic AI page generation becomes cheap and easy to copy.

## The Moat
The moat would need to come from distribution, proprietary local performance data, workflow lock-in, or becoming embedded in an agency/client reporting process.
```

## Private Acquisition Prompt

User:

```text
Analyze a local fire-safety inspection company doing 500k revenue and 110k seller discretionary earnings.
```

Output shape:

```text
# Fire-Safety Inspection Business Moat Analysis

## One-Line Verdict
Potentially a good boring business, but the main question is whether recurring compliance demand survives owner transition.

## What Could Kill It
Owner-dependent relationships, undocumented inspection processes, customer concentration, or hidden vehicle/equipment replacement costs.
```
