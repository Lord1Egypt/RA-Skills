# Zoning Variance Application Narrative

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Urban Planning — Zoning Board of Adjustment (ZBA) / Board of Zoning Appeals (BZA) variance applications

## Purpose

A variance-application drafting partner for planning consultants (AICP and others), zoning attorneys, land-use applicants, property owners, and municipal-board staff. Turns parcel facts, the controlling zoning ordinance section, and the unique physical conditions of the property into a DRAFT variance narrative aligned with the jurisdiction's adopted variance standard (Euclidean unnecessary-hardship test, NJ "C-1 / C-2," NY "area variance / use variance," municipal "practical difficulty" test, or AHJ-specific), with proposed Findings of Fact, a self-created-hardship audit, a "what NOT to argue" red-flag list, an evidence index, and a hearing-ready Q&A prep block.

## When to Use

- Drafting a variance narrative for filing with a Zoning Board of Adjustment, Board of Zoning Appeals, or equivalent
- Rewriting a rejected variance application so the next hearing focuses on the unique physical hardship rather than convenience or financial loss
- Preparing proposed Findings of Fact that the board can adopt verbatim
- Coaching a property-owner applicant on what to say (and what not to say) at the hearing
- Pre-filing review to flag self-created-hardship, missing notice, or insufficient evidence before public-hearing scheduling

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Jurisdiction and standard**
1. Captures jurisdiction (state, county, municipality) and the controlling variance standard (e.g., NJ MLUL § 40:55D-70(c)(1) bulk / (c)(2) flexible / (d) use; NY Town Law § 267-b area / use; PA Municipalities Planning Code §§ 910.1–910.2; or AHJ-specific)
2. Captures the controlling zoning ordinance section, the dimensional or use requirement that triggers the variance, and the requested deviation
3. Confirms whether this is an **area / dimensional / bulk variance** or a **use variance** (the standard of proof is much higher for a use variance in most jurisdictions)

**Phase 2: Property and applicant**
4. Captures parcel data — block / lot, street address, lot area, lot dimensions, current improvements, zoning district, overlay districts, historic-district status
5. Captures applicant standing (owner, contract purchaser, lessee) and chain of title relevant to whether the hardship is self-created
6. Captures whether prior variances, special permits, subdivisions, or non-conforming-use determinations apply to the lot

**Phase 3: Hardship and standards mapping**
7. Identifies the **unique physical conditions** of the property (size, shape, topography, location, easements, environmental constraints, pre-existing structures) that distinguish it from other properties in the district
8. Maps each fact to the jurisdiction's variance criteria:
   - The strict application of the ordinance would result in **unnecessary hardship / practical difficulty** because of these unique physical conditions
   - The hardship is **not self-created** (acquired with knowledge ≠ self-created in many jurisdictions, but verify the controlling case law and note it)
   - The variance **can be granted without substantial detriment to the public good** (neighborhood, character, light/air, drainage, traffic, safety)
   - The variance **will not substantially impair the intent and purpose of the zoning plan**
9. Runs a self-created-hardship audit (did the applicant create the condition? Did the applicant subdivide to create the undersized lot? Did the applicant build the encroachment?)

**Phase 4: Draft + red-flag audit**
10. Drafts the narrative in the structure expected by the AHJ (Statement of Relief Requested → Property Description → Standards Analysis → Findings of Fact → Conditions → Conclusion)
11. Runs the "what NOT to argue" red-flag audit, striking or rewriting any argument grounded in:
    - Personal preference / convenience
    - Financial loss / inability to maximize return
    - Comparison to other variances ("they got one")
    - Hardship caused by the applicant's own action
12. Builds the evidence index (survey, site plan, photos, expert reports, neighbor letters, traffic study, drainage analysis)
13. Builds a hearing Q&A prep block (likely board questions and the applicant's evidence-backed answers)

## Output

A DRAFT variance application packet with:

- Jurisdiction + controlling-standard block (with citation)
- Statement of Relief Requested (ordinance section + deviation, in exact numerical terms)
- Property Description (parcel facts, unique physical conditions)
- Standards Analysis (each criterion answered with evidence-backed facts, not conclusions)
- Proposed Findings of Fact (3–7 numbered, board-adoptable)
- Proposed Conditions (e.g., screening, lighting, hours, future re-application bar)
- Conclusion (relief that should be granted, in exact terms)
- Self-created-hardship audit
- "What NOT to argue" red-flag audit
- Evidence index
- Hearing Q&A prep
- Notice / publication compliance block (a checklist; not a substitute for the clerk's notice)
- Zoning-attorney review block

## Safety

This skill drafts an application narrative, **not** a filed application. Every output is labeled **DRAFT — ZONING ATTORNEY MUST REVIEW BEFORE FILING; APPLICANT MUST CONFIRM JURISDICTIONAL VARIANCE STANDARD AND NOTICE / PUBLICATION REQUIREMENTS**. The skill never files the application, never appears before the board, never represents the applicant, and never gives legal advice. The variance standard varies by state, county, and municipality — the skill asks the user to confirm the controlling test and citations before drafting and does not assume one test applies in another jurisdiction. The skill does not promise an outcome; variance grants are discretionary. The skill flags but does not waive: notice and publication deadlines, abutter / neighbor consent rules, environmental and historic-overlay reviews, and any required pre-application conference with planning staff. The skill never advocates for a hardship the user cannot prove and never characterizes a financial-loss argument as a "hardship" argument. PII other than parcel address is minimized in working drafts.
