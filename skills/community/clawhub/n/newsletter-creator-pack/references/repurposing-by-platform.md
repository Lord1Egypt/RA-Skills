# Platform-Specific Repurposing Rules

## Twitter / X
- Hook tweet must work standalone — assume nobody reads the thread
- Tweets 2–6: one insight each, max 2 sentences
- Final tweet: CTA ("Follow for more" or link back to newsletter)
- No hashtags on serious content — they look spammy
- Numbers outperform adjectives: "3 ways" beats "several ways"

## LinkedIn
- First line must create a scroll-stop — question or bold claim
- Use single-sentence paragraphs — walls of text get skipped
- Personal story framing outperforms pure advice
- End with a question to drive comments
- No more than 3 hashtags, in the last line only

## TikTok / Reels / Shorts
- First 3 words must be the hook — "Most people don't know..."
- Script in spoken language, not written language
- One idea per video — don't try to cover everything
- End with a pattern interrupt: "But here's the part nobody talks about..."
- 45–60 seconds is the sweet spot for educational content

## Instagram
- Caption should tell a story, not just describe the image
- Front-load value — Instagram shows 2 lines before "more"
- Hashtags at the bottom, not in the copy
- Mix sizes: 2–3 large (1M+), 5–7 medium (100k–1M), 2–3 niche (under 100k)

## Email (newsletter format)
- Subject line is 80% of the result — spend time here
- Preview text (the line after subject) should complement, not repeat
- First sentence hooks or you've lost them
- One main CTA per email — multiple CTAs kill click rates
