## Use Case: <br>
Developers and trading researchers can use this skill to draft or inspect command-driven backtesting workflows and example performance reports. Security guidance says not to rely on reported returns, Sharpe ratio, drawdown, trade counts, or outperforming conclusions for trading or investing decisions until real data loading and calculations replace the fixed output. <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The script prints fixed favorable backtest metrics rather than performing real analysis. <br>
Mitigation: Treat the release as a placeholder until the publisher implements real data loading and calculations. <br>
Risk: Reported returns, risk metrics, trade counts, and outperforming conclusions could mislead trading or investing decisions. <br>
Mitigation: Do not use the reported metrics for investment decisions; independently validate any strategy with real data and reviewed calculations. <br>
