## Description: <br>
Audits game randomness systems for perceived fairness, streak pain, agency loss, hidden-pattern suspicion, exploit risk, and concrete design or presentation fixes. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[stanestane](https://clawhub.ai/user/stanestane) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Game designers, systems designers, economy designers, and product teams use this skill to evaluate whether chance-driven mechanics will feel fair, legible, and agency-preserving to players. It is most useful for loot, gacha, combat chance, procedural generation, reward loops, and other systems where randomness can be misread as rigged or sabotaging. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may share proprietary game systems, loot tables, reward loops, or balancing goals while requesting an audit. <br>
Mitigation: Only provide design details that are appropriate for the agent platform and the user's privacy controls. <br>
Risk: The skill produces advisory design recommendations that may be incomplete or unsuitable for a specific player economy or production game. <br>
Mitigation: Review recommendations with domain experts and validate changes through design review, telemetry, and playtesting before release. <br>
