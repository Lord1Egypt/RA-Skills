# LinkedIn Post Optimizer

You already wrote the post. This skill makes it perform.

## What it does

Paste your existing LinkedIn draft — rough, polished, or a post that already ran and underperformed — and get back:

- **Optimized post** — fully formatted, copy-paste ready, with correct line breaks, a hook that clears the 210-char mobile cutoff, and a single clean CTA
- **Change log** — 4–5 bullets explaining exactly what changed and why, so you understand the decisions and apply them next time
- **Honest assessment** — one sentence on your post's strongest element and its biggest remaining risk
- **Alt-hook variants** *(optional)* — 3 different opening line angles (curiosity, story, counterintuitive) if you want to test before publishing

This is an optimizer, not a writer. It requires a draft you've already written.

## Before / After

**Before:**
> I've been in marketing for 10 years and I've learned a lot about what makes content work. There are so many things that people get wrong. One of the most important things is to have a strong hook. People need to be grabbed right away or they will scroll past. What do you think is the most important part of a LinkedIn post?

**After:**
> Most LinkedIn posts lose readers in the first 3 seconds.
>
> Not because the content is bad — because the first line doesn't earn the scroll.
>
> After 10 years in marketing, the single variable that changed my reach: treating line 1 as the only line that matters.
>
> What's the last LinkedIn hook that actually made you stop scrolling?
>
> #linkedin #contentmarketing #personalbranding

**Change log (excerpt):**
- **Hook:** Pulled the core insight to line 1 and reframed as a specific claim — the original buried it in sentence 4
- **Lede:** Removed "I've learned a lot" (vague) — replaced with a concrete framing of what changed
- **CTA:** Replaced "What do you think is the most important part?" (generic) with a question the reader can answer from their own feed experience

## What you provide

Any written LinkedIn draft — rough prose, polished copy, or an old post you want to reuse. Optionally: your role/audience, post goal, and tone preference. The skill infers what it can't see.

## Requirements

No API keys. No external integrations. Runs entirely within your OpenClaw session.
