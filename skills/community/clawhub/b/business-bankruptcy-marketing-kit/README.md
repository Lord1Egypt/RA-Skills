## 7 Compliance Moats

### 1. SCR 192 + RPC 7.1 + 11 USC § 528 [ANCHOR]
Three violations in one ad: no "This is an advertisement," no § 528 debt relief agency disclosure, "specialist" without State Bar certification. Business bankruptcy attorneys commonly assume § 528 applies only to consumer debtors — courts in the 9th Circuit have applied it broadly.

### 2. Subchapter V vs. Standard Chapter 11 — SBRA 2019 [SHARPEST]
Every Nevada business with ≤ $7.5M in debt qualifies for Subchapter V (11 USC §§ 1181–1195): no disclosure statement, no creditors' committee, debtor-only plan, modified absolute priority rule. Saves 60–80% in professional fees vs. standard Chapter 11. AI tools ignore it entirely. An attorney whose marketing describes only standard Chapter 11 is driving away their most common client profile.

### 3. § 547 Preference Actions [SHARPEST #2]
Trustee can claw back payments made within 90 days (1 year for insiders) before filing. AI tools routinely advise "pay off important creditors before filing" — creating preference action targets. Wrong advice costs the estate 3x the original amount to defend. Failure to warn = malpractice exposure.

### 4. § 365 Executory Contracts — Assume/Reject/Assign
120-day deadline to assume or reject commercial leases (§ 365(d)(4)). Assumption requires cure of all defaults. Rejection = breach at petition date, damages capped at § 502(b)(6) for leases. Software licenses: § 365(n) licensee election. Franchise agreements: anti-assignment clauses survive under § 365(c)(1). AI tools say "walk away from bad contracts" without any of this.

### 5. NRS 78 / NRS 86 Nevada Entity Law Intersection
Revoked articles (NRS 78.175 / 86.263) = debtor cannot file Chapter 11 in good standing. LLC manager fiduciary duty shifts toward creditors in zone of insolvency (NRS 86.274). Nevada UFTA (NRS 112.180) extends fraudulent transfer lookback to 4 years via § 544(b). Alter ego piercing survives bankruptcy filing. AI tools generate purely federal law content with zero Nevada entity law intersection.

### 6. § 1129(b) Cram-Down and Absolute Priority Rule
Standard Chapter 11: equity retains nothing while dissenting unsecured creditors go unpaid — absolute priority rule. Subchapter V: absolute priority modified if plan funded from disposable income. "Keep your business" means very different things in Sub V vs. standard Chapter 11. AI tools never make this distinction.

### 7. § 363 Cash Collateral and § 364 DIP Financing
Cannot use accounts receivable proceeds, inventory sale proceeds, or deposit account funds subject to a security interest without court authorization or secured creditor consent (§ 363(c)(2)). No cash collateral motion = no payroll on Day 1. DIP financing priming liens require court approval and adequate protection for existing secured creditors. AI tools say "continue operating" without mentioning this at all.

## Enforcement Layers

**Layer 1 — Nevada State Bar (SCR 192, RPC 7.1–7.3):**  
Attorney advertising complaints. SCR 192 disclaimer ($25 registration fee and disclaimer required for direct mail). Specialization claims without certification = bar discipline.

**Layer 2 — Federal Court (11 USC § 528):**  
§ 528 debt relief agency disclosure violations — sanctions possible in bankruptcy proceedings. Courts have applied § 528 to business bankruptcy attorneys.

**Layer 3 — U.S. Trustee Program:**  
U.S. Trustee can move to dismiss, convert, or impose sanctions for bad-faith filings, preference self-dealing, and failure to comply with DIP obligations. Attorneys who file Chapter 11 without cash collateral motions face U.S. Trustee scrutiny on Day 1.
