# Viral Hooks Reference

Library of hook patterns that consistently perform on Solana CT. Use as scaffolding for `/generate-viral-post`, `/generate-thread`, `/generate-replies`. Mix categories; never produce 10 variants all from the same template.

## Single-tweet hook patterns

### 1. Numeric specificity
Numbers stop the scroll because they imply a real story.

- "spent 4,200 sol on memecoins last cycle. here's what I'd do differently."
- "47 of 50 ai-agent projects launched in 2024 are already dead."
- "made $0 from my first 3 solana projects."

### 2. Named target / contrarian
Pick a fight with an idea, a project, or a tribe. Not a person you'd harass.

- "stop calling solana 'the ethereum killer'. it's its own thing and that framing is cope."
- "the 'no token utility' argument is the new 'bitcoin uses too much energy'."
- "every L2 is just an L1 with extra steps."

### 3. Mystery / unfinished sentence
Leaves a gap the reader has to close by clicking.

- "the real reason [project] is fading isn't what you think."
- "there's a wallet that's been quietly buying every dip since january."

### 4. Stakes / vulnerability
Real-feeling personal stakes. Must be true, or close to it.

- "almost gave up on this build last week. shipping anyway."
- "watched a friend lose 6 figures on perps last night. wild times."

### 5. Insider framing
"you're early to..." or "while everyone's watching X, Y is happening." Implies the author sees what others don't.

- "while ct argues about memecoins, the real story is what's happening in solana mobile builds."
- "you're early to [narrative]. nobody's talking about it yet."

### 6. Cope-bait / declarative correction
Position the reader's current belief as wrong, then correct.

- "you don't need a token to ship."
- "you don't need a vc to launch on solana. you need users."

### 7. Receipts on display
Show, don't tell. Numbers, screenshots, on-chain.

- "1 month ago: 0 users. today: 4,217 daily actives. here's the playbook."
- "[chart] zoom out."

### 8. Format hijack
Take a famous tweet format and twist it for the ecosystem.

- "name a more iconic duo than [X] and [Y]."
- "tell me you're new to solana without telling me you're new to solana."

### 9. Single-word power moves
For accounts with built-up audience. Compression to the floor.

- "early."
- "ngmi."
- "lfg."
- "bullish."

Don't suggest these to small accounts — they read as larping.

### 10. Question that filters
Reply-bait that filters for the audience you want.

- "what's the most underrated solana protocol right now? no memes."
- "founders: what's the one tool you'd 10x pay for that doesn't exist yet?"

## Thread hook patterns

A thread lives or dies on tweet 1. The rest doesn't matter if nobody opens it.

### A. Promise + scope
"how to [do thing] in [timeframe] / [number] steps."

- "how I got my first 1,000 followers on solana ct in 30 days. no farming, no buying."

### B. Receipt-led
Lead with the outcome, then teach the path.

- "$0 → $42k mrr in 6 months building on solana. here's the exact playbook."

### C. Counter-conventional
"everyone says X. I tried it. it's wrong."

- "everyone says you need a token to attract liquidity. we shipped without one and hit $2m tvl. here's how."

### D. Forensic
"I broke down X. here's what I found."

- "I read every solana governance proposal of the last 90 days. here's what's actually changing."

### E. Story spine
Personal arc → universal lesson.

- "I almost rage-quit crypto in 2022. building through that taught me 5 things about solana that finally make sense now."

## Thread structure rules

- **Tweet 1**: hook. nothing else.
- **Tweet 2**: stakes / why this matters / who it's for.
- **Tweets 3–N**: one beat per tweet. Each tweet should be screenshot-shareable on its own.
- **Final tweet**: payoff + CTA. CTA can be a follow ask, a product link, or a question.
- Keep ≤ 10 tweets unless the topic genuinely needs more. Most threads die after tweet 7.
- Number the tweets if it's a list (1/, 2/). Don't number if it's a story arc.

## Reply patterns

Replies under big accounts are the cheapest way to grow if done well.

### Reply types that earn follows

1. **Add a number.** OP says a thing. You add a number that confirms or contradicts with data.
2. **Add a counter.** Sharply disagree with one specific point, not the whole take.
3. **Add the unsaid.** "the real reason this matters is..."
4. **Add a meme.** Image reply that recontextualizes OP's tweet.
5. **Add the receipt.** Screenshot of onchain data, repo, or an old tweet that proves the point.
6. **One-line zinger.** Pure compression. Funny or sharp. No emojis.

### Reply anti-patterns (will not grow)

- "great post 🔥"
- "this 👆"
- "💯"
- "checkout my project [link]"
- 3-paragraph essays under a 12-word OP

## Quote-tweet patterns

QRTs work when you add a layer OP didn't provide.

- **Co-sign with a twist:** "this — and the reason nobody's saying it is..."
- **Disagree with respect:** "love [author], but this is wrong on [specific point]."
- **Extend:** "true. add to this: [your angle]."
- **Reframe:** "this isn't [OP's frame]. it's [your frame]."

Bad QRT: "interesting" or "thoughts?" — adds nothing, gets ignored.

## Variant generation rule

When the user asks for N viral posts on one topic, draw from at least 4 different hook patterns above. Producing 5 variants that all use "numeric specificity" looks like AI output. Diversity = native feel.
