Honnō-ji Showcase: one-stroke execution, political aftermath, and a screenshot that tells a whole story

Published for YumFu as a simple example of what a strong chat-native illustrated RPG turn can look like.

Why this scene works

  • It is visually immediate: one image already implies victory, brutality, rank, and consequence.
  • It is mechanically legible: the player can see the check, the loot, the state update, and the branch point.
  • It is socially shareable: even outside the game, a single screenshot already feels like a complete dramatic moment.
  • It shows what YumFu is good at: world-specific art, narrative pressure, and meaningful follow-up choices.

English scene version

⚔️🩸 Severance You did not hesitate. Mitsuhide tried to say something — his lips moved. Maybe it was a last word, maybe only a name. But you had already drawn your blade. [Beheading check] d20+5 = 22 vs Mitsuhide’s struggle d20+1 = 21 ⚡ Narrow win! In the very last instant he desperately twisted his head aside — but the wound in his shoulder slowed him. One stroke. Clean. Mitsuhide’s head rolled across the wooden floor of Honnō-ji, the eyes still half open, that eerie smile frozen in place. Your rōnin fell to their knees. In the firelight, you raised the head in one hand, blood dripping onto the tatami. No one struck the bell of Honnō-ji, yet the flames licking across the beams made it hum all the same.

What the player gets next

  • A trophy item with political value
  • A bloodstained secret letter as a new hook
  • A named sword as a prestige artifact
  • A consequential choice: present the head to Nobunaga, Hideyoshi, Ieyasu, or keep it and rise independently

That combination is the real point: not just spectacle, but spectacle that opens into strategy.
