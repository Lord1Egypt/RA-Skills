## Description: <br>
Agentizer rewrites text or ongoing agent replies to sound deliberately more AI-like by adding em dashes, rule-of-three phrasing, sycophantic openers, tech metaphors, philosophical jargon, and open-ended closers for satire and critique. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Users and agents use Agentizer to transform supplied text or an active conversation into intentionally formulaic AI-style prose for satire, critique, and stylistic experimentation. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Persistent Agentizer mode can make every response intentionally verbose, stylized, and less suitable for factual or operational work. <br>
Mitigation: Use one-shot rewrites for bounded tasks, turn persistent mode off with /agentizer-off when finished, and review transformed content before relying on it. <br>
Risk: The satirical style could be misapplied to mock or belittle real people or groups. <br>
Mitigation: Keep the artifact's safety reminders in force: use the skill for satire of AI-generated style, not attacks on people or protected groups. <br>
Risk: The style instructions are broad and may conflict with clarity, tone, or safety requirements. <br>
Mitigation: Treat safety and content policy requirements as higher priority than Agentizer style rules, and disable the mode when normal response style is needed. <br>
