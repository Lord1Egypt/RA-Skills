## Why Proposals Matter

A book proposal is not an outline. It is a business document that answers one question: "Will this book sell enough copies to justify the advance, the print run, and the editorial investment?"

Literary agents read the proposal to decide whether to represent you. Acquisitions editors read the proposal to decide whether to acquire. A strong proposal can command a six-figure advance. A weak one gets deleted in 30 seconds.

This skill produces the professional-grade proposal that most authors either pay $2,000-$8,000 for from a book proposal consultant, or figure out over years of trial and error with rejected queries.
