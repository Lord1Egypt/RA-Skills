# Prompt Library

Ready-to-use prompts for business research and strategy, built on a KPI-driven "No BS" framework: every prompt forces a specific output format, success metrics, risks, and a recommendation. Fill in the `[bracketed]` placeholders and submit.

## How to Use
1. Pick the prompt matching your decision.
2. Replace `[bracketed]` placeholders with your specifics.
3. Submit as-is or tune.
4. Expect a structured, decision-ready output.

---

## Market & Competitive Analysis

### Competitive Landscape
```
I'm analysing [market/product]. Give me a competitive landscape.

No BS. For 3–5 direct or adjacent competitors (global, not just regional):
1. A table: Competitor | Price | Positioning | Strength | Gap
2. The single biggest unmet market gap, with one supporting datapoint
3. One-sentence positioning we could own that none of them can honestly claim
4. The #1 fatal risk in this space and how to counter it
```

### Market Sizing
```
Size the market for [product/service] in [geography/segment].

Give me TAM, SAM, and Year-1 SOM — each with the explicit calculation and the assumptions behind it. Flag which inputs are sourced vs. estimated. End with the one number that matters most for a go/no-go and why.
```

## Investor & Deal Strategy

### Investor Pitch Validation
```
Here is my pitch for [opportunity]: [paste pitch].

Pressure-test it as a skeptical investor would. Give me:
1. The 3 strongest points
2. The 3 weakest / most likely objections, each with the rebuttal I'd need
3. The single question that would kill the deal if I can't answer it
4. A sharper one-paragraph version of the pitch
```

### Deal Structure Assessment
```
Assess this deal: [structure, terms, parties, valuation].

Give me: fair vs. unfair terms, hidden costs or clauses to watch, the counterparty's real incentives, the top 3 risks with mitigations, and a Proceed / Renegotiate / Decline recommendation with confidence level.
```

## Business Strategy

### Strategic Positioning
```
My business is [context]. My market is [context].

Give me one defensible, ownable positioning statement (one sentence), the 3 proof points that back it, and the 2 competitors it directly displaces. No generic claims — it must be something I can defend.
```

### Go-to-Market
```
I'm launching [product] to [audience].

Give me a 90-day GTM plan: the single highest-leverage channel and why, the first 3 moves in order, the 3 KPIs that prove it's working (30/60/90 day targets), and the #1 thing most likely to make this fail.
```

## Custom Prompt Template
```
I'm [context]. [Specific question about market/deal/strategy].

No BS — what would it take to [specific outcome]?

Give me:
1. [output 1 + format]
2. [output 2 + format]
3. [output 3 + format]
4. The #1 risk + mitigation
5. A specific recommendation
```

## Quality Standards
Every good prompt specifies: an output format (table/sections/JSON), KPI-driven questions, comparative benchmarks, an explicit risk, and a required recommendation — and demands evidence over speculation.
