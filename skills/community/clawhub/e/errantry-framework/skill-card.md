## Description: <br>
Mythological framework mapping Diane Duane's Young Wizards magic system to AI architecture patterns for agent workflow design, multi-agent systems, AI concept explanation, agent behavior debugging, alignment framing, and autonomous-system constraints. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Developers and AI practitioners use this skill as a conceptual reference for designing, explaining, debugging, and aligning agentic or multi-agent workflows through the Errantry metaphor. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Broad trigger wording may introduce Errantry metaphors into ordinary agent-design or debugging conversations where that framing is not desired. <br>
Mitigation: Invoke the skill explicitly when the metaphorical framework is useful, and fall back to normal technical language for routine design or debugging. <br>
Risk: Conceptual alignment guidance could be mistaken for a replacement for operational controls on real systems. <br>
Mitigation: Continue to use normal approvals, sandboxing, rollback, security review, and domain-specific safety practices for deployed systems. <br>
