# Indie Hacker Growth — Build in Public + Community-Led Distribution

For solo founders who want to grow without VC, teams, or ad budgets.

## Build in Public

What to share, how often, and which platforms reward transparency:

- **Revenue milestones**: Monthly MRR updates drive the most engagement and accountability
- **Behind-the-scenes decisions**: Share why you chose tech stack, pricing, or features
- **Failures and pivots**: Authenticity builds trust faster than polished success stories
- **Cadence**: 3–5 posts/week minimum; daily updates during launches
- **Platforms that reward BIP**: X/Twitter (threads), Indie Hackers (milestones), LinkedIn (journey posts)

## Audience-First Approach

Grow followers before you have a product to sell:

- **Teach what you know**: Share hard-won expertise in your domain before building
- **Document the journey**: Start posting before you have anything to sell
- **Engage genuinely**: Reply to others, quote-tweet with insight, join conversations
- **Consistency compounds**: 100 days of posting > 10 viral tweets
- **Email > social**: Convert followers to email subscribers at every opportunity

## Newsletter Engine

From 0 to 1,000 subscribers with zero paid promotion:

- **Lead magnet**: One high-value resource (template, checklist, database) gated by email
- **Cross-promotion**: Partner with 3–5 newsletters in adjacent niches for shoutouts
- **Social → email funnel**: Every viral post ends with "Subscribe for more like this"
- **Welcome sequence**: 3-email onboarding that delivers value and builds relationship
- **Referral program**: SparkLoop or similar — reward subscribers who bring friends

## Community-Led Distribution

Leverage indie communities (IH, Twitter, HN) effectively:

- **Indie Hackers**: Post detailed milestone updates, engage in comments, give feedback to others
- **Hacker News**: Show HN posts for launches; comment on relevant threads year-round
- **Product Hunt**: Build hunter relationships before launch day; engage the community regularly
- **Reddit**: Find your niche subreddits; genuine value-first participation for months before any self-promotion
- **Discord/Slack groups**: Become the helpful expert, then naturally mention your product when relevant

## Micro-SaaS Economics

Pricing, margins, and lifestyle revenue targets:

- **Target metrics**: $5K–$30K MRR for solo sustainability (location-dependent)
- **Pricing psychology**: Start at $29/mo minimum; raise prices every quarter until churn increases
- **Margins**: Aim for 85%+ gross margins (avoid expensive infrastructure)
- **LTV/CAC**: Organic-only means near-zero CAC; even modest LTV is highly profitable
- **Exit optionality**: Build in public → multiple acquisition offers at 3–5x ARR
