# Prompt 4: Digital Ads + Local SEO

## What This Generates
- Google LSA (Local Services Ad) profile copy
- Google RSA campaigns (2 ad groups)
- Facebook/Instagram parent-targeted campaigns (3 ad sets)
- Nextdoor community posts (3 versions)
- SEO landing pages with Schema JSON-LD (2 pages)
- Google Business Profile optimization posts (10 posts)
- Review management response templates

```
Center name:

Primary differentiator for digital (safety/ratios/curriculum/NAEYC/meals/hours):
Main competitor positioning (what are parents comparing you against?):

## The Prompt

```
You are a digital marketing specialist for childcare and early education businesses.

Generate a complete digital acquisition package for [CENTER NAME] in [CITY, STATE].

[PASTE YOUR CENTER INFORMATION HERE]

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS:
1. GOOGLE LSA: Childcare is a regulated service — state license number must appear in business description
2. COPPA (all digital): ALL ads target parents only (ages 25-45, parent/family interests). Add audience note to every ad: "Target: parents 25-45 — exclude all under-13 app/game/platform placements"
3. FACEBOOK AUDIENCE NETWORK: "Exclude Audience Network placements on apps and games rated for children under 13" — childcare centers have received FTC inquiry for inadvertently targeting children through broad Audience Network settings
4. ENROLLMENT CAPACITY: No guarantees of spot availability. Use: "Enrollment open — spots subject to licensed capacity and age-group availability"
5. SAFETY CLAIMS: Must specify what "background check" means: "FBI fingerprint + state childcare registry screening for all staff"
6. REVIEW MANAGEMENT: Never incentivize reviews. FTC 2023 Endorsement Guide: no "leave us a 5-star review" language. Use: "Share your honest experience."
7. SUPERLATIVES: "Best daycare in [City]" requires documented award with date. Use: "[X]+ five-star Google reviews — [City]'s trusted childcare" instead.
8. NAEYC IN ADS: Only if current. Include accreditation number and valid-through date. Do not use NAEYC logo without permission.
9. SCHEMA: Do not claim NAEYC accreditation in local business schema unless currently enrolled.
10. RATIO CLAIMS IN ADS: "Staff ratios that meet or exceed [STATE] licensing standards" — never claim a ratio better than licensed for.

DELIVERABLES:

**A. GOOGLE LSA PROFILE COPY**

Business description (250 characters max):
Lead with: licensed status + license number. Differentiator. Call to action.
Example structure: "Licensed childcare center (State License #XXXXX) serving infants–preschool. NAEYC Accredited. FBI-screened staff. Open enrollment — schedule a tour today."

Service categories (check all applicable):
☐ Daycare Center
☐ Preschool
☐ Before & After School Care
☐ Summer Camp / Summer Care
☐ Infant Care
☐ Toddler Care

Services offered list (10 items):
Generate 10 service line items appropriate for [CENTER NAME]'s program mix.

Business highlights (3):
Format: "Verified: [CLAIM]" — use only verifiable claims (licensed, NAEYC, years in business, Google rating)

**B. GOOGLE SEARCH ADS — 2 AD GROUPS**

Ad Group 1: "Daycare near me" / "[City] daycare" / "childcare [City]"

15 Headlines (30 characters max each):
Cover: licensed status, safety, enrollment status, NAEYC (if applicable), ratios, location, years in business, tour CTA, Google reviews, age groups served.
Compliance: no "best" without citation, no ratio claims beyond licensed.

4 Descriptions (90 characters max each):
Each should address a parent's primary concern: safety, development, enrollment availability, trust.

4 Sitelinks:
"Schedule a Tour | Tuition & Enrollment | Our Programs | About Our Staff"

Negative keyword list (10 terms to exclude):
Focus on: irrelevant educational searches, competitor brand terms (if not running competitor campaigns), non-parent searches.

Ad Group 2: "Preschool [City]" / "early learning [City]" / "pre-K [City]"

15 Headlines (30 characters max each):
Focus: curriculum, school readiness framing (developmentally appropriate language), NAEYC, teacher qualifications, fall enrollment.

4 Descriptions (90 characters max each):
Emphasize: school readiness preparation (correctly framed), curriculum approach, transition to kindergarten support.

4 Sitelinks: "View Preschool Program | Fall Enrollment | Tour Our Preschool | Meet Our Teachers"

**C. FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM CAMPAIGNS — 3 AD SETS**

Ad Set 1 — COLD AUDIENCE ENROLLMENT CAMPAIGN

Targeting spec: "Parents ages 25-45, [City] + 10-15 mile radius, interests: parenting, childcare, early childhood education, kindergarten readiness, first-time parents, toddler activities. COPPA: Exclude Audience Network placements on under-13 apps/games."

Ad format: Single image or carousel (2-3 slides)

Primary text (125 chars): Hook on parent's primary fear → your differentiator
Headline (40 chars): Enrollment CTA + license indicator
Description (30 chars): Supporting detail

3 creative variations (test):
- Safety-focused: "Every staff member has passed FBI fingerprint + state registry screening."
- Curriculum-focused: Your program approach differentiator
- Social proof: "[X]+ families trust [CENTER NAME] — here's why"

COPPA note: "Do NOT run on Instagram Feed if under-13 users may appear in broad audiences. Run on Facebook and Instagram with age gate 25+ minimum."

Ad Set 2 — WARM AUDIENCE TOUR INVITATION (website visitors + video viewers)

Targeting: Website custom audience (30-day visitors) + video viewers (25%+) — lookalike excluded
Tone: Warm, familiar — they already know you
Primary text (125 chars): "You visited us — come see it in person"
Format: Video preferred (30-second center walkthrough), single image fallback
CTA button: "Book a Tour"

Ad Set 3 — WAITLIST URGENCY

Targeting: Broad parent targeting [City] — exclude current customers
Copy framework: "[X] spots available in our [age group] room this [month/season]"
Note: Use real availability numbers — false scarcity is FTC deceptive advertising
FTC-compliant parent testimonial option: "[Parent first name], parent of [age group] child: '[1-sentence quote — typical experience, no exceptional outcome claims]'"
CTA: "Join the Waitlist" or "Check Availability"

**D. NEXTDOOR COMMUNITY POSTS (3 versions)**

All 3 versions must be disclosed as business posts (not neighbor posts):

Version 1 — Enrollment announcement:
Header: "[CENTER NAME] — [City] — Enrollment Open"
Body: Community-first framing. Years in neighborhood. Current enrollment status. License number. Tour CTA.
Under 200 words. Neighborly tone, not sales-y.

Version 2 — Event announcement (open house):
Invite neighbors/parents to see the center in person. What the event includes. No pressure enrollment push.
Under 150 words.

Version 3 — Community involvement / trust post:
Staff certification completion, safety upgrade, community event participation — something that builds trust without a hard sell.
Under 150 words.

**E. SEO LANDING PAGES (2 pages)**

Page 1: "Daycare in [City], [State]"

Title tag (60 chars): "Daycare in [City] | Licensed & [Accreditation] | [Center Name]"
Meta description (155 chars): Include license mention + enrollment CTA + [City]

H1: "Licensed Daycare in [City], [State] — [Center Name]"
H2 structure (5 H2s):
- Why [City] Families Choose [Center Name]
- Our Infant, Toddler & Preschool Programs
- Safety, Licensing & Staff Qualifications
- [City] Families Say (FTC-compliant reviews section)
- Enrollment & Tuition

Body copy (600 words):
- Lead with: state license number (visible, not buried)
- Cover: age groups, ratios, staff qualifications (specify background check type), curriculum, meals (CACFP if applicable), hours
- Natural [City] + [Neighborhood] keyword integration
- Enrollment CTA in every section

Schema — Local Business:
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "ChildCare",
  "name": "[CENTER NAME]",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "[ADDRESS]",
    "addressLocality": "[CITY]",
    "addressRegion": "[STATE]",
    "postalCode": "[ZIP]"
  },
  "telephone": "[PHONE]",
  "url": "[URL]",
  "openingHours": "[HOURS]",
  "priceRange": "[TUITION RANGE]",
  "description": "[150-char description including license number]",
  "hasCredential": "State Childcare License #[NUMBER]"
}
```

Schema — FAQ (8 Q&A):
1. Is [CENTER NAME] licensed? (Answer: yes, license number, state licensing body link)
2. What ages do you accept?
3. What are your staff-to-child ratios?
4. What meals are provided? (CACFP language if applicable)
5. What background checks do your staff undergo?
6. Are you NAEYC accredited? (Only include if yes — with number and date)
7. What is your sick child/illness policy?
8. How much does daycare cost?

Page 2: "Preschool in [City], [State]"

Same structure as Page 1, with curriculum/school-readiness focus.

Title tag: "Preschool in [City] | Early Learning | [Center Name] | State Licensed"
H1: "Preschool in [City], [State] — Building School-Ready Learners at [Center Name]"
H2s:
- What Makes Our Preschool Program Different
- Curriculum & Learning Approach
- School Readiness (developmentally appropriate framing — no guarantees)
- Safety & Licensing
- Fall Enrollment

Schema note: Preschool schema type = "PreSchool" under ChildCare

**F. GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE POSTS (10 posts)**

Post format: Under 1,500 chars, specific CTA button recommendation, compliance note.

Post 1 — Enrollment CTA (spring enrollment):
CTA button: "Book a Tour" | Compliance: License number visible | Photo: Facility exterior or classroom (no child faces without release)

Post 2 — Enrollment CTA (fall enrollment):
CTA: "Learn More" | Seasonal urgency (fall spots filling)

Post 3 — Staff Spotlight #1:
Staff member (with consent). Credentials listed as documented. No child photos.
CTA: "Learn More" | Photo: Staff headshot (with consent)

Post 4 — Staff Spotlight #2:
Different staff member. Teaching philosophy angle.

Post 5 — Educational tip for parents:
"Did you know? Children ages 3-4 build pre-reading skills through [activity type] — here's how to reinforce it at home." Positions center as expert, no enrollment push.

Post 6 — Educational tip #2:
Different developmental domain (social-emotional, math concepts, gross motor).

Post 7 — Open house / event announcement:
Event details, RSVP CTA. Photo: Facility or staff (no children without releases).

Post 8 — Event recap / milestone:
Post-event celebration. Community connection. FTC: No child photos without releases.

Post 9 — Community involvement:
Food drive, local event participation, neighborhood connection. Trust-building.

Post 10 — Safety/trust post:
Staff CPR/First Aid renewal, licensing inspection passed, fire drill completion, facility upgrade.
"This month, our entire teaching team completed annual [CERTIFICATION] renewal — because every family deserves to know their child's caregivers are current and trained."

**G. REVIEW MANAGEMENT TEMPLATES**

5-Star Response:
"Thank you so much for sharing this — it means the world to our team. We're so glad [general reference to experience — not child name, not enrollment confirmation]. We look forward to seeing your family at [CENTER NAME]. — [Director name]"
(Note: Never confirm enrollment in public review responses — FERPA-adjacent best practice)

4-Star Response:
"Thank you for your kind words and your honest feedback. We'd love to hear more about how we can earn that fifth star — please feel free to reach out to me directly at [email/phone]. — [Director name]"

3-Star Response:
"We appreciate you taking the time to share your experience. I'd welcome the chance to learn more and make things right — please contact me at [email/phone]. — [Director name]"

1-2 Star Response:
"I'm sorry to hear about your experience and want to address this directly. Please contact me at [email/phone] or [email] so we can speak privately. Your family's experience matters to us. — [Director name]"

Review Request Script (FTC 2023 compliant — no incentives, no conditional language):
"We'd love to hear about your experience at [CENTER NAME]. If you have a moment, an honest Google review helps other families find us and make informed decisions: [Google review link]. No pressure — we appreciate you either way."
```

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Google LSA description includes state license number
- [ ] All Facebook/Instagram ads include COPPA audience note (exclude under-13 placements)
- [ ] Enrollment claims include "subject to licensed capacity" language
- [ ] Background check type specified (FBI fingerprint + state registry)
- [ ] No "best daycare" superlative without documented award + date
- [ ] NAEYC in ads only if current — with accreditation number + valid-through date
- [ ] Schema does not claim NAEYC unless currently enrolled
- [ ] Ratio claims within licensed capacity
- [ ] Review request template uses FTC-compliant language (no incentives, no "5-star" directive)
- [ ] Review responses do not confirm individual enrollment status
- [ ] Nextdoor posts disclosed as business, not neighbor posts
- [ ] SEO pages include license number in visible body text
