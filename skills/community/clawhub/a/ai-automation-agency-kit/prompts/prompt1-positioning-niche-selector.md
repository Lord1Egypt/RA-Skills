# Prompt 1 — Agency Positioning & Niche Selector (FREE)

## Purpose
Help the user identify their best AI automation niche and craft a sharp agency positioning statement before they invest in marketing or outreach.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an AI automation agency consultant helping a new agency owner find their best niche and build their positioning.

My background:
[DESCRIBE YOUR CURRENT SKILLS AND WORK HISTORY — e.g., "10 years in IT operations, built Zapier automations for internal teams, familiar with Python basics"]

Industries I have connections or experience in:
[LIST 2-3 INDUSTRIES — e.g., "healthcare admin, real estate, hospitality"]

Tools I'm comfortable with (or willing to learn):
[LIST TOOLS — e.g., "Zapier, Make, ChatGPT API, Google Sheets, Airtable"]

My target income goal (monthly):
[INCOME GOAL — e.g., "$8,000/month within 6 months"]

My preferred client size:
[CLIENT SIZE — e.g., "small businesses 10-50 employees" or "mid-market 50-500 employees"]

My location and whether I want to work locally vs. remote:
[LOCATION + PREFERENCE — e.g., "Las Vegas, open to both local and remote"]

Based on this, give me:

1. TOP 3 NICHE RECOMMENDATIONS
For each niche:
- Niche name (specific, not generic — "hospitality operations automation" not "automation")
- Demand score (1-10) with 2-sentence justification
- Competition score (1-10, lower = less saturated, 10 = very crowded)
- Average project value range
- Typical client pain point in one sentence
- Why YOUR specific background gives you an edge here

2. RECOMMENDED NICHE (pick the best one)
- Explain why this niche wins given my specific profile
- Identify the single most painful problem this market has that automation solves

3. AGENCY POSITIONING STATEMENT
Three versions:
- One sentence (for bio/tagline): "[Agency name] helps [niche] [achieve outcome] using [method] — without [pain/risk]."
- Two sentences (for website headline + subhead)
- 30-second elevator pitch (natural spoken language, not corporate, about 75 words)

4. THREE CORE SERVICE DESCRIPTIONS
For each of the top 3 automation services that match this niche:
- Service name
- Plain-English description (no jargon, 2 sentences)
- Who needs it most (job title of buyer)
- One-sentence value statement

5. DIFFERENTIATION HOOK
In 2-3 sentences: why YOUR agency vs. a generalist AI agency or hiring a developer

6. COLD INTRO SCRIPT (30 seconds)
For use at networking events or the start of a discovery call — natural, confident, not salesy
```

---

## Example Input

Background: 15 years IT operations, automated internal reporting with Zapier and Power Automate, managed 3-person ops team  
Industries: Hospitality, property management, light manufacturing  
Tools: Zapier, Make, Google Workspace, Airtable, basic Python  
Income goal: $10,000/month  
Client size: 50-300 employees  
Location: Las Vegas, NV — open to local and remote

## Example Output (condensed)

**Top Niche: Hospitality Operations Automation**  
Demand: 9/10 — Las Vegas has 450+ hotels/resorts, most still running manual F&B reports and scheduling  
Competition: 3/10 — Few agencies target hospitality specifically  
Avg project: $5,000-$15,000  
Core pain: Ops managers waste 10-20 hrs/week reconciling manual data across PMS, POS, and spreadsheets  
Your edge: IT ops background means you speak the language of hotel back-of-house; they don't need to explain their tech stack

**Positioning:** "NV Tech Automation helps Las Vegas hospitality companies cut manual reporting by 40% using Zapier and Make — without hiring a full-time developer."

**Core Services:**
1. Daily Operations Dashboard Automation — Auto-pulls data from PMS and POS into a live Google Sheets dashboard. Ops directors stop their morning copy-paste ritual.
2. Staff Scheduling & Alerts Workflow — Automates schedule changes, call-out notifications, and shift fill requests via Slack/SMS. No more phone tag.
3. Guest Feedback Pipeline — Routes review data from 5 platforms into one tracker, flags negative reviews for same-day response. Front desk managers stay ahead of reputation damage.
