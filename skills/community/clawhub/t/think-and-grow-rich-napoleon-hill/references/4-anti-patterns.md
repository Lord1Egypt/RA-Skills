> Source: Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill, Chapter 14

# Anti-Patterns: The Six Basic Fears

## The Six Basic Fears

Hill identified six fundamental fears that hold human beings back. Every other fear is a form of one of these six.

| # | Fear | Symptom |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fear of Poverty | Worry about money, hoarding, over-caution |
| 2 | Fear of Criticism | Self-consciousness, fear of what others think, loss of individuality |
| 3 | Fear of Ill Health | Hypochondria, unhealthy lifestyle, fear of aging |
| 4 | Fear of Loss of Love | Jealousy, possessiveness, fear of abandonment |
| 5 | Fear of Old Age | Fear of slowing down, loss of usefulness |
| 6 | Fear of Death | Despair, hopelessness, inability to live fully |

## Anti-Pattern 1: Fear of Poverty

**The mistake:** Being paralyzed by the fear of being poor. This leads to over-caution — staying in a job you hate, never investing, never taking risks.

**The correction:** Poverty is a state of mind, not a bank balance. The fear of poverty is more damaging than poverty itself. Replace the fear with burning desire for wealth. Hill writes: "Poverty begins in the mind. The person who says 'I can't afford it' will never be rich."

> **Case: The miser** (Chapter 14): Hill describes a man who was wealthy but lived in constant fear of losing his money. He refused to invest, refused to spend, and refused to enjoy life. "He was richer than most men, but more miserable. His fear of poverty kept him poor in the only way that matters — in spirit."

## Anti-Pattern 2: Fear of Criticism

**The mistake:** Letting other people's opinions control your actions. Fear of what others will say stops you from pursuing your dreams.

**The correction:** Most criticism comes from people who are afraid to pursue their own dreams. Ignore them. The only opinion that matters is your own.

> **Case: Alexander Graham Bell** (Chapter 14): When Bell announced he was working on a device that could transmit the human voice over a wire, he was laughed at. "Everyone knew it was impossible." Bell persisted. Within a decade, every critic had a telephone in their home. The fear of criticism would have stopped him if he had let it.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Fear of Ill Health

**The mistake:** Obsessing over health to the point where the obsession makes you sick. Hypochondria is a real phenomenon.

**The correction:** The fear of illness can actually create the illness through the power of autosuggestion. Focus on health, not on the absence of disease. Think of yourself as strong and vital.

> **Case: The nervous woman** (Chapter 14): A woman who constantly feared she had diseases. She visited doctors regularly, but they found nothing wrong. Hill advised her to stop reading about diseases and start reading about health. Within months, her symptoms disappeared. "She had created her own illness through fear."

## Anti-Pattern 4: Indecision

**The mistake:** Procrastinating on decisions, waiting for the "perfect" moment, changing your mind repeatedly.

**The correction:** Make decisions quickly. A bad decision executed well is better than a perfect decision never made. The habit of decisiveness creates momentum. Indecision creates stagnation.

> **Case: The man who waited too long** (Chapter 7): A man spent five years researching the restaurant business before opening one. He had the best plan, the best location, the best research. But by the time he opened, the market had changed. He failed. "His indecision cost him more than any wrong decision would have."

## Anti-Pattern 5: Lack of Persistence

**The mistake:** Starting with enthusiasm and quitting at the first sign of difficulty.

**The correction:** Persistence is the only quality that guarantees success. Treat every obstacle as a test of your persistence, not a sign that you should quit. Most breakthroughs happen just after the point where most people give up.
