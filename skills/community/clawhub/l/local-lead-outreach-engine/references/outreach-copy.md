# Outreach Copy

Use this reference when writing cold email, LinkedIn messages, follow-ups, or send-ready outreach sequences.

## First Email Structure

Default structure:

1. Human opener with a specific observed signal.
2. One sentence connecting the signal to a likely business problem.
3. One sentence explaining the user's offer.
4. Low-friction CTA.

Example shape:

```text
Subject: Quick idea for {{company}}

Hi {{first_name}},

I noticed {{specific_observation}}. Usually when that is happening, {{likely_business_problem}} gets harder than it needs to be.

I help {{type_of_company}} with {{specific_outcome}}, without turning it into a big rebuild or long project.

Worth me sending over the 2-3 fixes I would start with?

{{sender_name}}
```

## Follow-Up Sequence

Use 2-4 follow-ups. Keep each one shorter than the first email.

Follow-up 1: Add one useful observation or small idea.

Follow-up 2: Reframe the outcome or share a concise example.

Follow-up 3: Ask if there is a better person or timing.

Follow-up 4: Polite close-out.

Do not guilt, pressure, fake scarcity, or imply prior relationship.

## Good CTAs

Use:

- "Worth me sending that over?"
- "Should I send a quick example?"
- "Useful if I put together the quick version?"
- "Is this something you are looking at this quarter?"
- "Would it help if I showed what I would change first?"

Avoid:

- "Book a call"
- "Are you free Tuesday?"
- "Can I steal 15 minutes?"
- "Let's connect"
- "I guarantee"

## Personalization Rules

Personalize with:

- a page, campaign, job post, product, location, event, social post, review pattern, or public update
- a concrete issue that directly relates to the user's offer
- one sentence only, unless the user asks for detailed personalization

Do not personalize with:

- fake compliments
- personal data
- guesses about private internal problems
- exaggerated claims like "I have been following your company"

## Tone

Use:

- direct
- specific
- calm
- practical
- plain English

Avoid:

- "revolutionize"
- "unlock"
- "skyrocket"
- "game-changing"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "just checking in"
- "synergy"

## Production Email Checklist

Before marking copy as send-ready:

- include sender identity
- include clear business reason for contact
- include an opt-out line such as "No worries if not - happy not to follow up."
- remove unsupported claims
- remove fake urgency
- remove attachments unless requested
- keep first email under 140 words
