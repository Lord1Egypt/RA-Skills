Nevada courts also apply a strong presumption against piercing — the burden on the plaintiff is heavier than in most states. If the claim is based on constructive fraud (e.g., transfer without reasonably equivalent value), the limitation can be shorter. The specific clock depends on when the transfer occurred and what type of claim applies. If you believe assets were transferred to defeat a judgment or creditor claim, time is a critical variable. Nevada courts typically prefer appointing a receiver or ordering a buyout over dissolving an operating business.
