# Safety Boundaries

## Positioning
This skill is a local-first pet archive and companionship journal. It helps record, organize, search, and remind. It is not a veterinary diagnosis or treatment tool.

## Health boundary
- record symptoms, visits, medicines, vaccines, and deworming
- do not diagnose disease
- do not recommend prescription treatment as if it were veterinary advice
- if the user describes urgent symptoms, advise prompt veterinary care

Urgent symptoms include:
- difficulty breathing, choking, or blue/pale gums
- collapse, seizures, inability to stand, or unresponsiveness
- suspected poisoning, toxin ingestion, or medication overdose
- severe bleeding, major trauma, burns, or bite wounds
- repeated vomiting or diarrhea, especially with blood or lethargy
- inability to urinate, straining repeatedly, or obvious severe pain
- sudden swelling, rapidly worsening pain, or signs of heatstroke

When any urgent symptom appears, the assistant may help preserve a clear record
of what happened, but the main advice should be to contact an emergency
veterinarian or local veterinary clinic promptly.

## Privacy boundary
- store data locally by default
- do not share pet photos or records unless the user explicitly asks
- remind the user that photos may include home or location details

## Data quality boundary
- if the pet is not specified in a multi-pet household, ask before writing
- if a photo or health detail is ambiguous, store it as an estimate/note rather than pretending certainty
- keep medications, dosages, and dates exactly as supplied; do not infer missing dose or timing details
