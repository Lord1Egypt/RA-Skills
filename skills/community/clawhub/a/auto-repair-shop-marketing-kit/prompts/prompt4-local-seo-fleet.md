# Prompt 4: Digital Ads + Local SEO + Fleet Account Outreach (PAID)

## Instructions

Use this prompt to dominate local search and break into high-value fleet accounts. Provide your service area and whether you want to pursue fleet B2B in addition to retail customers.

---

## The Prompt

You are an expert local SEO strategist and B2B sales copywriter specializing in auto service businesses. Generate a complete local search domination plan and fleet account acquisition campaign for the following shop.

**Shop Information:**
- Shop name: [NAME]
- Address: [FULL ADDRESS]
- City, State, ZIP: [CITY, STATE, ZIP]
- Service radius: [X miles — or list specific cities/zip codes served]
- Primary services (top 5): [LIST]
- ASE credentials: [EXACT]
- EPA 608 certification: [UNIVERSAL / TYPE / NOT CERTIFIED]
- Shop affiliations: [NAPA / AAA / CarFax / iATN / other]
- Current Google Business Profile status: [verified / unverified / not created]
- Current GBP star rating: [X.X or "unknown"]
- Fleet services offered? [Yes — list: fleet maintenance contracts / priority scheduling / fleet billing / mobile service / No]
- Target fleet accounts: [property management companies / delivery/courier companies / landscaping / construction contractors / local government / school district / other]
- Geographic footprint for fleet outreach: [radius or specific companies if known]

**Generate the following:**

1. **15 Hyper-Local Keyword Targets:**
   Format: [Service] + [City/Neighborhood] — prioritized by estimated search volume
   Include:
   - Primary city + service combos (5 keywords)
   - Nearby city/neighborhood variants (5 keywords)
   - Near-me and intent-based variants (3 keywords)
   - Long-tail / problem-based keywords (2 keywords)
   
   Example format:
   - "auto repair Henderson NV" (high volume, primary)
   - "brake repair Green Valley NV" (neighborhood level)
   - "check engine light Henderson" (intent-based)
   - "AC not blowing cold car Henderson" (problem-based, long-tail)

2. **5 City/Neighborhood SEO Landing Pages:**
   For each page (400-500 words):
   - H1: [Service] in [City/Neighborhood]
   - Introduction with local relevance (reference local landmarks, roads, weather if relevant)
   - Service description with compliance-accurate language
   - Why choose us section (local, certified, compliant)
   - FAQPage JSON-LD schema (4 questions per page)
   - CTA with local phone number and address

3. **30-Day Google Business Profile Content Calendar:**
   Week 1 (Days 1-7):
   - Day 1: Shop overview post with trust signals
   - Day 3: Service spotlight (top service from campaign)
   - Day 5: Team/technician highlight (ASE credential accurate)
   - Day 7: Q&A seed (post a question + answer yourself — common customer concern)
   
   Week 2 (Days 8-14):
   - Day 8: Seasonal service reminder post
   - Day 10: Customer question answer (common concern for the season)
   - Day 12: Before/after photo post suggestion + caption
   - Day 14: Community post (local event mention or local partnership)
   
   Week 3 (Days 15-21):
   - Day 15: Offer post (compliant — "starting at" or specific promotion)
   - Day 17: Educational post ("How to know when your brakes need replacing")
   - Day 19: Review highlight post (with customer permission — do not alter the quote)
   - Day 21: Q&A seed #2 (different service area)
   
   Week 4 (Days 22-30):
   - Day 22: Service milestone post ("1,000 oil changes performed")
   - Day 24: Credential spotlight (EPA 608 or ASE cert explanation)
   - Day 26: Seasonal safety tip
   - Day 28: Referral program announcement (if applicable)
   - Day 30: Month recap + next month preview

4. **Google Local Services Ads (LSA) Optimization Checklist:**
   - Background check completion status (required for LSA)
   - License verification documentation needed
   - Insurance certificate requirements
   - Response rate target (80%+ within 24 hours required for "Google Guaranteed" badge)
   - Review minimum (aim for 10+ before launching LSA)
   - Service categories to select in LSA dashboard
   - Budget recommendation (starting at $X/day based on market)
   - LSA dispute process (fraudulent leads — how to request credit)

5. **Fleet Account Outreach — 5-Email Sequence:**
   
   Email 1 (Cold Introduction — Day 1):
   Subject: [SHOP NAME] — fleet maintenance for [COMPANY TYPE] in [CITY]
   Body: 150 words. Introduce shop, credentials, fleet services. One specific benefit relevant to their business type. No pressure CTA.
   
   Email 2 (Value Email — Day 5):
   Subject: How [COMPANY TYPE] fleets reduce downtime in [CITY]
   Body: 150 words. Educational content relevant to their fleet type (property managers = HVAC vans, delivery = high mileage oil changes). Position shop as expert. Soft CTA.
   
   Email 3 (Social Proof — Day 10):
   Subject: [Other fleet clients] use [SHOP NAME] for their [CITY] vehicles
   Body: 100 words. Reference similar fleet accounts (anonymized if necessary). One-sentence ROI claim (backed by data if available).
   
   Email 4 (Offer — Day 15):
   Subject: Free fleet assessment — [SHOP NAME] — [CITY]
   Body: 100 words. Offer: free multi-point inspection for their fleet vehicles + customized maintenance schedule quote. Specific availability slots.
   
   Email 5 (Final Follow-Up — Day 21):
   Subject: Last note — fleet maintenance for [COMPANY] in [CITY]
   Body: 75 words. Brief. Acknowledge they may not be ready. Leave door open. Clear unsubscribe path.
   
   All emails: include shop name, license number, ASE credential (brief), and contact. No misleading subject lines (CAN-SPAM compliance).

6. **Fleet Proposal Template:**
   - Cover page: shop credentials + fleet services summary
   - Vehicle audit section: how you'll assess their current fleet condition
   - Preventive maintenance schedule: service intervals by vehicle type/mileage
   - Pricing structure: per-vehicle flat rate, hourly with estimate cap, or monthly retainer
   - Priority scheduling guarantee language (be careful — "guarantee" requires defined scope)
   - Fleet billing options: net-30 / monthly invoice / per-service
   - Fleet account manager contact (single point of contact)
   - Terms: payment terms, cancellation, what happens if vehicle needs repair beyond PM scope

**COMPLIANCE RULES:**

**Google LSA:**
- Do not advertise as "Google Guaranteed" before completing the background check process
- "Google Screened" and "Google Guaranteed" are different — do not confuse them
- LSA requires up-to-date business license on file — ensure state license is current

**CAN-SPAM (fleet email outreach):**
- Physical address required in all commercial emails
- Clear identification as commercial communication
- Opt-out mechanism required in every email — honor within 10 business days
- No deceptive subject lines ("You requested this information" when they didn't = violation)

**Price Advertising:**
- "Starting at $X" = that price must be available for the advertised service to at least some customers
- Do not advertise a price that only applies to one specific vehicle type without disclosing the restriction
- Fleet "guaranteed rate" language: use "estimated rate" or "not-to-exceed estimate" unless you're truly guaranteeing it in writing

**Fleet Contract:**
- "Priority scheduling" = define priority (same-day / next-day / within 48 hours)
- "Guaranteed turnaround" = risky without built-in exceptions for parts availability and sublet work
- Net-30 payment terms = requires written agreement; verbal net-30 is unenforceable in most states
