## Description: <br>
Use when writing or polishing Chinese business emails: drafting from scratch, adjusting tone, structuring requests, follow-ups, apologies, proposals, and internal communications for Mandarin professional contexts. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Employees and external business communicators use this skill to draft, polish, structure, and adjust the tone of Chinese business emails for internal collaboration, customer communication, supplier coordination, follow-ups, apologies, proposals, and meeting-related messages. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Generated email content may include wording that is inaccurate, too forceful, or not aligned with the sender's business context. <br>
Mitigation: Review and edit generated messages before sending, especially names, dates, commitments, deadlines, and tone. <br>
Risk: Prompts used to draft business email may contain confidential or sensitive business information. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid including passwords, credentials, or unnecessary confidential business information in prompts. <br>
