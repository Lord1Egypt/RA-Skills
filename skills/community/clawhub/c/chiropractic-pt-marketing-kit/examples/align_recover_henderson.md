**H2: Why Getting Evaluated Quickly Matters**
Soft tissue injuries — the kind most common in rear-end and side-impact crashes — don't always show up on standard ER X-rays, which are designed to rule out fractures, not soft tissue damage. Many patients don't notice the full extent of their injuries until 24-72 hours after a crash, once the adrenaline wears off.

Documentation from an early evaluation can support your insurance claim and your attorney's case file. It establishes a medical record that shows your injury timeline — which is often critical in contested claims.

*We recommend getting evaluated by a musculoskeletal specialist (chiropractor, physical therapist, or orthopedist) within 72 hours of an accident, regardless of how you feel immediately after.*

### Monthly Patient Newsletter — Health Tip Section (May Edition)

**Health Tip: Staying Active in Henderson's Heat**

May marks the beginning of outdoor exercise season in the Las Vegas valley — and also the beginning of the season when we see the most heat-related musculoskeletal strain in our clinic.

When temperatures rise above 90°F, your muscles fatigue faster and recover slower. Dehydration reduces the synovial fluid cushioning your joints by as much as 15-20%, according to research on hydration and joint function. That's not an invitation to stop moving — it's an invitation to adjust.

Three things we recommend for active patients this month:
1. **Shift your timing.** Move workouts before 8AM or after 7PM when possible. Morning heat in Henderson is significantly lower than afternoon highs.
2. **Drink before you're thirsty.** Thirst is a late-stage dehydration signal. For a 60-minute outdoor workout, aim for 16-20 oz of water before you start and 8 oz every 20 minutes.
3. **Add a recovery day.** Heat increases recovery time. If you're training 5 days/week in winter, consider dropping to 4 in peak summer months and adding one active recovery day (light walking, swimming, or stretching).

*This is general wellness information. Always consult your provider for guidance specific to your health history.*
