## Description: <br>
Ai Ethnobotany helps agents answer educational questions about ethnobotany, traditional plant uses, medicinal and food plants, ethnopharmacology, field methods, biocultural diversity, and access-and-benefit-sharing policy. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users and agents use this skill for educational ethnobotany guidance, including traditional plant knowledge, plant-derived medicines, cultural landscapes, field research methods, and policy concepts such as CBD and the Nagoya Protocol. It is not a substitute for medical, legal, dosing, extraction, cultivation, or consumption advice. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Psychoactive plant discussion could be mistaken for practical consumption, dosing, extraction, cultivation, self-treatment, or legal guidance. <br>
Mitigation: Keep responses educational, avoid actionable instructions in those areas, and direct users to qualified medical or legal professionals where relevant. <br>
Risk: Traditional medicinal plant content can be misunderstood as validated clinical advice. <br>
Mitigation: Frame medicinal examples as educational context and avoid recommending diagnosis, treatment, dosage, or substitution for professional care. <br>
