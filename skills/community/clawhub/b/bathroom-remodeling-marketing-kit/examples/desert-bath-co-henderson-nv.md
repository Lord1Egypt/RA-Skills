**H2: Designed for a Lifetime — ANSI A117.1 Voluntary Accessibility Standards**
Your bathroom should work for you for the next 30 years, not just today. Desert Bath Co. designs aging-in-place bathroom upgrades to ANSI A117.1 voluntary accessibility guidelines — the recognized standard for residential accessibility design. Not ADA (which governs commercial facilities only) — ANSI A117.1: designed for real residential use, tested to real load capacities, built to last.

**H2: What We Install**
- **Curbless shower entry** — no lip, no step, no trip hazard; designed for wheelchair access or walker use
- **Grab bars** — rated to 250 lb capacity per ANSI A117.1 §609.8; anchored to wall studs or blocking (never drywall anchors)
- **Comfort-height toilet** — 17–19" seat height (standard is 15"); dramatically reduces knee and hip strain
- **Widened doorway** — 36" clear opening (standard is 32"); required for wheelchair clearance; structural feasibility assessed; load-bearing wall coordination with PE if applicable
- **Non-slip floor tile** — DCOF ≥ 0.42 (ANSI A326.3 wet-area standard); slip-resistance tested, not just claimed
- **Handheld showerhead** — 6-foot hose; adjustable height bar; ADA 2010 §608.6 reference height (available for seated use)
- **Blocking in walls** — future grab bar installation blocking installed during demo phase; documented location in project file

**H2: What About ADA Compliance?**
ADA Title III applies to commercial facilities and places of public accommodation — offices, restaurants, hotels, retail. It does not apply to single-family homes. The Fair Housing Act requires accessibility features in new construction of 4+ unit multifamily buildings — not in single-family or small-unit residential renovation.

For your home, the relevant standard is ANSI A117.1 — the voluntary residential accessibility guideline. We design to these specifications because they represent best practice for lifetime usability, not because your home is legally required to comply. We will not tell you your bathroom "meets ADA" — because that claim would be wrong and potentially misleading. We will tell you it's designed for safe, accessible use for decades.
