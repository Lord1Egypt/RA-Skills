# Verdict Rubric

The verdict is **only** about causal impersonation. Side findings never alter it.

---

## Verdicts

**Causally aligned**
Main-line purpose-critical claims rest on A/B evidence. At least 3–5 high-risk patterns were checked and found clean, with named inspection sites. The Calibration Skeptic's "premature dismissal" branch has been addressed.

**Mostly aligned, minor gaps**
Main causal chain is authentic. Some non-critical claims at C/D, none implicating the patterns most relevant to the artifact type.

**Suspected impersonation**
At least one purpose-critical claim at C/D with a named high-risk position from `causal-impersonation-patterns.md`, but no E-level contradiction yet.

**Confirmed impersonation**
≥1 E-level evidence against a purpose-critical claim, *or* ≥2 purpose-critical claims at C-level plus a concrete failure scenario from Step 6.

**Undecidable**
Material required to judge the most pattern-relevant positions is unavailable. Must name the missing material and the minimal action to obtain it.

---

## Required attachments to every verdict

- Confidence in [0, 1].
- Symmetric pre-mortem result: the most likely way this verdict is wrong, in both directions.
- For "Causally aligned": the list of checked patterns and inspection sites.
- For "Confirmed" or "Suspected": the matched patterns with inspection sites and expected/actual chain trios.

---

## What is forbidden

- Issuing "Confirmed impersonation" without an E-level contradiction or the C-level + scenario combination.
- Issuing "Causally aligned" without listing checked positions.
- Letting side findings change the verdict tier.
- Treating absence of evidence as evidence of impersonation.
- Treating absence of evidence as evidence of authenticity.
