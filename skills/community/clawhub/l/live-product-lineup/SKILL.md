# Live Product Lineup

Plan the optimal product reveal order for TikTok Live sessions based on price anchoring, audience warmup, and peak engagement timing. This skill turns a flat product list into a sequenced live show script that maximizes audience retention, conversion per viewer, and average order value throughout a session.

## Quick Reference

| Decision | Strong | Acceptable | Weak |
|----------|--------|------------|------|
| Opening product | High visual appeal, low price (<$30), fast close | Mid-price, strong hook | High-ticket first |
| Price arc | Low ‚Üí Mid ‚Üí High ‚Üí Flash deal | Mostly consistent range | Random / descending |
| Session length | 60‚Äì90 min with 3 act structure | 30‚Äì60 min with 2 acts | <20 min or >2 hrs unplanned |
| Scarcity mechanic | Real limited stock with live counter | "Limited time" with countdown | No scarcity signals |
| Re-engagement hook | Teaser of next hero product every 15 min | Periodic teaser | No forward hooks |
| Transition pacing | 5‚Äì7 min per product | 8‚Äì10 min per product | <3 min (rushed) or >15 min (drops) |
| Closing product | High-AOV bundle or exclusive collab | Best-seller at discount | Weakest product last |

## Solves

1. **Viewer drop-off in the first 10 minutes** ‚Äî Opening with a high-ticket or boring product loses the audience before the hook is set; the fix is a fast, visual, low-cost opener
2. **Low GMV despite high viewership** ‚Äî Audience watching but not buying; usually caused by misaligned price points (too expensive for the audience warmth stage) or weak scarcity mechanics
3. **Flat session energy** ‚Äî No arc, no peaks, no surprises; planned emotional beats and "host excitement" cues create natural momentum
4. **Early exits before the best products** ‚Äî Without forward hooks ("stay for the mystery bundle at the 45-minute mark"), viewers leave before the high-margin products appear
5. **Low average order value** ‚Äî Products shown in isolation without bundle suggestions miss the cross-sell window that live commerce enables
6. **Creator fatigue and dead air** ‚Äî Unplanned transitions between products create awkward pauses; a tight script with segue lines eliminates dead air
7. **Inventory mismatch** ‚Äî Running out of the opening product kills momentum; stock calculations must be built into the lineup plan

## Workflow

### Step 1 ‚Äî Gather Inputs
Collect from the seller/creator:
- Full product list with prices, available stock quantities, and any existing sales data
- Session target duration (30 min / 60 min / 90 min / 2 hrs)
- Target audience: new viewers (discovery) vs. existing followers (loyalty)
- Platform: TikTok Live, Shopee Live, YouTube Shopping, Instagram Live
- Any confirmed flash deal or hero product the brand wants to feature
- Past session replay data: when viewership peaked and dropped

### Step 2 ‚Äî Classify Products by Role

| Role | Criteria | Position |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Hook product** | <$30, high visual appeal, satisfying reveal, fast demo | First 5 min |
| **Warm-up products** | $20‚Äì$60, easy demo, broad appeal, clear benefit | Min 5‚Äì25 |
| **Hero product** | Best-seller or new launch; mid-high price; core GMV driver | Min 25‚Äì45 |
| **Price anchor** | Highest-priced item in lineup; makes hero seem reasonable | Before hero |
| **Flash deal** | Time-limited discount on popular product; creates urgency spike | Min 45‚Äì60 |
| **Closing bundle** | 2‚Äì3 products grouped at AOV-boosting price; "show exclusive" | Final 10 min |

### Step 3 ‚Äî Build the Price Arc
Map product prices on a timeline:
- **Act 1 (0‚Äì20 min):** $10‚Äì$40 ‚Äî audience warmup, easy yeses
- **Act 2 (20‚Äì55 min):** $30‚Äì$120 ‚Äî hero products, peak viewer count
- **Act 3 (55‚Äìend):** Flash deal ($15‚Äì$60 special) ‚Üí Closing bundle ($80‚Äì$200)

The price arc should look like a wave, not a flat line or random scatter.

### Step 4 ‚Äî Write the Transition Script

For each product, write:
1. **Intro hook** (10 sec) ‚Äî Creates anticipation before reveal
2. **Demo/show** (3‚Äì4 min) ‚Äî Live demonstration, UGC clip playback, or feature walk-through
3. **Close mechanic** (60 sec) ‚Äî "We only have 47 left, add to cart now, I'll count down from 10"
4. **Forward hook** (15 sec) ‚Äî Teases the next product to hold viewers

Template:
```
[HOOK]: "Okay chat, you're not ready for what I'm about to show you..."
[DEMO]: [Product features + live demo + comment reading]
[CLOSE]: "Cart link is pinned ‚Äî we have [STOCK COUNT] left. First [X] orders get [BONUS]."
[FORWARD HOOK]: "Don't leave yet ‚Äî in about 10 minutes I'm dropping something I've literally never shown on live before."
```

### Step 5 ‚Äî Stock Allocation Planning
For each lineup slot, calculate safe stock allocation:
- **Hook product:** 30‚Äì50 units (fast close, high conversion rate expected)
- **Warm-up products:** 20‚Äì40 units each
- **Hero product:** 50‚Äì100 units (main GMV driver; ensure no stockout)
- **Flash deal:** 20‚Äì30 units (scarcity is real; counter reinforces it)
- **Reserve:** 10% buffer per product for re-show if energy spikes

### Step 6 ‚Äî Engagement Mechanics Schedule
Insert engagement beats every 10‚Äì12 minutes:
- **Comment prompt:** "Tell me in the comments: have you tried [product category] before?"
- **Poll/quiz:** "Which color do you want me to show next ‚Äî pink or blue?"
- **Countdown close:** "Pinning the link now ‚Äî 47 left, adding to cart takes 10 seconds"
- **Giveaway tease:** "The 500th person to share this live gets a free [hook product]"

## Common Mistakes

1. **Opening with the most expensive product** ‚Äî High-ticket items before trust is established lose cold viewers immediately
2. **No price anchor before hero product** ‚Äî Without showing a $150 item first, the $75 hero feels expensive; anchor high before presenting the value
3. **Forgetting to tease upcoming products** ‚Äî Viewers who don't know what's coming have no reason to stay
4. **Real inventory not counted before going live** ‚Äî Claiming "only 20 left" when there are 200 destroys trust when buyers check later; always verify stock
