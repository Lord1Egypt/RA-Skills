# Campaign Calendar Quality Checklist

Use this checklist to validate a completed campaign calendar before execution begins. Every item should be confirmed. Items marked as critical must pass without exception; items marked as recommended are strongly advised but may be acceptable to skip with documented justification.

---

## 1. Date Mapping and Event Coverage (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** All major platform events for the planning period are included with confirmed or tentative dates and sources cited (e.g., TikTok Shop Seller Center announcement, historical pattern).
- [ ] **[Critical]** Seasonal and cultural peaks relevant to the seller's category are mapped (e.g., back-to-school for apparel, holiday gifting for beauty, summer start for outdoor gear).
- [ ] **[Critical]** Brand-specific dates (product launches, restocks, anniversaries) are plotted on the same timeline as external events.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Tentative dates are clearly marked as tentative with a confirmation deadline set at least 3 weeks before the event.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** The calendar includes at least 8 actionable dates per quarter (fewer suggests gaps in research; significantly more suggests insufficient prioritization).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Supply chain dates that affect campaign execution are noted (factory closures, shipping cutoffs, inventory arrival dates).

---

## 2. Event Prioritization (5 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Every mapped event is assigned a tier (1, 2, or 3) based on explicit revenue potential and resource requirement scoring.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Budget allocation percentages across tiers sum to 100% and include a contingency reserve of at least 5%.
- [ ] **[Critical]** No single campaign exceeds 40% of total period budget unless it is a documented once-per-year tentpole event (e.g., Black Friday).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Tier assignments are justified with at least one data point each (historical sales data, platform traffic estimates, or category benchmarks).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** The prioritization was reviewed against the previous period's actual performance data to check for biases (e.g., consistently overrating brand-specific events vs. platform events).

---

## 3. Campaign Design and Phasing (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Every Tier 1 and Tier 2 campaign has all four phases defined (pre-heat, launch, sustain, wind-down) with specific start and end dates for each phase.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Pre-heat phases for Tier 1 campaigns begin at least 10 days before the launch date. Tier 2 pre-heat begins at least 5 days before.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Phase transition criteria are documented for each Tier 1 campaign (what conditions must be met to move from pre-heat to launch, launch to sustain, etc.).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** No two campaigns have overlapping launch phases. If overlap exists, it is acknowledged with an explicit rationale.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Each campaign brief includes defined success metrics (revenue target, ROAS target, conversion rate benchmark) for at least the launch phase.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Tier 3 events use the abbreviated framework (launch + wind-down only) and have upgrade criteria documented in case early performance warrants scaling up.

---

## 4. Content Planning (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Every campaign phase has specific content assets assigned with defined content types (video, graphic, email, livestream), quantities, and publish dates.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Production deadlines work backward from publish dates with realistic lead times: 5-7 days for short-form video, 10-14 days for creator/affiliate content, 3-5 days for livestream prep, 2-3 days for graphics, 3-4 days for email.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Each content asset has an assigned owner (person or team responsible for production).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Content format diversity exists within each campaign -- no phase relies on a single content type. At minimum, each Tier 1 launch phase includes video, static, and direct messaging (email/SMS) content.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Backup content plans are identified for each Tier 1 campaign (e.g., "if creator content is delayed, repurpose top 3 evergreen videos with updated promotional overlays").
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Creator/affiliate briefing materials are scheduled to be delivered at least 10 business days before the content publish date, accounting for shipping, production, and review time.

---

## 5. Budget Allocation (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Total budget is allocated across campaigns, phases, and channels with specific dollar amounts (not just percentages).
- [ ] **[Critical]** Phase-level budget allocation follows the recommended distribution: pre-heat 15-20%, launch 40-50%, sustain 20-25%, wind-down 5-10%. Deviations are justified.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Channel-level allocation includes daily spend caps or minimum daily spend floors to maintain ad platform delivery stability.
- [ ] **[Critical]** A contingency reserve of at least 5% of total budget exists with documented deployment rules (when and how the reserve is released).
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Budget allocation references historical ROAS data by channel. If no historical data exists, starting allocations are documented as assumptions to be validated.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Break-even ROAS is calculated for each channel and documented alongside target ROAS so that pivot triggers can be set against meaningful thresholds.

---

## 6. Promotions and Margin Protection (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Every promotion is documented with discount type, applicable SKUs, start/end dates, and stacking rules.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Maximum discount stack depth is calculated for every promoted SKU, accounting for all simultaneous promotions (shop discount + platform coupon + bundle deal + any other active offer).
- [ ] **[Critical]** A minimum gross margin floor is set and every SKU's effective price at maximum discount depth is validated against that floor. No SKU falls below the margin floor at maximum stack.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Promotion start and end times are documented precisely (date and time, not just date) to prevent unintended overlap between consecutive campaigns.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Flash deal or limited-time promotions have dedicated inventory allocations to prevent overselling.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** A pricing spot-check plan is included: verify the first 5-10 orders of each promotion to confirm effective prices match expectations before scaling ad spend.

---

## 7. Review Cadence and Adaptability (6 items)

- [ ] **[Critical]** Weekly review meetings are scheduled with specific dates on the calendar for the entire planning period.
- [ ] **[Critical]** At least one mid-campaign checkpoint is scheduled for every Tier 1 campaign, timed at or before the midpoint of the campaign duration.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Post-campaign reviews are scheduled within 3 business days of each Tier 1 and Tier 2 campaign's wind-down phase completion.
- [ ] **[Critical]** Predefined pivot triggers are documented with specific numeric thresholds and prescribed actions (e.g., "if ROAS below 1.5 for 3 days, pause ad set and reallocate to top performer").
- [ ] **[Recommended]** Weekly review agendas include a standard set of metrics to evaluate: spend pacing vs. plan, ROAS by campaign and channel, content publish rate vs. schedule, and top/bottom performer identification.
- [ ] **[Recommended]** The calendar includes a mechanism for incorporating learnings from earlier campaigns into later ones within the same planning period (e.g., post-BFCM review feeds directly into Holiday Mega Sale adjustments).
