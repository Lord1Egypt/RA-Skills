### Auto Refinishing at [Shop Name]

Automotive refinishing is a precision process — and a regulated one. [Shop Name] operates an OSHA 29 CFR 1910.94-compliant spray booth with proper ventilation, airflow rates, and VOC-compliant coating systems.

**What "compliant spray booth" actually means:**
Modern automotive clearcoats and primers use isocyanate chemistry (HDI — hexamethylene diisocyanate) as crosslinkers. These materials require supplied-air respiratory protection for application technicians, proper booth ventilation per OSHA standards, and documented hazard communication per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200.

We maintain these standards. Our technicians are trained and equipped appropriately. When we say "professional refinishing," we mean operations that comply with the standards that protect both our team and our customers.

## SERVICE PAGE 3 — Paintless Dent Repair (PDR)

**Title:** Paintless Dent Repair (PDR) in [City] | Hail Damage | [Shop Name]
**Meta description:** PDR (paintless dent repair) in [City] for hail damage, door dings, and minor collision dents. PDR appropriate where paint is intact and metal is not creased or edged.

**Page content:**

### Paintless Dent Repair at [Shop Name]

PDR removes dents by massaging the metal from behind the panel — no refinishing, no filler, no paint. This is the appropriate repair for hail damage, parking lot door dings, and minor collision dents where the factory paint is undamaged and the metal hasn't creased.

**When PDR is the right choice:**
- Hail dents on flat panels with intact paint
- Door dings on flat body sections without paint cracking
- Minor round dents on hoods, roofs, trunk lids away from panel edges

**When PDR is NOT the right choice:**
- Dents with paint cracking, chipping, or flaking
- Metal creases or sharp-impact points
- Dents near panel edges, body lines, or door jambs
- Dents on aluminum panels (different technique — not all PDR technicians are trained on aluminum)

We'll tell you honestly which applies to your vehicle. PDR estimate provided in writing. If PDR isn't appropriate, we'll quote traditional refinishing repair separately — you decide.

## SERVICE PAGE 4 — Frame & Structural Repair

**Title:** Frame & Structural Repair in [City] | Computerized Measuring | [Shop Name]
**Meta description:** Frame and structural collision repair in [City]. Computerized measuring system. ADAS recalibration. Written estimate before work begins.

**Page content:**

### Frame & Structural Repair at [Shop Name]

Structural damage from a collision affects vehicle safety — not just appearance. [Shop Name] uses computerized measuring systems to document pre- and post-repair structural alignment to manufacturer specifications.

**What we measure:**
- Frame/unibody dimensional measurement (datum plane, section measurements, diagonal measurements)
- Strut tower alignment
- Subframe and cradle positioning
- Documentation of pre-repair vs. post-repair measurements — provided to you

**ADAS recalibration after structural repair:**
Modern vehicles integrate ADAS sensors (forward collision, lane departure, blind spot monitoring, adaptive cruise) into structural panels, bumpers, and windshields. Any structural repair involving these panels or adjacent components requires sensor recalibration to manufacturer specification. We coordinate recalibration as part of complete structural repair.

**What this means for insurance claims:**
Complete structural repair documentation — measuring reports, parts records, ADAS recalibration records — supports accurate insurance claims and protects you from future disputes about pre-existing condition.

**A note on title history:**
If you bring us a vehicle with a salvage, rebuilt, or branded title for restoration, we'll help you restore it to the best standard possible. We'll also document the title status accurately. A beautifully restored vehicle with a rebuilt title is a beautiful vehicle — but its title history doesn't change. Our documentation reflects reality, and our marketing language for restoration results respects Nevada NRS 482.501 branded title disclosure requirements.

If you're selling the vehicle after restoration: buyers in Nevada are entitled to know the title history. "Fully restored" copy that implies a clean title when the vehicle has a branded title creates consumer fraud liability under NRS 598. We can help you market the restoration quality honestly alongside accurate title disclosure.

## FAQ (12 Questions) + FAQPage JSON-LD

**Q1:** Do you provide written estimates before starting work?
**A1:** Yes. We provide a written estimate and get your authorization before any work begins. We don't start repairs until you've approved the estimate in writing.

**Q2:** Do you use OEM parts?
**A2:** We use OEM, certified aftermarket, and LKQ recycled parts depending on availability, your insurance coverage, and your preference. The parts type used is disclosed on your repair order before we order. If you specifically require OEM, let us know when you bring the vehicle in.

**Q3:** What does "I-CAR Gold Class" mean?
**A3:** I-CAR Gold Class means our shop maintains ongoing annual training for all production staff in applicable I-CAR training roles. It's not a one-time certification — we maintain it by completing required training hours each year. [Include current year if applicable.]

**Q4:** What does "ASE-certified" mean for technicians vs. shops?
**A4:** ASE certifications are held by individual technicians, not shops. When we say our technicians are ASE-certified, we mean specific named technicians hold specific ASE collision and refinishing designations (B2 Painting & Refinishing, B3 Non-Structural, B4 Structural, B5 Mechanical/Electrical, B6 Damage Analysis). [List techs and designations if applicable.]

**Q5:** Do you offer a lifetime guarantee?
**A5:** [If yes:] We offer a written warranty on our paint and body work. The warranty covers [duration], [what's covered], and excludes [specific exclusions — impact damage, rust on different panels, modifications, etc.]. Warranty terms are provided in writing before delivery. [If no:] We provide a written warranty with specific terms — ask at estimate time for complete warranty disclosure.

**Q6:** Can you waive my deductible?
**A6:** No. Waiving insurance deductibles is not a practice we offer — in many contexts, it creates insurance fraud exposure. We provide complete, documented repairs and accurate supplement billing.

**Q7:** Do you work with all insurance companies?
**A7:** We work with all major insurers. We can document your damage for your claim, provide the supplement for review, and coordinate directly with adjusters. We are not exclusively a direct repair program shop — you have the right to choose your own collision repair facility regardless of what your insurer recommends.

**Q8:** Do you handle ADAS recalibration after repairs?
**A8:** Yes. Any repair involving bumper covers, windshields, or rear panel sections on ADAS-equipped vehicles may require sensor recalibration. We coordinate recalibration as part of complete collision repair and document it in your repair records.

**Q9:** What does "OSHA-compliant spray booth" mean?
**A9:** Our spray booth meets OSHA 29 CFR 1910.94 requirements for ventilation, airflow, and fire safety. Modern automotive clearcoats contain isocyanate compounds that require proper ventilation and respiratory protection during application. Our technicians use appropriate respiratory protection — this is an occupational safety standard, not a marketing claim.

**Q10:** Is your shop EPA-certified?
**A10:** Auto body shops are regulated as hazardous waste generators under EPA RCRA for spent solvents, paint wastes, and used oil. We maintain generator status documentation and proper waste disposal through a licensed transporter. EPA certification as a generic marketing phrase doesn't have a defined meaning — our compliance is documented through RCRA generator records.

**Q11:** What do you do with old paint and solvents?
**A11:** Spent solvents, paint wastes, and used oil are disposed of through a licensed hazardous waste transporter under EPA RCRA regulations. Disposal manifests are maintained as required by our generator status.

**Q12:** Do you restore salvage or rebuilt title vehicles?
**A12:** Yes. We perform bodywork and refinishing on salvage and rebuilt title vehicles. The vehicle's title history doesn't change through restoration — a rebuilt title vehicle remains a rebuilt title vehicle. We document accurately and advise on proper disclosure if you plan to sell after restoration.
