# Product Role Classification Guide

## Role Definitions and Selection Criteria

### Hook Product
The first thing shown on live. Must:
- Be visually satisfying to demo (color reveals, texture, transformation, unboxing)
- Price under $30 (low commitment = easy first purchase)
- Demo in under 2 minutes
- Generate immediate comments ("omg", "need this", color questions)

**Category examples:** lip gloss, phone case, snack/food sample, small kitchen gadget, beauty sponge, hair accessory

### Warmup Product
Products shown in minutes 5–25 while audience grows. Must:
- Have broad appeal across the viewer demographic
- Price $20–$60
- Natural connection to hook product (beauty → beauty, home → home)
- Easy to explain benefits without extensive backstory

**Warning:** Don't show your best products here — you're still building the room

### Price Anchor
Shown briefly before the hero product without a hard sell. Purpose is to make the hero feel like a bargain.

**How to present:** "Before I show you the deal of tonight — this is the full version at $[HIGH PRICE]. I'm not selling this tonight. What I AM selling is..."

Price anchor should be 30–50% more expensive than the hero product.

### Hero Product
The main revenue driver of the session. Plan:
- Show during peak viewer window (use analytics from past sessions)
- Have the most demo content prepared (before/after, UGC clips, live application)
- Allocate most stock here
- Have a genuine scarcity mechanic (real limited quantity, session-only price, or exclusive color)

**Session hero examples:** bestselling product, new launch product, seasonal hero, collab product

### Flash Deal
Creates an urgency spike in the second half when viewership naturally dips. Rules:
- Real discount (not manufactured; buyers check)
- Clear countdown (literal count or visible timer)
- Limited quantity stated and tracked live
- Can be a past product bundled differently or a surprise reveal

### Closing Bundle
Maximizes AOV from the highest-intent viewers who stayed to the end. Structure:
- 2–3 complementary products bundled at a price unavailable outside of this session
- "Show exclusive" framing — "This bundle is only available right now while I'm live"
- Positions as the smart buyer's choice for people who want multiple items

## Matching Products to Audience Warmth

| Viewer state | Warmth level | Best product type |
|-------------|--------------|------------------|
| Just joined, no purchase history | Cold | Hook (<$30, visual, impulsive) |
| Watched 10+ min, commented | Warming | Warmup ($20–$60, benefit-clear) |
| Asked about price/shipping | Interested | Hero (full presentation with demo) |
| Added to cart once already | Hot | Upsell / bundle / flash deal |
| Commented "I bought!" | Converted | Cross-sell to complementary product |

## Platform-Specific Timing Notes

### TikTok Live
- Peak viewership typically arrives 15–25 min after start (algorithm ramp-up)
- Use the "pin comment" feature for cart links during close mechanics
- Gifts and diamonds from viewers signal to TikTok's algorithm to push to more users — acknowledge gift-givers to encourage more

### Shopee Live
- Shopee boosts sessions with high "Add to Cart" rates early — prioritize a fast first purchase
- Use Shopee's built-in voucher system for flash deals (more visible than verbal discount)
- Shopee buyers respond to free shipping + bundle deals more than pure price discount

### Instagram Live
- Instagram viewers skew higher income than TikTok — price anchor can be set higher
- Use "link in bio" or shopping tags — direct cart link not always available
- Go live when your story engagement is highest (check Instagram Insights)

### YouTube Shopping
- YouTube sessions run longer (90 min–3 hrs is common)
- Viewers research before buying — have spec sheets and comparison ready
- Chapters/timestamps in description help viewers navigate to products they're interested in
