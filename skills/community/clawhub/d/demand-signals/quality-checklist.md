# Demand Signal Analysis Quality Checklist

Use this checklist to validate the completeness and rigor of your demand signal analysis before committing capital to inventory. Work through each category sequentially. A thorough analysis should check all items as complete. Items marked incomplete identify gaps that increase sourcing risk.

---

## Category 1: Signal Collection Completeness

- [ ] Checked TikTok for creator adoption velocity and engagement patterns related to the product
- [ ] Checked Instagram Reels and/or YouTube Shorts for cross-platform content spread
- [ ] Checked Reddit or niche online communities for organic product discussions and recommendations
- [ ] Reviewed Pinterest Trends for save rates and search growth (especially for home, lifestyle, and apparel categories)
- [ ] Pulled Google Trends data with at least a 90-day window and compared to the prior year's same period
- [ ] Checked Google Ads Keyword Planner for absolute search volume estimates and CPC trends
- [ ] Reviewed Amazon Movers and Shakers, New Releases, or Best Sellers in the relevant category
- [ ] Checked AliExpress or 1688.com for supplier-side order volume trends
- [ ] Contacted at least one existing supplier or sourcing contact for anecdotal demand intelligence
- [ ] Documented at least 3 independent source categories with data for the signal

## Category 2: Validation Rigor

- [ ] Signal confirmed by at least 2 independent source categories (social, search, marketplace, supplier)
- [ ] Growth has been sustained for a minimum of 14 consecutive days (not a single-day or single-week spike)
- [ ] Plotted the trend curve and identified the pattern (hockey stick, linear climb, spike-and-plateau, or spike-and-decline)
- [ ] Assessed whether engagement represents purchasing intent (comments asking "where to buy") versus entertainment engagement
- [ ] Verified that transactional search terms (product + "buy," "price," "Amazon") are growing alongside general product terms
- [ ] Screened for manipulation: checked whether social engagement appears organic or driven by paid/coordinated activity
- [ ] Confirmed Amazon BSR improvements correspond to genuine sales activity, not giveaway campaigns or listing manipulation
- [ ] Compared current data to the same period in prior years to rule out seasonal recurrence being mistaken for a new trend

## Category 3: Demand Sizing Accuracy

- [ ] Used both Google Trends (relative) and Keyword Planner (absolute) data to model search demand
- [ ] Applied a realistic conversion rate assumption (1-5% for product keywords) rather than using raw search volume as a demand proxy
- [ ] Identified 2-3 comparable past products/trends and analyzed their peak volume, duration, and decline pattern
- [ ] Pulled estimated sales data from a paid tool (Jungle Scout, Helium 10, Keepa, or equivalent) for current top sellers in the category
- [ ] Created conservative, moderate, and optimistic scenarios rather than a single-point estimate
- [ ] Applied a decay assumption to growth rate projections beyond 30 days (not extrapolating current growth rate indefinitely)
- [ ] Defined the addressable market specific to your channel, geography, and price point (not total global demand)

## Category 4: Competition Assessment

- [ ] Counted the number of active listings on your target marketplace for this product
- [ ] Assessed the quality of existing sellers (optimized listings with A+ content and strong reviews vs. basic low-effort listings)
- [ ] Tracked the rate of new seller entry (how many new listings per week)
- [ ] Searched USPTO trademark database for relevant trademarks in the product category
- [ ] Checked for design patents or utility patents that could restrict sourcing options
- [ ] Investigated whether any major retail brand has signaled entry into this product category
- [ ] Mapped current price points from lowest to highest and calculated the average selling price
- [ ] Obtained supplier quotes from at least 3 suppliers and calculated landed cost per unit
- [ ] Calculated gross margin at your target selling price after all costs (product, shipping, duties, marketplace fees, estimated advertising, returns)

## Category 5: Timing and Logistics

- [ ] Assessed the current trend lifecycle phase (early emergence, rapid growth, approaching peak, post-peak)
- [ ] Estimated the number of weeks remaining in the commercially viable demand window
- [ ] Mapped the complete supply chain timeline from order placement to first unit available for sale
- [ ] Calculated the "order by" date -- the latest date an order can be placed and arrive during the viable demand phase
- [ ] Compared sea freight versus air freight timelines and cost implications
- [ ] Checked for seasonal shipping disruptions (Chinese New Year, Q4 freight congestion, peak season surcharges)
- [ ] Cross-referenced trend timing with marketplace events (Prime Day, Black Friday, Back to School) for amplification or conflict

## Category 6: Financial and Risk Controls

- [ ] Calculated the total capital at risk for the initial order (units x landed cost)
- [ ] Confirmed the initial order capital fits within your inventory budget allocation (not exceeding your risk tolerance for a single unproven product)
- [ ] Set the initial order quantity as a test batch (typically 100-500 units) rather than a full production run
- [ ] Defined a specific liquidation trigger with measurable criteria (e.g., "fewer than 3 units per day after 21 days live")
- [ ] Created a liquidation strategy (price reduction schedule, bundling options, alternative sales channels, wholesale disposal)
- [ ] Identified at least one pivot option if the primary market does not materialize (different marketplace, different use case, complementary product bundle)
- [ ] Set specific review dates with defined decision criteria for reorder, hold, or liquidation

## Category 7: Documentation and Decision Quality

- [ ] All raw data sources are documented with access dates so the analysis can be audited or updated
- [ ] Key assumptions are explicitly stated (growth rate projections, conversion rates, market share estimates)
- [ ] Known data gaps and limitations are acknowledged (e.g., "no supplier data available" or "only 10 days of search data")
- [ ] The go/no-go decision is based on the 5-dimension scoring framework (signal strength, demand size, competitive difficulty, timing, margins)
- [ ] No dimension received a "No-Go" score while the overall decision is "Go"
- [ ] The analysis has been completed within the past 7 days (older analyses require re-validation due to fast-moving market conditions)

---

## How to Use This Checklist

**Before placing an order:** Work through every item. Any unchecked item in Categories 1-4 represents a gap in your analysis that increases risk. Categories 5-7 ensure your execution plan is sound.

**Minimum acceptable completion:**
- All items in Category 2 (Validation Rigor) must be checked. Skipping validation steps is the most common cause of false-positive sourcing decisions.
- All items in Category 6 (Financial and Risk Controls) must be checked. Every order must have predefined exit criteria.
- At least 80% of items across all other categories should be checked. Document reasons for any items left incomplete.

**For team review:** Share the completed checklist along with the demand signal report. Unchecked items should be discussed explicitly -- the team should consciously accept the risk of proceeding with gaps or require the gaps to be filled before the order is placed.

**After each product launch:** Revisit the checklist from the original analysis and note which items, if completed more thoroughly, would have changed the outcome. Use this retrospective to improve future analyses.
