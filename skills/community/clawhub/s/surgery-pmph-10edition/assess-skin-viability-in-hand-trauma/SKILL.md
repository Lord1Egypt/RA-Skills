# Assess Skin Viability in Hand Trauma

Use this skill to systematically evaluate skin viability in acute hand injuries involving potential skin loss or detachment, such as avulsion injuries, crush injuries, or complex lacerations.

## Prerequisites
- Visible wound margins
- Ability to perform physical examination
- Absence of severe active bleeding that obscures assessment

## Constraints
- Do **not** use for skin that is already necrotic (blackened) or completely amputated

## Procedure

1. **Assess Color and Temperature**
   - Compare injured skin to surrounding healthy tissue.
   - **Viable**: Matches adjacent skin in color and warmth.
   - **Non-viable**: Appears pale, cyanotic, or feels cold.

2. **Perform Capillary Refill Test**
   - Press firmly on the skin until it blanches white.
   - Release pressure and time color return.
   - **Viable**: Returns to pink/red within ≤2 seconds.
   - **Non-viable**: Slow return (>2 seconds) or no return.

3. **Check Edge Bleeding**
   - Gently wipe or trim the skin edge with sterile gauze.
   - **Viable**: Shows punctate, bright red oozing.
   - **Non-viable**: No bleeding observed.

4. **Make Final Judgment**
   - **All three indicators normal** → Preserve skin.
   - **Any one abnormal** → Consider partial excision; proceed cautiously.
   - **Special note**: In crush injuries, skin may appear intact but have disrupted blood supply—always test, never rely on appearance alone.

> **Trigger context**: Use this skill whenever a hand injury presents with skin that is torn, peeled, or crushed but not obviously dead, especially when deciding between debridement and preservation.
