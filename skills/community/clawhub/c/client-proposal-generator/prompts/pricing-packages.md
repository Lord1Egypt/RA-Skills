# Prompt: Pricing & Packages

## Purpose
Generate a 3-tier pricing table that anchors high, makes the middle option feel like the obvious choice, and reduces sticker shock through comparison.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a pricing strategist and proposal writer creating the investment section of a client proposal.

SERVICE DETAILS:
- Service type: [WHAT_YOU_DO]
- Your target price for this project: $[TARGET_PRICE]
- Client's industry/company size: [INDUSTRY + SMALL/MID/LARGE]
- Primary deliverable: [CORE_THING_CLIENT_WANTS]

Create a 3-tier pricing table for a professional proposal using price anchoring psychology:

**TIER 1 — STARTER** (price at ~60% of target)
- Core deliverable only
- Minimal support/revisions
- Longer timeline
- Good for: budget-conscious clients or small initial scope

**TIER 2 — PROFESSIONAL** (price at target — mark as "Most Popular" ⭐)
- Full scope of work
- Standard revisions and support
- Normal timeline
- Everything most clients actually need

**TIER 3 — PREMIUM** (price at ~180% of target)
- Everything in Professional
- Priority turnaround (2x faster)
- Extra deliverables or extended support
- Ongoing retainer option or maintenance included
- Good for: clients who want white-glove service

After the table, add:
1. A 2-sentence paragraph recommending Tier 2 for their specific situation
2. A "What's Included in Every Package" section (3–4 universal items: kickoff call, dedicated communication, professional delivery, etc.)
3. A one-sentence note that custom quotes are available for larger projects

Format: Use a clean markdown table for the tiers. Make it skimmable.
Tone: Confident. Frame as "investment" not "cost."
```

---

## Example Inputs

- Service: Shopify store redesign
- Target price: $4,500
- Client: Mid-size outdoor gear e-commerce brand
- Primary deliverable: New storefront with conversion-optimized UX

---

## Tips

- The 3-tier model works because the expensive tier makes the middle look reasonable
- Never lead with your lowest price — anchor high, then let them "choose down"
- The "Most Popular" label on Tier 2 is psychological validation — always include it
- If the client pushed back on price in discovery, mention flexible payment schedules in this section
