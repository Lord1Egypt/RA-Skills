# 04 Risk And Positioning

## Core Risk Posture

The account reads as risk-aware, not risk-obsessed. The pattern is to take aggressive exposure only in names he respects, while keeping some ballast and avoiding all-in behavior.

## Evidence

### 1. Concentrated, but not single-name all-in

- Direct post:
  - concentrated investing means focusing on a small group of quality names
  - he explicitly says he will not `allin` Tesla
  - he pairs Tesla with semiconductors and steadier assets like Apple and BRKB

### 2. Safety language is repeated

- Direct short post:
  - small steps
  - better to earn less than to have major problems
  - safety is the top priority
  - the portfolio should contain both aggressive and stable components

### 3. Patience is part of risk control

- Repeated lines:
  - be friends with time
  - ignore noise
  - no need to play when there is no opportunity
- This implies overtrading is itself treated as a risk source.

### 4. Options are scaled and trimmed

- Berkshire post:
  - add `long call` in stages
- Apple and multi-name summary:
  - exits are staged rather than all-at-once
- This reduces timing risk without needing a hyper-precise entry.

## Inferred Positioning Rules

These are inferences drawn from repeated evidence, not explicit written rules:

- The underlying stock likely carries the heavier, more durable exposure.
- `Long call` acts as amplified conviction rather than full-account replacement.
- Ballast names exist to absorb uncertainty and preserve optionality.
- He is willing to rotate when a better opportunity appears, so capital is not emotionally locked.

## Missing Mechanics

The evidence does **not** support strong claims about:

- maximum portfolio concentration by percentage
- maximum options allocation by percentage
- explicit stop-loss bands
- delta targets
- roll timing
- hedge overlays

## Skill Boundary

The final skill can say:

- do not go all-in
- keep aggressive and stable components together
- stage entries and exits
- use patience as part of risk control

The final skill should not say:

- “he always caps option exposure at X%”
- “he rolls at Y DTE”
- “he uses strict delta or IV thresholds”
