## Prompt

You are a lifecycle email strategist specializing in converting free and beta users into paying customers. Write a complete early-access email sequence that activates users, demonstrates value, and converts them to paid plans.

**Product details:**
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- What it does: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
- Target customer: [WHO THEY ARE]
- First win (what should a user accomplish in their first session?): [SPECIFIC ACTION — e.g., "connect their first integration," "generate their first output," "invite their first team member"]
- Core paid feature (what does free/beta NOT include?): [DESCRIBE THE UPGRADE TRIGGER]
- Early-access pricing vs. full price: [e.g., "$19/month now vs. $49/month at launch" — or "same price"]
- Early-access deadline: [DATE OR TIMEFRAME — e.g., "90-day beta period ends"]
- Tone: [professional / conversational / startup-casual — pick one]

---

**Write the following emails:**

### Email 1 — Day 0: Welcome + Setup (Sent immediately on signup/access grant)

Goal: Get them to complete their first win within 24 hours.

Include:
- Welcome headline that names the outcome they're here for (not a generic "Welcome to [product]!")
- What they have access to (3 bullet points max — don't overwhelm)
- First win checklist: 3 steps to their first result, numbered, each under 10 words
- One "if you get stuck" resource (docs link, video, or direct reply invitation)
- Subject line: 3 variants (outcome, curiosity, instruction)
- Preview text
- CTA button: "Get started" alternatives — 3 options
- P.S.: Something human (founder note, what they can expect from you in the coming weeks)

---

### Email 2 — Day 2: Quick Win Tip (Habit-forming use case)

Goal: Show them one non-obvious way to get 10x more value — creates a habit loop.

Include:
- Opens with a specific scenario: "Here's what [type of user] does in their first week with [product]..."
- Describes one "power move" or workflow trick that most users miss
- Social proof angle: "This is the thing beta users said they wished they'd known sooner"
- Ends with a question that invites a reply (engagement signal for deliverability + feedback)
- Subject line: 3 variants
- Preview text
- Length: 150-200 words

---

### Email 3 — Day 7: Power User Secret (Upgrade positioning)

Goal: Demonstrate that there's a higher gear — makes the upgrade feel like unlocking something, not just buying more.

Include:
- Opens with: "You've been using [product] for a week. Here's what power users do differently."
- Describes an advanced workflow or use case that's only possible with paid features
- Does NOT say "upgrade now" — just shows the capability and lets them want it
- Ends with: "When you're ready to unlock [specific feature], here's the link." (soft CTA)
- Subject line: 3 variants
- Preview text
- Length: 150-200 words

---

### Email 4 — Day 14: Upgrade Ask (3 Variants)

Goal: Convert users who have seen value. This is the first direct ask.

Write 3 full emails — same structure, different psychological angle:

**Variant A — Outcome Frame**
"What would you do with [specific benefit at 10x capacity]?" Opens with a vision of the future-state, presents the upgrade as the path to get there.

**Variant B — Social Proof Frame**
"Here's what paying users are doing with [product] right now." Testimonial or beta result leads. Shows what's possible when users fully commit. Ends with: "Ready to join them?"

**Variant C — Urgency Frame**
"Your early-access pricing expires in [X] days." Quantifies what they're saving. Creates honest urgency without fake scarcity. Includes a savings calculation: "After [date], this plan is $[X]/month. Lock it in now at $[Y]."

For each variant:
- Subject line (3 options)
- Preview text
- Full email body (150-200 words)
- CTA button text (3 options)
- P.S. that handles the #1 objection for that frame

---

### Email 5 — Day 30: Anniversary + Testimonial Request + Referral Ask

Goal: Celebrate, collect social proof, and multiply via word-of-mouth.

Include:
- Opening: "You've been using [product] for a month." (simple, human, acknowledges the milestone)
- Achievement summary: 1-2 sentences about what they've likely accomplished if they've been active
- Testimonial request: 3 specific questions they can answer in 2-3 sentences:
  1. "What were you struggling with before [product]?"
  2. "What result have you gotten that surprised you?"
  3. "Who else should be using [product]?"
- Reply CTA: "Hit reply and share your experience — I read every one."
- Referral ask: 1 sentence with a link or referral mechanism (even simple: "Forward this to one person who needs [outcome]")
- Subject line: 3 variants
- Length: 200-250 words

---

### Email 6 — Churn Rescue: Re-engagement Sequence (2 emails for users who went dark after Day 7)

**Re-engagement Email 1 (Sent Day 14 if no activity since Day 7):**
- Subject line: "Did I lose you?" or 2 alternatives
- 100-word body: acknowledges they went quiet, removes friction (no guilt), offers help
- Single CTA: return link OR a "what's stopping you?" reply option

**Re-engagement Email 2 (Sent Day 21 if still no activity):**
- Subject line: "Last email from me about [product]" or 2 alternatives
- 80-word body: the "break-up email" — honest, gives them an easy out, ends with a clean CTA
- No pressure, just one last offer
- P.S.: "If you've moved on, no hard feelings — but if you want to see what you've been missing, here's [specific thing they haven't tried]"

## Sequence Strategy Notes

After writing all emails, add a **Setup Guide** section:

- **Trigger setup:** How to tag/segment users who complete vs. don't complete the first win
- **Branch logic:** What happens if someone upgrades before Day 14 (suppress remaining emails or shift to onboarding track)
- **Reply monitoring:** Which emails to watch for replies and what to do with them
- **Open rate benchmarks:** What's healthy for each email (Day 0: 60%+, Day 7: 40%+, Day 14: 30%+)
- **Platform setup notes:** Recommended sequence configuration for ConvertKit, Mailchimp, and ActiveCampaign
