**What Nevada law requires of licensed addiction treatment programs**

All addiction treatment facilities operating in Nevada must hold an active facility license issued by the Nevada Division of Public and Behavioral Health (DPBH) under Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 449. Different license levels correspond to different services:

- **Medical Detoxification (ASAM Level 3.7):** Requires physician oversight and 24-hour medical coverage. Appropriate for individuals with significant alcohol, benzodiazepine, or opioid withdrawal risk.
- **Residential Treatment (ASAM Level 3.5):** 24-hour structured therapeutic environment. Requires clinical staff coverage but not necessarily 24-hour physician presence.
- **Partial Hospitalization Program/PHP (ASAM Level 2.5):** 20+ hours per week of structured programming while residing at home or in supported housing.
- **Intensive Outpatient Program/IOP (ASAM Level 2.1):** 9–19 hours per week. Appropriate for individuals with stable housing and moderate support systems.

You can verify a facility's current DPBH license status at dpbh.nv.gov.

**Accreditation: CARF and Joint Commission**

National accreditation through CARF International or The Joint Commission indicates that an independent review body has evaluated the program's clinical standards, safety practices, and treatment quality. Accreditation is voluntary — and the majority of programs are not accredited. An accredited program has undergone documented inspection and meets defined standards of care.

You can verify CARF accreditation at carf.org. Joint Commission accreditation can be verified at qualitycheck.org.

**LegitScript certification: required for digital advertising**

Since 2018, Google and Meta both require addiction treatment programs to hold active LegitScript certification before running any paid advertising. LegitScript independently verifies state licensing, accreditation, clinical credentials, and compliance with NAATP ethical standards before certifying a program. If a treatment center is running Google or Facebook ads but is not LegitScript-certified, their account will be suspended. You can verify LegitScript certification at legitscript.com.

**Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) in Henderson**

MAT — including buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), and methadone — is considered a first-line treatment for opioid use disorder and a clinically validated option for alcohol use disorder by SAMHSA, ASAM, and the American Society of Addiction Medicine. If MAT is a consideration, verify that the prescribing physician holds current DEA registration for buprenorphine prescribing (required under the MATE Act of 2023) and, for methadone, that the facility holds SAMHSA OTP (Opioid Treatment Program) certification.

**Insurance and publicly-funded options**

Nevada's Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) requires that commercial insurance plans provide addiction treatment benefits equivalent to medical/surgical coverage. If you have commercial insurance, call the program's clinical intake line and request a benefits verification — most DPBH-licensed programs will verify your specific benefits before you make an enrollment decision.

For individuals without insurance or with limited coverage, Nevada's Substance Abuse Prevention and Treatment Agency (SAPTA) administers state and federal funding for publicly-funded addiction treatment. The SAMHSA findtreatment.gov directory lists publicly-funded options statewide.

**If you're in crisis now**

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911. For substance use and mental health crisis support, call or text **988** (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24/7 nationwide). The Nevada Crisis Call Center is also available at 1-800-273-TALK (8255).
