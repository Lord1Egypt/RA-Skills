# Social Proof Collection Playbook

## Review Velocity Strategy

### Phase 1 — Launch (0–30 days post-launch)
Goal: Reach 25 reviews (visibility threshold on most platforms)

**Tactics:**
- Insert package insert with QR code linking to review page; include a simple "How was your experience?" prompt
- Send post-purchase email sequence: Day 5 (product check-in), Day 14 (review request with photo prompt)
- Activate Amazon Vine if brand-registered (free to enroll; Vine reviewers receive product at no cost)
- Seed product with 3–5 micro-influencers (1k–10k followers) for authentic early reviews

**Review request email — Day 14 template:**
Subject: "[First name], how's your [Product]?"
Body: "Hi [Name] — it's been two weeks since your [Product] arrived. We'd love to hear how it's going. If you have 2 minutes, a quick review on [platform] helps other shoppers like you make the right decision. [Review Link] — if anything isn't right, reply to this email and we'll fix it."

### Phase 2 — Acceleration (30–90 days)
Goal: Reach 100 reviews; 15%+ photo reviews

**Tactics:**
- Photo review prompt: "Share a photo and we'll send you a 10% discount on your next order" (compliant with FTC disclosure requirements)
- Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 48 hours
- Flag and report reviews that violate platform policies (incentivized reviews not disclosed, competing brand reviews)
- Use DSR (Detail Seller Rating) data on Amazon to identify product quality gaps causing negative reviews

### Phase 3 — Maintenance (90+ days)
Goal: Maintain 4.3+ star average; 200+ reviews

**Tactics:**
- Set automated review request via Jungle Scout or Helium 10 (Amazon-compliant automation)
- Refresh UGC every quarter — request new photos from loyal customers
- Highlight top reviews in A+ content and social media

## UGC (User-Generated Content) Collection

### Permission Framework
Always obtain explicit written permission before using customer content:
- Standard release: "May we feature your photo/video in our product listing and social channels? We'll tag you."
- Paid usage release: Required if UGC is used in paid advertising; use a formal written agreement

### UGC Brief for Customers
Send this prompt to encourage high-quality UGC:
"Show us [Product] in your daily routine. The best submissions get featured on our page and listing — no professional setup needed, just your real experience."

### UGC Curation Criteria
Select UGC that shows:
- Product in realistic use context (not just unboxed)
- Visible result or benefit (before/after, in-action shots)
- Authentic setting (real home, real outdoor environment)
- Diverse representation of customers

## Press & Authority Signals

### Getting Press Mentions
1. Submit to "Shark Tank"-style product discovery platforms (Product Hunt, Uncrate, Cool Hunting)
2. Pitch to gift guides: BuzzFeed, Wirecutter, Good Housekeeping — best submitted 3–4 months before seasonal peaks
3. Send samples to relevant YouTube reviewers with 50k–500k subscribers (mid-tier converts better than mega-influencers for niche products)

### Displaying Press Mentions
- Use "As seen in" bar with media logos above-the-fold on brand site
- On Amazon A+, add "Featured in [Publication]" as a call-out module
- On TikTok Shop, reference media features in pinned comment or product description

## Trust Signal A/B Test Ideas

| Test | Variant A | Variant B | Metric |
|------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Guarantee length | 30-day | 60-day | Add-to-cart rate |
| Badge count | 3 badges | 6 badges | Checkout completion |
| Review placement | Below price | Below bullets | Click-to-review rate |
| Testimonial type | Text quotes | Video clips | Time-on-page + conversion |
| Return policy | "Free returns" | "Free returns — we cover shipping" | Checkout abandonment |
