# Rewriting Guide

## Goal

Turn truthful but scattered candidate information into a focused resume for a specific job direction.

## Principles

- Keep facts unchanged
- Change emphasis, not reality
- Prefer role language over self-description
- Remove low-signal demographic details
- Make the first third of the resume match the target role
- Match the resume and cover letter to the same role narrative
- Prefer active, concrete verbs over passive phrasing

## Summary writing

Use 2-3 short sentences.

Include:

- strongest relevant background
- strongest relevant operating ability
- target job direction

Avoid:

- “认真负责”
- “学习能力强”
- “执行力强”
- “抗压能力强”

unless tied to evidence.

## Bullet writing

Prefer this structure:

- action
- scope
- method, tool, or context
- operational value or outcome

Example:

- Coordinated cross-team scheduling, partner communication, and on-site support to keep campaign execution on track.

Avoid:

- Helped with various tasks
- Responsible for daily work
- Strong communication ability

## Bullet checklist

For each bullet, ask:

- Does it begin with a strong action verb?
- Does it show what was done and for whom?
- Does it include scale, frequency, stakeholders, tools, or result when known?
- Is it a phrase rather than a personal narrative?
- Would a fast-scanning recruiter understand the value in one pass?

Useful verb families:

- Leadership: led, coordinated, supervised, organized, strengthened
- Communication: drafted, presented, negotiated, documented, translated
- Research: analyzed, evaluated, investigated, summarized, tested
- Technical: built, designed, automated, optimized, maintained
- Teaching: trained, coached, facilitated, instructed, guided
- Quantitative: forecasted, budgeted, modeled, audited, calculated
- Creative/content: created, edited, published, redesigned, produced
- Support/service: assisted, advised, resolved, supported, referred
- Organization/process: streamlined, implemented, monitored, validated

## Track heuristics

### Operations

Emphasize:

- workflow
- coordination
- scheduling
- documentation
- execution
- support

### Content

Emphasize:

- content maintenance
- asset handling
- live support
- material organization
- visual tools

### Training / Learning support

Emphasize:

- lesson support
- event planning
- stakeholder communication
- materials preparation
- classroom or program coordination

## Cover letter writing

Use when the user asks for a cover letter or application package.

Structure:

- opening: role, employer, source of interest, and fit
- middle: 1-2 evidence-backed examples connected to the role requirements
- closing: contribution, thanks, and interview availability

Rules:

- Keep to one page
- Address a real person when supplied
- Research or use supplied employer context before writing
- Do not repeat the full resume
- Avoid overusing "I"
- Match the resume font and general formatting
