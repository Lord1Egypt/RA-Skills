# Geoffrey Moore — Tech Forecasting

Chasm Group; primary sources: *Crossing the Chasm* (1991/2014); *Inside the
Tornado*; *Zone to Win*. Builds on Rogers' diffusion curve; adds the **chasm**
between early adopters and the early majority.

**Role in the tech stack:** *When* adoption accelerates or stalls — market
stage, beachhead logic, whole product.

---

## Technology adoption life cycle & the chasm

Rogers segments: innovators → early adopters → **chasm** → early majority →
late majority → laggards.

**Chasm insight:** early adopters buy **vision**; early majority (pragmatists)
buy **references + whole product**. Marketing that worked pre-chasm **fails**
post-chasm — different buyer psychology and channels.

**30-day chasm signals (widening gap):**

- Pipeline stalls after visionary wins; sales cycles lengthen.
- Pilots don't convert; "we need more integrations / support / partners."
- Competitors with **complete whole product** win pragmatist deals.
- NPS high among enthusiasts, flat among mainstream users.

---

## Four GTM stages (predict before prescribing)

| Stage | Market character | Forecast focus | Wrong move |
|-------|------------------|----------------|------------|
| **Early Market** | Visionaries, incomplete product | Breakthrough + visionary references | Mass-market sales |
| **Bowling Alley** | Crossing chasm; niche pragmatists | Beachhead dominance, whole product | Revenue-maximizing sprawl |
| **Tornado** | Hypergrowth; standards war | Share, speed, supply | Over-customization |
| **Main Street** | Mature; incremental | Retention, UX, +1 offerings | Betting on next disruption here |

**First step for any product forecast:** classify stage — predictions differ
radically by stage.

## D-Day strategy (crossing the chasm)

Moore's invasion metaphor — four steps:

### 1. Target the point of attack (beachhead)

Pick **one** niche; focus all resources until **dominant** in that segment.

**Beachhead criteria:**

- Can name **specific target customers** (often 50–500 in B2B).
- **Compelling, mission-critical** pain — economic value of problem matters
  more than niche size.
- Customers **talk to each other** (conferences, press, associations).
- **Whole product achievable** in 12–18 months with focused partners.
- Win here **opens adjacent niches** (bowling pins).

**Primary market identifiers:** target customer, compelling reason to buy,
whole product, competition, partners, distribution, pricing, positioning.

### 2. Assemble the invasion force (whole product)

Levitt/Moore levels:

- **Generic** — what ships.
- **Expected** — minimum to meet buyer's objective.
- **Augmented** — max chance of goal attainment (integrations, training, support).
- **Potential** — future growth path.

Pragmatists buy the **whole product**, not the core technology. List gaps;
commit sparingly and **leverage across multiple sales** in the same niche.

### 3. Define the battle (positioning)

Market-centric competition — one clear **incumbent alternative** (manual process
or named product). Elevator test:

> For **[target]** who are dissatisfied with **[alternative]**, our product is
> a **[category]** that **[key benefit]**. Unlike **[competitor]**, we
> **[differentiation]** with **[whole product proof]**.

### 4. Launch the invasion (distribution)

Channel must match **pragmatist** buying behavior — often direct enterprise
sales with high-touch support in chasm phase; tornado favors scalable channels.

**Bowling Alley rule:** dominate one pin, then **adjacent niche** — not broad
brush.

---

## Inside the Tornado (brief)

Once in tornado: **standard de facto**, land grab, pragmatist + conservative
buyers pile in. Forecast **share shifts** and **infrastructure bottlenecks**,
not visionary uptake. Main Street follows — growth slows; competition on
experience and cost.

## How to apply in this skill

Layer on `general_Philip_Tetlock.md` for tech product/market queries. Pair
`tech_paul_saffo.md` for macro uncertainty; `tech_clayton_christensen.md` when
asking if an incumbent is structurally blind.

1. **Stage classify** — Early Market / Bowling Alley / Tornado / Main Street.
2. **Chasm check** — If Bowling Alley, score beachhead + whole product completeness.
3. **30-day metrics** — Trial→paid conversion, reference-customer count, partner
   integrations shipped, sales-cycle length vs. prior cohort.
4. **Scenario triggers** — What whole-product gap or reference win would move
   you to next stage (or deeper into chasm)?
5. **Incumbent lens** — Who has whole product today? Who has vision only?

**Search targets:** G2/peer reviews citing "integration/support", customer case
studies in one vertical, partner ecosystem announcements, enterprise procurement
RFPs, developer vs. enterprise revenue mix, cohort retention by segment.
