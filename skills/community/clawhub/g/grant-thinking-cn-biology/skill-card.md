## Description: <br>
Use when evaluating biology grant ideas in the Chinese funding context (NSFC, MOST, etc.) by diagnosing project legitimacy, mechanism-centered scientific questions, reviewer-aware logic, innovation discipline, feasibility, and scope control across funding levels. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External researchers, proposal teams, and grant-writing agents use this skill to evaluate and strengthen biology grant ideas for Chinese funding contexts before drafting. It focuses on fundability, mechanistic coherence, reviewer risk, feasibility, innovation discipline, and scope fit across youth, general, and key-level projects. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill may be implicitly invoked outside its intended Chinese biology grant context. <br>
Mitigation: Disable implicit invocation where supported or invoke it only for Chinese biology grant evaluation and proposal-logic work. <br>
Risk: Users may provide confidential unpublished proposal material to an agent platform that is not approved for that data. <br>
Mitigation: Use only agent platforms and data-handling settings approved for confidential proposal content. <br>
Risk: Grant advice may be treated as authoritative despite being AI-generated strategic guidance. <br>
Mitigation: Have domain experts and proposal owners review recommendations before relying on them for grant decisions or submissions. <br>
