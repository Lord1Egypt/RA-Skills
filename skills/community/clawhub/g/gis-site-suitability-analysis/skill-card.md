## Description: <br>
Drafts methodology-first multi-criteria site suitability analysis reports for GIS analysts and planners, documenting criteria, spatial data sources, weighting rationale, overlay methodology, results, limitations, and review needs. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External GIS analysts, urban planners, environmental consultants, and site selection professionals use this skill to draft defensible documentation for multi-criteria site suitability analyses. It supports planning workflows for projects such as renewable energy siting, transit-oriented development, facility location, conservation prioritization, logistics hub selection, and hazard zone mapping. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Generated reports could be mistaken for regulatory, permitting, zoning, environmental, legal, or investment approvals. <br>
Mitigation: Keep outputs labeled as draft planning and decision-support aids, and require qualified professional review before decisions or submissions. <br>
Risk: GIS project details, site-selection criteria, parcel information, or planning data may be sensitive if shared in prompts. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid sharing sensitive site or stakeholder data unless approved for the agent environment, and redact details that are not needed for drafting. <br>
Risk: Suitability findings may be inaccurate if the user has not run the analysis, verified source layers, or confirmed area statistics. <br>
Mitigation: Use placeholders when results are not confirmed, require source documentation for each layer, and perform field verification and sensitivity analysis before relying on outputs. <br>
