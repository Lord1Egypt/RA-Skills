## Description: <br>
Guides solopreneurs through identifying, designing, building, testing, and maintaining no-code automation workflows across tools such as Zapier, Make, and n8n. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Solopreneurs and small-business operators use this skill to identify repetitive tasks, map triggers and actions, choose no-code automation tools, test workflows, and maintain automations that move business data between services. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Connecting third-party automation tools can transfer lead, customer, payment, usage, or social-posting data between services. <br>
Mitigation: Connect only intended accounts, grant minimum permissions, and confirm privacy, consent, retention, and vendor-review obligations before enabling workflows. <br>
Risk: Automations that create invoices, post content, or react to payment events can trigger business actions before a human reviews them. <br>
Mitigation: Test workflows with representative data, include approval or notification steps for sensitive actions, and monitor workflow logs for failures. <br>
