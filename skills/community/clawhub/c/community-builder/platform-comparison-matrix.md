## Audience Fit Guide

### Choose Discord if:
- Your audience includes anyone under 40 who games, codes, invests, or follows creator culture
- You want voice/video channels for live events without third-party tools
- You need granular channel permissions (VIP access, role-based visibility)
- Your brand has a passionate sub-niche that would form interest clusters

### Choose Facebook Groups if:
- Your audience is 35+ and already spending time on Facebook
- You want SEO benefit and discoverability via Facebook search
- You want members to invite friends easily (Facebook friend graph)
- Your team already uses Facebook for content and wants workflow simplicity

### Choose Reddit if:
- Your brand is in a category with active existing subreddits (you can participate before creating)
- You want community-driven moderation and high peer trust
- You accept lower brand control in exchange for authentic discussion
- SEO is a priority (Reddit threads rank highly in Google)
- Note: Do not create a brand subreddit unless you have strong organic demand — it will be seen as astroturfing

### Choose Circle if:
- You're building a paid community (courses, masterminds, membership)
- You want a professional/premium feel with no social media noise
- Your audience is willing to use a new platform if the value is clear
- You want native course + community integration

### Choose Slack (free) if:
- Your audience is B2B-adjacent or professional
- You need strong thread organization and async communication
- Community size will stay under 5,000 (message history limits on free plan)

## Migration Considerations

If your audience is already self-organizing in an unofficial space (third-party Facebook Group, competitor subreddit, Discord server), consider:
1. Joining and participating authentically for 4–8 weeks before announcing your official community
2. Asking existing community admins about partnership or migration rather than competing
3. Offering clear value-add for migration: exclusive content, founder access, early product access

Never attempt to "move" an existing organic community you don't own. Create compelling pull, not forced migration.
