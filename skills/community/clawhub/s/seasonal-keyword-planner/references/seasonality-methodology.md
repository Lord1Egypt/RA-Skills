# Seasonality Methodology

How to classify keywords, read demand curves, and find the windows that matter.

## Step 1 — Classify every keyword

- **Evergreen:** steady demand all year (e.g. "yoga mat", "phone case"). Maintain presence; don't chase a "season."
- **Seasonal:** predictable annual rise/fall driven by weather or behavior (e.g. "sunscreen", "down jacket").
- **Event-driven:** tied to a specific retail moment (e.g. "Valentine's gift for him", "Black Friday TV deal"). Sharp, short spikes.

A keyword can be hybrid: near-evergreen with a seasonal lift (e.g. "fleece jacket").

## Step 2 — Describe the curve

For each non-evergreen term, identify:

- **Peak month(s):** highest demand.
- **Rising shoulder:** the climb into the peak — the prep zone.
- **Falling shoulder:** demand declining — wind down spend.
- **Trough:** lowest demand — minimal investment, or pivot to off-season terms.

Express the curve in months, not vague quarters, so the plan is actionable.

## Step 3 — Find the early-mover window

The early-mover window is the **2–4 weeks before the rising shoulder accelerates**. In this window:

- Search interest is beginning to climb but competition (and CPC) is still low.
- Listings optimized now will be indexed and ranking by the time demand peaks.
- It is the single highest-leverage period for both organic and paid.

Mark it explicitly for every seasonal/event keyword.

## Step 4 — Account for lead times

| Action | Lead time before peak |
|---|---|
| Ad bid increases | 1–2 weeks |
| Listing copy/image refresh | 2–3 weeks (re-indexing) |
| New SEO content (guides, comparisons) | 4–6 weeks (time to rank) |
| Inventory / restock alignment | per supplier; plan earliest |

## Step 5 — Evidence vs assumption

- **Evidence:** multi-year search-volume patterns, the retail calendar, and the seller's own historical sales/traffic.
- **Assumption:** "it's a gift item so it must peak in December."

Always label which is which. A plan built on assumptions should say so and recommend validating with real data before committing budget.

## Step 6 — Coordinate to avoid cannibalization

When two keywords overlap (e.g. "rain jacket" vs "rain shell"), assign each to a distinct page/listing with a distinct intent, or consolidate. Don't let your own pages compete for the same term and split ranking authority.

## Hemisphere & market notes

- Weather-driven seasonality **inverts** between Northern and Southern hemispheres.
- Holiday-driven demand differs by country (e.g. Singles' Day in China, Boxing Day in UK/AU/CA).
- Plan each target market on its own calendar; never copy one market's curve onto another.
