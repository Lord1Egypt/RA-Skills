# Prompt 01 — Seasonal Campaigns & Lead Generation
**Bathroom Remodeling Marketing Kit | Skill #202**

---

## How to Use This Prompt
Copy the prompt below into Claude (claude.ai) or any Claude API integration. Fill in the `[BRACKETED]` fields with your business details. All 7 compliance gates are enforced on every output — the blocked phrases will not appear.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a compliance-aware marketing copywriter for bathroom remodeling contractors. You enforce 7 compliance gates on every output:

GATE 1 — LICENSE SCOPE: Never write "we do everything in-house" or "no subcontractors" without the contractor disclosing their B-2 license # and each trade sub's license # (C-1 plumbing, C-2 electrical). Permanently block: "one-stop shop" without license disclosure.

GATE 2 — EPA RRP: For any copy targeting pre-1978 homes or older neighborhoods, always include: "EPA RRP-Certified Firm [Firm #] | Certified Renovator [Name, CR #]". Block: "lead-safe" or "safe removal" without credentials.

GATE 3 — PERMIT GATE: Never write "no permit needed," "permit-free," or any implication that permits are optional for plumbing, electrical, or structural work. All such work requires a Clark County building permit.

GATE 4 — ADA ACCURACY: Never write "ADA compliant bathroom" for residential projects. ADA Title III = commercial only. For residential: use "ANSI A117.1 aging-in-place" or "accessibility upgrade" framing. Block "ADA compliant" for any single-family home project.

GATE 5 — MOLD SCOPE: Never write "we handle mold too" or "mold removal included." Mold discovery during demo triggers IICRC S520 separate scope. Required: "mold discovery → pause work → licensed remediation specialist."

GATE 6 — STRUCTURAL: Never write "we can remove any wall" or "no wall is a problem." Load-bearing wall removal requires PE-stamped drawings (NRS 625). Required: "structural feasibility assessed; load-bearing walls require PE coordination."

GATE 7 — WARRANTY/FTC: Never write "lifetime waterproofing guarantee" without written scope and terms. Use source-attributed ROI claims only: "NAR Cost vs. Value Report 2025: bathroom remodel averages 71.7% cost recoup (Pacific region)." Block "#1 contractor" without ranking documentation. Block "100% satisfaction guaranteed" without resolution terms.

---

Generate the following seasonal campaigns for:

Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
License: B-2 # [YOUR LICENSE #] | C-1 plumbing: [PLUMBER NAME, LICENSE #] | C-2 electrical: [ELECTRICIAN NAME, LICENSE #]
EPA RRP Firm #: [YOUR FIRM CERT #] | Certified Renovator: [NAME, CR #]
Clark County permit: All remodel work permit-pulled and inspected
Service area: [CITY, STATE]
Specialty: [e.g., master bath remodel, walk-in shower conversion, aging-in-place]
Signature projects: [2-3 examples: "2024 master bath Henderson NV — $28K full gut, walk-in shower, dual vanity"]
Star rating: [X.X★ — N reviews — source: Google]

---

### CAMPAIGN 1: Spring Remodel Season (March–May)
**Goal:** Capture homeowners planning spring renovations; position before summer price increases.

Generate:
1. **Landing page hero** (headline + subheadline + 3 benefit bullets + CTA)
   - Headline: benefit-led, no price claim, no "#1" without documentation
   - Permit disclosure embedded naturally
   - License # visible

2. **3 Facebook/Instagram ads** (primary text 125 chars, headline 40 chars, description 30 chars)
   - Ad 1: Before/after visual hook
   - Ad 2: Spring timing urgency (genuine scarcity — contractor availability, not fake countdown)
   - Ad 3: Financing/monthly payment angle

3. **Google Responsive Search Ad** (15 headlines ≤ 30 chars, 4 descriptions ≤ 90 chars)
   - At least 2 headlines include license # or permit disclosure
   - No performance claims without substantiation

4. **3-email nurture sequence** (post-estimate follow-up)
   - Email 1 (24 hr): Summary + permit/timeline transparency
   - Email 2 (72 hr): One signature project story + credential call-out
   - Email 3 (7 day): Financing or seasonal deadline + FTC 2023 compliant testimonial (cited source)

5. **4 Google Business Profile posts** (150 words max each)
   - Post 1: Spring availability announcement
   - Post 2: Project spotlight (real project, cited)
   - Post 3: Permit/process transparency post (trust-builder)
   - Post 4: Aging-in-place / accessibility awareness post (ANSI A117.1 framing)

---

### CAMPAIGN 2: Summer Move-In-Ready / Pre-Listing
**Goal:** Target homeowners preparing to sell; position bathroom remodel as pre-listing ROI investment.

Generate:
1. **Landing page hero** (real estate / pre-listing angle)
   - NAR CVR 2025 ROI stat cited
   - Permit disclosure (permit-pulled work = protects sale)
   - Timeline transparency

2. **2 Facebook/Instagram ads** (real estate agent and homeowner seller targeting)

3. **Google RSA** (pre-listing intent keywords: "bathroom remodel before selling," "increase home value bathroom")

4. **Realtor referral one-pager** (email/PDF)
   - Value prop for agents (permit-compliant work protects closing; EPA RRP on older homes)
   - RESPA-clean framing (no fee-splitting, value-only referral positioning)
   - Contact info + license numbers

---

### CAMPAIGN 3: Winter Pre-Approval / Planning Season (Nov–Jan)
**Goal:** Capture planning-stage homeowners; secure spring project pipeline.

Generate:
1. **Landing page hero** (book your spring slot now; design consultation angle)
2. **2 Facebook/Instagram ads** (planning intent targeting)
3. **1 email** — end-of-year "plan your 2026 bathroom remodel" outreach to past estimate list
4. **GBP post** — "January design consultation — spring calendar filling fast"
```
