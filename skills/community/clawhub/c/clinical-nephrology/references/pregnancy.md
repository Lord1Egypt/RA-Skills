# Pregnancy and kidney disease

Use this reference for CKD in pregnancy, AKI in pregnancy, preeclampsia, proteinuria, lupus nephritis, transplant pregnancy, hypertension in pregnancy, and medication safety.

## Core approach

1. Establish gestational age, baseline kidney function, current creatinine, proteinuria, blood pressure, symptoms, fetal status if known, and prior kidney diagnosis.
2. Distinguish pre-existing CKD/proteinuria from new pregnancy complications when possible.
3. Consider preeclampsia, HELLP, thrombotic microangiopathy, lupus nephritis flare, acute fatty liver, obstruction, infection, volume depletion, and medication-related AKI.
4. Note that normal pregnancy lowers serum creatinine; small creatinine increases can be clinically meaningful.
5. Emphasize multidisciplinary care: nephrology, maternal-fetal medicine/obstetrics, and relevant specialists.
6. Treat drug safety carefully. Do not invent medication safety claims; verify from authoritative pregnancy and nephrology sources.

## Red flags

- Severe hypertension, neurologic symptoms, pulmonary edema, oliguria/anuria, rapidly rising creatinine, thrombocytopenia, hemolysis, severe transaminitis, fetal compromise, severe proteinuria with systemic symptoms.
