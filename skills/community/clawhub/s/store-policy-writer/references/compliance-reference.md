# Compliance Reference

A practical map of the disclosure expectations that most affect ecommerce policies. This is operational guidance, not legal advice — regulated products and cross-border tax should be reviewed by an attorney.

## United States — CCPA / CPRA (California)

- Applies to many businesses serving California residents above certain thresholds.
- Disclose: categories of personal information collected, purposes, and categories shared.
- Provide rights: know/access, delete, correct, and opt out of "sale"/"sharing" for targeted ads.
- If you run ad-targeting pixels, include a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" mechanism.
- State plainly whether you sell personal information (most small stores do not).

## EU / UK — GDPR / UK GDPR

- Applies if you offer goods to people in the EU/UK or monitor their behavior.
- Lawful basis: contract (to fulfill orders), legitimate interest (fraud prevention), and consent (marketing, non-essential cookies).
- Rights to surface: access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, and objection.
- Cookies: obtain consent before loading non-essential cookies (a real accept/reject banner, not a notice-only bar).
- Name an EU/UK contact route for data requests.

## EU — Consumer Rights / Distance Selling

- A statutory **14-day cooling-off period** typically applies to online sales, allowing return without reason (with some exclusions like perishables, hygiene items, and customized goods).
- Your stated return window should meet or exceed the statutory minimum for EU customers.

## Marketplace buyer protection (align, never undercut)

- **Amazon:** returns generally accepted within 30 days; your policy must not be more restrictive than Amazon's return framework for the categories you sell.
- **TikTok Shop:** has its own buyer-protection and return windows; review-incentive and refund rules are platform-governed.
- **Shopee / regional marketplaces:** platform-managed return windows and dispute flows; policy must reference the platform's process where it governs.
- Rule of thumb: on a marketplace, the platform's buyer protection wins. Write store policies that complement, not contradict, it.

## Product-specific flags (route to attorney)

- Food, supplements, cosmetics: labeling and safety regimes (e.g. FDA in the US).
- Electronics/batteries: shipping restrictions and warranty obligations.
- Children's products: additional safety/data rules (e.g. COPPA for data from under-13s).
- Alcohol, CBD, medical devices: heavily regulated; generic policies are insufficient.

## Always include

- An effective date.
- A named contact channel for privacy and returns.
- A statement of the regions you ship to and who bears duties/taxes.
- A change-notification clause.
