## The 1-2-3 Magic System — 3 Jobs of Parenting

### The 3 Parenting Jobs

The entire 1-2-3 program is built on three distinct parenting jobs:

| Job | Focus | Strategy | Parent Mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| Job 1 | Control obnoxious behavior (Stop) | 1-2-3 Counting | Demanding-Firm |
| Job 2 | Encourage good behavior (Start) | 7 Strategies | Both warm + firm |
| Job 3 | Strengthen relationship | Listening + Fun + Problem-solving | Warm-Friendly |

### Job 1: The Counting Method

**The core technique:** When a child engages in Stop behavior (whining, arguing, fighting, backtalk), you calmly say "That's 1." Count to 1 again for continued behavior. "That's 2." If it continues: "That's 3. Take a break."

**The No Talking and No Emotion Rules:**
- **No Talking:** Once you're counting, do NOT explain, lecture, argue. Every word is a chance for the child to debate. Silence is authority.
- **No Emotion:** No anger, no frustration, no visible upset. The count is a calm, deliberate signal. When you get emotional, the child knows they've gotten to you—and testing increases.

> **Case: Sarah's transformation** (Introduction): A single mother of three, Sarah dreaded mornings. Her kids didn't cooperate, they fought, they treated her like she was invisible. She screamed, yelled, nagged. After learning 1-2-3 Magic, she used counting for sibling rivalry and disrespect, and Start behavior tactics for mornings. Within weeks she reported: "I missed them for the first time in my life." **Key takeaway:** Counting removes the emotional charge from discipline, allowing parent-child relationships to heal.

> **Case: The Twinkie conversation** (Introduction): A girl asks for a Twinkie before dinner. Mom says no. The child argues: "You never give me anything!" "You gave Joey one!" "I promise I'll eat my dinner!" The conversation spirals into "I'm going to kill myself and then run away from home!" — all because the parent engaged in argument. **Key takeaway:** Every argument you enter during discipline escalates. The count cuts the spiral before it starts.

**The 5-Second Pause:**
Count at minimum 5 seconds apart. This gives the child time to choose to stop. If you count too fast (1...2...3!), it feels like punishment. Slow counting teaches self-control.

**What counts as a break (timeout):**
- Age 2-3: 2-3 minutes in a boring spot
- Age 4-6: 4-6 minutes
- Age 7-12: 7-12 minutes
- Rule of thumb: 1 minute per year of age

### Job 2: Encouraging Good Behavior (The 7 Start Strategies)

For Start behaviors (morning routines, homework, chores, bedtime), counting alone doesn't work. You need different tools:

1. **Positive reinforcement** — Praise specific good behavior immediately
2. **Simple requests** — One request at a time, clearly stated
3. **Kitchen timer** — "Let's see if you can finish before the timer goes off"
4. **Natural consequences** — "If you don't eat dinner now, there won't be anything until breakfast"
5. **Behavior charts** — Visual tracking for routines and chores
6. **Counting for Start** — "That's 1 for not starting your homework" (works for refusal, not for slowness)
7. **Family meetings** — Collaborative problem-solving for recurring issues

### Job 3: Strengthening the Relationship

The most neglected job. Three core strategies:
- **Sympathetic listening** — Don't problem-solve. Just listen. "That sounds really hard. Tell me more."
- **One-on-one fun** — 15 minutes per child per day, no agenda, no correcting, just enjoying each other
- **Solving problems together** — Family meetings where everyone's input matters
