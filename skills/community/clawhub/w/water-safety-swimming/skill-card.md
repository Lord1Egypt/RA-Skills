## Description: <br>
Water safety, basic swimming skills, and emergency water rescue techniques for people who cannot swim, supervise children near water, plan open-water activities, or need basic pool, beach, river, and rescue guidance. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and agents use this skill to assess water competency, explain survival techniques, recognize drowning signs, and choose safer layperson rescue actions around pools, beaches, rivers, lakes, and cold water. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill gives general water-safety education that could be mistaken for certified training or emergency response. <br>
Mitigation: Use it as orientation only and rely on certified swim instructors, CPR training providers, lifeguards, and emergency services for training and real incidents. <br>
Risk: Rescue guidance may be applied under panic or in hazardous water, increasing danger to the rescuer. <br>
Mitigation: Prioritize calling emergency services, use reach or throw methods from safety, and enter the water only when trained and equipped with flotation. <br>
Risk: Pool, boating, beach flag, emergency number, and CPR guidance can vary by region and may change. <br>
Mitigation: Verify current local laws, facility rules, emergency numbers, and guidance from recognized local water-safety organizations before acting. <br>
