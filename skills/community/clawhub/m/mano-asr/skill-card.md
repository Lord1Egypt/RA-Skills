## Description: <br>
Guides OpenClaw users through configuring mano-asr to transcribe audio locally on Apple Silicon Macs and inject transcripts into OpenClaw conversations. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

Developers and OpenClaw operators use this skill to configure local speech-to-text for audio messages on Apple Silicon Macs, including model setup, OpenClaw configuration, service management, and troubleshooting. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Optional cloud fallback can send audio to a third-party provider. <br>
Mitigation: Use the local-only mano-asr configuration when privacy is important, and enable cloud fallback only after confirming that sending audio to the provider is acceptable. <br>
Risk: Transcript echo can expose or persist sensitive speech content in chat channels. <br>
Mitigation: Keep transcript echo disabled in untrusted or shared channels, or restrict audio transcription to private conversations. <br>
Risk: Installation depends on a third-party Homebrew tap. <br>
Mitigation: Review the Homebrew tap and package source before installing in managed or security-sensitive environments. <br>
