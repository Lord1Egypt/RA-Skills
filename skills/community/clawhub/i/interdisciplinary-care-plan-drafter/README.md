# interdisciplinary-care-plan-drafter

A structured documentation skill for hospice and palliative care interdisciplinary teams that converts goals-of-care conversations and clinical assessments into a CMS Conditions of Participation–compliant Interdisciplinary Care Plan (ICP).

---

## What It Does

Guides an attending physician, APRN, RN, social worker, or IDT coordinator through every required element of a Medicare hospice benefit ICP — patient and family goals of care, advance directive and surrogate decision-maker status, comfort care plan across medical/nursing/social/spiritual domains, IDT role assignments, visit frequency schedule, and family conference documentation — and produces a DRAFT ICP for licensed clinician and IDT co-review before any care delivery or billing use.

## When to Use

- Drafting an initial hospice Interdisciplinary Care Plan at admission (required within 5 days of admission per 42 CFR § 418.56)
- Updating an ICP at every IDT meeting (required at least every 15 days)
- Documenting a goals-of-care conversation or family conference in a structured care plan format
- Preparing palliative care consultation documentation for a seriously ill inpatient or outpatient

## What It Produces

- Patient identification block (PII-safe)
- Goals-of-care summary (patient/family preferences, advance directive status, surrogate/DPOA identification)
- Comfort care plan by domain: medical symptom management, nursing, social work, spiritual/chaplaincy, aide services
- IDT role assignment table with discipline, assigned staff (if known), and responsibilities
- Visit frequency schedule per discipline (CMS CoP–aligned)
- Family conference summary (date, attendees, key decisions)
- Open-items checklist (advance directive gaps, pending referrals, unresolved symptom management questions)
- Attending physician and IDT co-signature block

## Scope and Boundaries

- Produces DRAFT documentation only — all content requires licensed clinician and IDT review before any care delivery or billing use
- Goals-of-care conversations must be conducted by qualified clinicians; this skill documents and structures those conversations, it does not conduct them
- Prognosis and symptom management statements are labeled DRAFT — CLINICAL ASSESSMENT REQUIRED
- Does not constitute a medication order, care order, or clinical decision
- PII handling: collects initials + case reference only; never stores full name, date of birth, MRN, or insurance information

## Requirements

- Clinician (attending physician, APRN, or RN) to conduct the goals-of-care assessment and review the DRAFT
- IDT input from each discipline (nursing, social work, chaplaincy, aide) before finalization
- Applicable state hospice licensure requirements may add documentation elements beyond CMS CoP minimums
