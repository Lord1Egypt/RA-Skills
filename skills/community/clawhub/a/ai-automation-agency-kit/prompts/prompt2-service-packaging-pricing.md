# Prompt 2 — Service Packaging & Pricing Guide

## Purpose
Create a complete productized service menu, proposal template, and pricing system that converts prospects into paying clients at the right price point.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an agency pricing consultant helping an AI automation agency build their service packages and proposal system.

My agency:
Agency name: [AGENCY NAME]
Niche: [YOUR NICHE — e.g., "hospitality operations automation, Las Vegas"]
My skill level: [BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED]
Target client size: [e.g., "50-300 employees"]
Working style: [LOCAL / REMOTE / HYBRID]
My hourly value target: [e.g., "$150/hour"]

Core services I offer (from Prompt 1):
[LIST YOUR 3 SERVICES]

Build me a complete service packaging and pricing system:

1. THREE-TIER PRODUCTIZED SERVICE MENU
For each tier (Starter / Growth / Enterprise):
- Tier name and positioning tagline (who it's for, in one phrase)
- Exactly what's included (bullet list, specific deliverables)
- What's explicitly NOT included (prevents scope creep)
- Timeline from signed contract to delivery
- Price (flat fee, not hourly)
- Ideal client profile (size, pain level, urgency)
- Upsell path to the next tier

2. PROPOSAL TEMPLATE
Full 6-section proposal I can send as a Google Doc or PDF:
Section 1: Executive Summary (3-4 sentences, mirrors their words back)
Section 2: The Problem (what they're losing in time and money today)
Section 3: Our Solution (how your 3 services address the specific pain)
Section 4: Deliverables & Timeline (phase-by-phase, each with week number and owner)
Section 5: Investment (present all 3 tiers, recommend one, explain the selection)
Section 6: Next Steps (specific: "Reply to this email by [date] to hold your spot")

3. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY GUIDE
- Anchoring technique: how to present the 3 tiers so the middle tier gets chosen most
- Value framing script: 3-sentence formula to express ROI, not features
- ROI projection template: fill-in-the-blanks formula to calculate client savings
- "What does it cost to NOT do this?" reframe (use before they push back on price)

4. OBJECTION HANDLING SCRIPTS
For each of the 3 most common objections:
- "That's too expensive" — full reframe script (5-7 sentences)
- "We can build it in-house" — competitive positioning response
- "We need to think about it" — urgency and loss-aversion close

5. RETAINER MODEL FRAMING
- How to present a monthly retainer after project delivery
- Suggested retainer tiers (Monitoring / Active / VIP) with pricing and what's included
- Retainer pitch email template (send at project close)

6. CONTRACT SCOPE DEFINITION
- Standard inclusions list (what every project includes)
- Standard exclusions list (what's always out of scope)
- Change order trigger definition (what constitutes a new request)
- One-paragraph change order language to add to your contracts
```

---

## Example Output (condensed)

**Starter Tier — "First Automation" ($1,500 flat)**  
For: Businesses doing one painful manual process that they know needs fixing  
Includes: 1 Zapier/Make workflow (up to 5 steps), 1 Google Sheets dashboard, 1-hour training session, 30-day bug fix guarantee  
Excludes: API integrations requiring custom code, ongoing monitoring  
Timeline: 10 business days  
Upsell: "Once you see the time savings, most clients want to automate 3-5 more workflows — that's the Growth package."

**Growth Tier — "Ops Automation Package" ($5,500 flat)**  
For: Operations managers with 10+ hours/week of manual reporting or data moving  
Includes: 5 workflows (up to 8 steps each), 1 automated dashboard, staff training, 60-day support  
Ideal client: Hotel/resort with 50-200 employees, ops manager drowning in daily reconciliations  

**Enterprise Tier — "Department Automation System" ($14,500 + $750/month)**  
For: Companies wanting to automate an entire department's data flow  
Includes: Up to 15 workflows, custom dashboard, API integrations, staff training program, monthly retainer  

**ROI Projection Formula:**  
"Your team spends [X hours/week] on [task]. At [average salary of person doing it], that's $[X * salary * 52] per year in labor cost. Our automation reduces that time by 70%, saving $[0.7 * annual cost] in year one. The project pays for itself in [project cost / monthly savings] months."
