# Prompt 4: Estimate Follow-Up, Referral System & SMS Campaign

## Instructions

Fill in [BRACKETED] fields. Generates a 3-email estimate follow-up sequence, referral request system, and 5-message SMS campaign.

---

## Prompt

You are a customer lifecycle copywriter for Nevada home services contractors. Generate a complete post-estimate and post-project communication system for an asphalt driveway sealing contractor.

**Business Information:**
- Company name: [COMPANY NAME]
- Service area: [CITY]
- License: [TYPE + NUMBER]
- Owner/estimator name: [NAME]
- Booking link or phone: [BOOKING URL or PHONE]
- Referral offer: [e.g., "$25 credit for both you and your referral"]
- Review platform: [Google / Nextdoor / Facebook — pick primary]

**Compliance Rules:**
1. No guaranteed longevity claims beyond "3–5 years"
2. No coal tar references as positive
3. No "guaranteed" weather outcomes
4. HOA language: "We help coordinate HOA approval" not "HOA approved"
5. All warranty language: "workmanship warranty" not "product lifetime guarantee" unless documented

---

**Generate:**

**A) 3-Email Estimate Follow-Up Sequence**

Goal: Convert estimate to booked job within 5 days.

**Email 1 — Same day as estimate (Hour 4–6):**
Subject line options (3 variants)
Body: Warm, specific recap of what was assessed, sealer recommendation (asphalt emulsion, not coal tar), price anchor, next step CTA. 150–200 words. Include license number in signature.

**Email 2 — Day 3 (if no response):**
Subject line options (3 variants)
Body: Objection handling — address top 3 estimate objections (price, timing, "my neighbor did it cheaper"). Include coal tar differentiation as reason your price is correct, not high. Offer: schedule before [SEASON DEADLINE] for priority booking. 120–160 words.

**Email 3 — Day 5 (final follow-up):**
Subject line options (3 variants)
Body: Scarcity (schedule fills, weather window, HOA approval lead time), soft last-chance CTA, offer to answer any questions. Keep door open — non-pushy but clear close attempt. 100–130 words.

**B) Post-Project Referral Request System**

**Referral Email (send 48–72 hours post-job):**
Subject line options (3 variants)
Body: Thank-you, specific project reference, referral offer explanation, simple ask. Mention coal tar/asphalt emulsion differentiation as the "story worth sharing." 150–180 words.

**Referral Text (send 3 days post-job — shorter):**
3 SMS variants (under 160 chars each). Personal tone, specific to their project, referral offer clear.

**Referral Card Insert Copy:**
Physical card text (80 words max) for inclusion with invoice or door hanger. Referral code format.

**C) 5-Message SMS Campaign**

All messages: under 160 characters. Include STOP opt-out on messages 1 and 5.

**Message 1 — Appointment Reminder (day before):**
Time, address confirmation, crew arrival window, cure time reminder.

**Message 2 — Day-of Arrival (30 min before):**
"On our way" format. Crew name, arrival ETA, parking/access note.

**Message 3 — Post-Job Completion (same day):**
Job complete, cure time reminder (48 hours, no traffic), next steps. Compliance: do not guarantee results pending cure period.

**Message 4 — Review Request (Day 3 post-job):**
Satisfaction check, direct link to [Google/Nextdoor] review page. Keep short — link does the work.

**Message 5 — Annual Re-Seal Reminder (11 months later):**
"It's been a year" format. Reference their specific property, offer priority booking, seasonal angle. STOP opt-out included.

---

## Output Format

Label A, B, and C clearly. Emails formatted with Subject: / Body: / Signature: sections. SMS labeled Message 1–5. Total: 1,000–1,300 words.
