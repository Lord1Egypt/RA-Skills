# Techniques: The Practical Tools of Think and Grow Rich

## The Six-Step Desire Script

The most powerful technique in the book. Write this script and read it aloud twice daily:

```
I, [your name], will earn $[exact amount] by [exact date].
In return for this money, I will provide [specific value I will deliver].
My plan to achieve this is: [specific plan].
I already see myself in possession of this money. I feel it. I believe it.
```

**How to read it**: Stand in front of a mirror. Read with conviction. See yourself already rich. Feel the emotions of having achieved your goal. Repeat morning and night for 30 days.

## The Persistence Habit Builder

To develop the habit of persistence:

1. **Define your definite purpose**: write it down and burn it into your mind
2. **Create a plan**: even a bad plan is better than no plan — improve it as you go
3. **Shield yourself from negativity**: avoid people who tell you "it can't be done"
4. **Form a mastermind**: find one person who supports your goal and check in weekly
5. **Follow through**: decide that you will never quit before your deadline, no matter what

> **Case: The man who failed 20 times** (Chapter 8): Hill describes an inventor who failed 20 times before his product succeeded. "Most people would have quit after three failures. But he had made a decision: he would persist until his invention worked. He was not a genius — he was persistent. His persistence was worth more than raw talent."

## The Autosuggestion Script

Write your burning desire statement on a card. Read it aloud before sleep and after waking:

"At night, before falling asleep, give your subconscious mind a clear picture of your desire. As you sleep, your subconscious will work on it. In the morning, repeat the process. Your subconscious mind accepts what you tell it with feeling."

## The Mastermind Formation Guide

Steps to form a mastermind alliance:

1. **Define the purpose**: what specific goal are you working toward?
2. **Identify members**: find 2-5 people who have skills, knowledge, or connections you lack
3. **Establish harmony**: mastermind only works when there is complete harmony — no ego, no jealousy
4. **Set a meeting schedule**: weekly is best for active projects
5. **Commit to reciprocity**: each member serves the others' goals as well as their own

## The Decision Drill

To build the habit of quick, firm decisions:

1. **Set a deadline**: give yourself 24 hours to make any non-trivial decision. No more.
2. **Gather three facts**: identify three pieces of information that would help you decide. Seek them quickly.
3. **Decide**: announce your decision to someone. Public commitment reinforces resolve.
4. **Don't reconsider**: unless the facts change significantly, stick to your decision.

> **Case: The F. W. Woolworth decision** (Chapter 7): Woolworth decided to open a five-and-dime store despite having almost no money. He made the decision in one evening and never reconsidered. "I knew if I spent time thinking about it, I would talk myself out of it." Within a decade, he was one of the wealthiest retailers in America.

## The Fear Analysis Worksheet

When you feel fear, write this down:

1. What exactly am I afraid of? (Name it specifically)
2. What is the worst possible outcome?
3. Can I survive the worst outcome? (Answer: almost always yes)
4. What is the best possible outcome?
5. What is one action I can take today regardless of fear?
