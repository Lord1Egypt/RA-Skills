# Prompt 1: Customer Story Narrative

## Purpose
Write the full case study narrative — the human story behind the numbers. This is the section prospects read first and share internally. It must be credible, specific, and structured to mirror the buyer's own situation.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a B2B content strategist writing a case study for publication on [YOUR COMPANY NAME]'s website.

Write a customer case study narrative (700–1,000 words) using the details below. 

STRUCTURE:
1. **Opening hook** (2–3 sentences) — Start with the customer's pain, not a product pitch. Make the reader who has the same problem say "that's me."
2. **Customer background** (1 short paragraph) — Company, industry, size, what they do.
3. **The challenge** (2 paragraphs) — Describe the specific problem in detail. What were they doing before? What was failing? What was the cost (time, money, missed opportunities)?
4. **Why they chose [YOUR COMPANY]** (1 paragraph) — What did they evaluate? Why did your solution win? Be honest — include one or two hesitations they had before starting.
5. **Implementation** (2 paragraphs) — How did the solution get deployed? Who was involved? How long did it take? What was the first sign it was working?
6. **The transformation** (2 paragraphs) — What changed in their day-to-day? How do team members describe the difference? Use specific job roles and situations.
7. **Closing quote + forward look** (1 paragraph) — End with a quote from the customer and a sentence about where they're headed next.

CUSTOMER DETAILS:
- Company name: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Company size: [SIZE — employees, revenue, or team description]
- The problem: [PROBLEM DESCRIPTION]
- Your product/service: [YOUR SOLUTION]
- How you solved it: [IMPLEMENTATION DETAILS]
- Key results: [METRICS — be specific: numbers, percentages, time saved, cost recovered]
- Timeline to results: [TIMEFRAME]
- Customer contact name/title: [NAME, TITLE]
- Customer quote (if available): "[QUOTE OR WRITE: 'generate one']"

TONE GUIDELINES:
- Human and specific — write like a journalist, not a PR department
- No buzzwords: avoid "leverage," "synergies," "game-changing," "revolutionary"
- Use the customer's industry language naturally
- Third person throughout (refer to customer company by name)
- Vary sentence length — mix short punchy statements with longer explanation
- Every claim must be tied to a specific metric or named person

OUTPUT:
- Title: [Customer Company] + [Outcome] — e.g., "How CloudPath Solutions Recovered 8 Hours a Week with ReportLab"
- Word count: 700–1,000 words
- Subheadings for each section (use the section names above, or rewrite to feel natural)
```

---

## Usage Notes

- Run this first. Prompts 2–4 reference the story established here.
- If the customer hasn't provided a quote, write "generate one" and the AI will produce a quote consistent with the narrative. Have the customer approve it before publishing.
- For real case studies, get written approval from the customer before publishing. Include their name, title, and company in the approval request.
- Keep all metrics conservative — understate rather than overstate. Skeptical B2B buyers will dismiss inflated numbers.
