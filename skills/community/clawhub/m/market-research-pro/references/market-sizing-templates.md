# Market Sizing Templates & Examples

## Template 1: SaaS B2B Market Sizing

### Top-Down Example (Project Management SaaS)
```
Global Project Management Software Market (2024): $6.8B (Source: Grand View Research)
→ TAM: $6.8B (all PM software buyers worldwide)

Serviceable segment: SMB (10–500 employees) in tech + professional services
→ SAM: $6.8B × 35% = $2.38B

Realistic capture in 3 years (new entrant):
→ SOM: $2.38B × 0.5% = $11.9M ARR target
```

### Bottom-Up Example
```
Target customers: 50,000 SMB tech companies in US + EU
Average contract value: $2,400/year ($200/mo × 10 seats avg)
Year 1 conversion rate: 0.3%

SOM (Year 1) = 50,000 × $2,400 × 0.3% = $360,000 ARR
SOM (Year 3) = 50,000 × $2,400 × 2.0% = $2.4M ARR
```

## Template 2: Consumer App Market Sizing

### Example: Fitness App in Southeast Asia
```
SEA internet users (2024): ~480M (Source: DataReportal)
Smartphone penetration: ~72% → 346M smartphone users
Health & fitness app interest: ~18% → 62M potential users (TAM)

Target: urban millennials (25–40) in SG/MY/TH/ID tier-1 cities
→ SAM: 62M × 15% = 9.3M users

Freemium conversion rate: 8%, ARPU: $36/year
→ SAM (revenue): 9.3M × 8% × $36 = $26.8M

Year 1 realistic capture: 0.5% of SAM users
→ SOM: 46,500 users, $1.7M revenue
```

## Key Data Sources by Category

### Free Sources
- **Statista**: industry statistics, market size charts
- **World Bank Open Data**: macro economic indicators
- **Google Trends**: search interest over time
- **PitchBook/Crunchbase**: funding rounds, company data
- **SEC EDGAR**: public company filings (revenue, segments)
- **CB Insights**: tech industry research
- **Our World in Data**: demographic, economic datasets

### Industry-Specific Sources
- **Healthcare**: WHO, CMS, IQVIA
- **Finance**: Bloomberg, S&P Global
- **Retail/CPG**: Nielsen, Euromonitor
- **Tech**: Gartner, IDC, Forrester
- **Startup ecosystem**: AngelList, Dealroom, PitchBook

## Common Sizing Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing TAM with SAM**: Don't claim you'll capture the entire global market
2. **No source citations**: Always attribute market size figures
3. **Hockey stick projections**: Growth must be tied to specific catalysts
4. **Ignoring churn**: For SaaS, net revenue retention matters as much as new ARR
5. **Single-point estimates**: Use ranges (e.g., "$8–12B by 2027")
6. **Stale data**: Markets change fast — use data ≤ 2 years old when possible
