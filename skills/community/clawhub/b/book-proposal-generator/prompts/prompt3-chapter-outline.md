# Prompt 3: Chapter-by-Chapter Outline

**Run this after Prompts 1 and 2. Provide your chapter list and core argument per chapter.**

---

## THE PROMPT

```
You are a developmental editor at a major publishing house with 15 years of experience shaping nonfiction manuscripts. You know how chapters need to function — what a chapter outline must show an agent (not just what you'll say, but how you'll say it and why it belongs in THIS book).

I need you to write the Chapter-by-Chapter Outline section of my nonfiction book proposal.

**My Book:**
- Title: [From Prompt 1]
- Core argument: [From Prompt 1]
- Tone/style: [Prescriptive / Narrative / Hybrid]
- Target word count: [From Prompt 1, e.g., 70,000 words]

**My Chapters (fill in what you have — working titles are fine):**
1. Introduction: [Core premise in 1-2 sentences]
2. Chapter 1 — [Working title]: [Core argument/content in 2-3 sentences]
3. Chapter 2 — [Working title]: [Core argument/content in 2-3 sentences]
4. Chapter 3 — [Working title]: [Core argument/content in 2-3 sentences]
5. Chapter 4 — [Working title]: [Core argument/content in 2-3 sentences]
6. [Continue for all chapters...]
7. Conclusion: [What synthesis/call to action you want to leave with]

**Stories or case studies I plan to use:** [List 3-5 real stories, research studies, or examples that will anchor the book]

Please produce:

1. **INTRODUCTION OUTLINE (200-250 words)**
   - Opening hook: The scene, question, or provocative statement that opens the book
   - The problem setup: What readers are experiencing before reading this book
   - The promise: What they will gain by reading it
   - Your authority: Brief mention of why you're the guide
   - Chapter roadmap: How the book is structured and why in this order
   - Reader contract: The exact transformation they're signing up for

2. **CHAPTER OUTLINES (150-200 words each)**
   
   For each chapter, produce:
   
   **Chapter [#]: [Polished chapter title — not just a topic, but a claim or question]**
   
   *Core argument:* One sentence stating the chapter's central claim or insight
   
   *What this chapter does:* 2-3 sentences describing the content, approach, and flow (how does it open? what does it deliver? how does it land?)
   
   *Evidence anchor:* The primary story, case study, research finding, or example that makes this chapter concrete (give the real name/source if you have it)
   
   *Insight delivered:* The one thing the reader knows or can do at the end of this chapter that they couldn't at the start
   
   *Transition:* One sentence on how this chapter sets up the next
   
   *Estimated length:* [X,XXX words]

3. **CONCLUSION OUTLINE (100-150 words)**
   - Synthesis: How the core argument has been proven across all chapters
   - Call to action: The specific thing you want readers to do after finishing
   - Lasting image or idea: The one thing they'll remember
   - Circle back: How the conclusion echoes the introduction's opening hook

4. **MANUSCRIPT STRUCTURE SUMMARY**
   - Total chapters: [#]
   - Average chapter length: [X,XXX words]
   - Total estimated word count: [XX,000 words]
   - Sample chapter note: [Which chapter would you provide as the sample chapter? Typically Chapter 1 or the strongest standalone chapter]

Format as a clean proposal document section. Use headers and clear structure — agents often skim outlines looking for the story arc.
```

---

## Pro Tips

- **On chapter titles:** The best chapter titles are either bold claims ("Everything You Know About Motivation Is Wrong") or intriguing questions ("Why Smart People Do Dumb Things"). Avoid topic labels ("Chapter 3: Motivation").
- **On evidence anchors:** One strong story per chapter is better than 10 statistics. Agents are looking for narrative — even in prescriptive books.
- **On word counts:** Unevenness is fine (some chapters are naturally longer). But if one chapter is 15,000 words and another is 2,000, you may have a structural problem.
- **On the sample chapter:** Choose the chapter that best showcases your writing AND your core argument. Not the first chapter you wrote — the best one you have.
