# Writing Style Guide — Contractor Audience

## Core Principles

- **Lead with money or risk** — open with the dollar figure, the business impact, or the threat. Never bury the lede.
- **Active voice always** — passive voice signals AI. Rewrite every passive sentence.
- **Address the reader as "you"** — they're a business owner, not a trade hobbyist.
- **One actionable takeaway per section** — if a section doesn't tell them what to DO, rewrite it.
- **Numbers beat adjectives** — "saves 4 hours/week" not "dramatically reduces admin time."
- **Short paragraphs** — 2–3 sentences max. Contractors read on mobile between jobs.
- **Single CTA to close** — one clear action at the end. Not three options.

## Language Rules

**Use their vocabulary:**
- flat-rate pricing, service call fee, callback rate, dispatch, ticket average, close rate
- gross margin, overhead recovery, labor burden, material markup
- field tech, office manager, owner-operator

**Never use:**
- marketing funnel, leverage, synergy, robust, seamless, transformative
- "it's important to note", "in today's landscape", "game-changer"
- any word a contractor wouldn't use at a job site

## Structure

- Hook in the first paragraph — problem, dollar figure, or provocative fact
- H2s = one clear topic each, targeting a long-tail keyword variant where possible
- No fluff transitions (moreover, furthermore, additionally)
- End every section with the practical implication, not a summary

## The 90-Second Skim Test

If a contractor can skim in 90 seconds and still know:
1. What the problem is
2. What to do about it
3. What it's worth to them

...it passes. If not, it's too dense. Cut or rewrite.
