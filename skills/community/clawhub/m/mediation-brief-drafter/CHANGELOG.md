## [0.1.0] - 2026-05-25
Initial release. Six-phase workflow for drafting a two-part mediation brief: Phase 1 case and session intake (matter caption with confidentiality posture, jurisdiction, mediator name and mediation rules — JAMS / AAA / FINRA / private / court-annexed / EEOC / community — mediation date and session length, party roster with named representatives and authority levels, opposing counsel, prior demand and offer history, case theme in one sentence, audience analysis for the two briefs, brief-exchange convention — exchanged simultaneously / exchanged in advance / submitted only to mediator); Phase 2 SHARED brief drafting (procedural posture and confidentiality cover page, undisputed facts, disputed facts with party position, claims and defences with key authority cited, damages framework with itemised computation, key exhibits index, motions and rulings to date, opposing counsel's strongest arguments named candidly, settlement history with prior demand / offer ladder, settlement framework proposed for this session, signature block); Phase 3 CONFIDENTIAL mediator-only brief drafting (BATNA — best alternative to a negotiated agreement, named, costed, time-discounted, probability-weighted; WATNA — worst alternative, named, costed, time-discounted, probability-weighted; ZOPA hypotheses with floor / target / ceiling and the rationale anchored to BATNA / WATNA / litigation expected value; settlement authority structure — client authority ladder with named decision-maker, authority secured for the session, authority gates above which a phone call is required, presence of insurer / co-defendant / indemnitor / parent company on the authority chain; leverage and weakness candour — strengths the client will not lead with, weaknesses the opposing side has not yet exploited, evidentiary risks, expert risks, exposure on adverse rulings, regulatory or reputational risks, key witness-credibility issues; impasse-breaker proposals — bracket proposals, mediator's proposal request, structured-settlement options, non-monetary terms, escrow / Mary-Carter / Pierringer / high-low arrangements where applicable, confidentiality and release language preferences); Phase 4 do-not-share audit (rule-against-disclosure check against the mediation rules and the controlling mediation-confidentiality statute / common-law privilege — Federal Rule of Evidence 408, Uniform Mediation Act if adopted in the jurisdiction, state-specific mediation privilege, EU Mediation Directive 2008/52/EC and national transpositions, contractual confidentiality, court-annexed-mediation order), redaction checklist applied to the SHARED brief, dual-audience separation verified, attorney work-product carve-out flagged); Phase 5 settlement-authority worksheet (authority confirmed in writing pre-session, named decision-maker reachable during session, escalation protocol, opening number, target number, walk-away number, non-monetary deal terms ranked, signed-term-sheet template ready, post-mediation execution plan); Phase 6 assembly — produces a DRAFT brief pair (SHARED + CONFIDENTIAL) with redaction checklist, settlement-authority worksheet, BATNA / WATNA / ZOPA appendix, dual-audience separation audit, and an unresolved-information list — for licensed counsel review before any exchange with the opposing party or transmission to the mediator. Never transmits the brief, never represents the client, never decides settlement authority, never opines on the strength or weakness of the case as a legal conclusion, never substitutes for licensed counsel's judgement, and never breaches the mediation-confidentiality privilege.
