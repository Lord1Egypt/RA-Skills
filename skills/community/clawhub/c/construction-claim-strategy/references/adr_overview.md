# ADR in Construction Claims — Overview & Strategies

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) provides ways to resolve disputes without going to full court litigation. Choosing the right method depends on your goals: speed, cost, finality, cash flow, and relationship preservation.

---

## ADR Methods Comparison

| Method | Description | Binding? | Speed | Cost | Best Used When |
|--------|-------------|----------|-------|------|---------------|
| **Negotiation** | Direct discussion between parties | No | Fast | Low | Early stage, good relationship |
| **Mediation** | Neutral facilitator helps reach settlement | No (usually) | Fast | Low–Medium | Preserve relationship, complex issues |
| **Adjudication** | Fast interim decision by adjudicator | Yes (usually interim/temporary) | Very Fast | Medium | Cash flow or interim relief needed |
| **Arbitration** | Private tribunal makes final decision | Yes (final) | Medium–Slow | High | Final resolution, international disputes |
| **Litigation** | Court proceedings | Yes (final) | Slow | High | Last resort, complex legal points |

---

## ADR Strategy Recommendations

1. **Start with negotiation** where possible — many disputes can be resolved early if approached professionally
2. **Use mediation** when preserving the commercial relationship is important or when issues are complex
3. **Consider adjudication** (where available) for fast interim cash flow or time-related decisions
4. **Choose arbitration** for final, binding resolution, especially in international projects
5. **Use litigation** generally as a last resort due to cost, time, and relationship impact

---

## Adjudication vs Arbitration — Detailed Comparison

| Aspect | Adjudication | Arbitration |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Nature** | Fast interim decision | Final and binding decision |
| **Speed** | Very fast (usually 28–42 days in statutory regimes) | Slow to medium (6–24 months) |
| **Cost** | Relatively low | High |
| **Binding** | Binding until finally determined by arbitration or litigation | Final and binding |
| **Enforceability** | Enforceable in jurisdictions with statutory adjudication | Enforceable internationally (New York Convention) |
| **Right of appeal** | Limited (usually only jurisdiction or natural justice) | Limited, depends on seat of arbitration |
| **Procedure** | Relatively informal and fast | More formal, similar to litigation |
| **Evidence** | Often limited written submissions | Full hearing with witnesses and experts |
| **Relationship impact** | Can be less damaging if used early | Often more adversarial |

### When to Choose Adjudication

- Need **quick cash flow** relief
- Want to keep the project moving during dispute resolution
- Dispute is relatively straightforward
- Available under a statutory regime or contract

### When to Choose Arbitration

- Need a **final and binding** decision
- Dispute is complex, high-value, or multi-issue
- International enforceability matters
- Parties can choose arbitrators with relevant expertise

---

## Decision Guide

| Your Goal | Recommended Route |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Need money / relief quickly | **Adjudication** (if available) |
| Want a final and binding decision | **Arbitration** |
| Want to preserve commercial relationship | Start with **Negotiation → Mediation** |
| High-value, complex, international dispute | **Arbitration** |
| Domestic dispute needing fast interim relief | **Adjudication** |

## Common Law vs Civil Law Perspective

| Aspect | Common Law | Civil Law |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Adjudication** | Very common (often statutory) | Less common (more voluntary) |
| **Arbitration** | Widely used | Very widely used |
| **Preference** | Often adjudication first, then arbitration | Tend to go straight to arbitration or mediation |
| **Statutory support** | Strong statutory regimes for adjudication | Generally rely on contractual ADR clauses |
