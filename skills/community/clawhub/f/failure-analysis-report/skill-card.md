## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a licensed forensic engineer (PE/SE/ME/EE), expert witness, or engineering consultant needs to draft a failure analysis report for an insurance claim, litigation matter, or safety investigation. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External forensic engineers, expert witnesses, and engineering consultants use this skill to turn investigation findings into a structured draft failure analysis report for insurance claims, litigation matters, safety investigations, and product or construction defect reviews. The draft remains subject to licensed Professional Engineer review, revision, stamp, and signature before delivery. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: A draft report could be mistaken for a final engineering opinion. <br>
Mitigation: Require licensed PE review, revision, stamp, and signature before delivery to any attorney, insurer, court, regulator, or client. <br>
Risk: Missing measurements, test results, or observations could lead to unsupported conclusions. <br>
Mitigation: Keep unavailable values explicitly flagged and require the engineer to supply all quantitative findings and physical evidence observations. <br>
Risk: Using the wrong code or standard edition can undermine the analysis. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm the edition in effect at the time of construction, manufacture, or installation; flag unknown editions for PE confirmation instead of substituting current standards. <br>
Risk: Evidence handling or chain-of-custody gaps may affect admissibility or report reliability. <br>
Mitigation: Document evidence disposition, preservation status, destructive testing notice, and chain-of-custody gaps in the limitations and open-items sections. <br>
