# Growth Playbook Reference

For `/growth-strategy`, `/engagement-audit`, and any positioning question.

## Positioning archetypes

Before any cadence advice, pin down which archetype the user is building. Most accounts blend two; one should dominate.

### A. Builder
Shipping receipts. Repos, deployments, screenshots, milestones. Authority comes from output.
- Strong: founders mid-build, ICs at protocols.
- Content mix: 60% build updates, 20% takes on what they're learning, 10% memes, 10% replies.

### B. Founder / public CEO
Vision, strategy, narrative-shaping. Less code, more direction. Authority comes from being trusted to call shots.
- Strong: post-pmf founders, raisers, ecosystem leaders.
- Mix: 40% takes, 30% receipts (numbers, growth), 20% community, 10% memes.

### C. Analyst / researcher
Data, charts, frameworks, breakdowns. Authority comes from being right consistently.
- Strong: trader-builders, on-chain detectives, narrative writers.
- Mix: 50% analysis, 20% takes, 20% commentary on others' analysis, 10% memes.

### D. Degen / trader
Trades, calls, cycle commentary. Authority comes from PnL and timing.
- Strong: anons with screenshot wallets, cycle veterans.
- Mix: 40% market commentary, 30% memes, 20% calls (carefully), 10% takes.

### E. Insider / curator
"You should know about X." Surfacing alpha, builders, projects. Authority comes from access and taste.
- Strong: investors, scouts, ecosystem leads.
- Mix: 50% spotlights, 20% takes, 20% replies, 10% memes.

### F. Meme account
Pure culture. Authority is the meme cred itself.
- Specialist niche. Hard to convert to non-meme later.

### G. Educator
Threads, explainers, how-tos. Authority from clarity.
- High follower growth, low founder cred. Mix with another archetype for power.

## Content mix rules

For most user types, the 70/20/10 rule works:

- **70% culture / craft / commentary** — things that aren't about your product.
- **20% insight / receipts / build progress** — earned authority content.
- **10% explicit product / link / CTA** — the ask.

Accounts that flip this ratio (70% product) plateau or shrink. Even great products can't out-shill a bad ratio.

## Posting cadence

| Stage | Cadence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0–500 followers | 3–5 originals/day, 10–20 replies/day | Volume + reply-guy under bigger accounts |
| 500–5k | 2–4 originals/day, 5–10 replies/day | Refine voice. Find your 1–2 content pillars. |
| 5k–25k | 1–3 originals/day, 5+ replies/day | Quality > quantity. One viral/week >> daily mediocrity. |
| 25k+ | 1–2 originals/day, selective replies | Each post sets brand. Every weak post costs. |

## Active CT windows (UTC)

CT is global but Solana CT skews US + LATAM heavy with strong Asia overnight. Active windows:

- **13:00–17:00 UTC** — US east coast morning, EU end-of-day. Peak engagement.
- **20:00–00:00 UTC** — US west coast afternoon, prime degen hours.
- **01:00–04:00 UTC** — Asia waking up. Builder-heavy.

Worst window: **05:00–11:00 UTC** for US-targeted growth. Best for Asia-targeted.

Post 2–3 windows/day, not all in one cluster. Schedule with the user's audience in mind, not the user's timezone.

## Reply strategy

Replies under big accounts are the cheapest follower acquisition channel. Rules:

1. **Target ladder.** Reply under accounts 5–50x your size. Same-size = no leverage. Massive accounts = drowned.
2. **First-hour reply.** Reply within 60 minutes of OP for visibility. After hour 3, reply is mostly dead.
3. **Add a layer.** See viral-hooks.md "Reply patterns." Don't agree-and-emoji.
4. **Don't shill in replies.** Replies are credit-building, not direct-marketing. Save the ask for your own posts and DMs.
5. **Reply pods are dead.** Don't waste time in coordinated like/reply groups. Algorithm sees through them and they kill organic reach.

## Engagement farming — what's legit, what's cope

**Legit engagement tactics:**
- Strong hooks
- Question posts that filter for your audience
- Hot takes you'd defend in person
- Threads that earn the open
- Memes that resonate
- Replying fast under accounts you respect

**Cope tactics (don't bother):**
- "Like if you agree, RT if you disagree"
- Vague "thoughts?" posts
- Tagging 10 big accounts in a reply
- Buying engagement (algo penalizes and CT smells it)
- Spam-DMing for follows
- "Following everyone who likes this"

## DM and outreach

Cold DMs work for partnerships, deal flow, hiring. Not for follows.

Cold DM template (anti-template, just principles):
- One specific reason for outreach in line one.
- Show you've done homework — reference a recent ship, tweet, or post.
- Make the ask small and specific. Not "let's collab" — "would you be open to 15 min next week on X."
- No pitch deck unsolicited. No "great connecting!"

## Identity & positioning fixes

When auditing an account, these are the high-leverage fixes:

### Profile

- **Bio:** 2 lines max. Format: [what you do] · [proof or affiliation] · [contact / link]. No emoji rainbows. No "🇨🇷 | DeFi | Web3 | Builder | Dad |"
- **Pinned tweet:** Your single best receipt or your best thread. Update monthly.
- **PFP:** Anon (PFP NFT or custom illustration) or doxxed face. Either works. Half-anon (logo with a face peeking) reads as unsure.
- **Header:** Optional. If used, communicate one thing.
- **Handle:** Short, pronounceable, brandable. If long, ship a memorable nickname people can refer to you by.

### Pillars

Pick 2 content pillars and stick to them for 30+ days. "Solana growth" and "memes" is too broad. "Building gns on solana" and "founder lessons from building solo" is tight.

### Naming the thing

If the user is building a product, decide which of these to talk about:
- the product
- the category
- the user pain
- the personal arc of building it

Each is a valid pillar. Doing all 4 randomly looks like noise.

## Engagement audit prescription template

When outputting an audit, end with this exact structure:

```
== AUDIT PRESCRIPTION ==

KEEP DOING
- [thing]
- [thing]

STOP DOING
- [thing]
- [thing]

START DOING (next 7 days)
1. [specific action with example post]
2. [specific action with example post]
3. [specific action with example post]

METRIC TO WATCH
- [the one number that tells you if this is working in 14 days]
```

One metric only. Not five. Pick the one that matters most for the user's archetype.

## Growth psychology

What actually grows accounts on CT:

1. **Consistency over peaks.** 6 months of 2 posts/day > 1 viral tweet and quiet.
2. **One pillar held publicly.** People follow you for one specific reason, then stay for the rest.
3. **Receipts.** Anything that proves you're real. Ship logs, charts, screenshots.
4. **Friendships > followers.** 30 founders who actually know you beats 30k passive followers.
5. **Showing up under others' wins.** Congratulate genuinely, often. CT rewards graciousness more than people admit.
6. **Saying the thing nobody else will.** Calibrated contrarianism. Be wrong out loud occasionally — it humanizes.

What doesn't:

- Follower-for-follower
- Trying to be every archetype
- Posting only when you have something to sell
- Beefing for attention (you'll win followers and lose deal flow)
- Apologizing for hot takes after the fact
