# Crisis Comms Holding Statement

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Public Relations

## Purpose

Produces a fast, legally cautious holding statement set when a crisis breaks — before all facts are known. Generates tiered statements for internal, customer, press, and social channels, plus a spokesperson Q&A briefing, stakeholder notification timeline, and red-flag claims checklist that flags wording that could create legal or reputational exposure.

## When to Use

- A breach, outage, product recall, safety incident, executive misconduct event, regulatory action, or social-media flare-up has just occurred
- The comms team must publish a first-response statement within ~60 minutes
- A leadership team needs a unified message across channels while investigation continues
- A spokesperson needs a Q&A briefing for an inbound press call
- Pre-baking a holding-statement template for incident playbooks and tabletop exercises

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Incident Intake**
1. Captures incident type, what is confirmed vs. unknown, time of detection, and exposure scope
2. Identifies affected stakeholders, regulatory triggers, and the designated spokesperson
3. Surfaces any active legal hold, law-enforcement involvement, or embargo that constrains the statement

**Phase 2: Statement Design**
4. Drafts tiered statements: internal team, customers, press, and social media — each calibrated to channel and audience
5. Builds a spokesperson Q&A briefing with anticipated tough questions and approved responses
6. Produces a stakeholder notification timeline (T+0 / T+1h / T+4h / T+24h)

**Phase 3: Red-Flag Review**
7. Runs every draft through a red-flag claims checklist that flags admission of liability, premature cause attribution, speculative numbers, or apology language with legal exposure
8. Produces a final output with statements, briefing, timeline, red-flag report, and a hand-off checklist for legal and executive sign-off

## Output

A crisis comms packet with: incident summary, four tiered holding statements (internal, customer, press, social), spokesperson Q&A briefing, stakeholder notification timeline, red-flag claims report, and a legal-and-executive sign-off checklist. Every confirmed fact is sourced from the user; every unknown is explicitly flagged.

## Notes

The skill never invents incident facts, victim counts, root-cause attribution, regulator names, or timelines. If a detail is not confirmed by the user, the draft uses an explicit `[UNCONFIRMED]` flag rather than filler. The skill is not a substitute for legal counsel; every draft must clear legal sign-off before release. Confidential incident details, victim identities, embargoed regulator communications, and any personally identifiable information shared during the session must not be used in examples, tool calls, or external searches.
