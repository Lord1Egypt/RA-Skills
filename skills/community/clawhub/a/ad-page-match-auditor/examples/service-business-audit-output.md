# Example Output

## Verdict

Partial match. The ad promises a fast emergency HVAC repair, but the page opens with a general company headline and asks visitors to browse service categories before requesting help.

## Conversion Path

- Ad promise: same-day emergency AC repair in Austin.
- Page promise: general HVAC, heating, cooling, and maintenance services.
- Requested action: call or fill out a generic contact form.
- Likely drop-off: the visitor clicked for urgent AC repair but lands on a broad service page that does not immediately confirm availability, service area, or next step.

## Message-Match Gaps

- Service: ad says emergency AC repair; page says general HVAC.
- Urgency: ad says same-day; page does not repeat timing.
- Geography: ad targets Austin; page hides service area below the fold.
- CTA: ad implies immediate help; page says "Contact Us."
- Proof: page has reviews, but they are not placed near the emergency CTA.

## Conversion Risks

1. The first screen does not repeat the urgent repair promise.
2. The form asks for too much before confirming same-day availability.
3. The CTA is generic and does not match the visitor's immediate need.
4. Reviews are present but not tied to the advertised service.

## Variant Plan

### Variant 1: Emergency Match

- Hypothesis: Repeating the exact urgent AC repair promise above the fold will increase call starts.
- Headline: Same-Day AC Repair in Austin
- CTA: Call for emergency repair
- Page change: Move service-area and availability proof into the first screen.
- Measurement target: phone calls from paid traffic.

### Variant 2: Form Friction

- Hypothesis: A shorter form will increase repair requests from mobile visitors.
- Headline: Need AC repair today?
- CTA: Get a repair time
- Page change: Reduce the form to name, phone, ZIP code, and issue.
- Measurement target: completed form submissions.

### Variant 3: Proof Near CTA

- Hypothesis: Putting emergency-repair reviews beside the CTA will increase confidence.
- Headline: Austin AC repair when your system quits
- CTA: Request same-day help
- Page change: Add 2-3 relevant repair reviews near the first CTA.
- Measurement target: CTA clicks and calls.

## Measurement Plan

- Primary: calls and completed repair requests from paid traffic.
- Secondary: CTA clicks, form starts, and mobile scroll depth.
- Caveat: if traffic is low, review directionally over a month and test larger page changes instead of small copy tweaks.

## Next Step

Build one emergency-specific landing page and point the highest-intent ad set to it before testing smaller copy changes.
