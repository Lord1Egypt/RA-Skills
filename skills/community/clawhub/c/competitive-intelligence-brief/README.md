# competitive-intelligence-brief

Drafts a structured competitive intelligence brief from monitoring data, news signals, pricing changes, and win/loss notes — synthesizing recent competitor moves into actionable insights for sales, product, marketing, and executive audiences.

## Use When

A competitive intelligence analyst, product marketer, strategy professional, or sales enablement lead needs to turn raw monitoring signals into a structured CI brief. Useful for recurring monthly or quarterly competitive digests, reactive briefs triggered by a competitor announcement, or win/loss analysis after a competitive deal cycle.

## What It Produces

A DRAFT CI brief containing:

- Executive summary (key moves, top implications, most urgent action)
- Competitor snapshots table (tier, segment, current positioning, recent activity)
- Recent moves inventory per competitor (product, GTM, org, partnerships, funding, customer signals)
- Positioning analysis paragraphs (confirmed facts vs. inferred strategy)
- Win/loss synthesis with connections to competitor moves
- Battlecard update flags (DRAFT counter-messages for product marketing review)
- Strategic implications and recommendations (sales / product / marketing / executive)
- Source index with confirmed vs. inferred signal labels
- Analyst review block with distribution sensitivity reminder

## Not a Substitute For

- Legal review of competitive claims before external use or marketing materials
- Product marketing sign-off on battlecard counter-messages
- Primary market research or customer interviews
- Formal win/loss research programs with statistically significant sample sizes

## Scope

Works for any B2B or B2C software, technology, or services context. Supports single-competitor deep dives, segment-level competitive landscapes, and market-wide monitoring digests. Output is internal-use DRAFT only — requires analyst review and appropriate sign-offs before distribution.
