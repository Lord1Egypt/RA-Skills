# Compliance And Platform Boundaries

This reference is for policy-sensitive advice. It is not legal advice. For regulated industries, advise checking Meta's current policies and local law before launch.

## Meta Policy Checks

Before writing ads or recommending targeting, check whether the offer touches:

- Housing, real estate, mortgages, home loans, home insurance, housing repairs, or related opportunities.
- Employment, recruiting, certification, jobs, internships, or career opportunities.
- Financial products and services, including credit, loans, banking, insurance, investing, debt, or long-term financing.
- Social issues, elections, politics, public policy, or advocacy.
- Health, wellness, body image, medical treatments, supplements, addictions, mental health, or personal hardship.
- Legal, tax, immigration, debt, gambling, crypto, or other regulated services.

If yes, warn that targeting, claims, creative, and verification may be restricted.

## Personal Attributes

Avoid ad copy that asserts or implies sensitive personal attributes about the viewer.

Safer framing:

- "For business owners who want more booked consultations..."
- "A planning session for clinics looking to reduce no-shows..."
- "Learn how some teams approach..."

Riskier framing:

- "Are you broke?"
- "Do you have anxiety?"
- "Are you overweight?"
- "Are you in debt?"
- "Are you struggling because of your age/religion/health status?"

Shift from "you are X" to "people/businesses dealing with X often want Y."

## Claims And Proof

Do not fabricate:

- Testimonials
- Revenue screenshots
- Guarantees
- Scarcity
- Case studies
- Certifications
- Press mentions

Qualify outcomes:

- Mention effort, fit, sample size, and context when needed.
- Prefer "helped X achieve Y" over universal promises.
- Avoid guaranteed income, health, or financial outcomes unless legally approved and fully substantiated.

## Privacy And Consent

When discussing tracking:

- Respect consent, privacy rules, platform terms, and data-minimization expectations.
- Do not recommend bypassing Apple ATT, browser privacy protections, or user opt-outs.
- Use CAPI and CRM feedback for measurement/optimization, not as a privacy workaround.
- Keep privacy policy and data-use disclosures current.

## Instant Forms And Data

Lead forms often require privacy-policy links and clear consent language. Ask only for data the business needs and can protect.

Sensitive information should usually be collected on a secure website or later in the sales process, not casually inside a low-friction form.

## Platform-Dependent Advice

Flag current UI uncertainty:

- Ads Manager labels and defaults change.
- Advantage+ settings can vary by account, objective, region, and eligibility.
- Some features require CRM/CAPI integrations, event volume, or account eligibility.
- Regional rules may differ, especially for EU users and special categories.

Do not promise a specific button name unless verified against current Ads Manager or official docs during the task.
