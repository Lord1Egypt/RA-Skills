Individuals, families, financial planners, and insurance professionals use this skill to structure insurance planning conversations, compare policy options, interpret policy terms, and prepare claims-related checklists. ## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Insurance planning can involve sensitive personal, medical, and financial information. <br>
Mitigation: Share only the information necessary for the task and avoid unnecessary identifiers or private documents. <br>
Risk: Generated insurance guidance may be incomplete, outdated, or unsuitable for a specific policy or jurisdiction. <br>
Mitigation: Verify product details against official insurer documents and treat outputs as educational guidance, not binding financial or legal advice. <br>
Risk: Hong Kong insurance scenarios may involve cross-border legal, currency, and regulatory considerations. <br>
Mitigation: Review official rules and consult qualified professionals before relying on cross-border insurance recommendations. <br>
