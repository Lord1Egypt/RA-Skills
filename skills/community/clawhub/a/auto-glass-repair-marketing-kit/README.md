## The Problem With Generic AI for Auto Glass Marketing

Every auto glass shop using ChatGPT or a generic AI tool is generating content that:

- Says "safe to drive immediately" without verifying adhesive cure time (AGSC Standard 150 violation)
- Advertises "we handle everything" without disclosing ADAS recalibration — a NHTSA-flagged safety issue
- Uses "no out-of-pocket" or "we pay your deductible" — **explicitly illegal in Nevada** (NRS 686A.2825; DOI enforcement)
- Claims "OEM glass" while installing aftermarket — federal false advertising (FTC Act Section 5)
- Runs review incentives without FTC 2023 disclosure requirements

None of these are edge cases. They're daily violations in auto glass marketing. And no general AI tool knows any of these gates exist.

## The 7 Compliance Gates

| # | Gate | Why It Matters |
|---|------|----------------|
| 1 | **AGSC/AGRSS Certification** [ANCHOR] | Safe drive-away claims require certified adhesive cure time verification; NRS 598 deceptive trade if misrepresented |
| 2 | **ADAS Recalibration Disclosure** | Life-safety issue; NHTSA-flagged; "complete service" claim without disclosure = deceptive |
| 3 | **Nevada Deductible Waiver (NRS 686A.2825)** | "No out-of-pocket" = insurance fraud; Nevada DOI actively enforcing; criminal referral exposure |
| 4 | **Nevada Contractor Licensing (NRS 624/598)** | NSCB C-8 license required; license number required on advertising; NRS 598.0915 deceptive claims |
| 5 | **OEM vs. Aftermarket Glass (FMVSS 205)** | "OEM glass" without true OEM source = FTC Act Section 5 false advertising |
| 6 | **Insurance Anti-Steering (NRS 686A.220)** | No "preferred by insurer" without documented agreement; no guaranteed coverage claims |
| 7 | **FTC 2023 Endorsements + Safety Claims** | No review gating; no incentivized reviews without disclosure; no unsubstantiated "100% safe" claims |
