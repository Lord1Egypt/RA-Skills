# Toxic Chinese Herb Poisoning Prevention

## When to Use
- A toxic Chinese herb is prescribed or already in use
- Patient exhibits symptoms suggestive of herbal toxicity
- Conducting safety review for clinical protocols involving toxic herbs
- Evaluating medication errors or adverse events related to traditional Chinese medicine

## Core Workflow

### Step 1: Confirm Toxicity Level
Determine the herb’s official toxicity classification:
- **大毒 (Highly toxic)**: Causes severe organ damage or death (e.g., raw Chuan Wu, raw Cao Wu, Ma Qian Zi)
- **有毒 (Toxic)**: Can cause serious harm with overdose or prolonged use (e.g., Fu Zi, Wu Gong, Xiong Huang)
- **小毒 (Mildly toxic)**: Mild symptoms, no organ damage, non-fatal (e.g., Wu Zhu Yu, Xi Xin, Ku Xing Ren)

> Only proceed if the herb is classified as 大毒, 有毒, or 小毒. Do not apply to non-toxic herbs.

### Step 2: Investigate Potential Causes of Poisoning
Check for any of the following seven common causes:
1. **Excessive dosage** – Dose exceeds standard limits
2. **Prolonged use** – Long-term administration leading to accumulation (e.g., Zhu Sha causing renal or neurological damage)
3. **Improper processing** – Use of unprocessed (raw) toxic herbs instead of properly炮制 forms
4. **Incompatible combinations** – Violation of “Eighteen Incompatibilities” (十八反) or “Nineteen Antagonisms” (十九畏), or dangerous interactions with Western drugs (e.g., Zhu Sha + iodides)
5. **Faulty preparation** – Incorrect formulation (e.g., alcohol extraction increasing aconite toxicity) or insufficient decoction time (e.g., Fu Zi must be decocted 45–60 minutes first)
6. **Uncontrolled topical use** – Large-area or long-duration external application causing systemic absorption
7. **Misuse or misidentification** – Self-collection, substitution errors, or clinical dispensing mistakes

### Step 3: Apply Prevention Measures
- Verify herb identity, toxicity grade, and special handling requirements
- Adjust dose and duration based on patient constitution and disease severity
- Monitor closely for early signs of toxicity during treatment
- Immediately discontinue use and initiate emergency response if toxicity is suspected

> Trigger alert for dose adjustment, treatment discontinuation, or urgent medical intervention if risk is confirmed.
