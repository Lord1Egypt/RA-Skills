## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Advertising budget or strategy recommendations may be treated as final decisions without review. <br>
Mitigation: Use the recommendations as planning input and review them before changing spend, campaign settings, or channel strategy. <br>
Risk: Weak or conflicting evidence can lead to overconfident campaign guidance. <br>
Mitigation: State evidence limitations, rank conflicting hypotheses by evidence strength and recency, and include confidence notes before recommending action. <br>
Risk: Incomplete inputs can produce generic or poorly scoped research output. <br>
Mitigation: Require the research question, hypothesis set, and decision deadline, and ask only for missing required fields before proceeding. <br>
Risk: Fabricated metrics or policy outcomes could mislead advertising decisions. <br>
Mitigation: Separate observed facts from assumptions, avoid fabricated metrics or policy claims, and use measurable language for proposed actions. <br>
