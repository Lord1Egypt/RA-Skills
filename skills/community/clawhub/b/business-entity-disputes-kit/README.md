## The Problem With AI-Generated Business Litigation Marketing

Every AI tool writes business litigation content trained on Delaware LLC law, general common law piercing standards, and 2-year fraudulent transfer SOLs. For Nevada attorneys, this creates marketing that:

- Applies Delaware member-managed fiduciary duties where Nevada default imposes none
- Misses NRS 78.138(7)'s near-absolute director protection (strongest BJR in the US)
- Ignores the Clark County Business Court (faster scheduling, commercial expertise)
- Applies wrong SOLs (2 years, not 4) to fraudulent transfer claims under NRS 112
- Cites general alter ego factors instead of Nevada's codified NRS 78.747 analysis

This skill corrects all seven categories — pre-loaded with Nevada statutory citations.

## The 7 Moats at a Glance

| Moat | What AI Gets Wrong | Nevada Reality |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| NRS 78.138(7) BJR | Delaware BJR = rebuttable presumption | Nevada BJR = statutory near-immunity (intentional misconduct required) |
| NRS 86.286 duties | All LLC members owe fiduciary duties | Only managers owe duties by default; members do not |
| NRS 86.296 waiver | OA can limit but not eliminate duties | Nevada OA can *fully eliminate* fiduciary duties |
| NRS 78.747 alter ego | Common law factors (varies by state) | Codified 5-factor test with heightened plaintiff burden |
| NRS 112 UVTA | 2-year SOL for fraudulent transfer | 4-year SOL from transfer date; statutory badge list |
| NRS 78.347 deadlock | Dissolution available if deadlocked | Irreparable injury required; dissolution disfavored |
| Business Court | General district court filing | Department 32, Clark County — 60-day CMC, commercial judges |
