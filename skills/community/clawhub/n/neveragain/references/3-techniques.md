## The Digital Warfare Toolbox

### 1. Twitter-First Strategy
#NeverAgain didn't use Facebook as their primary channel. They chose Twitter because it's where journalists, politicians, and activists intersect. Every tweet was a potential news story.

**How to do it:**
- Short, punchy, authentic — never sound like a press release
- Tag journalists you want to cover you
- Reply to attacks publicly (don't DM — make it visible)
- Use the hashtag as a searchable archive of your movement
- Post at 1 AM when you can't sleep — that's when the BBC calls

> **Case: Lauren vs. Melania Trump** (Chapter 5): Lauren tweeted at First Lady Melania Trump about her anti-cyberbullying campaign after Trump Jr. liked a conspiracy theory tweet about their family. Tweet went viral. It amplified #NeverAgain to millions who hadn't heard of it.
> **Key takeaway:** Name-drop the powerful. Call them out publicly. The tweet that feels scariest to send is often the most effective.

### 2. Meme Warfare (Not a Joke)
When Marco Rubio tried to fight back, Sarah Chadwick's single tweet ("We should change the names of AR-15s to 'Marco Rubio' because they are so easy to buy") became front-page news. One line, written by a 16-year-old, generated more coverage than any press release could.

**The formula:**
1. Identify the target's vulnerability (Rubio = NRA money)
2. Reduce to one absurd, shareable comparison
3. Let the internet do the rest

### 3. The Clap-Back Cadence

| Attack on you | Don't do | Do this |
|---|---|---|
| "You're a crisis actor" | Write a long defense post | Ignore it OR post a one-liner that mocks the absurdity |
| "You're too young to understand" | Cite your credentials | "Try debating us. Just be ready to get your ass kicked back to 1776." |
| "You got rejected from colleges" | Get defensive | Publish their advertisers' contact info |
| "You're a useful idiot of Hollywood elites" | Argue about who funds you | Point to the GoFundMe with $4M from regular people |
| "You're a communist/Nazi/Hitler Youth" | Explain why you're not | "If that's the best you've got, we're doing something right." |

### 4. The Interview Game
David did interviews at 1 AM, 4 AM, 7 AM — whatever it took. He learned to repeat the same sound bite across different networks: "We're the kids, you're the adults. Please do something."

**Rules:**
- Have ONE message per appearance. Repeat it 3 times.
- Don't answer the question they asked. Answer the question you want them to ask.
- Be boring on their terms, explosive on yours.
- Wear the same clothes. It creates visual recognition.

> **Case: The barefoot interview** (Chapter 5): Emma González appeared on national TV in her pajamas, barefoot, because she couldn't find her shoes. It was authentic. It was real. It was shared millions of times. No PR firm would have approved it. That's why it worked.

### 5. The Advertiser Lever
When Laura Ingraham mocked David on Twitter, he didn't write a rebuttal. He went to her sponsors. **Direct action on the economic incentive is faster than winning a debate.**

**Playbook:**
1. Screenshot the attack
2. Research who advertises on their platform
3. Tag those companies publicly
4. Ask: "Do you support this?"
5. Let the market decide

### 6. The GoFundMe + Big Donor Mix
The group raised $4M from small-dollar donors on GoFundMe AND accepted large contributions from Spielberg, Bloomberg, Clooney. Both worked. **Don't let critics box you into a purity test about funding sources. Take money from wherever aligns with your mission.**
