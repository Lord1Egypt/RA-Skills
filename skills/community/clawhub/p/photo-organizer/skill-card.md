## Description: <br>
Photo Organizer helps users organize local photo collections by capture date and limited location-derived folders, with preview and undo-oriented workflows. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

MIT <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users and developers use this skill to prepare shell commands and guidance for organizing large local photo folders into dated output directories while preserving originals. It is suited for personal photo cleanup, phone photo exports, and archive preparation where users can preview changes before copying files. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Documentation overstates location sorting, tagging, and one-click undo behavior for this version. <br>
Mitigation: Use preview mode first and verify the resulting folder plan before running the organizer on important photo collections. <br>
Risk: Incorrect input or output directories could copy photos into an unexpected location or make undo behavior confusing. <br>
Mitigation: Choose input and output folders carefully and keep an independent backup of important photos before running the tool. <br>
Risk: Package-install instructions may not correspond exactly to the reviewed local script. <br>
Mitigation: Verify any pip package before installing; the reviewed evidence covers the local artifact script. <br>
