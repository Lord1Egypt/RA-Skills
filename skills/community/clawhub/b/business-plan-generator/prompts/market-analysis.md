# Prompt: Market Analysis + Competitive Landscape

Generate a thorough market analysis section for a business plan, including TAM/SAM/SOM sizing, customer persona, and a competitive landscape matrix.

---

## Your Input

```
Business Name: [e.g., FitTrack]
Industry: [e.g., fitness technology / SaaS]
Product/Service: [brief description, e.g., "client management + workout tracking platform for personal trainers"]
Target Geography: [e.g., United States, North America, Global]
Target Customer Segment: [e.g., "independent personal trainers, 1-5 clients each"]
Known Competitors: [list 3-5, e.g., "Trainerize, MyPTHub, ABC Trainerize, Google Sheets"]
Your Key Differentiator: [what makes you different or better?]
Price Point: [your pricing vs. competitors]
```

---

## Prompt

You are a market research analyst and business strategist. Write a detailed market analysis section for the following business.

**Business:** [Business Name]
**Industry:** [Industry]
**Product/Service:** [Product/Service]
**Geography:** [Target Geography]
**Target Segment:** [Target Customer Segment]
**Competitors:** [Known Competitors]
**Differentiator:** [Key Differentiator]
**Price Point:** [Price Point]

Write a market analysis section with the following components:

---

### 1. Market Overview (150-200 words)
Describe the overall market landscape. Include:
- Market size and growth rate (use realistic industry figures with sources cited as "[Source]")
- Key macro trends driving demand (3-4 trends)
- Why this market is attractive now

### 2. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis
Define each level with specific numbers and methodology:

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):** The full global/national market for this problem
- State the dollar value and how you calculated it (top-down from industry reports OR bottom-up from unit economics)

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** The portion [Business Name] can realistically serve given geography, segment, and product scope
- State the dollar value and the filter criteria that reduces it from TAM

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** What [Business Name] can capture in Years 1-3
- State the dollar value, target market share %, and the assumptions behind it

### 3. Target Customer Persona
Create one detailed persona representing the primary buyer:
- **Name:** [fictional name]
- **Demographics:** age, location, income, occupation
- **Role:** their job/business context
- **Pain Points:** top 3 problems they face that [Business Name] solves
- **Current Solution:** what they use today and why it falls short
- **Buying Trigger:** what event or realization makes them look for a new solution
- **Decision Criteria:** what matters most when evaluating options (price, features, ease of use, support, etc.)

### 4. Competitive Landscape

Create a comparison matrix table with these columns:
| Competitor | Price/Month | Key Features | Strengths | Weaknesses |

Include [Business Name] as the final row. Use the information provided plus realistic knowledge of the industry.

After the table, write a 150-word competitive analysis paragraph covering:
- Where [Business Name] wins
- The primary competitive threat and how to counter it
- Any "white space" (underserved segments or use cases)

### 5. Market Trends (3-5 bullet points)
List specific, data-backed trends that support the market opportunity. Each bullet = 1 trend + 1 sentence of relevance to [Business Name].

---

**Tone:** Analytical, credible, data-informed. Write for a sophisticated investor who knows how to spot inflated market numbers.  
**Format:** Use the section headers above. Include the comparison table as a markdown table.
