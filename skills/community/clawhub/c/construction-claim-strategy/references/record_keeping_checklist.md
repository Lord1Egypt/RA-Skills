# Record Keeping Checklist

Good record keeping is one of the most critical success factors in construction claims.

Use this checklist throughout the project and when preparing claims.

---

**Project:** _______________________________ **Date:** _______________

## A. Contractual Notices & Formal Correspondence

- [ ] All contractual notices submitted with proof of delivery/receipt
- [ ] Notice dates recorded against event dates
- [ ] All formal letters and emails organised by date
- [ ] Meeting minutes, especially those recording issues or agreements

## B. Instructions, Variations & Changes

- [ ] All instructions from the Authority/Engineer
- [ ] Variation orders and change directives
- [ ] Requests for Information (RFIs) and responses
- [ ] Revised drawings and specifications

## C. Programme & Progress Records

- [ ] Baseline programme
- [ ] All programme updates and revisions
- [ ] Weekly / monthly progress reports
- [ ] As-built records and progress photographs

## D. Daily / Site Records

- [ ] Site diaries and daily reports
- [ ] Labour allocation and deployment records
- [ ] Plant and equipment usage logs
- [ ] Material delivery and usage records
- [ ] Dated photographs and videos of site conditions

## E. Cost & Financial Records

- [ ] Invoices, payment certificates, and supporting documents
- [ ] Timesheets and payroll records
- [ ] Subcontractor and supplier claims
- [ ] Additional cost records, including preliminaries, plant, and materials

## F. General Record Keeping Standards

- [ ] Records are factual, objective, and made contemporaneously
- [ ] Records are properly filed and easy to retrieve
- [ ] Electronic backups are maintained and secure
- [ ] Key records are cross-referenced to specific events or claim items
