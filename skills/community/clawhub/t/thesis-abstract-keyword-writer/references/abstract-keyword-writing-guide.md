## Core Function of the Abstract

The abstract is a high-stakes standalone text. More readers may read the abstract than the full thesis, so it must make the study's topic, problem, method, findings, and value clear without requiring the reader to inspect the whole paper.

A strong abstract should:

- Identify the topic and problem quickly.
- State what the study aims to do.
- Briefly indicate the method or approach.
- Present the most important findings or outputs.
- Explain the conclusion, contribution, implication, or value.
- Make the central take-home message visible.

The abstract does not need to summarize every chapter. It should prioritize the central achievement or contribution.

## Evidence Boundaries

Never invent:

- Sample size, participants, instruments, reliability, validity, or statistical tests
- Findings, p values, coefficients, qualitative themes, interview quotes, or case details
- Literature citations, author names, dates, or theory claims not supplied by the user
- Practical implications not supported by the paper
- Intervention effectiveness when no intervention was implemented

If the user has not supplied enough information, use a provisional framework:

> 摘要：本文拟围绕……展开研究，旨在……。由于目前尚未提供具体研究方法、样本、结果和结论，以下摘要需在补充第四章结果和第五章结论后进一步完善。

English:

> Abstract: This study focuses on .... Since the specific method, sample, findings, and conclusions have not yet been provided, this abstract should be revised after the results and conclusion sections are available.

## Abstract Length and Timing

The abstract is normally written after the thesis is complete. If written early, it must be checked again after the paper changes.

Typical length:

- Chinese undergraduate abstract: 300-500 Chinese characters, unless the school requires another length.
- Longer Chinese thesis abstract: 500-800 Chinese characters when required.
- English abstract: often 150-250 words.
- Journal-style abstract: often 80-250 words.
- Structured abstract: length depends on required headings.

The word or character limit is not a target. Avoid filling the abstract with low-value details merely because space is available.

## Abstract Types

### Standard or Simple Abstract

One paragraph or several compact paragraphs without headings. It usually includes background/problem, purpose, method, findings, and conclusion.

### Structured Abstract

Uses headings such as:

- Background, Objective, Methods, Results, Conclusion
- Purpose, Method, Findings, Implications
- 目的、方法、结果、结论

Use structured format only when the user or school requires it.

### Abstract with Highlights or Significance Statement

Some papers include highlights or a significance statement in addition to the abstract. Treat them as adjuncts, not replacements. For a thesis, only create them if requested.

### Graphical Abstract

Not normally required for undergraduate theses. If requested, summarize the take-home message and visual structure, but do not make it part of the standard abstract unless required.

## Generic Abstract Model

Use these components selectively:

1. Significance of the topic or essential factual background
2. Challenge, problem, or gap
3. What the paper/study does
4. Method or materials
5. Results or comparison with existing results
6. Implications
7. Mapping to existing knowledge
8. Achievement, value, or contribution
9. Applications

For most thesis abstracts, use this order:

1. Background/problem
2. Purpose
3. Method
4. Results/findings
5. Conclusion/significance

For very short abstracts, combine sentence functions:

- Purpose + method
- Method + result
- Result + implication
- Achievement + contribution

## Writing from a Complete Thesis

Do not use the existing abstract as the only source. Extract from the full thesis:

| Thesis Element | Use in Abstract |
|---|---|
| Title | Core topic and keyword candidates |
| Introduction | Background, problem, aim, significance |
| Literature review | Gap and conceptual frame, only if needed |
| Methodology | Method, sample/data source, analysis |
| Results | Main findings only |
| Discussion | Meaning and contribution |
| Conclusion | Final take-home message |

Avoid:

- A chapter-by-chapter table of contents.
- Long literature review.
- Detailed procedures that do not affect the reader's decision to read.
- Detailed statistics unless they are central.
- New claims not in the thesis.

## Research-Type Patterns

### Quantitative Study

Include purpose, sample, instruments or variables, analysis, key results, and conclusion.

Template:

> 摘要：随着……，……问题逐渐受到关注。本文以……为研究对象，旨在探讨……。研究采用问卷调查法，使用……量表收集数据，并运用……方法进行分析。结果显示，……；……；……。研究表明，……。本研究可为……提供参考。

Use cautious wording for cross-sectional or correlational studies:

- "相关"
- "预测"
- "关联"
- "提示"
- "可能"

Do not write "导致" or "证明" unless the design supports causality.

### Qualitative Study

Include phenomenon, participants/data sources, method of data collection, analysis method, themes, and meaning.

Template:

> 摘要：为深入理解……，本文采用质性研究方法，以……为研究对象，通过半结构式访谈/文本分析/案例研究收集资料，并运用主题分析/编码分析进行整理。研究发现，……主要表现为……、……和……三个主题。结果表明，……。本研究有助于丰富对……的理解，并为……提供参考。

Avoid statistical generalization.

### Mixed Methods Study

Include both strands and what integration adds.

Template:

> 摘要：本文采用混合研究方法，首先通过问卷调查考察……，随后通过访谈进一步解释……。定量结果显示……；质性结果表明……。两类结果相互补充，说明……。研究为……提供了更全面的依据。

### Literature Review or Systematic Review

Include review scope, search/selection method if available, synthesis themes, and conclusion.

Template:

> 摘要：本文围绕……主题，对国内外相关研究进行梳理与分析。通过检索和整理……领域文献，研究发现现有研究主要集中在……、……和……方面，同时在……方面仍存在不足。本文在此基础上总结了……，并提出未来研究可从……方面进一步推进。

### Intervention-Scheme Design Without Implementation

Do not claim measured effectiveness.

Template:

> 摘要：针对……问题，本文基于……理论和相关文献，设计了面向……的心理干预方案。研究采用文献研究法和方案设计法，明确干预对象、目标、原则、流程和评估设想。方案包括……个主题活动，主要围绕……展开。本文仅完成方案设计，未实施干预和收集效果数据，因此不能直接判断方案效果。研究可为后续干预实践和实证检验提供参考。

### Theoretical Analysis Paper

Template:

> 摘要：本文围绕……问题展开理论分析，旨在阐明……。通过梳理相关概念和理论，本文认为……。研究进一步分析了……之间的关系，并提出……。本文有助于从……角度理解……，也为后续实证研究提供理论基础。

## Clarity and Coherence Rules

Abstracts should be easy to understand quickly.

Rules:

- Keep sentences reasonably short.
- Use consistent terms for the same concept, tool, scheme, model, or variable.
- Avoid ambiguous references such as "this", "it", or "which" when the antecedent may be unclear.
- Use direct sentence openings such as "本文", "本研究", "This study", and "The results show".
- Define necessary acronyms once; avoid acronym-heavy abstracts.
- Prefer clear academic language over ornate wording.
- Prioritize one central contribution rather than listing every minor result.

Useful Chinese transitions:

- 随着……
- 针对……
- 为探讨/理解/分析……
- 本文采用……
- 结果显示……
- 研究表明……
- 本研究可为……提供参考

## Contribution Ownership

Make it impossible for readers to miss what belongs to this study.

Use:

- 本研究发现……
- 本文通过……分析……
- 本文构建/设计/提出……
- 研究结果显示……
- This study investigates ...
- We/This thesis examines ...
- The findings show ...

Avoid ambiguous phrasing:

Weak:

> 社会支持与学习倦怠存在关系。

Stronger:

> 本研究发现，在所调查样本中，社会支持与学习倦怠呈负相关。

Weak:

> 正念训练可以降低压力。

Stronger when no intervention was implemented:

> 本文基于正念理论设计了压力管理干预方案，其实际效果仍需后续实施和评估。

## Keywords Rules

Keywords help retrieval. They should represent the core concepts of the thesis, not generic filler.

Select 3-8 keywords from:

- Core topic
- Main variables or qualitative concepts
- Target population or context
- Theory or intervention approach when central
- Research method when distinctive
- Application field

Chinese keyword rules:

- Use noun phrases, usually 2-6 Chinese characters or concise academic terms.
- Separate with semicolons or Chinese semicolons according to school format.
- Common format: `关键词：大学生；考试焦虑；认知行为疗法；干预方案`
- Avoid full sentences and overly broad words such as "研究", "影响", "问题" unless part of a fixed term.

English keyword rules:

- Use 3-8 noun phrases.
- Use semicolons or commas according to style.
- Capitalize consistently; sentence case is usually acceptable.
- Common format: `Keywords: college students; test anxiety; cognitive behavioral therapy; intervention program`

Keyword ordering:

1. Population/context
2. Core phenomenon or dependent variable
3. Main independent variable/intervention/theory
4. Method/design if important
5. Application field

Do not invent keywords that imply variables, methods, or theories not in the thesis.

## Bilingual Abstract Rules

When the user requests bilingual output:

- Write the Chinese abstract first if the thesis is Chinese, followed by English Abstract.
- Keep Chinese and English abstracts equivalent in content, not necessarily word-for-word.
- Keep terms consistent across languages.
- Translate keywords as academic terms, not literal fragments.
- Do not add findings to one language version that are absent from the other.

Suggested format:

```markdown
摘要：……

关键词：……；……；……

Abstract: ...

Keywords: ...; ...; ...
```

## Common Problems and Fixes

Problem: The abstract reads like a table of contents.

Fix: Replace chapter narration with problem-purpose-method-result-contribution flow.

Problem: The abstract hides the study's own contribution.

Fix: Add explicit ownership phrases such as "本研究发现" or "This study shows".

Problem: The abstract has too many details.

Fix: Keep only details needed to understand the method, findings, and value.

Problem: The abstract overstates implications.

Fix: Use cautious language such as "可能", "提示", "可为", "may", "could", or "suggests".

Problem: Keywords are too broad.

Fix: Replace generic words with discipline-specific concepts, variables, population, and method terms.

Problem: Chinese and English abstracts do not match.

Fix: Align purpose, method, findings, and conclusion across both versions.

## Revision Checklist

Before finalizing, check:

- Does the output include both abstract and keywords?
- Does it use the thesis language unless another language is requested?
- Is the abstract standalone?
- Does it include background/problem, purpose, method, findings, and contribution?
- Are findings based on the thesis rather than invented?
- Is the main contribution explicit?
- Is the wording clear enough for fast readers?
- Are acronyms defined or avoided?
- Are terms consistent throughout?
- Is the abstract within the requested length?
- Are keywords specific, searchable, and aligned with the title and thesis content?
- Does the abstract avoid unsupported causality, effectiveness, and broad generalization?
