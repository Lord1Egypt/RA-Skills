## Description: <br>
Analyze startup financing term sheets, including SAFEs, convertible notes, and priced equity rounds, by decoding economic terms, flagging founder-unfriendly clauses, modeling dilution, and comparing alternatives. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External founders, CFOs, and investors use this skill to understand the economic and control implications of startup financing term sheets before signing or negotiating. It helps prepare plain-language summaries, red-flag reviews, dilution scenarios, and side-by-side comparisons for counsel or advisor review. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Term sheet analysis can be mistaken for legal or financial advice. <br>
Mitigation: Treat outputs as business analysis, verify assumptions, and consult qualified counsel or advisors before signing, investing, or negotiating binding terms. <br>
Risk: Term sheets may contain confidential fundraising, ownership, and investor information. <br>
Mitigation: Only process documents in agent environments approved for the document's confidentiality level, and redact sensitive details when possible. <br>
Risk: Dilution and valuation outputs depend on complete and accurate input terms. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm the instrument type, valuation cap, discount, liquidation preference, option-pool treatment, share counts, and financing assumptions before relying on modeled scenarios. <br>
