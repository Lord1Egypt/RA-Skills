# Warranty Pre-Launch Checklist

Go through every item before publishing a new or revised warranty. 40 checks across 7 categories.

## 1. Policy design (7 checks)

- [ ] Warranty length matches product failure curve (not arbitrary)
- [ ] Coverage scope is enumerated (materials/workmanship, named components)
- [ ] Exclusions list has 6–10 plain-language items
- [ ] Remedy hierarchy decided (repair → replace → refund), not customer choice
- [ ] Transferability rule decided (original-buyer-only recommended)
- [ ] Consumable parts separately addressed (batteries, ear-tips, filters)
- [ ] "Lifetime" is defined — of product, of purchaser, or of brand

## 2. Financial (6 checks)

- [ ] Claim rate modeled from historical data or MTBF
- [ ] Average remedy cost calculated (weighted)
- [ ] Annual warranty cost computed and entered into P&L
- [ ] Reserve account funded at 1.5× annual warranty cost
- [ ] Quarterly review process owner assigned
- [ ] Per-unit cost folded into unit economics

## 3. Legal compliance (6 checks)

- [ ] US: Designated "Limited" or "Full" under Magnuson-Moss
- [ ] EU: Additive to Directive 2019/771 2-year statutory guarantee
- [ ] UK: Additive to Consumer Rights Act 2015, first 30 days right-to-reject acknowledged
- [ ] AU: Mandatory consumer guarantee notice displayed
- [ ] Translated into all supported market languages
- [ ] Controlling-language clause included

## 4. Claim operations (6 checks)

- [ ] Claim form has ≤3 required fields (photo, order number, description)
- [ ] 48-hour decision SLA documented and staffed
- [ ] No-return threshold set (usually 1.5× avg shipping cost)
- [ ] Prepaid return label process for above-threshold claims
- [ ] Support team T1/T2 escalation path documented
- [ ] Batch-anomaly trigger defined (e.g., >X claims in one week on one SKU)

## 5. Disclosure surfaces (6 checks)

- [ ] Warranty link on every PDP
- [ ] Warranty reassurance copy in checkout
- [ ] Mention in order confirmation email
- [ ] Mention in shipping notification
- [ ] Reference on insert card with QR to claim form
- [ ] Warranty page indexed (not noindex), clean URL

## 6. Copy and UX (5 checks)

- [ ] 3-line summary at top of policy
- [ ] No "5-star" or "satisfaction guaranteed" conflation with warranty
- [ ] "In addition to" statutory rights language present
- [ ] Claim page titled "Start a claim," not "Dispute"
- [ ] Mobile formatting reviewed (no giant legal paragraphs)

## 7. Operations readiness (4 checks)

- [ ] Refurb / B-stock pipeline capacity confirmed for expected claim volume
- [ ] Logistics partner can accept prepaid returns in all markets
- [ ] Support team trained on new policy (not just emailed)
- [ ] Effective date and version number visible on policy page
