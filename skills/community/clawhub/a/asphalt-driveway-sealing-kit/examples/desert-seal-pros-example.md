### D) FAQ Block

**Q: What type of sealer do you use?**
A: We use SealMaster AE-10 asphalt emulsion — a water-based, PAH-safe sealer. We specifically do not use coal tar-based sealers. Coal tar contains polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) at concentrations 1,000–100,000× higher than asphalt emulsion sealers. PAHs are classified as carcinogens under California Prop 65 and are restricted or banned in 7 US states. In Clark County, coal tar runoff into storm drains connects to the Las Vegas Wash and Lake Mead watershed — which is why SNHD actively monitors sealer PAH levels. Asphalt emulsion gives you a better compliance profile and a cleaner project.

**Q: Is coal tar sealer safe?**
A: No, and this is why we don't use it. USGS research (2005–2013) found PAH concentrations in storm water runoff from coal tar-sealed parking lots were 65× higher than from asphalt emulsion-sealed surfaces. Coal tar is banned in Austin TX, Washington DC, Minnesota, New York, Maine, Maryland, and several other jurisdictions. In Nevada, while not yet banned statewide, Clark County's SNHD storm water enforcement program tracks PAH discharge. We'd rather be ahead of the regulation than caught by it.

**Q: Do I need HOA approval before sealing my driveway?**
A: It depends on your HOA's CC&Rs. Most Las Vegas master-planned communities (Summerlin, Green Valley, Inspirada, Rhodes Ranch) have provisions covering driveway maintenance. Some require written approval, others require the contractor to submit an insurance certificate to the HOA management company. We've worked in 40+ HOA communities and will help you determine your specific HOA's requirements before we schedule — at no extra charge.

**Q: How long before I can drive on the sealed driveway?**
A: 48 hours minimum for foot traffic, 72 hours before vehicle traffic. Do not park on the sealed surface for 72 hours regardless of weather. Cure time depends on temperature and humidity — Las Vegas summers (95°F+) typically cure faster, but extreme heat can cause surface bubbling if sealer is applied mid-day. We schedule applications in morning hours during summer months.

**Q: How long will the sealer last?**
A: 3–5 years with proper maintenance, depending on traffic, UV exposure, and temperature cycles. Nevada's UV intensity degrades sealer faster than most climates — plan on re-sealing every 3 years for high-traffic driveways and every 4–5 years for light-use residential. We'll tell you exactly when based on your driveway's current condition.

**Q: Do you need permits for driveway sealing?**
A: Typically no permit is required for private driveway sealing. However, if your driveway apron (the section between the street and your property line) needs sealing, we obtain an encroachment permit from Clark County Public Works or the applicable municipality. This is standard — we handle it as part of the job.

**Q: Is sealing safe for my pets and kids?**
A: Asphalt emulsion sealer requires a 48-hour cure period before the surface is safe for foot traffic, pets, or children. During cure, the sealer releases water vapor — keep pets and children off the surface. After full cure (72 hours), asphalt emulsion-sealed surfaces are safe. We recommend rinsing the surface once before resuming normal use. This is why we specifically do not use coal tar — the PAH off-gassing period for coal tar products extends beyond the visible dry time.

**Q: What happens if it rains after sealing?**
A: If it rains within the first 24 hours after application, the sealer can wash, streak, or fail to cure properly. We monitor weather forecasts and will not apply sealer if rain is forecast within 48 hours. If unexpected rain occurs within 24 hours of application, we will return to assess the surface and re-apply at no charge — that's part of our workmanship warranty.
