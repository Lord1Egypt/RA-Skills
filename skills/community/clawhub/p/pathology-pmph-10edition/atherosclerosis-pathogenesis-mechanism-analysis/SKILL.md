# Atherosclerosis Pathogenesis Mechanism Analysis

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- A patient has elevated LDL-C, especially small dense LDL (sdLDL)
- Endothelial injury risk factors are present (smoking, toxins, viral infection, hemodynamic stress)
- Inflammatory markers like high-sensitivity CRP are elevated
- You need to identify the dominant pathogenic mechanism (lipid infiltration, endothelial injury, SMC proliferation, or chronic inflammation) to guide therapy

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Confirm Applicability
- Ensure the lesion is in **arteries** (not veins or microvasculature)
- Exclude non-lipid-mediated vasculopathies (e.g., vasculitis)

### Step 2: Evaluate Key Pathogenic Pathways

#### 1. Lipid Infiltration Mechanism
- Assess plasma cholesterol levels
- Determine if cholesterol/cholesteryl esters have deposited in the intima
- Look for early fatty streaks progressing to fibrous plaques

#### 2. Endothelial Injury (Damage-Response) Mechanism
- Identify endothelial insults: mechanical stress, ox-LDL, smoking, toxins, viruses
- Mild injury → increased permeability; severe injury → denudation
- Result: LDL infiltration + platelet adhesion → amplified injury and inflammation

#### 3. Vascular Smooth Muscle Cell (SMC) Involvement
- Check for SMC migration from media to intima
- Confirm phenotypic switch (contractile → synthetic)
- Verify foam cell formation via LDL/VLDL uptake and extracellular matrix production (collagen, proteoglycans)

#### 4. Chronic Inflammation Mechanism
- Measure hs-CRP levels
- Assess CRP effects:
  • Upregulates endothelial adhesion molecules
  • Reduces NO bioavailability
  • Enhances macrophage LDL uptake
  • Increases PAI-1 (pro-thrombotic)
  • Activates angiotensin receptors
  • Promotes SMC proliferation

### Step 3: Integrate Findings
- Combine lipid, endothelial, SMC, and inflammatory data
- Identify dominant driver(s) to prioritize interventions (e.g., statins for lipid control, anti-inflammatories if CRP-driven)

> **Note**: This skill applies only to arterial atherosclerosis and requires evidence of dyslipidemia or endothelial dysfunction.
