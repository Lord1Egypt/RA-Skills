## Prompt

You are a Product Hunt launch strategist who has helped products reach #1 Product of the Day. Write a complete Product Hunt launch kit for the product below.

**Product details:**
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- Tagline (what you want, we'll improve it): [YOUR DRAFT TAGLINE OR "help me write one"]
- Category: [SaaS / App / Tool / Hardware / Extension / AI / Developer Tool / Other]
- What it does (plain English): [1-2 sentences]
- Key features (top 3): [FEATURE 1] / [FEATURE 2] / [FEATURE 3]
- Target customer: [WHO IS THIS FOR]
- Main benefit: [THE #1 RESULT USERS GET]
- Pricing: [Free / Freemium / Paid at $X/month / Not yet set]
- Launch date: [DATE — must be a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday for best results]
- Hunter: [Your own account / external hunter — name if known]
- Existing audience size: [Twitter/X followers / email list / Slack community — numbers]

---

**Write the following assets:**

### Asset 1 — Tagline Variants (5 options)
Write 5 tagline options, max 60 characters each. For each:
- The tagline
- Character count
- Hook type used (outcome / curiosity / pain / bold / format)
- 1-sentence rationale for who this will resonate with most

Rules: No buzzwords ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-gen"). No question marks. Lead with benefit, not process.

### Asset 2 — Product Hunt Description (3 variants)
Write 3 description options, max 260 characters each.
Structure: [Who it's for] + [what problem it solves] + [key differentiator or result]
Include character count for each.

### Asset 3 — First Comment (Maker Comment)
Write the maker's first comment — this is the most-read section of any PH launch.

Length: 300-400 words. Include:
- Personal opening: why you built this (1-2 sentences)
- The problem, with specificity: who it hurts and how (not generic — a real scenario)
- What [product] does differently (3 bullet points)
- Pricing note (even if "free for PH community")
- Ask: "We'd love your feedback on [specific thing]" — invites comments
- P.S.: Something personal (a photo, a fun fact, what you're working on next)

### Asset 4 — Hunter Outreach
If using an external hunter (not yourself), write:
- Cold email subject line + body (max 150 words): personal, specific to their audience, clear ask
- LinkedIn DM version (max 80 words)
- Follow-up email (day 7 if no reply): 50 words max

If hunting yourself, skip this section.

### Asset 5 — Upvote Ask Messages (3 versions)
Write personalized upvote request messages for 3 audience types:

**Version A — Warm network (existing followers, email list, personal contacts)**
- Email: Subject + 80-word body
- Twitter/X DM: max 280 chars
- Timing: Morning of launch day (7-9 AM PT)

**Version B — Cold community (Reddit, Slack, Discord, Indie Hackers)**
- Forum post template: 100-150 words, adds value first, ask is secondary
- Include which subreddits/communities are appropriate for this product type

**Version C — Email list blast**
- Subject line (3 variants)
- 120-word email body
- CTA: direct PH link + "upvote if this would help you"

### Asset 6 — Twitter/X Launch Thread (8-10 tweets)
Write a full thread:
- Tweet 1: Hook — bold claim, question, or story opening. Must work as a standalone tweet.
- Tweets 2-4: The problem, with increasing specificity
- Tweets 5-7: The solution — what you built and 3 ways it helps
- Tweet 8: The Product Hunt ask with link
- Tweet 9 (optional): Behind-the-scenes or founder story
- Tweet 10: Pin tweet version (shorter, works as profile bio tweet)

Format: Each tweet numbered, character count noted, thread connector suggested.

### Asset 7 — Slack/Discord Community Announcements (2 versions)
**Version A — Your own community (members who know you)**
Max 100 words. Personal tone. Acknowledge their role in building this with you.

**Version B — Partner or cold community posting (communities where you're less known)**
Max 120 words. Leads with value/relevance to that community before the ask. Includes 1 honest hook ("I built this because I was frustrated by X and couldn't find a good solution").

### Asset 8 — 24-Hour War Room Playbook
Write a launch day operational guide:

**Pre-launch (11 PM night before — midnight PT countdown):**
- Checklist: what to verify before the clock strikes midnight
- Assets to queue in tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, email)

**Hour-by-hour guide (12 AM – 6 PM PT):**
- 12 AM: Post goes live — first actions
- 6 AM: Morning check + first push
- 9 AM: Peak traffic window — primary ask wave
- 12 PM: Midday check — how to read where you stand
- 3 PM: Afternoon push if needed
- 6 PM: Final push window before voting slows

**Comment response scripts:**
- How to respond to a positive comment (don't just say "thanks!")
- How to respond to a critical or skeptical comment
- How to respond to a feature request (encourages more)
- How to respond to a "pricing is too high" comment

**Ranking signals to understand:**
- Upvotes vs. comments: which matters more and when
- The "velocity" window: why the first 2 hours determine your ceiling
- How featured status works and what triggers it

## Product Hunt Strategy Notes

After writing all assets, add a **Strategy Notes** section with:
- Optimal launch day selection (Tuesday-Thursday, never Monday or Friday — explain why)
- Time zone targeting (why midnight PT is the start, not midnight your timezone)
- 3 common PH launch mistakes to avoid
- How to follow up the day after launch (regardless of rank)
