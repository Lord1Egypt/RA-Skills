> Source: The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen R. Covey, Chapters 5, 8, 13

# Techniques: The 7 Habits in Practice

## The 30-Day Proactivity Test

Practice being proactive for 30 days. Make a small commitment and keep it. Then make another. The goal is to build the habit of choice, not reaction.

**Exercise**: For 30 days, only speak about things within your Circle of Influence. No blaming the weather, the economy, or other people. If you catch yourself complaining, rephrase it into a proactive statement. "I can't because..." → "I choose to..."

## The Personal Mission Statement

The most powerful tool from Habit 2. Write a 1-page document that defines:
- Your values (what matters most to you)
- Your roles (parent, professional, partner, citizen)
- Your goals for each role
- A guiding principle for your life

**Exercise**: Imagine your 80th birthday. What would you want your family, friends, and colleagues to say about you? Write it down. This is the raw material for your mission statement.

## The Time Management Matrix — Quadrant II Planning

The single most practical tool in the book. Spend one hour per week on Quadrant II planning:

1. **Identify roles**: List your key roles for the week (manager, partner, parent, learner)
2. **Select goals**: Choose 1-2 Quadrant II goals per role
3. **Schedule**: Block time for these goals before anything else fills your calendar
4. **Adapt**: Adjust as the week unfolds, but protect your Quadrant II time

> **Case: The weekly planner** (Chapter 5): When executives started spending one hour per week on Quadrant II planning, their Quadrant I (crisis) time dropped dramatically over 6 weeks. The investment in prevention paid for itself.

## The Emotional Bank Account

Like a financial bank account, relationships have an emotional balance. Make deposits faster than withdrawals.

**Deposits**: Kindness, keeping promises, listening, clarifying expectations, apologizing
**Withdrawals**: Unkindness, breaking promises, not listening, ambiguous expectations, pride

**Exercise**: Think of one key relationship. Ask: "What is one deposit I can make today?" Then make it. A small deposit made consistently is more powerful than a big one made rarely.

## Empathic Listening — The 4 Levels

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ignoring | Not really listening |
| 2 | Pretending | "Uh-huh" while thinking about something else |
| 3 | Selective listening | Hearing only the parts that interest you |
| 4 | Attentive listening | Paying attention to words but missing the meaning |

**Level 5: Empathic listening — listening with the intent to understand, not to reply.**

**Exercise**: In your next conversation, try this: before responding, repeat back what you heard the other person say, in your own words. "So what I'm hearing you say is..." Don't add advice. Don't add judgment. Just show that you understand.

## Creative Cooperation — The Third Alternative

When two parties disagree, the synergistic response is not "your way" or "my way" — it's a third alternative that is better than either.

**Exercise**: In your next disagreement, instead of arguing your position, ask: "Are you willing to look for a solution that is better than either of our suggestions?" Then listen. Really listen. The third alternative emerges from combining perspectives.

## The Four Dimensions of Renewal

**Physical**: Exercise, nutrition, rest (30 min/day, 3x/week minimum)
**Mental**: Reading, writing, learning (30 min/day)
**Social/Emotional**: Service, empathy, synergy (practice one empathetic conversation per day)
**Spiritual**: Meditation, prayer, time in nature, clarifying values (15 min/day)

> **Case: The daily renewal** (Chapter 13): Covey himself practiced all four dimensions daily. He exercised every morning, read for 30 minutes each evening, spent time in nature on weekends, and served in his community. By preserving the asset (himself), his capacity to produce grew over time.
