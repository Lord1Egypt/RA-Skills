## The Problem

Every general AI tool generates marketing copy for business law attorneys that:
- Makes flat fee claims without scope disclosure (RPC 1.5 violation)
- Promises "ironclad non-competes" without disclosing Nevada's 2021 reform (NRS 613.195)
- Says "no Nevada income tax" without noting the Nevada Modified Business Tax (NRS 363B)
- Markets equity-for-fees arrangements without RPC 1.8(a) disclosure requirements
- Claims "we'll help you raise money from investors" without SEC Reg D compliance context
- Missing "This is an advertisement." on everything (Nevada SCR 192)

Every instance is a potential bar complaint, FTC action, or client malpractice exposure.

## The 7 Compliance Moats

1. **[ANCHOR] SCR 192 + RPC 7.1** — No outcome promises. No specialization claims. "This is an advertisement." on everything.
2. **RPC 1.5 Fee Transparency** — Flat fee ads disclose scope. State fees disclosed separately. Written fee agreement step in all intake sequences.
3. **NRS 86/78/363B Formation Accuracy** — Nevada Modified Business Tax disclosed. Annual list and registered agent costs disclosed. Series LLC limitations honest. Delaware C-Corp advantage for VC-backed startups acknowledged.
4. **NRS 613.195 Non-Compete Reform (2021)** — Hourly employee prohibition disclosed. Nevada choice-of-law requirement noted. No "ironclad non-compete" promises.
5. **RPC 1.8(a) Equity-for-Fees Gate** — Written disclosure, independent counsel advisement, written consent — all required before accepting equity from a client.
6. **NRS 7.285 UPL / Online Services Gate** — Accurate positioning vs. LegalZoom/Incfile. No false "same as document prep" comparisons. Attorney-client privilege differentiator.
7. **SEC Reg D / JOBS Act Investment Gate** — Rule 506(b) vs. 506(c) accurately described. Form D filing requirement noted. Nevada intrastate exemption accurately scoped.
