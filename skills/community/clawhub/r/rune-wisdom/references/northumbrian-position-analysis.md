# Northumbrian Rune Position Analysis: Final Decision

## 1. Background and Problem Statement

### 1.1 What Bednenko Actually Covers

Galina Bednenko's three-position system (Direct / Mirrored / Inverted) was developed **exclusively for the 24 Elder Futhark runes**. Her book provides detailed position meanings for each of these 24 runes only. The Younger Futhark (16 runes), the Northumbrian additions (9 runes), Solle, and Wyrd are **not covered** by her interpretive framework.

### 1.2 The Conflation Error

In previous versions of the skill, we treated the Western occult tradition's "reversed" meanings for Northumbrian runes as equivalent to Bednenko's "inverted" position. These are fundamentally different systems:

| Concept | Source | Logic |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Bednenko "Inverted"** | *Руническая школа* (Bednenko) | Position determined by **graphic logic**: flipping a tile top-to-bottom produces a visually distinct result. Only exists where the graphic shape permits it. A new principle in its own right, not merely a negation. |
| **Western Occult "Reversed"** | English-language rune books (Pennick, RuneDictionary, etc.) | Simpler binary: if the rune looks "upside down" to the reader, assign it a diminished or negative meaning. Does not check for graphic collisions. |

### 1.3 Decision: Separate Frameworks

**Decision made**: Use the Western occult tradition's binary framework (Direct / Inverted) for the Northumbrian runes + Solle + Wyrd. Do NOT attempt to apply Bednenko's three-position system to runes it was never designed for.

Rationale:
- Bednenko herself only covered Elder Futhark. Extending her graphic-logic framework to Northumbrian runes creates dilemmas (Yr/Calc inverting into Algiz's shape, Gar's full symmetry) that have no resolution within her system.
- The Western tradition has well-established "reversed" meanings that practitioners actually use.
- Applying two incompatible frameworks to the same set of runes creates confusion, not clarity.
- The Elder Futhark runes keep Bednenko's richer three-position system; the Northumbrian runes use the simpler Western binary.

**The two systems coexist in the skill but apply to different rune sets:**
- **Elder Futhark (24 runes + Wyrd)**: Bednenko three-position system (Direct / Mirrored / Inverted)
- **Northumbrian additions (9 runes + Solle)**: Western binary system (Direct / Inverted)

## 2. Position Reference for Northumbrian Runes

### 2.1 Ac (ᚪ) — Oak

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Strength, endurance, protection, connection to ancient wisdom, stability
- *Inverted:* Weakness, instability, lack of protection, disconnection from roots

### 2.2 AEsc (ᚫ) — Ash

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Connection to cosmic wisdom, ancestral knowledge, journey between worlds
- *Inverted:* Disconnection, lack of purpose, spiritual confusion

### 2.3 Yr (ᛦ) — Yew Bow

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Skill, precision, focused intention, craftsmanship, success through ability
- *Inverted:* Lack of skill, missed targets, scattered energy, poor craftsmanship

**Graphic note**: Inverted Yr visually resembles Algiz ᛉ direct position. In the Western binary framework, this collision is handled by algorithmic distinction (the numbered pool differentiates "Yr inverted" from "Algiz direct" by pool number). The inverted meaning follows the Western tradition, not Bednenko's Algiz-inverted principle.

### 2.4 Ior (ᛣ) — Beaver / Serpent

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Adaptability, building, resourcefulness, clever defense
- *Inverted:* Rigidity, destruction, lack of resources, vulnerability

### 2.5 Ear (ᛠ) — Earth / Grave

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Necessary ending, return to source, natural death, completion
- *Inverted:* Unnatural endings, resistance to change, stagnation

**Additional nuance from RuneDictionary**: *Reversed: Resistance to necessary endings, or a death that comes too soon and disrupts the natural order. This position warns against clinging to what has already run its course.*

### 2.6 Cweorth (ᛢ) — Ritual Fire / Purification

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Creative inspiration, purification, the spark of scholarship. Signals a time when new ideas and creative fires are kindled, or when something needs to be burned away to make space for the new.
- *Inverted:* Creative burnout, intellectual stagnation, or the wrong kind of fire — destructive rather than purifying. Advises reassessing whether creative or intellectual pursuits are truly aligned with purpose.

### 2.7 Calc (ᛤ) — Chalk / Chalice

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Sacred containment, the vessel that receives and holds. Signals a time of receiving — whether wisdom, inspiration, or nourishment. The chalk association suggests purity and connection to the Otherworld and the elves.
- *Inverted:* Emptiness, a vessel that cannot hold, or contamination of what should be pure. Warns against receiving carelessly or failing to honor what has been given. May indicate feeling displaced or disconnected from heritage.

**Graphic note**: Inverted Calc visually resembles Algiz ᛉ direct position. Same algorithmic-distinction approach as Yr.

**Maelinhon divergence**: Maelinhon treats Calc as inherently destructive ("death, fall, defenselessness") with no orientation variant. The Western tradition's "inverted Calc" meaning (emptiness, vessel that cannot hold) is offered by the RuneDictionary/English-scholarship tradition and represents a different interpretive lens.

### 2.8 Stan (ᛥ) — Stone

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Foundation, stability, preservation of knowledge. Signals a time to build on solid ground and ensure endurance. May indicate the need for an altar or sacred space.
- *Inverted:* Unstable foundations, crumbling structures, or knowledge being lost. Warns against building on shifting ground and advises reinforcing foundations before proceeding. May indicate disconnection from sacred space or ancestral altars.

**Graphic note**: Stan is horizontally symmetrical (like Perthro). Physically, flipping Stan upside-down produces a laterally-reversed (mirrored) image rather than a top-to-bottom inversion. However, per the **Position Fixedness Principle** (see `runic-divination-protocol.md` §3), when the inverted position falls in a draw, it is read as inverted across all traditions — the position is the position. The Western tradition provides inverted Stan meanings; Bednenko does not cover Northumbrian runes, so there is no Bednenko mirrored meaning to layer. No remapping occurs.

### 2.9 Gar (ᚸ) — Spear

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Focused strike, decisive action, union of war and magic. Completion of a cycle — Gar is the final rune of the Northumbrian row, encompassing all that has come before. Odin's influence is strong; seek wisdom and be willing to sacrifice for it.
- *Inverted:* Misdirected force, betrayal, or the destructive rather than providential use of power. Warns against using force without wisdom or sacrificing recklessly without understanding the cost. May indicate that a focused effort is missing its mark.

**Graphic note**: Gar is fully symmetrical (like Gebo). Physically, no orientation change produces a visually distinct shape — flipping Gar produces Gar, just as flipping Gebo produces Gebo. The Western tradition provides an inverted meaning (misdirected force) as an interpretive rather than graphic position. Per the **Position Fixedness Principle** (see `runic-divination-protocol.md` §3), when inverted Gar falls in a draw, it is read as inverted — the position is the position. No remapping based on graphic symmetry.

### 2.10 Solle (☀) — Sun at Zenith

**Positions**: Direct + Inverted

- *Direct:* Sun at zenith — maximum illumination, intuitive knowing, gradual growth, spiritual integration. The nurturing warmth of the sun as healer and life-giver. Where Sowilo strikes, Solle illuminates; where Sowilo demands action, Solle invites integration.
- *Inverted:* Sunset — the fading of light and the onset of darkness. Cyclical rather than terminal: the sun will rise again. The key teaching is impermanence and resilience: the darkness is temporary, and one should maintain clarity and extract wisdom from the experience. The surrounding runes clarify whether the coming night will be mild or severe.

**Graphic note**: No mirrored (face-down) position. The inverted position is the proper upside-down orientation — sunlight ascending instead of descending, sunset rather than zenith.

### 2.11 Wyrd (☐) — The Unknowable

**Positions**: Direct only

The Wyrd rune is blank on both sides. This symmetry reinforces its meaning: some mysteries are absolute and cannot be escaped or inverted. The blankness persists regardless of how you turn it, teaching that fate operates beyond the binary of positive and negative, upright and reversed.

## 4. Implementation Notes

### 4.1 Orientation Notes for the Protocol

The following replaces the old "Orientation notes for Northumbrian runes" section in `runic-divination-protocol.md`:

> **Position system for Northumbrian runes:** The 9 Northumbrian runes + Solle use the **Western binary framework** (Direct / Inverted), not Bednenko's three-position system. Bednenko's framework was developed exclusively for the 24 Elder Futhark runes. The inverted meanings for Northumbrian runes come from the English-scholarship and RuneDictionary traditions as documented in `rune-meanings-northumbrian.md`.
>
> **Graphic collisions:** Two Northumbrian runes — Yr ᛦ and Calc ᛤ — produce Algiz's ᛉ visual shape when inverted. In the Bednenko framework, this would mean they have no valid inverted position. In the Western binary framework, the inverted meaning is retained and the collision is handled by algorithmic distinction (the numbered pool differentiates these variants by number). When Yr or Calc appears inverted in a reading, the interpretation follows the Western tradition (Yr inverted = loss of skill; Calc inverted = emptiness/vessel that cannot hold), NOT Algiz's meaning.
>
> **Fully symmetrical Northumbrian runes:** Gar ᚸ is fully symmetrical (like Gebo in the Elder Futhark). In the Bednenko framework, this would mean no inverted position exists. In the Western binary framework, the inverted meaning (misdirected force) is retained as an interpretive rather than graphic position.
>
> **No mirrored positions for Northumbrian runes.** The Western binary framework does not use the mirrored position. This differs from the Elder Futhark, where some runes have mirrored positions per Bednenko's system.

## 5. Relationship to the Original Investigation

This analysis was triggered by the discovery that runes beyond Elder Futhark stopped appearing in spreads after mk3. The position-framework decision is separate from that original issue. The original problem (Northumbrian runes not appearing in draws at all) is likely a pool construction or draw algorithm problem. The position analysis addresses **interpretive correctness** for how those runes should be handled when they DO appear.

Both issues need to be fixed, but they are independent.
