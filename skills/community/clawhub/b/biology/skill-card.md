## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Biology explanations can be incorrect, oversimplified, or outdated for medical, laboratory, or safety-critical decisions. <br>
Mitigation: Treat outputs as educational guidance and verify medical, laboratory, and safety-critical advice with qualified professionals or authoritative sources before acting. <br>
Risk: Teaching or research support may not fit a learner's level, curriculum, species, or experimental context. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for the audience, course level, organism, constraints, and available materials before relying on detailed lesson, study, or experiment-planning guidance. <br>
