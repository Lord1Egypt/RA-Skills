# Seasonality Playbook

Seasonality is not a single multiplier. It is a combination of demand changes, auction changes, and conversion-rate changes, each with its own timing and magnitude. Treat them separately.

## The three layers

1. **Demand layer.** How many people are actively shopping in your category this week vs an average week? Measured from Google Trends, category-level marketplace data, or your own organic traffic.
2. **Auction layer.** How much do CPMs and CPCs rise during the same window? Driven by every other advertiser competing for the same eyeballs.
3. **Conversion layer.** How much does CVR lift (or drop) during the window? Driven by intent levels, promo strength, and competing offers.

A peak week typically has demand up 50-100%, CPMs up 25-40%, and CVR up 30-60%. The net effect on ROAS is usually positive but not as extreme as the demand number suggests.

## Common peak windows (US/EU baseline)

| Window | Demand lift | CPM inflation | CVR lift | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Cyber 5 (Thanksgiving–Cyber Monday) | +90% | +35% | +55% | Strongest 5-day window in retail. Plan creative by mid-October. |
| Prime Day (July) | +60% | +25% | +40% | Amazon-led; halo effect on DTC sites. |
| Back to School (Aug-Sep) | +40% | +15% | +20% | Category-dependent (strong for apparel, electronics, school supplies). |
| Valentine's Day (Jan-Feb) | +30% | +10% | +25% | Gifting categories only. |
| Mother's Day (Apr-May) | +35% | +15% | +30% | Gifting, beauty, home. |
| Father's Day (May-Jun) | +25% | +10% | +20% | Smaller than Mother's Day. |
| Singles Day (Nov 11) | +50% | +25% | +35% | Larger in APAC; growing in West. |
| Boxing Day (Dec 26) | +40% | +20% | +30% | UK/AU/CA primary. |

These are starting points. Pull your own data for category-specific accuracy.

## Trough windows

Don't overspend in flat weeks. Common troughs:
- Mid-January (post-holiday hangover)
- Mid-February to early March
- Late summer (mid-August in non-back-to-school categories)

Use trough weeks to test creative, refresh audiences, and refill BOF lists. Don't waste flagship-tier budget on flat demand.

## Building the seasonality overlay

1. Index each week from 0.7 (deep trough) to 1.8 (Cyber Monday).
2. Multiply baseline weekly allocation by the index.
3. Adjust the CPM inflation buffer per channel — paid social inflates more than search.
4. Pre-load creative and audience builds 2-3 weeks before each peak.
5. Hold reserve specifically for the peak window so you can pour into top performers in real time.

## Promo-stacking rule

If a brand promo overlaps a category peak (e.g., a sitewide 20% off during Cyber 5), do not double-count the lift. Use the larger of the two, plus 10% for the stacking effect, not the sum.
