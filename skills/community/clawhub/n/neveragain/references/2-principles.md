## The 11-Point Strategy for Legislative Change

After the shooting, the #NeverAgain group formed a concrete platform. These are not abstract ideals — they are specific, fought-for policies that have been tested in Florida and beyond.

| # | Policy | Why It Matters | Result (as of book publication) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Funding for gun violence research** | You can't solve what you don't measure. CDC hasn't been allowed to research gun violence as a public health issue. | Needs federal action |
| 2 | **Digitalization of ATF records** | Records still kept on paper — intentionally hard to share. | ATF modernization stalled |
| 3 | **Universal background checks** | Supported by 90%+ of Americans. Closes private sale loophole. | Passed in FL (post-Parkland) |
| 4 | **Ban high-capacity magazines** | Mass shooters reload less = fewer deaths. | Partial state-level success |
| 5 | **Assault weapon ban** | "Weapons of war don't belong on our streets and in our schools." | Banned in Deerfield, IL (fine: $1k/day) |
| 6 | **Funding for intervention programs** | Group Violence Intervention, Cure Violence models. | Community-level adoption |
| 7 | **Red flag laws (Extreme Risk Protection Orders)** | In Connecticut: 99% of risk warrants found at least one gun; average 7 guns removed. | Passed in FL post-Parkland |
| 8 | **Block domestic abusers from buying guns** | Federal law only covers spouse/former spouse — not dating partners or stalkers. | Loophole remains |
| 9 | **Federal solution to stop interstate trafficking** | Weak laws in one state flood strong-law states. | Needs federal action |
| 10 | **Safe storage + mandatory theft reporting** | Reduces child access, prevents gun theft. | State-level variations |
| 11 | **Register. Vote.** | Most powerful long-term play. Changes incentive structure. | March for Our Lives voter registration drive |

> **Case: Florida's gun bill** (Chapter 7): Within three weeks of the shooting, the Florida legislature — one of the most Republican in America, heavily supported by the NRA — passed a gun law. 63 Republican legislators ignored NRA money and voted for it. Key provisions: raised age to 21, red flag law, three-day waiting period, banned bump stocks.
> **Key takeaway:** Even the most conservative state can be moved when the pressure is sustained, visible, and impossible to ignore.

## Key Principles of the #NeverAgain Approach

### 1. Start Before You're Ready
Cameron's house was not a war room. They had no budget, no staff, no consultants. They had a couch and a WiFi password. **This is not a bug — it's the feature.** Starting without a plan forces you to iterate fast.

### 2. Let Each Person Play Their Role
The group didn't assign roles — they emerged. David was the field reporter. Emma was the moral center. Cameron was the engine. Ryan was the comedian. Delaney was the strategist. **Don't try to do everything yourself. Let people be who they are.**

### 3. Use Their Attack as Your Amplifier
When Laura Ingraham mocked David for college rejections, David posted her advertisers. Her sponsors dropped her. She went on "vacation." **Every conspiracy theory is a headline. Every smear is a stage. Use it.**

### 4. Be Authentic, Not Polished
"We didn't put out canned statements like 'I call on every citizen who loves this country and treasures its freedom.' Instead, we just talked like normal people." **Your real voice is your superpower. PR-speak is kryptonite.**

### 5. Localize the Fight
The group didn't wait for federal change. They passed a law in Florida. They banned assault weapons in Deerfield, Illinois. **National headlines are vanity. Local laws are reality.**

### 6. Never Let Them Set the Frame
When the NRA said teens were "too young to understand," the kids shot back: "Maybe you should try debating some of the ignorant teenagers at Stoneman Douglas. Just be ready to have your ass kicked back to 1776." **If they're attacking you, you're already winning.**

### 7. Register Voters
The longest lever. When 800,000 people marched and millions registered, politicians noticed. **The NRA's power depends on low turnout among young people. Break that pattern.**
