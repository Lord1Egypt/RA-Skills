# Scoring Rubrics — Anchored 1–10 Scales for All Five Dimensions

Anchors make scores comparable across suppliers and quarters. Score the band whose description fits; interpolate between anchors when evidence is mixed.

## Quality consistency (weight 30%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 10 | Defect rate <0.5%, zero spec deviations, no detectable batch-to-batch variance |
| 8 | Defects <1.5%, deviations rare and always pre-approved, minor cosmetic variance only |
| 6 | Defects 1.5–3%, occasional unapproved deviation caught at inspection |
| 4 | Defects 3–5%, or any unapproved material/component substitution |
| 2 | Defects >5%, or a defect reached customers and caused review damage |
| 1 | Systemic: golden sample bears little relation to production batches |

Count: incoming-inspection failures + customer-reported defects within 60 days, over units received.

## Reliability (weight 25%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 10 | 100% of orders complete, correct SKUs/quantities, documentation right |
| 8 | ≥95% complete-and-correct; shortfalls disclosed before shipment |
| 6 | 85–95%; at least one short-ship or wrong-variant resolved after arrival |
| 4 | 70–85%; recurring quantity or variant errors |
| 2 | <70%, or an order canceled after confirmation |
| 1 | Took deposit, then renegotiated price or terms mid-production |

## Lead time accuracy (weight 20%)

Score accuracy against PROMISE, not raw speed.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 10 | ±2 days of promised ship date on ≥95% of orders |
| 8 | ±4 days on ≥90%; delays always announced ahead |
| 6 | ±7 days on ≥80%; announcements sometimes late |
| 4 | Regular 1–2 week slips, discovered by asking |
| 2 | Slips >2 weeks, or seasonal collapse (e.g., pre-CNY orders silently parked) |
| 1 | Promised dates are fiction; every order is a negotiation |

## Communication (weight 15%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 10 | <4h business-hours responses; problems disclosed proactively with options; clear written confirmations |
| 8 | Same-day responses; problems disclosed when asked directly, honestly |
| 6 | 1–2 day responses; vague but eventually accurate answers |
| 4 | 3+ day responses, answers require chasing, confirmations verbal-only |
| 2 | Disappears under pressure; material facts emerge only at inspection |
| 1 | Misrepresentation discovered (certs, materials, subcontracting) |

Communication trend is a LEADING indicator — score the period, but flag deterioration separately even when the absolute score is acceptable.

## Cost competitiveness (weight 10%)

Compare LANDED cost (unit + tooling amortization + freight + duty + inspection + payment fees), not quote price.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 10 | Landed cost in best decile of comparable quotes; flexible MOQ; favorable terms (≤30% deposit, balance after inspection) |
| 8 | Within 5% of best comparable; standard terms |
| 6 | 5–15% above best comparable, justified by quality/reliability scores |
| 4 | >15% above market without offsetting scores |
| 2 | Above market AND rising without notice |
| 1 | Bait pricing: quoted low, raised after tooling lock-in |

## Scoring discipline

- Every score must cite at least one log entry; write "insufficient evidence — provisional" rather than guessing.
- Score periods independently — don't let last quarter's 9 drag this quarter's evidence up.
- Two people scoring? Score independently, then reconcile gaps >2 points by re-reading the log, not by averaging.
