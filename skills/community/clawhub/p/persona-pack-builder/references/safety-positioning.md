# Safety and Positioning

## Real-person inspiration

When the source request references a celebrity, creator, public figure, or private person:

- abstract the request into public-facing traits
- do not claim the persona is the real person
- do not market the output as a clone, copy, or digital twin
- avoid names, signatures, or identity claims that create confusion

## Safe framing examples

Prefer:

- “inspired by public interview energy”
- “top-star style companion persona”
- “direct, warm, high-confidence persona”
- “protective, stage-ready, low-oiliness lover persona”

Avoid:

- “I am [real person]”
- “100% clone of [real person]”
- “real celebrity digital double”

## Commercial positioning

The strongest commercial angle is usually:

- emotional effect
- style consistency
- plug-and-play packaging
- customization options

Not the borrowed name itself.
