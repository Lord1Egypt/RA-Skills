# Response Patterns

## Funnel Audit

Use this structure:

1. State the likely bottleneck.
2. Show the math that points there.
3. Explain the upstream cause.
4. Recommend 1-3 changes.
5. Say what to measure next and for how long.

Example:

`Your CTR is healthy but opt-in is weak, so I would not start by changing targeting. The ad is earning curiosity, but the page is not converting that curiosity into commitment. I would tighten message match, rewrite the above-fold headline around the outcome, and move the top objection FAQ above the form. Then watch opt-in rate and cost per booking for the next 7-14 days, depending on spend.`

## Missing Data

If the user says "my ads are not working" without numbers:

- Ask for spend, CPM, link CTR, CPC, opt-in rate, booking rate, show rate, close rate, AOV, and margin.
- If they cannot provide them, give a tracking checklist and a minimum dashboard.
- Avoid guessing the fix.

## Build Request

If asked to build a funnel:

1. Define ICP, offer, price, promise, proof, disqualifiers, and buying trigger.
2. Draft 3-5 ad angles.
3. Draft the page sections.
4. Draft the application questions.
5. Draft booking-page copy.
6. Draft nurture/reminders.
7. Define tracking events and CRM stages.

## Creative Request

For ad copy or scripts:

- Use one idea per ad.
- Make hooks specific to one pain/desire.
- Include enough qualification to attract the right person.
- Avoid personal-attribute violations.
- End with a CTA to the application/call, not an overstuffed pitch.

## Troubleshooting Map

- Low CTR: rewrite hook/message.
- High CTR and bad leads: add commitment/qualification and improve message match.
- Low opt-in: fix headline, proof, page speed, form sequence, and ad/page consistency.
- Low booking: fix booking page bridge and calendar friction.
- Low show: fix reminders, confirmation, expectation setting, and quality filters.
- Low close: review sales calls before blaming ads.
- High CPL but strong CPA/profit: do not panic; optimize for profit.
- Low CPL but poor sales: the cheap-lead metric is lying.

## Caveat Language

Use:

- "This is platform-dependent; verify the current Ads Manager label before launch."
- "These benchmarks are ranges, not rules."
- "For this offer, ROAS is less useful than net profit and payback period."
- "This method can be adapted, but it was designed for call-based service funnels."
