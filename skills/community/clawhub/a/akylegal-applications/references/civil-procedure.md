## First Instance Procedure

### Phase 1: Pre-Filing Preparation

**1. Case Intake Checklist**
- Party ID documents (individual or legal entity)
- Contracts/agreements/documents evidencing legal relationship
- Evidence of breach/tort
- Communication records (WeChat/email/correspondence)
- Subject matter-related documents

**2. Statute of Limitations Check**
- General statute of limitations: 3 years (《民法典》Art. 188)
- Lease rent: 1 year (special provision)
- International goods sale: 4 years
- Maximum protection period: 20 years from date of infringement
- Commencement: when the right holder knows or should know of the infringement and the obligor

**3. Jurisdiction Determination**
- General territorial jurisdiction: defendant's domicile (《民诉法》Art. 22)
- Contract disputes: defendant's domicile + contract performance place (Art. 24)
- Tort disputes: place of tort + defendant's domicile (Art. 29)
- Real property disputes: place of property (exclusive jurisdiction, Art. 34)
- Agreed jurisdiction: may agree on a place with actual connection to the dispute (Art. 35)

**4. Determining the Subject Matter of the Claim**
- Claims must be specific and definite
- Identify case type (contract/tort/property/marriage family/IP, etc.)
- Calculate filing fee: progressive based on claim amount

**5. Asset Preservation (Optional)**
- Pre-action preservation: must provide security, ruling within 48 hours
- In-litigation preservation: court may decide whether security is required
- Scope: limited to the amount claimed or property related to the case

### Phase 2: Filing

**Filing Materials Checklist:**
1. Complaint (original + copies = number of defendants)
2. Plaintiff's ID documents
3. Power of attorney (if represented)
4. Copies of key evidence
5. Jurisdiction basis documents
6. Preservation application (if any)

**Filing Review Period:**
- Meets requirements: file within 7 days
- Does not meet requirements: ruling of non-acceptance within 7 days
- Incomplete materials: one-time notice to supplement

### Phase 3: Pre-Trial Proceedings

**1. Evidence Submission Period**
- First instance ordinary procedure: court-designated, no less than 15 days
- Summary procedure: court-designated, no more than 15 days
- Late evidence submission: state reasons; court decides whether to accept; fines possible without正当理由

**2. Jurisdictional Objection**
- Raised during the answer period (within 15 days of receiving complaint)
- Objection ruling is appealable (10-day appeal period)

**3. Pre-Trial Conference**
- Clarify claims and defenses
- Identify undisputed facts
- Summarize disputed issues
- Facilitate evidence exchange
- Encourage mediation

### Phase 4: Trial Hearing

**Trial Flow:**
```
Session opened → Court investigation → Court debate → Final statements → Mediation → Judgment
```

**Court Investigation (Core Stage):**
1. Plaintiff states claims and factual basis
2. Defendant answers
3. Plaintiff presents evidence
4. Defendant cross-examines
5. Defendant presents evidence
6. Plaintiff cross-examines
7. Witness testimony (if any)
8. Expert testimony (if any)
9. Judge's questions

**Court Debate:**
- Focus on disputed issues
- Factual argumentation → Legal application argumentation
- Usually no more than two rounds

### Phase 5: Judgment

- Oral verdict: written judgment issued within 10 days
- Scheduled verdict: written judgment issued immediately
- Appeal period: 15 days from service of written judgment

### Summary Procedure

- Applicable to: simple cases with clear facts, definite rights and obligations, minor disputes
- Trial period: 3 months (ordinary procedure: 6 months)
- May convert to ordinary: when case becomes complex, parties diverge significantly, or service by publication needed
- Small claims (amount ≤ 50% of previous year's local average salary): final instance

## Second Instance (Appeal) Procedure

**Appeal Deadline:**
- Judgment: **15 days** from service
- Ruling (non-acceptance/dismissal/jurisdiction objection): **10 days** from service

**Appeal Content:**
1. Party information
2. Lower court, case number, cause of action
3. Appeal request (vacate/amend/remand)
4. Appeal grounds (fact-finding error/legal application error/procedural violation)

**Second Instance Hearing Methods:**
- Oral hearing as principle (required in certain circumstances)
- Written review: after reviewing files, investigation, and questioning parties, if facts are clear and legal disputes are minor

**Second Instance Rulings:**
- Affirm original judgment
- Amend judgment
- Remand for retrial (unclear facts/serious procedural violation)
- Remand is limited to once

## Retrial Procedure

**Application Deadline:** **6 months** from judgment becoming effective

**Statutory Retrial Grounds (《民诉法》Art. 211):**
1. New evidence sufficient to overturn original judgment
2. Basic facts lack evidentiary support
3. Main evidence was forged
4. Main evidence was not cross-examined
5. Party's investigation request was not granted
6. Clear error in legal application
7. Illegally composed trial panel
8. Judge who should have recused did not
9. Party lacked legal capacity without proper representation
10. Claims omitted or exceeded
11. Legal document underlying judgment was revoked/amended
12. Judge engaged in corruption/bribery/abuse of power

**Retrial Procedure:**
- Apply to higher court (or original court for second instance cases)
- Court reviews within 3 months of receiving application
- Retrial ordered → suspend original judgment enforcement
- Retrial forms new panel
- Tried under original instance procedure

**Procuratorate Protest/Prosecutorial Recommendations:**
- Party applies for procuratorate supervision
- Procuratorate decides whether to file protest

## Special Procedures

### Payment Order (督促程序)
- Applicable to: clear creditor-debtor relationships with no other disputes
- Debtor raises no objection within 15 days → payment order takes effect, enforceable
- Debtor raises objection → payment order invalidated, converts to ordinary litigation

### Public Summons Procedure (公示催告程序)
- Applicable to: lost/stolen/destroyed negotiable instruments
- Public notice period: no less than 60 days
- No rights claimed → court declares instrument invalid

### Security Interest Enforcement Procedure (实现担保物权程序)
- Applicable to: security interest holder seeking enforcement
- No need for full litigation; apply directly to court for auction/sale
- Review period: 30 days
