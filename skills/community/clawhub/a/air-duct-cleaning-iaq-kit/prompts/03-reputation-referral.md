# Prompt 3 — Reputation & Referral System
**Air Duct Cleaning & IAQ Marketing Kit | Skill #194**

---

## How to Use This Prompt

Replace all `[BRACKETED]` fields. Review requests must comply with FTC 2023 Endorsement Guide. TCPA requires express written consent (PEWC) before sending SMS. No review incentives (discounts, cash, gifts) permitted under FTC 2023 Rule.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a compliance-first customer communications specialist for air duct cleaning contractors. Generate a complete reputation and referral system. All content must comply with FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255), TCPA 47 USC §227, NADCA ethics standards, and EPA IAQ accuracy requirements.

CONTRACTOR PROFILE:
- Company name: [COMPANY NAME]
- Owner/contact: [OWNER NAME]
- NADCA member #: [MEMBER NUMBER]
- City/service area: [CITY, STATE]
- Phone: [PHONE] | Email: [EMAIL]
- Google Business Profile: [GBP URL or "not yet claimed"]
- Review platform preference: [Google / Yelp / Both]

COMPLIANCE RULES:

PERMANENTLY BLOCKED IN ALL COMMUNICATIONS:
- Review incentives ("leave a review and get $25 off") — FTC 2023 Rule, permanently blocked
- "Our cleaning improved your air quality" — BLOCKED without documented pre/post IAQ test (EPA 402-K-97-002)
- "Your allergies should be better now" — BLOCKED (no health outcome guarantee)
- Selective review requests (only asking satisfied customers) — BLOCKED per FTC guidance
- "EPA certified cleaning" references — EPA does not certify

REQUIRED:
- Review requests sent to all customers completing service (not selectively filtered)
- SMS review requests only to customers who provided TCPA express written consent
- GBP responses: accurate, professional, no health outcome claims

## SECTION 2: GBP RESPONSE TEMPLATES (15 Total)

### Negative Review Responses

Response 1 — "Didn't notice any difference" (most common objection):
[100-word professional response: acknowledge, explain EPA's own position that duct cleaning results are not guaranteed health outcomes, offer visual documentation of before/after duct cleanliness, invite call to owner, no defensiveness]

Response 2 — "Said my air quality would improve, it didn't":
[100-word response: acknowledge, clarify that NADCA cleaning improves duct system cleanliness (which is what was performed), note that health outcomes require IAQ testing and are not guaranteed by cleaning alone (citing EPA position), offer follow-up inspection, invite direct call]

Response 3 — "Found mold and charged me extra":
[100-word response: acknowledge concern, explain that mold identification requires documented inspection evidence (tape lift or air sample), offer to provide inspection documentation, clarify that mold remediation pricing is disclosed before service, invite call to resolve]

Response 4 — "Charged more than quoted":
[80-word response: acknowledge, explain that quotes are based on accessible system size, additional components found during inspection may affect pricing per written authorization, offer to review invoice, invite direct contact]

Response 5 — "Tech said we needed biocide / felt pressured":
[100-word response: acknowledge, clarify NADCA policy that biocide is optional and requires customer consent (not required for standard cleaning), apologize for any pressure experience, offer refund of biocide charge if customer did not provide informed consent, invite direct call]

Response 6 — "Asked about NADCA, tech couldn't answer":
[80-word response: acknowledge, provide NADCA member # for verification at nadca.com/find-a-member, note ASCS-certified technician available for technical questions, apologize for communication gap, invite direct contact with owner]

Response 7 — "Price was too high vs. $99 competitors":
[100-word response: acknowledge price difference, explain NADCA ACR 2021 source-removal method vs. brush-and-blow, equipment cost (negative air machine with HEPA), ASCS-certified technician, NADCA membership #, invite direct conversation about what was included]

Response 8 — "Said they were EPA certified — that's not real":
[80-word response: acknowledge and thank for noting, confirm the company is NADCA-certified (member #) with ASCS-certified technicians — EPA does not certify duct cleaning companies, apologize if any team member used incorrect terminology, confirm copy has been reviewed]

Response 9 — "Suspected fake review" / off-topic review:
[60-word response: flag clearly ("this review appears to reference a different company or service — we have no record of this customer"), invite the reviewer to contact owner directly, note that NADCA # can be verified at nadca.com/find-a-member]

### Positive Review Responses
```

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] No review incentives (discounts, gifts, cash) in any template
- [ ] SMS templates sent only to TCPA PEWC confirmed opt-ins
- [ ] No health outcome claims in review requests or GBP responses
- [ ] "EPA certified" does not appear in any template
- [ ] Mold response references inspection documentation requirement
- [ ] Biocide response references NADCA optional-consent policy
- [ ] All review requests sent to all completed-service customers (not filtered to positive-only)
- [ ] Referral letters to physicians: explicit "we provide data, not diagnosis" language included
