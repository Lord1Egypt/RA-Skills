# Sales Methodology Reference

## BANT Qualification Framework

**Budget** — Does the prospect have allocated budget or approval authority?
- Strong: Budget approved, line item exists
- Medium: Budget exists but not allocated
- Weak: No budget discussion yet

**Authority** — Are you talking to the decision maker?
- Champion: Has influence but needs sign-off
- Decision Maker: Can say yes directly
- Gatekeeper: Just passing you through

**Need** — How acute is the pain you solve?
- Strong: Active problem causing measurable cost
- Medium: Problem acknowledged, not urgent
- Weak: Nice-to-have, no urgency

**Timeline** — When do they want to solve this?
- Hot: Needs solution in <30 days
- Warm: 30–90 day window
- Cool: 90+ days or undefined

**Lead Score Guide:**
- 80–100: Immediately qualify and prioritize
- 50–79: Nurture, get more info, find champion
- 20–49: Long-term nurture only
- 0–19: Pass or deprioritize

## Pipeline Stage Criteria

### Prospecting (5%)
- Initial contact or inbound inquiry
- No qualification done yet
- **Exit criteria:** BANT partially confirmed, next meeting scheduled

### Qualified (20%)
- Budget/authority/need confirmed at basic level
- Pain point identified
- **Exit criteria:** Discovery complete, proposal requested or agreed

### Proposal (40%)
- Formal proposal or quote sent
- Stakeholders identified
- **Exit criteria:** Proposal reviewed, entering negotiation

### Negotiation (70%)
- Pricing/terms being discussed
- Strong buying signals
- **Exit criteria:** Verbal agreement or signed contract

### Closed Won (100%)
- Contract signed, payment terms agreed
- Handoff to delivery/CS underway

### Closed Lost (0%)
- No longer pursuing
- Always log a loss reason

### On Hold (10%)
- Prospect paused decision (budget freeze, reorg, etc.)
- Set a follow-up date

## Pipeline Health Indicators

**Healthy pipeline has:**
- 3-5x revenue target in pipeline value (weighted)
- Deal spread across all stages
- Average deal age < 60 days in early stages
- Activity logged on all deals within 14 days

**Warning signs:**
- >50% of pipeline in one stage
- Deals stale >14 days in proposal/negotiation
- Close dates repeatedly pushed
- No new deals entering top of funnel

---

## Velocity & Forecasting

**Pipeline velocity formula:**
`(# deals × avg value × win rate) / sales cycle days`

**Weighted forecast:**
Each deal's value × stage probability = weighted value
Sum of weighted values = expected closed revenue

**Commit vs. Best Case:**
- Commit: Negotiation stage + (some Proposal stage deals)
- Best Case: All non-lost deals
- Most Likely: Weighted total

## Follow-up Cadence

| Time Since Last Touch | Action |
|---|---|
| 0–3 days | Normal follow-through |
| 4–7 days | Send a value-add follow-up |
| 8–14 days | Pick up the phone |
| 15–30 days | Re-engagement email with new angle |
| 30+ days | Breakup email or move to long-term nurture |

---

## Loss Analysis

Always capture loss reason. Common categories:
- Lost to competitor (name if known)
- Lost to no decision / status quo
- Budget cut or freeze
- Champion left
- Product gap
- Timing issue
- Price too high

Loss analysis drives product, pricing, and ICP refinement.
