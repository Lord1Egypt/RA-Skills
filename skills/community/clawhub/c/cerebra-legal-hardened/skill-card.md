## Description: <br>
Think through any legal situation like a lawyer. Issue spotting, jurisdiction, risk assessment, actionable conclusions. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and legal-support workflows use this skill to structure legal-information responses around jurisdiction, facts, issue spotting, applicable law, risk, and action steps. It is intended to guide analysis and escalation, not to replace a licensed attorney. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may mistake legal information for legal advice tailored to their matter. <br>
Mitigation: Include a clear legal-information disclaimer in substantive responses and direct users to licensed counsel for situation-specific advice. <br>
Risk: Jurisdiction-specific rules, filing deadlines, and legal standards may be outdated or incorrect. <br>
Mitigation: Ask for jurisdiction, avoid definitive deadline claims without verification, and recommend checking counsel or official sources before relying on dates or rules. <br>
Risk: Legal scenarios can include sensitive documents, personal identifiers, or emergency safety disclosures. <br>
Mitigation: Ask users to redact sensitive details, prioritize emergency or legal-aid resources for urgent safety issues, and avoid treating professional framing as a reason to skip safety triage. <br>
