## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a Certified General appraiser or MAI designee needs to draft a USPAP 2026-2027 narrative appraisal report for an income-producing commercial property; it develops HBU analysis, Sales Comparison, Income, and Cost approaches, and produces a DRAFT report for the signing appraiser to certify. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Certified General appraisers, supervised trainees, review appraisers, report-production analysts, lenders, servicers, or counsel use this skill to structure and draft a USPAP 2026-2027 narrative appraisal report for income-producing commercial real estate. The output is a draft work product that must be verified, certified, and signed by the qualified signing appraiser before delivery. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Draft appraisal content may contain incorrect or incomplete comparables, assumptions, rates, calculations, USPAP interpretations, or reconciliation logic. <br>
Mitigation: Require the qualified signing appraiser to verify all sources, comparables, assumptions, rates, calculations, USPAP requirements, and certification language before delivery. <br>
Risk: The workflow may handle confidential appraisal work product such as client identities, rent rolls, lease abstracts, operating statements, and transaction terms. <br>
Mitigation: Use the skill only in environments approved for confidential appraisal data, and do not transmit assignment data to services the user has not authorized. <br>
Risk: A draft report could be mistaken for a signed appraisal or final value opinion. <br>
Mitigation: Keep the output labeled as DRAFT and require review, certification, and signature by the signing appraiser before it is delivered to any client or intended user. <br>
