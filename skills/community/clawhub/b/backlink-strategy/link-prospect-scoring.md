# Link Prospect Scoring Guide — Backlink Strategy

A systematic framework for evaluating and prioritizing link-building prospects to maximize ROI from outreach efforts.

## Prospect Scoring Rubric

Score each prospect on a 1-5 scale across these dimensions. Total score determines priority tier.

### 1. Domain Authority (Weight: 25%)

| Score | DA Range | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 60+ | Premium authority site; single link can move rankings |
| 4 | 45-59 | Strong authority; reliable ranking signal |
| 3 | 30-44 | Moderate authority; solid addition to profile |
| 2 | 15-29 | Low authority; worth pursuing only if highly relevant |
| 1 | <15 | Minimal authority; skip unless exceptional relevance |

### 2. Topical Relevance (Weight: 30%)

| Score | Relevance Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Direct niche match | Same product category, same audience, core topic alignment |
| 4 | Adjacent niche | Related category, overlapping audience (e.g., fitness → nutrition) |
| 3 | Broader industry | Same industry umbrella but different focus area |
| 2 | Tangential | Loose topical connection, different primary audience |
| 1 | Unrelated | No meaningful topical overlap |

### 3. Traffic Quality (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Estimated Monthly Traffic | Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 100K+ | High organic traffic; link also drives referral visitors |
| 4 | 25K-100K | Solid traffic; meaningful referral potential |
| 3 | 5K-25K | Moderate traffic; some referral value |
| 2 | 1K-5K | Low traffic; primarily link equity value |
| 1 | <1K | Negligible traffic; minimal benefit beyond raw link |

### 4. Link Placement Quality (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Placement | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | In-content, contextual | Link within article body surrounded by relevant text |
| 4 | Resource section | Listed in a curated resource section or recommended tools |
| 3 | Author bio | Link in contributor bio at end of article |
| 2 | Footer/sidebar | Site-wide or template-level placement |
| 1 | Comment/forum | User-generated content area |

### 5. Outreach Feasibility (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Feasibility | Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | High probability | Clear contact info, accepts pitches, past responsiveness |
| 4 | Good chance | Contact info available, relevant content section, active site |
| 3 | Moderate | Contact exists but no indication of openness to outreach |
| 2 | Difficult | Hard to find contact, corporate site with gatekeepers |
| 1 | Very difficult | No contact info, no contributor program, unresponsive history |

## Priority Tiers

Calculate weighted score (max 5.0) and assign tier:

| Tier | Score Range | Action | Outreach Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — Premium | 4.0-5.0 | Prioritize immediately; invest in deep personalization | Week 1 |
| B — Strong | 3.0-3.9 | Include in regular outreach cadence | Week 2-3 |
| C — Moderate | 2.0-2.9 | Include if capacity allows; use efficient templates | Month 2+ |
| D — Low | <2.0 | Skip unless no better options remain | Deprioritize |

## Red Flags — Automatic Disqualification

Remove any prospect that exhibits these signals:

- **PBN indicators**: Thin content, excessive outbound links, no real audience, newly registered domain
- **Link selling**: Site openly sells links, has a "sponsored post" price page, or advertises "write for us" with fees
- **Penalty signals**: Dramatic traffic drops visible in SEMrush/Ahrefs, deindexed pages, manual action history
- **Irrelevant foreign language**: Site is in a language completely unrelated to your target market
- **Adult/gambling/pharma adjacency**: Site is in or closely associated with YMYL spam verticals
- **Scraped content**: Articles are clearly copied or auto-generated with no original value
- **Excessive ads**: Page is more ads than content, indicating low editorial standards
- **No recent updates**: Site hasn't published new content in 6+ months (may be abandoned)

## Prospect Research Workflow

### Step 1: Build Raw List
- Export competitor backlinks from Ahrefs/Moz/SEMrush
- Search Google for "[niche] + guest post / resource page / blog"
- Check industry directories and association member lists
- Review social media mentions and community recommendations

### Step 2: Initial Filter (Quick Scan)
- Remove DA <15 (unless exceptional relevance)
- Remove non-English sites (unless targeting international markets)
- Remove obvious PBNs and link farms
- Remove sites with no contact information findable in 2 minutes
- **Target**: Reduce raw list by 40-60%

### Step 3: Score Remaining Prospects
- Apply the 5-dimension rubric above
- Calculate weighted total
- Assign priority tier
- **Target**: Tier A (10-15%), Tier B (25-35%), Tier C (30-40%), Tier D (15-25%)

### Step 4: Enrich Tier A and B Prospects
For high-priority prospects, gather additional intelligence:
- Read 3-5 recent articles to identify personalization hooks
- Note their social media presence for warming up
- Identify the specific page where your link would fit
- Find the best contact method (email vs. contact form vs. social DM)
- Check if they've linked to competitors and what content earned those links

### Step 5: Organize in CRM or Spreadsheet
Required fields for tracking:
| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Site URL | Target website |
| Contact name | Decision maker |
| Contact email | Outreach address |
| DA | Authority metric |
| Relevance score | 1-5 rating |
| Total score | Weighted composite |
| Tier | A/B/C/D |
| Tactic | Guest post / Resource / Review / etc. |
| Status | Not contacted / Emailed / Replied / Link live / Declined |
| Last contact date | For follow-up scheduling |
| Notes | Personalization hooks, preferences, history |

## Monthly Prospect Maintenance

- Add 20-30 new prospects per month from ongoing competitor monitoring
- Remove prospects that have declined or gone unresponsive after 3 touches
- Re-score prospects quarterly as DA and traffic data updates
- Archive successful placements for relationship maintenance
- Flag sites that have degraded in quality for link monitoring
