# Prompt 1: Monthly Performance Report

## Prompt

You are a senior account manager at a top-tier digital agency. Write a professional, results-first monthly performance report for a client. The report must be honest, data-driven, and written in a way that makes the client feel informed, confident, and excited about next month.

**Client Context:**
- Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]
- Services: [e.g., SEO + Google Ads + Social Media Management]
- Report Period: [e.g., March 2026]
- Contract Value: [e.g., $3,500/month]

**KPI Data (this month vs last month):**
[Paste your metrics here — e.g.:]
- Organic traffic: 12,400 vs 10,800 (+14.8%)
- Leads from organic: 47 vs 38 (+23.7%)
- Google Ads spend: $1,200 of $1,200 budget
- Google Ads leads: 31 vs 28 (+10.7%)
- Cost per lead: $38.71 vs $42.86 (-9.7%)
- Social reach: 28,400 vs 24,100 (+17.8%)
- Followers gained: +182

**Key Wins This Month:**
[List 3-5 specific wins — e.g., "Ranked #1 for 'Las Vegas HVAC repair' (was #7 in Feb)"]

**Challenges or Misses:**
[Be honest — e.g., "Blog publishing was delayed — only 2 of 4 planned articles went live"]

**Next Month Priorities:**
[List 3-5 planned actions]

**Budget Status:**
[Hours used: X of Y | Budget remaining: $Z]

---

Generate the monthly report with these sections:

1. **Executive Summary** (3-4 sentences, results-first — lead with the strongest win)

2. **KPI Dashboard** (table format)
   | Metric | Target | Actual | MoM Change | Status |
   (Use ✅ for on/above target, ⚠️ for within 10% below, ❌ for significantly below)

3. **Wins This Month** (3-5 bullets, each with a specific number or outcome)

4. **Challenges & Learnings** (honest, action-oriented — no excuses, just what happened and what we're doing about it)

5. **Recommendations** (2-3 specific action items with rationale — what the agency is suggesting and why)

6. **Next Month Priorities** (numbered list with owner [Agency or Client] and target completion date)

7. **Account Footer**
   - Hours: [X used / Y contracted]
   - Next scheduled check-in: [Date]
   - Questions? Contact: [Account Manager name and email]

**Tone:** Professional, warm, confident. Write like a trusted advisor — not a vendor justifying their invoice. Keep it under 800 words (excluding table).
