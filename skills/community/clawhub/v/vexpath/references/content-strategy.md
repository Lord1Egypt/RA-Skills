# Content Strategy Reference

## Overview

Content is the front-end of the business system. This reference covers content planning, idea generation, post scheduling, and platform-specific formatting.

---

## Content Purpose Framework

Every piece of content must serve one purpose:
1. **Build authority** — establish credibility and expertise
2. **Educate** — teach something useful to the audience
3. **Show proof** — demonstrate results (social proof, case studies)
4. **Drive offers** — move the audience toward a specific action
5. **Build connection** — behind-the-scenes, personality, relatability

Never post without a clear purpose. "Posting for visibility" is not a purpose.

---

## 5 Content Types

### 1. Authority Content
**Purpose:** Establish the business as the expert to trust  
**Formats:** Opinion takes, frameworks, industry commentary, predictions  
**Tone:** Confident, specific, direct  
**Example:** "Most [businesses] are [doing X wrong]. Here's what actually works."

### 2. Educational Content
**Purpose:** Teach the audience something they can use  
**Formats:** How-to, step-by-step, tips, breakdowns, explainers  
**Tone:** Clear, practical, actionable  
**Example:** "3 things to check before [common mistake]"

### 3. Behind-the-Scenes
**Purpose:** Build trust and relatability  
**Formats:** Process reveals, day-in-the-life, tool showcases, team moments  
**Tone:** Conversational, real, unpolished  
**Example:** "Here's how I actually [process/workflow] for a client"

### 4. Client Proof
**Purpose:** Reduce buying friction with social evidence  
**Formats:** Case studies, before/after, testimonials, results screenshots  
**Tone:** Specific, results-focused  
**Example:** "[Client] went from [problem] to [result] in [timeframe]"

### 5. Offer-Driven
**Purpose:** Direct call to action — convert audience into leads or clients  
**Formats:** Service announcements, limited availability, direct asks  
**Tone:** Clear, specific, no ambiguity  
**Example:** "If you're a [business type] dealing with [problem], this is for you. [CTA]"

---

## Content Ratio (Default)

| Type | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Authority | 30% |
| Educational | 30% |
| Behind-the-Scenes | 15% |
| Client Proof | 15% |
| Offer-Driven | 10% |

Adjust ratio based on business stage:
- **New business (0–6 months):** Increase educational + behind-the-scenes; reduce offer-driven
- **Established (6–24 months):** Balance all types; ramp client proof
- **Growth phase:** Increase offer-driven; maintain authority and proof

### Idea Generation Inputs

Use these to generate content ideas:
- Common questions from client emails and intake forms
- Problems mentioned in sales calls or consultations
- Workflow wins that produced measurable results
- Industry news or trends worth commenting on
- "What we wish clients knew before working with us"
- Behind-the-scenes look at a completed project
- Before/after from a recent client result

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### LinkedIn
- Hook in first line (no emojis in line 1 unless brand-specific)
- Short paragraphs (1–2 sentences max)
- Line breaks between every point
- End with a question or clear CTA
- Hashtags: 3–5, placed at the end
- Best for: Authority, Educational, Client Proof

### Instagram
- Caption: Hook → Story → Takeaway → CTA
- Use line breaks; emoji accents ok
- First 125 characters are critical (above "more" fold)
- Hashtags: 5–15 in caption or first comment
- Stories: behind-the-scenes, polls, quick tips
- Best for: Behind-the-Scenes, Client Proof, Offer-Driven

### Twitter/X
- Max 280 characters per tweet; thread for longer content
- Direct, punchy, no hedging
- Threads: hook tweet → numbered breakdown → closing CTA
- Best for: Authority, hot takes, Educational (threads)

### TikTok / Short-Form Video
- First 2 seconds = hook (on-screen text + spoken)
- 30–90 seconds optimal
- Captions: short, keyword-rich
- Best for: Educational, Behind-the-Scenes, Personality

### Facebook
- Conversational tone
- Slightly longer captions acceptable
- Strong CTA for local and community engagement
- Best for: Local Business announcements, Client Proof, Offer-Driven

### Discord / Community
- No markdown tables; use bullet lists
- Wrap links in `<>` to suppress embeds
- Keep messages concise; reactions over long replies
- Best for: Behind-the-Scenes, Community updates

### WhatsApp / SMS Broadcast
- No headers; use **bold** or CAPS for emphasis
- Short paragraphs (2–3 lines max)
- Single clear CTA per message
- Best for: Offers, Reminders, Announcements

## Content Production Workflow

```
IDEA → DRAFT → REVIEW → APPROVE → SCHEDULE → PUBLISH → REPURPOSE
```

1. **Idea:** Capture in content backlog (Notion/Sheet/Note)
2. **Draft:** Write full post with hook, body, CTA
3. **Review:** Check against decision filter — does it serve a purpose?
4. **Approve:** Owner approves before scheduling
5. **Schedule:** Queue in Buffer/Later/Meta Suite or native scheduler
6. **Publish:** Confirm live and correct
7. **Repurpose:** Identify 1–3 derivative formats (clip → post → quote card)

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Reach / Impressions | Content is getting in front of people |
| Engagement rate | Content is resonating (not just visible) |
| Saves / shares | High-value signal — educational content score |
| Profile visits | Audience wants to know more |
| DMs / inquiries | Content is converting to conversations |
| Leads generated | Content is producing business outcomes |

Review metrics monthly. Cut content types with low engagement after 60-day trial.

## Content Brief Format

When producing content for a client or campaign:

```
CONTENT BRIEF
─────────────
Topic: 
Type: [Authority / Educational / Behind-the-Scenes / Client Proof / Offer-Driven]
Platform: 
Goal: [what this post should make the audience do or feel]
Audience: [who this is for]
Key Message: [one sentence — what must land]
Supporting Points: [2–4 bullet points]
CTA: [exact ask]
Tone: [formal / conversational / direct / energetic]
Reference: [any links, examples, or assets to reference]
```
