# Concussion Return-to-Play Protocol

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Sports Medicine — Concussion Management

## Purpose

A drafting partner for team physicians, athletic trainers, sports physiotherapists, school medical officers, and primary-care sports clinicians. Turns athlete intake, mechanism of injury, current symptom burden, and stage history into a DRAFT individualized graduated Return-to-Sport (RTS) and parallel Return-to-Learn (RTL) plan aligned to the CISG 2023 Amsterdam consensus statement and SCAT6, with ≥ 24-hour stage minimums, symptom-aggravation regression rules, a clearance gate before full-contact practice, and an unsigned supervising-HCP sign-off block.

## When to Use

- Drafting an individualized RTS / RTL plan after a confirmed concussion in a high-school, collegiate, professional, club, or recreational athlete
- Documenting the governing-body rule (NFHS, NCAA, FIFA, World Rugby, IIHF, IOC, state youth-sport law) and any stricter minimum it imposes over CISG 2023
- Producing a parallel RTL plan with school adjustments that must reach Stage 4 (full academic load) before RTS Stage 5 (full-contact practice)
- Recording modifying factors (prior concussions, migraine, mood disorder, ADHD / LD, sleep, sport, age) that warrant extended minimums and earlier sub-specialist referral
- Standardizing regression rules for new or worsening symptoms during stage progression
- Preparing the documentation an athletic-training room can use across an episode of care

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Intake**
1. Captures clinician role, governing body, and controlling framework
2. Captures athlete reference, age, sport / position, concussion history, modifying medical history, mechanism of injury, and same-day removal
3. Captures current clinical status using the named assessment tool (SCAT6 within 72h; SCOAT6 from day 3 onward; Child-SCAT6 ages 5–12)

**Phase 2: Modifying-factor and stage mapping**
4. Flags modifying factors with extended minimums or referral implications
5. Maps the athlete to current RTS and RTL stages

**Phase 3: Plan drafting**
6. Drafts a 6-step RTS table with permitted / prohibited activities, minimum intervals, and symptom gates
7. Drafts a parallel 4-step RTL table with school accommodations and the RTL-4-precedes-RTS-5 rule
8. Records regression rules and red-flag escalation
9. Leaves clearance and return-to-sport signature lines unsigned

## Output

A DRAFT plan with:

- Clinical summary and modifying-factor list
- Current status (symptom score, tool used, days since injury)
- Verbatim SCAT6 red-flag list reviewed
- RTS 6-step table with stage minimums and symptom gates
- Parallel RTL 4-step table with academic adjustments
- Modifiers and extended-minimum citations
- Regression rules and follow-up cadence
- Unsigned clearance gate
- Evidence matrix
- Unresolved-questions list

## Safety

This skill drafts a plan, **not** a clearance. Every output is labeled **DRAFT — SUPERVISING HEALTHCARE PROFESSIONAL MUST REVIEW AND SIGN**. The skill never clears an athlete, never overrides governing-body policy, never recommends pharmacology or imaging, and never applies to penetrating head injury, suspected cervical-spine injury, persistent vomiting, GCS < 15 at 30 minutes post-injury, deteriorating mental status, focal neurological deficit, seizure, suspected skull fracture, or anticoagulation use. Red flags trigger immediate emergency referral and pause the plan. Athlete health information is treated as confidential and is never written to external services; the draft uses an athlete reference (initials, jersey #, internal ID) rather than full name.
