# Arbitration Tactics & Prolongation Claim Strategies

---

## Arbitration Tactics — Common Law vs Civil Law

### Common Law Arbitrations

- Heavy emphasis on **contemporaneous documents**, detailed witness statements, and expert reports
- Cross-examination is often rigorous — be prepared to defend methodology and credibility in detail
- Broader **document disclosure** is common; failure to disclose can damage credibility
- Significant reliance on **case law and precedents** in areas such as notice, prevention principle, and quantum methods
- **Strategy:** Build a document-heavy, precedent-supported case with strong expert evidence

### Civil Law / Mixed Arbitrations

- Greater emphasis on overall **fairness, good faith, and commercial equity**
- Cross-examination may be less confrontational, depending on tribunal and rules
- Disclosure is usually **narrower**
- More reliance on principles of good faith and fairness than strict precedent
- **Strategy:** Combine strong factual evidence with clear arguments on equity and the parties' reasonable expectations

## Prolongation Claim Strategies in Arbitration

### Common Law Approach

- Strong focus on proving **EOT first** (critical path impact) before claiming prolongation costs
- Expect detailed scrutiny of quantum methods and baseline assumptions
- Concurrent delay arguments are common and must be addressed **proactively**
- **Strategy:** Secure the EOT foundation first, then build a well-supported prolongation claim — preferably based on actual costs with formula methods as fallback

### Civil Law Approach

- The link between EOT and prolongation costs may be assessed **more flexibly**, depending on governing law and tribunal approach
- Actual costs remain preferred, but good faith and fairness arguments may help bridge gaps in strict causation proof
- **Strategy:** Emphasise the overall impact and equity of the situation while still providing clear cost evidence

---

## Practical Recommendations

1. **Know your tribunal** — research the arbitrators' backgrounds and preferred approaches
2. **Prepare for cross-examination** — ensure witnesses and experts are thoroughly prepared
3. **Disclosure strategy** — balance completeness with risk of revealing unhelpful documents
4. **Quantum foundation** — always anchor prolongation to a clear EOT finding
5. **Address concurrent delay proactively** — don't leave it for the other side to raise
6. **Clear visuals** — timelines, charts, and cost summaries are often more effective than dense narrative
