# Warriors / 猫武士 · Canon Reference (canon.md)

> **world id:** `warrior-cats`
> **Source:** Erin Hunter, *Warriors* series (2003– ). Animal-clan survival fantasy.

## 1. One-line premise
Wild cats live in organized warrior **Clans** in the forest, governed by a strict warrior code, guided by the spirits of their ancestors — and an ordinary cat can rise from kit to warrior to leader while the Clans face starvation, rival aggression, prophecy, and threats from the human ("Twoleg") world.

## 2. World & era
- **Setting:** the forest territories (and later the lake territories), home to the warrior Clans.
- **Tone:** survival drama with mythic/prophetic undertones; loyalty, honor, betrayal — told entirely from cats' point of view.
- **The Clans:**
  | Clan | Trait | Domain |
  |---|---|---|
  | **ThunderClan** | brave, loyal (the "home" Clan) | forest |
  | **ShadowClan** | ambitious, tough | pine marsh |
  | **WindClan** | swift, open-moor runners | hills/moor |
  | **RiverClan** | sleek swimmers, well-fed | river |
  | **StarClan** | warrior ancestors (spirit guides) | the night sky |

## 3. Clan structure (ranks)
- **Leader** (name ends in *-star*, holds nine lives from StarClan) → **Deputy** → **Medicine Cat** (healer + StarClan link) → **Warriors** → **Apprentices** (*-paw*) → **Queens** (nursing mothers) & **Kits** → **Elders**.
- The **Warrior Code** governs everything: loyalty to your Clan, defend your territory, respect StarClan, no killing for cruelty, etc.

## 4. Main plot scaffolding (stages for anchoring)

**Stage 1 · The outsider joins**
- A house cat (e.g. Rusty/**Firepaw**) leaves the soft life to join ThunderClan as an apprentice — the classic entry arc.

**Stage 2 · Apprentice to warrior**
- Training, mentorship, first battles and hunts; proving loyalty; earning a warrior name (*-fur*, *-heart*, *-claw*, etc.).

**Stage 3 · Treachery within**
- Ambition corrupts: a deputy or warrior (the Tigerclaw/Tigerstar archetype) schemes for power, betrays the code, and threatens the Clan from inside.

**Stage 4 · Prophecy & destiny**
- StarClan sends omens and prophecies; a chosen cat must interpret signs and rise to meet a coming danger (rival Clan war, a tyrant, a great threat to all Clans).

**Stage 5 · Leadership & survival**
- The hero becomes deputy, then **leader** (gaining nine lives); the Clans face existential threats (famine, fire, flood, Twoleg destruction, or a unifying enemy) that force cooperation across Clans.

## 5. Key archetypes (canon roster shifts by arc)

| Archetype | Role | Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| **Firestar (Rusty/Firepaw)** | kittypet → legendary leader | loyalty, courage, the outsider who belongs |
| **Tigerstar** | ambitious traitor | power-hunger, cruelty, the great villain |
| **Graystripe** | the hero's best friend | warmth, divided loyalty (RiverClan love) |
| **Bluestar** | wise ThunderClan leader | mentor, secrets, sacrifice |
| **Sandstorm / Spottedleaf** | warrior / medicine cat | love interest & healer figures |
| **Medicine Cat** | healer + StarClan seer | herbs, omens, no mate/kits by code |

## 6. Setting anchors
- **StarClan** — ancestral spirits who walk in dreams, send prophecies, and grant leaders nine lives.
- **The Moonstone / Moonpool** — where cats commune with StarClan.
- **Gatherings** — truce nights at the full moon when all Clans meet in peace.
- **Twolegs / monsters / kittypets** — humans, cars, and house cats as the outside world's intrusions.
- **The Warrior Code** — the moral spine; breaking it drives much of the drama.

## 7. Theme & "anchor vs. freedom"
- **Core themes:** loyalty vs. ambition; finding your place; the cost of honor; nature and survival; destiny vs. choice.
- **Canon anchors (stay close):** the five Clans + the Warrior Code, the kittypet-to-warrior rise, StarClan prophecies, the ambitious-traitor threat, leadership via nine lives.
- **Free to diverge:** the player can be any Clan's apprentice, a medicine cat, a loner, or a future leader; rivalries, prophecies, and Clan politics branch from there.

## 8. Notes for YumFu
- Prefer **real Clan structure / names / lore** (ThunderClan/ShadowClan/WindClan/RiverClan/StarClan, the Warrior Code, *-paw/-star* naming) for moves, hunts, battles, and NPC pressure.
- Keep cats *as cats* — territory, prey, scent, seasons (leaf-bare/greenleaf), and the code should shape every decision.
- Let StarClan omens and the Warrior Code create moral stakes, not just fights.
