# Prompt 1: Center Brand & Enrollment Acquisition (FREE)

## What This Generates
- Center positioning statement (3 versions)
- Open enrollment email sequence (3 emails)
- Facebook/Instagram ad set (3 ads, parent-targeted)
- Google RSA enrollment campaign
- Tour invitation sequence (email + SMS)
- Parent referral program announcement
- Parent FAQ landing page copy with Schema JSON-LD

## Required Input

Fill in the fields below before running the prompt:

```
Center name:
Location (city, state):
State childcare license number:
Ages served:
Capacity (licensed):
Staff-to-child ratios by age group (e.g., infants 1:4, toddlers 1:6, preschool 1:10):
Key program approach (Montessori, play-based, academic, faith-based, Reggio Emilia, etc.):
NAEYC accredited? (yes/no — if yes: accreditation number and valid-through date):
CACFP meal program participant? (yes/no):
Current enrollment status (open spots / waitlist / specific age groups open):
Top 3 differentiators (what makes you different from the daycare down the street?):
Tuition range (optional — e.g., $350-450/week depending on age):
```

## The Prompt

```
You are a childcare marketing specialist with expertise in FTC compliance, COPPA, state childcare licensing regulations, and NAEYC accreditation standards.

Generate a complete enrollment acquisition package for the following childcare center:

[PASTE YOUR CENTER INFORMATION HERE]

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS — apply to every piece of copy generated:
1. LICENSING: Include state childcare license number in all printed and digital ads using the format "State License #[NUMBER]" or per-state required format
2. NAEYC: Only mention accreditation if NAEYC=yes AND enrollment is current and confirmed. Use exact language: "NAEYC Accredited (Accreditation #[ID], valid through [DATE])". Never use "NAEYC-quality" or "NAEYC-level" without current enrollment.
3. CACFP: Only mention meal program if CACFP=yes. Approved language: "Proud participant in USDA CACFP — nutritious meals at no extra cost to families"
4. RATIOS: Only state ratios at or within licensed capacity. Never claim better ratios than licensed for.
5. FTC TESTIMONIALS: Any parent quote must include typical-results context: "[Name], parent of [age-group] child" — no exceptional outcome claims ("my child was reading at age 2") unless documented as typical and disclosed
6. COPPA / DIGITAL ADS: Target PARENTS only — ages 25-45, parent/family interests. Add to every ad: "Audience: parents — exclude under-13 app/game placements"
7. BACKGROUND CHECKS: Specify the type: "All staff complete FBI fingerprint background checks and state childcare registry screening" — not just "background-checked"
8. PHOTO NOTE: Add to any copy using child images: "[Note: Use only photos with signed photo release on file — see parent handbook]"
9. RATIOS IN ADS: Frame as minimum standard, not promise: "Staff-to-child ratios that meet or exceed [STATE] licensing standards"
10. SUPERLATIVES: "Best daycare in [City]" requires award citation with date. Use instead: "Families' top-rated: [X]+ five-star Google reviews"

DELIVERABLES:

**A. CENTER POSITIONING STATEMENT (3 versions)**
- 1 sentence (for Google Business Profile, social bio)
- 3 sentences (for email signature, directory listings)
- Full paragraph (for website About section, brochure)
Lead with primary differentiator. Include license status and NAEYC if applicable.

**B. OPEN ENROLLMENT EMAIL SEQUENCE (3 emails)**

Email 1 (Week 1):
- Subject line options (3): Urgency + specificity
- Preview text (90 chars)
- Body: Opens with parent's core fear (is my child safe? will they thrive?). Addresses it with specific, compliance-safe proof points. Lists top 3 differentiators. CTA: Schedule a tour. P.S.: License number + any accreditation.
- Word count: 250-350 words

Email 2 (Week 2 — "Day in the life"):
- Subject: "What does [Child's Name]'s day look like at [Center Name]?"
- Body: Narrative walkthrough of a typical day (arrival through pickup). Weave in curriculum, meals (if CACFP), outdoor time, safety routines. No child names — "a 3-year-old in our preschool room."
- CTA: Schedule a tour to see it yourself
- Word count: 300-400 words

Email 3 (Week 3 — Urgency):
- Subject options (3): Scarcity + specific age group
- Body: Remaining spots (be truthful — use placeholder [X spots remaining in infant/toddler/preschool room]). Social proof (1 FTC-compliant parent quote). Final CTA with tour link.
- Word count: 200-250 words

**C. FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM ADS (3 ads)**

Ad 1 — Enrollment Campaign (cold audience):
- Primary text: 125 chars max
- Headline: 40 chars max  
- Description: 30 chars max
- Audience spec: "Parents 25-45, [City] + 10-mile radius, interests: parenting, childcare, early childhood education, kindergarten readiness — COPPA note: exclude Audience Network placements on apps/games rated for under 13"
- Compliance check: license number in ad or landing page; no child-targeted language

Ad 2 — Tour Invitation (warm audience — people who visited website):
- Retargeting copy: warmer tone, social proof hook
- "Come see what a day looks like" format
- 125 char primary text + 40 char headline

Ad 3 — Waitlist Urgency:
- "[X] spots remaining in our [age group] room"
- FTC-compliant parent quote (1 sentence, typical results, parent first name + child age group only)
- No false scarcity — use placeholder for actual availability

**D. GOOGLE SEARCH AD (RSA format)**
- 15 headlines (30 characters max each)
  * Include: safety/license claim, NAEYC if applicable, enrollment CTA, ratio claim, location
  * Do NOT use: "best," "top-rated" without citation, ratio claims beyond licensed
- 4 descriptions (90 characters max each)
- 2 sitelink extensions: "Schedule a Tour" + "View Our Programs"
- 1 negative keyword list: 5 terms to exclude

**E. TOUR INVITATION SEQUENCE**

Email: Tour Confirmation (immediately after request submitted):
- Subject: "Your tour at [Center Name] is confirmed — [Date/Time]"
- Include: what to bring, what to look for checklist (frames their evaluation in your favor), parking/entrance info
- Under 200 words

Email: Tour Reminder (24 hours before):
- Subject: "See you tomorrow at [Time] — [Center Name]"
- "What to look for on a childcare tour" checklist (5 items — positions your center's strengths naturally)
- Under 150 words

SMS Reminder (2 hours before):
- Under 160 characters
- Center name, time, address
- Include: "Reply STOP to opt out"

**F. PARENT REFERRAL PROGRAM ANNOUNCEMENT**

Email (to current enrolled families):
- Subject: "Know a family looking for childcare? Help them — and earn a thank-you"
- Fixed-dollar reward (never percentage-based): "[X]-week tuition credit for each new family who enrolls"
- FTC compliant: "Receive a [X]-week tuition credit when a family you refer completes 30 days of enrollment. Full terms at [URL]."
- Under 200 words

Social media post (Facebook/Instagram):
- Under 150 words
- No child photos in ad (parent-facing only)
- CTA: DM or call to get your referral link

**G. PARENT FAQ LANDING PAGE COPY**

8 Questions:
1. Is your center licensed? (Display license number, state licensing link)
2. What are your staff-to-child ratios? (Use licensed ratios, not aspirational)
3. What does a typical day look like? (Age-appropriate curriculum narrative)
4. What meals are provided? (CACFP language if applicable; if not: "families provide lunch/snacks" or catering partner)
5. What is your illness/sick child policy? (Clear return-to-care standards)
6. How do I authorize pickup? (Security procedures without alarming language)
7. How do you communicate with parents? (App/portal/daily reports)
8. What is your tuition and what does it include? (All fees disclosed; no hidden fees)

Schema FAQ JSON-LD block (for Google People Also Ask):
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "[QUESTION 1]",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[ANSWER 1 — include license number]"
      }
    }
    // ... repeat for all 8 questions
  ]
}
```
```
