## Description: <br>
Mood Diary is a Chinese-language mood tracking skill that records natural-language journal entries, detects seven emotions with 1-10 scoring, extracts tags, and generates daily, weekly, monthly, and trend reports while storing data locally. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Individuals and agents assisting them use this skill to keep a local Chinese-language mood journal, review emotional patterns, and generate text or JSON reports for personal reflection. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Mood and diary history may contain sensitive personal information stored on the user's device. <br>
Mitigation: Use the skill only on a trusted device, review file access expectations, and back up or delete ~/.openclaw/workspace/data/journal/entries.json as needed. <br>
Risk: Mood scores and wellness suggestions are informal tracking outputs, not professional mental-health advice. <br>
Mitigation: Treat reports as personal reflection aids and seek professional support for serious, persistent, or urgent mental-health concerns. <br>
