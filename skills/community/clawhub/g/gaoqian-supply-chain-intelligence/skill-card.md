## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Useful results may require sensitive supply chain data such as SKUs, warehouse quantities, supplier names, delivery history, and quality metrics. <br>
Mitigation: Use appropriate internal data-handling controls and provide only authorized data needed for the analysis. <br>
Risk: Disruption monitoring can be incomplete or stale without current web or alert sources. <br>
Mitigation: Verify high-impact disruption claims against trusted current sources before making procurement, logistics, or inventory decisions. <br>
Risk: Forecasts and optimization recommendations are probabilistic and can be wrong when inputs are incomplete or assumptions do not match operating conditions. <br>
Mitigation: Review confidence, assumptions, and business constraints before acting on route, supplier, inventory, or cost recommendations. <br>
