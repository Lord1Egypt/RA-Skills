# TCM Safety Rules (安全用药规则)

**⚠️ CRITICAL**: These rules must be checked for EVERY prescription before delivery.

---

## 1. Eighteen Incompatibilities (十八反)

Traditional incompatibility pairs — herbs that should **never** be used together in the same formula.

| Group | Herbs | Cannot Be Used With |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | 乌头类 [Chuanwu, Caowu, Fuzi] | 半夏 (Banxia), 瓜蒌 (Gualou), 贝母 [Chuanbeimu, Zhebeimu], 白蔹 (Bailian), 白及 (Baiji) |
| 2 | 甘草 (Gancao) | 海藻 (Haizao), 大戟 (Daji), 甘遂 (Gansui), 芫花 (Yuanhua) |
| 3 | 藜芦 (Lilu) | 人参 (Renshen), 丹参 (Danshen), 沙参 (Shashen), 玄参 (Xuanshen), 细辛 (Xixin), 白芍 (Baishao), 苦参 (Kushen) |

**Note on Fuzi (附子):** Fuzi is processed Aconite (Chuanwu). The incompatibility with Banxia and Gualou applies. However, in classical formulas, Fuzi and Banxia co-appear in some formulas — this remains controversial. **When in doubt, avoid the combination.**

## 4. Toxic Herbs & Maximum Dosages (毒性药材)

| Herb | Toxicity Level | Max Safe Dose | Special Processing Required | Notes |
|------|:--------------:|:-------------:|:---------------------------:|-------|
| 附子 (Fuzi) | High | 15g | 先煎30-60min | Most commonly used toxic herb; proper decoction reduces aconitine by 90%+ |
| 川乌 (Chuanwu) | High | 6g | 先煎30-60min | Similar to Fuzi; must be processed (制) |
| 草乌 (Caowu) | High | 6g | 先煎30-60min | More toxic than Chuanwu |
| 半夏 (Banxia) | Moderate | 10g | Must be processed (法半夏/姜半夏) | Raw Banxia irritates throat |
| 天南星 (Tiannanxing) | Moderate | 6g | Must be processed (制南星/胆南星) | Raw causes throat numbness |
| 马钱子 (Maqianzi) | Very High | 0.3-0.6g | Highly processed only | NEVER use raw; causes tetanus-like spasms |
| 朱砂 (Zhusha) | High | 0.5g | 研粉冲服，入丸散 | Heavy metal (HgS); do not use long-term |
| 雄黄 (Xionghuang) | High | 0.15-0.3g | 入丸散，不入煎剂 | Arsenic sulfide |
| 细辛 (Xixin) | Moderate | 3g (decoction) / 1g (powder) | — | Overdose causes respiratory paralysis |
| 甘遂 (Gansui) | High | 1.5-3g | Stir-fried with vinegar | Strong purgative |
| 斑蝥 (Banmao) | Very High | 0.03-0.06g | Must be processed | Can cause renal failure |
| 全蝎 (Quanxie) | Moderate | 5g | — | Toxin reduced by decoction |
| 蜈蚣 (Wugong) | Moderate | 3g | — | Toxin reduced by decoction |
| 巴豆 (Badou) | High | 0.1-0.3g | Must be processed (巴豆霜) | Strong purgative |
| 牵牛子 (Qianniuzi) | Moderate | 6g | Stir-fried | Strong purgative |

**General safety principles for toxic herbs:**
1. Start at the lowest effective dose
2. Never use beyond maximum dose
3. Always use processed (制) forms
4. Always use proper decoction methods (先煎 for toxic herbs)
5. Limit duration — do not use for extended periods
6. No toxic herbs during pregnancy (see above)
7. Monitor for adverse reactions (numbness, dizziness, palpitations, GI distress)

## 7. Decoction Method Guidelines (煎服法)

| Herb Type | Preparation |
|-----------|------------|
| 先煎 (Decoct first) | Fuzi, Chuanwu, Caowu, Shijueming, Longgu, Muli, Cishi — decoct 30-60 min before adding other herbs |
| 后下 (Add later) | Bohe, Gouteng, Sharen, Doukou, Dahuang (when used as purgative) — add in last 5-10 min |
| 包煎 (Wrap-decoct) | Cheqianzi, Puhuang, Xixin powder — wrap in gauze to prevent clouding |
| 烊化 (Dissolve separately) | Ejiao — dissolve in hot strained decoction |
| 冲服 (Take as powder) | Sanqi, Shexiang, Zhusha — take with strained decoction |
| 煎汤代水 (Decoct as base) | When specified herbs are decocted first for their liquid |

**Standard decoction:**
1. Soak herbs in cold water for 30 min (water 2-3cm above herbs)
2. Bring to boil, then simmer 20-30 min (调节火候: high → low)
3. Strain; repeat for second decoction
4. Combine two batches; take in 2-3 divided doses
5. 1 dose per day, take warm
