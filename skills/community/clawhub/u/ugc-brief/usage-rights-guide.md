# UGC Usage Rights Guide

Content rights are frequently the source of disputes in brand-creator relationships. Be explicit in every brief.

---

## Core Rights Dimensions

### 1. Platform Scope
Which platforms can you use the content on?
- **All platforms** — TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, Amazon, Pinterest, website, email, etc.
- **Platform-specific** — Only the platform specified
- **Whitelist rights** — You can run paid ads from the creator's account (not just from your brand account)

### 2. Use Type
How can you use the content?
- **Organic** — Post on your brand's own channels
- **Paid** — Run as paid advertising
- **Both** — Organic + paid (most brands want this)

### 3. License Duration
How long can you use the content?
- **6 months** — Short-term; good for trend-based content
- **12 months** — Standard
- **24 months** — Long-term; worth the extra cost for evergreen content
- **Perpetual/In perpetuity** — No expiration; standard for best-performing creative assets

### 4. Editing Rights
Can you modify the content?
- **No edits** — Use as-is
- **Caption/subtitle only** — Can add text overlays
- **Trim/crop** — Can cut for different formats
- **Full edit rights** — Can combine clips, add voiceover, recut entirely

### 5. Exclusivity
Can the creator make similar content for competitors?
- **No exclusivity** — Creator can work with competitors
- **Category exclusivity** — Creator cannot work with direct competitors for the license period
- **Brand exclusivity** — Creator cannot mention competing brands

---

## Standard Rate Guidance (UGC, not influencer post)

| Rights Package | Typical Additional Cost |
|---|---|
| Organic only, 6 months, no edit | Base rate |
| Organic + paid, 12 months, trim/caption edits | Base + 20–30% |
| Full buyout, all platforms, perpetual, full edit | Base + 50–100% |
| Whitelist rights (run ads from creator account) | Additional $150–$500/month or one-time fee |

*These are industry norms, not fixed rates. Negotiate based on creator audience size and content quality.*

---

## Usage Rights Language (for brief)

**Standard language to include in brief:**

> "By accepting this brief and delivering the agreed content, Creator grants [Brand Name] a [duration] license to use, reproduce, distribute, and display the Content across all platforms (including paid advertising) in any format. Creator retains ownership of the original content. [Brand] may edit, crop, or combine content for advertising purposes. Creator agrees not to create substantially similar content for direct competitors in the [category] space during the license period."

---

## What Happens Without Clear Rights

- You cannot legally run paid ads using creator content without explicit paid use rights
- Creator can request content removal if no rights agreement exists
- Dispute over "organic repost" vs. "paid ad use" is common and expensive
- No specified duration = creator can revoke license at any time

---

## Whitelisting vs. Dark Posts

- **Whitelisting:** Creator gives your brand access to run ads from their handle. The ad appears to come from the creator's account, not your brand. Higher trust signal.
- **Dark Post:** You run an ad from your brand account that uses creator content. Requires full usage rights.
- Both are valid; whitelisting typically produces better CTR due to perceived authenticity.
