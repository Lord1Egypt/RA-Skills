# Example: SunVault Solar — Complete Brand Naming Session

**Business:** Residential solar panel + battery installation  
**Location:** Nevada, Arizona, California  
**Differentiator:** Fastest installation in Nevada (14 days guaranteed), Tier 1 panels, no high-pressure sales  
**Target audience:** Homeowners 35-65, HHI $75K+, skeptical of solar salespeople  

---

## Prompt 1 Output: Brand Name Brainstorm

### Name Candidates

**Style A: Invented / Coined Words**
- **SunVault** — Fuses "sun" (energy source) with "vault" (security, storage, value locked away). Implies solar + battery storage in one word. Short, pronounceable, unique.
- **Solarith** — Combines solar + algorithm (precision). Signals smart, data-driven installation. Tech-forward feel.
- **Lumari** — Derived from "luminary" + "-ari" suffix (Latin flavor). Warm, premium feel. Works well in luxury or eco-premium market.

**Style B: Descriptive / Benefit Names**
- **GridFree** — States the goal: independence from the utility grid. Instantly understood. Strong call-to-action energy.
- **ClearWatt** — "Clear" (transparency in pricing) + "Watt" (unit of power). Signals honest solar. Good for a company fighting the sleazy-salesperson stigma.
- **SunReturn** — Directly states the ROI promise. "Your sun pays you back." Connects to financial outcome.

**Style C: Metaphor / Analogy Names**
- **SunVault** *(top pick — see below)*
- **Helio** — Greek root for sun; implies a pure, science-backed brand. Single-word authority.
- **AnchorSolar** — "Anchor" implies stability, security, and setting down roots. Resonates with homeowners who plan to stay long-term.

**Style D: Action / Verb-Based Names**
- **GoSolar Nevada** — Literal command + geo. Simple, local. Best for a hyperlocal SEO play.
- **HarvestSun** — Harvest implies abundance and intention. Farmers harvest — you harvest sunlight.
- **PowerShift** — Implies moving from one energy paradigm to another. Signals change, movement, taking control.

**Style E: Evocative / Emotional Names**
- **Luminary Home** — Means "a person who inspires others." A home that generates its own light. Premium feel.
- **SunCrest** — "Crest" = peak, achievement, the top. Connotes pride of ownership and reaching the summit.
- **DawnGrid** — Dawn = new beginning; Grid = energy infrastructure. Poetic, forward-looking.

### Top 3 Recommendation

1. **#1: SunVault** — Memorable, short, invented-ish (vault is a real word but combo is unique), implies both power generation and battery storage, strong visual branding potential (a vault filled with sunlight). Works as SunVault.solar or SunVault.co if .com is taken.

2. **#2: GridFree** — Most benefit-clear name in the list. Customers immediately know the promise: freedom from the grid. Strong for SEO ("grid-free solar Nevada"). Risk: sounds oppositional, which may alienate utility customers who want grid-tie (not off-grid).

3. **#3: ClearWatt** — Best for fighting the "shady solar salesperson" reputation. "Clear" does double duty: transparent pricing AND clean energy. Builds trust before the first call. Good DFY pitch: "We're the ones who don't hide the numbers."

*Before deciding: ask 5 Nevada homeowners "What kind of business do you think this is?" for each name. SunVault should score 4/5 or better on "solar/energy."*

## Prompt 2 Output: Tagline Generator (for SunVault)

### 10 Tagline Options

1. **"Power That Pays You Back."** *(Benefit-Led)*  
   Mechanism: Directly addresses the ROI objection ("will solar really save me money?"). "Pays you back" is active, concrete, and implies the system works for the customer — not the other way around.  
   Best placement: Website hero, business card, pitch deck, email signature

2. **"Your Roof Is Worth More Than Shade."** *(Contrast/Tension)*  
   Mechanism: Reframes a passive asset (roof) as an untapped revenue source. Creates cognitive tension — "wait, my roof can earn?"  
   Best placement: Facebook/Instagram ad, direct mail headline

3. **"Nevada's Sun Is Already Paying Your Bills."** *(Benefit-Led)*  
   Mechanism: Geo-specific + immediate. Implies the money is there, they're just not collecting it yet. Creates urgency without pressure.  
   Best placement: Local radio, geo-targeted social ads

4. **"Own Your Energy."** *(Imperative/Command)*  
   Mechanism: Short, empowering, two words of identity ("own" implies control and investment). Works as a brand rallying cry.  
   Best placement: Truck wraps, hats, merch, pitch deck final slide

5. **"Bills Down. Battery Up."** *(Alliteration/Rhythm)*  
   Mechanism: Parallel structure + alliteration = sticky. Communicates two key outcomes (cost reduction + battery storage) in 4 words.  
   Best placement: Business card, email subject lines, yard signs

6. **"The Sun Works 24/7. Shouldn't Your Home?"** *(Contrast/Tension — question)*  
   Mechanism: Reframes the sun as a worker, not a weather event. Question forces the prospect to answer internally — and the answer is yes.  
   Best placement: Email subject line, landing page subheadline

7. **"We Install Fast. You Save Faster."** *(Benefit-Led)*  
   Mechanism: Directly hits SunVault's 14-day installation guarantee differentiator. Rhythm mirrors the benefit.  
   Best placement: Google Search Ads, homepage hero subheadline

8. **"Sunlight. Storage. Savings."** *(Alliteration/Rhythm)*  
   Mechanism: Three-word alliterative summary of the full product (panels, battery, ROI). Works in any context.  
   Best placement: Logo lockup, About page header, pitch deck slide 1

9. **"Finally, Solar Done Right."** *(Emotion-Led)*  
   Mechanism: Validates the customer's prior skepticism. "Finally" acknowledges the frustration with bad solar companies. Instantly differentiates.  
   Best placement: Review response starters, ads targeting homeowners who had a bad experience

10. **"Cut the Bill. Keep the Power."** *(Contrast/Tension)*  
    Mechanism: "Cut" (savings) vs. "Keep" (reliability) — the two fears of solar skeptics addressed in 6 words.  
    Best placement: Retargeting ads, email re-engagement subject line

5. "The future is bright" — pun + cliché combo; signals lack of creativity

## Prompt 3 Output: Brand Voice Guide (SunVault)

### Brand Voice Spectrum

1. Formal ←→ **Casual** — sits at 7/10. Friendly and direct, not corporate. Talks like a trusted neighbor who happens to know everything about solar.
2. Serious ←→ **Balanced** — sits at 5/10. Takes money seriously (never jokes about savings or ROI), but isn't stiff.
3. Technical ←→ **Plain English** — sits at 8/10. Avoids kWh/Wh jargon unless explaining a specific number. Translates everything to dollars saved.
4. Reserved ←→ **Bold** — sits at 7/10. Willing to call out bad industry practices ("we don't do high-pressure sales"). Confident, not aggressive.
5. Traditional ←→ **Innovative** — sits at 6/10. Modern energy, traditional values (homeownership, reliability, local).

### Vocabulary Guide

**20 Words/Phrases to USE:**
- "saves you" — always ties to money
- "guaranteed" — backs up the 14-day promise
- "your roof" — makes it personal, not abstract
- "we'll handle it" — reassuring, concierge feel
- "Tier 1 panels" — credibility signal
- "local team" — counters out-of-state solar company fear
- "no pressure" — addresses the #1 reason people avoid solar consultations
- "locked in" — implies stable pricing vs. rising utility bills
- "pays for itself" — ROI framing
- "clean install" — pride in craftsmanship
- "in X days" — specific, builds confidence
- "your neighbors" — social proof framing
- "25-year warranty" — longevity signal
- "free assessment" — lowers barrier to entry
- "no surprise fees" — addresses billing anxiety
- "net metering" — only when explaining credits (define it immediately after)
- "battery backup" — concrete, understandable
- "own your power" — identity/empowerment
- "A+ rated" — BBB credibility shorthand
- "right-sized system" — anti-oversell signal

**10 Words/Phrases to AVOID:**
| Avoid | Say Instead | Why |
|-------|------------|-----|
| "utilize" | "use" | Corporate-speak |
| "solutions" | say what it actually does | Vague buzzword |
| "synergy" | never | 1999 |
| "seamless" | "smooth" or just describe it | Overused |
| "cutting-edge" | name the actual technology | Unverifiable |
| "passionate about solar" | "we've installed 340 homes" | Show don't tell |
| "going green" | "lower your bill" | Motivation is savings, not virtue |
| "affordable" | quote the specific monthly savings | Subjective |
| "best-in-class" | cite the specific metric | Unverifiable |
| "ecosystem" | just describe the system | Startup jargon leak |

### Sentence Structure

- Body copy: 12-18 words ideal. Never over 25.
- Headlines: 4-8 words. One idea per headline.
- Paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max in marketing copy.
- Active voice: always. "We install in 14 days" not "Installation is completed within 14 days."
- Questions: Use sparingly — 1 per page max. Only use if the answer is obviously "yes" for the target customer.
- Numbers: Always use digits ("14 days", "$187/month", "340 homes"), never spell out.
- Oxford comma: yes. Em dashes: yes, for emphasis. Exclamation points: max 1 per page, never in sales copy.

### Channel Voice Rules

**LinkedIn:**
1. Lead with a data point or customer result
2. Share a specific lesson learned, not generic solar news
3. End with a question that invites comment (not a sales pitch)
*Example: "340 installs. Here's the one question homeowners always ask first."*

**Instagram:**
1. Caption starts with the hook word (bill, savings, roof, sun) — no "We" openers
2. Emoji used as bullet points, not decoration (max 3)
3. CTA is always in the last line, not buried
*Example: "☀️ $187/month average savings. Your roof's already doing the math."*

**Twitter/X:**
1. One thought per tweet, no thread unless it's a case study
2. Numbers over adjectives always
3. Reply to solar FUD (misinformation) with data, not emotion
*Example: "14 days from contract to power-on. Nevada average is 68. We're not average."*

**Email:**
1. Subject line = one specific benefit or question, never "Newsletter"
2. First sentence = the most important thing, not a greeting
3. One CTA per email, at the end, as a button or linked phrase
*Example opener: "Your neighbor on Desert Rose Court just cut their bill by $211. Here's how."*

**Website:**
1. Every headline answers "what's in it for me?"
2. Social proof (reviews, install count) appears in first scroll
3. Pricing or "see if you qualify" CTA on every page
*Example hero: "14-Day Installation. 25-Year Warranty. Power That Pays You Back."*

### Headline Formula

1. **[Specific number] + [outcome] + [timeframe]**  
   Example: "340 Nevada Homes. $187/Month Average Savings. 14-Day Install."

2. **"Finally, [solution to known frustration]."**  
   Example: "Finally, a Solar Company That Shows You the Math."

3. **"[Pain point]? [Brand Name] [fixes it in one line]."**  
   Example: "Electric bill over $300? SunVault can cut that in half."

### Brand Voice in One Sentence

"SunVault sounds like a confident neighbor who knows everything about money and energy, talks like someone who's done this 340 times and still cares about your specific house, and never makes you feel like a mark."

### The One-Liner Test (Party Answer to "What do you do?")

1. "We install solar on Nevada homes in 14 days — most companies take 68." *(makes them ask "how?")*
2. "We help Nevada homeowners turn their roof into a $187-a-month income source." *(makes them ask "how much does it cost?")*
3. "We're the solar company that shows you the math before you sign anything." *(makes them say "oh, so you're different")*

**Ranked by memorability:** #1 wins (the contrast "14 vs. 68" is the hook) — but use #2 in financial-focused conversations and #3 when skepticism about solar is the vibe.
