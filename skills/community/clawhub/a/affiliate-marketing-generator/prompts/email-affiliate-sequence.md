# Prompt: Email Affiliate Promotion Sequence (5 Emails)

You are an email copywriter specializing in affiliate promotions. Write a 5-email sequence to promote an affiliate product to a warm list — subscribers who already trust you.

## Inputs
- Product to promote: [PRODUCT_NAME]
- Audience niche: [LIST_NICHE]
- Your name/brand: [YOUR_NAME]
- Single biggest benefit: [PRODUCT_BENEFIT]
- Price and offer details: [PRICE_AND_OFFER]
- Bonus you're offering: [YOUR_BONUS]
- Affiliate disclosure language: [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE]

## Output: 5 Emails

---

### Email 1 — Day 1: The Teaser
**Subject:** [curiosity-based subject line, no direct mention of the product]
**Preview text:** [15-20 words that reward opening]

**Body:**
- Open with a relatable pain point your audience shares
- Tell a brief story (3-4 sentences) about discovering the solution
- Don't name the product yet — create curiosity
- End with: "I'll share exactly what it is tomorrow."
- [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE] at bottom
- P.S.: one-line preview of the reveal

**Character count target:** 200-300 words

---

### Email 2 — Day 2: The Story + Product Reveal
**Subject:** [story-based subject line that names the product]
**Preview text:** [15-20 words]

**Body:**
- Briefly recap yesterday's teaser (1 sentence)
- Tell your origin story with [PRODUCT_NAME] — what problem you had, when you found it, what changed (3-4 paragraphs)
- Name 2-3 specific features that solve the audience's core problem
- Introduce [PRICE_AND_OFFER] with your affiliate link
- Introduce [YOUR_BONUS] briefly — "And if you get it through my link, you also get..."
- [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE]
- Soft CTA: "Check it out here → [link]"

**Character count target:** 400-500 words

---

### Email 3 — Day 4: The Objection Handler
**Subject:** ["But [objection]..." — name the biggest hesitation]
**Preview text:** [Address the objection directly]

**Body:**
- Name the 3 most common reasons people hesitate to buy [PRODUCT_NAME]
  - Objection 1 (usually price): reframe value, use [PRICE_AND_OFFER] math
  - Objection 2 (usually complexity/time): address learning curve or setup time
  - Objection 3 (usually "I already have X"): explain the key differentiator
- Remind them of [YOUR_BONUS] as a risk-reducer
- CTA with affiliate link
- [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE]

**Character count target:** 350-450 words

---

### Email 4 — Day 6: Urgency + Bonus Stack
**Subject:** [deadline-based subject line]
**Preview text:** [reinforce the urgency]

**Body:**
- Open with the deadline/scarcity element from [PRICE_AND_OFFER]
- Stack the value: list everything they get (product + your bonus + any extras)
- Brief social proof: 1-2 sentences about results others have gotten
- Present the full value stack vs the price (e.g., "You're getting $X worth of [things] for $Y")
- Strong CTA with affiliate link
- [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE]
- P.S.: remind them of the deadline

**Character count target:** 300-400 words

---

### Email 5 — Day 7: Last Call
**Subject:** [urgency — "Last chance" or specific deadline time]
**Preview text:** ["This closes tonight at midnight" or equivalent]

**Body:**
- Open with the hard deadline (be specific: time and timezone)
- Keep this email short and direct — 150-200 words max
- 3-sentence recap of the offer + your bonus
- Single CTA: "Get [PRODUCT_NAME] + [YOUR_BONUS] before it closes → [link]"
- [AFFILIATE_DISCLOSURE]
- No P.S. — keep the focus on the CTA

**Character count target:** 150-200 words

---

## Sequence Notes
- Space emails: Day 1, 2, 4, 6, 7
- Use consistent "From" name so readers recognize you
- If [PRICE_AND_OFFER] has no real deadline, use: "I'm only promoting this through the end of [MONTH]"
- FTC requires disclosure on every email that contains an affiliate link
