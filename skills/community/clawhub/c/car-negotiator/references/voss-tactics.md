# Voss Negotiation Tactics for Car Deals
Source: Never Split the Difference – adapted for auto dealerships.

## 1. Mirroring
Repeat the last 1-3 words the dealer says as a question. Creates silence that forces them to elaborate.
> Dealer: "That's the best we can do on this trim."
> You: "Best you can do?"
> [Silence. Wait. They'll fill it.]

## 2. Labeling Emotions
Name what you think they're feeling. Defuses pressure and builds rapport.
> "It seems like you're working hard to find something that works for both of us."
> "It sounds like this inventory is moving fast."
> "It feels like there's not much room on the MSRP, but maybe elsewhere?"

## 3. Calibrated Questions ("How" and "What")
Never ask yes/no. Open-ended questions make them solve your problem.
> "How am I supposed to make that work with my budget?"
> "What would need to happen to get this closer to $X?"
> "How does that payment compare to what competitors are offering?"
> "What can you do on the acquisition fee / doc fee / MF?"

## 4. Ackerman Bidding Model
Make 4 offers at: 65% → 85% → 95% → 100% of your target price.
Each offer goes up by smaller increments. Final offer is an odd number (signals precision).
> Target: $40,000
> Offer 1: $26,000 (65%) — anchor low, no apology
> Offer 2: $34,000 (85%) — after they counter
> Offer 3: $38,000 (95%) — add a non-monetary item (e.g., free mats, tank of gas)
> Offer 4: $40,250 (odd number) — "This is truly everything I can do."

## 5. Strategic Silence
After making an offer, stop talking. Whoever speaks first loses leverage.

## 6. The "No" Technique
"No" gives the other side control and makes them feel safe. Invite it early.
> "Would it be ridiculous to ask for X?" (They say no → you've opened negotiation)
> "Is this a bad time to talk numbers?" (No → good time)

## 7. Late-Stage Add-Ons
After price is set, push back on:
- Doc/dealer fees (often negotiable, $200–$900)
- Acquisition fee (fixed by lender, not dealer — but worth asking)
- Paint protection / fabric guard / nitrogen (always say no)
- Extended warranty (price separately, buy later or online)

## 8. The Walk
"I appreciate your time — this isn't going to work for me today."
[Stand up. 70% of the time they'll call you back.]
