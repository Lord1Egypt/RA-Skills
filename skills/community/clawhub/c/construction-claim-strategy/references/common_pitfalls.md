# Common Claim Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

Most claims fail or are significantly weakened due to **process issues** rather than weak facts.

---

## The 10 Most Common Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Why It's Harmful | How to Avoid |
|---|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Jumping straight to drafting** without proper strategy | Leads to weak arguments and poor structure | Complete the 7-Dimension Strategy Worksheet first |
| 2 | **Volunteering unrequested information** | Creates new issues or admissions the other side can exploit | Apply strict Scope Control — respond only to what was asked |
| 3 | **Using the weakest argument first** | Damages credibility from the outset | Follow the argument ranking: Textual → Factual → Concede-then-Distinguish → Implied Terms |
| 4 | **Over-disclosing sensitive information too early** | Gives the other side material to attack your position | Match disclosure level to argument strength; defer where appropriate |
| 5 | **Mixing EOT and disruption claims** without clear separation | Creates confusion and weakens both claims | Keep them clearly separated unless both are genuinely present and can be distinguished |
| 6 | **Quantifying costs or delay before records are complete** | Numbers can be attacked if not fully supported | Defer detailed quantum until records are complete |
| 7 | **Ignoring or missing contractual notice requirements** | Claim can be time-barred or rejected | Check notice provisions early in Risk Assessment; use the Notice Requirements Checklist |
| 8 | **Using a poor or aggressive tone** in correspondence | Damages commercial relationship and credibility | Use professional, factual language throughout |
| 9 | **Failing to include a reservation of rights** | Can limit future claims or entitlements | Always include a clear Reservation of Rights |
| 10 | **Not conducting honest risk assessment** before submitting | Surprised by strong counter-arguments later | Complete the Risk Assessment dimension honestly |

## Key Prevention

Complete the **7-Dimension Strategy** first and apply strict **Scope Control** and **Disclosure Control**.
