# Brand Upgrade Competitor Analysis

Use when the task is to analyze repositioning, rebranding, young-ification, premiumization, or diversification.

## Core idea

Brand upgrade is not a slogan or logo refresh. It is a system-level redefinition of external perception and internal strategic direction.

## Common upgrade scenarios
- **Young-ification**: win younger users
- **Premiumization**: support higher price / stronger value perception
- **Diversification**: expand into new categories or growth curves

## Four questions to answer

1. What was the brand before?
2. What problem made change necessary?
3. What was the desired upgraded state?
4. What changes were actually made? (positioning, visual identity, product, communication, channel, organization)

## What to look for
- Was the upgrade necessary?
- Was it feasible given brand assets and organizational capability?
- Was the change systemic or just cosmetic?
- Did user perception actually shift?
- Were product and communication aligned?

## What to conclude

End by stating:
- whether the upgrade was structural or superficial
- what made it believable or unbelievable
- which elements are reusable for us
- which organizational or perception risks we should avoid
