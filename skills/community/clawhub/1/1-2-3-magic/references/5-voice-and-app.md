## 5 Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Whining Machine
**Your child whines every time you say no. It follows you through the house.**
- **Fix:** 1) Calmly say "That's 1." 2) No explanation. 3) If continues: "That's 2." 4) At 3: "Take a break." 5) Post-break: return to normal. No rehashing. 6) Within a week, whining will decrease dramatically.

### Scenario 2: Morning Madness
**Getting kids up, fed, dressed, and out the door is a daily battle.**
- **Fix:** 1) Create a visual routine chart (pictures of each step). 2) Use a timer for each step. 3) Praise compliance immediately. 4) For refusal: "That's 1 for not starting your morning checklist."

### Scenario 3: Sibling Warfare
**Kids fight constantly — toys, TV, whose turn it is.**
- **Fix:** 1) Both get counted. Don't judge who started. 2) Separate break locations. 3) Post-break, no rehashing. 4) For fighting over objects: "You can either share or the toy goes away."

### Scenario 4: Public Meltdowns
**Your child throws a tantrum at the grocery store and you're mortified.**
- **Fix:** 1) Same counting procedure. 2) Use a boring spot (bench, corner, wall). 3) Stay calm. You're not being judged — you're being observed using a real technique. 4) If it's truly unmanageable, leave the store. You're not giving in — you're exiting an impossible situation.

### Scenario 5: "But You Don't Understand!"
**Your child has emotions they can't process and acts out.**
- **Fix:** This requires Job 3, not counting. Use sympathetic listening: "That sounds really hard. Tell me more." Don't problem-solve. Don't minimize. Just listen. The child needs to feel heard before they can calm down enough to respond to discipline.
