## Use Case: <br>
Employees, external users, and developers use this skill to structure uncertain decisions by comparing hypotheses, stating priors, weighing evidence, updating probabilities, and identifying remaining uncertainty. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Bayesian framing can make uncertain judgments appear more precise or reliable than the available evidence supports, especially in medical, legal, financial, security, or other high-stakes decisions. <br>
Mitigation: Treat the analysis as advisory, require domain-specific safeguards or expert review for high-stakes use, and ask the agent to state assumptions and uncertainty clearly. <br>
Risk: Broad reasoning guidance may produce conclusions that depend heavily on flawed priors, incomplete alternatives, or weak evidence quality. <br>
Mitigation: Review the stated hypotheses, priors, likelihoods, and evidence quality before relying on the result, and gather additional evidence when it could change the decision. <br>
