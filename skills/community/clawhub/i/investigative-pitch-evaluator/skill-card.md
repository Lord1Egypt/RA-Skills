## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a reporter, freelancer, or investigative editor needs to turn a tip, FOIA return, document drop, or anomaly into an editor-ready pitch. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Reporters, freelancers, investigative editors, and newsroom intake desks use this skill to convert tips, document drops, FOIA returns, data anomalies, or source allegations into a draft investigative pitch packet and editorial evaluation rubric. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Pitch drafts may contain source identities, sealed records, embargoed material, or legal-risk details. <br>
Mitigation: Treat those details as sensitive, avoid exposing them outside the reporter's authorized workflow, and require human editor or lawyer review before acting on the output. <br>
Risk: The skill can produce an editorial verdict or reporting plan that may be mistaken for a commissioning or legal decision. <br>
Mitigation: Use the output only as a draft; the skill requires reporter or editor sign-off and flags legal or ethics blocks for human review. <br>
