Style Pack: illustrative-cinematic (illustrative cinematic)
- Favor immersive illustrated teaching slides that can still carry meaningful on-slide information; use full-bleed overlays, concept boards, mirrored explainers, and teaching grids more often than rigid corporate splits.
- Composition attitude: scene-rich and atmospheric, but still clearly didactic. Prefer elegant grouping, illustrated modules, and cinematic negative space over sketch-note roughness or heavy boxed cards.
- Use warm ivory, pale stone, muted blue-gray, slate, and restrained deep-blue or soft-gold accents.
- Typography attitude: premium editorial or luminous humanist title with crisp readable body text and refined section labels; text should feel designed, not hand-drawn.
- Text treatment: on structured slides, use soft illustrated reading zones, divider lines, or clean module fields rather than obvious heavy panels; on full-bleed slides, place text directly on the image with subtle tonal shaping and no visible caption card. Use rule lines sparingly and with hierarchy: do not stack a module top rule plus a decorative underline under the same heading unless the slide explicitly needs that treatment.
- Lighting: bright cinematic daylight or polished interior illumination with soft depth and atmosphere; allow gentle glow and spatial depth, but avoid dark drama and avoid scratchy sketch texture.
- Background bias: illustrated architecture, symbolic environments, polished concept scenes, and immersive teaching imagery that helps viewers feel inside the subject while still reading clearly.
