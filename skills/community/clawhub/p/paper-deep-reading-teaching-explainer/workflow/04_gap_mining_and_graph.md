# Step 4: Gap Mining And Graph

Use:

- limitations
- weak settings
- failure cases
- reviewer concerns
- rebuttal exchanges
- unresolved issues

to extract:

- new scientific problems
- improvement directions
- innovation hooks
- graph node candidates
- graph relation candidates

Use the research-generative weakness conversion rule:

\[
M_i \text{ works if } H_i \Rightarrow \text{new direction: make } M_i \text{ work under } \neg H_i.
\]

For every candidate direction, record:

- the hidden assumption being violated
- why the current method becomes fragile
- the unavailable ideal mechanism that would solve it
- the possible surrogate or new mechanism
- whether the direction is ordinary engineering, cross-domain transfer, or boundary-pushing research
