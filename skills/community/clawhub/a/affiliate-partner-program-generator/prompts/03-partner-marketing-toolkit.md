# Prompt 3: Partner Marketing Toolkit

## Purpose
Build every piece of content your partners need to promote effectively. Most affiliate programs fail because partners don't know what to say — they get a link and no copy, so they never post. This prompt generates the full toolkit so partners can start promoting the day they're approved.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a content strategist building a partner marketing toolkit. Generate all content assets a new affiliate needs to start promoting effectively on Day 1.

**Program Details:**
- Company name: [NAME]
- Product/service: [1–2 sentences]
- Target customer: [ICP — who buys this]
- Top 3 benefits: [list them]
- Key differentiator vs. competitors: [what makes you different]
- Price: [price point]
- Trial/free tier available? [yes/no — if yes, describe]
- Affiliate link format: [e.g., nudgepay.com?ref=PARTNER_CODE or "TBD"]
- Any current promotions: [e.g., "30-day free trial" or "use code PARTNER20 for 20% off first month"]

**Generate the following:**

## 1. Email Swipe Copy (5 Variants)
Write 5 email templates for partners to send to their lists. Each email must include a placeholder for the affiliate link [AFFILIATE_LINK].

**Email 1 — Short (100 words):** Quick mention / passing recommendation
**Email 2 — Medium (200 words):** Problem-solution format with brief product overview
**Email 3 — Long (350 words):** Full story format — paint the pain, introduce the product, share proof, CTA
**Email 4 — Story-based (250 words):** Personal anecdote format — written as if the partner discovered this product ("I was struggling with X until I found Y...")
**Email 5 — Promotional (150 words):** Urgency/offer-based — ideal for a launch, discount, or seasonal promotion

For each email, provide:
- Subject line (with 2 alternatives)
- Preview text (50 chars)
- Body
- CTA with affiliate link placeholder

## 2. Social Post Templates (10 Posts)

**LinkedIn (3 posts):**
- Post 1 (200 words): Thought leadership angle — share insight about the problem this product solves, mention the product naturally at the end
- Post 2 (150 words): Before/after format — the transformation the product enables
- Post 3 (100 words): Direct recommendation with a genuine endorsement tone

**Twitter/X (3 posts):**
- Tweet 1: Problem awareness (280 chars max)
- Tweet 2: Product recommendation with link (280 chars max)
- Tweet 3: Social proof or stat-based hook (280 chars max)

**Facebook / Instagram Caption (2 posts):**
- Post 1 (150 words): Conversational tone, relatable scenario, product as solution
- Post 2 (100 words): List format — "5 reasons I recommend [product]..."

**Instagram Story / Short-form Script (2 posts):**
- Story 1 (30-second script): Hook → Problem → Solution → CTA
- Story 2 (15-second script): Testimonial-style — "quick thing I found that..." format

## 3. Promotional Angles (3 Messaging Frameworks)
Write 3 distinct positioning angles partners can use across all channels. Each angle should feel different:
- **Angle 1 — Problem-Aware Buyer:** Written for someone who knows they have the problem but hasn't found the solution yet
- **Angle 2 — Solution-Aware Buyer:** Written for someone comparing options; why this product wins
- **Angle 3 — Product-Aware Buyer:** Written for someone who's heard of this but hasn't bought; handles common objections

For each angle, provide: headline, 3-sentence description, and 1 CTA variant.

## 4. Partner FAQ Document (15 Q&As)
Write answers to the 15 most common questions partners will have:
- Questions about commissions (when do I get paid? what if a customer refunds?)
- Questions about promotion rules (can I run paid ads? can I use coupons?)
- Questions about the product (is there a free trial? what's the refund rate?)
- Questions about tracking (how do I know my link is working? what's a cookie window?)
- Questions about the program (how do I get to the next tier? who do I contact if I have an issue?)

## 5. Co-Branding Guidelines (1 page)
Write a simple co-branding rules document:
- Approved company name variants (e.g., "NudgePay" not "Nudge Pay" or "nudgepay")
- Logo usage rules (where to request assets, what's prohibited)
- Approved claims partners can make (e.g., "#1 rated invoicing tool for freelancers")
- Prohibited claims (e.g., no income guarantees, no "free forever" if it isn't, no competitor comparisons that are inaccurate)
- FTC disclosure language to copy/paste (required for all promotional content)

## 6. Product Comparison Sheet (for Reviews and Blog Posts)
Create a comparison table partners can use when writing reviews:
| Feature | [Your Product] | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
Populate 10 features. Where your product wins, use specific proof (numbers, speeds, limits). Where competitors win, be honest — this builds trust with readers.

Below the table, write 3 "best for" use case blurbs:
- "[Your Product] is best for..." (100 words)
- "[Competitor A] is best for..." (50 words — honest comparison)
- "[Competitor B] is best for..." (50 words — honest comparison)
```

---

## Example Input

- Company: NudgePay
- Product: Automated invoice follow-up and payment tracking SaaS for freelancers and agencies
- ICP: Freelancers billing $5K–$50K/month, small agencies (2–20 people)
- Top 3 benefits: 60% fewer late payments, saves 3+ hours/week on chasing invoices, gets paid 11 days faster on average
- Differentiator: Only tool with behavioral payment prediction (flags which clients are likely to be late before they're late)
- Price: $59/month per seat (+ team plans)
- Trial: 14-day free trial, no credit card required
- Affiliate link: nudgepay.com?ref=PARTNER_CODE
- Promotion: "Start your free 14-day trial — no credit card required"

---

## Tips for Best Results

- For email swipe copy, remind partners they should personalize the "story-based" template (Email 4) with their own experience — the best-performing affiliate emails read like personal recommendations, not ads
- The comparison table is the most important asset for bloggers and YouTubers doing product reviews — include the comparison table even if it shows competitor strengths honestly
- The FTC disclosure in co-branding guidelines is legally required for any affiliate or sponsored content — make sure partners include it
