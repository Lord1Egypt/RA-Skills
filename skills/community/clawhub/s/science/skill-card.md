## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Scientific claims can be outdated, incomplete, or misleading when presented without current verification. <br>
Mitigation: Verify important or current claims against reliable scientific sources, and label consensus, active debate, and emerging speculation clearly. <br>
Risk: Suggested hands-on experiments can create safety hazards, especially for children or activities involving heat, chemicals, tools, or biological materials. <br>
Mitigation: Require adult supervision where appropriate, include hazard warnings, and favor low-risk demonstrations with clear materials and setup guidance. <br>
Risk: Preliminary findings, small samples, correlation, or weak replication can be mistaken for settled science. <br>
Mitigation: Call out sample size, replication status, confounders, and the difference between correlation and causation. <br>
