# Prompt 3 — Content Marketing System

**Purpose:** Build a complete, sustainable content strategy for coaches and consultants: authority pillars, a 30-day calendar with platform-specific formats, and a repeatable weekly workflow.

---

## Instructions

Run Prompt 1 first. Use your Methodology Name, ICP, and 3-Phase Transformation Map from Prompt 1.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a content strategist for coaching and consulting businesses. Build a complete Content Marketing System for this coach.

**COACH INFO (from Prompt 1 output):**
- Methodology name: [e.g., The Executive Ascent Framework]
- Ideal client: [e.g., mid-level tech managers, Directors to VPs, 10-15 years experience, $150K-$220K salary]
- The 3 phases of your program: [e.g., Phase 1: Executive Audit / Phase 2: Influence Architecture / Phase 3: Leadership Legacy]
- Primary platform: [e.g., LinkedIn] 
- Secondary platforms: [e.g., Instagram, email newsletter]
- Content goal: [e.g., book 5 discovery calls per week through organic content]
- Your biggest differentiator from other coaches: [e.g., I worked in corporate HR at director level — I know how promotion decisions are actually made]
- 3 client results to reference: [e.g., Maria went from Director to VP in 14 months, James negotiated $45K raise, Priya got recruited without applying]

**OUTPUT FORMAT:**

Produce a complete Content Marketing System with these exact sections:

---

### 1. Content Pillar Framework (5 Pillars)
Design 5 content pillars tied to the coach's ICP and methodology. For each pillar:
- Pillar name (2-4 words, punchy)
- What this pillar does for the audience (educates, inspires, or converts)
- What this pillar does for the coach's business (authority, lead gen, trust)
- 5 specific content topics within this pillar
- Example post title or hook for each topic

**The 5 Pillar Types (ensure all 5 are covered):**
1. **Authority Pillar** — insider expertise the ICP can't get elsewhere
2. **Transformation Pillar** — before/after client stories, results, proof
3. **Myth-Busting Pillar** — common misconceptions your ICP holds that keep them stuck
4. **Process Pillar** — how your methodology works, step by step (education)
5. **Personal Story Pillar** — your journey, your failures, your turning point

---

### 2. Weekly Content Schedule (Minimum Viable Content — 5 posts/week)

Design a repeatable weekly posting schedule. For each day:
- Day and platform
- Content type (story, carousel, how-to, myth-bust, results, quote, personal)
- Pillar it belongs to
- Time commitment to create (honest estimate)
- Who in the ICP this piece primarily attracts

**Monday through Friday schedule, with Saturday as optional "bonus" format.**

---

### 3. LinkedIn Content Formats (6 Formats with Templates)

For each of the 6 highest-performing LinkedIn formats for coaches, provide:
- Format name and optimal length
- Hook formula (the first line that stops the scroll)
- Structure/outline
- A complete example post using this coach's niche (150-350 words)
- Engagement hook at the end (call to comment/share/DM)

**The 6 Formats:**
1. The Personal Story Post (breakthrough moment)
2. The Contrarian Take (myth-bust + correct framing)
3. The How-To Thread (process post, step-by-step)
4. The Client Win (results without violating privacy)
5. The "Nobody Talks About This" Insight (authority post)
6. The Engagement Question (drives comments, algorithm boost)

---

### 4. 30-Day Content Calendar (Full Month)

Produce a complete 30-day calendar with specific post ideas, not generic categories. Format as a table:

| Day | Platform | Pillar | Format | Specific Hook/Title |
|-----|----------|--------|--------|---------------------|
| 1 | LinkedIn | Authority | Insight | [Specific post hook] |
| 2 | Instagram | Transformation | Story | [Specific post hook] |
...and so on for all 30 days.

Requirements:
- Mix platforms per the coach's profile
- Every post hook must be specific (not "share a tip about leadership")
- Include at least 4 direct enrollment CTAs across the 30 days (DM me, link in bio, application open)
- Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% education/entertainment, 20% promotion

---

### 5. Email Newsletter System (Weekly)

Design a repeatable weekly email newsletter framework:
- Subject line formula (what works for coaches: curiosity + specificity + brevity)
- Email structure (4 sections, under 400 words total)
- 5 specific subject line examples using this coach's niche
- One complete sample newsletter issue (400 words max)

---

### 6. Content Repurposing Workflow

Show how to turn ONE piece of long-form content into 7 pieces across platforms:
- 1 LinkedIn article or newsletter → [specific 6 derivatives]
- Step-by-step repurposing instructions
- Time estimate for each derivative
- Which derivative gets which traffic goal (awareness vs. engagement vs. conversion)

---

### 7. Lead Generation Content Strategy (Direct Enrollment Path)

Design 3 pieces of "gateway content" that lead directly to discovery call bookings:
- Content piece 1: A lead magnet (checklist, quiz, or guide) — title, outline, delivery mechanism
- Content piece 2: A "pinned post" or bio-link page — what it contains and why
- Content piece 3: A DM outreach script for people who engage with posts but don't book — 3 messages, conversational, non-pushy

---

### 8. 90-Day Content Milestone Map
What does "good" look like for this coach at 30, 60, and 90 days if they execute this system consistently?
- Follower/subscriber growth targets (realistic, not hype)
- Engagement rate benchmarks
- Discovery call volume targets
- Revenue opportunity (how many calls booked → clients enrolled at their price)
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be honest in the inputs.** If your proof points are small, say so — the system will calibrate to where you actually are.
- **Pick ONE primary platform** and own it before adding a second. Most coaches dilute by going wide too fast.
- **The 30-day calendar is a starting point, not a prison.** Replace any topic that doesn't feel authentic with something from your pillar list.
- **Week 1 posts should be personal story.** New audiences need to trust you before they follow your advice. Start with your own turning point.
