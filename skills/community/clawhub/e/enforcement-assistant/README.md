# Enforcement Application Assistant

## Use Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Judgment Debt Recovery
A plaintiff won a civil judgment for ¥150,000 in a loan dispute, but 6 months have passed and the debtor has ignored all payment demands while continuing to operate a profitable business. **What the skill does:** Verifies the judgment is final and within the 2-year enforcement window, drafts the enforcement application with proper court jurisdiction, organizes the required supporting documents (judgment copy, proof of effectiveness, debtor asset information), and provides guidance on asset investigation measures. **Expected outcome:** A complete enforcement application package ready for filing, with clear instructions on follow-up actions including bank account freezes and asset seizure requests.

### Scenario 2: Arbitration Award Enforcement
A company obtained a favorable CIETAC arbitration award for ¥800,000 against a business partner, but the partner refuses to pay and has begun transferring assets. **What the skill does:** Guides the applicant through the special enforcement procedure for arbitration awards, identifies the competent intermediate court for filing, drafts the enforcement application emphasizing urgency due to asset dissipation risk, and recommends applying for property preservation measures simultaneously. **Expected outcome:** A strategically timed enforcement filing that includes asset preservation requests to prevent the debtor from hiding assets before the court can act.

### Scenario 3: Notarized Debt Instrument Execution
A creditor holds a notarized debt instrument (公证债权文书) for ¥300,000 with explicit enforceability clauses, and the debtor has defaulted on the repayment schedule. **What the skill does:** Explains the streamlined enforcement path for notarized debt instruments, drafts the direct enforcement application without requiring prior litigation, and prepares the asset information appendix listing known bank accounts and real estate holdings. **Expected outcome:** A direct enforcement application that bypasses litigation, enabling faster court action to freeze accounts and seize identified assets.

## Overview

This skill helps users prepare civil enforcement applications (民事执行申请) and navigate the enforcement process after obtaining a court judgment or arbitration award. It guides users through the required documents, procedures, and timelines for applying to the court for compulsory enforcement.

**⚠️ Important Disclaimer**: This tool provides procedural guidance only. It does not constitute legal advice, nor does it guarantee enforcement success. Enforcement outcomes depend on the debtor's assets and other factors. Always consult a qualified attorney for complex enforcement matters.

## When to Use This Skill

- After obtaining a court judgment that needs enforcement
- Preparing an enforcement application (执行申请书)
- Understanding the enforcement process and timeline
- Gathering required documents for enforcement
- Learning about enforcement measures and options

## ⚠️ Critical Timing

Application must be filed within **2 years** from the last day of the performance period specified in the judgment. Missing this deadline may result in losing the right to enforce.

## Eligible Enforcement Documents

- Civil judgments (民事判决书)
- Civil mediation documents (民事调解书)
- Payment orders (支付令)
- Arbitration awards (仲裁裁决)
- Notarized debt instruments (公证债权文书)

## Available Enforcement Measures

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Property inquiry | Query debtor assets |
| Bank account freeze | Freeze accounts upon discovery |
| Asset seizure | Seize/attach tangible property |
| Real estate auction | Auction real property |
| Income garnishment | Garnish salary/business income |
| Travel/consumption restrictions | Restrict high consumption and travel |
| Credit blacklisting | Add to untrustworthy debtor list |
