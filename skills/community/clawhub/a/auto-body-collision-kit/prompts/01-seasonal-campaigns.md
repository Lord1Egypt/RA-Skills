# Prompt 01 — Seasonal Campaigns & Promotions
## Auto Body & Collision Repair Marketing Kit v1.0

**Purpose:** Generate compliance-audited seasonal and event-driven marketing campaigns for auto body and collision repair shops.

**All 7 compliance moats active in every output:**
1. OSHA isocyanate/spray booth gate — no "fume-free" or "non-toxic paint" claims
2. EPA NESHAP 6H/AQMD gate — no "zero-emission" or "eco-friendly" without documentation
3. EPA RCRA gate — "licensed disposal" requires EPA ID/manifest reference
4. I-CAR/ASE/OEM certification accuracy — correct credential claims only
5. OEM vs. aftermarket parts disclosure — parts type disclosed on all offer copy
6. FTC/NRS 598 warranty & estimate gate — no deductible waiver; written estimate language
7. NRS 487 salvage title gate — no "like new" without branded title disclosure

## CAMPAIGN 2 — Summer Road Trip Prep (June–August)

### Email Campaign — Summer Safety Prep

**Subject Line A:** "Before you drive to Zion/Bryce/Sedona — 15 minutes that could save your trip"
**Subject Line B:** "Summer road season: is your collision damage documented?"
**Subject Line C:** "[Shop Name] summer check: undisclosed damage becomes your problem at the border"

**Body:**

---

Every summer, thousands of Clark County residents load up for Zion, Bryce Canyon, Sedona, and the Pacific Coast. A vehicle with existing undisclosed collision damage that fails in another state — or gets flagged at a rental return — becomes your problem at the worst possible time.

**Pre-trip structural check:**
Existing frame damage, improperly repaired collision history, or compromised structural components create real risk on desert mountain roads. We can identify whether prior repairs meet structural standards before you load the car.

**Undisclosed prior repairs:**
If you bought your vehicle used and aren't sure about its repair history, a pre-trip inspection documents current condition. If problems exist, knowing before you leave is better than knowing on US-89 in Utah.

**ADAS recalibration after any bodywork:**
Any repair involving bumper covers, windshields, or front/rear panels on modern vehicles may require ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance System) sensor recalibration. Cameras, radar modules, and ultrasonic sensors are embedded in body panels. Replacing a bumper without recalibrating the collision warning system defeats the system entirely.

We perform or coordinate ADAS recalibration as part of complete collision repair. Written documentation provided.
