## Use Case: <br>
External researchers, students, and research-focused developers use this skill to build effective Google Scholar searches, evaluate academic results, find full text, track citations, and avoid common citation mistakes. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Google Scholar results can include predatory journals, retracted papers, preliminary preprints, or misleading citation signals. <br>
Mitigation: Verify venue quality, methodology, publication date, citation context, and retraction or correction status before citing or relying on a paper. <br>
Risk: Google Scholar coverage and ranking are incomplete and opaque, with bias toward English-language and STEM literature. <br>
Mitigation: Use multiple search queries and supplement Scholar with domain databases such as PubMed or IEEE when coverage matters. <br>
Risk: Private or unpublished research material may be exposed if supplied to an agent during a search session. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid sharing private or unpublished research material unless it is intended to be used in that session, and follow institutional and publisher access rules. <br>
