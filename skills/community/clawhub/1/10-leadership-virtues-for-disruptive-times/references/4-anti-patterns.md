> Source: *10 Leadership Virtues for Disruptive Times* by Tom Ziglar, Key Ideas 3-4

## Anti-Patterns: What Not to Do

### 1. Command-and-Control Leadership

**The Old Way:** Giving orders, expecting compliance, measuring presence over performance. The mentality that "because I'm the boss, you do what I say."

**Why It Fails in Disruptive Times:**
- Remote teams can't be micro-managed — you literally can't see them
- Commands create compliance, not commitment
- Authoritarian leadership crushes initiative and creativity
- People disengage when they feel like cogs

**Instead:** Shift to Coach Leadership. Ask questions. Empower decisions. Trust your team.

### 2. Taking the Bigger Piece of Cake

**The Old Way:** Taking credit for team wins, deflecting blame for failures, putting your own interests ahead of the team's.

**Why It Destroys Trust:**
- Team members stop going the extra mile when their contributions aren't acknowledged
- Blame-shifting creates a culture of fear and CYA (Cover Your Ass)
- Selfish leadership is quickly noticed and deeply resented

**Instead:** Give credit generously. Take blame personally. Ask "How can I serve my team?" before "What can my team do for me?"

### 3. Emotional Reactivity Under Pressure

**The Old Way:** Firing off angry emails, raising your voice in meetings, making decisions out of fear or frustration.

**Why It Damages Your Leadership:**
- Reactive leaders lose respect faster than any other type
- Emotional outbursts create an unsafe environment
- Decisions made in emotional states are almost always bad

**Instead:** Practice the Self-Control Pause. Respond, don't react. Let the emotion pass before the words leave your mouth.

### 4. Neglecting the Atmosphere

**The Old Way:** Focusing only on deadlines, metrics, and deliverables while ignoring team dynamics and morale.

**Why It Backfires:**
- A toxic or disconnected atmosphere directly reduces productivity
- Burnout rates skyrocket when human needs are ignored
- The best people leave bad atmospheres first

**Instead:** Make atmosphere your first priority. Solve the environment before trying to solve problems.

### 5. Positivity Without Substance

**The Old Way:** Forced cheerfulness and empty pep talks that ignore real problems.

**Why It Feels Insincere:**
- Teams see through fake positivity immediately
- Ignoring real issues erodes trust
- Positivity without action is just noise

**Instead:** Acknowledge the difficulty first, *then* provide encouragement and a path forward.

> **Case: The Micromanager CEO** (Key Idea 3)
> A CEO demanded hourly check-ins from his remote team, approved every expense under $50, and rewrote all client emails himself. Productivity plummeted, three top performers quit, and the company missed two quarters of targets. By the time the board intervened, the damage was done.
> **Key takeaway**: Control is not leadership. The tighter you grip, the more slips through.

> **Case: The Blame Shifter** (Key Idea 4)
> A department head blamed her team for a missed deadline in a company-wide meeting, even though the delay was caused by her own indecision. The team never trusted her again. The next year, attrition in her department hit 60%.
> **Key takeaway**: Taking credit for wins and deflecting blame for losses is the fastest way to lose your team.
