# Budget and Bidding Reference Card

## Budget Allocation Frameworks

### The 70/20/10 Rule (New Accounts)

| Category | Allocation | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Proven Campaigns | 70% | Audiences and creatives with demonstrated performance |
| Scaling Tests | 20% | Expanding winners to new audiences or budgets |
| Experimental | 10% | New concepts, untested audiences, new formats |

Shifts to 50/30/20 by month 3-4 as proven campaigns grow.

### Funnel Allocation by Business Stage

| Stage | TOF | MOF | BOF | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch | 80% | 10% | 10% | No retargeting audiences exist |
| Early ($1-5K/mo) | 70% | 15% | 15% | Small retargeting pools |
| Growth ($5-25K/mo) | 60% | 20% | 20% | Balanced funnel |
| Scaling ($25-100K/mo) | 55% | 25% | 20% | Larger MOF audiences |
| Mature ($100K+/mo) | 50% | 25% | 25% | Full funnel at scale |

### Breakeven CPA Calculator

**Formula**: Breakeven CPA = AOV x Gross Margin %

| AOV | Margin | Breakeven CPA | Recommended Target (70%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| $35 | 60% | $21.00 | $14.70 |
| $55 | 55% | $30.25 | $21.18 |
| $85 | 50% | $42.50 | $29.75 |
| $120 | 45% | $54.00 | $37.80 |
| $200 | 40% | $80.00 | $56.00 |

Setting targets at 70% of breakeven provides margin for overhead, returns, and profit. Adjust if LTV justifies higher first-purchase CPA.

## Bidding Strategies Comparison

### Lowest Cost (Automatic)
- **How**: Spends full budget pursuing most conversions at lowest cost. No caps.
- **Best for**: New campaigns, learning phase, data gathering, flexible CPA requirements
- **Pros**: Full budget utilization, fast data collection, simplest setup
- **Cons**: No cost ceiling, CPA can spike, costs rise with scaling
- **Switch when**: 50+ conversions/week achieved and target CPA known

### Cost Cap
- **How**: Sets average CPA target. Individual conversions may exceed cap but average approximates it.
- **Best for**: Scaling with known CPA target, transitioning from Lowest Cost
- **Pros**: Balances cost and volume, more predictable than Lowest Cost
- **Cons**: May underdeliver if cap too aggressive, learning period may exceed cap
- **Setting**: Start 20-30% above actual target. Tighten 5-10%/week. Loosen if delivery drops below 70%.

### Bid Cap
- **How**: Hard maximum bid per auction. Never exceeded.
- **Best for**: Strict CPA ceilings, hard margin constraints, advanced advertisers
- **Pros**: Absolute cost control, prevents spikes
- **Cons**: Significant underdelivery risk, limits algorithm, requires frequent adjustment
- **Setting**: Set at maximum acceptable CPA. Understand you miss all conversions requiring higher bids.

### Minimum ROAS
- **How**: Sets minimum return on ad spend. Prioritizes higher-value conversions.
- **Best for**: Ecommerce with strong purchase value data, variable AOV, BOF campaigns
- **Pros**: Optimizes for revenue not just volume, accounts for variable order values
- **Cons**: Requires accurate pixel value data, may underdeliver, biases toward high-AOV products
- **Setting**: Start at breakeven ROAS, increase 10-20% increments. Typical range 2.0-4.0x.

### Selection Matrix

| Scenario | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Brand new campaign | Lowest Cost |
| Exiting learning, CPA acceptable | Cost Cap |
| Hard margin ceiling | Bid Cap |
| Variable AOV retargeting | Min ROAS |
| ASC launch | Lowest Cost |
| ASC mature | Cost Cap or Min ROAS |
| Seasonal sale, limited time | Lowest Cost |

## Daily vs. Lifetime Budgets

**Daily**: Max spend per day (Meta may exceed by 25% but balances weekly). Use for evergreen campaigns — 80% of all campaigns.

**Lifetime**: Total budget across campaign duration. Meta optimizes spend across days. Use for promotions, launches, and seasonal sales. Enables dayparting.

## Scaling Methodologies

### Vertical Scaling
Increase budget on winning ad sets incrementally:
- 20-30% increase every 3-5 days
- Never exceed 50% at once (risks resetting learning)
- If CPA rises 30%+, revert and wait 5 days
- Stop when CPA consistently exceeds target

### Horizontal Scaling
Duplicate winning ad sets with new audiences:
- Only duplicate ad sets profitable for 7+ days
- Target non-overlapping audiences
- Start at same budget as original winner
- Allow 5-7 days for learning per new ad set
- Cap at 3-5 new tests per week

### ASC Scaling
- 30-50% budget increases are generally safe
- Monitor audience split (new vs. existing customers)
- Add more creative variants if existing customer % exceeds cap

## Seasonal Adjustment Guidance

### Annual CPM Cycle

| Period | Trend | Action |
|---|---|---|
| January | Low (-20-30% from Q4) | Increase prospecting; best CPMs of the year |
| Feb-May | Gradually rising | Normal ops, good for creative testing |
| Jun-Aug | Moderate to low | Summer dip; efficient prospecting opportunity |
| September | Rising | Pre-holiday scaling; lock in audiences and creative |
| Oct-Nov | Peak (+50-100%) | Highest CPMs; finalize holiday creative |
| December | Very high, drops post-25th | Gift peak mid-month; clearance opportunity late |

### Key Recommendations
- **Pre-Holiday (8 weeks out)**: Increase TOF 30-50% to build retargeting pools before CPM spikes
- **Peak Holiday**: Shift toward BOF retargeting with larger audiences from pre-holiday prospecting
- **Post-Holiday (January)**: Aggressive prospecting at 20-30% CPM discounts
- **Q2/Q3**: Maintain spend while competitors pull back; test new creative and audiences
