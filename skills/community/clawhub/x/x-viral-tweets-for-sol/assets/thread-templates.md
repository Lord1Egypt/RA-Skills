# Thread Templates

Threads on CT live or die on tweet 1 (hook) and tweet 2 (why-care). Use a template only as scaffold — voice in every tweet.

## A. Receipts → playbook

```
1/ [outcome with number, e.g., "$0 → $42k mrr in 6 months building on solana"]. here's the exact playbook.

2/ first, context. [who you are, what you were building, what audience]. so the playbook is calibrated.

3/ [step 1 — usually a positioning or scoping move].
   what i did:
   why it worked:

4/ [step 2 — usually a distribution or audience move].

5/ [step 3 — usually a product or pricing move].

6/ [step 4 — usually compounding / retention].

7/ what i'd do differently:
   - [thing]
   - [thing]

8/ if you're building on solana right now, dms open. shipping > theorizing.
```

## B. Counter-conventional teardown

```
1/ everyone says [popular claim]. i tried it on [your context]. it's wrong. here's what actually works.

2/ the conventional argument:
   - [point]
   - [point]
   - [point]

3/ what they miss: [the one thing the conventional wisdom doesn't account for].

4/ what i tried instead: [your approach, concrete].

5/ result: [number / outcome].

6/ [bonus nuance — when the conventional wisdom is right, when it isn't].

7/ tl;dr: [10-word compression of the whole thread].
```

## C. Forensic breakdown

```
1/ i read [every X / 90 days of Y / all proposals from Z]. here's what's actually changing in solana right now.

2/ method: [one-line on how you gathered the data, so the thread feels rigorous, not vibes].

3/ finding 1: [non-obvious pattern].

4/ finding 2: [non-obvious pattern].

5/ finding 3: [non-obvious pattern].

6/ what nobody is saying: [synthesis claim].

7/ implication for builders: [actionable].

8/ implication for traders: [actionable].

9/ full notes / data: [link or "dm me"].
```

## D. Personal arc → universal lesson

```
1/ [emotional opening, e.g., "almost rage-quit crypto in 2022."]. here's what building through it taught me about solana.

2/ context: [who you were, where you were].

3/ the moment: [the specific event that broke or rebuilt you].

4/ what i thought it meant at the time: [wrong interpretation].

5/ what it actually meant: [correct interpretation, in retrospect].

6/ lesson 1: [generalizable].

7/ lesson 2: [generalizable].

8/ if you're in that moment right now: [direct address, short, real].
```

## E. Narrative primer

```
1/ everyone's about to be talking about [narrative]. you're early if you understand it now. here's the primer.

2/ what it is, in one sentence: [tight definition].

3/ why now: [the catalyst — tech unlock, regulatory shift, capital rotation, culture].

4/ who's building in it on solana: [3–6 names with one-line on each].

5/ what it actually unlocks for users: [the use-case, not the tech].

6/ what's overhyped about it: [contrarian filter, builds credibility].

7/ what to watch in the next 30 days: [signals].

8/ if you're a builder, the opportunity is [specific gap].
```

## Thread voice rules

- Tweet 1 is the *only* tweet that gets read by everyone. Treat it as a standalone post.
- Every middle tweet should be screenshottable on its own. If someone quotes tweet 4, it should still slap.
- Numbering only for list threads. For story arcs, no numbers.
- Last tweet = payoff + soft CTA. Hard CTAs ("follow me!") underperform soft ones ("dms open").
- 10 tweets is a hard ceiling for almost every thread. If you need more, it's actually a long-form piece.
- No "follow for more!" — assumes value not yet earned.
- No "let me know your thoughts in the comments" — corporate.
