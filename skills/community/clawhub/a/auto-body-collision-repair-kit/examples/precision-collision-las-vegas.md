Marcus and the team at Precision Collision Center,

Las Vegas averages 4-7 significant hail events between June and August. If your vehicle took a hit this storm season, here's what you need to know before the heat makes it worse.

**Paintless Dent Repair (PDR) works — with limits.** PDR is the fastest, most cost-effective option for hail dents where the paint is fully intact. No primer, no filler, no paint matching required. If your dents are visible but the paint surface is smooth, PDR can restore your panels in a single day.

However: if paint is cracked, chipped, or has any white stress marks, traditional repair is required. PDR cannot fix damaged paint — attempting it extends the timeline and increases cost. We assess every vehicle with a hail light before quoting, so you know exactly what you're working with.

**Nevada's UV index works against you.** Once paint is cracked, the UV exposure in a Las Vegas summer accelerates clearcoat breakdown and oxidation. What's a PDR job today can become a paint job in 60 days if left untreated.

**Insurance claims:** Most comprehensive policies cover hail damage minus your deductible. We document every dent with photos and provide a full written estimate. If hidden damage is found during repair, we file a supplement — your insurer makes the final payment determination.

As an independent shop (not on any insurer's preferred network), we work exclusively for you. Our job is to document your actual damage accurately and repair it correctly — not to minimize cycle time for an insurer's cost targets.

**H1:** Las Vegas Collision Repair — I-CAR Gold Class, Independent, Working For You

**Page Body:**

When your vehicle is in an accident, the shop you choose matters more than your insurance company's recommendation. Precision Collision Center is an I-CAR Gold Class certified, independent collision repair shop serving Las Vegas, Henderson, and Summerlin.

**What "Independent" Means for You**

Many shops near you participate in insurance company Direct Repair Programs (DRPs) — preferred vendor networks with negotiated labor rates and parts sourcing requirements. DRP shops work within their insurer partner's cost parameters.

We don't. As an independent shop, we work exclusively for you. Our estimate documents your actual damage based on your vehicle's condition and OEM repair specifications — not an insurer's cycle time target.

**The Collision Repair Process**

1. **Free Written Estimate** — We photograph every impact point, scan for structural deformation, and provide a written estimate with part type disclosed (OEM or, if required by your insurer, aftermarket — you'll know before we order)
2. **Teardown + Supplement** — Hidden damage discovered during teardown is documented and photographed. We file a supplement with your insurer for approval. Your insurer makes the payment determination.
3. **Structural Repair** — Frame and unibody straightening using computerized measuring (Car-O-Liner CV3) to OEM tolerance specifications
4. **Parts + Assembly** — OEM parts by default. If your insurer specifies LKQ/aftermarket parts, we notify you in writing before proceeding
5. **PPG Envirobase Refinish** — Waterborne topcoat system (2.8 lb VOC/gal, ≤EPA NESHAP 3.5 lb/gal limit); HVLP application; computer color matching with spectrophotometer
6. **ADAS Calibration** — If your vehicle has cameras or sensors in the bumper, hood, or pillars, we perform post-repair ADAS recalibration using OEM-level scan tools
7. **Quality Control + Delivery** — Written warranty terms provided at delivery: covers paint and labor; excludes subsequent collision, improper washing; transfers to new owner

**I-CAR Gold Class: What It Means**

I-CAR Gold Class is the collision repair industry's primary quality credential. It requires annual training in structural, non-structural, refinishing, and mechanical/electrical repair roles. Our current Gold Class status can be verified at collision.org/goldclass.

Not every shop in Las Vegas maintains active Gold Class status — it requires annual renewal and role coverage that lapses if training is missed.
