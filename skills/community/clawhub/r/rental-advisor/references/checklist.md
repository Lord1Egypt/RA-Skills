# Rental Contract Review Detailed Checklist

## Pre-Signing Must-Do Items

### Identity Verification

- [ ] Request landlord's original ID card
- [ ] Verify property certificate matches ID information (name, ID number, property address)
- [ ] If renting through agent, request authorization letter
- [ ] If subletting, verify original lease allows subletting

### Property Condition Confirmation

- [ ] Physical viewing with photos taken for record
- [ ] Check water, electricity, gas, furniture, appliances are functional
- [ ] Confirm how property fees, utilities are paid (by owner or tenant)
- [ ] Record current water/electricity/gas meter readings

## Contract Key Clause Review

### Rent Related

| Check Item | Normal Standard | Abnormal Alert |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Rent Amount | Clear, both in words and numbers | Only one format or vague |
| Payment Method | 1+3 (deposit+quarterly) / 1+2 | 3+1 or higher |
| Payment Date | Specific day of month | Vague wording |
| Late Fees | Below 0.05% daily | 1%+ per day |
| Rent Increase Clause | None or clearly agreed | "Landlord can increase anytime" |

### Deposit Related

| Check Item | Normal Standard | Abnormal Alert |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Deposit Amount | Not exceeding one month's rent | Exceeding two months |
| Refund Conditions | Specified refund timeframe (within X days after move-out) | No specific timeframe |
| Deduction Conditions | Listed specific deduction scenarios | "Landlord has right to deduct" vague |
| Deposit Receipt | Receipt issued | No receipt at all |

### Lease Term Related

| Check Item | Normal Standard | Abnormal Alert |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Start/End Dates | Specific dates | Vague wording |
| Lease Duration | Primarily 1 year | Too short (<6 months) or too long (2+ years without agreement) |
| Renewal Terms | How much notice, how rent adjusts | No renewal terms or unlimited renewal rights |

### Early Termination/Subletting

| Check Item | Normal Standard | Abnormal Alert |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Advance Notice | 30 days written notice | Requires 60+ days |
| Penalty | Not exceeding one month's rent | Two months or full rent |
| Subletting Terms | Allowed with landlord's knowledge | Unrestricted or completely prohibited |

## High-Risk Clauses (Red Alerts)

### ❌ Must Refuse Situations

1. **All oral promises**
   - All agreements must be in writing
   - Oral promises are invalid

2. **Rent significantly below market**
   - 30%+ below market should raise alarm
   - May involve fraud or problematic property

3. **Requiring large advance payments before viewing**
   - Transferring money without ever seeing the property
   - Collecting fees to "lock in" the property

4. **"Pay monthly, no agent fee" but requires annual**
   - Possible financial pyramid scheme
   - Ponzi scheme

5. **Excessive penalties**
   - Forfeiting full rent for early termination
   - Penalties exceeding two months' rent

6. **No subletting but overly restrictive**
   - Can't sublet if circumstances change, deposit lost
   - Should have reasonable subletting conditions

7. **Utilities and fees vague**
   - "Other fees borne by tenant" unclear wording
   - Should specify which fees are included

## Contract Addendum Recommendations

### Recommended Clauses to Include

1. **Property Inventory and Condition**
   ```
   At handover: AC x2 (Midea/2022), Refrigerator x1 (Haier/2021)...
   Return at move-out per this list, damage at market price
   ```

2. **Maintenance Responsibility Division**
   ```
   Minor repairs (under XXX) borne by tenant
   Major repairs borne by landlord
   Damage due to property quality borne by landlord
   ```

3. **Early Termination Clause**
   ```
   Tenant may terminate with 30 days written notice
   Deposit refunded (after deducting damage compensation)
   No additional penalty required
   ```

4. **Landlord Assistance for Registration**
   ```
   Landlord cooperates with tenant's residence permit application materials
   If unable to process due to landlord's reason, tenant may terminate contract
   ```

5. **Dispute Resolution Clause**
   ```
   First priority: friendly negotiation
   If negotiation fails: submit to court where property is located
   ```

## Post-Signing Self-Protection Actions

### Important Document Backup

- [ ] Original contract (2+ copies)
- [ ] Original deposit receipt
- [ ] ID card copies (landlord + yourself)
- [ ] Property certificate copy
- [ ] Photos of initial meter readings
- [ ] Photos of property condition at move-in

### Additional Recommended Actions

1. **Change locks**: Replace door locks after moving in, cost negotiable with landlord
2. **Inform property management**: Confirm you're new tenant, verify landlord information
3. **Change passwords**: If smart lock or code lock, change initial passwords

## How to Handle Disputes

### Priority: Negotiation

- First negotiate directly with landlord
- Keep communication records (WeChat screenshots, etc.)

### Seek Help

| Situation | Help Channel |
|-----------|--------------|
| Agent/Sublandlord disputes | Local housing bureau |
| Deposit not returned | Street mediation/Court (small claims very convenient) |
| Fraud suspicion | Police |
| Contract fraud | Court, can claim contract invalid |

### Legal Weapons

- **Housing rental dispute litigation**: Small claim amount, can use small claims procedure
- **Local rental regulations**: Major cities have local rental management regulations, use as basis
- **12345 Government Hotline**: Complaints and reports

## Sublandlord Identification Guide

### Risk Characteristics

- Rent significantly below market
- Unwilling to provide original property certificate
- Lease about to expire (about to run away)
- Requires semi-annual/annual payment

### Self-Protection Suggestions

1. Request to see original lease, confirm remaining term covers your lease
2. Have sublandlord provide written proof of landlord's subletting authorization
3. Directly contact landlord to verify
4. Don't pay too much deposit
