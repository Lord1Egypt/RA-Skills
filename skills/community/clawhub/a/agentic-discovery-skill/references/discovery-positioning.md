# CompleteTech LLC Discovery Positioning

## Discovery Frame

CompleteTech LLC agentic development discovery is practical workflow scoping. The goal is to find one repeated workflow that can be made more reliable with tool routing, retrieval, draft generation, approval gates, logs, monitoring, documentation, and handoff.

## What To Learn

- repeated workflow and business owner
- trigger/input that starts the work
- current steps and handoffs
- systems, tools, documents, APIs, and data sources
- decisions made by people today
- actions that require human approval
- retrieval sources and source-of-truth rules
- data sensitivity and retention constraints
- risks and excluded uses
- evaluation examples and success criteria
- logging and monitoring expectations
- documentation, training, support, and handoff needs

## Language To Use

Use:

- "bounded workflow scoping"
- "reviewed agent workflow"
- "human approval gates"
- "evaluation examples"
- "systems and data readiness"
- "proposal/SOW handoff brief"

Avoid:

- "fully autonomous business decisions"
- "replace your staff"
- invented ROI or savings
- regulated-use assurances
- legal, medical, financial, employment, housing, insurance, credit, biometric, surveillance, or safety-critical final-decision claims

## Proof Rules

Do not invent proof. During discovery, proof is replaced by evidence-gathering:

- sample inputs
- representative edge cases
- workflow volume
- current turnaround time if supplied
- error/rework examples
- reviewer feedback
- acceptance examples

## Output Standard

A good discovery artifact should make it easier to draft a proposal. It should clearly state:

- what is known
- what is unknown
- what the recommended next artifact is
- what must be excluded or handled by human approval
