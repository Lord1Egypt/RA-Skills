## Types of Evidence

### 8 Statutory Evidence Types (《民诉法》Art. 66 / 《刑诉法》Art. 50)

| Type | Common Forms | Review Focus |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Documentary evidence | Contracts, IOUs, letters, receipts, records | Original vs. copy, signature/seal authenticity, creation time |
| Physical evidence | Items, tools, products involved | Chain of custody, preservation, original condition |
| Audio-visual materials | Recordings, video, photos | Completeness, editing, original medium |
| Electronic data | WeChat/email/SMS/transfer records, backend data | Originality, integrity, timestamps, storage location |
| Witness testimony | Eyewitness statements, knowledgeable person accounts | Interest relationship, perception ability, corroboration |
| Expert opinions | Handwriting/fingerprint/appraisal/audit reports | Authority and qualifications of expert/institution, procedure |
| Inspection records | Scene inspection, examination records | Procedure legality, record completeness |
| Party statements | Complaint/answer/trial statements | Effect of admissions, consistency |

## Burden of Proof Allocation

### Civil Litigation

**Basic Rule: He who asserts must prove**
- Party asserting existence of legal relationship: proves basic facts establishing it
- Party asserting change/extinction/obstruction of legal relationship: proves those facts

**Reversed Burden (Special Torts):**

| Cause of Action | Burden Allocation | Legal Basis |
|----------------|------------------|-------------|
| Environmental pollution | Polluter proves exemption/reduction + no causation | 《民法典》Art. 1230 |
| Medical malpractice | Patient proves injury + causation | 《民法典》Art. 1218 |
| Product liability | Manufacturer proves exemption | 《民法典》Art. 1202 |
| Ultra-hazardous activities | Operator proves exemption | 《民法典》Art. 1239 |
| Building collapse | Builder/constructor proves no fault | 《民法典》Art. 1252 |
| Labor disputes | Employer proves lawful termination/overtime paid | 《劳动争议调解仲裁法》Art. 6 |

**Special Proof Issues:**
- Contract performance defense: performing party bears proof
- Force majeure: party claiming it bears proof
- Statute of limitations interruption: party claiming interruption bears proof

### Criminal Litigation

- Prosecution bears burden to prove defendant's guilt (《刑诉法》Art. 51)
- Defendant does not bear burden to prove innocence
- In dubio pro reo: when evidence insufficient, decide in favor of defendant
- Exception: in the crime of unexplained巨额财产来源不明, defendant must explain source (《刑法》Art. 395)

### Administrative Litigation
- Defendant (administrative organ) bears burden to prove legality of its act (《行政诉讼法》Art. 34)
- Plaintiff only needs to prove: filing meets conditions + damage (compensation cases)

## Three-Dimension Evidence Test

### Authenticity

**Review Dimensions:**
1. Whether evidence is original
2. Whether copies have been verified against originals
3. Whether evidence may be forged or altered
4. Electronic data originality and integrity (hash value verification, timestamps, digital signatures)
5. Whether audio-visual materials have been edited or spliced

**Common Objections:**
- "This evidence is not the original, and no valid reason was given for not producing the original"
- "The signature on this contract is clearly different from the defendant's signature on other documents"
- "This WeChat chat record was not displayed from the original phone; authenticity cannot be confirmed"

### Legality

**Review Dimensions:**
1. Whether the collection subject was legally authorized
2. Whether the collection procedure was lawful (no serious violations)
3. Whether the evidence form is lawful (belongs to statutory evidence category)

**Common Illegality Grounds for Exclusion:**
- Torture/coercion (criminal)
- Illegal search/seizure (criminal/civil)
- Evidence obtained by violating public interest or others' lawful rights (civil)
- Secret recordings that violate others' lawful rights or legal prohibitions (civil)
- Evidence collected in serious violation of statutory procedures

### Relevance

**Review Dimensions:**
1. Whether the evidence has a logical connection to the facts to be proved
2. What facts the evidence proves
3. Whether those facts substantively affect the case outcome
4. Direction of the evidence (favorable to whom)

**Common Objections:**
- "This evidence is irrelevant to this case and does not prove the opponent's claimed fact"
- "This evidence only proves a secondary fact and cannot directly prove the core disputed issue"

## Building an Evidence Chain

### Evidence Chain Logic

```
Evidence 1 → Fact A
Evidence 2 → Fact B
Evidence 3 → Fact C
            ↓
      A + B + C → Legal Fact (fact to be proved)
            ↓
      Legal Rule → Legal Effect (claim established)
```

### Evidence Chain Design Principles

1. **Completeness**: every element of the legal cause of action has supporting evidence
2. **Exclusivity**: evidence leads to only one conclusion — your claim
3. **Corroboration**: different types of evidence support each other
4. **Priority**: original > derivative; direct > circumstantial

### Case-Specific Evidence Chain Frameworks

**Contract Dispute Evidence Chain:**
```
Contract formation: Original contract / electronic contract + signature records
Contract performance: Payment receipts + delivery notes + acceptance forms + invoices + communications
Breach: Demand letters + reconciliation statements + breach evidence
Damages: Damage calculation basis + supporting proof
```

**Tort Dispute Evidence Chain:**
```
Tortious act: Objective records of the act + physical evidence
Injury/harm: Expert reports + medical records + assessment reports
Causation: Expert opinions + appraisal + timeline
Fault: Evidence of duty of care breach
```

**Private Lending Evidence Chain:**
```
Loan agreement: Original IOU / loan contract
Funds transfer: Bank transfer receipts + WeChat/Alipay records + cash receipts
Repayment records: Partial repayment records + reconciliation statements
Collection records: Demand letters + WeChat collection records + call recordings
```

## Special Evidence Rules

### Admissions/Confessions (《民诉证据规定》Art. 3-9)
- During litigation, a party's admission of facts unfavorable to itself → relieves opponent of burden of proof
- Exceptions: matters involving family status/national interest/public interest/third-party rights
- Withdrawable: with opponent's consent / under duress or material mistake
- Agent's admission deemed party's admission (except where power of attorney expressly excludes)

### Facts Exempt from Proof (《民诉证据规定》Art. 10)
- Laws of nature / facts of common knowledge
- Presumed facts (legal presumption / factual presumption)
- Facts confirmed by effective court judgments
- Facts confirmed by effective arbitration awards
- Facts proven by effective notarized documents

### Illegal Evidence Exclusion
- Criminal: testimony obtained by torture or other illegal means → absolutely excluded
- Criminal: illegally obtained physical evidence → may be admitted after remediation
- Civil: evidence obtained through serious violation of others' lawful rights/violation of legal prohibitions/violation of public order → shall be excluded

### Corroboration Requirements
- Minor's testimony
- Testimony of interested parties
- Unverifiable copies
- Questionable audio-visual materials
- Above evidence alone is insufficient; corroborating evidence required

### Evidence Submission Deadlines
- Court-designated or mutually agreed and court-approved
- Late submission: state reasons; may be rejected or subject to reprimand/fine without正当理由
- New evidence: discovered after first instance evidence period; newly discovered for appeal

## Evidence Strategy

### Evidence Checklist Integrity Review

Check each evidence item:
- [ ] Does it cover every element of my legal claim?
- [ ] Is the source and form of each evidence item lawful?
- [ ] Are there foreseeable opponent cross-examination attacks?
- [ ] Is core evidence supported by corroborating evidence?
- [ ] Has consideration been given to unfavorable evidence opponent may hold?
- [ ] Are documentary originals prepared?

### Evidence Strategy Options

**Full Disclosure:**
- Use when: ample evidence, clear facts
- Advantage: forms complete evidence chain, strengthens persuasion
- Risk: reveals all evidence too early

**Selective Disclosure:**
- Use when: some evidence has defects / surprise effect desired
- Advantage: controls evidence release pace
- Risk: may be seen as insufficient evidence

**Counter-Evidence Strategy:**
- Focus attacks on weak points in opponent's evidence system
- No need to build full defense evidence chain, concentrate on attacking opponent's evidence
- Use when: opponent bears heavy burden of proof, and you cannot provide direct counter-evidence

### Evidence Index Format

```
┌────────┬─────────────────┬────────────┬──────────┬─────────────────────┐
│ No.    │ Evidence Name   │ Source     │ Type     │ Purpose of Proof    │
├────────┼─────────────────┼────────────┼──────────┼─────────────────────┤
│ 1      │ [Name]          │ [Source]   │ [Doc]    │ [Facts to prove]    │
│ 2      │ [Name]          │ [Source]   │ [Type]   │ [Facts to prove]    │
└────────┴─────────────────┴────────────┴──────────┴─────────────────────┘
```

**Evidence Book Binding Standards:**
1. Cover: case name + case number + "Evidence Book" + submitting party
2. Index page: evidence index
3. Pages: each evidence item individually numbered
4. Tabs: grouped tabs (by disputed issue / by evidence type)
5. Copies: 1 for court + N for opponent + 1 for own file
