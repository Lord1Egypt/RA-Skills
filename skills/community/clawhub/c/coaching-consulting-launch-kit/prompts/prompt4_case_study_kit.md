# Prompt 4 — Client Results Case Study + Testimonial Kit

**Purpose:** Build the complete proof asset system — the case studies, testimonial scripts, and social proof assets that convert skeptical prospects into paying clients.

---

## Instructions

Use real or composite client results. If you're new and have no clients yet, use your own transformation story or a hypothetical composite (labeled as such). Run Prompt 1 first for methodology and positioning context.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a conversion copywriter specializing in coaching and consulting proof assets. Build a complete Client Results Case Study + Testimonial Kit for this coach.

**COACH INFO:**
- Your name and methodology: [e.g., Sarah Kim, The Executive Ascent Framework]
- Your niche: [e.g., mid-level tech managers seeking C-suite promotion]

**CLIENT RESULT TO FEATURE:**
- Client first name or alias: [e.g., Maria or "a Director at a Fortune 100 tech company"]
- Client's starting situation: [e.g., Director of Product at Microsoft, 8 years, repeatedly passed over for VP, losing confidence, considering leaving]
- What they tried before working with you: [e.g., read every leadership book, did 360 reviews, asked for feedback — nothing stuck]
- The specific problem you solved together: [e.g., she was operating as a senior IC, not a strategic leader — her peers saw her as a doer, not a visionary]
- Your methodology applied: [e.g., Phase 1 Executive Audit revealed she was hiding her strategic thinking. Phase 2 Influence Architecture changed how she showed up in leadership meetings. Phase 3 built her internal sponsor relationships]
- The result achieved: [e.g., promoted to VP of Product in 14 months, $42K raise, now leads a team of 28]
- Timeframe: [e.g., 14 months, ended in March 2026]
- A quote from the client (real or reconstructed): [e.g., "I'd been telling myself I wasn't ready. Sarah helped me see I'd been ready for 3 years — I just didn't know how to show it."]

**OUTPUT FORMAT:**

Produce a complete Client Results Case Study + Testimonial Kit with these exact sections:

---

### 1. Full Case Study (Long-Form, 600-800 words)

Write a complete narrative case study following this 5-act structure:

**Act 1 — The Before (100-150 words)**
Paint the picture of where the client was. Use their emotions, not just their job title. What was the daily reality? What was the cost — financially, emotionally, relationally?

**Act 2 — The Turning Point (50-75 words)**
What made them decide to get help NOW? The specific event, feeling, or realization. This is the "I couldn't ignore it anymore" moment.

**Act 3 — The Journey (150-200 words)**
Walk through the methodology, phase by phase. What did they discover? What changed? Include 1-2 specific moments of breakthrough (the thing they said, the thing that clicked).

**Act 4 — The Result (100-150 words)**
The specific, measurable outcome. Include numbers where possible: promotion date, salary increase, team size, deal won, revenue generated, hours saved. Add 1-2 soft results (confidence, relationship quality, clarity).

**Act 5 — The New Life (75-100 words)**
Where are they now? What's possible that wasn't before? End with their own words (quote from the client).

---

### 2. One-Paragraph Social Version (100-125 words)

A condensed case study for LinkedIn posts, Instagram captions, and email newsletters. Same 5-act arc compressed. Ends with a 1-sentence CTA relevant to the platform (DM me / link in bio / reply to this email).

---

### 3. One-Sentence Soundbite (3 Versions)

Three ultra-compressed versions of this result for:
- A sales page headline
- A LinkedIn About section bullet point
- A conference or podcast introduction

Format: "[Client type] went from [starting point] to [result] in [timeframe]."

Provide 3 versions with different emotional angles (credibility, aspiration, urgency).

---

### 4. Pull Quotes Extraction (8 Quotes)

From the client quote provided (and the result), generate 8 pull quotes in different formats:
- 2 outcome-focused quotes (what changed)
- 2 process-focused quotes (what working with you was like)
- 2 emotion-focused quotes (how they feel now)
- 2 recommendation quotes (why someone should hire you)

Format each quote for:
- LinkedIn post graphic (under 80 characters)
- Website testimonial block (under 200 characters)
- Note: Flag which quotes need client approval before publishing

---

### 5. Video Testimonial Script for Client

A 90-120 second video script your client can record on their phone. Include:

**Opening (15 sec):** Who they are and where they were when they hired you
**Problem (20 sec):** What they'd tried, why it hadn't worked, what they were afraid of
**Experience (25 sec):** What the coaching process was actually like (specific, not "it was amazing")
**Result (25 sec):** The specific outcome, with numbers
**Recommendation (15 sec):** Who should work with this coach and why

Provide stage directions (look at camera here / pause here / smile here) for a natural, non-scripted feel.

---

### 6. Testimonial Request Outreach Email

A 200-word email to send to past clients asking for a testimonial:
- Opens with a genuine check-in (not a pitch)
- Explains what you're building and why their story matters
- Makes it easy (gives them the 5 questions to answer in writing OR the video script option)
- Sets a soft deadline (2 weeks)
- Offers a thank-you (referral to their business, public endorsement, etc.)

---

### 7. ROI Metrics Table

Build a professional ROI summary table for this result:

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Salary/Revenue | $X | $X | +$X (+X%) |
| Time to Goal | [months stuck] | [months to result] | X months faster |
| Team/Responsibility | [before] | [after] | [change] |
| Confidence (1-10) | [before] | [after] | +X points |
| [Custom metric] | [before] | [after] | [change] |

Include a "Cost vs. Value" row: Investment in coaching vs. first-year ROI.

---

### 8. Case Study Usage Guide

Where and how to use each asset from this kit:
- Full case study: [best placements]
- Social version: [best formats and timing in content calendar]
- Soundbites: [sales page / discovery call / bio]
- Pull quotes: [website / sales page / LinkedIn graphic]
- Video testimonial: [ads / sales page / email / LinkedIn]
- ROI table: [sales page / discovery call slide / proposal document]

One-paragraph note on privacy and how to use composite/anonymized results ethically.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Real results are always better than hypothetical ones.** If you're new, use your own transformation story as the case study (you are your first client).
- **Composite case studies are ethical** when clearly labeled. "Based on results from clients in this situation" is transparent and acceptable.
- **The video script is often the most valuable asset.** Clients who record even a 90-second testimonial convert at 3-5x the rate of written testimonials on sales pages.
- **Run this prompt once per client** every time you get a result worth documenting. Build a library of 5-10 case studies targeting different buyer segments.
