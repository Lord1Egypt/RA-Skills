# Policy Quality & Compliance Checklist

Run before publishing any generated policy.

## Accuracy to the business
- [ ] Business name is correct and consistent across all policies.
- [ ] Return window matches the seller's actual operation.
- [ ] Carriers, processing times, and shipping costs are real, not placeholder.
- [ ] Payment processors and ad partners named in privacy match the actual stack.
- [ ] Fulfillment method (self/3PL/dropship/marketplace) is reflected in logistics language.

## Returns & refunds
- [ ] Return window has a stated start trigger (delivery date).
- [ ] Condition requirements are specified.
- [ ] Who pays return shipping is stated for both defect and change-of-mind.
- [ ] Refund method and business-day range are stated.
- [ ] Exclusions are itemized and fair (no unlawful "all sales final").

## Shipping
- [ ] Processing time and delivery estimates are given.
- [ ] Domestic vs international handling is clear, including duties responsibility.
- [ ] Lost/damaged shipment process is described.

## Privacy & data
- [ ] Data collected, purposes, and sharing are disclosed.
- [ ] GDPR rights covered if serving EU/UK; CCPA/CPRA rights if serving California.
- [ ] Cookie consent behavior matches the markets served.
- [ ] Clear statement on whether personal information is sold.
- [ ] Retention and security addressed.

## Terms of service
- [ ] Pricing-error, IP, liability, and dispute/governing-law clauses present.
- [ ] Acceptable-use and termination addressed.

## Compliance & marketplace fit
- [ ] EU cooling-off period respected for EU customers.
- [ ] Store policy does not contradict the marketplace's buyer protection.
- [ ] Regulated products flagged for attorney review.

## Presentation
- [ ] Plain second-person language; scannable headings.
- [ ] Effective date on every policy.
- [ ] Contact channel + response-time expectation included.
- [ ] Consistent voice and formatting across all four documents.
- [ ] "Not legal advice / review for regulated categories" note included where relevant.
