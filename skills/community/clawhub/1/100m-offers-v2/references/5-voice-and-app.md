## 5 Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I Keep Competing on Price"
**You charge $50/session and keep losing clients to cheaper competitors.**
- **What to do:** 1) You have the Commodity Problem. Your offer is easily compared. 2) Redesign using the Value Equation—focus on Dream Outcome and Guarantee. 3) Stack bonuses (meal plan, check-ins, accountability group). 4) Rename by dream outcome. 5) Charge 4-10x your old price for the new Grand Slam Offer.
- **Book quote:** "A Grand Slam Offer makes you appear to have a totally different product — and that means a value-driven purchase instead of a price-driven purchase."

### Scenario 2: "I Have a Great Product but Nobody Buys"
**You know your product is good but you can't get traction.**
- **Self-diagnosis:** The problem is either (a) you have a commodity problem (easily compared), (b) you're in the wrong market (no starving crowd), (c) your offer lacks enhancements (no guarantee, no scarcity, no bonuses). 99% of the time it's one of these three.
- **Book quote:** "It takes no more effort to make a Grand Slam Offer than to strike out. The difference is skill."

### Scenario 3: "I'm Afraid to Offer a Strong Guarantee"
**You worry customers will cheat.**
- **What to do:** 1) Most people never claim guarantees. 2) A strong guarantee is a sales tool, not a cost. 3) Start with "I'll work with you until you get results"—it signals confidence. 4) Track claims. You'll be surprised how rarely they're used.
- **Book quote:** "The guarantee is your best salesperson."

### Scenario 4: "I Don't Know What to Charge"
**You have no idea what price is right.**
- **What to do:** 1) Price based on value, not cost. How much is the outcome worth? 2) The 10x rule: if your customer gets $10K of value, charge $1K. 3) Test. Start higher than you're comfortable with. You can always add more value, but you can't un-lower a price. 4) Use the "raise by 20% every 5 customers" rule until you find resistance.
- **Book quote:** "A Grand Slam Offer gets you 22x more cash up front for the same work."

### Scenario 5: "I Need to Revive a Stagnant Business"
**Your business is running but not growing.**
- **What to do:** 1) Check the 3 Growth Levers—which is failing? 2) If customers = stagnant, fix the offer. 3) If average purchase value = stagnant, add enhancements (bonuses, guarantee, naming). 4) If purchase frequency = stagnant, add a subscription or continuity component to the offer. 5) Apply Trim & Stack: cut low-value components, stack high-value ones into one Grand Slam Offer.
- **Book quote:** "If your company isn't growing, it's dying. Maintenance is a myth."

> **Case: The $100K credit card gamble** (Chapter 1): After losing everything to a fraudulent partner, Hormozi's only remaining asset was a business credit card with a $100K limit from his gym days.
