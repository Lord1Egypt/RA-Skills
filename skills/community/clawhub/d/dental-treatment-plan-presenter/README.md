# dental-treatment-plan-presenter

Convert clinical findings (exam notes, radiograph reads, periodontal charting, patient priorities) into a phased, CDT-coded, patient-facing dental treatment plan — for licensed dentist review before any presentation, signature, or treatment.

---

## What It Does

Guides intake of the patient's chief complaint, medical history flags, exam findings (caries, defective restorations, missing teeth, periodontal status, occlusion, esthetic concerns), and patient priorities and constraints (urgency, budget posture, insurance benefits). Sequences the plan into four standard phases — Phase 1 Emergency / Disease control, Phase 2 Restorative, Phase 3 Definitive / Reconstruction, Phase 4 Maintenance — and produces a DRAFT treatment plan with per-procedure CDT code, tooth/site, chair time estimate, the clinical alternative(s) considered, risks, benefits, and an informed-consent prompt sheet the dentist can use during the case-presentation appointment.

Never delivers a final treatment plan, never signs informed consent, never quotes binding fees, never overrides the licensed dentist's clinical judgment.

---

## When To Use

- A general dentist, prosthodontist, periodontist, or dental resident needs to convert a comprehensive exam into a phased plan before the case-presentation appointment.
- A treatment coordinator needs a structured patient-facing presentation document for a multi-visit plan.
- A dental student or new associate needs scaffolding to phase a plan and surface the alternatives and risks they must discuss for informed consent.
- A practice is standardizing how plans are sequenced and presented across providers.
