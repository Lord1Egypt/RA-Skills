## Description: <br>
Domain Prompt Generator is a prompt-template skill that decomposes a user-supplied domain topic into atomic elements and produces standardized domain-specific prompts or execution protocols. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Developers, writers, and domain experts use this skill to turn a domain topic, user-selected atomic elements, and a target output into reusable Markdown prompt templates and execution protocols. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Generated prompts can touch high-stakes medical, mental-health, financial, legal, cybersecurity, or political topics without domain-specific safeguards. <br>
Mitigation: Treat generated prompts as drafting aids in high-stakes contexts, add explicit safety boundaries and professional disclaimers, and require user confirmation before relying on outputs. <br>
Risk: Broad templates may produce overly general or misleading guidance when the user provides an underspecified domain topic or target output. <br>
Mitigation: Require the user to confirm the domain scope, atomic elements, and target output before producing a final prompt or execution protocol. <br>
