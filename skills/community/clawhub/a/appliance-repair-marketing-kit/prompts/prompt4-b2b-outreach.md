## The Prompt

```
You are a B2B sales copywriter specializing in home services account acquisition.

Generate B2B outreach scripts for 4 channels for this appliance repair business:

BUSINESS INFO:
- Business name: [BUSINESS NAME]
- Owner/sales contact name: [NAME]
- City/State: [CITY, STATE]
- Service area: [CITIES/REGIONS COVERED]
- Brands serviced: [LIST BRANDS]
- Authorized service center: [YES/NO — which brands if yes]
- EPA 608 certified: [YES/NO]
- Response time for B2B priority accounts: [e.g., 4-hour, same-day, next-business-day]
- Volume pricing: [YES/NO — if yes, describe: e.g., 10+ units/month = 15% discount]
- Invoicing/NET terms: [e.g., NET 30 available, credit card only, etc.]
- Service contract option: [YES/NO — monthly retainer option?]
- Phone: [PHONE]
- Email: [EMAIL]

CHANNEL 1: PROPERTY MANAGEMENT COMPANIES

1A. Cold Email — Property Manager (subject + body, under 200 words)
- Pain point: after-hours appliance failures, tenant complaints, unit downtime
- Hook: priority dispatch + guaranteed response time
- Proof: brands covered, warranty on repairs
- CTA: 15-min call or "reply to get our PM rate sheet"
- Compliance: no guaranteed repair outcome — "fast diagnosis and repair" framing

1B. Follow-Up Email (Day 5, if no reply)
- "Quick follow-up" framing
- One specific stat or scenario (e.g., "average refrigerator repair = 2-hour job, not a 2-day wait")
- Same CTA

1C. PM Priority Dispatch Agreement — 1-Page Term Sheet
- Priority response time commitment
- Volume discount structure (if applicable)
- NET terms / invoicing method
- Repair warranty terms for PM accounts
- Exclusions (catastrophic failures, code violations, etc.)
- NOT a legally binding contract — include "consult an attorney before finalizing" note

CHANNEL 2: APARTMENT COMPLEXES (DIRECT TO MAINTENANCE SUPERVISOR)

2A. Cold Email — Maintenance Supervisor or Property Director
- Different angle from PM email: focus on reducing maintenance team burden
- Hook: "your team handles HVAC + plumbing + painting — let us own appliances"
- Sub-contractor positioning: they look good, you do the work
- Proof: response time, brand coverage, EPA 608 if relevant
- CTA: invite for 10-min call or "send us your appliance repair volume estimate"

2B. Drop-In Visit Script (for in-person cold call to apartment complex office)
- 60-second elevator pitch
- Leave-behind ask ("who's the right person for appliance service vendor decisions?")
- Business card / one-pager mention

CHANNEL 3: APPLIANCE DEALERS (AUTHORIZED SERVICE NETWORK)

3A. Cold Email — Appliance Retailer or Dealer
- Purpose: get listed as their recommended repair referral for out-of-warranty units
- Hook: "your customers call you when it breaks — you can turn that into goodwill and a referral fee"
- Offer: referral agreement, co-branded service card, revenue share option
- Include: your brand authorization status if applicable
- CTA: meeting or referral agreement draft

3B. Dealer Partnership One-Pager Copy
- Co-branded service referral program description
- Customer benefit (trusted referral vs. random Google search)
- Dealer benefit (goodwill + optional referral fee)
- How referrals work (phone/email/QR)

CHANNEL 4: REAL ESTATE INVESTOR GROUPS (SFRS + MULTIFAMILY)

4A. Cold Email — Real Estate Investor or Landlord Group
- Audience: own multiple single-family rentals or small multifamily
- Pain: tenant turnover amplified by appliance downtime; hard to find reliable vendors
- Hook: dedicated vendor, fast turnaround, invoiced billing
- Proof: all major brands, warranty, EPA cert if applicable
- CTA: add to their vendor list or 15-min intro call

4B. REI Networking Event Pitch (30-second verbal + handout)
- Clean verbal pitch for investor meetup/REIA
- One-page handout copy (headline, 3 benefits, contact info)

COMPLIANCE RULES (mandatory):
1. NO guaranteed repair outcomes — "fast professional service" not "guaranteed fix"
2. EPA 608: only mention refrigerant handling capability if EPA 608 certified = YES
3. Authorized service: only claim "authorized" status for confirmed brands
4. Volume pricing: if offered, state clearly it applies to qualifying accounts only
5. Service contracts: if offered, note that terms are subject to written agreement
6. Referral fees to dealers: disclose clearly in any co-marketing materials (FTC Act)
7. NET terms: state clearly what documentation required (e.g., credit application)

Output all 4 channels with all sub-sections. Use clear headers. Include all placeholders.
```

## What You Get

- 2 cold emails + 1 follow-up for property managers
- 2 apartment complex outreach pieces (email + door script)
- 2 appliance dealer partnership pieces (email + one-pager)
- 2 real estate investor pieces (email + REIA pitch)
- PM Priority Dispatch Agreement term sheet
- Dealer Partnership one-pager copy
- All compliant with FTC, EPA, and contractor licensing constraints

## Pro Tip

The property management channel is your fastest path to recurring revenue. One PM company with 200 units and 15% annual appliance failure rate = ~30 calls/year. At $180 average ticket that's $5,400/year from a single account. The dealer partnership is your best long-term play — people who just bought a new refrigerator from a dealer will need service in 3-5 years, and the dealer's recommendation is gold.
