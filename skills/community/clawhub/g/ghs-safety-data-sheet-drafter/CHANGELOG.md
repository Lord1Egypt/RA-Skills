## [0.1.0] - 2026-05-24
Initial release. Six-phase workflow that turns product identification, composition, and hazard data into a DRAFT 16-section Safety Data Sheet aligned to OSHA Hazard Communication Standard 29 CFR 1910.1200 as amended in May 2024 (UN GHS Rev. 7 alignment) — covering scope intake (substance vs mixture, intended use, jurisdictions in scope, compliance deadline tracking against the May 19, 2026 substances deadline and the January 19, 2027 mixtures deadline), product and supplier identification (Section 1), composition and concentration ranges with trade-secret CBI handling (Section 3), hazard classification walkthrough using ingredient hazards plus mixture rules (Sections 2, 11, 12), physical-and-chemical property capture (Section 9), first-aid / firefighting / accidental-release / handling-and-storage / exposure-controls drafting (Sections 4–8) anchored to OSHA PELs and ACGIH TLVs where available, stability and reactivity (Section 10), toxicological and ecological data with explicit data-gap flags (Sections 11–12), disposal (Section 13), transport classification placeholder for UN / DOT / IMDG / IATA (Section 14), regulatory inventory cross-reference (Section 15 — TSCA, DSL / NDSL, EINECS / ELINCS, AICS, IECSC, ENCS / ISHL, KECI, PICCS, NZIoC), revision and version-control block (Section 16), a GHS label preview (signal word, pictograms, H-statements, P-statements, product identifier, supplier identifier), a concentration-range disclosure worksheet for CBI claims, a section-by-section authoring-gap list, an evidence index linking every assertion to a source, and an open-questions list — for SDS author / industrial hygienist / EHS manager / regulatory affairs review and sign-off before publication, never delivered as a final SDS and never used to satisfy classification without qualified human review.
