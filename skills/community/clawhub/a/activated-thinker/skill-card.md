## Description: <br>
Cognitive protocols for AI agents: anti-binary thinking, gardener mindset, friction protocol, and behavioral mode detection. Teaches agents HOW to think about problems. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[MouseRider](https://clawhub.ai/user/MouseRider) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users, developers, and agent operators use this skill to make an AI agent adapt its response style to crunch, exploratory, or standard work, including when to suggest alternatives, slow down for creative decisions, or coach instead of immediately delivering. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill may make an agent slow down, suggest alternatives, or coach when a user expected immediate execution. <br>
Mitigation: Review the skill's behavioral modes before deployment and ensure crunch-mode handling is appropriate for time-sensitive workflows. <br>
Risk: Reasoning-style guidance can change an agent's response posture even though it does not request tools or data access. <br>
Mitigation: Test representative user interactions after installation to confirm the agent's tone, speed, and coaching behavior match the intended use case. <br>
