# Retirement Readiness Analysis

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Financial Planning — Retirement

## Purpose

A retirement-readiness drafting partner for CFP® professionals, financial advisors, paraplanners, RIA associates, employee-benefits counselors, and retirement-plan specialists. Turns one household's age, savings by account type, contributions, expected retirement age, expected expenses, Social Security and pension expectations, risk tolerance, and tax posture into a structured DRAFT report — funded ratio, gap, savings rate, withdrawal-rate stress, sequence-of-returns sensitivity, SECURE 2.0 / Roth-conversion / RMD / IRMAA flags, and a prioritized client-action checklist.

## When to Use

- Preparing for a discovery, mid-year, annual, or pre-retirement planning meeting with a single household
- Producing a draft readiness analysis a CFP® can refine, present, and use to drive a client action plan
- Aligning a multi-account picture (pre-tax, Roth, taxable, HSA, pension, real estate, business) to a single funded-ratio and withdrawal-stress view
- Surfacing SECURE 2.0 windows, Roth-conversion candidacy, RMD posture, IRMAA proximity, and gap-year planning opportunities for the tax professional
- Standardizing a structured readiness format across a book of households

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Intake**
1. Captures advisor role, planning framework, tax year, and residency
2. Captures household profile, citizenship / residency tax flags, dependents, longevity, and health flags
3. Captures balance sheet by account type with owner, custodian, balance, as-of date, and beneficiary status
4. Captures current cash flow: income by source, in-flight pre-tax and after-tax savings, expenses, debts, insurance
5. Captures retirement goal: target ages, target spending with fixed-vs-discretionary split, one-time goals, replacement-rate target, legacy goal
6. Captures Social Security claiming-age assumption, pension and other guaranteed income, working-in-retirement expectations
7. Captures risk tolerance, current asset allocation, return and inflation assumptions, longevity assumption, and sequence-of-returns posture
8. Captures tax landscape and SECURE 2.0 windows (gap years, Roth conversion candidacy, RMD age, catch-up contributions, HSA / QCD / NUA / 72(t) candidacy)
9. Restates every fact with Confirmed / Assumed / Unknown tags and shows headline metrics before drafting

**Phase 2: Drafting**
10. Drafts a household summary, balance sheet, cash flow, retirement goal, and guaranteed-income tables
11. Computes net worth, retirement asset base, annual savings rate, projected income gap, funded ratio, initial safe-withdrawal stress, and implied withdrawal rates at ages 65 / 70 / 75
12. Builds a sensitivity table across return, inflation, longevity, retirement-age, and spending scenarios
13. Recommends a Readiness Tier (On Track / Near Track / Off Track / Critical Gap) with drivers
14. Flags risk-tolerance vs. allocation gaps for advisor decision

**Phase 3: Action plan**
15. Produces a prioritized action checklist with owner (client / advisor / employer / tax preparer / estate attorney), timing, expected impact, and required confirmation
16. Produces an Evidence Matrix, an Unresolved-Questions list, and a Disclosure Block

## Output

A DRAFT readiness report with:

- Header declaring tax year, residency, inflation, longevity, and capital-market assumption source
- Household summary
- Balance sheet by account type
- Current cash-flow snapshot
- Retirement goal and guaranteed-income tables
- Risk-return-longevity assumption block
- Tax and SECURE 2.0 landscape with flags for the tax professional
- Headline metrics with formula, inputs, and source
- Scenario-based sensitivity table
- Recommended Readiness Tier with drivers
- Risk-tolerance vs. allocation gap flag
- Prioritized action checklist with owner, timing, expected impact
- Evidence Matrix and Unresolved-Questions list
- Disclosure block

## Safety

This skill drafts a planning analysis, **not** investment, tax, or legal advice. Every output is labeled **DRAFT — LICENSED ADVISOR REVIEW REQUIRED — NOT INVESTMENT, TAX, OR LEGAL ADVICE**. The skill never recommends a security, fund, ETF, annuity, insurance product, or platform; never executes a trade, transfer, beneficiary change, conversion, or contribution change; never issues a final tax conclusion; never overrides the client's stated risk tolerance; never invents a tax-law figure (contribution limits, IRMAA brackets, RMD ages, SS PIA, COLA); and never uses real client PII in chat. Roth conversion, RMD strategy, NUA, NIIT, IRMAA, AMT, and QBI items are flagged for the licensed tax professional. Estate items are flagged for the estate attorney. Monte Carlo / probability framing is used only with named assumptions; otherwise the report uses scenario ranges. All client data is treated as confidential and is never pasted to external services.
