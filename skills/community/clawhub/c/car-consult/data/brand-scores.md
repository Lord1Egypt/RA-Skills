# Brand reference for NEV buying decisions

> ⚠️ Subjective reference, not hard standard. Use latest news and reviews to adjust.

## Established brands (reliable after-sales, stable resale)

| Brand | Tier | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| BYD (比亚迪) | Tier 1 | Largest NEV market share, widest service network (3,000+ dealers), stable resale. BEV/PHEV leader. |
| Tesla | Tier 1 | Strong brand, advanced tech (OTA, FSD), good resale. Service center density lower than BYD in China. |
| Li Auto (理想) | Tier 1.5 | Strong EREV products, good build quality, decent resale. EREV-focused. |
| AITO / SERES (问界) | Tier 1.5 | Huawei tech (ADS + HarmonyOS), fast-growing sales. After-sales still scaling. |
| NIO (蔚来) | Tier 1.5 | Premium, battery swap + BaaS, strong service. Higher price, uncertain profitability. |
| Geely / Zeekr (吉利/极氪) | Tier 2 | Traditional OEM backing, solid build. Zeekr is the premium BEV arm. |
| Changan / Deepal (长安/深蓝) | Tier 2 | OEM-backed, Deepal has Huawei ADS option. Growing fast. |
| Leapmotor (零跑) | Tier 2 | High value-for-money, fast sales growth (10k+/month). Service network still expanding. |
| Xpeng (小鹏) | Tier 2 | Strong in smart driving (XNGP), good BEV tech. Financial pressure persists. |
| GAC Aion (广汽埃安) | Tier 2 | Good volume, popular in ride-hailing. Consumer brand perception is mixed. |
| Neta (哪吒) | Tier 3 | Price-competitive but sales declining. After-sales risk is rising. |

## Legacy JV brands (gas-to-EV transition)

| Brand | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| VW ID series | Solid build, conservative tech, decent dealer network. |
| BMW / Mercedes EV | High initial price, fast depreciation. Brand premium doesn't translate to EV. |
| Toyota / Honda bZ | Very conservative EV approach. Low volume, limited options. |

## Risky brands (use caution)

- **Neta, WM Motor, Hozon, HiPhi, etc.** — declining sales, potential after-sales black hole
- **Any new brand < 1 year** — give it 3-6 months of market validation
- **Rebadged/co-developed models** — check which OEM is responsible for service

## Brand scoring guidelines

| Factor | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| Service network | Number of dealers/service centers in the user's city |
| Monthly sales | >5,000 = safe; 1,000-5,000 = watch; <1,000 = risky |
| OEM backing | State-owned or major private OEM = safer than VC-funded |
| Warranty | Remaining warranty on used cars; transferability on 2nd owner |
| Resale data | Check recent used car platform prices for 2-3 year old models |
