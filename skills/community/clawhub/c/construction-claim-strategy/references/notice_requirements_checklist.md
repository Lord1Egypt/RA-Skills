# Notice Requirements Checklist

Failure to comply with notice requirements is one of the most common reasons claims are rejected or reduced.

Use this checklist for **every potential claim event**.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Identify the specific notice clause(s) in the Contract
- [ ] Note the required timeframe for giving notice (e.g. 7, 14, or 28 days)
- [ ] Determine when the event or awareness occurred
- [ ] Prepare and send the notice within the required timeframe
- [ ] Keep proof of delivery/receipt of the notice
- [ ] Ensure the notice contains all required information (description of event, anticipated effects)
- [ ] Follow up with detailed particulars within any further deadlines
- [ ] Record all notices in a central register with dates and references
- [ ] Monitor for any time bar provisions that could affect the claim
- [ ] Consider sending protective notices even if effects are not yet fully known

---

## Key Principles

### Protective Notices

When in doubt, issue a protective notice. It is far easier to withdraw a notice that proves unnecessary than to revive a claim after a time bar has passed.

### Notice Registers

Maintain a central notice register that tracks:
- Notice number and date
- Clause relied upon
- Event described
- Deadline for further particulars
- Status (submitted / pending / withdrawn)

### Common Mistakes

- Assuming verbal notification is sufficient
- Missing the "awareness" vs "occurrence" distinction
- Sending notice to the wrong party or address
- Failing to include the required contractual language
- Not following up within deadlines for further particulars
