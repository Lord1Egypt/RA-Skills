# Prompt: Executive Summary

## Purpose
Generate the opening section of a client proposal — a compelling executive summary that shows deep understanding of the client's problem and positions your solution as the obvious answer.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a senior business development consultant writing a proposal executive summary.

CLIENT CONTEXT:
- Client name/company: [CLIENT_NAME]
- Client industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Problem they described: [PROBLEM_IN_THEIR_WORDS]
- Business impact of the problem: [IMPACT — lost revenue, wasted time, missed opportunities, etc.]

YOUR SOLUTION:
- Your name/company: [YOUR_NAME]
- Service you're proposing: [SERVICE_TYPE]
- Core approach: [HOW_YOU_SOLVE_IT]
- Key differentiator: [WHY_YOU_NOT_SOMEONE_ELSE]

Write a 150–200 word executive summary for a professional proposal. Structure:
1. Opening sentence that mirrors the client's problem back to them (shows you listened)
2. Brief statement of the business cost of inaction
3. Your proposed solution in 2–3 sentences (clear, specific, no jargon)
4. 2–3 bullet points: specific outcomes the client will achieve
5. One-sentence confidence statement about your track record or approach

Tone: Professional but direct. Confident, not arrogant. Focused on outcomes, not features.
Format: Flowing paragraphs with the bullet points embedded. No headers inside the summary.
```

---

## Example Inputs

- Client: "TahoePeak Outdoors, e-commerce retailer. Their problem: 'Our website looks dated and we're losing customers to competitors with better UX — cart abandonment is at 78%.'"
- Your solution: "Complete Shopify redesign focused on mobile-first UX and conversion optimization"
- Your differentiator: "Specialized in outdoor/lifestyle brands, average client sees 35% cart abandonment reduction"

---

## Tips

- Use the client's exact phrasing when describing the problem — it signals you were truly listening
- Keep outcomes measurable: "reduce cart abandonment" beats "improve conversions"
- Never mention your price in the executive summary — that comes later
