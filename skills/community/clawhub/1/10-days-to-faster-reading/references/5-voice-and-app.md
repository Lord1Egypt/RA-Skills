## Author Voice & Personality

### Abby Marks Beale
- **Professional reading trainer:** Founder of Rev It Up Reading. Has taught speed reading to corporate employees for two decades. Her classes run 12 hours across multiple days.
- **Race car metaphor throughout:** She transforms a potentially dry topic into an engaging "reading race" with pace cars, engines, pit stops, and victory laps.
- **Empathetic and practical:** She was once a slow reader herself — "I procrastinated on school reading assignments and rarely picked up a book for pleasure." She understands the struggle.
- **Key phrase:** "You are a reader who wants to learn how to become skilled at speed-reading. A comparable relationship exists between car drivers and those who want to learn how to become skilled at race car driving."

## 5 Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I'm a Slow Reader"
**Baseline WPM under 200. You subvocalize every word.**
- **Fix:** 1) Measure your baseline WPM on a 400-word passage. 2) Start with the white card (above the line). 3) Practice the 10 ways to reduce subvocalization. 4) Track daily progress. 5) Expect to feel awkward for 3-5 days. Within 10 days, expect 200-400% improvement.

### Scenario 2: "My Mind Wanders When I Read"
**You get to the bottom of a page with no memory of what you just read.**
- **Fix:** 1) Preview first — 2 minutes of previewing builds the mental map. 2) Read faster — the 250 wpm gap causes daydreaming. 3) Use a pacer to force forward motion. 4) Take mini-breaks after each section to mentally summarize.

### Scenario 3: "I Have No Time to Read"
**Your "to read" pile is overwhelming.**
- **Fix:** 1) Do a Reading Tracker Inventory for one month — list everything you receive. 2) Rate each for value. 3) Cancel subscriptions for anything with 5+ unread back issues. 4) Use previewing and skimming to decide which articles are worth full reading. 5) Read key sections only — not every page of every book.

### Scenario 4: "I Can't Skim — That Feels Like Cheating"
**Elementary school conditioning: "read every word."**
- **Fix:** 1) Recognize this as one of the 10 Fallacies. 2) Skimming is not cheating — it's efficient reading. 3) Practice on newspapers and magazines where 70% comprehension is sufficient. 4) Use the previewing protocol + key-word reading.

### Scenario 5: "I Want to Read More Books This Year"
**Goal: read 1-2 books per week.**
- **Fix:** 1) Preview every book before deciding to read it fully. 2) Set a purpose for each book. 3) Most nonfiction can be read in 3-4 sessions using preview + key sections. 4) Read faster = more books in the same time. 5) Track reading time — not pages. 30 minutes of focused, paced reading per day compounds dramatically.

> **Case: The Power of Personal Progress Tracking** (Day 2): Marks Beale's participants who tracked their WPM and comprehension daily improved 2-3x faster than those who didn't. The act of measurement itself creates accountability and reveals which strategies actually work for each individual reader. **Key takeaway:** What gets measured gets improved. The Personal Progress Chart is not optional — it's the engine of the program.

> **Case: The stick shift analogy** (Day 2): Marks Beale compares learning speed reading to learning a stick shift after a lifetime of automatics. First attempt: the car lurches forward and stalls. You feel like a bad driver. But by day 2-3, it's better. By day 7, you're drinking coffee while driving stick. The same progression happens with reading. **Key takeaway:** Discomfort in the first 3-7 days is not a sign of failure — it's proof the skill is being remapped. Push through.
