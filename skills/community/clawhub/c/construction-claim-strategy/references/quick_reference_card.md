# Quick Reference Card

**Construction Claim Strategy v1.1 — One-Page Reference**

---

## Argument Strength Ranking (Always start from the top)

| Rank | Argument Type | Strength | When to Use | Recommendation |
|------|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Textual Interpretation | **Strongest** | Wording supports your position | Lead with this |
| 2 | Factual Distinction | **Strong** | Facts are materially different | Strong alternative |
| 3 | Concede-then-Distinguish | Moderate-Strong | Clause appears unfavourable but facts are exceptional | Use carefully |
| 4 | Implied Terms / Prevention | **Weakest** | No stronger argument available | Last resort only |

## Disclosure Control Levels

| Level | Use For | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **State Precisely** | Clause references, formal letters, key facts, legal reasoning | Preferred for strong arguments |
| **Keep General** | Operational context, high-level impact | Use for moderate/weak arguments |
| **Defer** | Detailed quantification & analysis | Almost always defer full details |

## Response Architecture Patterns

- **Standard Query Response** — Acknowledge → Address points → Reserve rights
- **Risk Allocation Rebuttal** — Concede normal position → Distinguish current facts → Conclude
- **Chronology-Based Response** — Timeline of events → Current status → Next steps
- **Cost / Quantum Claim Response** — Contractual basis → Heads of claim → Status → Reserve
- **Always close** with a Reservation of Rights paragraph

## Scope Control Rules (Critical)

1. Respond **only** to what was asked.
2. Do **not** volunteer new claims or unrequested information.
3. Preserve future rights using "reserves its position".
4. Keep operational details general until necessary.

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Citing the wrong or weakest clause first
- ❌ Leading with defensive language ("the clause does not apply")
- ❌ Volunteering extra information
- ❌ Quantifying costs or delay too early
- ❌ Using implied terms when stronger arguments exist

---

## Practical Tips

- Always **start** by checking whether Textual Interpretation is available.
- Only move down the ranking when stronger options are weak or unavailable.
- **Combine arguments**: primary + fallback in the same response.
- Match disclosure level to argument strength: stronger = more precise.
- **Run the strategy wizard even for simple replies** — prevents mistakes.
- Apply Scope Control strictly — one of the biggest value-adds.
