## The Discipline Toolbox

### The Counting Quick Reference

| Situation | What to Say | What NOT to Say |
|---|---|---|
| Child whines | "That's 1." (calmly) | "Stop whining! How many times have I told you?!" |
| Child argues | "That's 2." (5+ seconds later) | "Don't talk back to me!" |
| Child continues | "That's 3. Take a break." | "That's it! Go to your room!!" |
| After break | Nothing. Welcome them back. | "Are you ready to behave?" or lecture |

### Counting in Public
Same rules apply, but the "boring spot" is anywhere: a chair, a bench, against a wall at the mall. You don't need a room. Use a timer on your phone. You may feel embarrassed, but the alternative — a full meltdown — is worse.

### Sibling Rivalry Protocol
- Count both kids. 90% of the time, both are doing something wrong.
- Don't try to referee "who started it." You're not a judge.
- Separate breaks in different spots.
- If only one is clearly innocent, count only the aggressor.

### The 6 Types of Testing & How to Respond

| Type | What They Do | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Badgering | "Please please please please..." | Stay silent or "That's 1" |
| 2. Temper | Screaming, door slamming | Count if in your presence. Ignore if in their room. |
| 3. Threatening | "I'm running away!" | "That's 1." Don't engage the fantasy. |
| 4. Martyr | "You don't love me!" | "That's 1." Don't defend. |
| 5. Buddy-Buddy | "You're the best mommy ever!" | Kind smile. "Nice try, sweetie." |
| 6. Physical | Hitting, pushing, biting | Immediate "That's 3" (no 1 and 2) for dangerous behavior |

### Start Behavior Strategies for Common Routines

| Routine | Strategy | Key Tip |
|---|---|---|
| Morning (getting up, dressed, breakfast) | Positive routine chart + timer | Do everything in the same order every day |
| Mealtime | Natural consequences + praise | "If you don't eat now, food goes away" |
| Chores | Behavior chart + counting for refusal | Connection before correction |
| Homework | Timer + break schedule + "first/then" | "First 15 minutes of math, then 5-minute break" |
| Bedtime | Wind-down routine (30 min) + timer | Same order every night: bath → teeth → story → lights-out |

### The Count-Down Method for Bedtime
"I'm going to check on you in 5 minutes. If you're still awake/out of bed, that's 1."

### One-on-One Fun Rule
15 minutes per day per child. No phone. No TV. No corrections. Let them choose the activity. This fills the emotional bank account that discipline draws from.
