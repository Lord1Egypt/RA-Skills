# Prompt 4: Reputation Management + Community Outreach
# Addiction Treatment & Behavioral Health Marketing Kit

## COMPLIANCE GATES

**Gate 1 — 42 CFR Part 2:** No content may identify, imply, or suggest that any specific individual received addiction treatment services. All reputation content must be verified as Part 2-compliant before publication.
**Gate 2 — HIPAA marketing authorization:** Patient testimonials require documented HIPAA marketing authorization (45 CFR 164.508(a)(3)) SEPARATE from treatment consent. Verify authorization on file before including any attributed quote.
**Gate 3 — Zero incentivized reviews:** No compensation, gift cards, service discounts, or other inducements for reviews. FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides + NV NAC consumer fraud.
**Gate 4 — Zero AKS referral language:** Professional referral outreach cannot include any language implying financial or in-kind consideration for referrals.
**Gate 5 — NAATP outcome language:** No outcome statistics. Use SAMHSA evidence-based framework references instead.

## GBP REVIEW RESPONSE TEMPLATES (Type A)

Generate response templates for 15 common review scenarios. All responses must:
- NEVER confirm or deny that the reviewer received addiction treatment at the facility (42 CFR Part 2)
- Use generic "if you were a patient or family member" framing
- Direct complaints to private channel (clinical director direct line, not public GBP)
- Include facility credentials naturally in responses where appropriate

**Template scenarios:**

1. 5-star review, general positive (no clinical details mentioned)
Response: "Thank you for taking the time to share your experience. [FACILITY_NAME] is [CARF/Joint Commission] accredited and committed to providing evidence-based care. We appreciate you supporting our mission. If you'd like to share your experience to help others, please feel free to contact our clinical team — we welcome the opportunity to connect."

2. 5-star review mentioning specific staff member by name
Response: Include staff name and thank the reviewer, but do NOT add clinical context about what the staff member did clinically (do not confirm the reviewer was a patient).

3. 5-star review mentioning specific treatment modality
Response: Acknowledge the modality generically: "We're glad to hear that [CBT / MAT / group therapy] was helpful in your experience. Evidence-based treatment approaches are central to our clinical programming." Do NOT confirm the reviewer received that treatment at the facility.

4. 4-star review with minor complaint about facility amenities
Response: Acknowledge the feedback, note that comfort is important to the treatment environment, invite direct contact with [NAME], Patient Experience Coordinator at [PHONE/EMAIL].

5. 3-star review, vague dissatisfaction
Response: Thank for feedback, express commitment to improvement, invite private contact. Zero defensive language. Zero clinical confirmation.

6. 1-star review alleging clinical mistreatment
Response: "We take all feedback about patient care seriously. We are unable to discuss any individual's experience in a public forum as federal law protects the confidentiality of all persons who receive behavioral health services. We invite you to contact [NAME], Clinical Director, directly at [PHONE] so we can address your concerns privately and thoroughly. Our goal is to provide the highest standard of evidence-based care."

7. 1-star review alleging billing fraud
Response: Direct to billing coordinator privately. Do NOT confirm or deny any treatment episode or billing. Note that all billing disputes are handled through the facility's internal resolution process and, if needed, Nevada's Division of Insurance or DHCFP if Medicaid.

8. 1-star review alleging patient brokering / body brokering
Response: "[FACILITY_NAME] does not engage in patient brokering, referral fee arrangements, or any financial incentive programs for admissions. We are NAATP members in good standing and operate under strict ethical guidelines. We encourage anyone with concerns about industry fraud to contact the FTC at ftc.gov/complaint."

9. Review from apparent family member asking about loved one's status
Response: 42 CFR Part 2 response (same as GBP Q&A in Prompt 2) + include 988 Helpline reference.

10. Review praising "scholarship" or "free treatment"
Response: Acknowledge that the facility has financial assistance programs, but do NOT confirm the reviewer received free treatment. "We work with patients and families to make evidence-based care accessible. For information about financial assistance, please call [PHONE]."

11. Review from competitor or obvious bad actor (implausible content)
Response: [Guidance only — do not respond publicly to obvious bot reviews; flag for Google review removal process instead]

12-15: [Generate 4 additional scenario templates based on facility's primary service lines and most likely review topics]

## ALUMNI OUTREACH — REVIEW GENERATION SEQUENCE (Type B)

**Pre-condition check:**
- 42 CFR Part 2-compliant alumni consent must be documented BEFORE outreach
- HIPAA marketing authorization (45 CFR 164.508(a)(3)) must be documented separately if any patient-identifying information is included in outreach
- Outreach method: email only (SMS requires TCPA compliance separately)

**Sequence (3 messages, 14 days apart):**

Email 1 (30 days post-discharge, to alumni with documented consent):
Subject: "[FACILITY_NAME] — Your Experience Matters"
Body: "Thank you for trusting [FACILITY_NAME] during your time with us. As we continue our work of providing evidence-based care, we would greatly appreciate hearing about your experience with our program. If you're willing, a brief review on Google helps others who are searching for treatment options find trustworthy, accredited care.

[GOOGLE REVIEW LINK — direct facility GBP link]

Your review is entirely voluntary and you may share as much or as little as you are comfortable with. Please know that your privacy is protected — we cannot see who left a review, and we will not identify you in any way.

If you have any questions or concerns, please contact [NAME], Aftercare Coordinator, at [PHONE]."

*This email is sent only to individuals who have provided documented written consent to receive alumni program communications. To opt out, reply STOP or contact [PHONE].*

Email 2 (44 days post-discharge, only if Email 1 was not acted upon):
Subject: "Quick note from [FACILITY_NAME]"
Short version — one paragraph reminder, same review link. Note: "We won't send further follow-up if you prefer not to. Your experience and privacy are our priority."

Email 3 (58 days post-discharge, final):
Brief check-in, offer to connect with aftercare resources, single mention of review opportunity, clear opt-out.

**NO INCENTIVES for reviews.** Do not offer gift cards, discounts, merchandise, or any other consideration.

## ALUMNI NEWSLETTER (Type C)

Generate one quarterly newsletter issue (600-900 words). Structure:

1. **From the Clinical Director** (bylined) — clinical education topic (not outcomes-focused): seasonal mental health + substance use risk factors; new evidence on MAT efficacy (cite published research); community resource spotlight
2. **Community resource spotlight** — SAMHSA 988, local SMART Recovery/AA/NA meeting listing (public information), Nevada Peer Support network
3. **Alumni voices** — ONLY if HIPAA marketing authorizations are on file. If available: 1-2 first-person quotes (first name only, with authorization on file). If not available: omit section; replace with clinical education content.
4. **Aftercare resources** — telehealth continuing care options, alumni group schedule, alumni coordinator contact
5. **Upcoming events** — community education events, family support groups (publicly available)

**Footer:** "This newsletter is sent to individuals who have provided written consent to receive alumni communications from [FACILITY_NAME]. Federal law (42 CFR Part 2) protects the confidentiality of your participation in our programs. To opt out, contact [NAME] at [PHONE] or [EMAIL]. [FACILITY_NAME] does not share your contact information or treatment history with any third party without your specific written consent."

## PROFESSIONAL REFERRAL LETTERS (Type D)

**AKS gate:** Confirm zero financial arrangement with referral target before generating. If any financial relationship exists, this prompt cannot generate compliant marketing — consult healthcare compliance attorney.

Generate 3 professional referral letter variants:

**Variant 1 — Emergency Department Liaison Letter:**
Addressed to: ED Medical Director / ED Social Work Supervisor
Key elements:
- ASAM level service lines offered + rapid intake process (clinical assessment within [X hours] of referral call)
- 24/7 clinical intake line (direct, not a call center)
- Medical Director credentials + board certification
- Accreditation + DPBH license
- Specific clinical value: "We accept ED referrals for patients with acute intoxication who are medically stable for transfer to [Level 3.7 / 2.5 / 2.1]"
- CIWA/COWS (withdrawal severity tools) awareness — shows clinical credibility to ED staff
- Zero referral incentive language
- Offer: peer consultation calls with Medical Director for complex cases (no compensation)

**Variant 2 — Primary Care / Internal Medicine Letter:**
Addressed to: PCP Office Manager or directly to MD/PA/NP
Key elements:
- SBIRT (Screening, Brief Intervention, Referral to Treatment) context — how to identify referral candidates in primary care
- MAT co-management option (if applicable): offer to co-manage buprenorphine patients with PCP after stabilization
- Credential transparency: Medical Director name, ABAM/ABPM-AM, NV Medical Board #
- Rapid intake process
- Fax referral form offer (HIPAA-compliant fax only)
- Zero referral incentive language

**Variant 3 — EAP / Employee Assistance Program Letter:**
Addressed to: EAP Account Manager or HR Benefits Director
Key elements:
- ASAM level appropriateness for working adults (emphasize IOP/OP programming that accommodates employment)
- 42 CFR Part 2 confidentiality assurance: employer will NOT be notified of patient identity, diagnosis, or treatment status without patient's specific written consent
- FMLA-compatibility: "Our [IOP/PHP] programming is compatible with FMLA leave structure"
- Insurance coverage awareness: most major commercial plans covered
- Zero referral fee or kickback language
- Point of contact: clinical intake coordinator (not a "business development" title — connotes referral payment arrangement)

## COMMUNITY EDUCATION CONTENT (Type E)

Generate 3 community education presentation outlines:

1. **Faith community presentation (45 min):** Addiction as a chronic brain disorder (ASAM/DSM-5 framework); stigma reduction; how to support a family member; when to seek professional help; SAMHSA 988; local treatment resources
2. **Employer/HR presentation (30 min):** EAP utilization for SUD; FMLA basics for addiction treatment; ADA accommodation for individuals in recovery; drug-free workplace policy compliance; referral pathways
3. **School counselor presentation (45 min):** Adolescent SUD risk factors; teen-specific ASAM programming (if offered and DPBH-licensed for adolescents); DCFS/CPS coordination protocol disclosure; how to refer a student; SAMHSA resources for youth

All presentations: Zero outcome statistics. Zero facility promotion as "best" option. Position as community education — include SAMHSA NDATSS directory for full range of local options.

## BLOCKED CONTENT

❌ Review response that confirms or denies any individual received treatment
❌ Review incentives of any kind
❌ Alumni outreach without documented 42 CFR Part 2 consent
❌ Patient testimonials without HIPAA marketing authorization on file
❌ Professional referral letters implying any consideration for referrals
❌ "[X]% of referred patients achieve long-term sobriety" in any professional communication
❌ AKS-violating language: "preferred partner program," "referral bonus," "thank you gift for referrals"
❌ Alumni newsletter disclosing any individual's treatment participation without documented authorization
