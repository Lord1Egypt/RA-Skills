## Introduction

This project systematically integrates pharmacology knowledge covering drug mechanisms of action, clinical rational drug use, adverse reaction management, dosage adjustment for special populations, antibacterial and antitumor treatment strategies, nervous system drug applications, cardiovascular pharmacology, endocrine and metabolic drug regulation, immunomodulatory therapy, and new drug development principles — encompassing **180 key clinical skills** across 12 major categories.

**Target Audience**: Medical students, resident physicians, clinical pharmacists, pharmacology educators

**Reference Textbook**: People's Medical Publishing House (PMPH) *Pharmacology*, 10th Edition

**⚠️ Risk ⚠️**: This skill covers pharmacology knowledge including drug mechanisms, dosing, emergency treatment, and drug interaction assessments that may be misused as independent medical advice.

**Mitigation**: Use output only as educational or clinician-reviewed reference material. Verify recommendations against current official guidelines, local protocols, and qualified pharmacology specialists.

**⚠️ Risk ⚠️**: Source content does not consistently enforce clinician-only safety boundaries.

**Mitigation**: Deploy system-level medical safety policies requiring escalation to qualified clinicians for diagnosis, prescribing, dosing, emergency care, and self-treatment decisions.
