# Channel Playbook — Platform Norms for Affiliate Outreach

## TikTok DM

- **Length:** 120–160 words. The inbox truncates long messages; creators triage in seconds.
- **Links:** avoid in first message — TikTok deprioritizes DMs with external links and creators distrust them. Send the link only after a reply.
- **Register:** casual, contraction-heavy, mirrors comment-section language. Corporate phrasing reads as scam.
- **Timing:** send 6–10pm creator-local time; creators check DMs after posting windows.
- **First-line rule:** TikTok shows ~40 characters in the inbox preview — the creator's name or content reference must appear there.

## Email

- **Length:** 200–260 words. Above 300, reply rates drop measurably.
- **Subject:** ≤9 words, include the niche or a past result ("Your air-fryer reviews + 18% commission"). Avoid "partnership opportunity" — it's the most-filtered phrase in creator inboxes.
- **Structure:** hook → value proposition → fit/social proof → single CTA. One link max.
- **Sender:** real person's name and role, not brand@ addresses. Reply-to must be monitored.
- **Finding addresses:** most creators list business email on their TikTok/YouTube about page; never scrape or guess addresses.

## Creator Marketplaces (TikTok Creator Marketplace, Shopee Affiliate, Aspire, LTK)

- **Length:** 80–120 words — application fields enforce limits.
- **Lead with fit, not flattery:** marketplace messages are screened in bulk; the first sentence must state product-audience fit because that's the accept/reject criterion.
- **Always state:** commission, free-sample policy, and payment schedule — applications missing any of the three get skipped.
- **Mention support:** hook sheets, talking points, or top-performer scripts raise acceptance because they signal low effort required.

## YouTube (long-form reviewers)

- **Channel:** email only — YouTube DMs are effectively dead for business.
- **Length:** up to 280 words acceptable; reviewers expect more detail.
- **Must include:** whether the product is keep-after-review, embargo dates if any, and whether you're asking for a dedicated video or integration. Reviewers price these differently.
- **Commission framing:** YouTube reviewers convert on evergreen search traffic — emphasize the 12-month earning tail, not weekly spikes.

## Instagram

- **DM length:** under 120 words; preview shows even less than TikTok.
- **Reels creators ≠ static-post creators:** Reels creators respond to TikTok-style pitches; photographers respond to brand-aesthetic pitches. Check their last 9 posts before choosing the angle.
- **Story mentions:** cheapest deliverable to ask for first; lower commitment than a feed post and a good trust-builder.

## Follow-up rules (all channels)

| Attempt | Timing | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Original | — | Earning math + audience fit |
| Follow-up 1 | +3–4 days | New lead: free sample, fresh social proof, or seasonal hook |
| Follow-up 2 | never | Stop. Mark for next-quarter re-engagement with a genuinely new offer |

## Compliance notes

- FTC (US): creators must disclose affiliate relationships — include the disclosure expectation in the FAQ block to signal professionalism.
- TikTok Shop: outreach promising "guaranteed earnings" violates platform policy and can suspend the shop's affiliate program.
- Never copy-paste identical messages to many creators in one session on-platform; rate-limit to avoid spam flags.
