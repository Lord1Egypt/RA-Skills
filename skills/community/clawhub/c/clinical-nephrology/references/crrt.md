# CRRT and ICU renal replacement therapy

Use this reference for CRRT, CVVH, CVVHD, CVVHDF, SLED, intermittent hemodialysis in ICU, citrate anticoagulation, ultrafiltration, dose, and drug adjustment in critical illness.

## Core approach

1. Define indication: refractory hyperkalemia, severe acidosis, pulmonary edema/volume overload, uremic complications, toxin removal, severe AKI with fluid/metabolic needs, or local ICU protocol.
2. Identify hemodynamic status, vasopressor needs, fluid balance target, catheter function, anticoagulation contraindications, liver failure, lactate trend, and bleeding risk.
3. Choose modality conceptually:
   - CVVH: convection-focused clearance.
   - CVVHD: diffusion-focused clearance.
   - CVVHDF: combined diffusion and convection.
   - SLED: hybrid intermittent/prolonged therapy.
4. Discuss prescribed dose vs delivered dose, interruptions, filter clotting, and fluid balance.
5. Review electrolytes frequently: potassium, phosphate, magnesium, calcium, bicarbonate, ionized calcium if citrate is used.
6. Warn that drug dosing during CRRT depends on modality, effluent rate, residual kidney function, protein binding, volume of distribution, and local pharmacy guidance.

## Safety notes

- Do not invent exact protocols for citrate, calcium infusion, or antibiotic dosing. Recommend local ICU/nephrology/pharmacy protocol and current references.
- For unstable patients, emphasize bedside nephrology/ICU decision-making.
