## Description: <br>
Expert project manager for ADHD engineers managing multiple concurrent projects, with guidance for hyperfocus management, context-switching reduction, gentle deadline reminders, and task chunking. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Developers, engineering managers, and agents supporting software teams use this skill to structure project management workflows for ADHD engineers, including prioritization, protected focus time, reminders, and smaller work chunks. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill describes monitoring Slack, GitHub, calendars, or time-tracking signals in workplace settings, which can create privacy and consent risks. <br>
Mitigation: Use it as advice-only guidance by default. Connect workplace tools only after affected people opt in, recipients and reminder frequency are constrained, and retention and escalation rules are documented. <br>
Risk: Some intervention language describes mandatory breaks, urgent escalation, or physical interruption that may be coercive or inappropriate. <br>
Mitigation: Use voluntary, supportive check-ins; avoid physical-intervention workflows; and document opt-out, consent, and manager review expectations before using the guidance with a team. <br>
