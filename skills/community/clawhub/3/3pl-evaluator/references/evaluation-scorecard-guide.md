# Evaluation Scorecard Guide

Scoring anchors for the weighted 3PL scorecard. Score 1-5; never score from marketing material alone — every 4 or 5 needs a verified source (demo, contract language, reference call, published SLA with credits).

## Criterion 1 — Landed cost (default weight 30%)

Convert each candidate's landed cost per order to a score: cheapest = 5; score = 5 × (cheapest ÷ candidate), floor 1. Use the regular-month number; flag if peak ranking differs. Never score from rate cards — only from the normalized basket model.

## Criterion 2 — Service & SLAs (default 25%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Contractual SLAs with financial credits: ≥99.5% order accuracy, ≥95% same-day ship at stated cut-off (≥2pm local), dock-to-stock ≤48h, returns processed ≤3 days |
| 4 | Same metrics contractual but thinner credits, or cut-off before 1pm |
| 3 | Published targets, no credits; reference calls confirm performance roughly matches |
| 2 | Verbal targets only, or references report inconsistent peak performance |
| 1 | No measurable commitments, or known accuracy <98.5% |

Verify: ask for the last 12 months of actual accuracy and same-day-ship data. A provider that won't share actuals caps at 2.

## Criterion 3 — Technology & integrations (default 20%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Native, maintained integration with all the seller's channels; real-time inventory sync (<15 min); branded returns portal; webhooks/API with docs; sandbox demo done with seller's own store |
| 4 | Native integrations for primary channels, minor gaps bridged by middleware the 3PL maintains |
| 3 | Integration via third-party connector (extra fee), daily batch inventory sync |
| 2 | CSV uploads or email-driven workflows for any core process |
| 1 | Proprietary closed system, no API, manual reporting |

Watch for: per-order integration surcharges, "integration roadmap" promises, channel limits on the base tier.

## Criterion 4 — Network & category fit (default 15%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Warehouse nodes put ≥80% of the seller's customers within 2-day ground; proven category experience (same handling class, compliance, e.g., cosmetics lot tracking, battery certification); seller volume sits in provider's middle 50% of client sizes |
| 4 | One criterion slightly off (e.g., 70% 2-day coverage) |
| 3 | Single node with acceptable zone profile, or thin category track record |
| 2 | Seller would be in bottom 10% of client sizes, or category requires exceptions process |
| 1 | Wrong network geometry for customer base, or no category capability |

Compute zone-weighted average from the seller's destination data — do not eyeball the map.

## Criterion 5 — Contract flexibility (default 10%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Month-to-month or ≤12-month term, no/low minimums first 6 months, 60-day no-cause exit, data export and inventory release terms written, surcharges capped |
| 4 | 12-month term with reasonable minimums and exit clause |
| 3 | 12-month, auto-renewal with 90-day notice window, uncapped peak surcharges |
| 2 | Multi-year term or minimums above current volume |
| 1 | Multi-year + onboarding fee + punitive exit/wind-down terms |

## Adjusting weights

Shift weight toward service for high-AOV or fragile goods (cost 25 → service 30). Shift toward technology for multi-channel sellers (tech 25-30). Shift toward cost for commodity low-margin goods (cost 35-40). Record final weights in the output; never change weights after seeing scores.

## Reference call script (15 minutes)

1. How long have you been with them, and at what volume?
2. What does your invoice accuracy look like — have you caught errors?
3. How did the last two peak seasons actually go (ship times, surcharges, communication)?
4. When something goes wrong, how fast is resolution and who do you talk to?
5. Have fees crept since year one? Which ones?
6. What would you negotiate differently if signing today?
7. Would you choose them again? (pause; let them fill silence)

Two references minimum, in the same category and volume band. A 3PL that cannot produce two is a red flag in itself.
