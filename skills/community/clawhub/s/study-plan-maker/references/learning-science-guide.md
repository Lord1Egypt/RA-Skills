# Learning Science & Productivity Guide for Study Plans

## Part 1: Evidence-Based Learning Techniques

### The Big 5 (Ranked by Research Effectiveness)

| Rank | Technique | Effectiveness | Best For |
|------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | **Practice Testing** (active recall) | Very High | Any subject |
| 2 | **Distributed Practice** (spaced repetition) | Very High | Memory-heavy topics |
| 3 | **Interleaved Practice** | High | Problem-solving subjects |
| 4 | **Elaborative Interrogation** (asking "why") | Moderate | Conceptual understanding |
| 5 | **Self-Explanation** (Feynman technique) | Moderate | Complex topics |

### Techniques to Avoid (Low Research Support)
- ❌ Re-reading (feels productive, is not)
- ❌ Highlighting (passive, minimal learning)
- ❌ Keyword mnemonics (shallow memory only)
- ❌ Learning styles matching (myth — not research-backed)

## Part 2: Spaced Repetition Schedule

### Forgetting Curve & Review Timing (Ebbinghaus)
```
After learning new material:
- Review 1: within 24 hours (recall ~80%)
- Review 2: 3 days later (recall ~90%)
- Review 3: 7 days later (recall ~95%)
- Review 4: 14 days later (recall ~97%)
- Review 5: 30 days later (recall ~99%)
```

### Anki Default Intervals
For digital flashcards (Anki):
- New card → 1 day → 3 days → 8 days → 21 days → ...
- "Hard" response: shorter interval
- "Easy" response: longer interval
- Daily practice: 20–30 minutes of reviews keeps 100s of cards fresh

### Manual Spaced Review System (No App)
```
Create 5 folders/notebooks:
- Box 1: Review daily (new/hard material)
- Box 2: Review every 3 days
- Box 3: Review weekly
- Box 4: Review bi-weekly
- Box 5: Review monthly (mastered)

When you get a card right: move forward one box
When you get a card wrong: move back to Box 1
```

## Part 3: Focus & Productivity Systems

### Pomodoro Technique (for Study Sessions)
```
Standard Pomodoro:
- 25 minutes focused study → 5 minute break
- After 4 Pomodoros → 30 minute break

Modified for deep learning:
- 45 minutes focused study → 10 minute break
- After 3 sessions → 30 minute break

Key rules:
- Phone in another room / airplane mode
- No multitasking during session
- Write down distractions (don't act on them)
- Stand up and walk during breaks
```

### Study Environment Optimization
| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| Noise | White noise or nature sounds (not music with lyrics) |
| Temperature | 68–72°F (20–22°C) optimal for focus |
| Lighting | Bright, natural light if possible |
| Phone | Out of sight = out of mind (reduces urge by 40%) |
| Hydration | Water on desk — dehydration reduces cognition |
| Posture | Upright (sitting = alertness > lying down) |

### Pre-Study Ritual (5 minutes)
1. Write today's specific goal: "By end of session I will be able to..."
2. Review previous session notes for 2 minutes
3. Set timer for your first Pomodoro
4. Close everything except what you need

| Session Length | Focus Quality | Best For |
|---------------|--------------|---------|
| 15–30 min | High (warm-up) | Review, flashcards, reading |

## Part 5: Common Study Plan Pitfalls

### ❌ Planning Pitfalls
1. **Overloading Day 1**: Schedule only 70% of your max capacity
2. **No buffer time**: Add 20% extra time to each phase for overruns
3. **Ignoring review time**: Retention work = 25–30% of total study time
4. **Linear progression**: Hard topics should be revisited spirally, not once
5. **No practice tests**: Never discover real knowledge gaps without testing

### ❌ Execution Pitfalls
1. **Passive learning**: Re-reading and highlighting feel like progress but aren't
2. **Comfort zone studying**: Always practicing what you know well
3. **Skipping review sessions**: Breaks spaced repetition chain
4. **Cramming everything at end**: Works short-term, fails long-term retention
5. **Perfectionism on notes**: Note-taking ≠ learning; testing = learning

### ✅ Recovery Strategies When Behind
```
Falling behind by 1 week:
→ Identify lowest-priority topics and cut or shrink them
→ Add 30 min Saturday and Sunday to catch up
→ Switch to 80/20 mode: what 20% of content covers 80% of exam?

Falling behind by 2+ weeks:
→ Recalibrate the entire plan with new deadline awareness
→ Have honest conversation: is original goal still achievable?
→ Consider modified goal (e.g., pass at lower score, extend timeline)
```

## Part 6: Motivation & Habit Formation

### Habit Loop for Studying
```
CUE → ROUTINE → REWARD

CUE: Same time, same place every day
ROUTINE: Study session (use plan as guide)
REWARD: Track streak, small treat, progress mark

Example:
CUE: 8pm, desk cleared, phone away
ROUTINE: 45-min focused study session
REWARD: Mark day complete on habit tracker, then watch 1 YouTube video
```

### Progress Tracking Methods
1. **Streak tracker**: Mark each day on calendar (don't break the chain)
2. **Hours log**: Total hours studied this week vs. goal
3. **Topic completion**: X of Y topics mastered
4. **Practice test scores**: Track trend over time
5. **Skill demonstrations**: Weekly "can I do X yet?" check

### When Motivation Drops (Week 3–4 Slump)
- Reconnect with your "why" — write it down again
- Find a study partner or community (accountability multiplies follow-through)
- Review early wins and progress (you've come further than you think)
- Shrink the session: "Just 10 minutes" often turns into more
- Change the environment: coffee shop, library, outdoors
