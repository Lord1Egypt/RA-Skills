# double-materiality-assessment

Facilitate a CSRD/ESRS-compliant **double materiality assessment** (DMA) — the foundational exercise that determines which sustainability topics a company must disclose under the European Sustainability Reporting Standards.

## When to use

A sustainability lead, ESG analyst, reporting manager, or assurance preparer needs to:

- determine which ESRS topics are **material** for a specific reporting entity and reporting period,
- score **impact materiality** (effects on people and the environment, including the upstream and downstream value chain) and **financial materiality** (effects on enterprise value, cash flows, cost of capital, and access to finance) **separately**, and
- produce an audit-ready record of methodology, stakeholder engagement, scoring, thresholds, and conclusions.

EFRAG's 2025 implementation review flagged the most common DMA failures: treating it as a checklist, **aggregating** impact and financial scores into a single matrix, omitting **value-chain** impacts, and using stakeholder engagement that cannot be evidenced. This skill is designed to prevent each of those failures.

## What it produces

- Reporting-entity profile and value-chain map (upstream / own operations / downstream)
- ESRS topic longlist working from the topical standards (ESRS E1–E5, S1–S4, G1) plus entity-specific candidate topics
- Stakeholder engagement log (groups, channel, evidence)
- Per-topic **Impact Materiality** scoring: Scale × Scope × Irremediability × Likelihood, across Short / Medium / Long term, separately for **actual** vs **potential** impacts and **negative** vs **positive** impacts
- Per-topic **Financial Materiality** scoring: Magnitude × Likelihood, across ST / MT / LT
- Threshold setting and rationale (separate thresholds for impact and financial)
- Ranked **material-topic list** with topic-level rationale, value-chain location, and time horizon
- Audit trail: methodology, sources, decisions, dissent, version
- DRAFT label and required ESG-lead and (where applicable) assurance-provider sign-off

## Inputs the agent will ask for

One question at a time:

1. Reporting entity name, sector (NACE), and reporting period
2. Wave (Wave 1–4 under CSRD), first-time or subsequent assessment
3. Geographies of own operations
4. Value-chain map (upstream tiers, own operations, downstream)
5. Stakeholder groups identified and how each has been or will be engaged
6. ESRS topic longlist starting point (sector benchmark / peer review / EFRAG sector guidance / internal)
7. Scoring scale to be used (1–5 or 1–4) and thresholds
8. Time horizons (ST / MT / LT) and definitions (e.g., ≤1 yr / 1–5 yr / >5 yr)
9. Assurance posture (limited / reasonable / none) and assurance provider
