**Resolution (mk26):** Confirmed by fetching and analyzing the original Raduga article — Algiz inverted meaning is indeed absent despite being classified as invertible. **Status:** Documented as permanent gap in Raduga source material. Cannot be filled without Raduga providing the missing content. Position is still well-covered by 4 other traditions.

## Systematic Non-Gaps (❌ Explained)

The following ❌ entries are NOT gaps — they represent traditions that legitimately don't recognize or cover certain positions:

### Bednenko ❌ for all Northumbrian/Solle/Wyrd
Bednenko's three-position system was developed exclusively for the 24 Elder Futhark runes. She explicitly does not cover Northumbrian additions. This is by design.

### Western ❌ for all Mirrored positions
The Western/RuneDictionary tradition uses a binary Upright/Reversed system. It has no concept of a "Mirrored" position. This is not a gap — it's a structural difference between binary and three-position frameworks.

### Raduga ❌ for all Northumbrian/Solle/Wyrd
Raduga's article only covers the 24 Elder Futhark runes. She does not address Northumbrian additions.

### Velya/Kys ❌ for all Northumbrian/Solle/Wyrd
The Velya and Kys schools only provide meanings for the 24 Elder Futhark runes. They do not cover Northumbrian additions.

### Velya/Kys ❌ for Mirrored positions
Like the Western tradition, Velya and Kys use binary Upright/Reversed. They have no "Mirrored" concept.

### Maelinhon ❌ for Inverted/Mirrored positions
Maelinhon's tradition provides position-agnostic meanings (galdrastav, arcana, meditation, key values). She does not distinguish between Direct, Mirrored, and Inverted positions for any rune.

### DuhMaga ❌ for Inverted positions of Northumbrian runes
DuhMaga's 4-aettir system provides direct/structural meanings only. No inverted meanings are documented in our files.

### DuhMaga ❌ for Elder Futhark
Our reference files only contain DuhMaga's Northumbrian rune entries. DuhMaga covers all 33 runes in its aettir system (including Elder Futhark), but those entries are not in our reference files.

## Structural Observations (Not Gaps, But Worth Noting)

### 1. The Mirrored Position Coverage Gap Is Structural
Only 2 of 8 traditions (Bednenko and Raduga) recognize the Mirrored position. The other 6 use binary (Direct/Inverted or Upright/Reversed). This means when a Mirrored position falls in a reading, the reader can only layer Bednenko and Raduga meanings — no Western, Velya, Kys, Maelinhon, or DuhMaga perspective is available for that position. This is inherent to the tradition landscape, not a documentation failure.

### 2. Northumbrian Runes Have Only One Position Tradition
For inverted Northumbrian runes, ONLY the Western (English Scholarship) tradition provides meanings. No Russian esoteric tradition covers them. This means Northumbrian inverted positions have no interpretive depth-layering — a single voice rather than a choir. Consider whether Velya/Kys-style interpretations could be developed for Northumbrian runes.

### 3. Maelinhon's Position-Agnostic Approach
Maelinhon provides the richest magical-application layer (galdrastav, arcana, meditation, key values) but treats all runes as position-agnostic. This is a lost opportunity: inverted Algiz in a galdrastav could have very different practical applications than direct Algiz. Consider developing position-specific Maelinhon galdrastav applications for runes that appear frequently in inverted positions.

### 4. DuhMaga's Missing Inverted Meanings
DuhMaga's 4-aettir system is structurally deep (cosmological correspondences, aettir dynamics) but provides only Direct meanings. For a tradition that otherwise excels at philosophical depth, the absence of inverted meanings for Northumbrian runes is notable. Investigate whether DuhMaga's published works include position-variant interpretations.
