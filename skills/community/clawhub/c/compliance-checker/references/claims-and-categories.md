# Claims & Categories Reference

Claim rules by type and category, with compliant rewrite patterns. Platform policies change and vary by market — treat this as the audit framework, and verify current category lists in seller center for the target market.

## 1. Claim taxonomy and risk

| Claim type | Example | Risk | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disease/treatment/cure | "cures acne", "treats eczema", "anti-inflammatory" | Critical | Prohibited for consumer products; drug-level claims need drug-level approval |
| Prevention/medical outcome | "prevents hair loss", "boosts immunity against illness" | Critical | Same class as treatment |
| Measurable performance | "10x collagen", "removes 99% of bacteria", "lasts 72 hours" | High | Allowed ONLY with substantiation on file matching the exact claim |
| Time-bound results | "results in 14 days" | High | Needs evidence for that timeline specifically |
| Superlative | "best", "#1", "world's strongest" | High | Substantiate (verifiable award/ranking) or remove |
| Authority endorsement | "doctor recommended", "FDA approved" | Critical | False authority claims are top enforcement targets; FDA does not "approve" cosmetics/supplements this way |
| Guarantee/absolute | "100% safe", "guaranteed results", "permanent" | High | Convert to actual policy terms or remove |
| Comparative vs. named competitor | "better than [brand]" | High | Invites both platform and legal action |
| Subjective/experience | "feels silky", "customers love" | Low | Generally safe; keep honest |
| Appearance/cosmetic effect | "reduces the appearance of fine lines" | Low-Med | The standard compliant pattern for beauty |

## 2. Compliant rewrite patterns

- Treatment → appearance/experience: "eliminates wrinkles" → "smooths the appearance of fine lines".
- Cure → support/maintain (market-dependent for supplements): "cures brittle nails" → "supports healthy-looking nails".
- Measured → qualitative unless evidenced: "kills 99.9% of germs" → "helps keep surfaces clean" (or keep the figure WITH the test report on file and cited).
- Guarantee → policy: "results guaranteed" → "30-day return policy if you're not satisfied".
- Authority → disclosed testimonial: "dermatologist approved" → "'I use this in my routine' — Dr. X, paid partner" (where testimonial rules allow).
- Urgency → truthful scope: "today only!" (recurring) → real end-dated promotion or remove.
- Rule of thumb: keep the emotional benefit, drop the mechanism/medical assertion. Sell the outcome the buyer can see and feel.

## 3. Restricted & prohibited categories (verify current list per market)

**Commonly prohibited outright:** drugs and drug paraphernalia, weapons, tobacco/vapes (most markets), adult content, counterfeit/replica goods, recalled products, medical devices above low-risk classes (varies).

**Commonly restricted (allowed with approval/documents):**
| Category | Typical requirements |
|---|---|
| Supplements/vitamins | Category approval, COA/ingredient docs, market-specific claim standards |
| Skincare/cosmetics | Ingredient compliance, sometimes notification numbers (e.g., NMPA, CPNP analogs by market) |
| Food & beverage | Production/import licenses, labeling, shelf-life data |
| Kids' products & toys | Safety certifications (e.g., CPC, EN71 analogs) |
| Electricals/electronics | Safety certs (UL/CE analogs), battery documentation |
| Branded goods | Brand authorization letter or invoice chain |
| OTC topicals (acne, pain) | Often pushed into drug-adjacent rules — strictest review |

**Audit rule:** category gate FIRST. Claim-perfect copy on an unapproved restricted product is still a removal.

## 4. Pricing & promotion rules

- Reference (strikethrough) price must be a price the product actually sold at recently in meaningful volume — not the launch-day placeholder, not MSRP never charged.
- Discount math must compute ("was $40 now $20" = 50%, not "60% OFF").
- Countdown/urgency must reflect a real offer end; perpetual or auto-resetting countdowns are deceptive-pricing violations.
- "Free" claims: shipping/gift must be genuinely free at checkout — no fee resurfacing.
- Price baiting (advertised price unavailable at checkout for the shown variant) is detected automatically by price-consistency checks.

## 5. Imagery and format rules

- No before/after transformation comparisons for health, beauty, or weight categories (the highest-enforcement format).
- No medical settings/uniforms implying clinical endorsement.
- No text overlays making claims the description couldn't make ("FDA approved" in a render is OCR-detected).
- No shock/fear formats (disease imagery, side-effect horror stories) as hooks.
- Product must match images: same variant, accurate color/size; AI-generated imagery must not misrepresent the physical product.

## 6. Platform vs. law

Passing platform policy does NOT satisfy consumer-protection and ad law (FTC, ASA, EU UCPD analogs), which apply to the same claims with their own penalties — and legal substantiation standards are often stricter. For supplements, topicals, kids' products, or financial-adjacent offers, recommend a legal review in the report. This skill audits for platform enforcement risk; it is not legal advice.
