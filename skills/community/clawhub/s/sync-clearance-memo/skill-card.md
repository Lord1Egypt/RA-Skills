## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a music supervisor, sync licensing director, music publisher, or artist manager needs to evaluate and document the clearance path for a song placement in film, TV, advertising, video games, or branded content. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Music supervisors, sync licensing directors, music publishers, and artist managers use this skill to assess song placement rights and produce a draft clearance memo before deal negotiation. It supports composition and master rights mapping, sample-chain review, territory and term checks, indicative fee ranges, and open-item tracking. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Draft rights-holder contacts, ownership shares, restrictions, and fee ranges may be incomplete or inaccurate. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm all rights information with authoritative sources and actual rights holders before negotiation or placement. <br>
Risk: A draft clearance memo could be mistaken for a license, legal opinion, authorization, or binding quote. <br>
Mitigation: Keep outputs labeled as draft-only, not a license, and treat all fee ranges as indicative until reviewed by the licensing team. <br>
Risk: Uncleared samples or unresolved rights issues can block the proposed placement. <br>
Mitigation: Flag uncleared samples and unresolved rights gaps as blocking or open items before proceeding. <br>
