## Description: <br>
Provides a structured workflow for deep public web research and summarization, using entity mapping, advanced search operators, multilingual queries, source cross-checking, and dated source attribution to retrieve hard-to-find target information. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External researchers, analysts, and agents use this skill to plan and execute deep public web searches for cross-border industry research, policy and market access questions, competitor intelligence, and technical standards traceability. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Broad web research on competitor, pricing, procurement, or regulatory topics can surface sensitive or misleading information if sources are not constrained. <br>
Mitigation: Use only public or authorized sources, disclose translated or local-language queries when relevant, and include source links, dates, and confidence levels for important claims. <br>
Risk: Multilingual and third-party source research can introduce translation errors, stale details, or conflicts between official and third-party records. <br>
Mitigation: Separate official confirmations from third-party references, prefer dated or audit-marked sources, and call out conflicts instead of merging them into a single unsupported conclusion. <br>
