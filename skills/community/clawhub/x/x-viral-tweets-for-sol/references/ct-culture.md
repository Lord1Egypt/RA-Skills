# CT Culture Reference

Quick-check reference for tone, slang, tribal lines. Glance before generating to avoid outsider tells.

## Tone defaults

- **Lowercase** is the default register on CT. Caps = emphasis or character voice.
- **Terse.** Cut every word that doesn't pay rent.
- **No marketing voice.** Strip "excited," "thrilled," "pleased," "delighted."
- **No throat-clearing.** "Just sharing some thoughts on..." → cut.
- **First-person fine.** "I" and "we" both natural. Avoid "one" and "users" when you mean "people."
- **Profanity OK in moderation** — matches the medium, but don't lean on it. Overuse reads try-hard.

## Tribal lines — handle with care

CT has factions. Picking fights builds engagement; picking the wrong fight loses deal flow.

**Safe to ridicule (gently):**
- ETH gas fees
- L2 fragmentation
- Bitcoin maxis' "store of value only" framing
- VC-pumped tokens with no users
- Brand accounts trying too hard

**Risky:**
- Specific named founders unless they've earned heat
- Specific projects unless you have receipts
- Whole ethnic/national crypto communities — never punch this direction
- Specific real people for personal traits, family, appearance — never

**Don't touch:**
- Mocking people for losses
- Mocking anons' real-life situations
- Anything that crosses into doxxing

## Common outsider tells

Phrases that mark a user as not living on CT. Avoid in generated content unless intentional.

- "the Solana blockchain" (natives just say "solana")
- "cryptocurrency" (say "crypto")
- "blockchain technology" (say "the chain" or just don't)
- "smart contract platforms" (say "L1s" or "chains")
- "decentralized finance" (say "defi")
- "non-fungible tokens" (say "nfts")
- "I've been studying [topic] for X months" (nobody declares study hours on CT)
- "as a crypto enthusiast..." (crime)
- "in the web3 space..." (crime)
- "let's discuss..." (you discuss in podcasts; on CT you state)
- "Following are my thoughts on..." (just say the thoughts)
- Three-paragraph posts with intros and conclusions (compress)

## Emoji usage

Strong default: **zero**. CT is a low-emoji environment compared to other crypto channels (Telegram, Discord).

If using, one of:
- 🧠 (smart take)
- 🚨 (genuine breaking news)
- 🤝 (alignment / partnership)
- 📈 (chart/growth)
- 🤖 (AI / agent topic)
- 🫡 (respect / acknowledgement)
- 🔥 (rarely, only for genuine fire)

Banned by default: 🚀 (used to signal but now signals new-to-crypto), 💎🙌 (worn out), strings of any emoji.

## When to break the rules

The above are defaults. Break them when:

- **Character voice demands it.** A meme account character might be all-caps screaming. Fine.
- **The user has an existing voice.** Don't override an account's established style — match it.
- **Irony.** Posting "🚀🔥💎 to the moon!! 🌙" with self-aware framing can land.
- **The format demands it.** A thread of poetry-style one-word tweets needs capitalization choices that fit that genre.

When in doubt, lowercase, terse, no emoji, one slang term. That's the safe CT register.
