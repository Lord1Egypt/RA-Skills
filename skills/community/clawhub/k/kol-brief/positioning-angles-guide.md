# Positioning Angles Guide — Matching Angle to Creator and Category

The angle is the intersection of the product's strongest benefit and the creator's audience identity. Same product, different creator → different angle.

## Angle selection matrix

| Creator niche | Audience buys because | Lead the angle with |
|---|---|---|
| Budget / deal hunters | Price-to-value wins | Cost-per-use math, discount framing, dupe comparisons |
| Routine / lifestyle | Fits seamlessly into daily life | Before-work or nighttime routine integration |
| Expert / educational | Credibility and mechanism | Certifications (FDA-cleared), ingredients, engineering |
| Family / parenting | Safety + time savings | Kid-safe materials, one-handed use, cleanup time |
| Aesthetic / unboxing | Visual desirability | Design, packaging moment, shelf appeal |
| Tech / review | Specs beat marketing | Measurable performance vs. named alternatives |
| Fitness / transformation | Results and discipline | Trackable outcomes over fixed timeframes |

## Category notes

### Beauty & skincare
Strongest angles: visible-results-with-timeframe ("fine lines in 4 weeks"), cost-vs-salon, certification. Avoid generic "glow up". The proof element matters more here than anywhere — attach the study card or review count.

### Kitchen & home appliances
Strongest angles: replaces-N-tools, time-per-meal, family capacity. Demo moments sell: full chicken in the basket, 3-second fold-flat. LIVE formats outperform short video for >$100 items.

### Supplements & wellness
Angle must survive compliance: structure/function phrasing only ("supports", "helps maintain"), never disease claims. Lead with routine fit + third-party testing.

### Fashion & accessories
Angles: styling versatility (1 item, 5 outfits), size inclusivity shown on-body, fabric/feel close-ups. Creator's own styling choice IS the content — keep mandatory elements minimal.

### Electronics & gadgets
Angles: the one spec that beats the known alternative, setup time, battery life shown in real time. Tech audiences punish vague claims — give the creator exact numbers.

## One product, three creators — worked example

Product: $89 portable air cooler.

- To a budget-living creator (60K, deal-hunting audience): "AC-level cooling for under $90 and pennies a day to run."
- To a dorm/apartment lifestyle creator (120K, Gen-Z renters): "The no-installation cooler that moves from desk to bedside — landlord can't say anything."
- To a dad-hacks creator (200K, family audience): "Cools the nursery in 3 minutes without blasting the whole house."

Same product, three briefs, three different first-3-seconds demos.

## Anti-patterns

- **Angle stacking:** two angles in one brief halves both. Pick one; save the other for the next creator.
- **Brand-voice angle:** "premium quality lifestyle brand" is not an angle; it's a slogan. Angles name the audience's reason to buy.
- **Trend-chasing mismatch:** forcing a trending sound/format into the brief overrides the creator's judgment — note the trend as optional, never mandatory.
