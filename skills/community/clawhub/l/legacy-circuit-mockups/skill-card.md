## Description: <br>
Generate breadboard circuit mockups and visual diagrams using HTML5 Canvas drawing techniques for retrocomputing and vintage electronics projects. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Developers, educators, and electronics hobbyists use this skill to plan and explain breadboard layouts for LED, 555 timer, 6502, EEPROM, VIA, SRAM, LCD, and logic circuits before building or documenting them. It can also guide generation of supporting code or markup for Canvas-based visual mockups. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill can surface chip-programming and firmware-building guidance beyond visual circuit diagrams. <br>
Mitigation: Review generated hardware and programming instructions before use, especially when they go beyond mockup-only output. <br>
Risk: EEPROM programmer write or erase commands can permanently modify physical chips. <br>
Mitigation: Back up chip contents, confirm the exact device and image, verify voltage and orientation, and treat minipro or T48 commands as real hardware operations. <br>
Risk: Physical circuit wiring mistakes can damage components or connected equipment. <br>
Mitigation: Disconnect power before rewiring and verify pin orientation, polarity, supply voltage, and ground connections before applying power. <br>
