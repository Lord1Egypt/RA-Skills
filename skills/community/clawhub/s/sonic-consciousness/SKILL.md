# Sonic Consciousness Engineering

Design music that alters consciousness. Grounded in psychoacoustics, clinical psychology, logotherapy (Frankl), and existential philosophy.

## Core Framework: The Four Layers

### Layer 1 — Psychoacoustics (the physics of perception)
Ref: [references/psychoacoustics.md](references/psychoacoustics.md)

- Brainwave entrainment (binaural beats, isochronic tones, amplitude modulation)
- Frequency perception (Fletcher-Munson curves, critical bands, masking)
- Spatial perception (Haas effect, ITD/ILD, phantom center, mid/side)
- Temporal perception (attack transients, temporal masking, echo threshold)
- Harmonic perception (combination tones, roughness, consonance/dissonance)

### Layer 2 — Clinical Psychology (the neuroscience of the journey)
Ref: [references/clinical-psychology.md](references/clinical-psychology.md)

- Altered States of Consciousness (ASC) via rhythmic auditory stimulation
- Autonomic nervous system: sympathetic (energy) vs parasimpathetic (calm)
- Polyvagal theory: sub-bass and chest resonance as vagal activation
- Flow state engineering (Csikszentmihalyi): challenge-skill balance in musical structure
- Induced synesthesia: frequency combinations that provoke visual imagery
- Emotional contagion and mirror neurons in musical contexts
- Auditory fatigue: prevention and recovery in long sessions

### Layer 3 — Logotherapy & Meaning (the purpose)
Ref: [references/logotherapy-music.md](references/logotherapy-music.md)

- Frankl's three value pathways applied to music creation
- The noetic dimension: music as portal to specifically-human experience
- Creative values: producing as meaning-making
- Experiential values: the listener's journey as meaning-receiving
- Attitudinal values: transforming darkness/nostalgia into art
- Self-transcendence through sound: losing the ego in the mix

### Layer 4 — Existential Philosophy (the depth)
Ref: [references/existential-philosophy.md](references/existential-philosophy.md)

- Heidegger's Stimmung: music as mood-revealer, not mood-creator
- Kierkegaard's angst: tension-release as existential confrontation
- Camus and the absurd: creation as rebellion against meaninglessness
- Sartre's authenticity: trance as liberation from social roles
- Merleau-Ponty's embodiment: bass as bodily knowledge, not just hearing

## Production Workflow: Designing a Journey

### 1. Define the Consciousness Arc
Before touching the DAW:
- **Entry state**: Where is the listener when they press play? (alert, stressed, curious)
- **Target state**: Where do you want to take them? (trance, flow, catharsis, introspection)
- **Return state**: How do they come back? (gently, abruptly, transformed)

### 2. Map the Autonomic Curve
Plot your track's nervous system activation over time:
```
Sympathetic ▲  ╭──╮        ╭────╮
(energy)    │ ╭╯  ╰╮  ╭──╯    ╰╮
            │╯     ╰──╯        ╰──╮
Parasympath ▼                     ╰──  
            0:00  2:00  4:00  6:00  8:00
            Intro Build  Peak Valley  Outro
```

### 3. Layer the Entrainment Stack
- **Foundation**: Kick/bass pattern (isochronic entrainment)
- **Middle**: Harmonic content (emotional color)
- **Top**: Textural elements (spatial environment)
- **Hidden**: Subtle binaural/beating elements in stereo pairs

### 4. Apply Existential Structure
- **Intro** = Thrownness (Heidegger): the listener is thrown into your world
- **Build** = Angst (Kierkegaard): rising tension, confronting the unknown
- **Peak** = Self-transcendence (Frankl): ego dissolution, unity
- **Breakdown** = The Absurd (Camus): silence as confrontation with void
- **Return** = Authenticity (Sartre): the listener returns changed

### 5. Check the Meaning Layer (Frankl)
- Does this track give something to the world? (creative value)
- Does it offer a genuine experience? (experiential value)
- Does it transform suffering or darkness into beauty? (attitudinal value)

## Anti-Patterns

- **Fake depth**: Adding reverb everywhere ≠ spatial design. Use reverb with intention.
- **Volume = emotion**: Loudness war kills dynamics. Dynamic range IS the emotional range.
- **Complexity = quality**: A single well-placed note can be more powerful than 40 layers.
- **Ignoring fatigue**: Ears tire. Build rest points. Breakdowns exist for psychoacoustic recovery.
- **All theory, no feeling**: These frameworks serve intuition, not replace it. If it sounds right, the science is secondary.
