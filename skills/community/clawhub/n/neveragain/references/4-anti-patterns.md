## The Anti-Patterns of Movement-Building

### Anti-Pattern 1: Waiting for Permission

**The mistake:** "I need to be older. I need more experience. I need a better plan. I need adults to approve. I need a nonprofit structure first."

**#NeverAgain's reality:** David Hogg had no permission. He was 17, dyslexic, a "slow learner" who couldn't read until 4th grade. He went back to the school on his bike because nobody would drive him. **The movement started because he refused to wait for someone to say yes.**

**Symptoms:** Research paralysis, "let's form a committee first," waiting for a leader, adults saying "that's not how things work."

**The fix:** Do one thing today. Not a plan. A thing. A tweet. A sign. A conversation.

> **Case: The first meeting wasn't a meeting** (Chapter 5): Cameron Kasky invited people to his house. They talked, joked, vented. No agenda. No minutes. No action items. That "unproductive" session was what built the trust to act fast when the opportunity came.
> **Key takeaway:** If your first meeting requires a facilitator, you're already over-organizing.

### Anti-Pattern 2: Performing Decorum

**The mistake:** Being polite to people who are killing you. Respecting institutions that don't respect your life. Using "we respectfully disagree" language when seventeen of your classmates are dead.

**#NeverAgain's reality:** Cameron Kasky asked Marco Rubio to promise not to take NRA money — on live CNN. Rubio dodged. Cameron asked again. Rubio dodged again. Cameron asked a third time. Rubio wouldn't answer. That moment — "disrespectful" by traditional standards — defined the movement.

**Symptoms:** Apologizing for emotions. Writing carefully worded statements. Avoiding confrontation.

**The fix:** "We don't respect you just because you have 'senator' in front of your name."

> **Case: Fred Guttenberg vs. Rubio** (Chapter 6): Fred Guttenberg, a father who lost his daughter at Parkland, tried the "respectful" approach with Rubio at the CNN town hall. He asked a calm, direct question. Rubio filibustered. Fred got nowhere. Cameron then went viral by doing what Fred couldn't — breaking decorum.
> **Key takeaway:** Civility is a privilege of the safe. If you're fighting for your life, don't play by their rules.

### Anti-Pattern 3: Letting Them Frame You

**The mistake:** Defending yourself against accusations. "No, we're not crisis actors." "No, we're not communists." "No, we're not too young." Every denial reinforces their frame.

**#NeverAgain's reality:** They never denied being "crisis actors." They mocked the absurdity. "So we're good actors AND we're on TV all the time? Sounds like a successful career launch." They refused to play defense.

**Symptoms:** Long Twitter threads explaining yourself. Press releases correcting the record. Spending more time rebutting critics than advancing your agenda.

**The fix:** Don't explain. Attack. Or ignore. Never defend.

### Anti-Pattern 4: Ignoring the Race and Class Gap

**The mistake:** Building a movement that only speaks for privileged communities and wondering why it doesn't scale.

**#NeverAgain's reality:** The authors explicitly call this out: "If people only listen when privileged white kids get killed — and even then, only when the number of dead kids is high enough to make the news — we're never going to fix this problem." They name Liberty City kids who had the same protest energy but no media coverage. They name Tyra Hemans, who stood with them but never became famous.

**Symptoms:** All-white leadership. No outreach to communities most affected by gun violence. "School shootings" treated as a separate problem from "urban gun violence."

**The fix:** Share the mic. Go to Liberty City. Learn from communities who have been fighting this for decades.

### Anti-Pattern 5: Burnout Culture

**The mistake:** "We have to work 24/7 because people are dying." Sleeping on couches. Skipping meals. No boundaries.

**#NeverAgain's reality:** David acknowledges he was running on "anger fumes and dopamine hits of human decency." He says explicitly: "That's not a sustainable model." The group survived because they became a family, not a startup.

**Symptoms:** Core team collapse after 6 months. Infighting. Resentment. The movement falls apart from the inside.

**The fix:** Build love into the structure. Meetings should have laughing time. Conflicts should be addressed as "entropy" — the enemy — not as personal failings.

### Anti-Pattern 6: Letting Trolls Steal Your Energy

The conspiracy machine is designed to exhaust you. Alex Jones. 4chan. Gateway Pundit. Donald Trump Jr. liking smear tweets. It's not random — it's a coordinated entropy strategy.

**The fix:** "Don't feed the trolls." Have one person (like Ryan Deitsch) whose job is to fire back. Everyone else: stay focused on the mission.
