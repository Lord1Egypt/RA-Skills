## PROMPT 4A — FTC-Compliant Review Request Sequence

```
Generate a post-matter review request sequence for a Nevada business litigation attorney. All content must comply with the FTC's 2023 Endorsements and Testimonials Guidelines (no incentivized reviews without disclosure), RPC 1.6 (no client information disclosure), and RPC 7.1. Do not reference case outcomes. Do not offer anything of value for a review.

Attorney inputs:
- Firm name: [FIRM NAME]
- Attorney name: [ATTORNEY NAME], NV Bar No. [BAR NUMBER]
- Google Business Profile link: [GBP LINK]
- Matter type: [LLC dispute / corporate dispute / fiduciary breach / fraudulent transfer / general business]
- Client role: [plaintiff / defendant / neutral (general matter)]

Generate a 3-touch review request sequence:

**Touch 1 — Immediately After Matter Closes (Email)**
Subject: Thank you — [Firm Name]
- Warm, professional close
- Express appreciation for trust placed in the firm
- Invite honest feedback if they found value in the representation
- Clear: no obligation, no incentive, purely voluntary
- Google review link
- Disclosure: "We are not offering anything of value in exchange for a review. Any review you choose to leave should reflect your genuine experience."

**Touch 2 — 7 Days Later (Email, if no review)**
Subject: A small ask — [Firm Name]
- Brief: one honest sentence from a client can help another Nevada business owner find qualified counsel
- Reiterate: no pressure, no incentive
- Google review link
- Optionally: offer a 10-min check-in call instead ("if you have feedback you'd prefer to share directly, I'm happy to take a call")

**Touch 3 — 21 Days Later (Final — Email or Text)**
Subject: Last note from [Firm Name]
- Final brief ask
- Acknowledge: this is the last reach-out on this topic
- "If you know another business owner who might need Nevada business litigation help, I appreciate any introduction."
- Google review link (optional: also include Avvo or Martindale if firm uses those)
- Disclosure repeated

**Opt-out footer on all emails:**
"To stop receiving follow-up emails from [Firm Name], reply STOP or click [unsubscribe link]. [Firm Name] | [Address] | NV Bar No. [Number]"
```

## PROMPT 4C — Referral Letter Templates (5)

```
Generate 5 professional referral letter templates for a Nevada business litigation attorney to send to strategic referral sources. All letters must comply with RPC 7.2 (no payment for referrals), RPC 7.3, and SCR 192. Letters should be educational and relationship-focused, not transactional.

Attorney inputs:
- Firm name: [FIRM NAME]
- Attorney name: [ATTORNEY NAME], NV Bar No. [BAR NUMBER]
- Phone: [PHONE]
- Email: [EMAIL]
- Practice focus: LLC disputes, corporate deadlock, fiduciary breach, fraudulent transfer, Business Court

Generate 5 letters:

**LETTER 1 — CPA / Accountant**
Context: CPAs frequently see the first signs of business disputes (distributions, capital account disagreements, year-end disputes, suspicious transfers)
Key referral triggers to educate them on:
- Client mentions they haven't received distributions they're entitled to → LLC dispute
- Client asks about a transfer made before a lawsuit started → NRS 112 fraudulent transfer
- Client's business partner is taking actions without their approval → NRS 86.286 fiduciary duty breach
- Client mentions a 50/50 deadlock in management → NRS 78.347 corporate deadlock
Nevada-specific hook: "Nevada LLC law is often more favorable to business owners than CPAs expect — particularly around director liability (NRS 78.138(7)) and duty elimination (NRS 86.296)"
CTA: Lunch/coffee meeting; offer CLE credit if available

**LETTER 2 — Business Broker**
Context: Business brokers see disputes arising from business sales, non-compete enforcement, and acquisition representations/warranties
Key referral triggers:
- Buyer and seller dispute post-close (reps and warranties)
- Non-compete enforcement in Nevada (NRS 613.200 limitations)
- Asset vs. stock purchase characterization dispute
- Fraudulent transfer allegation related to a business sale
Nevada-specific hook: "Nevada has some of the least enforceable non-compete agreements in the country under NRS 613.200 — if a client is facing a non-compete dispute, that's often a quick win I can evaluate in one call"
CTA: Referral call protocol; how to introduce a client

**LETTER 3 — Commercial Real Estate Attorney**
Context: Commercial real estate attorneys regularly encounter business disputes that fall outside their practice area
Key referral triggers:
- LLC members dispute over property held in an LLC
- Operating agreement governs real property distribution — member disagreement
- Fraudulent transfer allegation involving real estate transfer
- Corporate deadlock involving a real estate holding company
Nevada-specific hook: "LLC operating agreements that govern Nevada real estate often fail to include NRS 86.296 duty elimination clauses — which creates fiduciary duty exposure between members. That gap is often the source of a dispute I can help resolve."
CTA: Regular case referral cadence; reciprocal introduction opportunities

**LETTER 4 — Bankruptcy Attorney**
Context: Bankruptcy filings frequently generate fraudulent transfer lookback claims; business debtors often have pre-petition business disputes
Key referral triggers:
- Chapter 7 trustee asserts NRS 112 / 11 USC 548 fraudulent transfer claim against non-debtor
- Business owner in bankruptcy faces derivative suit or fiduciary claim in state court
- Creditor wants to pierce corporate veil of debtor's LLC
- Pre-petition business dispute that needs to be resolved during or after bankruptcy
Nevada-specific hook: "Nevada's 4-year SOL under NRS 112 means fraudulent transfer claims from 2021 are still actionable. I see a lot of cases where the bankruptcy estate is undervaluing potential NRS 112 recoveries."
CTA: Joint client approach; referral protocol for state court matters during bankruptcy

**LETTER 5 — Corporate / Transactional Attorney**
Context: Transaction attorneys create the entities and agreements that later produce litigation; they're ideal referral sources when clients return with disputes
Key referral triggers:
- Client returns with a dispute arising from an agreement the transactional attorney drafted
- Client formed a new entity and now faces a partner/member dispute
- Operating agreement needs Nevada-specific amendment (NRS 86.296 duty clause, manager authority)
- Client faces post-closing M&A dispute
Nevada-specific hook: "I find that operating agreements drafted without Nevada-specific counsel often don't include NRS 86.296 duty elimination clauses or clearly define manager vs. member authority under NRS 86.291 — two provisions that would have prevented the dispute entirely."
CTA: Mutual referral protocol; offer to co-present at Nevada State Bar Business Law Section

**All letters:**
- Professional letterhead format (attorney name, bar number, contact)
- 300-400 words
- Educational first, relationship second, referral third
- No payment offer for referrals (RPC 7.2 compliance)
- Include: "I comply with Nevada State Bar advertising rules. This letter is for informational purposes. NV Bar No. [Number]."
```

## PROMPT 4D — Compliance Master Checklist

```
Generate a complete compliance checklist for a Nevada business litigation attorney running digital marketing campaigns using this skill's outputs. Cover all applicable rules and regulations.

**NEVADA STATE BAR RULES**

SCR 192 — Attorney Advertising
[ ] All ads identified as "attorney advertising" or equivalent
[ ] No unjustified claims of superiority ("best," "top," "leading," "most experienced") unless objectively verifiable
[ ] Firm name and at least one attorney's NV Bar number on all ads
[ ] No statements reasonably likely to create unjustified expectations about results
[ ] Required disclosure on all advertising: "Attorney advertising. Results depend on the specific facts of each case."

RPC 7.1 — Communicating About Services
[ ] No false or misleading communications about attorney or services
[ ] Statutory citations are accurate and current (verify NRS against current Nevada statutes)
[ ] No comparisons with other attorneys unless objectively verifiable
[ ] No omissions that make the overall communication misleading

RPC 7.2 — Advertising
[ ] No payment to a non-attorney for recommending the attorney's services
[ ] Any lawyer referral service used is approved by the Nevada State Bar
[ ] All advertising content retained for 4 years (SCR 199 recordkeeping)

RPC 7.3 — Solicitation
[ ] No in-person or live telephone solicitation of prospective clients for pecuniary gain
[ ] No real-time electronic contact solicitation
[ ] Email solicitation includes opt-out mechanism
[ ] No solicitation of accident victims within 30 days of incident (SCR 183)

RPC 1.6 — Confidentiality
[ ] No client information disclosed in marketing materials without written consent
[ ] GBP review responses do not confirm representation or disclose case facts
[ ] Testimonials obtained with written client consent (RPC 1.6(a) exception or waiver)

**FTC GUIDELINES (2023)**

[ ] Reviews are not incentivized without clear, prominent disclosure
[ ] Any paid or incentivized review identifies that it was incentivized
[ ] No review gating (filtering out negative reviews before asking)
[ ] Endorsements reflect honest opinions; no suppression of negative feedback
[ ] Any material connection between endorser and attorney is disclosed

**GOOGLE ADS POLICIES**

[ ] Legal services ads comply with Google's Healthcare and Medicines policy
[ ] No misleading ad copy (outcomes, guarantees, comparison)
[ ] Landing pages match ad content
[ ] Legal disclaimer present on landing pages

**META ADVERTISING POLICIES**

[ ] Legal services do not trigger Meta's Special Ad Category (credit, employment, housing) — business litigation is generally exempt
[ ] No sensitive targeting on protected characteristics
[ ] Lead ads comply with Meta's data use policy
[ ] Privacy notice accessible from lead form

**GOOGLE LOCAL SERVICES ADS**

[ ] Attorney verification completed (NV Bar license number verified by Google)
[ ] Google Guarantee / Google Screened badge requirements met (if applicable)
[ ] Service area is accurate (Clark County / Nevada)
[ ] Review management complies with FTC 2023 and Google review policies

**ONGOING COMPLIANCE**

[ ] Statutory citations reviewed annually against current NRS
[ ] Advertising content reviewed when Nevada State Bar issues new ethics opinions
[ ] CLE credit sought for legal marketing ethics (Nevada offers periodic programs)
[ ] File of all advertising maintained for 4 years (SCR 199)
[ ] Retainer agreements updated to reflect current advertising language if referenced

**NRS CITATION ACCURACY VERIFICATION**

Verify these citations before publishing any content from this skill:
- NRS 78.138(7) — Business Judgment Rule (director/officer liability standard)
- NRS 86.286 — LLC manager fiduciary duties
- NRS 86.296 — Operating agreement authority to modify/eliminate duties
- NRS 78.747 — Codified alter ego test
- NRS 112.190 — Badges of fraud (UVTA)
- NRS 112.250 — Statute of limitations (fraudulent transfer)
- NRS 78.347 — Corporate deadlock / dissolution
- Clark County Business Court (Dept. 32) — Verify current threshold and procedures at clarkcountycourts.us

Last statutory verification: [DATE — attorney to complete]
Verified by: [ATTORNEY NAME], NV Bar No. [NUMBER]
```
