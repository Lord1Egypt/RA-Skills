## Description: <br>
Methods for teaching physical and embodied skills to others, including hands-on skills such as riding a bike, swimming, cooking, driving, using tools, or any skill that requires physical demonstration. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users, educators, parents, coaches, and instructors use this skill to plan and explain how to teach physical skills through demonstration, practice, feedback, readiness checks, and learner-specific adaptation. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Video recording during coaching can create privacy risk for learners and bystanders. <br>
Mitigation: Get consent from the learner or a parent or guardian, keep recordings local and temporary, avoid unnecessary capture of faces or surroundings, and do not upload or share recordings without explicit permission. <br>
Risk: Higher-risk activities such as swimming, driving, knives, and power tools can cause physical injury if taught without appropriate safeguards. <br>
Mitigation: Use proper supervision, safety equipment, local rules, and professional instruction when the teacher is not confident they can keep the learner safe. <br>
Risk: Pushing a frightened or frustrated learner too quickly can create avoidance or unsafe behavior. <br>
Mitigation: Reduce stakes, give the learner control over progression, use one correction at a time, and return to an easier step when readiness checks are not met. <br>
