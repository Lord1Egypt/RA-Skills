# Prompt 1 — Signature Offer Framework (FREE)

**Purpose:** Build the core positioning document for your coaching or consulting business — the foundation every other asset is built on.

---

## Instructions

Copy the prompt below into Claude. Fill in the `[BRACKETED]` fields with your specific information. Run and iterate until the positioning feels unmistakably yours.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a positioning strategist specializing in coaching and consulting businesses. Build a complete Signature Offer Framework for this coach/consultant.

**COACH PROFILE:**
- Name and title: [e.g., Sarah Kim, Executive Leadership Coach]
- Background and credentials: [e.g., 12 years as Fortune 500 HR director, ICF-certified coach, MBA Wharton]
- Niche and ideal client: [e.g., mid-level managers (Director to VP) at tech companies who want to reach the C-suite within 3 years]
- The problem you solve: [e.g., Talented managers stall because they keep doing individual contributor work instead of leading. They get overlooked for promotions despite strong performance.]
- The transformation you deliver: [e.g., From "best worker in the room" to "the leader everyone wants in the room" — promotion, raise, and recognition within 12 months]
- Your unique approach or insight: [e.g., I use a 3-phase Executive Presence Framework built from observing 200+ C-suite leaders at companies like Microsoft, Google, and Salesforce]
- Proof points you have: [e.g., 47 clients promoted, average salary increase $28K, clients at Amazon, Meta, and Deloitte]
- Price range you're considering: [e.g., $3,000-$5,000 for 3-month 1:1, $997 for group cohort, $297 for self-study]

**OUTPUT FORMAT:**

Produce a complete Signature Offer Framework with these exact sections:

---

### 1. Methodology Name
Create 3 options for a proprietary methodology name that:
- Contains 2-4 words
- Uses an alliterative phrase, acronym, or metaphor
- Sounds credible and specific (not generic)
- Can be trademarked
- Example formats: "The [Outcome] Formula," "[Letter] Framework," "The [Metaphor] Method," "[Initials] System"

For each option, explain WHY it works and who it appeals to.

---

### 2. Outcome Promise (The Big Claim)
Write 3 versions of your outcome promise in this format:
"I help [SPECIFIC CLIENT] achieve [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] in [TIMEFRAME] without [COMMON OBJECTION/OBSTACLE]."

Make each version:
- Concrete (quantified where possible)
- Credible (achievable, not hype)
- Differentiated (different from generic "I help people reach their potential")

---

### 3. Ideal Client Profile
Write a one-paragraph ICP description covering:
- Demographics (role, industry, years of experience, income, geography)
- Psychographics (what they believe, what they fear, what they've tried before)
- Buying trigger (what just happened that makes them ready to hire a coach NOW)
- Red flags (who is NOT a good fit)

---

### 4. Program Architecture
Design 3 tiers of the signature offer:

**Tier 1 — 1:1 Intensive (Premium)**
- Duration: [weeks/months]
- Deliverables: [sessions, materials, support channels]
- Who it's for
- Price anchor

**Tier 2 — Group Cohort (Core)**
- Duration, cohort size, format
- Deliverables
- Who it's for
- Price anchor

**Tier 3 — Self-Study / Digital (Entry)**
- Format (course, templates, community)
- Deliverables
- Who it's for
- Price anchor

---

### 5. The 3-Phase Transformation Map
Map the client journey through exactly 3 phases (name each phase, describe what changes, what deliverables belong in each):

**Phase 1 — [Name: Foundation/Diagnostic/Assessment]**
- What the client discovers/shifts
- Key deliverable or milestone

**Phase 2 — [Name: Build/Implement/Practice]**
- What the client creates/changes
- Key deliverable or milestone

**Phase 3 — [Name: Perform/Sustain/Scale]**
- The integrated result
- Key deliverable or proof of transformation

---

### 6. Positioning Statement (Website + Sales Page Hero)
Write a 3-sentence positioning statement for the website hero section:
- Sentence 1: Who you help and what they want
- Sentence 2: Why they've been stuck (the real problem)
- Sentence 3: What you do differently and the outcome

Also write a 1-sentence LinkedIn headline version (under 220 characters).

---

### 7. Offer Naming Guide
Provide 5 rules for naming the program itself (not the methodology) so it:
- Sounds like a destination, not a process
- Includes an outcome or identity shift
- Can be said aloud without sounding strange
- Examples: "The VP Accelerator," "The Executive Edge Intensive," "The C-Suite Launchpad"

Give 3 name options specific to this coach's offer.

---

### 8. Quick-Start Checklist
List the 10 things this coach should do in the first 30 days to launch, in priority order.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific in the Coach Profile.** Vague inputs produce generic positioning. The more specific your background and proof points, the more differentiated the output.
- **Run Prompt 1 before any other prompt.** Every subsequent output depends on the positioning established here.
- **Test your methodology name** by saying it out loud to a potential client. If they ask "What does that mean?" — it's working. Curiosity = sales conversations.
- **The 3-Phase Map** becomes your curriculum, your sales conversation structure, and your content calendar pillar topics.
