## The Speed Reading Toolbox

### The 3 Passive Habits and How to Fix Them

| Habit | Active Version | How to Reduce |
|---|---|---|
| **Mind Wandering** (daydreaming) | Active: connecting new info to existing knowledge (brain glue) | Read faster (fills the 250 wpm gap), preview first |
| **Regression** (re-reading words/lines) | Active: intentional going back for a specific purpose | White card above the line; trust your brain; read faster |
| **Subvocalization** (mental talking) | Active: reading poetry/dialogue/legal text word-for-word | 10 strategies (see below), most effective = read faster |

### 10 Ways to Reduce Subvocalization

1. **Catch yourself** — Awareness is the first step
2. **Read faster** — The single best strategy
3. **Read key words** — Skip structural words (the, a, of, and)
4. **Use a pacer** — Any movement-based tool
5. **Hush it** — Press index finger to lips
6. **Mumble** — Say "1-2-3" or "la-la-la" while reading silently
7. **Hum** — Hum a tune while reading
8. **Chew gum** — 3+ pieces, rhythmic chewing
9. **Toothpaste on lips** — Taste reminder if you move lips
10. **Silence your tongue** — Press tongue to roof of mouth

### The Pacer Progression

| Pacer | How to Use | When |
|---|---|---|
| White Card | Place ABOVE the line. Move down at steady pace. Blocks regression. | Day 1 (start here) |
| Hand | Run index and middle finger down the page margin. | Day 2-3 |
| Pen | Point at text, move slightly faster than comfortable. | Day 3-4 |
| The "V" | Move hand in V pattern down the page, reading 2-3 lines per sweep. | Day 4 |

**Pacer Rules:**
1. Keep moving DOWN, not across
2. Do not stop or go back
3. Move slightly faster than comfortable

### Previewing Protocol (Day 5)

Spend 2-3 minutes before reading:
1. Read the **title and subtitle** — what's the book about?
2. Read the **table of contents** — what are the major sections?
3. Read the **introduction** — what's the author's promise?
4. Read **headings and subheadings** in the chapter
5. Read the **first paragraph** of each section
6. Read **any** summaries, charts, or callouts
7. Ask: "What do I want from this reading?" (purpose setting)

### Skimming vs Scanning

- **Skimming:** Looking for the gist. 70-80% comprehension. Read headings, first sentences, key words. For: newspapers, magazines, familiar topics.
- **Scanning:** Looking for specific information. Skip everything until you find what you need. For: reference, research, finding a specific fact.

### The Eye Span Pyramid Training

Train peripheral vision to see more per eye stop:
1. Focus on center of a line
2. Notice words on left and right without moving eyes
3. Gradually expand the number of words per stop
4. Use the Eye Span Pyramid exercise (numbers in center, see both sides)
5. Goal: from 1-2 words per stop to 5-7

### How to Read Nonfiction Efficiently

1. **Preview first** — 2-3 minutes
2. **Set a purpose** — "I want to learn X from this chapter"
3. **Read key sections** — Not every page of every chapter is equally valuable
4. **Summarize as you go** — Mental checkpoint after each section
5. **Avoid passive regression** — Don't go back unless it matters
6. **Vary your speed** — Dense parts slow, familiar parts fast, examples medium

> **Case: The emergency croup reading** (Introduction): When Marks Beale's infant son had croup in the middle of the night, she didn't have time to read carefully. She scanned parenting book indexes, skimmed text, and applied the advice within minutes. Her faster reading skills directly helped her son breathe better before the doctor called back. **Key takeaway:** Speed-reading isn't just for productivity — it's a real-world survival skill for overwhelmed parents.

> **Case: The Reading Tracker Inventory** (Day 1): Marks Beale recommends tracking every publication you receive for one month, rating each for value, and canceling anything with 5+ unread back issues. Most participants discover they're subscribing to 3-5 publications they never read. **Key takeaway:** The fastest way to read more is to read less — eliminate the noise, focus on the signal.

### Online Reading Tips (Day 8)

- Reduce screen glare and adjust font size
- Use browser reader mode to remove clutter
- Preview the page before scrolling — assess length and structure
- Use the "find" function (Ctrl+F) for specific terms instead of reading everything
- Set a time limit for email processing — batch and move on
