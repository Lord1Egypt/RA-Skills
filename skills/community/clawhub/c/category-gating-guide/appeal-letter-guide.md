# Appeal Letter Guide

Templates and guidance for writing effective ungating appeal letters when an initial category application is rejected by Amazon.

---

## Appeal Letter Principles

Every successful appeal letter follows these principles:

1. **Acknowledge the rejection reason explicitly.** Restate the specific reason Amazon gave. This demonstrates you read and understood their feedback.
2. **Take responsibility without excuses.** Do not blame Amazon, the system, or circumstances. Simply state what went wrong and what you did to fix it.
3. **Describe corrective actions with specifics.** Vague promises to "do better" are ignored. Name the exact documents you corrected, photos you retook, or suppliers you contacted.
4. **List attached documentation clearly.** The reviewer should be able to cross-reference your letter against the uploaded files without guessing.
5. **Keep it concise.** Three to five paragraphs maximum. Reviewers process hundreds of appeals. Shorter letters with clear structure get better results.
6. **Use professional, neutral tone.** No frustration, urgency, or emotional language. Write as though you are submitting a business report.

---

## Appeal Letter Structure

Every appeal letter should contain five elements in this order:

1. **Subject line**: `[Category] Category Application Appeal -- [Your Legal Business Name]`
2. **Identification**: State your legal business name and the application date.
3. **Acknowledgment**: Restate the exact rejection reason Amazon provided.
4. **Corrective actions**: Numbered list of specific changes made (not vague promises).
5. **Documentation list**: Name every attached file so the reviewer can cross-reference.

Keep the letter to 3-5 short paragraphs. Close with a single professional sentence.

## Templates by Rejection Reason

### Template A: Invoice Name Mismatch

Use when the rejection states the invoice name does not match the seller account.

> Dear Amazon Seller Performance Team,
>
> I am writing to appeal the rejection of my [Category] category application. I understand the invoice previously submitted displayed an incorrect business name that did not match my Seller Central account registration.
>
> The original invoice was issued to "[incorrect name]" rather than my registered legal entity name "[correct legal name]." I have contacted my supplier, [Supplier Name], and obtained a corrected invoice issued to "[correct legal name]," which matches my Seller Central registration exactly.
>
> Corrective actions taken:
> 1. Requested and received a revised invoice from [Supplier Name] with the correct legal entity name.
> 2. Verified that all other invoice details (product descriptions, quantities, dates) remain accurate.
> 3. Confirmed with [Supplier Name] that they are available to verify the business relationship if contacted by Amazon.
>
> Attached documentation:
> - Revised invoice: [filename.pdf]
> - [Any additional supporting documents]
>
> Thank you for reviewing my corrected application.
>
> [Your Legal Business Name]

### Template B: Missing Safety or Compliance Documentation

Use when the rejection cites missing COA, CPSC certificate, or other regulatory documents.

> Dear Amazon Seller Performance Team,
>
> I am writing to appeal the rejection of my [Category] category application. I understand that required safety/compliance documentation was missing from my original submission.
>
> I have obtained the necessary [document type -- e.g., "Certificate of Analysis," "Children's Product Certificate," "CPSIA test report"] to demonstrate product compliance.
>
> Corrective actions taken:
> 1. Obtained a [document type] from [Lab/Certifying Body Name], an [accreditation -- e.g., "ISO 17025 accredited" or "CPSC-accepted"] testing facility.
> 2. The report (reference number: [report ID]) specifically covers the product identified by ASIN [ASIN] / UPC [UPC] and tests for [relevant parameters -- e.g., "heavy metals, microbial contamination" or "lead content, phthalates, ASTM F963 compliance"].
> 3. Report is dated [date] and valid for [duration if applicable].
>
> Attached documentation:
> - [Document type]: [filename.pdf]
> - Supplier invoice: [filename.pdf]
> - Product photographs: [count] images per ASIN
>
> Thank you for your consideration.
>
> [Your Legal Business Name]

## After Submitting the Appeal

- **Wait for a response.** Manual reviews take 1-7 business days. Do not resubmit during this period.
- **Monitor Seller Central notifications.** The response will appear in your Performance Notifications.
- **If rejected again**, carefully read the new rejection reason. It may differ from the first. Repeat the diagnosis and correction cycle.
- **Maximum attempts**: There is no hard limit on appeals, but repeated rejections for the same reason signal a fundamental issue with your supplier, documentation, or account health that must be resolved before further attempts.
- **Escalation path**: If you believe a rejection is in error after multiple well-documented appeals, open a case through Seller Support referencing your appeal history. Include case IDs from previous submissions.
