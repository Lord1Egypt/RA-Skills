# Phase I ESA Drafter

**Platforms:** Claude · Openclaw · Codex
**Domain:** Environmental Consulting

## Purpose

Drafts an ASTM E1527-21 Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA) report for a single subject property. Covers scoping, user / purchaser responsibilities, records review, site reconnaissance, interviews, historical-use research, REC / CREC / HREC / de minimis classification, significant data gap discussion, the photo log requirement, the 180-day component-date convention, and the Environmental Professional (EP) opinion and conclusions section.

**The output is always DRAFT.** A qualified Environmental Professional must verify every finding, citation, opinion, and conclusion, and physically sign and seal the report before it is delivered to a client or relied on for CERCLA Landowner Liability Protection (LLP) under the EPA AAI Rule.

## When to Use

- Pre-acquisition due diligence on commercial, industrial, agricultural, or undeveloped real estate
- Refinance, recapitalization, or SBA / USDA loan due diligence requiring an ASTM-compliant Phase I
- Brownfields-program eligibility documentation
- Innocent Landowner / Bona Fide Prospective Purchaser / Contiguous Property Owner defense documentation
- EP-supervised drafting by a junior consultant, intern, or report-production team
- Lender or counsel review of a third-party Phase I against the E1527-21 standard

## What It Does

**Phase 1: Scoping**
1. Captures user (purchaser / client) information, project-specific objectives, and user-responsibility responses (specialized knowledge, environmental liens, AULs, valuation discrepancy, commonly known information, reason for ESA)
2. Defines the subject property (address, parcel ID, acreage, current and intended use, structures) and confirms whether non-scope items (asbestos, lead-based paint, lead in drinking water, mold, radon, wetlands, PFAS, vapor intrusion) are in or out of scope

**Phase 2: Records Review**
3. Logs federal, state, tribal, and local regulatory database results within minimum ASTM search distances per source type
4. Logs historical-use sources (aerials, topographic maps, Sanborn fire insurance maps, city directories, recorded land title, prior ESA reports, building department records)
5. Tracks the completion date of every component so the 180-day and 1-year shelf-life rules can be applied
6. Identifies and explicitly discusses any significant data gaps per the E1527-21 definition

**Phase 3: Site Reconnaissance and Interviews**
7. Captures site observations across interior, exterior, and adjoining-property categories (ASTs, USTs, drums, stained soil, hydraulic equipment, transformers, sumps, floor drains, vent pipes, lab/medical waste, dry-cleaning, vapor concerns)
8. Captures interview content (current and past owners and occupants, key site manager, local government officials where applicable, and the User)
9. Builds a photo log with REC location captions, site features, and property-boundary references

**Phase 4: Findings, Opinions, Conclusions**
10. Classifies every identified condition as REC, Controlled REC (CREC), Historical REC (HREC), de minimis, or business environmental risk (BER, non-scope)
11. Writes the Environmental Professional opinion connecting each REC to the supporting findings
12. Drafts the conclusions section using one of the four allowed ASTM conclusion forms
13. Drafts the EP qualification and signature block, the deviations / limitations / exceptions section, and a deliverable cover page

## Output

A DRAFT E1527-21 Phase I ESA report with: cover page, executive summary, table of contents, sections 1–13 in ASTM order, REC / CREC / HREC / de minimis findings table, significant data gap discussion, photo log (with REC and feature captions), records-review evidence tables, interview-record summaries, EP-opinion section, conclusions section using one of the four allowed forms, signature blocks (unsigned), and an appendices index — labeled `DRAFT — for Environmental Professional review and signature`.

## Notes

This skill never determines AAI compliance, never opines on Phase II scope or contamination concentrations, and never issues a final, signed Phase I ESA. Only a qualified EP can sign the report. The skill also flags the 180-day component-date shelf life and the 1-year viability rule and warns when any component is approaching either limit.

It treats every property record, photo, and interview note as confidential client work product. It does not paste subject-property identifiers, owner names, or specific transaction details into examples or external lookups.
