# Quality Checklist

Use this before publishing or handing off final content.

## Evidence

- Every concrete performance claim must map to a supplied proof point.
- If evidence is missing, label it as `待补充证据`.
- Do not invent test data, rankings, endorsements, prices, discounts, or inventory.

## Specificity

- Name the audience and situation.
- Replace generic praise with observable product attributes.
- Include at least one tradeoff when writing reviews or recommendation copy.

## Platform Fit

- Xiaohongshu: personal and scannable.
- Douyin: spoken and visual.
- Live selling: demo flow plus objection handling.
- Moments: short and natural.
- Zhihu: analytical and balanced.
- Marketplace: searchable and structured.
- Ads: one angle per variant.

## Claim Safety

Treat these as high risk unless backed by reliable evidence:

- Medical or body-effect claims: cure, heal, anti-inflammatory, pain relief, weight loss.
- Absolute claims: best, number one, guaranteed, 100%, permanent, zero risk.
- Official claims: certified, authorized, exclusive, patented, clinical.
- Scarcity claims: last chance, only today, final stock.
- Financial/legal claims: compliant, risk-free, guaranteed return.

## Final Pass

Before final output, include:

- assumptions made
- missing evidence
- risky claim edits
- one next experiment or A/B test idea
