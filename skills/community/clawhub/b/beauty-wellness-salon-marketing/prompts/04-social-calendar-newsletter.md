# Prompt 4: 30-Day Social Calendar + Email Newsletter

## Purpose
Generate a complete month of content: 30-day Instagram/Facebook calendar, monthly client newsletter, reusable templates, Reel/TikTok scripts, and Google Business Profile posts.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a beauty and wellness social media strategist. Generate a complete 30-day content system for the business below.

BUSINESS DETAILS:
- Business name: [NAME]
- Business type: [hair salon / nail studio / med spa / massage / esthetician / day spa]
- Location: [CITY, STATE — include neighborhood if known]
- Primary platform: [Instagram / Facebook / both]
- Top 3 signature services: [LIST]
- Team size: [solo / 2-5 / 6-10 / 10+]
- Current posting frequency: [how often they currently post]
- Any upcoming promotions or events: [e.g., "Mother's Day special running May 5-12"]
- Brand tone: [e.g., "warm and educational," "luxury and aspirational," "fun and approachable," "clean and clinical"]

GENERATE THE FOLLOWING:

1. 30-DAY INSTAGRAM / FACEBOOK CONTENT CALENDAR
   Format as a table: Day | Content Type | Caption | Notes

   Post 4 times per week following this content mix:
   - 40% Education (tips, tutorials, ingredient spotlights, debunk myths)
   - 30% Transformation / Results (before/after with FTC disclosures, client stories with permission note)
   - 20% Behind-the-scenes / Team (day in the life, meet the team, tools, process)
   - 10% Promotional (offers, seasonal, events — never two promo posts back-to-back)

   For each post include:
   - Content type label
   - Full caption (100-200 words)
   - Hashtag line (15 tags per post)
   - Visual direction note (what to photograph/film)
   - Best time to post (based on beauty industry benchmarks)

   Milestone posts to include:
   - Week 1: Introduce team or solo owner (trust-building)
   - Week 2: Education deep-dive (most shared content type in beauty)
   - Week 3: Client transformation (peak engagement week)
   - Week 4: Upcoming promotion or seasonal hook

2. MONTHLY CLIENT EMAIL NEWSLETTER (800-1,000 WORDS)
   Sections:
   a) Subject line (under 50 characters) + 2 A/B variants
   b) Preview text (under 90 characters)
   c) Opening: personal note from owner (150 words) — what happened at the salon this month, a client win (anonymized), a personal reflection
   d) Featured service of the month (200 words) — education-first, not sales-first
   e) Team spotlight (100 words) — feature one team member with a fun fact
   f) Beauty tip of the month (150 words) — actionable, home care focus
   g) This month's special offer (100 words) — one clear offer, deadline, CTA
   h) Rebooking reminder footer (50 words) — "Spots fill fast in [month]"
   i) Subject line for next month's newsletter (teaser)

3. FOUR REUSABLE CONTENT TEMPLATES
   Templates that can be reused every month with minor edits:

   Template A — "Transformation Tuesday":
   - Caption formula: [service] before/after → timeline → client's words (quoted) → what made this transformation possible → CTA
   - FTC disclosure line (embed naturally, not as footnote)
   - 15 hashtags optimized for transformation/beauty content

   Template B — "[Day] Tips & Education":
   - Caption formula: Hook question → myth vs. fact → 3 actionable tips numbered → expertise signal → soft CTA
   - 15 hashtags optimized for educational content

   Template C — "Team Spotlight [Day]":
   - Caption formula: Name + title → specialty → fun fact + why they love their work → how to book with them → tag the team member
   - 15 hashtags for team/culture content

   Template D — "Client Love [Day]":
   - Caption formula: Quote the review (with permission note) → service received → what the result meant to the client → invitation to be next → review link CTA
   - 15 hashtags for testimonial/review content

4. THREE INSTAGRAM REEL / TIKTOK SCRIPTS
   For each script include:
   - Title / hook (first 3 seconds — the scroll-stopper)
   - Shot list (numbered, with B-roll and talking head notes)
   - Voiceover or on-camera script
   - Text overlay suggestions
   - Audio recommendation (trending sound category or specific style)
   - Caption (150 words) + 20 hashtags

   Reel 1 — Transformation Reveal (most viral format in beauty)
   Structure: dramatic "before" close-up → process shots → slow "after" reveal → client reaction → CTA overlay

   Reel 2 — "Day in My Life" (trust-building, POV format)
   Structure: morning routine → first client → behind chair moments → team interaction → end of day → "come see us" CTA

   Reel 3 — Tutorial / Educational (most saved content type)
   Structure: hook ("Stop doing this with your [hair/skin/nails]") → 3 tips numbered → result demonstration → CTA to book for professional version

5. FOUR GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE POSTS
   For each: post type (What's New / Event / Offer) + copy (150-300 words) + CTA button + local SEO keywords embedded

   Post 1 — Service spotlight (What's New)
   Post 2 — Seasonal offer (Offer — include expiration date, required for GBP offer posts)
   Post 3 — Team/credibility (What's New)
   Post 4 — Community or local angle (What's New — mention neighborhood, local events, or community connection)
```

## The 80/20 Rule for Beauty Content

80% of content should educate, inspire, or entertain. Only 20% should directly sell. Salons that post only "book now" and promotional content average 1.2% engagement. Salons that follow the 80/20 split average 4.8% engagement on the same platform.

Education content (tips, tutorials, myth-busting) gets 3x more saves than promotional content. Saves signal to the algorithm that content is high-value — driving organic reach.

## Hashtag Strategy

Beauty industry benchmark (Instagram):
- 10 high-volume (1M+): #haircolor #nails #skintok #beauty #selfcare
- 10 medium-volume (100K-1M): #[servicetype] #[citybeauty] #[salonlife]
- 10 local/niche (<100K): #[cityname]salon #[neighborhood]beauty #[specialtyservice]

Never repeat the same 30 hashtags on every post — rotate sets to avoid "shadowbanning."
