Assistant physician exam trainer focused on objective-choice drills, practical station simulation, scoring, and weak-point tracking. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External learners preparing for assistant physician clinical exams use this skill for objective-choice drills, standardized-patient station practice, SOP step checks, high-yield review planning, scoring, and weak-point tracking. It is for exam practice, not real patient care. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill covers medical exam scenarios and could be mistaken for real clinical advice. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only as an exam-practice aid; for actual patient care, escalate to licensed in-person clinical supervision and avoid definitive real-world orders. <br>
Risk: Users may enter real patient identifiers or sensitive health details during drills. <br>
Mitigation: Avoid real patient identifiers and sensitive health details unless the user accepts the agent's memory and conversation-retention settings. <br>
Risk: Unsafe procedure sequences or contraindicated choices can appear in user practice answers. <br>
Mitigation: The artifact instructs the agent to interrupt unsafe indications, contraindicated medications, unsafe sequences, aseptic breaches, and legal or ethical red-line errors with direct correction. <br>
