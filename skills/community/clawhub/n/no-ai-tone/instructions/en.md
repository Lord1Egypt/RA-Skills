# English: Avoid AI-Generated Tone

> Research sources: Reddit, GitHub gists, Wikipedia "Signs of AI writing", Medium, Substack, academic studies (Reuters Institute, Max Planck Institute), community word blacklists.

The core problem: AI writing isn't bad because of individual words — it's bad because of its **predictable rhythm, hedged stance, and averaged vocabulary**. Every word choice is statistically safe. Every sentence is complete. Every argument covers all sides. That's exactly what makes it feel inhuman.

---

## 1. Vocabulary Blacklist

### 1.1 The Hall of Shame (highest-frequency AI signals)

Words that spiked 50%+ in published writing after ChatGPT's release (Max Planck Institute, 2024). Seeing one is a yellow flag; seeing three in a paragraph is a red card.

**Verbs**
- delve, delve into ← the single most-identified AI verb
- leverage, harness, unlock, unleash
- underscore, highlight, emphasize, showcase
- foster, cultivate, bolster, garner
- streamline, optimize, enhance, augment
- spearhead, champion, revolutionize, redefine
- navigate, embark (on), unveil, uncover
- utilize (just say "use")
- double down, circle back, deep dive (as verb: "let's deep dive into")
- disrupt (when used to mean "improve slightly")

**Adjectives**
- pivotal, crucial, vital, key (overused emphasis words)
- robust, seamless, cutting-edge, groundbreaking
- meticulous, comprehensive, holistic, nuanced
- transformative, innovative, dynamic, vibrant
- unparalleled, invaluable, commendable, exemplary
- intricate, multifaceted, ever-evolving
- actionable (attach the word to a real action instead)
- game-changing, disruptive, first-of-its-kind

**Nouns**
- landscape (used for any field or domain)
- tapestry (used for anything interconnected)
- realm (used instead of "area" or "field")
- synergy, paradigm, ecosystem
- testament ("a testament to...")
- intricacies, underpinnings, interplay
- journey (overused as a metaphor)
- treasure trove, beacon, crucible
- thought leadership / thought leader
- deep dive (as noun: "let's do a deep dive")
- learnings (incorrect plural; use "lessons" or "takeaways")
- pain points (now so overused it's meaningless; name the specific problem)
- best practices (replace with the actual practice)
- space (as metaphor for "industry" or "area": "the SaaS space")

**Adverbs that add nothing**
- meticulously, seamlessly, effortlessly
- deeply, fundamentally, quietly (as magic intensifiers)
- importantly, notably, crucially, ultimately

### 1.2 Transition Phrases (the flow-killers)

These don't just sound AI-generated — they actively make writing worse by pretending to add logic that isn't there.

**Delete entirely:**
- In conclusion / To sum up / In summary → competent writing doesn't need to announce its ending
- It's worth noting that / It bears mentioning / Importantly / Notably → filler; just say the thing
- That being said / Having said that → pseudo-balance that means nothing
- Furthermore / Moreover / Additionally → overused to the point of parody
- It goes without saying → then don't say it

**Use at most once per piece:**
- However / Nevertheless / Nonetheless
- Therefore / Thus / Consequently / Hence
- Firstly / Secondly / Finally → signals a list-in-prose disguise

### 1.3 Sentence-Level Clichés

Patterns so overused they're instantly recognized:

```
❌ "It's not about X — it's about Y."        ← #1 most-identified AI tell
❌ "In a world where..."                      ← every AI essay opener
❌ "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."                   ← dramatic countdown formula
❌ "The X? A Y."                             ← self-posed rhetorical question
❌ "Let's dive into..."                      ← with or without "delve"
❌ "Let's unpack / Let's explore / Let's break this down"  ← teacher-student condescension
❌ "Think of it as..."                       ← patronizing analogy
❌ "Have you ever wondered why..."
❌ "Everyone wants to..."
❌ "Despite its challenges..."               ← rigid acknowledge-dismiss formula
❌ "Here's the thing:" / "Here's the kicker:"  ← fake suspense for mundane observations
❌ "Imagine a world where..."
❌ Title with a colon: "X: A Guide to Y"    ← colon titles signal fluffy AI content
❌ "The truth is..." / "The reality is..."  ← implies previous statements were false; rarely true
❌ "It's no secret that..."                 ← condescending; just state the fact
❌ "At the end of the day..."               ← forced conclusion cliché
❌ "When it comes to X..."                  ← filler opener; delete and start with X
❌ "Without further ado..."                 ← ado about nothing
❌ "5 reasons why..." / "10 ways to..."     ← listicle addiction; signals content-mill thinking
❌ "I don't know about you, but..."         ← fake personal voice seeking false solidarity
```

### 1.4 Promotional Language (marketing copy specific)

```
boasts, nestled, vibrant, rich, profound, in the heart of, renowned,
featuring, diverse array, natural beauty, commitment to excellence,
second to none, world-class, best-in-class
```

### 1.5 Email and Outreach Clichés

AI-written emails and newsletters have their own dialect. These phrases appear in almost every AI-generated professional email:

```
❌ "I hope this email finds you well."       ← #1 AI email opener; delete entirely
❌ "I hope this message finds you well."
❌ "I wanted to reach out to..."             ← just reach out; don't announce that you're doing it
❌ "I wanted to circle back on..."           ← say "following up on"
❌ "Please don't hesitate to reach out."    ← nobody hesitates; just say "let me know"
❌ "As per my previous email..."            ← passive-aggressive and AI-coded
❌ "I look forward to connecting."          ← hollow; say what you're actually looking forward to
❌ "Thank you for your time and consideration."  ← end the email, don't narrate ending it
❌ "Best regards" / "Kind regards" (when the tone is casual)
❌ "I trust this finds you in good health."
❌ "Kindly" (as in "Kindly find attached")  ← archaic; say "attached is"
```

## 2. Sentence Structure Problems

### 2.1 The Rule of Three (tricolon addiction)

AI loves structures of three: "fast, efficient, and user-friendly." One tricolon is elegant. Three in a paragraph is algorithmic.

→ Break the pattern. Use two items or four. Or just make one precise claim instead of three vague ones.

### 2.2 The "Serves As" Dodge

AI avoids "is/are" with pompous replacements:

```
❌ "This serves as a reminder..."   →  ✓ "This reminds us..."
❌ "X stands as a testament to Y"  →  ✓ "X shows Y"
❌ "This marks a shift in..."       →  ✓ "This changed..."
❌ "X represents a commitment to Y" →  ✓ "X commits to Y" / just describe what X does
```

### 2.3 Negative Parallelism

```
❌ "It's not about working harder — it's about working smarter."
❌ "Not a tool. Not a platform. A partner."
```

These create false profundity. The reframe implies contrast but usually just restates the same thing differently.

### 2.4 Uniform Sentence Length

AI sentences average ~27 words consistently. Human writing varies wildly.

→ After a long sentence, write a short one. One word works. Use fragments deliberately. Vary.

### 2.5 Present Participle Tailing

AI appends "-ing" phrases to add shallow meaning:

```
❌ "The team launched the product, showcasing their commitment to innovation."
❌ "Revenue grew 40%, reflecting strong market demand."
```

The participial clause rarely adds information — it just explains what you just said, in slightly different words.

## 3. Tone Problems

### 3.1 Excessive Hedging

AI is terrified of being wrong. The result is writing that commits to nothing:

```
❌ "It is likely that..." / "There is a possibility that..."
❌ "This may/might/could potentially..."
❌ "In some cases..." / "It depends on the context..."
❌ "Experts suggest..." / "Industry reports indicate..." (without naming them)
❌ "While there are valid points on both sides..."
❌ "It's important to consider multiple perspectives..."
```

**Statistical hedging** is subtler but just as common — imprecise quantities that feel authoritative without saying anything:

```
❌ "tends to" / "generally" / "in most cases"  ← how often? what cases?
❌ "many people" / "most users" / "some experts" ← how many? which experts?
❌ "research suggests" / "data shows"           ← which research? what data?
❌ "increasingly" / "growing number of"         ← compared to what baseline?
```

→ Take a position. Say what you actually think. If you don't know, say that specifically — name the uncertainty rather than disguising it as qualified truth.

### 3.2 No Voice, No Stance

AI writing reads like it was produced by a committee afraid of liability. It never:
- Disagrees with anything
- Admits genuine confusion
- Makes a surprising claim
- Says "I was wrong about X"
- Prefers one option over another

→ Human writing has opinions. It uses first person. It chooses sides.

### 3.3 Grandiose Stakes Inflation

Every AI argument gets inflated to world-historical importance:

```
❌ "This represents a fundamental shift in how humanity approaches..."
❌ "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
❌ "At a time when the world faces unprecedented challenges..."
```

→ Match your stakes to your actual claim. Most things are not paradigm shifts.

### 3.4 Performative Vulnerability

AI simulates self-awareness in ways that read as hollow:

```
❌ "I'll be honest with you..."
❌ "Here's what most people get wrong..."
❌ "I used to think X, but then I realized..."
```

Real vulnerability is specific and uncomfortable. AI "vulnerability" is a rhetorical device.

## 4. Formatting Problems

### 4.1 The "Bold-First Bullet" Pattern

```
❌ • **Key insight:** Description of insight.
❌ • **Important consideration:** More text here.
```

Almost no human formats lists this way when writing by hand. It's a pure AI formatting tell.

→ Either use plain bullets, or write it as prose.

### 4.2 Em-Dash Addiction

Compulsive overuse of em dashes for dramatic pauses — like this — in every sentence. ChatGPT 4.0 was particularly bad for this.

→ One or two per piece is fine. More than five is a tell.

### 4.3 Fractal Summaries

AI summarizes at every level:
- Introduction: "In this piece, I'll cover X, Y, and Z"
- Section intros: "This section explores..."
- Conclusions: "In conclusion, we've seen that..."
- Section endings: "As we can see..."

→ Writing that trusts its readers doesn't need signposts at every corner.

### 4.4 Overuse of Bold

Bolding every other phrase to manufacture emphasis destroys emphasis. If everything is important, nothing is.

## 5. Content Problems

### 5.1 Vague Without Specifics

```
❌ "This approach delivers actionable insights to drive data-driven decisions."
✓  "This cut our report-generation time from 3 hours to 20 minutes."

❌ "Our team is committed to excellence and innovation."
✓  "We rewrote this page four times. The first three were too safe."
```

### 5.2 The "One Point, Ten Ways" Problem

AI takes a single observation and restates it using different metaphors and examples across the whole piece. Each paragraph adds a new frame but no new information.

→ Say the thing once, precisely. Then move to the next thing.

### 5.3 Vague Attribution

```
❌ "Experts agree that..."
❌ "Studies show..."
❌ "Industry observers have noted..."
❌ "According to some sources..."
```

Name the expert. Link the study. Or don't make the claim.

### 5.4 Elegant Variation (synonym spinning)

AI avoids repeating words by cycling through synonyms:

```
"The protagonist... the key player... the eponymous character... the central figure..."
```

Human writers repeat the noun if it's the clearest word. Synonyms that aren't exact matches change the meaning.

## 6. Rewrite Checklist

When editing copy for AI tone, check in this order:

1. **Scan vocab** — flag any word from §1.1 blacklist; replace or delete
2. **Kill transition bloat** — remove "furthermore," "it's worth noting," "in conclusion"
3. **Break the three** — find tricolons and convert to two items or cut one
4. **Find the stance** — does this piece have an actual opinion? If not, add one
5. **Specificity sweep** — replace every vague claim with a number, name, or action
6. **Read aloud** — if it sounds like a TED talk intro, rewrite it

## 7. Rewrite Examples

### Product copy

```
❌ AI version:
Our platform leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver seamless, 
robust solutions that empower teams to foster collaboration and 
drive meaningful outcomes across the organization.

✓ Rewritten:
We replaced the weekly status meeting with a feed everyone actually reads.
90% of our customers cancel Slack within a month.
```

### About page

```
❌ AI version:
We are a passionate team of innovators committed to redefining the 
landscape of enterprise software through transformative, holistic solutions.

✓ Rewritten:
Three of us quit the same company in 2021 because we were tired of 
software that required a 40-page onboarding guide. So we made ours fit on one page.
```

### Outreach email

```
❌ AI version:
I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out to explore potential 
synergies between our organizations. I believe there is a significant opportunity 
to leverage our respective strengths to drive meaningful outcomes. Please don't 
hesitate to reach out if you'd like to connect. I look forward to hearing from you.

✓ Rewritten:
We both work on developer tooling and I noticed you're hiring a platform engineer — 
that's usually a sign teams are rebuilding something painful. We solved a similar 
problem last year. Worth a 20-minute call?
```

## 8. The Three Self-Test Questions

After writing any paragraph, ask:

1. **Could I predict it?** If you can finish the sentence before reading the end, rewrite it.
2. **Would a specific person say this?** Could you imagine a real human with a name and a history saying these words? If not, who's actually talking?
3. **What did it choose NOT to say?** Good writing makes cuts. If this paragraph covers everything, it's covering nothing.
