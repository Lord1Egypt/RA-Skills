# Home Inspection Report

Draft an ASHI/InterNACHI-compliant residential property inspection report from field notes and observations.

## Overview

Home inspectors must deliver written reports that document the condition of all inspected systems and components, clearly communicate deficiencies with severity ratings, and protect both the client and the inspector with proper scope and limitation statements. This skill guides structured intake of field observations across all major systems and produces a DRAFT report for inspector review before delivery to the client.

## Use Cases

- Single-family home inspection reports (buyer, seller, or new-construction)
- Condominium and townhome inspection reports
- Pre-listing inspection reports
- New-construction phase inspections
- Mobile home inspections (with limitations noted)

## Audience

- ASHI- and InterNACHI-certified home inspectors
- Licensed residential inspectors
- Buyer's agents with inspection coordination duties
- New inspectors learning report structure

## What the Skill Produces

1. **Inspector and property summary block** — credentials, address, date, weather, attendees, scope limitations
2. **System-by-system findings** — Inspected / Not Inspected / Not Visible per component
3. **Deficiency table** — Location, Description, Severity (Safety Hazard / Major Defect / Maintenance Item)
4. **Executive summary** — plain-language overview for client reading
5. **Inspector review block** — certification statement and professional disclaimer

## Standards Alignment

- ASHI Standards of Practice (latest edition)
- InterNACHI Standards of Practice (latest edition)
- State licensing requirements (flagged as jurisdiction-specific)

## Safety Boundaries

- All output is labeled DRAFT — must not be delivered to clients without inspector review and signature.
- Cost estimates are never included; the skill directs clients to obtain licensed contractor quotes.
- Safety hazards are prominently flagged and never buried in body text.
- This skill does not replace the physical inspection — it documents findings from a completed inspection.
