**H2:** When the EPA says duct cleaning is warranted

EPA (402-K-97-002) identifies three conditions where duct cleaning is appropriate:

1. **Visible mold growth** on hard duct surfaces or HVAC components (requires confirmation by inspection)
2. **Pest infestation** — evidence of rodents or insects in the duct system
3. **Ducts clogged with debris** — excessive particles visibly released into living space

We perform a pre-cleaning inspection to document which of these conditions is present before we quote. If none are confirmed, we tell you honestly.

**H2:** What NADCA cleaning does (and doesn't) guarantee

NADCA source-removal documents that your duct system meets NADCA cleanliness criteria after cleaning. EPA is clear that studies have not conclusively shown that duct cleaning prevents health problems or improves indoor air quality by a guaranteed percentage. If you have physician-documented respiratory concerns, we recommend an IAQ assessment with pre/post particle count — we provide the data; your physician interprets the clinical implications.
