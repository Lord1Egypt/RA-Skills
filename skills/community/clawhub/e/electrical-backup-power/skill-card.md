## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill to get safety-focused guidance for outage preparedness, breaker-panel awareness, generator connection choices, basic generator sizing, and solar or battery backup planning. It emphasizes when work must stop and a licensed electrician should be used. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Electrical backup power advice can be unsafe if applied outside local code or beyond the user's qualifications. <br>
Mitigation: Treat the skill as general educational guidance, verify local electrical codes, and use a licensed electrician for transfer switches, interlock kits, panel work, new circuits, permits, or uncertain tasks. <br>
Risk: Incorrect generator use can create carbon monoxide or backfeed hazards. <br>
Mitigation: Keep generator guidance centered on outdoor placement away from openings, working CO detectors, and approved transfer equipment or isolated extension-cord use. <br>
