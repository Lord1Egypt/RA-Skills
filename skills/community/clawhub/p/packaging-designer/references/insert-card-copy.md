# Insert Card Copy Patterns

Every insert card has one job: make the customer do one thing before they forget about the package. Adding a second job roughly halves the response rate on the first.

## Choose the primary CTA based on product economics

- **Low repeat rate, high-review-value product** (e.g., a $40 kitchen gadget): primary = review.
- **High repeat rate, high-LTV product** (e.g., consumables, skincare, supplements): primary = repurchase with discount.
- **Social-native brand, soft goods or lifestyle**: primary = UGC / share.
- **Referral-friendly, considered purchase**: primary = referral code.

Pick one. The others go on the post-purchase email flow, not the card.

## Pattern library — review CTA

### Pattern R1 — soft ask, incentive

"Love it? Leave an honest review and get 15% off your next order. Scan below."

Works because: asks for honest, not positive. Incentive is for the review, not for the rating.

### Pattern R2 — branded voice, single sentence

"Your honest thoughts help us make this better — 60 seconds here [QR]."

Works because: frames the review as helping the brand improve, not as a favor to get stars.

## Pattern library — repurchase CTA

### Pattern P1 — replenish nudge

"Running low in about 30 days. Set up auto-refill and save 20%. [QR]"

Works because: names a timeline, creates urgency without fake urgency.

### Pattern P2 — next-step upsell

"The next thing people buy is [product B]. Here's 15% off, code THANKS15."

Works because: names the specific next product, avoids generic "10% off anything."

## Pattern library — referral CTA

### Pattern F1 — mutual benefit

"Tell a friend — they get $10 off, you get $10 back. Your code: LEROY10."

Works because: both parties benefit, code is personalized so it feels real.

## Pattern library — UGC / share

### Pattern U1 — tag and repost

"Tag @brand in your unboxing and we'll repost the best shots every Friday — plus 10% off for featured posts."

Works because: tangible reward (feature + discount), specific cadence (Friday).

## What NOT to write

- "Please leave a 5-star review." Policy violation on most marketplaces, and reads as desperate.
- "Thank you for your order." Burns the card on zero new information.
- "Follow us on Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook and subscribe to our newsletter." Five CTAs, zero conversions.
- A wall of brand-story paragraphs. The card is a nudge, not a manifesto.
- "Scan QR" without telling the customer where it goes. Say "Scan to leave a review" not "Scan me!"

## Copy checklist before print

- [ ] One primary CTA, stated in one sentence.
- [ ] One QR code, ≥2.5 cm square, on matte or uncoated stock.
- [ ] URL under the QR in small type, in case the QR doesn't scan.
- [ ] Discount code, if any, is trackable and monitored for abuse.
- [ ] Brand mark present but not dominant.
- [ ] Legal: no explicit request for positive or 5-star rating.
