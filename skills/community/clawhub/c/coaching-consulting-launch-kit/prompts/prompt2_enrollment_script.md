# Prompt 2 — Enrollment Conversation Script + Objection Handler

**Purpose:** Generate a complete discovery call guide with word-for-word scripts, diagnostic questions, offer presentation sequence, and objection handlers for every common pushback.

---

## Instructions

Run Prompt 1 first. Use your Methodology Name, Outcome Promise, and ICP from Prompt 1 to fill in the fields below.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a sales conversation designer for coaching and consulting businesses. Build a complete Enrollment Conversation System for this coach.

**COACH INFO (from Prompt 1 output):**
- Methodology name: [e.g., The Executive Ascent Framework]
- Ideal client: [e.g., mid-level tech managers (Director to VP) at companies 500+ employees]
- Outcome promise: [e.g., Reach the C-suite within 18 months without burning out or sacrificing your values]
- Program name and price: [e.g., The VP Accelerator — $4,500 for 3 months 1:1]
- Top 3 results/proof points: [e.g., 47 promotions, avg salary increase $28K, clients at Amazon/Meta/Deloitte]
- Common objections you've faced or fear: [e.g., too expensive, spouse needs to agree, needs to think about it, not ready yet]
- Call format: [e.g., 45-minute Zoom discovery call, booked via Calendly]

**OUTPUT FORMAT:**

Produce a complete Enrollment Conversation Script with these exact sections:

---

### 1. Pre-Call Confirmation Email
A 150-word email sent 24 hours before the call that:
- Reminds them of the time and Zoom link
- Builds anticipation (they're about to get a diagnosis, not a pitch)
- Asks them to come prepared with their #1 challenge
- Sets the tone: collaborative, expert, no pressure

---

### 2. Call Opening (First 5 Minutes)
Word-for-word script for:
- The first 30 seconds (make them feel seen, not sold to)
- Agenda-setting statement that positions you as the expert
- Permission question that shifts the dynamic ("Would it be okay if I ask you some direct questions?")

---

### 3. Diagnostic Questions (10-15 Minutes)
Write 12 questions in 3 categories:

**Category A — Current Situation (4 questions)**
Uncover where they are now. Questions should surface the gap between current reality and desired outcome. Start factual, get emotional.

**Category B — Pain Depth (4 questions)**
Amplify the cost of inaction. Questions that reveal what staying stuck is costing them: financially, relationally, psychologically. Include one "what happens if nothing changes in 12 months?" style question.

**Category C — Vision & Desire (4 questions)**
Pull out their specific version of success. These questions build urgency through their own words. Include one "who would you become?" question.

After each category, include a brief coach note on what you're listening for and how to bridge to the next section.

---

### 4. Diagnostic Summary (2 Minutes)
Word-for-word script to mirror back what you heard:
- Open with: "Here's what I'm hearing..."
- Summarize their situation in their words (not yours)
- Name the gap between where they are and where they want to be
- Transition: "Let me share what I see..."

---

### 5. Offer Presentation (5-7 Minutes)
Script for presenting the program without sounding salesy:
- Bridge from their specific pain to your specific solution
- Introduce the methodology name with a one-sentence explanation
- Walk through the 3-phase transformation map (reference Prompt 1)
- State the investment last (price reveal after value is established)
- Use this exact price reveal structure:
  1. "The investment for [program name] is [price]."
  2. Pause. Let it land. Do not keep talking.
  3. "What that includes is..." (list deliverables)
  4. "The way I think about the ROI is..." (connect to their specific numbers)

---

### 6. Objection Handlers (Word-for-Word Scripts)

Write complete responses for each of these 6 objections:

**Objection 1: "I need to think about it."**
- Acknowledge + curiosity question
- Uncover the real objection underneath
- Close or set a follow-up with a specific timeline

**Objection 2: "I can't afford it / it's too expensive."**
- Validate without discounting
- Reframe value vs. cost
- Offer payment plan if applicable
- If they truly can't afford it: graceful redirect to lower tier

**Objection 3: "I need to talk to my spouse/partner first."**
- Honor the relationship
- Offer to schedule a call with both of them
- Ask: "If [partner] were on this call right now, what do you think their concern would be?"

**Objection 4: "I'm not ready yet / bad timing."**
- Explore the "not ready" — is it circumstance or fear?
- If circumstance: schedule a future date check-in
- If fear: "What would need to be true for you to feel ready?"

**Objection 5: "I've tried coaching before and it didn't work."**
- Validate the frustration
- Ask what specifically didn't work
- Explain what's different about your approach (specific, not defensive)
- Use a proof point that addresses their specific past failure

**Objection 6: "Let me look at some other options first."**
- Permission to understand what they're comparing
- Clarify your unique position vs. alternatives
- The urgency question: "What's the cost of waiting 30-60 days while you research?"

---

### 7. Closing Script (2 Minutes)
Three closing options by buyer type:

**The Decisive Buyer:** Direct close script (ready to say yes, just needs permission)

**The Thoughtful Buyer:** Commitment-to-next-step close (not ready today, schedule follow-up with deadline)

**The No For Now:** Graceful no-save script (keep relationship warm, plant seeds for future)

---

### 8. Post-Call Follow-Up Sequence (3 Emails)

**Email 1 — Sent within 1 hour of call**
Subject: [Their name] — notes from our call + next steps
150-word recap of what they shared, what you'd work on together, clear next step with link/deadline.

**Email 2 — Sent 48 hours later (if no response)**
Subject: One question for you, [first name]
60-word check-in: simple, direct, no desperation. Ask the one question that surfaces the real sticking point.

**Email 3 — Sent 5 days later (final follow-up)**
Subject: Closing the loop on our conversation
80-word graceful close. Leave the door open without chasing.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Practice the diagnostic questions** before using them in real calls. They're designed to be conversational, not interrogative.
- **Pause after naming the price.** Coaches who keep talking after stating the investment signal uncertainty. Let the number land.
- **Keep objection scripts short.** The purpose is to open dialogue, not overwhelm. Say less, ask more.
- **Personalize Post-Call Email 1** with 2-3 phrases they used on the call. Verbatim repetition of their words shows you listened.
