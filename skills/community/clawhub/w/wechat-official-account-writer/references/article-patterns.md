# Article Patterns

## Common Article Types

### Opinion / Insight

Use when the article argues a point of view.

Structure:

1. Hook: name the change, conflict, or misconception.
2. Thesis: state the main judgment in plain language.
3. Evidence: give 2-4 supporting observations, examples, or cases.
4. Implication: explain what readers should rethink or do differently.
5. Close: return to the opening and sharpen the takeaway.

### Practical Guide

Use when readers want methods, checklists, prompts, workflows, or decisions.

Structure:

1. Pain: describe the failure mode readers recognize.
2. Principle: give the mental model.
3. Steps: 3-7 actionable steps, each with a concrete example.
4. Pitfalls: common mistakes and how to avoid them.
5. Quick checklist: make the article usable after reading.

### Product / Project Story

Use when introducing a product, feature, project, or internal initiative.

Structure:

1. Problem: who had what problem, in what scenario.
2. Old way: why the old solution was insufficient.
3. New approach: what changed and why it matters.
4. Details: show the workflow, capability, or experience.
5. Proof: include available evidence without exaggeration.
6. Next step: invite trial, feedback, or discussion.

### News / Trend Analysis

Use when turning recent events into a public-account post.

Structure:

1. What happened: concise factual summary.
2. Why it matters: identify the affected audience and stakes.
3. What changed: compare with the prior state.
4. What to watch: list uncertainties and next signals.
5. Reader takeaway: explain the practical implication.

## Opening Patterns

- **Scene**: start with a real or typical user moment.
- **Contrast**: "过去 X，现在 Y".
- **Misconception**: name a belief that sounds plausible but is incomplete.
- **Question**: ask the exact question the reader already has.
- **Data/event**: use only when the source is available or user-provided.

Avoid vague openings:

- "随着时代的发展..."
- "在这个信息爆炸的时代..."
- "众所周知..."
- "本文将从以下几个方面..."

## Title Patterns

Generate titles that are specific, honest, and easy to scan.

- `不是 X，而是 Y`
- `为什么 X 越来越难？`
- `我用 X 做了 Y，发现 Z`
- `给 X 的一份 Y 指南`
- `X 的真正问题，不在 A，而在 B`
- `从 X 到 Y：一次 Z 的复盘`
- `别急着 X，先想清楚这 3 件事`
- `X 正在改变，但多数人还在用旧方法`

Title checks:

- Keep the core noun visible.
- Avoid unsupported absolutes such as "彻底", "唯一", "颠覆", "必看", "全网最全" unless the user explicitly requires a marketing style and the claim is defensible.
- Prefer curiosity from specificity, not clickbait.

## WeChat Readability

- Use short paragraphs, usually 1-3 Chinese sentences.
- Keep section headings concrete; avoid decorative headings that hide the meaning.
- Use lists for workflows, options, and comparisons.
- Use bold sparingly for key conclusions.
- Use blockquotes only for short, high-signal statements.
- Avoid dense tables unless the user asks for a structured comparison.

## Endings

- **Takeaway ending**: summarize the central judgment in 2-3 sentences.
- **Checklist ending**: give readers a reusable list.
- **Question ending**: invite comments with one concrete question.
- **Action ending**: ask readers to try, reply, subscribe, or contact, only when appropriate.
