## Description: <br>
Urgent, time-boxed protocol for the first 72 hours after losing a job, covering document preservation, unemployment filing, health insurance decisions, emergency budgeting, severance review, and family communication. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users who have just lost a job use this skill to stabilize during the first 72 hours, prioritize urgent benefits and budget decisions, and create checklists, reminders, and comparison notes before beginning a job search. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
United States primary; global use requires local adaptation for unemployment, health insurance, severance, final pay, and employment-law references. <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill can lead an agent to store sensitive layoff, benefits, financial runway, pay-stub, and account-balance details. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only when comfortable with that storage, limit saved data to what is necessary, and ask the agent not to persist exact balances or pay-stub details unless explicitly chosen. <br>
Risk: Document preservation guidance can accidentally pull employer, client, vendor, or proprietary material into personal storage. <br>
Mitigation: Limit preservation to the user's own employment records and avoid copying client lists, vendor lists, trade secrets, or other proprietary employer material. <br>
Risk: US-specific benefits, health-insurance, and severance guidance may not apply in other jurisdictions. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm the user's country or region, substitute local equivalents, and recommend local employment-law review for severance decisions. <br>
Risk: Job loss can involve acute distress or self-harm risk. <br>
Mitigation: Start with the required safety check and provide crisis resources before continuing if the user reports thoughts of self-harm. <br>
