## Key Principles of the $100M Leads Method

### 1. Engaged Leads > Leads
A lead is someone you can contact. An engaged lead is someone who SHOWS interest. The 9-word email ("Are you still looking to X?") is the fastest way to turn a dead list into engaged leads. Those who reply are your gold.

### 2. Lead Magnet First
Before you sell your core offer, sell a narrow, high-value solution that reveals a bigger problem. The salty pretzels theory: give them free pretzels (narrow solution) → they get thirsty (problem revealed) → they buy a drink (your core offer). Your lead magnet should be valuable enough that you COULD charge for it.

### 3. You Don't Need to Go Viral
Warm outreach alone can take you to $100K+/year:
- 500 reachouts/week → 5 customers/week
- $400 product → $2,000/week → $104,000/year
- That's 2x the US median household income. From one method. One. No ads. No content. Just texting people you know.

### 4. The Money Is in the Follow-Up
80% of sales happen on follow-ups 4-12. Most people give up after 1-2 touches. Hormozi's rule: reach out 3 times, once per day, then move on. For warm audience: follow up across different platforms (text, email, DM).

### 5. Scarcity + Urgency = Action
Two psychological triggers that matter most:
- **Scarcity:** Limited supply. "I can only take 5 people this month."
- **Urgency:** Limited time. "This offer ends Friday at midnight."
Combine them when possible. Bonus: any reason (even manufactured) works better than no reason. "Because it's my birthday" still outperforms no reason.

### 6. Test Everything. Headlines First.
David Ogilvy: "When you have written your headline, you have spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar." Five times more people read your headline than the rest. Small changes in headlines can 2x, 3x, 10x engagement. Hormozi tested "Advertising" vs "Promotion" vs "Leads" vs "Marketing" for his book — "Leads" won. Tested "How to get strangers to buy" vs "How to get strangers to want to buy" — "How to" + "want to" won. Two words made the difference.

> **Case: The headline tests that determined this book's title** (Chapter 3): Hormozi ran multiple rounds of split tests for the book title. Round I: "Advertising" vs "Promotion" → Advertising won. Round II: "Advertising" vs "Leads" → Leads won. Round III: "Marketing" vs "Leads" → Leads won. Subheadline: "How to get strangers to want to buy your stuff" beat all alternatives — including "Get strangers to want to buy your stuff" (the only difference being two words: "How to"). **Key takeaway:** Never guess what works. Test. Two words can be the difference between a bestseller and a dud.

> **Case: The webinar that failed vs the case study that printed money** (Chapter 3): Hormozi spent 8 Sundays building a webinar. $450 in ads, 80 leads, 0 sales. The lead magnet? A "free case study" video showing exactly what worked — 13 minutes, no slides, no presenting, just screen sharing his ad account. Result: Leila's calendar booked solid for a week. **Key takeaway:** Don't guess what your audience wants. Test a simple version first. The simpler version often wins.

### 7. Content = Audience = Money
The compounding asset is not the content itself — it's the audience the content attracts. "I thought making content was a waste of time. I was wrong. It wasn't about the content at all — it was about the audience." Hormozi went from 0 to 1.4M followers by posting 10x more content. Volume works. Simple, not easy.
