# Funnel Assets

## Ad Creative

Use the rule of one:

- One idea
- One story
- One promise
- One emotion
- One call to action

Strong service lead ads usually include:

- Clear who-it-is-for language.
- A problem the buyer already feels.
- A concrete outcome or mechanism.
- Proof that fits the claim.
- Enough commitment language to prevent cheap curiosity from flooding the funnel.
- A CTA that sells the next step, not the whole program/service.

Creative diagnosis:

- If CTR is low, test new angles and hooks.
- If CTR is very high but leads are poor, make the hook more specific, committed, and qualifying.
- If an organic post worked, extract why it worked; do not simply boost it unchanged. Rebuild it as an ad with a stronger CTA and conversion intent.

## Landing/Application Page

The page must quickly answer:

- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- What outcome can I reasonably expect?
- Why should I trust this?
- What is the next step?
- What will happen after I submit?

Prioritize:

- Outcome-led headline, not a label for the service.
- Message match with the ad.
- Fast proof: testimonials, case snippets, recognizable credentials, before/after logic where policy-safe.
- FAQs high enough to close common doubts.
- Visual explanations where they reduce reading.
- Mobile-first speed and readability.
- Qualification questions placed after the visitor understands the value.

Application questions:

- Ask only what is needed for qualification, routing, or sales context.
- Put high-commitment questions later unless they are essential hard gates.
- Use disqualification pages for poor-fit answers.
- Do not fire qualified-lead/schedule events for disqualified people.
- Consider predictive red-flag questions unique to the business.

## VSSL

Use a VSSL when the buyer needs education before booking. Structure it as:

1. Name the problem in the buyer's language.
2. Explain why common solutions fail.
3. Introduce the mechanism or strategic shift.
4. Prove it with examples, data, or story.
5. Set expectations about effort, fit, price level, or process.
6. Sell the application or call as the next step.

Do not make the page depend on a visitor watching the entire video. Many visitors scan first.

## Booking Page

The booking page should make the calendar feel obvious after qualification:

- Confirm they are in the right place.
- Explain what happens on the call.
- Explain what they should bring or decide before the call.
- Use benefit-framed reminders about decision makers, punctuality, and preparation.
- Keep scheduling options broad enough to convert, but do not open low-quality slots that hurt show rate.

## Nurture And Show Rate

After booking, assume motivation decays unless reinforced.

Use:

- Immediate confirmation page.
- Calendar invite.
- Email/SMS/WhatsApp reminders where compliant and expected.
- A short "what to expect" message.
- Proof or objection-handling messages before the call.
- A final reminder close to the call.

Nurture content should prevent buyer remorse, not start a new sales pitch from scratch.

## Sales Call Feedback Loop

Review calls and CRM notes for:

- Objections that show up repeatedly.
- People who expected a free consultation instead of a buying conversation.
- Poor-fit patterns from application answers.
- Moments where the rep over-consulted and slowed momentum.
- Missing tie-down questions before the offer.

Turn repeated objections into upstream assets:

- "I do not know if this works for my industry" -> landing-page section or FAQ.
- "I need to ask my partner" -> nurture/booking expectation about decision makers.
- "I do not trust the proof" -> stronger proof placement or credibility email.
- "Too expensive" from unqualified leads -> ad/page qualification and pricing expectation.
