# 🧊 Food Storage Knowledge Base (English)

> Reference data for the Fridge Manager skill. Shelf life refers to opened/loose items. For sealed packaged food, follow the label.

## 🥩 Meat

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Fresh pork/beef/lamb | 2-3 days | 4-6 months | Portion into sealed bags, squeeze out air; wrap tightly for fridge |
| Fresh chicken/duck/turkey | 1-2 days | 6-9 months | Poultry spoils faster than red meat — freeze if not using same day |
| Ground meat/mince | 1 day | 3-4 months | Flatten in bag for faster thawing |
| Cured meat/sausage/bacon | 1-2 weeks | 2-3 months | Wrap in paper towel then bag to prevent moisture |
| Cooked meat/deli | 3-4 days | 2-3 months | Sealed container, store above raw meat |

## 🐟 Seafood

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Fresh fish | 1-2 days | 3-6 months | Gut before freezing; place on ice in fridge |
| Shrimp/crab | 1 day | 3-6 months | Shell-on freezing preserves freshness better |
| Shellfish (clams/oysters) | 1-2 days | Not recommended | Live shellfish: open container, cover with damp cloth |
| Frozen shrimp/fish fillets | 1 day after thawing | 6-12 months | Never refreeze after thawing |

## 🥚 Dairy & Eggs

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Eggs | 3-5 weeks | Not recommended (in shell) | Pointed end down, away from strong odors |
| Fresh milk (opened) | 3-5 days | 1-3 months | Never store on the door (temperature fluctuates) |
| Yogurt | Use by label date | 1-2 months | Frozen yogurt changes texture — good for smoothies |
| Butter | 1-3 months | 6-9 months | Seal well to prevent odor absorption |
| Hard cheese | 3-4 weeks | 6 months | Wrap in wax paper, then plastic wrap |
| Soft cheese | 1-2 weeks | Not recommended | Consume soon after opening |

## 🥬 Vegetables

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Leafy greens (spinach/lettuce) | 3-5 days | Blanch first, 3 months | Wrap in paper towel, store in bag with opening |
| Broccoli/cauliflower | 5-7 days | Blanch first, 6 months | Don't wash before storing |
| Carrots/radish | 2-3 weeks | Blanch & dice, 6 months | Remove tops — they draw moisture |
| Tomatoes | 5-7 days | Whole, 3 months | Ripen at room temp first, then refrigerate |
| Cucumbers | 5-7 days | Not recommended | Wrap individually in plastic to prevent moisture loss |
| Potatoes | Not recommended | Cooked only | Cool, dark, ventilated spot; avoid light to prevent sprouting |
| Onions | Not recommended | Chopped, 3 months | Cool, dry place; keep away from potatoes |
| Garlic/ginger | 2-3 weeks | 3-6 months | Ginger: slice and freeze for easy use |
| Mushrooms | 3-5 days | Cook first, 3 months | Paper bag, NOT plastic (causes sliminess) |

## 🍎 Fruits

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Apples | 1-2 months | Sliced, 6 months | Store alone — releases ethylene that ripens other fruit |
| Bananas | Not recommended (skin blackens) | Peeled, 2 months | Room temp; frozen bananas are great for smoothies |
| Grapes | 1-2 weeks | 3 months | Don't wash before storing |
| Berries (strawberry/blueberry) | 3-5 days | 6 months | Don't wash or hull; spread in single layer |
| Citrus (oranges/lemons) | 2-3 weeks | Not recommended | Refrigeration extends shelf life |
| Watermelon (cut) | 3-5 days | Freeze for slush | Press plastic wrap directly onto cut surface |
| Avocado | 3-5 days when ripe | Halved & pitted, 3 months | Ripen at room temp; add an apple to speed ripening |

## 🍚 Grains & Staples

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Cooked rice | 1 day | 1 month | Portion while hot and freeze immediately for best texture |
| Bread | 3-5 days | 3 months | Room temp or freeze — do NOT refrigerate (accelerates staling) |
| Steamed buns/baozi | 2-3 days | 1-2 months | Steam from frozen for best result (not microwave) |
| Raw dumplings/wontons | 1 day | 2-3 months | Freeze in single layer first, then bag to prevent sticking |
| Tofu | 3-5 days | Frozen tofu 1 month | Change water daily to extend life |
| Fresh noodles | 2-3 days | 1-2 months | Dust with flour, portion before freezing |

## 🫙 Condiments & Sauces

| Food | After Opening (Fridge) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------------|--------------|
| Soy sauce | 3-6 months | Room temp OK but fridge is better |
| Oyster sauce | 3 months | **Must refrigerate** — most commonly overlooked |
| Ketchup | 6 months | Store upside-down to reduce oxidation |
| Mayonnaise | 2 months | Never double-dip with used utensils |
| Miso | 1 year | Press plastic wrap directly on surface |
| Peanut butter | 3 months | Natural: must refrigerate; processed: room temp OK |
| Chili paste/doubanjiang | 6 months | Use clean, dry utensils |

## 🥡 Leftovers

| Food | Fridge (0-4°C) | Freezer (-18°C) | Storage Tips |
|------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| Cooked dishes/soups | 3-4 days | 2-3 months | **Refrigerate within 2 hours** — don't wait until cool |
| Cooked rice/pasta | 1-2 days | 1 month | Spread out to cool faster |
| Hot pot broth | 1 week | 3 months | Boil before re-refrigerating |

## ⚠️ General Rules

1. **2-Hour Rule**: Discard cooked food left at room temp over 2 hours (1 hour in summer)
2. **Safe Thawing**: Fridge thaw (safest) > running water > microwave. **Never thaw at room temp**
3. **First In, First Out**: New items go to the back, older items to the front
4. **Zone Storage**: Cooked food on top shelf, raw meat on bottom, produce in drawers — prevent cross-contamination
5. **No Refreeze**: Never refreeze thawed food (unless cooked first)
6. **Fridge ≠ Safe**: Refrigeration slows spoilage but doesn't kill bacteria
7. **70% Full**: Overpacking reduces air circulation and cooling efficiency
