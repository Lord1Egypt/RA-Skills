## Cold Outreach

### cold-problem-pilot
Subject: Agentic workflow idea for {company}

Hi {prospect_name},

I noticed {trigger}. If {company} has a repeated workflow around {workflow}, there may be a practical agentic pilot here: draft the work, route it through the right tools, require human approval, and log what happened.

I build scoped agentic development pilots with evaluation, approval gates, monitoring, and handoff documentation.

Worth a 15-minute look at whether {workflow} is a fit?

### cold-operations-bottleneck
Subject: Reducing manual triage around {workflow}

Hi {prospect_name},

Teams often have a workflow where requests arrive faster than people can classify, draft, check, and route them. If that is happening with {workflow} at {company}, an agentic workflow could handle the prep work while keeping approvals with your team.

I can map the current path, identify the safe automation points, and outline a small pilot.

Open to a quick workflow review?

### cold-technical-evaluation
Subject: Evaluation-first agentic workflow

Hi {prospect_name},

Many AI pilots stall because the demo comes before the evaluation plan. My approach is to start with the workflow, tool boundaries, approval gates, test cases, logs, and deployment constraints.

If {company} is exploring {workflow}, I can help design a bounded agentic pilot that is testable before it touches production.

Would a short architecture conversation be useful?

### cold-executive-risk
Subject: AI progress without uncontrolled automation

Hi {prospect_name},

If {company} wants AI progress but not uncontrolled production actions, a bounded agentic workflow is often the right middle path.

The pilot can draft, retrieve, classify, and route work while requiring human approval for external communications, production changes, purchases, or data export.

Should I send a one-page pilot outline for {workflow}?

### cold-revenue-team
Subject: Agentic help for sales follow-up

Hi {prospect_name},

If your team is losing time to research, recap notes, draft follow-ups, CRM updates, and next-step reminders, an agentic workflow can help without taking final judgment away from reps.

The system can prepare the work, cite source context, queue approvals, and log actions.

Would you be open to mapping one sales workflow?

### cold-support-team
Subject: Agentic triage for support queues

Hi {prospect_name},

Support teams are a strong fit for agentic pilots because the workflow is repeatable: classify the issue, retrieve policy or product context, draft a response, escalate exceptions, and log the path.

I can help scope this with approval gates and evaluation examples before rollout.

Worth discussing for {company}?

### cold-ops-knowledge
Subject: Turning internal knowledge into reviewed workflows

Hi {prospect_name},

When internal knowledge is spread across docs, tickets, Slack, and inboxes, people spend too much time finding context before doing the actual work.

An agentic workflow can retrieve the right context, draft the next action, and route it for review.

Could I show how that might apply to {workflow}?

### cold-founders
Subject: A scoped AI workflow pilot

Hi {prospect_name},

For smaller teams, the useful AI opportunity is usually not a broad transformation project. It is one repeated workflow with clear inputs, clear approval points, and measurable time saved.

I build those pilots: workflow design, implementation, evaluation, documentation, and handoff.

Is there one workflow at {company} worth mapping?

## Objection Handling

### close-objection-budget
Subject: Re: Budget

Hi {prospect_name},

That makes sense. If budget is the constraint, I would reduce the first step rather than stretch the scope.

A smaller pilot could focus on workflow mapping, prototype of one path, evaluation examples, and a handoff plan. That keeps the decision tied to evidence instead of a broad commitment.

Should I revise the proposal to that version?

### close-objection-risk
Subject: Re: Risk controls

Hi {prospect_name},

The risk concern is valid. I would not recommend starting with autonomous production action.

The safer first step is a bounded pilot: limited tools, test cases, logs, and human approval for anything external, production-facing, financial, or material.

Would that address the concern, or is there a specific policy we need to design around?

### close-objection-timing
Subject: Re: Timing

Hi {prospect_name},

If now is not the right time for a build, we can separate the decision.

One option is a lightweight workflow assessment now, then implementation when the team has capacity. You would still get the map, pilot scope, risks, and effort estimate.

Would that be more useful?

### close-objection-internal-team
Subject: Re: Internal team

Hi {prospect_name},

If your internal team may build this, I can still help by defining the workflow, controls, evaluation plan, and implementation handoff so they are not starting from a blank page.

That can be a smaller advisory engagement instead of a full build.

Would that fit better?
