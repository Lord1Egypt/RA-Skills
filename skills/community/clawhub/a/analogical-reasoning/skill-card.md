## Description: <br>
Apply analogical reasoning to transfer knowledge from familiar domains to unfamiliar ones. Use when the user needs creative problem-solving by finding structural parallels, wants to understand something new through comparison, or needs to evaluate whether an analogy holds or breaks down. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users and developers use this skill to analyze problems by mapping familiar source domains to unfamiliar target domains, extracting transferable insights, and identifying where analogies break down. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Analogical reasoning can overemphasize surface similarities and produce misleading conclusions. <br>
Mitigation: Use the skill's breakdown and fidelity checks to test causal structure, identify disanalogies, and treat predictions as hypotheses before acting on them. <br>
Risk: The skill may make responses longer and more structured than the user needs. <br>
Mitigation: Ask the agent to summarize or focus on the most decision-relevant analogies when brevity matters. <br>
