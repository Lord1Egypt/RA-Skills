# Prompt Patterns

Use these patterns when the user asks for reusable prompts or wants to adapt the workflow.

## Research Paragraph Polish

```text
I am writing a paper on [topic] for a [field] academic audience.
This paragraph appears in the [section].
My intended meaning is: [plain-language meaning].
Please revise the text for clarity, coherence, concision, and professional academic tone.
Preserve all claims, citations, technical terms, and uncertainty levels.
Do not add new claims or references.
Flag any sentence whose meaning is ambiguous.

Text:
[paste text]
```

## Abstract Polish

```text
I am revising an abstract for a [field] paper.
The study's core contribution is: [contribution].
The target tone is concise, concrete, and journal-ready.
Please improve flow and readability without changing the claims, results, methods, or implications.
Flag any sentence that sounds overstated or underspecified.

Abstract:
[paste abstract]
```

## Discussion Section

```text
I am revising the discussion section of a [field] paper.
The key finding is: [finding].
The evidence supports: [supported interpretation].
The evidence does not support: [boundary].
Please improve clarity and logical flow while keeping claims appropriately cautious.

Text:
[paste text]
```

## Reviewer Response

```text
I am responding to a reviewer comment.
Reviewer comment:
[paste comment]

What we changed:
[state actual change]

What we did not change, if relevant:
[state boundary]

Please draft a respectful, specific response.
Do not claim that we changed something unless it appears in my notes.
Separate the response to the reviewer from any suggested manuscript wording.
```

## Peer Review Notes

```text
I have read a manuscript and wrote the following review notes.
Please organize them into a concise, professional peer-review report.
First summarize the manuscript's core contribution, then list major concerns in order of importance, then minor comments.
Do not invent details beyond my notes.

Notes:
[paste notes]
```

## Editorial Feedback

```text
I am preparing editorial feedback based on my decision notes.
Please draft a direct, respectful, and actionable letter.
Make the key reason for the decision clear.
Avoid vague phrases and do not add criticisms that are not in my notes.

Decision notes:
[paste notes]
```
