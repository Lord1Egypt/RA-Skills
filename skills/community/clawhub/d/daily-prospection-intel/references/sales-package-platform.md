### Risks (eCTD 2.5.6.3) and the Three-Way AE Classification

**Populated by Adverse Events Reports and Benefit-Risk Assessment**

**Mechanism-Dependent AEs**
AEs inherent to the antiplatelet mechanism of action that will occur regardless of formulation (e.g., bleeding risk, intracranial haemorrhage). These cannot be addressed by reformulation and must be carried forward in the eCTD 2.5.6.3 risk profile. Baigent et al., Lancet, 2009: GI bleeding OR 1.5 to 2.0 vs. placebo.

**Formulation-Dependent AEs**
AEs that are route-dependent and that IV administration directly addresses (e.g., GI erosion from direct mucosal contact, dyspepsia, aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease from enteric coating). These constitute the reformulation's safety argument.

**Formulation-Introduced Risks**
New risks introduced by the IV route (e.g., injection-site reactions, infusion errors, IV excipient (lysine salt) safety profile). These require prospective risk management under ICH E2E and must be addressed in the Benefit-Risk Assessment's RMP recommendations.

This three-way classification is the analytical structure that maps directly to eCTD 2.5.6.3. It is also structurally identical to the AE-as-signal methodology used in the glioblastoma project: CNS-related AEs signalled BBB crossing; GI-related AEs signal route-dependent toxicity addressable by reformulation.
