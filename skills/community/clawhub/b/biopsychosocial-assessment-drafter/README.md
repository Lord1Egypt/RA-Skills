# Biopsychosocial Assessment Drafter

Drafts a comprehensive biopsychosocial-spiritual (BPSS) assessment for a new client, covering all required clinical domains for hospital, community mental health, child welfare, outpatient, and case management settings.

## What It Does

Guides the social worker through structured intake across four domains and produces a DRAFT BPSS assessment covering:

- Presenting problem and client-stated goals
- Biological domain: medical history, medications, physical health, substance use
- Psychological domain: mental health history, trauma, current symptoms, cognitive and emotional functioning
- Social domain: family system, relationships, housing, employment, finances, cultural identity
- Spiritual/existential domain: values, meaning, and practices (offered, not required)
- Strengths and protective factors inventory
- Risk and safety screen (suicidality, homicidality, abuse/neglect)
- Provisional DSM-5-TR diagnosis category prompts
- Summary of service needs and prioritized recommendations

## Who It's For

- Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSW) in clinical settings
- Master of Social Work (MSW) practitioners and interns
- Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) case managers and community practitioners
- Social work supervisors reviewing and co-signing assessments

## When To Use

Use when you need to convert intake interview notes, a clinical intake form, or a raw conversation summary into a structured BPSS assessment. Applicable to initial intakes in mental health clinics, hospitals, child welfare agencies, and case management programs.

## Important Limitations

All output is a **DRAFT** for licensed social worker review and signature. The skill does not render a clinical diagnosis; it only suggests DSM-5-TR categories for the clinician to evaluate. Output must never be used for billing, clinical decision-making, or legal proceedings without licensed SW review and signature. If safety risk data is present, escalate immediately to a licensed professional.
