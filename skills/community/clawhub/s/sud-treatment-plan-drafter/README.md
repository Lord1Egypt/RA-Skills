# SUD Treatment Plan Drafter

Drafts ASAM criteria–aligned individualized treatment plans for substance use disorder (SUD) clients, covering level-of-care recommendation, DSM-5-TR diagnosis documentation, measurable goals, interventions, and discharge criteria.

## Use Cases

- Addiction counselors (CADC, LCADC, LADC) drafting initial or updated treatment plans
- LMHC, LCSW, LPC, or psychiatrists documenting SUD treatment in integrated care settings
- SUD treatment teams preparing plans for managed-care authorization or Joint Commission review
- Behavioral health documentation specialists supporting licensed clinicians

## Scope

**In scope:**
- ASAM criteria six-dimension rating and level-of-care (0.5–4.0) recommendation
- DSM-5-TR substance use disorder coding (mild/moderate/severe specifier, remission status)
- Problem list, measurable SMART goals, objectives, and intervention mapping
- Counseling modality and frequency plan (individual, group, family, MAT coordination)
- Discharge criteria and step-down/transition planning narrative
- 42 CFR Part 2 confidentiality reminder block

**Out of scope:**
- Prescribing or adjusting medication-assisted treatment (MAT/MOUD) dosing — physician or APRN only
- Rendering a clinical diagnosis — licensed-counselor review and signature required
- Submitting claims, prior-authorization requests, or billing documents
- Mandated-reporting decisions — follow agency policy and local law

## Output

A DRAFT individualized treatment plan document containing:
- Level-of-care recommendation with ASAM dimension justification narrative
- DSM-5-TR diagnosis with severity specifier
- Problem list with corresponding SMART goals, measurable objectives, and interventions
- Counseling modality, frequency, and MAT coordination plan
- Discharge criteria and transition-of-care plan
- Unresolved-information list and counselor sign-off block

**This document is a DRAFT only.** A licensed addiction counselor, LMHC, LCSW, LPC, or psychiatrist must review, modify as clinically indicated, and sign before the plan is entered into the medical record, submitted for authorization, or used for billing.

## Safety and Boundaries

- All SUD records are protected under 42 CFR Part 2. Do not share draft outputs with any party not authorized under the patient's written consent.
- Never include full patient names, SSNs, dates of birth, or other direct identifiers in agent conversation logs.
- Critical safety concerns (suicidal ideation, active overdose risk, child welfare) flagged during intake must follow agency protocol and local mandatory-reporting laws — this skill does not substitute for crisis assessment.
- MAT decisions (methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone dosing/initiation) require a licensed prescriber.
