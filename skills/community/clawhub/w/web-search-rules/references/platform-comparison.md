# Platform Comparison

## Quick choice

- Choose Obsidian for local privacy, Markdown, and direct file control.
- Choose Tencent Docs, Feishu Wiki, or DingTalk Docs for cloud collaboration and workspace sharing.
- Choose IMA for cloud knowledge-base workflows with AI search or knowledge graph features.
- Choose NotebookLM only when Google-hosted AI research features are worth the cloud-upload and browser-automation risk.
- Choose Custom only when the user declares capabilities and auth requirements.

## Risk comparison

| Platform | Privacy | Collaboration | Automation risk | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Obsidian | Highest | Low | Low | Local files, path safety matters. |
| Tencent Docs | Medium | High | Medium | Cloud upload and workspace permissions matter. |
| Feishu Wiki | Medium | High | Medium | Confirm wiki space and node. |
| DingTalk Docs | Medium | High | Medium | Prefer API/connector over browser automation. |
| IMA | Medium | Medium | Medium | Cloud knowledge-base upload. |
| NotebookLM | Lowest | Low | High | Google upload plus browser/OAuth concerns. |
| Custom | Unknown | Unknown | Unknown | Deny undeclared capabilities. |

## Migration guidance

Migrate by copying first. Do not move or delete source data in the same operation. Always validate counts and keep a manifest.
