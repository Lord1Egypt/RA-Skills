# Violation Response Guide

Receiving a policy violation notice is stressful but manageable. The key is to respond quickly, accurately, and with documented evidence of root cause resolution.

---

## Response Timeline

| Hour | Action |
|---|---|
| 0–4h | Read the notice in full. Understand exactly what was flagged. |
| 4–24h | Investigate root cause. Do not submit an appeal without understanding why. |
| 24–48h | Draft appeal with: (1) acknowledgment, (2) root cause, (3) corrective action, (4) preventive measure. |
| 48–72h | Submit appeal via official channel. Document submission date/time. |
| 72h–7d | Follow up if no acknowledgment received. |

---

## Appeal Structure (POA — Plan of Action)

Every effective appeal has four parts:

### 1. Acknowledgment (1–2 sentences)
Acknowledge the specific violation without being defensive. "We acknowledge that listing [ASIN/SKU] contained a health claim that violated Amazon's supplement claims policy."

### 2. Root Cause (2–3 sentences)
Identify the specific cause. "This occurred because our copywriter was not trained on the updated supplement claims guidelines from [date]. The claim was added during a listing refresh in [month] without a compliance review."

### 3. Corrective Action (what you already did)
"We have already: (1) removed the non-compliant claim from the listing, (2) reviewed all 47 listings in our supplement catalog for similar language, and (3) corrected 3 additional listings that contained similar non-compliant language."

### 4. Preventive Measures (what you'll do to prevent recurrence)
"Going forward: (1) all listing copy must be reviewed against our Compliance Checklist before publishing, (2) our copywriter has completed Amazon's Seller University compliance training, (3) we will conduct a monthly listing compliance audit."

---

## Common Violations and Root Causes

| Violation Type | Common Root Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| Prohibited health claims | Unevidenced efficacy claims in bullets | Remove claim, add disclaimer |
| Image non-compliance | Text overlay on main image | Remove text, resubmit clean image |
| Intellectual property | Stock photo with IP, or competitor brand mention | Replace with original imagery |
| Counterfeit/inauthentic | Unable to provide invoice to platform's satisfaction | Obtain and submit supplier invoice |
| Review manipulation | Asking for positive reviews or offering incentives | Remove the request, train team |
| Keyword misuse | Competitor brand names in backend search terms | Remove all competitor brand terms |

---

## Escalation Path

If initial appeal is denied:
1. Review denial for specific additional documentation requested
2. Supplement appeal with additional evidence (invoices, test results, etc.)
3. Request Account Health Support live chat (Amazon) or dedicated case manager
4. If persistent: contact Seller Support and reference the original case number

---

## Prevention: Monthly Compliance Scan

To prevent violations before they occur:
- [ ] Review all listings against current prohibited claims list (monthly)
- [ ] Check all main images for text overlay or non-white background
- [ ] Scan backend keywords for competitor brand names
- [ ] Review any new product additions against category-specific requirements
- [ ] Check platform policy newsletter for policy changes
