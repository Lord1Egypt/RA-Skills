## Key Principles

### 1. The Hard Problem Is Not a Science Problem
It's not that we need better brain scanners. It's that the very framework of objective third-person science may be unable to account for first-person subjective experience. Consciousness may require a new kind of science — one that integrates the first-person perspective.

### 2. The Explanatory Gap Is a Real Discovery
The gap between brain activity and experience is not a failure of neuroscience — it's information about the structure of reality. Something is missing from our current models. That gap IS the discovery.

### 3. Correlation Is Not Causal Explanation
We can map every NCC and still not know why brain activity produces experience. This is not a temporary limitation — it's a category error to think correlation alone will bridge the gap.

### 4. The Zombie Argument Is Not About Zombies
The philosophical zombie is a logical thought experiment testing whether consciousness is physically necessary. If it's conceivable, physicalism is incomplete. Debate continues on whether conceivability implies possibility.

### 5. Different Theories Capture Different Aspects
IIT captures the integration aspect. GWS captures the access/broadcast aspect. HOT captures the reflection aspect. No single theory captures the full phenomenon.

### 6. Consciousness May Be Fundamental
Like mass, charge, and spacetime in physics — consciousness might be a fundamental feature of reality. Panpsychism takes this seriously.

### 7. The Mystery Is Generative
It pushes us to think differently about the nature of reality, the limits of science, and the relationship between mind and world.
