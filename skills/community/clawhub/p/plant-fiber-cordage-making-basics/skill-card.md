## Description: <br>
This skill guides users through making plant-fiber cordage by having an agent support plant identification, yield and strength calculations, retting and drying schedules, quality logs, safety checks, and reusable templates while the human performs all physical handling and testing. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill to plan, document, and safety-check low-tech cordage production from local plant fibers. The agent provides identification support, calculations, schedules, logs, and templates while the user performs harvesting, retting, twisting, and load testing. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may overtrust handmade plant cordage for life-safety, rescue, towing, structural, or animal-restraint situations. <br>
Mitigation: Use certified commercial rope for any use where rope failure could injure someone, and limit this skill to educational, repair, craft, or non-critical cordage use. <br>
Risk: Plant photos and approximate location details may reveal sensitive personal or site information. <br>
Mitigation: Share only the minimum plant imagery and location context needed for identification, and avoid including private addresses, faces, or unrelated surroundings. <br>
Risk: Incorrect plant identification or handling can expose users to toxic lookalikes, skin irritants, polluted sites, or unsafe processing conditions. <br>
Mitigation: Follow the skill's halt-and-verify workflow for uncertain identification, use gloves and patch tests, avoid polluted or chemically treated harvest areas, and stop if rash or respiratory symptoms occur. <br>
