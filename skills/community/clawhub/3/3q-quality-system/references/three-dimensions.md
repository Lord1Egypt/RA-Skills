# Three-Dimensional Expansion (三维扩展)

> The Six Questions check **quality**. The Three Dimensions check **scope** — making sure you're solving at the right level, across the right time frame, and within the right competitive landscape.

---

## 1. Vertical Dimension (阶维) — Level Matching

Attached to: **Logic Q**

**Core insight**: Execution-level problems get execution-level solutions. Don't upgrade a tactical issue to a strategic one, and don't apply strategy-level thinking to a procedural bug.

### Common mismatches

| Problem level | Right fix | ❌ Wrong escalation |
|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Execution** (code bug / process hiccup) | Fix the bug / optimize the flow | "We need to restructure the org" |
| **Tactical** (campaign underperforming) | Tweak channels / messaging | "We need a new 3-year vision" |
| **Strategic** (market shrinking) | Evaluate new sectors / pivot | "Hire more people to optimize existing process" |

### In practice

- **Investing**: A losing trade → is it execution (stop-loss too tight / position calc wrong) or strategy (stock selection logic itself is flawed)? Don't overhaul the system for an execution mistake.
- **Product**: Low conversion → is it UI (button color / copy) or product positioning? Don't do a major redesign for a micro-optimization.
- **Writing**: Bad article performance → is it the headline (execution) or topic direction (tactical)?

---

## 2. Temporal Dimension (纵维) — Time Evolution

Attached to: **User Q**

**Core insight**: A user need isn't a static thing. It has a past, present, and future trajectory. Fixing only the current snapshot misses the real problem.

### The three time horizons

| Horizon | Question | Why |
|---------|----------|-----|
| **Past** (Source) | Where did this need come from? | Was it always there? Did something trigger it? |
| **Present** (State) | What does the need look like right now? | Is it growing, stable, or fading? |
| **Future** (Trend) | Where is it heading? | Will this need still exist in 3/6/12 months? |

### In practice

- **Feature request**: User asks for X. Temporal check → is this a one-time pain (fix it fast) or a structural shift (build properly)? Past: what triggered it? Future: will it still matter next quarter?
- **Market analysis**: A sector is hot right now. Temporal check → is this a cycle (go up, go down) or a permanent shift? Past: what catalyzed it? Future: what would reverse it?
- **Career / skill**: Learning something new. Temporal check → will this skill be more or less valuable in 2 years? Past: what made it valuable in the first place?

---

## 3. Horizontal Dimension (横维) — Niche Positioning

Attached to: **Competition Q**

**Core insight**: You don't compete with everyone. You compete within a specific niche. Direct competitors, indirect competitors, substitute competitors — each demands a different differentiation strategy.

### The competitive landscape

| Type | Who | Strategy |
|------|-----|----------|
| **Direct** | Same solution, same audience | Out-execute or out-feature |
| **Indirect** | Different solution, same need | Own the positioning or the relationship |
| **Substitute** | Different need entirely, but same budget / attention | Expand the pie or pivot |

### Finding your differentiation fulcrum

1. List all three competitor types for your context
2. For each: what are they best at? What are they bad at?
3. Your differentiation = bridge the gap between what they're bad at and what your audience actually needs
