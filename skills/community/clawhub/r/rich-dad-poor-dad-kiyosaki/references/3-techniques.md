> Source: Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki, Chapters 4-6, 8-9

# Techniques: Building Your Asset Column

## The Asset Builder's Checklist

Start building your asset column today:

1. **Set aside 10-30% of every paycheck** for your asset column before paying any bills
2. **Create a balance sheet**: list everything you own, classified as asset (generates income) or liability (consumes income)
3. **Identify one liability to eliminate** this quarter
4. **Identify one asset to acquire** this quarter
5. **Track your passive income monthly** — it should grow every quarter

## The Four Types of Assets to Acquire

1. **Businesses that don't require your presence** — You own the system; you don't need to work in it
2. **Real estate** — Rental properties that generate monthly cash flow
3. **Paper assets** — Stocks, bonds, notes
4. **Intellectual property** — Books, patents, content that generates royalties

> **Case: The real estate beginner** (Chapter 8): Kiyosaki's first real estate investment was a small house. He bought it for $18,000 with no money down (seller financing), fixed it up over the weekend, and sold it for $48,000. The lesson: "Start small. Start with what you have. Start where you are. The first deal is the hardest."
> **Key takeaway**: Don't wait until you have "enough money" to start investing. Begin with what you have.

## The "Pay Yourself First" System

A practical method for building financial discipline:

1. **Automated investing**: Set up an automatic transfer from your paycheck to your investment account before you see the money
2. **The minimum payment rule**: Pay only the minimum on your bills so you have surplus to invest
3. **The challenge of discipline**: When the pressure is on (and it will be), don't dip into your investment pool — find another way to pay the bills

**The psychological trick**: When you feel the pain of paying yourself first (when bills are due and your investment account is growing), the pain reminds you you're doing something different from the crowd.

## The 5 Steps to Building Financial Genius

1. **Find a deal that no one else sees** — Look where others aren't looking (distressed properties, niche markets)
2. **Raise the money** — Learn to raise capital from others (partners, private lenders)
3. **Find great people** — Smart partners are more valuable than great deals
4. **Structure the deal** — Understand legal and financial structures
5. **Manage the cash flow** — Once the deal is running, manage it well

> **Case: The $10,000 deal** (Chapter 6): Kiyosaki found a small investment opportunity that required $10,000. He had $2,000. He found a partner who had $8,000 and was willing to invest if Kiyosaki did the work. "I made $20,000 on that deal. It wasn't the amount that mattered — it was the lesson: you don't need all the money. You need the financial intelligence to structure the deal."

## The Corporate Structure Advantage

Set up the right entity for your investment activities:

| Structure | Tax Treatment | Liability Protection |
|---|---|---|
| Employee (W-2) | Taxed first, spend what's left | Limited, employer-dependent |
| Self-Employed (Sole Prop) | Report on Schedule C | Personal liability |
| LLC/S-Corp/Corporation | Expense before tax | Limited liability |

**The strategy**: Earn through a corporate entity, expense business-justified costs before paying yourself, and invest the surplus through the corporation.
