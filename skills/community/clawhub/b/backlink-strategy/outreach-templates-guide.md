# Outreach Templates Guide — Backlink Strategy

Comprehensive outreach email templates for every major link-building tactic. Each template includes a personalization framework, subject line variants, and follow-up sequences.

## Template Design Principles

Before using any template, internalize these principles:

1. **Lead with value, not with ask**: The first sentence should be about them, not you
2. **Be specific**: Generic compliments ("I love your blog!") signal mass outreach
3. **Keep it short**: 5-7 sentences max for initial outreach. Busy people skim
4. **One clear CTA**: Don't give multiple options — ask for one specific thing
5. **Sound human**: Read your email aloud. If it sounds like a robot wrote it, rewrite it

## Guest Post Outreach

### Template GP-1: The Idea Pitch
**Best for**: Blogs that accept guest contributors and have a clear editorial voice

**Subject line options**:
- Quick idea for [Blog Name] readers
- [Specific topic] piece for [Blog Name]?
- Contributor pitch: [Topic angle]

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

I've been following [Blog Name] for a while — your piece on [specific article title] was especially useful. The section on [specific point] changed how I think about [topic].

I'm [Your Name], [brief credential — founder of X / specialist in Y]. I'd love to contribute a piece on [proposed topic] that I think would resonate with your audience.

Here's the angle: [2-3 sentence outline of what the post would cover and why it's timely/useful].

I've previously written for [1-2 relevant publications if applicable]. Happy to send an outline or draft first — whatever works best for your process.

Best,
[Name]

**Follow-up 1 (Day 4-5)**:
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [First Name], just floating this to the top in case it got buried. The [topic] piece I pitched would cover [one key takeaway] — something I haven't seen covered well elsewhere. Worth a look?

**Follow-up 2 (Day 9-10)**:
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [First Name], last ping on this — I know you're busy. If [topic] isn't a fit right now, I have a couple of other angles that might work better:
- [Alternative topic 1]
- [Alternative topic 2]

Either way, no worries. Keep up the great work on [Blog Name].

### Template GP-2: The Data-Led Pitch
**Best for**: Publications that favor research-backed content

**Subject line**: I have data your readers haven't seen — [topic]

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

We recently analyzed [data source — e.g., "12 months of transaction data from 500+ ecommerce stores"] and found some surprising patterns around [topic]. For example, [one specific, compelling finding].

I'd love to write this up as a contributor piece for [Publication]. The data is proprietary and hasn't been published anywhere else.

The piece would cover:
- [Finding 1 with specific number]
- [Finding 2 with specific number]
- Practical takeaways for [audience]

Interested? I can have a draft ready within [timeframe].

[Name]

## Product Review / Seeding Outreach

### Template PR-1: The No-Strings Review
**Best for**: Bloggers and YouTubers who do honest product reviews

**Subject line**: Would you review [Product] for your audience?

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

I watched your [specific video/read your review] of [competitor or similar product] — your take on [specific point] was refreshingly honest, and that's exactly why I'm reaching out.

I'm with [Brand], and we make [product brief — one sentence]. I'd love to send you [product] for an honest review — no strings attached. If you don't like it, say so. Your credibility matters more to us than a puff piece.

Would you be interested? I can ship [product] this week with full product specs and ingredient/material details.

Best,
[Name]

## Resource Page Outreach

### Template RP-1: The Helpful Suggestion
**Best for**: Actively maintained resource pages in your niche

**Subject line**: Suggestion for your [topic] resource page

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

I was using your [specific resource page title] to research [topic] — it's one of the most comprehensive lists I've found. Really useful.

I noticed you don't have anything covering [specific subtopic]. We recently published [your resource title] that goes deep on [what it covers]. Here's the link: [URL]

If you think it'd be a good fit for the page, I'd be happy to write a short blurb/description to make it easy to add.

Thanks for maintaining such a great resource.
[Name]

## Broken Link Building

### Template BL-1: The Helpful Alert
**Best for**: Sites with broken outbound links to resources you can replace

**Subject line**: Broken link on your [page topic] page

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

I was reading your post on [article title] and noticed the link to [describe the broken resource — e.g., "the 2023 industry benchmarks from CompanyX"] appears to be broken — it's returning a 404.

We actually have a similar resource that covers [topic]: [Your URL]. It was published [recently/this year] and includes [brief description of what makes it useful].

If you'd like to swap it in, here's the exact section where the broken link appears: [quote a few words around the link]. No worries either way — just wanted to flag it.

Best,
[Name]

## Supplier / Manufacturer Links

### Template SL-1: The Partnership Ask
**Best for**: Suppliers, manufacturers, and distributors you have a buying relationship with

**Subject line**: Adding [Your Brand] to your retailer/partner page?

**Body**:
Hi [First Name],

We've been sourcing [product/ingredient/component] from [Company Name] for [duration] now and have been really happy with the quality.

I noticed you maintain a [retailers/partners/where to buy] page on your website. Would you be open to adding [Your Brand] as a listed retailer? I can send over our logo, a short description, and our URL to make it easy.

Happy to reciprocate with a mention of [Company Name] as a trusted supplier on our sourcing page as well.

Thanks,
[Name]

- [Data point 1]
- [Data point 2]
- [Data point 3]

I can share the full dataset, methodology, and expert commentary if you'd like to cover this. We can also provide custom cuts of the data for specific angles.

Best,
[Name]

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending any outreach email, verify you've included:

- [ ] Recipient's first name (correctly spelled)
- [ ] Reference to a specific piece of their content (with detail that proves you read it)
- [ ] Why your resource/pitch is relevant to their specific audience
- [ ] A clear, single call-to-action
- [ ] Your real name and brief credential
- [ ] No more than one link in the initial email
- [ ] Subject line that's specific, not generic
- [ ] Email length under 150 words
- [ ] Proofread for typos and formatting
