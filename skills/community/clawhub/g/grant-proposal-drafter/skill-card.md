## Description: <br>
Use this skill when a nonprofit grant writer, program manager, or development director needs to turn a funder RFP or NOFO into a structured grant proposal. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[archlab-space](https://clawhub.ai/user/archlab-space) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External nonprofit grant writers, program managers, and development directors use this skill to transform funder RFPs, NOFOs, or LOI invitations into structured proposal drafts with eligibility checks, requirement traceability, and unresolved-information flags. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Grant drafts can include sensitive funder, donor, financial, personnel, or unpublished program information. <br>
Mitigation: Share only the information needed for the draft and keep confidential organizational data within the active session. <br>
Risk: A proposal may be submitted with unverified eligibility, budget figures, citations, or funder-specific requirements. <br>
Mitigation: Manually verify eligibility, budget totals, citations, attachments, deadlines, and final submission instructions before filing. <br>
Risk: The agent could produce plausible narrative where required organizational facts or evidence are missing. <br>
Mitigation: Use the skill's unresolved-information flags and citation markers to block unsupported claims until a human supplies or verifies the missing details. <br>
