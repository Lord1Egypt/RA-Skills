# StoryScope AI Writing Tells

Source noted by Leo: StoryScope, Russell et al. (2026), arXiv:2604.03136.

Study context as stored in memory:

- Same writing prompts given to humans and five AI model families/models: Claude, GPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, Kimi.
- Total corpus: 61,608 stories.
- Researchers stripped surface features such as word choice, sentence length, punctuation, em dashes, and obvious contrast formulas.
- Human vs AI writing remained detectable 93% of the time.
- Even after obvious AI giveaways were cleaned from AI stories, the detector still caught them.

## Core Interpretation

The real AI-writing smell lives one level below words. It is in narrative choices:

- how the piece builds
- where it places emotion
- what it chooses to explain
- how cleanly it closes
- whether it allows mess, side threads, contradiction, direct address, and real-world specificity

## AI Does More Often

- Spells out the moral: `AI / Human: 77% / 52%`
- Describes feelings as body stuff: `81% / 38%`
- Talks about smells a lot: `82% / 57%`
- Makes characters sound like philosophers: `59% / 34%`
- Keeps one neat storyline: `79% / 57%`
- Has the hero drive the ending: `69% / 46%`
- Wraps up calm and happy: `47% / 27%`
- Describes looks first: `52% / 30%`

## Humans Do More Often

- Talk straight to the reader: `Human / AI: 28% / 7%`
- Just say the feeling: `29% / 8%`
- Name real things: `47% / 24%`
- Write flawed people: `59% / 38%`
- Run a second storyline: `42% / 21%`

## Practical Rewrite Moves

- Replace a stated moral with a concrete consequence.
- Replace body-emotion packaging with plain judgement or actual context.
- Replace generic nouns with real tools, companies, products, places, dates, and numbers.
- Break suspiciously clean arcs with a side thread or honest caveat.
- Let an ending land with the real unresolved tension instead of a tidy lesson.
- Keep direct address if it sounds like a person talking to another person.

## Source-Voice Application

When writing in a specific person's voice, preserve:

- rough human cadence
- dry operator humour
- concrete technical detail
- blunt security/production/business judgement
- real names and references
- occasional awkwardness when it is honest
- deliberate typos or rough edges when the writer chooses them

Avoid:

- polished motivational closure
- generic content-marketing rhythm
- fake-clever openers
- over-explained lessons
- symmetrical contrast formulas
- smoothing the piece until it sounds like everyone else
