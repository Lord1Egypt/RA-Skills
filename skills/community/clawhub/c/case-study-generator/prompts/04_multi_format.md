# Prompt 4: Multi-Format Package

## Purpose
One case study should become four pieces of content. This prompt packages everything into the formats your team actually uses: a blog post for SEO, a one-pager for sales, a LinkedIn story for social, and an email snippet for outreach sequences. Write once, publish everywhere.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a B2B content strategist and copywriter. Using the case study below, produce all four output formats.

CASE STUDY SUMMARY (paste output from Prompt 1 + 2 here, or provide the key details):
- Customer company/industry: [COMPANY, INDUSTRY]
- Core problem: [PROBLEM]  
- Solution: [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Key result: [HEADLINE METRIC]
- Secondary results: [OTHER METRICS]
- Timeline: [TIMEFRAME TO RESULTS]
- Best quote: [STRONGEST PULL QUOTE FROM PROMPT 3]

---

### OUTPUT 1: SEO Blog Post (600–800 words)

Write a blog post optimized for search, designed to rank for "[industry] + [problem type] + solution" queries.

Structure:
- **H1 Title:** How [Customer] [Achieved Outcome] with [Your Solution] — include primary keyword
- **Introduction (100 words):** Open with the reader's pain point (not the customer). Hook with a specific number.
- **Customer background (50 words):** Brief context. 2–3 sentences.
- **The challenge (150 words):** Describe the problem. Use industry-specific language.
- **The solution (150 words):** How [your product] addressed it. Avoid jargon.
- **The results (200 words):** Metrics-first. ROI callout box (3 stats). Quote.
- **Takeaway for readers (100 words):** What can YOUR reader do with this? 2–3 actionable lessons.
- **CTA (50 words):** Soft CTA — invite readers to "see if this applies to your situation."
- **Meta description (150 chars):** Include primary keyword + the headline result.

---

### OUTPUT 2: Sales One-Pager (400 words, PDF-ready layout)

Write a concise one-pager structured for B2B sales conversations and procurement review.

Structure:
**[COMPANY LOGO PLACEHOLDER]**

**Headline:** [Customer Company] + [Outcome in one line]

**The Challenge** (50 words): 3 bullet points describing the pre-solution state.

**The Solution** (50 words): 3 bullet points on what was implemented and how.

**The Results** (100 words): 
- Stat callout 1: [NUMBER] — [What it means]
- Stat callout 2: [NUMBER] — [What it means]  
- Stat callout 3: [NUMBER] — [What it means]
- ROI: [X]x return in [TIMEFRAME]

**In Their Own Words** (50 words): Best quote from Prompt 3, Results Quote.

**About [Your Company]** (50 words): 3-sentence company description. Who you serve, what you do, differentiator.

**Contact/CTA** (30 words): Website, contact email, and one-sentence call to action.

Footer: "[Customer Company] Case Study | [Your Company Name] | [Year]"

---

### OUTPUT 3: LinkedIn Post (300–400 words)

Write a LinkedIn post in the "story hook" format — designed for shares and saves, not just likes.

Structure:
- **Line 1 (hook):** Single sentence. Make it stop the scroll. A surprising number or a bold claim.
- **Lines 2–3:** The problem context. Empathize with the reader who has the same situation.
- **Lines 4–8:** The story. Tell it in short paragraphs. Build tension — what was failing, what changed, what happened first.
- **Lines 9–12:** The results. Specific numbers. The quote. The downstream impact.
- **Lines 13–15:** The lesson. What can YOUR reader do with this? Make it about them, not your product.
- **CTA:** 1 sentence. "Comment [word] if you want the full case study" or "Link in first comment."
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 relevant hashtags at the end.

Tone: Personal, direct, story-driven. Not a press release. Write in first person from the author's perspective.

---

### OUTPUT 4: Email Outreach Snippet (150 words)

Write a 3-paragraph cold/warm email snippet referencing this case study. For use in sales sequences or re-engagement campaigns.

Structure:
- **Para 1:** Open with the prospect's likely problem (mirror the case study customer's situation). 1–2 sentences.
- **Para 2:** The case study proof point. Name the customer (or industry + size if anonymized), the result, the timeframe. 2–3 sentences.
- **Para 3:** Soft pivot to them. Is this relevant to their situation? Invite a reply — not a demo. 1–2 sentences.

Tone: Human, direct, no hype. This is a peer-to-peer message, not a marketing blast.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- Run last. Paste the outputs from Prompts 1–3 as the "case study summary" input.
- Blog post: submit to your content team with meta description pre-written.
- One-pager: hand to a designer with this copy — layout should be single page, brand colors, customer logo if permitted.
- LinkedIn post: schedule 3–7 days after the case study blog goes live. Tag the customer company.
- Email snippet: slot into deal-stage sequences after a demo, or in re-engagement campaigns at 30/60/90 days.
