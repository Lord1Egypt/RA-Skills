## 1. One-line premise
An orphaned boy discovers he is a wizard, goes to a hidden boarding school of magic, and learns that the dark wizard who murdered his parents — and once "died" trying to kill him as a baby — is clawing his way back to power, with Harry at the center of the prophecy that decides it.

## 2. World & era
- **Setting:** a hidden magical Britain layered under the ordinary ("Muggle") world; primarily **Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry**.
- **Timeline anchor:** roughly **1991–1998** (Harry's age 11–17, the seven school years).
- **Tone:** starts whimsical/cozy, darkens steadily into war, loss, and moral weight.
- **Geography anchors:** Hogwarts (the four Houses, Great Hall, Forbidden Forest, Quidditch pitch), Diagon Alley, Hogsmeade, the Ministry of Magic, Gringotts, Privet Drive, Grimmauld Place.

## 4. Main plot scaffolding (stages for anchoring)

**Stage 1 · The boy who lived**
- Harry learns he's a wizard, enters Hogwarts, is sorted into Gryffindor, befriends **Ron Weasley** and **Hermione Granger**, and clashes with **Draco Malfoy**.

**Stage 2 · Year-by-year mysteries**
- Each school year centers on a mystery/threat: the Philosopher's Stone, the Chamber of Secrets (basilisk, Tom Riddle's diary), Sirius Black & the Dementors, the Triwizard Tournament (and Voldemort's return), etc.

**Stage 3 · Voldemort returns**
- The Dark Lord **Voldemort** regains a body; the wizarding world denies it at first. The **Order of the Phoenix** (Dumbledore's resistance) reforms; the Ministry is infiltrated.

**Stage 4 · War and Horcruxes**
- Dumbledore reveals Voldemort split his soul into **Horcruxes**; destroying them is the only way to kill him. Harry, Ron, and Hermione hunt them while on the run.

**Stage 5 · The Battle of Hogwarts**
- The final battle at the school; major sacrifices; the prophecy ("neither can live while the other survives") resolves. Love, sacrifice, and choice — not raw power — decide the end.

## 6. Magic / setting anchors
- **Wands, spells, potions** — *Expelliarmus*, *Expecto Patronum*, *Avada Kedavra* (the Unforgivables).
- **Quidditch** — the broomstick sport; Harry as Seeker.
- **Horcruxes** — soul-fragments anchoring Voldemort to life; the core endgame mechanic.
- **The Deathly Hallows** — Elder Wand, Resurrection Stone, Invisibility Cloak.
- **Magical creatures** — basilisk, Dementors, house-elves (Dobby), hippogriffs, Patronuses.

## 7. Theme & "anchor vs. freedom"
- **Core themes:** love and sacrifice over power; the choices that define us more than abilities; prejudice (pure-blood ideology) and resistance; mortality.
- **Canon anchors (stay close):** the four Houses & Hogwarts life, Voldemort's return, the Order vs. Death Eaters, Horcruxes, the prophecy, the Battle of Hogwarts.
- **Free to diverge:** the player need not be Harry — they can be a student of any House, an Order member, or an Auror; relationships, House politics, and the war can branch.

## 8. Notes for YumFu
- Prefer **real wizarding geography / characters / lore** (Hogwarts Houses, Diagon Alley, the Ministry; Dumbledore, Snape, Voldemort) for moves, classes, threats, and NPC pressure.
