# Mental Models

Use these models to sharpen the inversion.

## Inversion

Ask:

- What would guarantee failure?
- What would make this embarrassing?
- What would make customers leave?
- What would make the economics ugly?
- What would make this not worth doing even if it works?

## Incentives

Ask:

- Who gets rewarded?
- Who pays?
- Who bears the risk?
- Who has to change behavior?
- Who loses if this works?

Bad incentives beat good intentions.

## Opportunity Cost

Ask:

- What does this prevent the user from doing?
- Is this the best use of scarce time, cash, attention, or reputation?
- Is there a faster path to the same outcome?

## Circle Of Competence

Ask:

- Does the user understand the buyer?
- Does the user understand the sales channel?
- Does the user understand the operations?
- Is the hard part inside or outside their competence?

## Margin Of Safety

Ask:

- Can the idea survive weak conversion?
- Can it survive slower sales?
- Can it survive a competitor copying it?
- Can it survive the founder having less time than expected?

## Second-Order Effects

Ask:

- If this works, what problem does it create next?
- Does growth increase complexity?
- Does more demand make the product better or worse?
- Does the first win create future options?

## Reversibility

Ask:

- Can the user test this cheaply?
- Can they stop without damage?
- What commitments become hard to unwind?
- Is the test reversible but the full bet irreversible?
