# Case Brief Drafter

**Domain:** Legal · Legal Research & Writing
**Platforms:** Claude · Codex

## Purpose

Turns a single judicial opinion into a structured, IRAC-format case brief: facts, procedural posture, issue, rule, application, holding, disposition, and concurrence/dissent summary. Built for law students, paralegals, and junior associates who need consistent, accurate briefs for class, research memos, moot court, or matter reading.

## When to Use

- A 1L or 2L preparing for class on a casebook opinion
- A paralegal building a case-law packet for a supervising attorney
- A junior associate briefing a precedent for a memo or motion
- A student preparing moot court arguments and counter-citations

## What It Does

1. Collects the case name, citation, opinion text, and the user's intended use through one-question-at-a-time intake
2. Confirms a short source summary with the user before drafting
3. Drafts ten labeled sections: citation block, facts, procedural posture, issue(s), rule(s), application, holding, disposition, separate opinions, and use-specific notes
4. Runs a gap and accuracy check covering missing citations, holding bleed, dicta-as-holding errors, quotation accuracy, and fact invention
5. Flags any unresolved information the user must supply or verify before relying on the brief

## Notes

This skill produces a **study/work aid**, not legal advice. Every quotation must trace to the supplied opinion text, and the brief should be verified against the official reporter before reliance. Names and identifiers from any client matter should be redacted by the user before pasting opinion excerpts or facts.
