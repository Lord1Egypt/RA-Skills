# Chronic kidney disease

Use this reference for CKD/ERC staging, albuminuria, risk stratification, progression, nephroprotection, anemia, mineral and bone disorder, metabolic acidosis, diet, cardiovascular risk, and preparation for kidney replacement therapy.

## Core approach

1. Stage by eGFR category and albuminuria category, not creatinine alone.
2. Ask for baseline creatinine/eGFR, trend, urine ACR or PCR, blood pressure, diabetes status, cardiovascular disease, medications, potassium, bicarbonate, calcium, phosphate, PTH, hemoglobin, iron indices, and urinalysis/sediment.
3. Identify reversible accelerants: NSAIDs, contrast, obstruction, volume depletion, uncontrolled BP, hyperglycemia, nephrotoxins, recurrent AKI, and active glomerular disease.
4. Discuss kidney-protective therapy at a conceptual level: BP control, RAAS blockade when appropriate, SGLT2 inhibitors when indicated, glycemic control, lifestyle, sodium restriction, avoiding nephrotoxins, and cardiovascular prevention.
5. Address complications: anemia, CKD-MBD, acidosis, hyperkalemia, malnutrition, volume overload, pruritus, and medication dose adjustment.
6. Mention timely education and planning for dialysis, transplant, or conservative kidney management when advanced CKD is present.

## Sources

Prefer KDIGO CKD, KDIGO diabetes in CKD, KDIGO BP in CKD, KDOQI, and major nephrology society guidance.
