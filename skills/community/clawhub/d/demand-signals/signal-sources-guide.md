# Signal Sources Guide

This guide covers the primary sources for detecting early demand signals, how to set up monitoring for each, what data to extract, and how to interpret the signals correctly. Sources are organized by category, with practical setup instructions and known limitations for each.

---

## Social Media Platforms

### TikTok

**What to monitor:**
- Trending hashtags in product-relevant categories (e.g., #TikTokMadeMeBuyIt, #AmazonFinds, #HomeDecor, #KitchenHacks, category-specific tags)
- Creator adoption velocity -- how many independent creators are posting about a product per week
- Comment sentiment -- ratio of "where to buy" comments versus passive entertainment reactions
- Sound/audio reuse patterns -- products associated with trending sounds get amplified by the algorithm

**How to set up monitoring:**
- Follow 20-30 product discovery accounts and hashtags in your target categories.
- Check the TikTok Creative Center (ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter) for trending hashtags and keywords. This is a free tool that shows trending topics with growth metrics.
- Use TikTok's search bar to check specific product keywords weekly. Sort by "This week" to see recent content velocity.
- Set up Google Alerts for "[product category] TikTok viral" to catch cross-platform coverage of TikTok trends.

**Signal interpretation:**
- A single viral video (even 10M+ views) from a major influencer is a weaker signal than 10+ videos from smaller creators with combined views of 2M. Distributed organic adoption indicates genuine consumer interest rather than one-time algorithmic amplification.
- Videos showing actual product use and unboxing carry stronger purchase intent signals than reaction or comedy content that happens to feature a product.
- Comment volume matters less than comment content. Analyze the top 20-30 comments for purchase intent language.

**Limitations:**
- TikTok's algorithm can create artificial-looking trend patterns. A product can appear to trend for a week due to algorithmic clustering and then disappear entirely.
- View counts are unreliable as a demand signal in isolation. TikTok inflates view counts for entertainment content that may have zero commercial intent.
- Regional TikTok trends may not translate across markets. A product trending on Australian TikTok may not gain traction in the US market.

### Instagram and Pinterest

**What to monitor (Instagram):**
- Reels engagement rates for product-related content compared to account baselines
- Hashtag growth velocity for product-specific tags
- Shopping tag adoption -- are creators tagging products for direct purchase?
- Story mention volume for product keywords

**What to monitor (Pinterest):**
- Pinterest Trends (trends.pinterest.com) for keyword search volume and growth rates
- Pin save-to-impression ratio for product-related content -- high save rates indicate purchase intent
- Board creation patterns -- new boards being created around a product category signal planning-stage intent

**Signal interpretation:**
- Instagram signals tend to lag TikTok by 2-4 weeks for viral product trends. If a product is trending on both platforms simultaneously, the trend is likely further along than the Instagram data alone would suggest.
- Pinterest signals are strong purchase intent indicators because users save content specifically for future reference and buying. A product trending on Pinterest with high save rates is a more reliable demand signal than equivalent engagement on TikTok.
- Pinterest trends are particularly valuable for seasonal and home/lifestyle categories where consumers plan purchases weeks or months in advance.

### Reddit and Online Communities

**What to monitor:**
- Product recommendation threads in relevant subreddits (r/BuyItForLife, r/shutupandtakemymoney, r/AmazonTopRated, plus category-specific subreddits)
- Upvote-to-comment ratios on product posts -- high ratios indicate broad agreement rather than controversial discussion
- "What's the best X" threads where specific products are repeatedly recommended
- Cross-posting patterns -- a product discussed in multiple unrelated subreddits simultaneously

**Signal interpretation:**
- Reddit signals are particularly valuable for durability and quality-focused products. Reddit communities tend to be skeptical of marketing, so organic recommendations carry high credibility.
- A product that appears in both Reddit recommendations and TikTok virality is a strong dual signal -- it has both social proof and mass-market appeal.
- Reddit signals often emerge earlier than marketplace signals for niche products, making it a useful leading indicator.

## Search Data

### Google Trends

**What to monitor:**
- Relative search interest for product keywords over 90-day and 12-month windows
- "Breakout" and "Rising" related queries -- these are terms growing more than 200% and 50% respectively
- Geographic distribution of search interest -- look for demand spreading from initial markets to broader regions
- Year-over-year comparison for the same time period to distinguish new trends from seasonal recurrence

**How to set up monitoring:**
- Bookmark Google Trends comparison views for your top 10 product category keywords.
- Set the date range to "Past 12 months" and compare against "Previous period" to see year-over-year changes.
- Check related queries weekly. The "Rising" queries section often reveals product variants or adjacent opportunities.
- Use the geographic breakdown to identify whether demand is concentrated or broadly distributed.

**Signal interpretation:**
- Google Trends shows relative interest, not absolute volume. A keyword at 100 might represent 1,000 searches or 1,000,000 searches. Always cross-reference with Google Ads Keyword Planner for absolute volume estimates.
- A steady climb over 3-4 weeks is a more reliable signal than a spike. Spikes often correspond to news events or viral moments that do not sustain.
- Comparing related queries across time periods reveals whether consumer intent is shifting. For example, if "product name review" is rising faster than "product name," consumers are in the evaluation phase, which precedes purchasing.

### Google Ads Keyword Planner

**What to monitor:**
- Absolute monthly search volume estimates for product keywords
- Suggested bid (CPC) trends -- rising CPCs indicate that sellers see commercial opportunity in the keyword
- Competition level changes from Low to Medium to High over time
- New keyword suggestions that appear in the planner that were not present previously

**How to set up monitoring:**
- Access through a Google Ads account (a free account with no active campaigns is sufficient).
- Run keyword discovery reports monthly for your product categories. Save reports to track changes over time.
- Focus on the "three-month change" and "year-over-year change" columns for trend detection.

**Limitations:**
- Keyword Planner groups similar keywords and provides ranges rather than precise numbers. Use it for directional sizing, not exact forecasting.
- Data updates monthly, not in real time. For fast-moving trends, Google Trends provides more timely directional data.

## Marketplace Data

### Amazon

**What to monitor:**
- Movers and Shakers list (top sales rank gainers in the past 24 hours) -- check daily for your target categories
- New Releases list -- products gaining immediate traction after launch
- Best Sellers Rank (BSR) movement for specific ASINs tracked over time
- Search result count for product keywords -- a growing number of listings indicates seller awareness of the opportunity
- Amazon Brand Analytics Search Query Performance (if you have Brand Registry access) -- shows actual search frequency rank for terms

**Tools for Amazon data:**
- Keepa (keepa.com) -- tracks price and BSR history for any ASIN. The BSR chart reveals whether a product's sales rank improvement is sustained or temporary. Free tier available; paid tier provides more data.
- Jungle Scout or Helium 10 -- provide estimated monthly sales, revenue, and category-level data. Paid subscriptions required but essential for serious demand sizing.
- Amazon's own "Product Opportunity Explorer" (in Seller Central) -- shows search volume, click-through rates, and conversion data for product niches. Free for registered sellers.

**Signal interpretation:**
- A BSR improvement from 50,000 to 5,000 in a category is a much stronger signal than an improvement from 500 to 200. The former represents a 10x volume increase; the latter might be a 2x increase.
- Track the number of reviews being added to top listings weekly. A sudden increase in review velocity suggests increased sales volume (reviews typically represent 1-3% of purchases).
- A growing number of listings for a product keyword is a lagging indicator -- it tells you other sellers have already identified the opportunity. By the time there are 100+ listings, you are not early.

### AliExpress and 1688.com

**What to monitor:**
- AliExpress trending products and top seller lists in your categories
- 1688.com (Chinese domestic marketplace) order volumes -- high domestic order volumes for a product often precede international trend adoption by 4-8 weeks
- Supplier "ready to ship" inventory levels -- suppliers building stock in advance signal anticipated demand
- New product listings from established suppliers -- when factories create new product lines, they are responding to order inquiries from buyers

**Signal interpretation:**
- AliExpress dropshipping trends are a leading indicator for Amazon and DTC product opportunities. Products that sell well via dropshipping at higher prices validate consumer willingness to pay, and the arbitrage opportunity attracts private-label sellers.
- 1688.com data is particularly valuable because it shows what Chinese manufacturers are producing at scale. A product with high order volumes on 1688 but limited presence on Western marketplaces represents a potential sourcing opportunity.

## Supplier and Industry Data

### Direct Supplier Intelligence

**What to monitor:**
- Order volume reports from your existing suppliers. Ask directly: "What products are your other clients ordering more of this season?"
- Trade show product highlights and "new product" showcases from Canton Fair, Global Sources, and category-specific trade shows.
- Supplier newsletters and catalogs -- new product additions reflect what suppliers expect to sell.
- Raw material price changes. Rising prices for specific materials (e.g., bamboo fiber, silicone, a specific type of LED) often indicate growing demand for products made from those materials.

**How to set up monitoring:**
- Maintain relationships with 3-5 suppliers in your product categories, even if you are not actively ordering. Quarterly check-in emails asking about trending products yield valuable intelligence.
- Subscribe to trade show recap newsletters from sourcing publications.
- Join supplier communities on WeChat or WhatsApp groups where factory representatives share new product information.

### Industry Reports and News

**What to monitor:**
- Category-specific trade publications (e.g., Home Textiles Today, Furniture Today, Consumer Electronics Daily)
- NRF (National Retail Federation) reports on consumer spending trends
- Euromonitor, Statista, and IBISWorld for category growth rates and projections
- Retail earnings call transcripts -- when major retailers mention category growth, it confirms demand at scale

**Signal interpretation:**
- Industry reports are lagging indicators for trend detection but valuable for demand sizing and market context. Use them to validate and size opportunities identified through faster-moving signals.
- When multiple industry publications cover the same product category trend, it is likely in the late-growth or peak phase. Use this as a timing signal rather than a discovery signal.

## Setting Up a Signal Monitoring Routine

### Daily (15 minutes)

- Scan TikTok trending content in your product categories
- Check Amazon Movers and Shakers for your target categories
- Review Google Alerts for product category keywords

### Weekly (45 minutes)

- Run Google Trends comparison reports for your top 10 product keywords
- Check Pinterest Trends for category keywords
- Review Reddit product recommendation threads in your target subreddits
- Count new Amazon listings for products on your watchlist

### Monthly (2 hours)

- Run Google Ads Keyword Planner reports and compare to prior month
- Contact key suppliers for order trend updates
- Review AliExpress and 1688.com trending products
- Update your signal tracking spreadsheet with all new data points
- Conduct a full validation review on any signals that have persisted for 3+ weeks

### Quarterly (half day)

- Review the past quarter's signal performance -- which signals converted to successful products, which were false positives
- Update your source reliability ratings based on track record
- Add or remove monitoring sources based on category relevance changes
- Recalibrate your signal strength thresholds based on actual outcomes
