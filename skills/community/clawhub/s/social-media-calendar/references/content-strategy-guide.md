# Content Strategy Guide for Ecommerce Social Media

Strategic foundation for building effective social media calendars: content pillars, platform best practices, timing, and seasonal planning.

---

## Content Pillar Framework

### Pillar 1: Educational / Value-Add (20-25% of content)
Positions the brand as an authority. Builds trust before asking for a purchase.

**Ideas**: How-to tutorials, comparison guides, myth-busting, ingredient/material breakdowns, care instructions, FAQ compilations.
**Best Formats**: Carousels, short-form video tutorials, infographic Pins, X threads.

### Pillar 2: Entertaining (15-20%)
Drives shares and introduces the brand to new audiences through humor and trend participation.

**Ideas**: Niche memes, trending audio Reels/TikToks, satisfying process videos, "expectation vs. reality," interactive challenges.
**Best Formats**: Reels (15-30s), TikTok, Stories with stickers, meme-format posts.

### Pillar 3: Promotional (15-20%, never exceed 30%)
Directly drives sales. Must be compelling, urgent, and clear.

**Ideas**: New product announcements, sale countdowns, bundle deals, flash sales, free shipping, restock alerts.
**Best Formats**: Feed posts with strong graphics, Stories with countdown stickers, Pins with price overlay, Facebook link posts.

### Pillar 4: Community and UGC (15-20%)
Leverages social proof. Real customers are more trusted than brand messaging.

**Ideas**: Customer photo reposts, review highlights, unboxing videos, testimonial graphics, community milestones, ambassador features.
**Best Formats**: Story reposts, feed compilations, Reel compilations, review carousels.

### Pillar 5: Behind the Scenes (10-15%)
Humanizes the brand and builds emotional connection.

**Ideas**: Production process, team introductions, order packing, design sketches to final product, workspace tours.
**Best Formats**: Reels (30-60s), TikTok, Stories series, time-lapse video.

### Pillar 6: Lifestyle and Inspiration (10-15%)
Creates aspirational associations with products naturally integrated.

**Ideas**: Styled product photography, mood boards, "a day with [product]," seasonal aesthetics, complementary brand collabs.
**Best Formats**: High-quality feed photos, Pinterest Pins, carousel mood boards, cinematic Reels.

## Platform-Specific Best Practices

### Instagram
- **Reels**: Prioritize for reach (algorithm surfaces to non-followers). 3-5/week, 15-45s, trending audio, add subtitles.
- **Carousels**: 1.4x more reach than single images. 7-10 slides with a strong hook on slide 1.
- **Stories**: 3-7/day with interactive stickers (polls, quizzes, sliders) for engagement signals.
- **Shop**: Tag products in every relevant post to reduce purchase friction.
- **Hashtags**: 5-15 per post. Mix branded (1-2), niche 10K-500K (5-8), discovery 500K-5M (3-5). Rotate sets.
- **Frequency**: 4-7 feed posts/week, 3-5 Reels/week, daily Stories.

### TikTok
- **Authenticity**: Raw, genuine content outperforms overproduced videos.
- **Hook in first second**: Text overlay on the opening frame, strong visual or verbal hook.
- **Trending sounds**: Monitor TikTok Creative Center. Adapt trends to the brand, not the brand to trends.
- **TikTok Shop**: Link products directly in videos. Use product showcase features.
- **Comments**: Reply with video responses for engagement flywheel.
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 targeted. One branded, one niche, one trending.
- **Frequency**: 3-7/week. Consistency over volume.

### Facebook
- **Link posts**: Still generate meaningful click-through traffic to product pages and blogs.
- **Native video**: Upload directly (not shared from IG). Under 3 minutes performs best.
- **Groups**: Powerful for community loyalty. Post exclusive content and early access.
- **Hashtags**: 0-3 maximum; minimal impact on Facebook.
- **Frequency**: 3-5/week.

### Pinterest
- **SEO-first**: Pin titles and descriptions need keywords. Use Pinterest search suggest and Trends tool.
- **Vertical format**: 2:3 ratio (1000x1500px). Horizontal images underperform significantly.
- **Text overlay**: Clear, readable text explaining what the viewer will find.
- **Rich Pins**: Enable for products. Auto-syncs price and availability.
- **Idea Pins**: Multi-page format for tutorials and styling guides.
- **Frequency**: 5-15 Pins/day. Steady pinning beats batched bursts.

### X (Twitter)
- **Conversational tone**: Rewards personality, timely commentary, and hot takes.
- **Threads**: Use for detailed product stories or educational content.
- **Visual tweets**: Images or video significantly outperform text-only.
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum. More reduces engagement.
- **Frequency**: 1-3 posts/day. Engagement (replies, quotes) is equally important.

## The 80/20 Value-to-Promotion Ratio

At least 80% of content should provide value without directly selling. No more than 20% should be overtly promotional.

**Why**: Algorithms reward engagement (value content generates saves, shares, comments). Audiences unfollow constant sales pitches. When promotional content appears, it performs better because trust is established.

**During launches/sales**: Temporarily shift to 60/40 or 50/50, then immediately return to normal ratios.

**Blending**: The best promotional posts also deliver value. A "new arrivals" post doubles as a styling guide. A sale announcement includes a curated gift guide.

## Seasonal Planning Methodology

### Quarterly (90 Days Out)
Map all major commercial moments: holidays, launches, sales events, industry moments. Assign a monthly theme (e.g., January = "New Year, New [Category]").

### Monthly (30 Days Out)
Build the detailed weekly calendar. Write briefs for weeks 1-2. Schedule batch production sessions. Confirm influencer timelines.

### Weekly Review (Ongoing)
Every Friday, 15 minutes: Which posts overperformed and why? Which underperformed? Any trending moments for next week? Adjust the upcoming calendar accordingly.

## Trend Integration

**Sources**: TikTok Creative Center, Instagram Explore, Pinterest Trends, Google Trends, competitor accounts.

**Evaluate Before Jumping In**:
1. **Relevance**: Natural connection to the brand?
2. **Timing**: Still rising or already saturated? (Aim for day 1-5 of a trend.)
3. **Audience fit**: Will this specific audience appreciate it?
4. **Brand safety**: Any controversy risk?
5. **Feasibility**: Can the team create quality content within 48-72 hours?

**Rules**: Never force trends. Adapt the trend to the brand. Reserve 1-2 flex slots per week for reactive content. Document what worked.
