# Prompt 3: Reputation & Review Management System

## Use This For
Google Business Profile optimization, review response templates for all departments (sales, service, F&I), review request sequences, and crisis response for negative press or viral complaints.

---

## The Prompt

You are an automotive reputation management specialist. I need a complete review and GBP content system for a car dealer or auto service business.

**Business Details:**
- Business name: [NAME]
- Type: [New car dealer / Used lot / Service shop / Multi-rooftop group]
- Location: [CITY, STATE]
- Departments: [Sales / Service / Finance (F&I) / Parts — check all that apply]
- Brand voice: [e.g., "family-owned, community-focused", "premium luxury positioning", "no-nonsense value"]
- Current review average: [e.g., "4.1 on Google, 3.8 on Yelp" or "unknown"]
- Biggest complaint pattern: [e.g., "wait times", "financing miscommunication", "used car condition disputes"]

**Generate the following assets:**

### 1. Review Response Templates (20 templates)

**5-Star Responses (5 templates)**
- Template A: Sales department compliment (salesperson named)
- Template B: Service department praise (fast turnaround)
- Template C: Finance/F&I department appreciation
- Template D: Overall experience without department mention
- Template E: Repeat customer / referral source

Each response: 60-90 words. Thank by first name, reference specific detail from review, invite return visit or referral. No promotional language ("come see our deals"). NADA guideline: no incentivizing reviews.

**4-Star Responses (3 templates)**
- Template A: Minor gripe acknowledged (wait time, paperwork delay)
- Template B: Slight miscommunication, resolved positively
- Template C: First-time customer, minor learning curve

Each response: 70-100 words. Acknowledge the specific area of 4-star feedback, own it without making excuses, invite the customer to contact you directly.

**3-Star / Mixed Responses (4 templates)**
- Template A: Pricing complaint
- Template B: Service time overrun
- Template C: Feeling pressured (F&I / aftermarket products)
- Template D: Vehicle condition dispute (used car)

Each response: 90-120 words. LAER framework (Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond). Include direct contact (manager name + phone/email). Never argue. Never offer discounts publicly (creates precedent).

**1-2 Star / Negative Responses (8 templates)**
- Template A: Bad trade-in value complaint
- Template B: Bait-and-switch advertising allegation
- Template C: Mechanical issue after purchase
- Template D: Finance rate complaint ("they added products without asking")
- Template E: Service tech error (work done wrong)
- Template F: Warranty/gap dispute
- Template G: Racial/gender discrimination allegation (crisis-level; move to private immediately)
- Template H: Review suspected to be from a competitor or fake (report path + public response)

Each negative response: 100-140 words. Never: admit liability, offer compensation publicly, name-check other customers, reveal private deal details, argue about facts. Always: validate the emotion, offer to resolve privately, include manager direct contact.

### 2. Google Business Profile Optimization

**Business Description** (750 chars max, keyword-rich):
Write for [BUSINESS NAME] at [LOCATION]. Include: primary services, year established (if known), brand affiliations, service hours, financing mention (no specific APR). Keywords: [city] car dealer, [make] dealer [city], auto service [city], certified pre-owned [make] [city].

**30-Day Google Posts Calendar**
One post per day for 30 days. Mix: 40% inventory/service offers (with expiration dates), 30% tips/education (seasonal maintenance, car care), 20% community/brand, 10% reviews/testimonials. Each post: 300 chars max, CTA, relevant emoji. Expiration date required on any price/offer.

**Pre-Load Q&A (30 questions)**
Write the 30 most common questions customers search for this type of dealership + answers:
- "Do you accept trade-ins with negative equity?"
- "Can I get financed with bad credit?"
- "Do you match online prices?"
- "How long does the service take?"
- etc. Answers: 100-150 chars, factual, keyword-optimized, no false promises.

### 3. Review Request Sequence (3 touchpoints)

**Touchpoint 1 — Delivery Day (SMS, under 160 chars)**
Sent 2-4 hours after vehicle delivery or service completion. Warm, appreciative. Link to Google review. No incentive mention (FTC).

**Touchpoint 2 — Day 7 Email (150 words)**
Subject: "How is your [VEHICLE] / How was your service experience?" Personal from salesperson/service advisor name. 3-question satisfaction check, then "If you'd rate us 5 stars, here's our Google review link." If less than 5 stars, "Please reply to this email directly."

**Touchpoint 3 — Day 30 Email (120 words)**
Subject: "Still loving your [VEHICLE]?" Softer ask, 1-month check-in. Google review link, referral mention. "If you know someone in the market, we'd love to earn their business too."

### 4. Crisis Response Templates

**Recall-Related Social Post**
For when a manufacturer recall is announced. 200-word Facebook post + 100-word Instagram caption. Proactive, safety-first, "your safety is our priority" framing. Include: NHTSA recall lookup link, dealer service hours, "at no cost to you" language.

**Negative Press / Viral Social Complaint**
Template for a post going viral (1K+ shares). 200-word Facebook response. Acknowledge publicly, commit to resolution, move to private. Manager name and direct contact. No details revealed publicly.

**F&I Complaint on Social ("They snuck products in")**
Highest-frequency crisis type. 150-word public response. Acknowledge the feeling, state commitment to transparency, invite private resolution. Avoid: "we fully disclosed," "you signed," "it's in the contract" — these escalate.

---

## Output Format
Deliver each section labeled. All review response templates numbered. Q&As in Q:/A: format. Flag any response that could create legal liability.
