# Techniques: The 20/20/20 Formula in Practice

## The 20/20/20 Formula

The core executable of the book. Divide your Victory Hour (5-6 AM) into three 20-minute blocks:

### Block 1: Move (5:00-5:20) — Healthset

Vigorous exercise to wake up the body and flood it with endorphins. Options:
- Running or brisk walking
- Yoga or stretching
- Bodyweight exercises (push-ups, squats)
- High-intensity interval training

**The key:** The goal is to sweat. Not to train for an event. To activate the body and signal to the brain that the day has begun.

### Block 2: Reflect (5:20-5:40) — Mindset + Heartset

Quiet reflection to calibrate your inner state. Options:
- **Journaling**: Write three things you're grateful for, one thing you'll focus on today, and one affirmation
- **Planning**: Review your top 3 priorities for the day
- **Meditation**: 20 minutes of mindfulness practice
- **Visualization**: See yourself executing your day at your best

### Block 3: Grow (5:40-6:00) — Soulset + Mindset

Deep learning and skill development. Options:
- Read a book (nonfiction, personal growth)
- Listen to a podcast or audiobook
- Study a subject relevant to your mastery
- Practice a skill (language, instrument, coding)

## The 4-Part Habit Pattern

To program a new habit (like waking at 5 AM), use this sequence:

1. **Trigger** — Set a specific alarm. Place it across the room.
2. **Routine** — Execute the 20/20/20 Formula immediately. No phone. No email.
3. **Reward** — Give yourself something you genuinely enjoy: a great coffee, a favorite podcast during the Move block, the satisfaction of a checkmark.
4. **Repetition** — Do it every day. Victory demands consistency and persistency.

> **Case: The entrepreneur's habit-building** (Chapter 12): She started with just the first 20 minutes of exercise. After a week, she added the Reflect block. After two weeks, she added the Grow block. She didn't try to do all 60 minutes on day one — she built up gradually.

## The Bedtime Protocol

Waking at 5 AM requires an aligned bedtime. The formula:

**5 AM wake time → 10 PM lights out (7 hours of sleep)**

Practical steps:
- No screens 60 minutes before bed
- Keep the bedroom cool and dark
- Read fiction (not work-related) to wind down
- Set a specific sleep trigger: "After I brush my teeth, I will read 10 pages of fiction in bed"

## Overcoming the Snooze

The biggest barrier to the Victory Hour. Techniques to overcome:
1. Move the alarm clock across the room — you must physically get up to turn it off
2. Sleep in your workout clothes — reduces friction between waking and moving
3. Have a pre-commitment — tell someone you'll text them at 5:05 AM
4. Use a light-based alarm that simulates sunrise

## The Reward System

The brain needs an immediate payoff. Design your Victory Hour so that the reward is built in:
- The Move block releases endorphins (natural high)
- The Reflect block creates clarity (reduces anxiety)
- The Grow block compounds expertise (builds confidence)
- Track every completed Victory Hour on a calendar — the visual streak is its own reward

## The Weekend Protocol

Don't break the chain. On weekends:
- Do a lighter version: 20 minutes (move only) or skip to 6 AM
- But don't sleep past 7 AM — it disrupts your circadian rhythm
- Use the extra morning time for deeper reflection or creative projects
