## Description: <br>
Food preservation techniques including fermentation, pickling, canning, drying, and smoking for preserving harvests, extending shelf life, reducing waste, making fermented foods, and building food resilience. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
External users, home cooks, gardeners, and resilience-focused households use this skill to choose and follow food preservation methods such as lacto-fermentation, pickling, canning, dehydration, smoking, and sourdough starter care. It emphasizes practical steps, localization, and safety checks for preservation workflows. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Canning guidance is safety-sensitive, especially for botulism prevention and altitude-dependent processing. <br>
Mitigation: Verify recipes, acidity, processing times, and pressure-canning requirements against official local food-safety sources before relying on the guidance. <br>
Risk: Reminder triggers or agent memory may store harvest, fermentation, canning, or sourdough state. <br>
Mitigation: Review the agent's memory and automation settings before enabling reminders or storing preservation project details. <br>
