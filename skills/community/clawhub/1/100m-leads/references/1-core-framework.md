## The Core Four Framework — The Only 4 Ways to Get Leads

There are exactly 4 ways to let anyone know about anything. Cross two audience types (Warm vs Cold) with two communication modes (1-to-1 vs 1-to-many) and you get the Core Four:

| | **1-to-1 (Private)** | **1-to-many (Public)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm Audience** (knows you) | #1 Warm Outreach | #2 Post Free Content |
| **Cold Audience** (strangers) | #3 Cold Outreach | #4 Run Paid Ads |

These are the building blocks of every lead generation strategy ever invented. If you master all four, you never worry about leads again.

### #1 Warm Outreach — The Foundation (1-to-1, Warm)

**How it works:** You contact people who already know you (phone contacts, email list, social media followers, past customers) one at a time.

**The 10-Step Warm Outreach Process:**

1. **Get your list** — Your phone. Your email accounts. Your social media followers. Add them up. You have more than you think.
2. **Pick a platform** — The one with the most contacts.
3. **Personalize** — Use something you know about them as your opening. "Saw you just had a baby! Congrats!"
4. **Reach out to 100 people/day** — Follow up 3 times max, once per day, until they respond.
5. **When they reply → ACA Framework:** **A**cknowledge what they said (restate), **C**ompliment them (tie to positive trait), **A**sk another question (guide toward your offer).
6. **Make an offer** — After 3-4 exchanges. Use the Value Equation to frame it.
7. **Make it free** — Your first 5 customers should be free. "I'll do it for free if you promise to use it, give feedback, and leave a review."
8. **Start at the top** — Platform with most contacts → second most → third most.
9. **Start charging** — Once people refer you, swap "free" for "80% off for next 5," then "60% off," etc. Raise by 20% every 5 customers.
10. **Keep your list warm** — Send regular value. Use Dean Jackson's 9-word email: "Are you still looking to [4 word desire]?"

> **Case: Hormozi's first 6 clients via warm outreach** (Chapter 1): With $51K in savings and zero business experience, Hormozi called, texted, and messaged everyone he knew offering free 12-week training + custom nutrition plan in exchange for a charitable donation and testimonial. Six people said yes — two high school friends, one college friend, and three referrals. That little business made $4,000/month and replaced his job income. **Key takeaway:** You don't need fancy marketing. You need to ask 100 people. Six will say yes. That's enough to start.

> **Case: The $150K refund crisis → breakthrough** (Chapters 1-2): After losing $150K to refunds from dishonest gym partners, Hormozi called 8 gym owners offering to license his "launch system" instead flying out. First one said yes at $6K. Second at $8K. Third at $10K. In one day: $60K collected. That single shift from doing-it-yourself to selling-the-system became Gym Launch, doing $25M/year within 24 months. **Key takeaway:** The thing that scares you most (giving away your secrets) is often your biggest breakthrough. Sell the system, not your labor.

### #2 Post Free Content — Scale (1-to-many, Warm)

**How it works:** You create free content that your warm audience consumes and shares. Content gets attention. Attention grows audience. Audience = more people to sell to.

**The Content Unit — 3 Components:**

1. **Hook** — Give them a reason to stop scrolling. Use surprising stats, specific results, controversy, or curiosity gaps.
2. **Retain** — Keep them consuming. Storytelling, short sentences, visual aids, pattern interrupts.
3. **Reward** — Satisfy the reason they started. Deliver the value you promised. A tip, a framework, an insight, a story with a lesson.

**5 Topic Categories:**
- **Far Past:** Important life lessons, stories without the scar.
- **Recent Past:** What happened this week/month — meetings, conversations, wins, failures.
- **Present:** What you're doing right now. Behind the scenes.
- **Trending:** Comment on current events in your industry.
- **Manufactured:** Create an experiment or challenge specifically for content.

> **Case: The $120K personal branding lesson** (Chapter 13): Hormozi resisted building a personal brand for years — "I wanted to be rich, not famous." His friend's quote changed his perspective: "If getting weird messages from strangers is the price I have to pay to make the impact I want to have, I'd pay that price any day of the week." After Leila paid $120K for coaching, the mentor said simply: "Just gotta do more, bro." Hormozi posted 10x the content, and his audience grew 10x — from 200K to 1.2M in 6 months. **Key takeaway:** Content is not the compounding asset — the audience is. 10x volume = 10x growth.

### #3 Cold Outreach — The Growth Lever (1-to-1, Cold)

**How it works:** Contact strangers who have NOT given you permission. Harder, but the pool is infinite.

**The C-C-B Framework:**
- **C**ompliment: "Loved your post about X. I learned Y from it."
- **C**onnection: "I help people like you achieve Z."
- **B**ridge: "Would you be open to a quick call?"

**Benchmark:** 1 in 100 cold outreach attempts → a conversation. 1 in 10 conversations → a lead. 1 in 10 leads → a sale. That's 1 sale per 10,000 cold outreaches. Volume is everything.

### #4 Paid Ads — The Accelerator (1-to-many, Cold)

**How it works:** Pay platforms (Facebook, Google, Instagram, etc.) for access to their cold audiences. The most scalable but requires skill and money.

**The Chain:** Ad → Landing Page → Lead Magnet → Engaged Lead → Follow-up → Sale

**Hormozi's track record:** Lifetime average ROAS of 36:1. $250M+ portfolio revenue. 20,000+ leads/day across all companies.

> **Case: The case study that beat the webinar** (Chapter 3): Hormozi spent 8 Sundays building a webinar funnel — $450 in ads, 80 leads, 0 sales. He switched to a simple 13-minute case study video ("How we added 213 members and $112K to a small gym"). The next morning: Leila's calendar was stacked with appointments. **Key takeaway:** Your audience doesn't want a polished presentation. They want proof. Show them what worked, don't tell them.

### The Lead Magnet Engine

A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem, offered for free or low cost, designed to:
1. Engage ideal customers when they see it
2. Get more engagement than your core offer alone
3. Be valuable enough that they consume it
4. Make the right people more likely to buy

**7 Steps to Create One:**
1. Pick a narrow problem + target customer
2. Choose solution type: Reveal Their Problem / Sample & Trial / One Step of Multi-Step
3. Choose delivery: Software / Information / Services / Physical Product
4. Test the name (headline tests can 2-10x results)
5. Make it easy to consume (every format: video, text, audio, images)
6. Make it darn good (give away the secrets, sell the implementation)
7. Clear CTA with scarcity + urgency

**The math:** A lead magnet costing $25 to deliver can lower your cost to acquire a customer by 3x. If your core offer makes $10K profit and you spend $1K to get someone on a call (closing 1 in 3 → $3K CAC), a lead magnet can drop CAC to $1K. That's a 10:1 return on ad spend.
