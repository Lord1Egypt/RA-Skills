# TikTok Shop Compliance Quick Guide

A launch can be frozen mid-flight by a compliance flag. Clear these before T-3.

## Claims

- Avoid medical / treatment claims for cosmetics and supplements ("treats", "cures", "clinically proven" without evidence).
- Soft, benefit-led language is usually safe ("helps brighten", "designed for", "supports").
- Superlatives ("#1", "best", "guaranteed results") need substantiation or removal.
- No before/after that implies a medical outcome.

## Restricted & Sensitive Categories

- Supplements, cosmetics, electronics with batteries, and kids' products carry extra documentation requirements.
- Check whether your category requires certificates (e.g., safety, ingredient, or origin docs) before listing.
- Some categories cannot use certain promo types — verify coupon eligibility.

## Keywords & Trademarks

- Do not use other brands' trademarked names in your title or tags.
- Remove gated terms (varies by region) that trigger automated review.
- Keep the title descriptive and accurate to the product to avoid mismatch flags.

## Listing Integrity

- Images must show the actual product; no borrowed or stock imagery that misrepresents.
- Variant prices and stock must be real and fulfillable.
- Shipping time must match your true handling capacity.

## Region Notes

- Rules differ by marketplace region; confirm against the local TikTok Shop policy center.
- Restricted-term lists and required documents are region-specific — never assume parity.

## Pre-Launch Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Claims reviewed and softened/removed where needed
- [ ] Category and attributes correct
- [ ] Required certificates uploaded (if any)
- [ ] No trademarked terms in title/tags
- [ ] Images represent the real product
- [ ] Shipping time matches capacity
- [ ] Promo type allowed for the category

> This is operational guidance, not legal advice. Always confirm against the current TikTok Shop policy center for your region.
