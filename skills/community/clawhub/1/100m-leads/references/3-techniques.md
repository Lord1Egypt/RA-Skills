## The Lead Gen Toolbox

### 1. The ACA Framework (for Warm Conversations)

The single most useful conversation framework in the book:

**Acknowledge** what they said → Restate in your own words (active listening)
**Compliment** them → Tie to a positive character trait
**Ask** another question → Guide toward your offer naturally

Example:
- Them: "I'm an accountant with two kids."
- You: "Two kids! And you're an accountant? Wow, supermom! So hardworking. Do you ever get time to work out?"

This works because: people love talking about themselves, they love compliments, and it feels natural. After 3-4 exchanges, make your offer.

### 2. The 9-Word Email (Dean Jackson)

Subject: [their name]

Body: "Are you still looking to [4 word desire]?"

That's it. No images. No links. No frills. Just a question. The people who reply are your engaged leads. Priority one: reach out to them.

Examples:
- "Are you still looking to buy your dream home?"
- "Are you still looking to get more sales leads?"
- "Are you still looking to tone up your arms?"
- "Are you still looking to open an online store?"

### 3. The Value Equation (for Crafting Offers)

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Four levers to adjust:
1. **Dream Outcome** — State the best possible result, from people like them. Example: "One of my clients lost 40 pounds in 12 weeks while working full-time and traveling."
2. **Perceived Likelihood** — Include testimonials, reviews, awards, certifications, guarantees. Example: "I guarantee you'll get results or I work with you until you do."
3. **Time Delay** — Show how fast they start getting results. Example: "Most clients see visible changes in the first 2 weeks."
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** — Show what they DON'T have to give up. Example: "No gym required. No meal prep. No counting calories."

> **Case: Hormozi's free-for-results offer** (Chapter 12): When starting Gym Launch, his offer was: "I'll fill your gym in 30 days for free. You pay nothing. I pay for everything. I sell new members and keep the first 6 weeks as payment. If I don't fill your gym, I lose money. You spend nothing either way." This maximized Dream Outcome (filled gym), Likelihood (no-risk), minimized Time Delay (30 days), and minimized Effort (he did everything). **Key takeaway:** The strongest offer is one where you lose if you fail. Skin in the game = trust.

### 5. The Content Unit Formula

Every piece of audience-building content needs three parts:

**Hook** → **Retain** → **Reward**

**Hook examples:** "The day I lost $150K in 5 minutes" / "How a 13-minute video replaced my entire sales funnel" / "The 7-word email that made me $60K in one day"

**Retain techniques:** Short sentences. Stories. Visuals. Curiosity gaps. "I almost gave up. Here's what happened next..."

**Reward types:** A framework. A lesson. A script. A result. A story with a punchline. If they don't get something valuable, they won't come back.

### 6. The "5 Free → Paid" Ramp

When launching anything new:
- First 5 customers: FREE (in exchange for testimonial + feedback + referrals)
- Next 5: 80% off
- Next 5: 60% off
- Next 5: 40% off
- Continue until you find your sweet spot

Why free first: 1) You suck (for now). People forgive free. 2) You learn what works. 3) Free customers turn into paying customers, referrers, and testimonial-givers. 4) All three make you money eventually.

### 7. Cold Outreach C-C-B Script

**Compliment:** "Loved your post about X. I learned Y from it."
**Connection:** "I help [people like you] achieve [Z]."
**Bridge:** "Would you be open to a [specific ask]?"

Benchmark: 1 in 100 cold outreaches → conversation. 1 in 10 conversations → lead. 1 in 10 leads → sale. That means 10,000 cold outreaches → 1 sale. Most people give up at 100. Don't.

### 8. The CTA Formula

Every call to action needs two things:
1. **What to do** — "Call now" not "don't delay." Be clear, not clever.
2. **Why to do it now** — Scarcity (limited spots), Urgency (time-limited offer), or any reason at all. Harvard study: any reason beats no reason. "Because I need to know by Friday" works better than no reason.

### 9. The One-Page Advertising Plan (from Section V)

1. Post valuable free content daily to grow warm audience
2. Run paid ads to content (not directly to sales)
3. Content generates engaged leads organically
4. Promote your top 1-3 offers to engaged leads
5. Build multiple lead magnets for different segments
6. Scale what works. Kill what doesn't.

> **Case: The accidental discovery of the $6K product** (Chapter 1): Hormozi was trying to quit the gym business. A desperate gym owner begged him to "just show me how to do it." Hormozi threw out $6K hoping to scare him off. The owner said "Done." In a single day, calling 8 gyms at increasing prices ($6K → $8K → $10K) collected $60K in cash with zero fulfillment cost. This became Gym Launch's licensing model — $6.8M first year, $25.9M second year. **Key takeaway:** Your biggest wins come from doing what scares you most. Sell the system, not your time.
