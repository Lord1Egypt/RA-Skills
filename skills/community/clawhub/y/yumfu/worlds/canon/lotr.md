## 1. One-line premise
A small, unremarkable hobbit must carry the one artifact that could let the Dark Lord conquer the world — the **One Ring** — to the only place it can be destroyed, deep inside the enemy's own land, while a fragile alliance of free peoples buys him time with war.

## 2. World & era
- **Setting:** **Middle-earth**, during the **Third Age**, the **War of the Ring** (~TA 3018–3019).
- **Tone:** mythic, moral, elegiac; the magical/elvish world is fading, and even victory means loss.
- **Peoples:** Hobbits, Men, Elves, Dwarves, Wizards (Istari), Ents; against Sauron's Orcs, Uruk-hai, Trolls, Nazgûl.
- **Geography anchors:** the Shire, Rivendell, Moria, Lothlórien, Rohan (Edoras/Helm's Deep), Gondor (Minas Tirith), Isengard, and Mordor (Mount Doom).

## 3. Main plot scaffolding (stages for anchoring)

**Stage 1 · The burden**
- Frodo inherits the One Ring from Bilbo; Gandalf confirms it is Sauron's master ring. Frodo must leave the Shire.

**Stage 2 · The road to Rivendell & the Council**
- Pursued by the Nazgûl; reaches Rivendell. The **Council of Elrond** forms the **Fellowship of the Ring** (Frodo, Sam, Merry, Pippin, Aragorn, Boromir, Legolas, Gimli, Gandalf).

**Stage 3 · The Fellowship breaks**
- Through Moria (Gandalf falls fighting the Balrog) and Lothlórien. At Amon Hen the Fellowship splinters: Boromir falls; Frodo & Sam go on alone toward Mordor; Merry & Pippin are taken by Orcs.

**Stage 4 · Two fronts**
- **War front:** Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli rally Rohan; Helm's Deep; Gandalf returns as the White. Ents destroy Isengard.
- **Quest front:** Frodo & Sam, guided by **Gollum**, creep toward Mount Doom.

**Stage 5 · The Return of the King**
- Siege of Minas Tirith; Aragorn claims the crown of Gondor. A diversionary army marches on the Black Gate to draw Sauron's eye.
- At the Crack of Doom, Gollum's lust for the Ring ironically completes the quest; Sauron falls. The age of Elves wanes; the hobbits go home changed.

## 5. Magic / setting anchors
- **The One Ring** — grants invisibility but corrupts and enslaves; must be unmade in the fire that forged it.
- **The Nazgûl (Ringwraiths)** — nine fallen kings, Sauron's terror-hunters.
- **The Istari (wizards)** — Gandalf, Saruman; angelic emissaries, not free to simply dominate.
- **Elves fading / the West** — Elves depart to the Undying Lands; magic recedes with them.
- **Ents, Balrog, Palantíri, Mithril** — living trees, ancient demon of Moria, seeing-stones, precious metal.

## 6. Theme & "anchor vs. freedom"
- **Core themes:** the corrupting nature of power; mercy and pity (Gollum); the small and humble changing history; victory as bittersweet loss.
- **Canon anchors (stay close):** the Ring must go to Mount Doom; the Fellowship and its breaking; the two fronts (war in Rohan/Gondor + the secret quest); Gollum's role.
- **Free to diverge:** the player need not be Frodo; they can ride with Rohan, hold Gondor, scout for the Fellowship, or carve a side-path — as long as the Ring's shadow and the war remain the spine.
