# Launch Phases Guide

Detailed task inventory for each phase. Pull from this when generating a runbook; include only the tasks relevant to the SKU's category and fulfillment model.

## Pre-Launch (T-7 → T-1)

### Listing
- Title with primary keyword in first 30 characters.
- All variants (size/color) created with correct SKUs and barcodes.
- 5+ images: hero on white, in-use, scale, detail, packaging.
- Video on the product card (3–15s, shows product in motion).
- Specs, materials, dimensions, and care info complete.
- Shipping template assigned; handling time accurate.

### Pricing & Promo
- Anchor (list) price set above launch price to show a saving.
- Launch coupon created (percent or fixed) with a cap or end condition.
- Bundle / quantity break configured if applicable.
- Margin checked at the fully-promoted price including fees.

### Affiliates
- Target creator list built (niche fit, audience size, engagement rate).
- Outreach sent with commission rate and sample offer.
- Samples shipped 4–5 days before live date.
- Brief shared: hooks, do/don't claims, key selling points, link.

### Inventory & Fulfillment
- Stock on hand confirmed against forecast peak demand.
- 3PL receipt confirmed and pickable (if applicable).
- Restock PO drafted with the trigger level defined.

### Compliance
- Claims reviewed against category rules.
- Restricted / trademarked terms removed.
- Category and attributes correct.

## Launch Day (T-0)

- Buy button live and purchasable in the target region.
- Promo and coupon active and visible on the card.
- First creator posts live; paid boost or Spark Ads queued.
- First orders monitored hourly; checkout path verified.
- Customer-service replies and FAQ ready for launch questions.

## Post-Launch (T+1 → T+7)

- Review-request flow triggered at delivery + 2–3 days.
- Daily check of units, conversion, quality score, inventory cover.
- Restock PO fired when inventory hits the trigger level.
- Creator content cadence maintained (aim 2–3 posts/day in week one).
- Negative reviews / defects triaged within 24 hours.
- Escalation contact confirmed for policy or fulfillment holds.

## Phase Timing Rules

- Compress, do not skip, when lead time is short: keep compliance and a test order even at T-2.
- Never launch with a Weak inventory or listing-quality row; move the date instead.
- Treat T+1 → T+3 as the highest-leverage window for reviews and velocity.
