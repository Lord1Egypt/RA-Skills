# AMSTAR-2 Assessment Checklist (topic-stage self-check)

AMSTAR-2 is the international standard tool for assessing the methodological quality of systematic reviews / meta-analyses (Shea BJ et al., BMJ 2017;358:j4008). This file is a simplified version used at the topic stage to self-check "whether my topic and future methodology can pass a high AMSTAR-2 rating".

The full 16 items are in the original publication. This file focuses on items that can be previewed at the topic stage.

> **AMSTAR-2's 16 items (for reference)**:
> 1. PICO in title/abstract
> 2. Review methods established prior to conduct (prior protocol)
> 3. Explanation for inclusion of study designs
> 4. Comprehensive literature search
> 5. Duplicate study selection
> 6. Duplicate data extraction
> 7. List of excluded studies with justification
> 8. Description of included studies
> 9. Risk of bias assessment using appropriate tool
> 10. Sources of funding for included studies
> 11. Appropriate methods for statistical combination
> 12. Impact of risk of bias on estimates
> 13. Explanation for heterogeneity
> 14. Investigation of publication bias
> 15. Robustness of results to risk of bias
> 16. Source of funding for the review

## AMSTAR-2 key items previewable at the topic stage

### Item 1 — Is PICO explicit at the topic stage?
- ✅ Met: all four PICO elements explicitly decomposed
- ❌ Not met: only a research direction given
- Must be met at the topic stage

### Item 2 — Prior protocol established before conduct
- ✅ Met: protocol registered (e.g., on PROSPERO) or drafted before data extraction
- ❌ Not met: no prior protocol; methods determined post hoc
- At the topic stage, commit to registering on PROSPERO and drafting the protocol before formal search

### Item 3 — Explanation for inclusion of study designs
- ✅ Met: explicit design type (RCT / NRSI / cohort / case-control / mixed) with rationale
- ❌ Not met: design not stated or not justified
- AMSTAR-2 does not require RCT-only, but the design must be stated and justified

### Item 4 — Comprehensive literature search
- ✅ Met: ≥3 databases + gray literature + trial registries
- ⚠️ Partial: only 2 databases; gray literature incomplete
- ❌ Not met: PubMed only
- Confirm accessible resources at the topic stage

### Item 7 — List of excluded studies with justification
- ✅ Met: can list excluded studies with exclusion reasons
- ❌ Not met: only lists included studies
- Reserve an exclusion-list field in the data-extraction form at the topic stage

### Item 9 — Risk of bias assessment using appropriate tool
- ✅ Met: appropriate tool by study type (RoB 2 / ROBINS-I / QUADAS-2)
- ❌ Not met: wrong tool or not done
- See the tool-selection guide in prisma-2020-checklist.md

### Item 11 — Appropriate methods for statistical combination
- ✅ Met: effect size, synthesis model (fixed/random), heterogeneity handling, subgroups prespecified
- ❌ Not met: not prespecified
- Complete the methodological plan at the topic stage

### Item 13 — Explanation for heterogeneity
- ✅ Met: prespecified ≥3 subgroup analyses + ≥2 sensitivity analyses
- ⚠️ Partial: total pool only, no subgroups
- ❌ Not met: not considered

### Item 14 — Investigation of publication bias
- ✅ Met: Egger test / funnel plot / Trim-and-fill possible
- ⚠️ Partial: only visual funnel-plot judgment
- ❌ Not met: not assessed
- Preview whether the included-study count supports publication-bias tests (recommend ≥10)

### Item 15 — Robustness of results to risk of bias
- ✅ Met: sensitivity analysis excluding high-RoB studies prespecified; discuss impact on conclusions
- ❌ Not met: not considered
- Reserve a "RoB sensitivity analysis" paragraph in the protocol at the topic stage

### Item 16 — Source of funding for the review
- ✅ Met: review funding source reported
- ❌ Not met: not reported
- Reserve a funding-source field in the data-extraction form at the topic stage

## Topic-stage AMSTAR-2 self-assessment rating

Simplified version of the original AMSTAR-2 algorithm (the full 16 items cannot all be assessed at the topic stage):

| Rating | Criterion | Topic suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| High confidence | All key items above ✅ | Strongly recommend proceeding |
| Moderate confidence | 1–2 ⚠️, no ❌ | Proceed; shore up in the protocol |
| Low confidence | ≥3 ⚠️ or 1 ❌ | Revise methodology before proceeding |
| Critically low confidence | ≥2 ❌ | Not recommended; re-pick the topic |

## Critical weaknesses list

AMSTAR-2 defines 7 "critical weaknesses" (items 2, 4, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15); any one drops the rating to "critically low confidence". Avoid at the topic stage:

1. **Item 2** — no prior protocol (methods established post hoc)
2. **Item 4** — literature search not comprehensive (e.g., single database, no gray literature)
3. **Item 7** — list of excluded studies with justification not provided
4. **Item 9** — risk of bias assessment not done or wrong tool used
5. **Item 11** — inappropriate methods for statistical combination (or not prespecified)
6. **Item 13** — heterogeneity not explored (no prespecified subgroups)
7. **Item 15** — robustness of results to risk of bias not assessed (no RoB sensitivity analysis)

If any critical weakness is "unavoidable" at the topic stage → hold or abandon.

### Critical-weakness avoidance action list

The table below gives concrete avoidance actions for each critical weakness at the topic stage:

| Critical weakness | Topic-stage action | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Item 2 no prior protocol | Commit to PROSPERO registration + draft the protocol before formal search; write the commitment into report Section 11 (next steps) | Stage 2 done |
| Item 4 search not comprehensive | At Stage 4, confirm ≥3 databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL) + gray literature (ClinicalTrials.gov, ICTRP, conference abstracts); write the full list into report Section 6 | Stage 4.1 done |
| Item 7 excluded-studies list not provided | Prespecify an "excluded studies table" field in the data-extraction form (study ID + exclusion stage + exclusion reason); do not rely on post-hoc memory | Stage 5 report |
| Item 9 RoB not done or wrong tool | Match the tool by study type (RCT→RoB 2, NRSI→ROBINS-I, DTA→QUADAS-2, observational→ROBINS-E); specify per-domain judgment criteria in the protocol | Stage 4.4 |
| Item 11 inappropriate statistical combination | Prespecify effect size, synthesis model (fixed/random), heterogeneity threshold, ≥3 subgroups, ≥2 sensitivity analyses; write into report Sections 3 and 9 | Stage 3.4 + Stage 5 |
| Item 13 heterogeneity not explored | Prespecify ≥3 subgroup analyses + ≥2 sensitivity analyses; write into report Section 9; prespecify an I² threshold | Stage 3.4 + Stage 5 |
| Item 15 robustness to RoB not assessed | Reserve a "RoB sensitivity analysis" paragraph in the protocol's discussion framework; write "re-pool after excluding high-RoB studies" as a sensitivity analysis | Stage 5 |

Practical points:
- Each action must leave a trace in the corresponding report section (not just "will do" in conversation)
- Typical scenarios where a critical weakness is "unavoidable": single-person project cannot guarantee two-reviewer screening (item 5/6), too few included studies to assess publication bias (item 14) → mark 🔴 high risk at Stage 4.4 and trigger Decision gate 4 to pause

## Relationship with PRISMA 2020

PRISMA 2020 focuses on "reporting completeness"; AMSTAR-2 focuses on "methodological rigor". They are complementary:
- PRISMA 2020: did you write it down
- AMSTAR-2: did you do it right

At the topic stage, cross-check both lists to ensure the future study can be both completely reported and pass methodological review.

## Practical advice

To raise the future AMSTAR-2 rating, at the topic stage we recommend:
1. Fully register on PROSPERO (forces advance solidification of methodology; addresses item 2)
2. Pre-write a complete protocol (recommend the Cochrane Handbook Chapter 3 template)
3. Reserve all AMSTAR-2-related fields in the data-extraction form (funding source, study design, key RoB items, etc.)
4. Bring in a second reviewer to review the protocol
5. If possible, have a methodologist externally review the protocol
