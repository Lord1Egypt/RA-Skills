# Google AI Optimization Guide Reference

Source: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide.md.txt

Use this reference as the official Google Search guidance basis for AI Overviews, AI Mode, and generative AI Search optimization tasks.

## Main Position

Google says SEO is still relevant for generative AI Search. Generative AI features on Google Search are rooted in Google's core Search ranking and quality systems. They use techniques such as retrieval-augmented generation and query fan-out, but they still rely on content from Google's Search index.

From Google's perspective, optimizing for generative AI search is optimizing for the Search experience. AEO and GEO terminology may be common, but Google frames the work as SEO.

## What To Prioritize

### Valuable, Non-Commodity Content

Content likely to perform well over time tends to be unique, compelling, useful, and people-first.

Prioritize:

- Unique point of view.
- First-hand experience or expert perspective.
- Original insight beyond common knowledge.
- Human-readable organization with useful headings and sections.
- High-quality images and video where they help users.
- Content that satisfies visitors after they arrive.

Avoid recycling generic information that could come from anyone or be generated by a model without added expertise.

### Technical Structure

Google AI Search features depend on Google being able to find, process, index, and serve the page.

Review:

- Search technical requirements.
- Indexability and snippet eligibility.
- Crawlability and crawl budget, especially for large or frequently updated sites.
- JavaScript SEO basics for JS-heavy sites.
- Semantic HTML where practical.
- Page experience across devices.
- Duplicate content and unnecessary URL variants.

Meeting requirements does not guarantee crawling, indexing, serving, or AI feature inclusion.

### Local Business And Ecommerce Details

For local business and ecommerce sites, review details that can feed Google Search experiences:

- Merchant Center.
- Merchant Center feeds.
- Product listings and product information.
- Google Business Profile.
- Business details on Google Search.
- Emerging conversational experiences such as Business Agent where relevant.

### Agentic Experiences

Browser agents may inspect visual renderings, DOM structure, and accessibility trees. For businesses where agent interaction matters, review agent-friendly site UX and emerging protocols such as Universal Commerce Protocol.

## Myths And Low-Value Tactics

Google says these are not required or are often distractions:

- llms.txt and other special AI-only machine-readable files.
- Special markup or Markdown files for generative AI Search.
- Chunking content into tiny pieces for AI systems.
- Writing in a special AI-only style.
- Capturing every long-tail query or fan-out variant.
- Seeking inauthentic mentions.
- Overfocusing on structured data as a generative AI visibility unlock.

Structured data can still be useful for regular SEO and rich results, but there is no special schema.org markup required for generative AI Search.

## Policy Risks

Scaled pages created mainly to manipulate rankings or generative AI responses can violate Google's scaled content abuse spam policy.

AI-assisted content is not automatically disallowed, but it must meet Search Essentials and spam policies. Judge the final output by helpfulness, reliability, originality, and people-first value.

## Audit Rubric

Use this rubric for recommendations:

1. Is the page/indexable content eligible for Google Search and snippets?
2. Is the content original enough to be worth citing or surfacing?
3. Does it show first-hand experience, expertise, data, examples, or a viewpoint?
4. Is it organized for humans with clear headings and navigable structure?
5. Are images/video useful and optimized when relevant?
6. Does technical implementation hide or delay important content from crawlers?
7. Are there duplicate/near-duplicate pages wasting crawl attention?
8. For local/ecommerce, are official Google business/product surfaces complete?
9. Are proposed tactics aligned with users, or are they AI-search manipulation attempts?
10. What evidence is missing before making stronger claims?
