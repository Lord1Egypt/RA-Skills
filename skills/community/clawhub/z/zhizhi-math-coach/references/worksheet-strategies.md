# Worksheet Strategies

## Confirmation First

If the parent only says "出一张练习卷", ask for:

- purpose: wrong-question variants, weak-point drill, exam review, diagnostic probe, spaced review, or mixed maintenance;
- content range: recent wrong questions, a weak point, current unit, this semester, or exam scope;
- length: daily short practice, focused drill, or exam review;
- output: printable worksheet only, worksheet plus answer key, or explanation plus worksheet.

If the parent says "按最近情况出", choose a mixed maintenance worksheet based on recent mistakes, active weak points, and due reviews.

## Strategies

- `wrong_question_variant`: keep the core ability from a wrong item and vary numbers, scenario, wording, condition order, or distractors.
- `weak_point_drill`: focus one weak point and avoid broad mixed drills.
- `exam_mistake_review`: midterm/final review weighted by this semester's mistakes and relapse history.
- `relapse_repair`: repair a weak point that failed after prior improvement.
- `spaced_review`: revisit older weak points at 1/3/7/14-day intervals.
- `transfer_check`: verify that the student can handle changed wording or context.
- `post_explanation_check`: 3 to 6 items after a parent explanation.
- `diagnostic_probe`: short set to separate concept, reading, modeling, calculation, and checking causes.
- `mixed_maintenance`: current unit plus old weak points plus light fluency.
- `fluency_speed`: facts and speed when understanding is already stable.
- `contrast_discrimination`: similar surface stories with different required models.
- `geometry_drill`: deterministic SVG geometry problems from structured specs.

## Length Defaults

- Daily short practice: 10 to 15 minutes.
- Focused weak-point drill: about 20 minutes.
- Exam review: 30 to 45 minutes or split into multiple shorter papers.
- Post-explanation check: 3 to 6 items.
- Diagnostic probe: enough to separate causes, usually 4 to 8 items.

## Answer Key Requirements

Every worksheet answer key should include:

- correct answer and important intermediate values;
- likely error labels;
- grading and reassessment rules;
- next action if weak, consolidating, or mastered.
