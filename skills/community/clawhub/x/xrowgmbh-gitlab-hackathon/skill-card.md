## Description: <br>
Plan and execute fair GitLab hackathon participation, including Quarterly and Transcend Hackathons, by analyzing rules, selecting qualifying issues/MRs, tracking scoring levers, and keeping an exploit watchlist. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Developers and engineers use this skill to plan fair GitLab hackathon participation, identify qualifying issues and merge requests, track scoring requirements, and avoid exploit-like behavior while contributing. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: GitLab actions such as comments, labels, issue closures, and merge requests may affect public projects or hackathon scoring. <br>
Mitigation: Use a least-privileged GitLab token and review each proposed GitLab action before execution. <br>
Risk: Optimizing for points can lead to spam comments, padded commits, incorrect labels, invalid issue closures, or placeholder submissions. <br>
Mitigation: Follow the skill's fair-use guidance: make real contributions, keep merge requests reviewable, link only relevant issues, and avoid exploit-like behavior unless maintainers explicitly approve it. <br>
