## The Problem Generic AI Has With Cabinet Marketing

Cabinet and kitchen remodeling marketing is a compliance minefield. Generic AI tools produce copy that sounds good but creates serious legal exposure:

- **"Solid wood cabinets"** — the most litigated term in cabinet marketing; 80% of "solid wood" cabinets use MDF for door panels and veneer for box sides
- **"No-VOC eco-friendly cabinets"** — triggers FTC Green Guides violation without CARB Phase 2 or GREENGUARD Gold certification
- **"Safe kitchen remodel in older home"** — EPA RRP Rule makes this statement illegal without certified firm disclosure in pre-1978 homes
- **"Lifetime soft-close guarantee"** — Magnuson-Moss violation unless backed by written warranty with specific terms
- **"Best kitchen remodeler in Henderson"** — FTC §5 unsubstantiated superlative without third-party citation

This skill blocks all of it. Every output is compliant before it leaves the prompt.

## 7 Compliance Moats at a Glance

| Moat | Authority | What's Blocked |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| NV B-2/C-3 License | NRS 624 | "No license needed"; unlicensed work marketing |
| KCMA/ANSI A161.1 | KCMA certification program | Unverified "high-quality" / "solid wood" claims |
| CARB Phase 2 / TSCA Title VI | 40 CFR Part 770 | "Non-toxic" / "eco-friendly" without certification |
| EPA RRP Rule | 40 CFR Part 745 | "Safe remodel" in pre-1978 homes without RRP disclosure |
| Soft-Close Hardware Warranty | Magnuson-Moss §104(a) | "Lifetime guarantee" without brand + written terms |
| GREENGUARD Gold | FTC Green Guides §260.6(a) | "GREENGUARD certified" without Gold vs. standard distinction |
| FTC 2023 + Magnuson-Moss | FTC §5, 16 CFR Part 255 | "#1 contractor"; "guaranteed" without terms; testimonial violations |
