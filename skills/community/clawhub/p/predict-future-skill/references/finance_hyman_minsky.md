# Hyman Minsky — Finance Forecasting

Levy Economics Institute; Washington University. Primary sources: *Stabilizing
an Unstable Economy* (1986); "The Financial Instability Hypothesis" (Levy WP 74);
empirical work: Bernard et al., *Cambridge Journal of Economics* (2019); Levy
Institute corporate-sector regime studies (2024–25).

**Role in the finance stack:** *When* systems break — credit structure &
fragility.

---

## Two theorems (FIH)

1. An economy has **stable** and **unstable** financing regimes.
2. **Prolonged prosperity** shifts the system from hedge-dominated toward
   **speculative and Ponzi** finance — fragility becomes endogenous; no
   external shock required (Minsky, Levy WP 74).

**Stability is destabilizing:** safety nets and long booms reward risk-taking;
each cushioned crash sets up the next boom (Wray/Levy synthesis, WP 659).

---

## Three financing regimes

Classify units (firms, sectors, shadow banks) by **cash flow vs. debt service**:

| Regime | Cash-flow test |
|--------|----------------|
| **Hedge** | Operating cash flow covers interest **and** principal |
| **Speculative** | Covers interest; must **roll over** principal |
| **Ponzi** | Cannot cover interest from operations; needs new debt or asset sales |

**Tightening path:** monetary/credit constraint turns speculative → Ponzi;
Ponzi units forced to sell → falling collateral → cascade (**Minsky moment**).
Clear only in hindsight, but **regime mix** is diagnosable ex ante.

## What empirics say (use nuance)

**Supports FIH:**
- Share of fragile (speculative + Ponzi) firms matters for systemic vulnerability.
- US nonfinancial corporate data: long-run rise in Ponzi share among **small
  listed** firms since 1980s (Levy 2024–25); fragility concentrated in
  negative-profit entrants.
- 2008 widely cited as vindication of endogenous fragility.

**Caveats for forecasting:**
- **Aggregate leverage alone ≠ fragility** — Pedrosa et al. (2019): economy-wide
  leverage trends do not map cleanly to speculative/Ponzi frequency.
- **Business-cycle frequency:** 1 std-dev GDP decline ≈ **+0.7 pp** Ponzi
  probability (unconditional ~17.5%) — cycle helps, but long-wave **composition**
  of who lists/finances matters more (Levy 2025).
- Minsky describes **long waves**; short-horizon timing of crises remains hard.

Do not treat "high debt" as automatic near-term crash — classify **who** is
Ponzi and **what** triggers forced deleveraging.

## Bubble stages (Kindleberger–Minsky mapping)

Often used together: **displacement → boom → euphoria → profit-taking →
panic**. For 30-day forecasts, prioritize **structure**:

- Margin debt, corporate net debt / EBITDA, LTV in property
- Covenant-lite share, maturity walls, refi calendars
- Credit spreads, primary-market issuance quality
- Fed **Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey** (SLOOS)

## How to apply in this skill

Layer on `general_Philip_Tetlock.md` for finance queries.

1. **Regime map** — Key sectors/actors: hedge, speculative, or Ponzi?
2. **Trigger** — What policy, rate, or collateral shock converts speculative
   to Ponzi?
3. **Minsky downside scenario** — Forced selling path with granular probability
   (pairs with Klein premortem).
4. **Empirical humility** — State when fragility is structural (long wave) vs.
   cyclical (small near-term shift).

**Search targets:** margin debt (FINRA), HY spreads, LME/refi walls, SLOOS,
covenant-lite issuance, sector cash-flow coverage ratios.
