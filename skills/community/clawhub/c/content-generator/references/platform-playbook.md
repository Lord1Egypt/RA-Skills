# Platform Playbook

Use this reference when choosing platform-specific structure and style.

## Xiaohongshu

Best for discovery, personal experience, comparison, and practical lists.

Use:
- 3-5 title candidates with concrete audience or scenario.
- A first-person hook grounded in a situation, not hype.
- Sections: why I noticed it, who it fits, what worked, tradeoffs, buy/no-buy advice.
- Hashtags that match category and scenario.

Avoid:
- Unsupported "best", "must buy", "zero risk", "medical effect" claims.
- Overloaded emoji and vague adjectives without proof.

## Douyin

Best for fast attention and spoken demonstration.

Use:
- 1-2 second hook.
- 15-45 second spoken script.
- Shot-by-shot cues if the user wants video production help.
- CTA that matches intent: save, compare, comment, trial, wait for discount.

Avoid:
- Dense paragraphs.
- Claims that cannot be demonstrated visually.

## Live Selling

Best for real-time persuasion and objection handling.

Use:
- Opening promise.
- Demo sequence.
- Proof point.
- Common objections and replies.
- Offer explanation.
- Calm close.

Avoid:
- Fake urgency.
- "Only today" or inventory claims unless supplied by the user.

## Moments

Best for trusted, lightweight personal recommendations.

Use:
- Short, natural language.
- One concrete reason.
- Low-pressure recommendation.

Avoid:
- Overly polished ad tone.

## Zhihu

Best for rational evaluation and purchase decision support.

Use:
- Clear conclusion first.
- Background and selection criteria.
- Pros, cons, suitable users, alternatives, and when not to buy.
- Evidence/source separation.

Avoid:
- Fan-style hype.

## Marketplace Listing

Best for search and conversion on product pages.

Use:
- Search-friendly title.
- Bullet benefits tied to customer pain points.
- Detail sections: scenario, specs, proof, after-sales, FAQ.

Avoid:
- Keyword stuffing.
- Absolute comparative claims without evidence.

## Ads

Best for testing angles.

Use:
- 3-5 angles: pain point, proof, comparison, scene, offer.
- One claim per variant.
- CTA variants.

Avoid:
- Mixing too many promises into one ad.
