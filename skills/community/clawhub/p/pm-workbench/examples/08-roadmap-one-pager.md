# Example 08 — Roadmap One-Pager

## Scenario

A product lead needs to turn a set of priorities into a staged near-term roadmap.

## Input

> "Help me turn our next-quarter priorities into a one-page roadmap. Our main goal is to improve activation and early retention for the AI workspace product, while keeping enterprise reliability stable."

## What good output looks like

A strong `pm-workbench` response should:

- make the stage goal obvious
- group work into believable themes or phases
- sequence work in a realistic order
- call out what is intentionally not the focus this period
- surface risks and capacity constraints honestly
- make the roadmap feel like a real portfolio choice, not a backlog dump with dates

## Expected output shape

**Roadmap One-Pager**

## Example target output

# Roadmap One-Pager

## 1. Planning period

Next quarter.

## 2. Stage goal

Improve activation and early retention for the AI workspace product while keeping enterprise reliability stable.

## 3. What this roadmap is protecting

This roadmap protects focus and product trust. The product should become easier to adopt and more reliable before the team expands into broader feature surface area.

## 4. Strategic focus

1. **Activation cleanup** - reduce first-use friction.
2. **Early retention quality** - make the product valuable beyond the first session.
3. **Enterprise reliability guardrail** - avoid reliability regression while improving the user path.

## 5. Proposed sequence

### Phase 1 - First-use friction

Simplify onboarding, reduce setup steps, and clarify the path to first meaningful value.

### Phase 2 - Retention support

Improve the workflows that bring users back after initial activation, such as saved context, recent work, or useful next actions.

### Phase 3 - Reliability hardening

Address the enterprise reliability issues that could block adoption or expansion, without widening into a full enterprise roadmap.

## 6. What is intentionally not prioritized

- Broad growth loops before activation is healthier.
- Large collaboration expansion before early retention is stronger.
- Nice-to-have enterprise controls that are not tied to reliability risk.

## 7. Risks and watchouts

- Activation work may become too broad if the team does not pick the top friction points.
- Retention work may need more behavioral data before final scope.
- Enterprise reliability work must stay constrained or it will consume the roadmap.

## 8. Success indicators

- activation rate improves
- time-to-first-value decreases
- early retention improves
- onboarding-related support tickets decrease
- reliability incidents do not increase

## 9. Immediate next steps

Confirm the top onboarding friction points, define the first retention-support workflow, and agree on which enterprise reliability issues are true guardrails for the quarter.

## Why this is useful

Many roadmap drafts are backlog dumps with dates. A one-pager should reveal focus, sequence, and trade-offs clearly.
