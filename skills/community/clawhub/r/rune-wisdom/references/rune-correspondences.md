### Notes on the Correspondence Table

**Ogham correspondences:** The Irish tree-alphabet (Ogham) is mapped to runes, creating a bridge between the Norse and Celtic tree-traditions. This is a modern synthesis, not historical, but provides a useful comparative framework.

**Zodiac correspondences:** The zodiac assignments follow a counterclockwise progression through the Elder Futhark (starting with Fehu at Sagittarius and moving backward through the zodiac). This is a modern esoteric system, not attested in historical sources.

**Chakra correspondences:** Only the 3rd aett (Týr through Othala) has chakra correspondences, mapping from Sahasrara (crown) to Muladhara (root) — i.e., top-down rather than the more common bottom-up progression. This suggests the 3rd aett as the path of spiritual descent-into-matter, complementing the 1st aett's path of material-creation and the 2nd aett's path of challenge-and-transformation. For detailed per-chakra rune interpretations, see §4 below.

**Element system:** The dual elements (e.g., Fehu = Fire/Earth, Hagalaz = Water/Air, Dagaz = Fire/Air, Ingwaz = Water/Earth) reflect the Norse cosmological principle of opposing forces in dynamic tension (see `norse-cosmology.md` §1). Each dual-element rune sits at the boundary between two realms.

## 2. The Annual Runic Cycle — Aswynn's System

From Freya Aswynn's *Northern Magic* (Северная магия), this system maps the 24 runes onto the **8 pagan festivals** of the year, assigning 3 runes to each festival. The cycle begins with the 3rd aett (taking it as the "first" aett), reflecting Aswynn's view that the path of the runes begins with the spiritual/martial (Týr) rather than the material (Fehu).

### Spring Equinox — Тейваз, Беркана, Эваз

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Tiwaz** | Heavenly Father | The sky-god principle; justice and sacrifice as the foundation of the new year |
| **Berkano** | Earth Mother | The birch-goddess; fertility and birth as the earth awakens |
| **Ehwaz** | New Life | The horse/steed; harmonious movement and the partnership of sky-father and earth-mother producing spring |

**Mythological pattern:** Tiwaz (heaven) + Berkano (earth) → Ehwaz (new life). This mirrors the divine marriage (hieros gamos) that generates the annual cycle.

### Beltane / Walpurgis Night — Манназ, Лагуз, Ингуз

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Mannaz** | Mind/Reason | The conscious, rational principle; "Mannaz" also means "man" (male) |
| **Laguz** | Intuition | The watery, unconscious principle; the attraction that draws opposites together |
| **Ingwaz** | Union of Mind & Intuition | "Ingwaz" also means "woman" (female); the seed that results from the union of conscious and unconscious |

**Mythological pattern:** The "divine wedding" — Wodan and Frija (Germanic tradition), or the twin-pairs Njörðr/Nerþus and Freyr/Freyja. Mannaz (masculine consciousness) is drawn by Laguz (feminine intuition) into Ingwaz (the sacred union).

### Midsummer — Отила, Дагаз, Феху

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Othala** | Óðinn | The ancestral heritage; the god who rules the heights of summer |
| **Dagaz** | Summer solstice / Baldr's death | The moment of maximum light that simultaneously contains the seed of darkness — Baldr, son of the Sun, dies at the peak of summer |
| **Fehu** | Freyja/Frigg | Wealth and abundance at the height of summer's productivity |

**Key insight:** Dagaz at Midsummer represents the paradox of the solstice — the longest day is also the moment when days begin to shorten. Baldr's death at the peak of light mirrors this cosmic truth: every apex contains the beginning of decline.

### Lammas / Lughnasadh — Ансуз, Уруз, Турисаз

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Ansuz** | Óðinn's inspiration | The divine breath; the poetic and prophetic inspiration of the chief god |
| **Uruz** | Primal rage | The raw, undirected force of nature; Þórr's storm-power |
| **Thurisaz** | Moderating force | The giant-principle as the buffer between Óðinn's mind and Uruz's rage |

**Mythological pattern:** The struggle between Óðinn and the giants, with Þórr as the protective energy between them (Þórr is half-giant himself). This can be enacted as Þórr's battle with the giant Hrungnir for possession of the Sun, or as Óðinn's contest with the giant Vafþrúðnir.

**Psychological interpretation:** Uruz = primitive rage; Ansuz = reason; Thurisaz = the moderating force between them.

### Autumn Equinox — Кеназ, Райдо, Гебо

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Kenaz** | Knowledge (Light) | The torch of conscious understanding |
| **Raido** | Co-Knowledge (Darkness) | The path/journey of experiential understanding |
| **Gebo** | Balance | The gift of equilibrium; the x-shaped rune as the crossroads where Light and Darkness meet as equals |

**Mythological pattern:** Óðinn's initiation at Yggdrasil — suspended between the forces of Light and Darkness, achieving the highest degree of awareness. Gebo as the equinox-point itself: not the victory of one force over another, but the sacred exchange between them.

### Halloween / Samhain — Вуньо, Хагалаз, Наутиз

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Wunjo** | Shamanic Óðinn | Óðinn in his shamanic aspect, consulting the dead through the völva |
| **Hagalaz** | The Völva (hægtesse) | The hail-rune as the völva herself; the hag who dwells between worlds |
| **Nauthiz** | Óðinn's intention | The necessity/driving force behind Óðinn's journey to Hel — his need to understand Baldr's dream |

**Mythological pattern:** Óðinn's journey to Hel, during which the *Völuspá* (Prophecy of the Völva) is recited. The Anglo-Saxon month is *blótmonaþ* ("blood-month") — the time of ritual sacrifice. The dead can cross into the world of the living.

**Additional meanings:** Hagalaz = the forces of Winter, the Frost Giants, and the Purifying Fire. In Holland and Germany, young men wore frightening masks and practiced ritual theft — symbolizing the Einherjar transforming into dead heroes.

### Winter Solstice — Иса, Йера, Эйваз

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Isa** | The frozen stillness | The ice of deepest winter; concentration and preservation |
| **Jera** | The year's turning | The cycle-point where winter turns toward summer; the promise of Baldr's return |
| **Eihwaz** | Yggdrasil | The eternal world-tree sustaining life through the darkness; the Christmas tree as a survival of this belief |

**Mythological pattern:** The greeting of the returning Sun. In eastern Holland, a burning wheel was rolled down a hill on this day, symbolizing the Sun's return.

### Candlemas / Imbolc — Перто, Альгиз, Совуло

| Rune | Role | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **Perthro** | Frigg / the Norns | The goddess of fate and hidden knowledge; the lot-cup from which destiny is drawn |
| **Algiz** | Erda / the Valkyries | The protective rune; Erda as mother of the Valkyries; the swan-maiden's sanctuary |
| **Sowilo** | The returning Sun | Solar force gathering strength; the return of life-forces to the world |

**Mythological pattern:** The ritual kindling of fire. Perthro can be replaced by Erda (as mother of the Valkyries, whose rune is Algiz). Frigg as mother of the sun-god Baldr parallels Erda as mother of the Valkyries — both are goddess-mothers who give birth to the forces that will save/renew the world.

## 4. Rune-Chakra Correspondences: Detailed Per-Chakra Interpretations

**Source:** Compiled from chernayamagiya.com forum thread "Значения рун" (Post #58, author Пузя), originally from magura.mypage.ru. Supplemented with community observations from the same thread.

### Overview

This section provides a detailed system for interpreting runes in chakra-based spreads. Unlike the brief chakra column in §1 above (which only maps the 3rd aett to chakras top-down), this system assigns meaning to every rune at each of the seven chakras, providing nuanced physical, emotional, and spiritual readings. The system is particularly useful for energy-body diagnostics, healing work, and understanding how rune energies manifest at different levels of the human subtle body.

### Chakra Framework

The seven chakras correspond to the seven levels of human existence:

1. **Муладхара (Muladhara)** — Root — Survival, physical vitality, groundedness, genetic heritage
2. **Свадхистхана (Svadhisthana)** — Sacral — Sexuality, relationships, pleasure, creative flow
3. **Манипура (Manipura)** — Solar Plexus — Will, power, self-assertion, initiative
4. **Анахата (Anahata)** — Heart — Love, compassion, emotional openness, giving/receiving
5. **Вишуддха (Vishuddha)** — Throat — Communication, self-expression, purpose, creativity
6. **Аджна (Ajna)** — Third Eye — Intuition, concentration, thought patterns, inner vision
7. **Сахасрара (Sahasrara)** — Crown — Spiritual awareness, wholeness, transcendence, unity

### Methodological Notes

1. **Diagnostic Use:** In chakra-based rune spreads, draw one rune per chakra position (7 runes total) and interpret according to the chakra-specific meanings above. Reversed runes indicate blockages or challenges at that particular level.

2. **Therapeutic Application:** When a blockage is identified at a specific chakra, the upright version of the same rune can be used as a talisman or meditation focus for that chakra to restore balanced energy flow.

3. **Cross-Reference with Position Systems:** The chakra readings align naturally with the position frameworks (see `rune-three-positions-bednenko.md` for Elder Futhark, `northumbrian-position-analysis.md` for Northumbrian runes):
   - **Direct** position at a chakra = the principle manifests healthily
   - **Mirrored** position at a chakra (Elder Futhark only) = conscious modulation of the principle
   - **Inverted** position at a chakra = the principle is blocked or distorted
   - **Note:** Northumbrian runes use the Western binary framework (Direct / Inverted) rather than Bednenko's three-position system

4. **Limitations:** The chakra system is Indian, not Norse. This correspondence is a modern syncretic development. Traditional Norse energy-body models use different frameworks (e.g., the hamr/hugr/fylgja system). However, practitioners find the chakra-rune mapping productive for healing and diagnostic work.

5. **Comprehensive Source Note:** The Fehu chakra readings above are provided as a complete example from the original source. Detailed chakra readings for all 24 runes would follow the same pattern but are extensive. The original source (magura.mypage.ru) contained similar detailed entries for other runes, organized by rune name in the URL structure (e.g., fehu.html, uruz.html, etc.).

### 5.2. The Uthark System (Agrell's Reordered Numerology)

#### Historical Background

**Sigurd Agrell** (1881–1937), Swedish professor of Slavic languages at Lund University, proposed the **Uthark theory** in a series of publications:

- *Runornas talmystik och dess antika förebild* (1927) — "The Number-Mysticism of the Runes and Its Ancient Model"
- *Senantik mysteriereligion och nordisk runmagi* (1931) — "Late-Antique Mystery Religion and Nordic Rune-Magic"
- *Die spätantike Alphabet-Mystik und die Runenreihe* (1932) — The definitive work linking runic numerology to late-antique Greek/Hebrew alphabet mysticism
- *Die pergamenische Zauberscheibe und das Tarockspiel* (1936) — Connecting the Pergamon magical disk and Tarot
- *Rökstenens chiffergåtor* (1930) — Analysis of the Rök Stone's cipher inscriptions

#### The Core Principle

The Uthark shifts Fehu from position 1 to position 0 (or 24), making Uruz the first rune. The name "Uthark" derives from the first three runes in this new ordering: U-Th-A-R-K (Ur-Thurs-As-Reid-Ken). This creates a "nightside" ordering — revealing hidden, chthonic forces that the standard Futhark's "dayside" conceals.

#### The Uthark Sequence

### 5.4. Numerical Formulas in Runic Inscriptions and Magic

#### The Sigtuna Amulet Formula

The most explicit numerical formula found in the archaeological record. From the copper amulet found near Sigtuna, Sweden (dated 1050–1100 CE):

> "I say: **three [runes] Thurs, nine [runes] Need**. I conjure as the keeper of the sanctuary."

In the original: *þrír þurs, níu nauðr*

This is an **alliterative formula** — a recognized type of runic incantation where specific runes are called out with their repetition count:
- **Three Thurs-runes** (ᚦ Thurisaz × 3): Giant-force, protective/threatening power, Thor's hammer
- **Nine Need-runes** (ᚾ Nauthiz × 9): Constrained necessity, the fire born from limitation — amplified by the sacred number

The formula **þrír þurs, níu nauðr** is not merely a description but an incantation — the act of speaking it activates the specified rune-forces in the specified quantities. The eril who carved this was performing a ritual act by writing it, and the alliterative pairing of 3 and 9 (the two most sacred numbers) creates a powerful resonance.

### 5.6. Runic Cipher Systems

Runic cipher systems encode runes using their position within the aett structure, creating a two-number code: **(aett number, position within aett)**. This is both a concealment technique and a magical amplification — the cipher adds layers of numerological meaning to the inscribed text.

#### Elder Futhark Cipher Grid

| Position → | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
|-------------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st Aett** (Freyr) | ᚠ F | ᚢ U | ᚦ Th | ᚨ A | ᚱ R | ᚲ K | ᚷ G | ᚹ W |
| **2nd Aett** (Heimdall) | ᚺ H | ᚾ N | ᛁ I | ᛃ J | ᛇ Ï | ᛈ P | ᛉ Z | ᛊ S |
| **3rd Aett** (Tyr) | ᛏ T | ᛒ B | ᛖ E | ᛗ M | ᛚ L | ᛜ Ŋ | ᛟ O | ᛞ D |

Example encodings: (1,3) = ᚦ Thurisaz; (2,2) = ᚾ Nauthiz; (3,7) = ᛟ Othala

#### Encoding Methods

**1. Branch/Twig Runes:** A vertical trunk with strokes on left and right. **Left strokes = aett number; right strokes = position within aett.** For example, a trunk with 2 left strokes and 5 right strokes encodes (2,5) = ᛇ Eihwaz.

**2. Tent Runes** (used on the Rök Stone): An X-shape with strokes on the four arms. Read clockwise: arm 1 = aett, arm 2 = position, arm 3 = aett of next rune, arm 4 = position of next rune. Each X encodes **two runes simultaneously**.

**3. Isruna System:** Long strokes indicate aett, short strokes indicate position. For example, 2 long + 5 short = (2,5) = ᛇ Eihwaz.

**4. Rune-specific ciphers:** A particular rune shape is used as the base; left-facing branches = aett, right-facing = position.

#### The Rök Stone (Ög 136, early 9th century)

The longest known runic inscription in stone (~760 characters), the Rök Stone uses **two encryption methods simultaneously**:
1. **Caesar cipher** (displacement): letters shifted by a fixed number
2. **Cipher runes** (aett-and-position system): tent runes and branch runes

The inscription contains bracketed cipher sequences like `[3:3 = ᚦ]` meaning "3rd aett, 3rd position = ᚦ (Thurisaz)" and `[2:5 = ᛌ]` = 2nd aett, 5th position = Sowilo. The stone is intentionally obscure, possibly as part of a magic ritual where the act of encoding itself holds power.

**Important note:** In some cipher systems, Freyr's ætt and Týr's ætt swap places (1st becomes 3rd and vice versa). The order of runes within the Younger Futhark changed post-13th century (l and m swapped). This is critical for correct decipherment — Jón Ólafsson's *Runologia* (1732–1752) preserves the earlier Viking Age order.

#### Cipher Interpretation of Repeated Runes

Some scholars interpret repeated runes as **aett-and-position ciphers** rather than simple multiplication. For example:
- `kk` = Kaunan (1st aett, position 6) + count 2 = 2nd rune in 1st aett = ᚢ Uruz
- `kkk` = Kaunan + count 3 = 3rd rune in 1st aett = ᚦ Thurisaz
- `iiii` = Isa (2nd aett, position 3) + count 4 = 4th rune in 2nd aett = ᛃ Jera

Under this interpretation, the Lindholm amulet's sequence `aaaaaaazzznnn` is not "8 gods + 3 protection + danger" but rather a cipher encoding specific rune-words — making the inscription both a numerical formula AND a coded message.

### 5.8. Sklyarova's Numerological Method

#### The "Absorption" Method (Метод «Поглощения» Футарка)

Vera Sklyarova (Вера Склярова) — Russian tarologist-occultist, TV personality, and author — developed a proprietary numerological technique for analyzing runic divination spreads, documented in her book *Золотые руны* (Golden Runes, 2018).

The method uses the **numerological positions of runes** in the Futhark to create a deeper layer of interpretation beyond the rune's traditional meaning. When runes appear in a spread, their **positional numbers** are analyzed through numerological reduction, and the resulting number "absorbs" (поглощение) or subsumes additional interpretive layers.

Sklyarova does NOT assign different number values beyond the standard 1–24 positions — her innovation is the **method of interpretation** using those positional numbers through "absorption" and "mandala" constructions.

#### Rune Mandalas and Numerological Codes

Part Two of *Golden Runes* covers:
- **Понятие числовой мандалы** (Concept of the Numerical Mandala) — a visual diagram of rune positions forming a numerological pattern
- **Соответствия чисел и планет** (Correspondences of Numbers and Planets) — each number 1–9 (and beyond) associated with a planetary force
- **Чакры и планеты влияния** (Chakras and Planets of Influence) — esoteric anatomy mapped to rune positions
- **Трактовка чисел мандалы** (Interpretation of Mandala Numbers) — specific divinatory meanings for each number in the mandala
- **Нумерологический код любого конкретного события** (Numerological Code of Any Specific Event) — calculating the "number code" of an event by combining rune positions
- **Comparative analysis** of rune mandalas in Latin and Russian transcription

#### The Fifth Element: Time

Sklyarova's unique contribution is the addition of a **fifth element — Time** — to the standard four-element system (Fire, Air, Water, Earth). In her classification:
- **Fire runes**: Fehu, Thurisaz, Raidho, Kenaz (and others)
- **Earth runes**: Uruz (and others)
- **Air runes**: Ansuz (and others)
- **Water runes**: (various)
- **Time element**: Dagaz (and others)

The Time element does not have a direct number correspondence in the standard 1–24 system but adds an interpretive dimension when calculating rune mandalas — it introduces the concept of temporal sequence and kairos (opportune moment) into the numerological framework.

**Single rune value** = position in the Futhark (Fehu = 1, Uruz = 2, ..., Othala = 24)

**Name calculation**: Convert a person's name to runes, then sum the position numbers. For example:
- The name "Odin" → ᛟ(24) + ᛞ(23) + ᛁ(11) + ᚾ(10) = 68 → 6+8 = 14 → 1+4 = **5**

**Formula verification**: A spell's "target number" can be verified by summing the runes used. For example:
- Protection formula (Algiz + Thurisaz) = 15 + 3 = 18 → 1+8 = **9** (the sacred number — a well-constructed formula)
- Victory formula (Sowilo + Tiwaz) = 16 + 17 = 33 → 3+3 = **6** (harmony and magnification)

**Aett value**: The sum of any aett carries its own power:
- 1st Aett (1–8) = 36 → 3+6 = **9** (the sacred nine)
- 2nd Aett (9–16) = 100 → **1** (unity, the source)
- 3rd Aett (17–24) = 164 → 1+6+4 = 11 → **2** (duality, partnership)

#### Numerological Reduction

Numbers are reduced to a single digit (1–9) through addition, except for the **master numbers** 11 and 22, which are sometimes preserved:

| Digit | Runic Meaning |
|-------|--------------|
| **1** | Origin, primal force, the All; Uruz/Fehu energy; new beginnings |
| **2** | Duality, partnership, balance; Gebo energy; the need for exchange |
| **3** | Creation, Odin's power, divine triad; Ansuz energy; expression |
| **4** | Structure, order, foundation; Raidho/Jera energy; stability |
| **5** | Change, freedom, transformation; Eihwaz/Laguz energy; the pivot |
| **6** | Harmony, magnification, responsibility; Kenaz/Ingwaz energy; the balance point |
| **7** | Mystery, spiritual seeking, the quest; Perthro/Algiz energy; the sacred path |
| **8** | Power, material mastery, cosmic order; Wunjo/Sowilo energy; the generative force |
| **9** | Sacred completion, Odin's number, transformation; Hagalaz/Nauthiz energy; the threshold |

#### Important Caveat

As noted in the Velya forum's original Numerical Formulas section: "This is a vast subject with much modern speculation in the sphere of numerology, which itself as an established system appeared relatively recently, though its beginnings are indeed known from Early Hermeticism."

Runic gematria exists as a **modern reconstruction** (Agrell, Thorsson, Karlsson, Sklyarova) but has **no continuous historical tradition** comparable to Hebrew or Greek gematria. The position-value system is the only numerological framework with any historical basis (via the aett-and-position ciphers), while all interpretive overlays are modern. Practitioners should be transparent about this distinction.

### Notable Correspondence Patterns

**Cards with dual runes:** Three Major Arcana cards carry two runes instead of one, indicating composite or dual-aspect archetypes:
- **The Lovers** (Gebo + Ehwaz): The gift/partnership principle (Gebo) joined with the harmony/loyalty principle (Ehwaz) — the tarot's "two becoming one" expressed through two complementary runes rather than a single glyph
- **The Moon** (Algiz + Othala): Protection under threat (Algiz) combined with heritage/belonging (Othala) — the card's struggle between darkness (Höðr) and light (Baldr) for Nanna's rose maps to the tension between shielding and inherited identity
- **The World** (Hagalaz + Perthro): Cosmic destruction-creation (Hagalaz) anchored by fate/hidden pattern (Perthro) — Ymir's body becoming the world is the primordial act of chaos giving birth to structured existence

**Uthark resonance:** Agrell's Uthark system (§5.2) already noted that Fehu at position 0/24 mirrors the Tarot Fool. This deck places Fehu on The Tower (XVI) instead, and Jera on The Fool (0). The Fool = Jera mapping suggests the Fool's journey IS the cycle itself — not the "zero before one" but the eternal return. This is a genuinely different philosophical take from the Uthark's Fehu-Fool correspondence and may be useful as a contrasting interpretation when a querent's question touches on cycles vs. beginnings.

**Deity-rune alignment insights:**
- **Thurisaz = The Emperor (Thor)** rather than the more common Odin-Fehu or Tyr-Emperor mapping: this deck makes the Emperor about raw protective force rather than governance or law, which aligns with the Velya school's Thurisaz interpretation (obstacle/gate/blade)
- **Ansuz = Death (Odin)** rather than the more common Magician/Emperor Odin: Odin as psychopomp leading souls to Valhalla makes Ansuz the rune of divine transition rather than divine speech
- **Fehu = The Tower**: Wealth/abundance placed on the card of catastrophic collapse — Fehu reversed's "loss of everything" as the Tower's destruction, and Fáfnir's cursed hoard as wealth that destroys its holder
