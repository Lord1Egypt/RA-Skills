# Fragment Thoughts Organizer

Catch stray thoughts, half-sentences, overheard snippets, people, places, links, and gut feelings you toss in throughout the day. File them into a same-day archive that preserves your original voice. Over time, surface latent connections — semantic, temporal, emotional, people, scene — that you didn't notice yourself.

## What It Does

- **Catch**: Toss in any fragment — a sentence, a name, a feeling, a half-thought. The guide echoes it back and files it. No questions asked.
- **Organize**: Say "organize my day" and get a structured archive grouped by type (People / Ideas / Scenes / Feelings / Follow-ups), not by topic. Original words preserved.
- **Connect**: Say "what have I been thinking about" and the guide surfaces cross-period patterns across 5 threads — semantic, temporal, emotional, people, scene. Facts only, no interpretation.

## What It Doesn't Do

- Not a knowledge base
- Not meeting notes
- Not a task manager
- Not a diary
- Does not think for you

## Design Principles

1. Fragments are not clutter — they are uncrystallized insight
2. Organizing is not summarizing
3. Connections surface; the guide does not interpret
4. Silence beats filler
5. Cross-period is the gold mine
