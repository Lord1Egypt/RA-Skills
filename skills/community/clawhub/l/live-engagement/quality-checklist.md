# Live Show Quality Checklist

Validate every TikTok Shop live selling plan against this checklist before going live. All items should be confirmed. Any unchecked item represents a risk to session performance.

---

## Category 1: Pre-Show Technical Setup (6 items)

- [ ] 1.1 Primary camera, lighting, and microphone tested and producing clear, well-lit, broadcast-quality output
- [ ] 1.2 Internet upload speed verified at 5 Mbps or above within the last hour
- [ ] 1.3 Backup mobile device fully charged, logged into the TikTok account, and ready to take over streaming if the primary device fails
- [ ] 1.4 All overlay graphics (price comparisons, countdown timer templates, product labels, brand lower-thirds) loaded and tested in streaming software
- [ ] 1.5 Physical product samples arranged in presentation order within arm's reach of the host, with packaging removed where applicable for faster on-camera access
- [ ] 1.6 Streaming environment free of background distractions, confidential information, and unintended brand visibility

## Category 2: TikTok Shop Configuration (5 items)

- [ ] 2.1 Every product in the session lineup has a live product link created in TikTok Shop Seller Center
- [ ] 2.2 Each product link tested on a separate device confirming correct product page, correct live price, accurate inventory count, and functional add-to-cart
- [ ] 2.3 All discount codes and promotional pricing configured in the backend and verified with a test cart (then voided)
- [ ] 2.4 Product pin order matches the run sheet sequence so pins can be activated quickly during transitions
- [ ] 2.5 Shipping settings and estimated delivery times reviewed and accurate for all promoted products

## Category 3: Show Structure and Pacing (6 items)

- [ ] 3.1 Run sheet completed with specific start/end times for every segment, product, and engagement break
- [ ] 3.2 Hero SKU positioned within the expected peak viewership window (typically 20-40 minutes into the session)
- [ ] 3.3 Doorbuster or opening deal placed in the first product segment with a genuinely compelling discount
- [ ] 3.4 Product segments alternate between price points and energy levels to maintain variety
- [ ] 3.5 Non-selling engagement breaks (polls, giveaways, challenges) scheduled at least every 20-25 minutes
- [ ] 3.6 Closing segment includes a product recap, final flash deal, giveaway draw, and next-session announcement with a planned duration of 15-20 minutes

## Category 4: Engagement Plan (6 items)

- [ ] 4.1 At least three giveaways planned with prizes, entry conditions, announcement times, and draw times documented
- [ ] 4.2 Giveaways distributed across the session (not clustered) with the final draw in the last 20 minutes to sustain retention
- [ ] 4.3 Comment trigger keywords assigned to each product segment and documented in the run sheet
- [ ] 4.4 At least two flash deals planned with specific products, discount levels, and countdown durations
- [ ] 4.5 At least one viewer poll or vote planned with a clear question, response options, and commitment to act on the result
- [ ] 4.6 Social proof narration points identified (sales milestones, viewer count targets, customer testimonial quotes to read aloud)

## Category 5: Script and Messaging (5 items)

- [ ] 5.1 Opening line scripted -- the exact first sentence the host will say on camera, rehearsed aloud
- [ ] 5.2 Price reveal formula documented for each product with retail price anchor, competitor reference (if applicable), and live-only discount clearly framed
- [ ] 5.3 Scarcity language prepared with actual inventory numbers for limited-stock products
- [ ] 5.4 Calls to action are specific and directive ("Tap the yellow basket at the bottom of your screen right now") rather than vague ("Check out the link")
- [ ] 5.5 Transition scripts between segments and clusters drafted to maintain flow and tease upcoming content

## Category 6: Team Coordination (4 items)

- [ ] 6.1 Host briefed on full run sheet including segment timing, product order, engagement triggers, and key talking points
- [ ] 6.2 Moderator briefed on comment response guidelines, giveaway entry tracking, and escalation protocol for technical issues or inappropriate comments
- [ ] 6.3 Backstage assistant has live inventory dashboard open and a communication channel to the host (earpiece, text, or off-camera signal) for real-time stock updates
- [ ] 6.4 Roles and handoff triggers clearly defined for co-host segments, if applicable, including who manages which products and engagement beats

## Category 7: Post-Show Readiness (5 items)

- [ ] 7.1 Post-show data collection template prepared with all metric fields ready to fill (GMV, units, peak viewers, average watch time, comments, shares, follower growth)
- [ ] 7.2 Per-product tracking sheet ready to record units sold and revenue by SKU immediately after the session
- [ ] 7.3 Giveaway fulfillment process confirmed: winner contact method, prize shipping logistics, and timeline communicated to the responsible team member
- [ ] 7.4 Customer support team alerted to expect increased inquiry volume in the 24 hours following the session, with product-specific FAQ responses prepared
- [ ] 7.5 Next session date tentatively selected so it can be announced during the closing segment, even if the full plan is not yet built
