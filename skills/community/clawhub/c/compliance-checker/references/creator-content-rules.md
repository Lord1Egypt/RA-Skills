# Creator & Affiliate Content Rules

Creator content sells harder and violates faster than listings. Everything a creator says about your product in shoppable content counts against your shop — audit it with the same severity as your own copy.

## 1. What gets shops penalized via creators

1. **Claims** — creators improvising health/results claims ("this cured my acne") in videos or lives. The claim taxonomy in `references/claims-and-categories.md` applies verbatim to spoken words, captions, and on-screen text.
2. **Missing disclosure** — affiliate/sponsored content without the platform's paid-partnership label and, where local law requires, verbal/visible #ad disclosure. Platform label alone may not satisfy ad law; require both.
3. **Prohibited formats** — before/after transformations, weight-loss journeys tied to the product, medical costumes/settings, shock hooks, fake "I found this hack" pretending no commercial tie.
4. **Deceptive promo mechanics** — creators inventing discounts ("90% off today only!") that don't exist at checkout, or misstating free-shipping/gift terms.
5. **IP violations** — commercial-use music not licensed, third-party clips, competitor brand mentions or logo appearances.
6. **Off-platform redirection** — telling viewers to buy elsewhere or move to other apps in shoppable content.

## 2. The creator brief (do/don't sheet)

Issue per product, in the creator's language, one page max:

**DO say (pre-approved claim bank):**
- 5-10 compliant, punchy claims written for speech ("my skin looks brighter since I started", "it holds my hair all day")
- The actual offer terms verbatim (price, discount, dates)
- Required disclosure wording and where it goes (verbal in first seconds + caption tag + platform label)

**DON'T say (with the reason in one phrase each):**
- Treatment/cure words for this category (list them concretely: "cures", "heals", "anti-aging", "burns fat"…)
- Specific numbers without evidence ("10x", "99%", "in 7 days")
- "FDA approved", "doctor recommended", "clinically proven"
- Invented urgency or discounts beyond the approved offer
- Before/after comparisons (this category)

**Logistics:** script/storyboard pre-check required before filming for restricted categories; final cut review before posting for the first 3 videos of any new creator; whitelist of approved music.

## 3. Live-stream specifics

Live commerce is the highest-risk surface: spontaneous, recorded, and monitored. Rules: pre-approved claim bank pinned in the studio; a moderator with a kill list of banned words; no diagnosis-style interaction with viewers' health questions ("that rash sounds like X, this helps" = treatment claim); price announcements must match the live checkout price in real time; replays inherit violations — purge non-compliant segments before publishing replays.

## 4. Auditing a creator deliverable — SOP

1. Transcribe speech and on-screen text (don't audit from memory of one viewing).
2. Run the claim taxonomy over every sentence; grade Critical/High/Medium/Low.
3. Check disclosure: platform label set? Verbal disclosure within the first moments? Caption tag present? Local-law check for the target market.
4. Check format: B/A, medical staging, fear hooks, fake-discovery framing.
5. Check offer accuracy against the live checkout state.
6. Check audio/IP licensing.
7. Verdict: approve / approve-with-edits (timestamped edit list) / reject-and-rebrief.

## 5. Managing repeat offenders

- First violation: rebrief with the specific clip timestamped; require pre-post review for next 3 videos.
- Second: pause the collaboration; violations are now a pattern attached to your shop.
- Track per-creator compliance in your roster (strikes, categories, pre-check status). The 20% of creators producing 80% of violations should not be promoting your restricted-category products.
- Contract clauses to require: compliance with platform policy and the brief, pre-approval right for restricted categories, takedown-on-request within 24h, liability for invented claims.

## 6. Evidence file (substantiation vault)

Maintain per product: test reports/COAs, certifications, the approved claim bank with the evidence each claim rests on, brand authorization documents, and dated screenshots of offers. Appeals are won by sellers who can attach the file the same day; "we'll get the lab report from the supplier" loses appeal windows.
