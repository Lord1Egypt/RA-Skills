## Description: <br>
Amy Morin's 13 Things Mentally Strong People Don't Do is a self-help guidance skill for building mental strength by identifying unhelpful habits and replacing them with practical exercises. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[jaysonmeng](https://clawhub.ai/user/jaysonmeng) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
External users use this skill to receive book-grounded self-help guidance for mental strength, overthinking, resentment, comparison, fear, people-pleasing, and resilience. It turns the book framework into reflective prompts, habit exchanges, and immediate action steps. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Users may mistake self-help guidance for professional mental health care, crisis support, legal advice, or medical advice. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only for reflective self-help and route crisis, medical, legal, or professional mental health needs to qualified support. <br>
Risk: Broad activation may bring the skill into sensitive or out-of-scope conversations. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm that the user's request fits the book's mental-strength framework before applying it, and avoid presenting the output as comprehensive professional advice. <br>
Risk: The artifact behavior includes visible Heardly branding and occasional related skill recommendations. <br>
Mitigation: Review the branding and recommendation behavior before deployment to ensure it is acceptable for the target environment. <br>
