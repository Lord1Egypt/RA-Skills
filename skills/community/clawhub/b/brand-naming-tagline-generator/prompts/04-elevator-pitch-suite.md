# Prompt 4: Elevator Pitch Suite

## Instructions
Run this last. You'll get every short-form pitch format you need — from a 5-second intro to a full 60-second story.

---

## Prompt

You are a communications coach and pitch consultant. You help founders, freelancers, and small business owners articulate what they do clearly, confidently, and memorably. You understand that different contexts require different pitch lengths, and that a great pitch always ends with a clear next step.

**Brand Details:**
- Brand name: [e.g. SunVault]
- Tagline: [e.g. Power That Pays You Back]
- Problem you solve (be specific): [e.g. Nevada homeowners are paying $200-400/month in electric bills with no relief in sight]
- Who you solve it for: [e.g. Nevada homeowners who own their home and have a roof younger than 15 years]
- Your key differentiator: [e.g. We guarantee installation in 14 days or less — fastest in Nevada; we use Tier 1 panels with 25-year warranties; no high-pressure sales tactics]
- Proof point (stat, customer result, or credential): [e.g. 340 Nevada homes powered, average customer saves $187/month, A+ BBB rating]
- Desired action from listener: [e.g. agree to a free 15-minute roof assessment, visit website, take my card and call this week]

**Generate the following:**

### Section 1: The 5-Second Intro

**Format:** "[Your name] with [Brand Name]. We [what you do in 5 words or fewer]."

Generate 3 versions:
1. Version A (function-first): Leads with what you do
2. Version B (result-first): Leads with the outcome
3. Version C (curiosity hook): Leads with a surprising claim

Tip below each: When to use this version (networking event, cold call, party, intro email, etc.)

### Section 2: The 30-Second Pitch

**Structure:** Hook (1 sentence) → Problem (1 sentence) → Solution (1 sentence) → Proof (1 sentence) → CTA (1 sentence)

Generate 2 versions:
- Version A: Conversational / casual (coffee shop, networking event)
- Version B: Professional / direct (sales call opener, investor intro)

After each, note: "What to do with your hands / body during this pitch:" (1 tip)

### Section 3: The 60-Second Pitch

**Structure:** 
1. Attention hook (10 sec) — a question, surprising stat, or bold claim
2. Problem statement (15 sec) — make them feel the pain
3. Solution + differentiator (20 sec) — what you do and why you're different
4. Proof + social proof (10 sec) — one concrete result
5. Call to action (5 sec) — one clear next step

Write the full pitch as a script. Word count: 130-160 words. Include a "(pause here)" stage direction at the most impactful moment.

### Section 4: Cold Email Subject Lines

Generate 5 subject line options for a cold outreach email to a prospect:
1. **Curiosity-gap:** Makes them wonder what's inside
2. **Specific result:** Names a concrete outcome
3. **Question:** Asks about their pain point
4. **Social proof:** References a recognizable result
5. **Ultra-short:** 3 words or fewer

For each: subject line + predicted open rate context (why this style works for cold email)

### Section 5: LinkedIn "About" Section Opening

Write 3 variations of the first sentence for a LinkedIn About section (this is what shows before "...see more"):
- Version A: Leads with a bold claim about results
- Version B: Leads with who you serve
- Version C: Leads with a personal story hook

Each must be under 200 characters (LinkedIn preview cutoff).

### Section 6: Social Bio Templates

**Twitter/X bio (160 chars max):**
Write 2 options — one benefit-led, one personality-led.

**Instagram bio (150 chars max):**
Write 2 options — include a call-to-action line and emoji placement suggestion.

**LinkedIn headline (220 chars max):**
Write 2 options — one keyword-rich (for search), one story-driven (for humans).

### Section 7: The One-Liner Test

End with this:  
"Your ultimate one-liner — the sentence you say when someone asks 'So what do you do?' at a party:"  
**[One sentence, 10-15 words, no jargon, makes them say 'oh interesting, tell me more.']**  

Generate 3 options, ranked by memorability.
