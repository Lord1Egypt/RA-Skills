## Description: <br>
Instructions for creating engaging, viral-optimized, and SEO/GEO-friendly blog content for general human audiences across platforms like Medium, Zhihu, and LinkedIn. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Writers, marketers, founders, and research teams use this skill to turn ground-truth material such as papers, logs, interviews, or bullet points into high-utility blog drafts optimized for reader engagement, SEO, and answer-engine visibility. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Drafting from private logs, interviews, or unpublished research can expose sensitive source material in generated blog content. <br>
Mitigation: Provide only material intended for publication, or explicitly instruct the agent which excerpts must remain private. <br>
Risk: The skill encourages first-person practitioner framing, which can create inaccurate personal claims if the agent overstates the user's experience. <br>
Mitigation: Review first-person statements and remove or revise claims that the user did not personally verify. <br>
Risk: The skill may insert promotional links and calls to action into public-facing drafts. <br>
Mitigation: Review links, tracking destinations, and calls to action before publishing. <br>
Risk: The skill may propose creating draft, metadata, reference, note, resource, or section files during the writing workflow. <br>
Mitigation: Tell the agent before use whether file creation is allowed and where draft materials should be stored. <br>
