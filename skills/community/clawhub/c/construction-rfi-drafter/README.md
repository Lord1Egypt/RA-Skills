# Construction RFI Drafter

Turns a field question into a properly formatted **Construction Request for Information (RFI)** with drawing- and spec-referenced context, a contractor's proposed interpretation, explicit schedule and cost impact, a required-response-by date, and a clarity checklist that blocks vague RFIs.

## When to use

- A field condition does not match the drawings or specifications.
- The drawings and specifications conflict with each other.
- A material or product substitution is being proposed.
- A clarification is needed before a sub can fabricate, order, or install.

## What it produces

- Standard RFI document: number, project, date, subject, references (sheet number, detail, spec section), question, contractor's proposed interpretation/answer, schedule impact, cost impact, required-response-by date, distribution list.
- Clarity checklist that blocks transmittal if the RFI has no drawing reference, no proposed interpretation, or no impact statement.
- Follow-up trigger language for use when the response date passes without an answer.
- A short subject line suitable for the project's RFI log.

## What it does **not** do

- It does not give engineering opinions or signed/sealed design direction.
- It does not invent drawing numbers, detail callouts, spec sections, or contract clauses; missing references must be supplied by the user.
- It does not commit to a cost or schedule impact; the contractor must verify and approve numbers before transmittal.

## Distribution

All transmittals are project-confidential. The skill produces a draft RFI only — the project manager must approve before it goes to the design team, owner, or owner's rep.
