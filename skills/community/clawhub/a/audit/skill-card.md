## Use Case: <br>
External users and developers use Audit to collect evidence, analyze discrepancies, and produce audit-style findings for technical, legal, financial, and ethical artifacts. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill asks an agent to inspect sensitive code, contracts, and financial materials while promising immutable logging without clear consent or retention limits. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only on materials you are authorized to analyze, and avoid submitting secrets, private contracts, proprietary code, or regulated financial data unless logging, access, redaction, deletion, and retention behavior are acceptable. <br>
Risk: Audit-style findings may be treated as authoritative even when the skill has not documented its standards, evidence handling, or validation process. <br>
Mitigation: Review findings before relying on them, and ask the publisher to clarify what standards are used, how evidence is stored, and whether logs can be disabled or deleted. <br>
