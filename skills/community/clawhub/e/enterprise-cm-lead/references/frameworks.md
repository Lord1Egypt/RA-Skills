# Enterprise Change Management — Framework Reference

## Five-Part Individual Adoption Model

| Stage | Description | Diagnostic Question |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Awareness | Understanding why the change is necessary | Do they know why this is happening? |
| Commitment | Willingness to support and participate | Are they willing to engage? |
| Knowledge | Understanding what to do and how | Do they know the new process/system/expectation? |
| Ability | Demonstrated capability in live conditions | Can they perform under real conditions? |
| Reinforcement | Mechanisms sustaining the change | Are leaders reinforcing new behaviours? |

## Three-Phase Organizational Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Prepare the Approach
- Change characteristics assessment
- Stakeholder impact profile
- Readiness and risk summary
- Leadership alignment assessment
- Initial change strategy

### Phase 2: Manage the Change
- Master change plan
- Sponsor action plan
- Communications plan
- People manager coaching plan
- Training and learning plan
- Resistance response plan

### Phase 3: Sustain the Outcomes
- Adoption and proficiency review
- Sustainment plan
- Reinforcement actions
- Transition to operations handoff
- Lessons learned and capability recommendations

## Change Success Triangle

| Condition | What It Means | Risk When Weak |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Leadership & Sponsorship | Active, visible, consistent executive support | Initiative loses momentum and credibility |
| Delivery Execution | Technical delivery aligned with business readiness | Launch before people are prepared |
| Change Readiness & Adoption | People prepared to operate in the future state | Technical success but business failure |

## Delivery Phase Alignment

| Project Phase | Change Activities |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Initiation | Change risk assessment, leadership alignment, stakeholder mapping |
| Design | Impact assessment, readiness planning |
| Build | Communications, manager preparation, learning design |
| Test | Role validation, practice, readiness checks |
| Deploy | Adoption support, issue escalation, hypercare |
| Stabilise | Reinforcement, performance review, BAU transfer |

## Impact Dimensions

When assessing change impact, evaluate across these dimensions:
1. Processes
2. Systems and tools
3. Controls and approvals
4. Decision rights
5. Workload
6. Service model
7. Team interactions
8. Required knowledge
9. Performance expectations
10. Mindset and behaviours

## Resistance Sources & Responses

| Source | Response |
|--------|----------|
| Unclear value | Connect to specific business outcomes with examples |
| Loss of autonomy | Give input on implementation approach |
| Increased workload | Show long-term efficiency gain, remove low-value tasks |
| Role confusion | Clear role-specific guidance, test understanding |
| Leadership distrust | Increase sponsor visibility, address directly |
| Change fatigue | Acknowledge volume, sequence changes |
| Weak manager support | Equip managers with guides, coach directly |
| Insufficient training | Add hands-on practice, extend support |
| Compliance fear | Clarify expectations vs penalties, provide safety nets |

## Measurement Framework

| Category | Key Measures |
|----------|-------------|
| Adoption | % using new process/tool, usage by role, behaviour completion |
| Proficiency | Task accuracy, error rates, reduced workarounds, manager validation |
| Readiness | Sponsor effectiveness, manager prep, comms reach, learning confidence |
| Sustainment | Continued use, reduced workarounds, reinforcement actions, BAU ownership |

## Escalation Triggers

Flag when any of these conditions exist:
1. Sponsor engagement is weak or inconsistent
2. Impacted groups don't understand the reason for change
3. Manager readiness is low
4. Training exists but live performance is weak
5. Legacy workarounds remain attractive
6. Launch timing exceeds absorption capacity
7. Critical controls implemented technically but not behaviourally

## Governance Questions

This skill should answer:
- Is the organization ready for deployment?
- Which groups face the greatest adoption risk?
- What sponsor action is required now?
- Are line managers prepared to lead through the change?
- What needs to happen before ownership moves to BAU?
