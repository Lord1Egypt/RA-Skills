# Scoring Rubric

The score is a review aid, not a hiring decision.

## Score Basis

- `resume_plus_jd_company`: total score is resume quality out of 60 plus JD/company match out of 40.
- `resume_quality_only`: no JD/company context was evaluated; total score is normalized from the resume-quality dimensions to 100.

Batch summaries must show score basis and must not compare the two bases as equivalent.

## Enterprise Hiring Calibration

Score with recruiter judgment for enterprise roles, not as an academic ranking or resume-writing contest.

- Treat education as an important foundation signal, especially for research-heavy or highly technical roles.
- Treat papers and competitions as supporting evidence of learning ability, research taste, modeling skill, or technical depth.
- Do not let a small number of moderate papers or competition awards outweigh a concrete, technically plausible project with visible business, production, customer, delivery, cost, quality, scale, or efficiency impact.
- Give more weight to projects that show scenario constraints, personal ownership, implementation details, deployment or delivery status, measurable effects, and post-launch operation or iteration.
- When no JD is provided, still distinguish landed enterprise projects, pilot delivery, PoC, academic research, coursework, and vague project claims. A plausible landed project with clear effects should generally be stronger evidence than generic academic output alone.
- When JD is provided, prioritize JD-relevant landed projects and technology fit over impressive but unrelated signals.
- Avoid rigid caps or automatic penalties. Use holistic model judgment, explain the tradeoff, and make deductions evidence-based.
- Do not infer facts that are not visible in the resume, JD, or company background.

## Resume Quality, 60 Points

| Dimension | Points | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| information_completeness | 6 | Education, experience, projects, skills, achievements, contact details. |
| structure_readability | 5 | Clear hierarchy, readable density, concise wording, ATS-friendly structure. |
| experience_credibility | 9 | Coherent timeline, specific ownership, responsibilities consistent with results. |
| project_delivery_impact | 18 | Enterprise or real-world project delivery, production or pilot status, customer or business scenario, measurable effects, scale, cost, quality, efficiency, reliability, users, revenue, or operational value. |
| technical_depth_and_feasibility | 10 | Concrete algorithms, systems, tools, data, architecture, constraints, implementation path, personal contribution, and technical plausibility. |
| education_research_competition_signal | 7 | Degree fit, school or lab context, papers, patents, competitions, awards, and research outputs as supporting signals rather than substitutes for delivery evidence. |
| risk_review_signals | 5 | Contradictions, vague claims, missing context, sensitive information, manual review items. |

## JD and Company Match, 40 Points

| Dimension | Points | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| hard_requirement_match | 8 | Visible match with job-related hard requirements. Gaps require manual review, not automatic rejection. |
| core_capability_match | 10 | Match to JD duties and required capabilities. |
| jd_relevant_project_match | 14 | Concrete projects whose scenario, technology, delivery status, and effects align with the JD. |
| domain_industry_relevance | 4 | Relevant business domain, customer type, product form, technical stack, or industry context. |
| seniority_match | 3 | Ownership, complexity, leadership, influence, delivery scope. |
| interview_validation_value | 1 | Produces useful verification questions. |
