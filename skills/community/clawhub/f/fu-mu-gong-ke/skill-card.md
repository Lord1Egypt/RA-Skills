## Description: <br>
Fu Mu Gong Ke is a Chinese-language parenting psychology support skill for structured dialogue, emotion recognition, scenario matching, safety checks, and optional local tools. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[yun520-1](https://clawhub.ai/user/yun520-1) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Parents, caregivers, and support-oriented agents use this skill to structure Chinese-language conversations about child development, family conflict, emotional distress, and parent self-reflection. It is not a substitute for licensed mental health, medical, or crisis care. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill may process sensitive family, child-related, or emotional-support information. <br>
Mitigation: Use it only in contexts where that sensitivity is acceptable, avoid sharing unnecessary personal details, and review outputs before acting on them. <br>
Risk: Optional tools can save local assessment, feedback, insight, or session data when local persistence is enabled. <br>
Mitigation: Keep local persistence disabled unless that storage is intended, and review or remove local data according to the user's retention needs. <br>
Risk: The skill discusses self-harm, abuse, medical, psychological, and crisis situations but is not professional care. <br>
Mitigation: Use local emergency services, crisis resources, or licensed professionals for imminent risk, diagnosis, treatment, or abuse-related intervention. <br>
