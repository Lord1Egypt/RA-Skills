# 03 Options Playbook

## Highest-Confidence Pattern

The clearest option pattern is **owning long-dated long calls on high-conviction names, often alongside underlying shares**.

## Direct Evidence

### 1. Repeated direct use of `long call`

- Berkshire earnings post:
  - If Monday gives a chance, he wants to add `long call` in stages.
- Realized gains summary:
  - He states that first-half `long call` positions in Tesla, TSM, and AMD were fully exited.
  - Google was mostly exited.
  - Apple was being staged out.
  - Alibaba and BRKB were still being held patiently.
- Apple post:
  - He waits patiently to stage out Apple `long call`.
  - If Tesla offers a better price, he wants to rotate into it.

### 2. Underlying stock plus `long call`

- The 2025 summary quoted on Longbridge explicitly says `正股 + longcall` was the core route around AI.
- Even though this is seen through a repost/snippet, it aligns tightly with direct posts and should be treated as medium-confidence reinforcement.

## Second-Hand Reinforcement

- Another user attributes the following rules to him:
  - `long call` is leveraged investment with certainty
  - `short call` is short-term speculation
  - buy `long call` on large dips
  - use at least six months of duration
  - balance options and underlying shares rather than going extreme
- Another user references his Apple call management:
  - `210` calls sold first, `230` calls still held

These details are second-hand, so they should inform the skill cautiously rather than be stated as unquestioned fact.

## Practical Playbook

### Setup selection

- Use options mainly on top-tier names already inside the core watchlist.
- The option is not a separate casino instrument; it is an amplified expression of an already accepted stock thesis.

### Timing

- He appears to prefer buying when:
  - markets are fearful
  - a quality name gives a “good price”
  - post-earnings or macro dislocation creates discomfort
- He appears to avoid chasing random upside in names he does not trust.

### Position management

- Scale in:
  - Berkshire post explicitly mentions staged adding.
- Scale out:
  - Apple and broader portfolio posts explicitly mention staged exits.
- Rotate:
  - If one thesis matures and another high-conviction name offers a better price, rotate rather than sit still.

## Thin or Missing Evidence

- No strong direct evidence for:
  - put buying as a core tool
  - short premium as a systematic strategy
  - multi-leg spread structures as the default expression
  - Greek-based decision rules
  - explicit rolling mechanics

## Working Conclusion

The final skill should treat `long call` as the default option language:

- high-conviction names
- non-random entries
- staged buying
- staged exits
- optional pairing with underlying shares

It should not pretend the source supports a full options-engineering playbook.
