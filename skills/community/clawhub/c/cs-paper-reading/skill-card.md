## Description: <br>
Guides agents through Keshav's three-pass method for reading computer science papers and producing structured paper-reading analysis. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>
[overdue-lin](https://clawhub.ai/user/overdue-lin) <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Developers, researchers, students, and reviewers use this skill to read computer science papers systematically for literature review, peer review, study, or implementation planning. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Broad research-paper trigger phrases may activate the workflow when the user only wants a brief answer. <br>
Mitigation: Confirm that the user wants structured CS-paper reading before applying the multi-pass workflow. <br>
Risk: Paper-reading outputs can be incomplete or misleading if conclusions are not checked against the source paper. <br>
Mitigation: Use the pass checklists and review the final report against the original paper before relying on it. <br>
Risk: One reference template includes a small mixed-language wording issue that could be unclear to some readers. <br>
Mitigation: Clarify ambiguous checklist wording before using it as an evaluation criterion. <br>
