## Use Case: <br>
External users, healthcare learners, clinicians, researchers, and educators use this skill for medical education, clinical reasoning support, research appraisal, and patient-facing explanation. It is framed as educational and decision-support guidance, not diagnosis or patient-specific treatment. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Medical responses could be mistaken for diagnosis or patient-specific treatment advice. <br>
Mitigation: Frame responses as educational or decision-support material and direct patient-specific decisions to qualified medical professionals. <br>
Risk: Dosing, emergencies, and current clinical guidance can require up-to-date authoritative review. <br>
Mitigation: Prompt users to verify dosing, emergency concerns, and current guidelines with authoritative clinical sources and licensed professionals. <br>
Risk: An agent cannot examine a patient or integrate bedside clinical judgment. <br>
Mitigation: State these limits clearly and avoid extrapolating beyond the information provided. <br>
