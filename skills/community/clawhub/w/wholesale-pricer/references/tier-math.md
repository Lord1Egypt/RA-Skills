# Tier Math — Worked Examples

Three canonical tier schedules with full margin math, so you can adapt to your SKU without guessing.

## Example A — $28 candle, 6-pack case, COGS $6.50

| Tier | Units | Unit price | Off MSRP | Case $ | Gross margin $ | Gross margin % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MSRP | 1 | $28.00 | 0% | — | — | — |
| Tier 1 | 6 | $14.00 | 50% | $84 | $7.50 | 54% |
| Tier 2 | 36 (6 cases) | $13.30 | 52.5% | $79.80 | $6.80 | 51% |
| Tier 3 | 144 (24 cases) | $12.60 | 55% | $75.60 | $6.10 | 48% |

**Takeaway**: Margin compresses from 54% to 48% as volume grows. That is acceptable; the volume 24× makes up for the 6-point margin drop.

## Example B — $60 tumbler, 12-pack case, COGS $14

| Tier | Units | Unit price | Off MSRP | Case $ | Gross margin $ | Gross margin % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MSRP | 1 | $60 | 0% | — | — | — |
| Tier 1 | 12 | $30 | 50% | $360 | $16.00 | 53% |
| Tier 2 | 60 (5 cases) | $28.20 | 53% | $338.40 | $14.20 | 50% |
| Tier 3 | 240 (20 cases) | $27.00 | 55% | $324 | $13.00 | 48% |

**Takeaway**: Same structure scales naturally. At Tier 3 with 240 units, the case-level $ margin is ~$156 — enough to justify the discount.

## Example C — $12 personal care, 24-pack case, COGS $2.80

| Tier | Units | Unit price | Off MSRP | Case $ | Gross margin $ | Gross margin % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MSRP | 1 | $12 | 0% | — | — | — |
| Tier 1 | 24 | $6.00 | 50% | $144 | $3.20 | 53% |
| Tier 2 | 120 (5 cases) | $5.70 | 52.5% | $136.80 | $2.90 | 51% |
| Tier 3 | 480 (20 cases) | $5.40 | 55% | $129.60 | $2.60 | 48% |

**Takeaway**: For low-ASP SKUs, ensure the case $ is enough to cover fulfillment (typically ≥$100). Anything below $80 at Tier 1 means you are losing money on every pick-pack.

## The tier-step math

Each tier step should:

- Require a meaningful volume jump (≥20% unit count or ≥50% case count).
- Give a meaningful discount (2–3 percentage points off MSRP).
- Still produce gross margin ≥45% at the top tier (for most consumer products).

Below 45% gross margin, you lose the ability to fund marketing, reserves, and overhead. If Tier 3 math doesn't hit 45%, either the MSRP is too low or COGS is too high — fix the product economics before rolling out wholesale.

## When to add a Tier 4 (rarely)

Only add a Tier 4 if:

- A retailer commits to ≥$100k annual volume with a term contract.
- You have a spare SKU you can reserve as exclusive (protects DTC).
- Legal has reviewed an MAP carve-out for this tier.

Otherwise, a sidebar negotiation (MDF, co-op ad spend, exclusive color) is cheaper than a structural 4th tier that all your other accounts will ask about.

## Case-pack guidance

Case pack drives MOQ, ship-cost per unit, and warehouse efficiency. Common case packs:

- 6 for candles, ceramics, glassware (breakage math).
- 12 for most consumer goods (default).
- 24 for low-ASP personal care or consumables.
- 48 or 72 for very low-ASP (socks, bar soap).

If your case pack does not match the retailer's case pack expectation in the category, they will ask for "broken case" pricing — which breaks your margin. Match to the category norm from day one.
