## Description: <br>
WIT is a whatifthen framework that helps agents clarify early-stage product ideas, challenge assumptions, reduce scope, and turn vague proposals into concrete decisions. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Publisher: <br>

### License/Terms of Use: <br>
MIT-0 <br>


## Use Case: <br>
Product teams, founders, developers, and planning-focused agents use WIT when a feature idea, project proposal, rewrite, integration, or strategic choice needs problem clarification before execution. The skill guides the conversation toward observed facts, explicit assumptions, a narrow wedge, non-goals, a decision state, and a concrete next step. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
Global <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: The skill has a broad activation scope and can shape product, planning, and strategy discussions beyond narrow implementation tasks. <br>
Mitigation: Use it when clarification or decision review is the goal; switch to a narrower execution skill once the problem, wedge, decision, and next action are clear. <br>
Risk: Product decisions may be distorted if assumptions are treated as observed evidence. <br>
Mitigation: Keep observations, assumptions, and inferences separate, and validate the most important uncertainty before committing to a larger build. <br>
